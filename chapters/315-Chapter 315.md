The blade found the cultist's throat before the man could finish his war cry. Kael wrenched the steel free, already dropping into a combat crouch as two more figures emerged from the smoke billowing across the Ashenmoor Wastes. Shell craters pockmarked the battlefield behind him, and the distant thunder of artillery shook loose dirt from the trench lip where he'd positioned himself.
"You dare!" The nearest cultist swung a serrated axe wreathed in sickly violet light. "The Abyss wakes!"
Kael sidestepped. The axe whistle past his ribs, close enough to part his coat. His left hand shot out, fingers closing around the man's wrist. Spiritual energy flooded into him—not much, a cultivator barely past the first threshold, but enough to tell him this fool carried combat techniques worth cataloging.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 2/5 Awakened essence from the remains. Iron Skin acquired.]
The notification flickered across his vision. Kael absorbed the defensive technique, feeling muscle memory unspool in his mind. He redirected the man's momentum, using the cultist's own swing to spin him into his companion. Both bodies tumbled into the mud.
A third enemy charged from his left flank. Kael's fingers found the Soul Reaver's hilt, and violet-black energy crackled along the blade's edge. One thrust, one kill. He touched the cooling flesh of the fallen to harvest the residual power.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 3/7 Awakened essence from the remains. Combat Intuition (Lesser) acquired.]
The battlefield stretched before him in chaos. Trenches carved through ash-grey earth, communication lines shattered, supply depots burning with unnatural green flames that no water could smother. Three days ago, the Ashenmoor front had held steady. Now the abyssal cultists pushed through every gap the Orders left unguarded.
Kael pulled himself from the trench and surveyed the carnage. Bodies lay in tangled heaps—Awakened and common soldiers alike, their spiritual signatures fading into the ambient noise of a realm bleeding out. His kind of hunting ground, if he cared to admit it. The dead offered everything the living guarded so jealously.
"Reaver!"
The shout came from behind. Kael spun, Soul Reaver raised, only to find a Six Orders commander striding toward him through the smoke. The woman's armor bore the sunburst insignia of the Order of Dawn, and her eyes carried the hollow exhaustion of someone who'd watched too many subordinates die.
"You're the one they call Kael Ashborn." She didn't phrase it as a question. "The one who harvests from corpses."
"Word travels fast."
"Not fast enough." She gestured toward the burning horizon. "We've lost four supply convoys in two days. The eastern flank collapsed an hour ago. Iron Vanguard pulled back to consolidate the center, which means—"
"Which means the cultists will flank them within the hour."
The commander's jaw tightened. "You're tactical."
"I read battlefields for a living. Or I did, before someone decided execution was the proper response to false accusations." Kael sheathed his blade. "What's your name, Commander?"
"Vessa. Commander Lyria Vessa, Order of Dawn, Fourth Strategic Division." She studied him with the assessing gaze of someone calculating odds. "I know what you are, Soul Reaver. The Council of Six has your file. Death-row prisoner, executed for murder, resurrected through unknown means. You've been operating in Thornwick for—"
"How long have the cultists been running coordinated strikes?"
Vessa blinked at the interruption. "What?"
"The supply convoys. They weren't ambushed randomly. Look at the pattern." Kael pointed toward a burning wagon trail. "They're targeting communication nodes and provisioning depots specifically. This isn't opportunistic raiding. Someone planned this campaign weeks in advance."
Silence stretched between them. The artillery barrage resumed, shells screaming overhead to explode somewhere behind the cultist lines.
"You're suggesting organization," Vessa said finally.
"I'm stating fact. Six Orders have been fighting the Abyss for centuries. How often do you see abyssal cultists execute coordinated military strategy?"
"Never." A new voice, deep and resonant, came from the smoke. A massive figure emerged—the commander had brought an escort, a mountain of a man whose spiritual pressure filled the air like weight. Stage four, Sovereign class, just like Kael. "The Entities don't think tactically. They corrupt and consume. The cults that serve them are fanatics, not strategists."
"And yet." Kael spread his hands. "Here we are, watching fanatics dismantle a front that held for three months."
The newcomer stepped closer, and Kael got his first clear look at him. Scarred face, close-cropped grey hair, eyes that had seen too much death to flinch from more. The Order of Dawn emblem on his chest bore additional markings—a field commander, then.
"I'm Marshal Aldric Thorn, Order of Dawn, First Army." The old soldier extended a hand. Kael took it, noting the calluses from decades of blade work. "Commander Vessa speaks highly of your capabilities. I see she wasn't exaggerating."
"She hasn't seen me harvest yet."
"I've heard enough." Thorn released his grip. "The Ashenmoor front is failing. The Iron Vanguard is stretched thin, the Arcane Spire lost their command structure to a suicide cult three days ago, and the Veiled Circle is too busy fighting each other to send reinforcements. Which leaves the Order of Dawn holding a line that should take three divisions."
"How many do you have?"
"Two thousand Awakened. Ten thousand spiritually inert support staff." Thorn's expression hardened. "Against what our scouts estimate as fifteen thousand cultists, with more arriving hourly. And at the center of their formation—"
"Zarek." The name escaped Kael before he could stop it. His hand drifted to the Soul Reaver's hilt. "Prime Zarek commands this offensive?"
The two commanders exchanged glances.
"You know him?" Vessa asked.
"We've met." Kael's voice went flat. "He framed me for the Thornwick massacre. Got me sentenced to death."
Thorn's eyebrows rose. "Then this battle just became personal for you."
"It became personal the moment I woke up in my own grave." Kael turned toward the smoke-shrouded horizon, where the green flames of the cultist camps burned brightest. "Tell me about the seventy-two seals."
The request hung in the air. Vessa's hand moved toward her sword. Thorn only folded his arms.
"That's classified information," the marshal said. "Only Awakened above stage three are supposed to—"
"I'm stage four. Sovereign. That information asymmetry disappeared the moment I broke through." Kael kept his gaze fixed on the distant flames. "The seal network is failing. The cultists aren't just attacking supply lines. They're hitting the seals themselves. This entire offensive is a distraction while they weaken specific points in the network."
Thorn exhaled slowly. "You're more informed than your file suggests."
"I wasn't always a corpse-robber." Kael remembered fragments—the first Soul Reaver, whoever they'd been, had carved knowledge into his bones alongside the harvesting ability. "The seal network contains the seventy-two Entities. The Entities are the original inhabitants of the Abyss. The cultists want to free them."
"And if they succeed?" Vessa's voice carried genuine fear.
"Then the Veil tears completely. The Material and the Abyss merge. Everything humanity built becomes raw spiritual energy for the Entities to consume." Kael turned back to face the commanders. "But you already know this. The question is how much time we have before the seals fail completely."
"Projections suggest six months before catastrophic degradation." Thorn's admission came reluctantly. "The cultists have been targeting specific weak points for the past two years. Prime Zarek has a map of the most vulnerable seals. If he coordinates a simultaneous assault on all of them—"
"The Entities break free."
"All seventy-two. At once."
The weight of it settled over the battlefield like ash. Kael processed the implications. A war spanning centuries, maintained by the Six Orders through sacrifice and vigilance, balanced on a knife's edge because one fanatical cult possessed the right knowledge.
"The Sepulcher tablets," he murmured.
Vessa stiffened. "What did you say?"
"The first Soul Reaver. Whoever they were, they recorded something in those tablets. Something about the seals, about the Entities, about the nature of the Abyss itself." Kael's mind raced through fragments. "Zarek wants them. The Shadow Court wants them. Every faction with enough power to matter has been hunting those tablets for decades."
"And you?" Thorn asked. "Do you want them?"
"I want Zarek dead. The tablets are a means to that end."
A commotion at the eastern trench line interrupted their conversation. Soldiers scrambled back from the earthworks, shouting warnings. Shapes moved through the smoke—humanoid but wrong, their proportions stretched and twisted, spiritual signatures blazing with abyssal corruption.
"Contact east!" A sergeant's voice cracked with panic. "We have Entity-touched in the line! Three—no, six of them!"
Kael's hand found his blade. "Marshal. With your permission."
Thorn studied him for a long moment, then nodded. "Commander Vessa, take a squad and flank from the north. I'll hold the center. Let the Soul Reaver work."
Kael didn't wait for further instruction. He vaulted the trench wall and sprinted toward the eastern position, Soul Reaver singing with eager energy. The Entity-touched were faster than normal humans, their corrupted flesh granting unnatural speed and strength. But they were still just corrupted Awakened—spiritual signatures twisted but recognizable. And wherever there was a spiritual signature, there was essence to harvest.
The first creature lunged at him with claws extended. Kael ducked beneath the swing, drove his blade through its chest, and slammed his palm against its cooling flesh the moment death registered.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 7/10 Entity-touched essence from the remains. Abyssal Resistance (Minor) acquired.]
The notification pulsed through his vision. Kael absorbed the resistance trait, feeling his body adjust to better tolerate abyssal corruption. One down, five remaining.
The second creature came at him from the right, moving with surprising coordination. Kael parried its strike, felt the impact shudder through his arm, and riposted with a thrust that took it through the throat. Another harvest, another notification.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 5/8 Entity-touched essence from the remains. Sinew Eater acquired.]
The third and fourth attacked simultaneously, flanking him. Kael twisted away from one swipe, felt claws tear through his coat, and countered with a spinning slash that opened both creatures from hip to shoulder. They collapsed in a spray of corrupted blood.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 9/12 Entity-touched essence from the remains. Dual Wielding Mastery (Lesser) acquired.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 4/7 Entity-touched essence from the remains. Iron Will acquired.]
Two more harvests in rapid succession. Kael's mind processed the incoming techniques, filing them alongside the combat knowledge he was accumulating. The Soul Reaver worked exactly as it always had—extract essence, acquire abilities, grow stronger with every corpse.
But these Entity-touched were different. Their essence carried something else. Residues of the Entities themselves, fragments of Abyssal power woven into their corrupted souls.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 2/3 Abyssal Entity essence from the remains. Soul Anchor (Fragmentary) acquired.]
The notification hit Kael like ice water. He stared at the two remaining corpses, aware of what he'd harvested.
Soul Anchor. The technique the original Soul Reaver had used to maintain existence after death.
"Soul Reaver!"
Vessa's voice cut through his shock. Kael looked up to find the commander sprinting toward him, her blade drawn, her expression twisted with something between fear and determination.
"The entity-touched weren't random," she panted as she reached him. "They came straight for your position. Someone told them where you'd be."
"I noticed."
"The Shadow Court." Vessa grabbed his arm, pulling him toward cover. "They have agents among the cultists. Spies in every Order. If they know you're here—"
"They've always known where I am." Kael's mind raced through the implications. The Soul Reaver's origin, the Sepulcher tablets, the Shadow Court's interest in harvesting techniques. "Since Thornwick, probably. Since before my execution."
"Then why wait until now to send Entity-touched after you?"
"Because I just became more valuable alive." Kael's grip tightened on his blade. "The Soul Reaver that walks through this battle, harvesting Entity-touched, absorbing Abyssal essence—that's information. That's proof of concept. Whoever controls the Shadow Court wants to study how the original technique worked."
"And if they capture you?"
"Then they get a working Soul Reaver without having to breed one from scratch." Kael met her gaze. "I'm not letting that happen."
The artillery barrage intensified, shells falling closer to their position. Somewhere in the distance, a horn sounded—cultist war call, answering horn for horn from the Order lines. The battle for the Ashenmoor front had entered its critical phase.
"Marshal Thorn!" A runner skidded to a halt beside them, gasping for breath. "Eastern scouts report the main cultist force is moving. Thirty minutes, maybe less, until they hit our flank."
Thorn appeared at the trench line, his massive form silhouetted against the smoke. "Then we hold for thirty minutes. Send word to the Iron Vanguard—tell them if they don't reinforce within the hour, the Dawn banner falls and the seal network loses its primary maintenance division."
The runner sprinted away. Thorn descended into the trench beside them, his expression grave.
"Soul Reaver. I've fought alongside Awakened from every Order. I've seen miracles and atrocities in equal measure. But I've never seen anyone harvest from Entity-touched before." He studied Kael with new interest. "What did you gain from those creatures?"
"Resistance to their corruption. Combat techniques. And something else." Kael decided honesty served better than concealment. "A fragment of the original Soul Reaver's power. Soul Anchor. The technique that lets someone persist after death."
Vessa's face went pale. "The technique is real? We thought it was myth."
"It's real. I just absorbed it from corrupted essence." Kael turned the fragmentary knowledge over in his mind. "The original Soul Reaver used it to survive their own execution. Maybe that's how I woke up in my grave instead of moving on to whatever comes after."
"Or maybe," Thorn said slowly, "someone else used it to resurrect you. Someone who wanted a Soul Reaver operating in the world."
The implication hung between them. Kael had assumed his resurrection was accidental—the Soul Reaver activating in his final moments, preserving enough essence to reconstitute his body. But Thorn's suggestion carried a darker weight. What if his existence served someone else's purpose?
The thought was interrupted by a new commotion at the western trench line. Shouts of alarm, the clash of steel, the shriek of abyssal creatures. The cultists had found a gap in the defenses.
"Go," Thorn ordered. "Whatever schemes surround you, Soul Reaver, the battle comes first. Hold the line for thirty minutes. After that—" He shrugged massive shoulders. "After that, we improvise."
Kael didn't waste words. He climbed from the trench and sprinted toward the western breach, Soul Reaver already humming with accumulated power. The battlefield stretched around him in chaos—soldiers fighting, dying, screaming orders that no one followed. And everywhere, the dead. Awakened and commoner alike, their spiritual signatures fading into the ash-grey air.
His hunting ground. His harvest.
He reached the western breach to find a squad of cultists pouring through a gap in the earthworks. Kael waded into them without hesitation. His blade sang, his palm found flesh, and notifications flooded his vision with every kill.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 3/5 Awakened essence from the remains. Spirit Sight (Basic) acquired.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 4/6 Awakened essence from the remains. Blade Counter acquired.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 2/4 Awakened essence from the remains. Fortitude acquired.]
Techniques accumulated. Combat knowledge layered upon combat knowledge. Kael moved through the cultist assault like a reaper through wheat, his Soul Reaver carving a path of harvested power through enemy after enemy. Each corpse taught him something new. Each extraction brought him closer to understanding what he had become.
And then, cutting through the chaos of battle, a familiar voice.
"Kael Ashborn."
He spun. Across the breach, standing in the smoke like a specter from his worst nightmares, Prime Zarek regarded him with cold amusement. The man who had framed him. The cultist who had orchestrated the Thornwick massacre. The architect of his death and resurrection both.
"You survived," Zarek continued, his tone conversational despite the carnage surrounding them. "I wondered if you would. The Soul Reaver technique is unpredictable. So many variables, so many ways for the resurrection to fail."
"You knew." Kael's blade lowered slightly. "You knew what I would become when they executed me."
"I arranged for the execution, yes. A death-row prisoner with unfinished business and rage to spare—the perfect vessel for the Soul Reaver's return." Zarek spread his hands, gesturing at the battlefield. "Did you think this war was about the seals? About the Entities? This entire conflict exists to give you a battlefield. A place where the dead accumulate faster than anyone can burn them. A harvest in constant supply."
"Why?"
"Because you need to grow stronger. Because the Seventy-Two require a specific configuration of power to break free, and a Soul Reaver at full strength is the key that makes everything possible." Zarek smiled, and there was nothing human in it. "You're not a prisoner, Kael. You're not a survivor. You're a tool. A means to an end that was set in motion before you were born."
The words struck harder than any blade. Kael felt his certainty waver—not in his abilities, but in his understanding of his own existence. Everything he'd done since waking in his grave, every choice, every battle, every harvest—had any of it been truly his?
"You're lying."
"Am I?" Zarek turned slightly, gesturing toward the smoke-shrouded horizon. "Ask yourself why the Shadow Court has been watching you since Thornwick. Ask why every Order has a file on your activities. Ask why I'm standing here talking instead of killing you where you stand."
"Because you're not here to fight." Kael's grip tightened on his blade. "You're here to talk. To plant doubt. To—"
"To show you the truth." Zarek's spiritual pressure flared, and for a moment, the air itself seemed to thicken. Stage five. Demigod. A full realm above Kael's current position. "I could kill you, Soul Reaver. I could end your existence with a thought. But that would waste a perfectly prepared vessel. So instead, I'll tell you what happens next."
Kael held his ground, despite every instinct screaming at him to run. Zarek was stronger. Zarek had always been stronger. But the Soul Reaver wasn't about matching power for power. It was about taking what others had built and making it his own.
"Talk, then." Kael raised his blade. "I've got thirty minutes to hold this line. Let's see how much truth you can deliver before the Dawn soldiers arrive."
Zarek laughed—a sound like breaking glass. "The truth is simple, Kael Ashborn. The Soul Reaver was created by the first Entity to break free of the seal network. The Seventy-Two didn't wait for humanity to weaken the barriers. They built an escape route of their own. A walking key that could harvest power from any corpse, accumulate techniques without limit, and eventually become strong enough to unmake the seals from the inside."
"And the original Soul Reaver? The one who carved their knowledge into my bones?"
"Lost. Destroyed. Absorbed into the Entity that created the technique in the first place." Zarek's expression shifted to something almost like sympathy. "The Soul Reaver is a parasite, Kael. A fragment of Abyssal power wearing a human face. Every harvest you make brings you closer to what you truly are."
"Which is?"
"A door. Nothing more. A door that will open when the time comes, and let the Entities walk free into a world that doesn't know how to fight them."
The artillery barrage reached a new crescendo. Shells fell closer, shaking the earth beneath Kael's feet. He had maybe twenty minutes left before Thorn's deadline, and a Demigod-stage cultist Prime standing in front of him, spouting revelations about his true nature.
"Why tell me this?" Kael demanded.
"Because doubt is a more effective leash than chains." Zarek turned away, beginning to fade into the smoke. "Harvest well, Soul Reaver. Every death you take brings you closer to understanding what you are. And when you're ready—when you've grown strong enough to choose your own path—the real war begins."
He vanished into the smoke like he'd never existed.
Kael stood frozen for a long moment, processing the flood of information. The Soul Reaver's origin. The Entity's manipulation. The door waiting to open when he became strong enough.
A hand gripped his shoulder. He spun, blade rising, only to find Commander Vessa at his side.
"The reinforcements arrived," she said. "Iron Vanguard pushed through the cultist lines. The eastern flank is holding."
"And Zarek?"
"What about him?"
Kael looked at the empty space where the cultist Prime had stood. "Nothing. He was never here."
Vessa's expression suggested she didn't believe him, but she didn't press. "The marshal wants a report on the Entity-touched harvest. And there's something else—you need to see this."
She led him back toward the command trench, where a group of soldiers had gathered around something in the mud. Kael pushed through the crowd and felt his stomach clench.
A body. But not a cultist, not an Order soldier. A woman in Shadow Court blacks, her throat cut, her eyes staring at the smoke-shrouded sky. And clutched in her dead fingers, a note written in cipher.
"She appeared ten minutes ago," Vessa explained. "Walked right through our lines like she belonged there. Started running when our soldiers noticed her, but one of the Awakened caught up before she could escape."
Kael crouched beside the corpse, reading the cipher with growing unease. The message was fragmented, written in haste:
"—following Ashborn since Thornwick. Soul Reaver identification confirmed. Requesting extraction authorization. If he refuses—"
The rest was obscured by blood.
"Requesting extraction authorization," Vessa repeated. "They're going to try to grab you, Soul Reaver. The Shadow Court has been tracking you since before your execution, and now they're making moves."
Kael touched the woman's cooling flesh, harvesting what information he could.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 1/3 Awakened essence from the remains. Cipher Key acquired.]
The notification pulsed through his vision. A cipher key—not the message itself, but the means to decode messages like it. The Shadow Court communicated in layers, each level requiring different keys to access deeper information.
"What did you get?" Vessa asked.
"Enough to know I've been a pawn longer than I realized." Kael stood, wiping his palm on his coat. "The Shadow Court isn't just monitoring me. They're managing my development. Every move I've made since Thornwick, they've been tracking it, recording it, analyzing it."
"For what purpose?"
"To determine if I'm useful enough to recruit. Or dangerous enough to eliminate." Kael looked toward the horizon, where the green flames of the cultist camps still burned. "Zarek's revelation about the Soul Reaver's origin—it changes things. But I can't verify any of it. For all I know, everything he said was designed to manipulate me."
"So what do you do?"
Kael's hand found the Soul Reaver's hilt. "I keep harvesting. I keep growing stronger. And when I'm ready—when I understand what I've become—I'll find the truth for myself. The Entity that created this technique. The Shadow Court's true purpose. The location of the original Soul Reaver's tomb."
"And Zarek?"
"Zarek dies. Not because he framed me. Not because he's a cultist. Because he's the key to understanding what the Seventy-Two are really planning." Kael turned back toward the command trench. "The battle for the Ashenmoor front is just the beginning. The real war is hidden, and I'm only now starting to see its shape."
The sun was setting behind the smoke, painting the battlefield in shades of red and gold. Kael walked toward the command post, his mind churning with new information. The Soul Anchor technique, still fragmentary but growing clearer. The Shadow Court's surveillance network, exposed but not dismantled. The Entity's manipulation, real or fabricated, weaving through his existence like a poison in his blood.
A runner intercepted him before he reached the trench.
"Soul Reaver!" The young soldier's face was pale with shock. "Marshal Thorn sent me. There's been a breakthrough on the northern line. Cultists breached the main defense. They're heading for the seal maintenance facility."
Kael's blood went cold. The seal maintenance facility. If the cultists destroyed it, the Ashenmoor seal network would collapse within days. The Entities trapped beneath would begin to stir. The entire front would fall.
"How long until they reach the facility?"
"Twenty minutes. Maybe less."
Kael looked at the command trench, where Thorn and Vessa were still discussing strategy. He had new knowledge to process, a cipher key to decode, revelations about his nature to untangle. But twenty minutes meant twenty minutes before the war escalated beyond control.
He changed direction, heading north instead of toward the command post.
"Soul Reaver!" The runner scrambled to follow. "You can't go alone. The breach has at least a hundred cultists, and there are Entity-touched with them—"
"Then I'll harvest from a hundred and one."
Kael didn't slow down. The northern line would be chaos—cultists fighting to break through, Order soldiers fighting to hold, Entity-touched adding their corrupted power to the assault. A perfect hunting ground for someone with his abilities.
And maybe, if he was lucky, a chance to harvest something from a high-ranking cultist. Something that would tell him whether Zarek's revelations were truth or manipulation.
The Soul Reaver's blade hummed against his palm as he ran.
Behind him, the sun finished setting. Darkness swallowed the Ashenmoor front, and somewhere in the distance, an alarm bell began to ring.
