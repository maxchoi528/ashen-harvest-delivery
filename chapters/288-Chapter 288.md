The Drowned Quarter occupied Thornwick's oldest section, where wooden buildings sagged into marshland that had once been the city's harbor. The streets flooded at high tide, forcing residents to navigate on elevated walkways or accept waist-deep immersion twice daily. Population density had plummeted as the wealthy moved uphill, leaving crumbling tenements occupied by those with nowhere else to go.
Kael found the dead-drop location in the basement of a collapsed warehouse - a waterproof cache box anchored to foundation stones that had survived the building's decay. The cipher key opened three sequential locks, each mechanism more complex than the last. Inside, wrapped in oiled cloth, lay a single sheet of parchment.
The message contained no greeting, no signature. coordinates, a time, and a single sentence:
*The Soul Reaver who founded your path rests beneath the city. Ask where the first stone was laid.*
Kael read the words twice, committing them to memory. Then he held parchment to the cache box's small brazier, burning the evidence.
The first Soul Reaver. Not a predecessor but the origin point. The practitioner who'd discovered the harvesting ability, who'd developed the techniques Kael now wielded, whose death or disappearance had left the path open for others to stumble upon.
The Whisper King wanted him to find a tomb.
Kael climbed from the basement into the Drowned Quarter's perpetual twilight. Gas lamps rarely functioned in this district - too expensive to maintain, too often submerged. He navigated by feel and memory, his Sovereign-tier senses compensating for the darkness.
A sound reached him. Footsteps on the elevated walkway ahead, multiple sets moving with trained coordination. Kael flattened against a building's facade, melting into shadow through instinct than conscious effort.
The Shadow-Step technique served him well. He observed without being observed, tracking the patrol's movement across rotting planks.
Three figures in dark clothing, faces covered, weapons designed for quiet elimination. Iron Vanguard enforcement style, though they carried no identifying insignia. They moved with purpose, checking structures systematically.
They knew he would come here. Someone had predicted his response to the dead-drop message.
Kael waited until the patrol passed, then reversed his route, seeking alternative egress. The Drowned Quarter offered limited escape vectors, but he found one - a collapsed section of seawall creating a passage to the adjacent district, passable for someone willing to get wet.
He emerged from marsh water onto cobblestone streets, the transition from ancient poverty to industrial decay immediate and jarring. Steam-powered machinery rumbled in nearby factories. Workers trudged toward or from shifts, their faces blank with exhaustion.
A carriage waited at the intersection. Black lacquer, no identifying marks, horses stamping impatiently. As Kael approached, the door swung open of its own accord.
Inside, a single seat faced a small table bearing tea service and a covered dish that steamed gently. No occupant. No driver visible on the carriage's exterior.
An invitation. Or a test.
Kael approached the carriage cautiously, scanning for traps or ambush. The tea service appeared genuine - ceramic from the eastern provinces, leaves imported from the Amber Coast. The covered dish contained rice and roasted vegetables, simple fare prepared with care.
A note rested beside the plate, handwritten in elegant script:
*You survived the warehouse. Impressive. But the Whisper King's game extends beyond cult cells and seal manipulation. Your sister remains alive because she serves a purpose. That purpose aligns with neither the Orders nor the Shadow Court. Maybe it aligns with you.*
*The original Soul Reaver's tomb exists beneath the Ashworth Chapel. The chapel was demolished forty years ago. The basement remains accessible through the old crypt network. Inside, you will find answers about your abilities, your origins, and the true cost of what you do.*
*Your sister knows more than she has revealed. Ask her about the night she was taken. Ask her what she was doing before the Mortician's Guild acquired her.*
*Trust no one who claims to serve your interests. The Whisper King included.*
The note contained no signature, no indication of authorship. But the information about his sister - information Kael himself didn't possess - suggested either genuine alliance or elaborate manipulation.
He ate the rice, hunger outweighing caution. The food contained no poison, no spiritual contamination. Simple nourishment, freely given.
The carriage door closed softly. From somewhere in the distance, a voice spoke - not audible in the normal sense but projected directly into Kael's awareness through spiritual means.
"The Whisper King watches with interest. You have exceeded projections. But projections exist to be revised."
The voice vanished, leaving Kael alone with the emptied dish and the weight of impossible choices.
