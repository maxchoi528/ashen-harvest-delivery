Kael's blade sang through the smoke-thick air, severing the arm of a cultist who lunged too close. The man's scream cut short as Kael twisted the weapon free and drove an elbow into his throat, dropping him to the cobblestones.
"Contact left!" Gwen's voice cracked like a whip. Crossbow bolts sang from her position on the collapsed balcony, punching through two figures emerging from a doorway. "They knew we were coming."
"They weren't supposed to know," Lyra hissed. Her hands crackled with violet light as she deflected a blast of corrupted spiritual energy aimed at the group's rear. "Someone talked."
The alley narrowed ahead, funneling them toward the old foundry that served as their target. Kael counted the bodies already littering the street—eight, no, nine—and smelled copper mixing with the coal dust. Standard cult foot soldiers. Three dead before his team even entered the district. Someone had started this fight without waiting for the main engagement.
"Fall back to the junction," he ordered, scanning the rooftops. Shadows moved against the gaslight. "We're funneled."
"No." Gwen dropped beside him, reloading with practiced speed. Blood darkened her sleeve where a bolt had grazed her shoulder. "Fall back means they control the exit. We push through or we die in place."
Kael's enhanced perception caught the faint hum of spiritual energy concentrated in the building ahead. A trap, yes. But traps worked both directions.
"How many inside?"
"Twenty, maybe more." Lyra's eyes had gone distant, her shadow-sight painting the world in dark energy signatures. "But there's something else. Something wrong. The energy feels... Heavy. Like the air before a storm."
"They brought a Rift-Touched," Kael said. His stomach tightened into a hard knot, and the muscles across his shoulders drew taut as cold sweat prickled along the base of his neck. Cultists willing to use Abyssal corruption meant they weren't playing for captures anymore. "That's why they set up here. They're holding ground instead of fleeing."
"Meaning they want something specific." Gwen's jaw tightened. "This route. This location. Us."
The foundry loomed ahead, its broken windows like hollow eyes staring down at them. Wind carried the scent of old iron and something else—ozone, the smell of spiritual discharge. Kael mapped the streets in his mind. Three exits from the square. Two were blocked by rubble. The third...
"The sewers," Lyra said, reading his thoughts. "But they'll have covered them. This is a box."
"Then we don't play their game." Kael sheathed his blade and drew two of the knives he'd taken from the Merchant Quarter warehouse three nights ago. Simple weapons. No spiritual signature. "I'm going through the front."
"Through—" Gwen grabbed his arm. "Kael, there's a Rift-Touched in there. You can't just walk into—"
"I'm not walking." He met her eyes. "I'm drawing. You two take the roof access, come in from above. Whatever they're using to hold that many cultists in one place, it's not loyalty. It's compulsion. Break their formation, and they scatter."
"And if the Rift-Touched kills you before we arrive?"
"Then you finish the mission without me." He pulled free of her grip. "But I don't plan on dying today."
He crossed the square at a run.
The foundry's interior swallowed sound. Dust motes drifted through shafts of pale moonlight filtering through the shattered skylight. Kael's boots crunched on broken glass and rusted metal fragments. The main floor stretched before him, filled with the hulks of abandoned casting equipment—furnaces cold for decades, conveyor systems collapsed into tangles of iron.
And in the center, standing atop a platform built from shipping crates, a figure waited.
She was young. Barely twenty, dressed in the tattered robes of a common laborer. But her skin crawled with black veins, pulsing beneath the surface like something alive. Her eyes held no light—only hunger.
"Rift-Touched," Kael said. "You're controlling the others?"
"They're not controlled." Her voice came out layered, wrong—like two people speaking in imperfect unison. "They're... Freed. The Veil is thin here. The Abyss whispers truth."
"The Abyss whispers corruption."
"The Abyss whispers what you fear." She smiled, and her teeth had grown sharp. "They told me you'd come. The Soul Reaver who steals from the dead. They want you to know something, Kael Ashborn."
The use of his full name sent ice down his spine. This wasn't random cult activity. This was targeted. Personal.
"I'm listening."
"The one you hunt—Zarek—he serves something older than the cult. Older than the Orders. He carries a piece of the first seal, and he knows you're coming for it." She tilted her head at an inhuman angle. "But you won't reach him in time. The Whispering King has already given the command. Twelve seals break tonight. Twelve rifts open before dawn."
Twelve. Not one. A coordinated assault across the city.
"You're stalling," Kael said. "The cultists aren't here to kill us. They're here to keep us busy."
"You're perceptive." She raised her hand, and the shadows throughout the foundry writhed. Figures emerged from behind the furnaces—more cultists than he'd estimated, moving with the jerky precision of puppets. "The others are already in position. Your team is about to walk into a slaughter."
Gwen and Lyra. On the roof. Cut off from him.
"How do I know you're not lying?"
"You don't." Her smile widened. "But the rifts are real. I can feel them weakening. And when they open, everything changes. The Orders fall. The Concordat shatters. The world learns what waits on the other side of death."
She lunged.
Kael moved before conscious thought caught up. His body twisted sideways as corrupted energy carved a trench through the air where his head had been. The Rift-Touched moved faster than any human should—her joints bending in directions that made his eyes water.
He threw a knife. She caught it from the air without looking, fingers closing around the blade. Black blood welled from the cut, but she didn't seem to notice.
"Steel can't harm me anymore." She dropped the knife. "I am the door. I am the threshold. I am—"
Kael was already moving, circling left, drawing her attention while his eyes tracked the cultists filtering through the shadows. Fifteen, no, twenty. Armed with blades and crude spiritual amplifiers—ritual knives designed to disrupt Awakened abilities. They'd been trained to counter his kind.
Trained. Not the usual desperate conscripts who'd joined the cult after losing everything. These were deliberate. Prepared.
The Six Orders fight the Abyss together, Kael thought. But someone in those Orders had fed information to the cult. Someone who knew his routes, his team composition, his targets.
Dorian. The name surfaced unbidden. Lyra's contact at the Order of Dawn. He'd been the one to provide the tip about the foundry. He'd been the one to confirm the cult leadership's presence.
But the Rift-Touched was faster than his suspicions. She closed the distance in a heartbeat, claws raking across his chest. Kael threw up his arm—pain exploded through his forearm as her nails opened three parallel gashes through fabric and skin. He stumbled back, blood dripping onto the dusty floor.
"You taste different from the others." She licked her fingers, collecting his blood. "Stronger. Richer. Your soul must be... Exquisite."
"I get that a lot." Kael dropped into a fighting stance, ignoring the throbbing in his arm. Transcendent stage gave him advantages beyond raw power—his senses operated faster, his reactions sharper. But this woman had been fed on Abyssal energy for months, maybe longer. She was stronger than any cultist he'd faced.
A cultist broke from the group, charging with a spiritual amplifier raised. Kael sidestepped the swing, caught the man's wrist, and drove his palm into the cultist's solar plexus. The amplifier clattered to the ground. Before the man could recover, Kael grabbed his collar and threw him into two others, buying himself three seconds of space.
"The Soul Reaver abilities don't work the way you think," the Rift-Touched continued, circling. "Everyone assumes you steal power from the dead. But that's not accurate, is it? You don't take. You... Borrow. And eventually, you have to give back."
"Interesting philosophy." Kael grabbed a fallen cultist's weapon—a short sword, poorly balanced but serviceable. "Did the Whispering King teach you that?"
Her expression flickered. Just for an instant. Recognition.
"He didn't tell you I'd ask about him." Kael pressed the advantage. "He's not just running the cult, is he? He's something else entirely. Something from before."
"You presume to understand things beyond your stage." Her voice lost the layered quality, becoming singular and cold. "You are Transcendent. One step above Awakened. Do you know how many have reached Transcendent and believed themselves wise? How many have walked into the Abyss thinking they could bargain with its depths?"
"Enlighten me."
"They died." She raised both hands, and the shadows coalesced into a wall of living darkness between them. "As you will."
The wall surged forward.
Kael dove beneath it, rolling across the dusty floor. Shrapnel from the wall's passage shredded through the air above him. He came up running, not toward the Rift-Touched but toward the platform where she'd stood. If he could reach the spiritual energy source she was drawing from—
His boot struck something buried beneath the debris. A metal circle, inscribed with runes. A containment seal, cracked and bleeding raw spiritual energy into the air.
Not a trap. A lure.
"They wanted you to find it," the Rift-Touched said behind him. "The seal. The source. They want you to understand what you're facing."
Kael knelt beside the seal, his hand hovering above its surface. The energy hummed against his palm, calling to something deep within him. Something that recognized this signature.
"The first Soul Reaver made this," he said slowly. "This is from the original. From before the cult. Before the Orders."
"The first Soul Reaver didn't just fight the Abyss." The Rift-Touched landed on the platform beside him, her movements almost graceful now. "He became part of it. Became the first seal. The anchor that holds the rifts closed. And the Orders have been trying to replicate his power ever since. Soul essence harvested from the dying, stored in vials, used to Awaken new soldiers. All of it—poor imitations of what he sacrificed."
She knelt across from him, mirror images separated by the bleeding seal.
"Zarek carries a fragment of that original power. That's why the Whispering King chose him. That's why you're meant to kill him. Not because he betrayed the Orders. Because killing him will break the seal further, and the Whispering King needs the Veil to fall completely."
"Why?"
"Because he's waited two hundred years to return." Her eyes met his. "He was something like you, once. A harvester. A borrower of dead men's secrets. The power ate at him the way fire eats at wood—slowly, from the inside, until nothing remained but ash and appetite. The Abyss reshaped what was left. Gave it a throne. Gave it a purpose."
Her black-veined fingers traced the cracked edge of the seal. "The one you call the Whispering King walks the line between what he was and what the Abyss made him. Not living. Not dead. Something in between that remembers what it felt like to harvest souls, even as it commands the things that devour them."
Kael's jaw tightened. The comparison landed like a blade between his ribs—too close, too precise. A harvester. A borrower. Words that described what he did, what he was, wrapped around a figure who had become the very thing the Orders had spent centuries fighting.
"You're saying he was like me."
"I'm saying the road you walk has an end, Soul Reaver. And at that end waits a throne you can't imagine." She rose, her form flickering between solid and shadow. "Every time you harvest a soul, you take on a piece of their story. Their fears. Their failures. The first Soul Reaver did that for two hundred years. Do you know what it feels like to carry ten thousand deaths inside you? A hundred thousand? To remember every face, every name, every final moment?"
"I can guess."
"Stop." The word came sharp. "You can't know. You're Transcendent. You haven't harvested enough yet. But you will. And when you do, you'll understand why he chose to become the seal. Why he chose to forget."
Footsteps on the stairs behind him. Kael spun, blade raised—
Gwen emerged from the shadows, crossbow in hand. Blood matted her hair. A deep gash ran from her temple to her jaw.
"The roof was a trap," she gasped. "Lyra's cut off. There are more of them coming. We need to—"
"Wait." Kael held up a hand. "She's talking. There's something—"
The Rift-Touched laughed again, and this time it built into a shriek that shook dust from the rafters. The cultists moved, and violently, surging toward the platform with weapons raised.
"Time's up, Soul Reaver." The woman's form began to dissolve into shadow. "Run or fight. But know this—Lyra Vane isn't who you think she is. Ask her about the Shadow Court. Ask her why they let her leave. Ask her who ordered her last mission into the Abyss."
She vanished.
The cultists attacked.
Kael moved. His blade carved through the first cultist's guard, opening his throat. Blood sprayed across the platform. Kael dropped beside the body, hand pressing against the still-warm flesh.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted X from the remains. Strength of Conviction acquired.]
The ability settled into his awareness—a minor enhancement to his physical resilience. Standard harvest. Useful but unremarkable.
More cultists came. Gwen fired twice, dropping two men trying to flank. Kael waded into the press of bodies, letting his enhanced senses guide his strikes. Blade work wasn't his specialty, but the Soul Reaver abilities had sharpened his instincts. He moved like water through sand, finding gaps in defenses, exploiting openings before they fully formed.
A cultist's spiritual amplifier caught his shoulder, sending agony racing down his arm. Kael ignored the pain, pivoting, driving his elbow into the man's face. Cartilage crunched. The cultist dropped.
"Gwen, the door—"
"Already moving."
They fell back toward the exit, trading ground for time. The cultists followed, their attacks growing more desperate as Kael and Gwen carved through their ranks. Bodies fell in increasing numbers. Kael harvested three more—minor abilities, nothing significant, but each one adding to his growing pool of accumulated power.
The foundry's main doors exploded inward.
Lyra came through the gap like a wraith, violet light wreathing her fists. Two cultists who jumped to intercept her dissolved into screaming shadows, their forms unraveling as her power consumed them.
"The woman?" Gwen shouted.
"Gone." Lyra's voice was flat. Cold. "She had a teleportation seal. Someone gave her an escape route."
The remaining cultists faltered. Their Rift-Touched commander had abandoned them. Their numbers had been halved in minutes. Whatever compulsion had held them to this place was breaking.
Kael pressed the advantage. "Break them."
He and Gwen swept forward while Lyra covered their flanks. The cultists scattered, fleeing through windows and doors, abandoning their positions. The foundry stood empty except for the dead.
Kael leaned against a rusted furnace, catching his breath. His arm throbbed from the Rift-Touched's claws. Blood from the amplifier strike had soaked through his shirt. But nothing was broken. Nothing that wouldn't heal.
"Talk," he said to Lyra. "What did she tell you before she fled?"
Lyra's expression didn't change. "Nothing important. Just cultist rhetoric. The usual threats about the Abyss and the coming darkness."
"She's lying." Gwen's voice cut through the air. "I saw her face when that woman mentioned the Shadow Court. She knows something."
"Enough." Kael pushed off from the furnace, facing Lyra directly. "The Rift-Touched said to ask you about the Shadow Court. Said to ask why they let you leave. Said to ask who ordered your last mission into the Abyss."
Lyra went very still.
"That's not a conversation I'm going to have in a room full of corpses." Her eyes met his, and for the first time since he'd known her, Kael saw something like fear in them. "But if you're asking whether the Shadow Court let me go because they wanted me to, the answer is no. They let me go because I was useful out here. Because every move I make, every contact I develop, gets reported back to them."
"Even me."
"Even you." She didn't look away. "They've been tracking you since before we met, Kael. The Soul Reaver abilities made you valuable. The question isn't whether I'm reporting on you. The question is what I'm telling them."
Gwen's hand drifted toward her crossbow. "And what's the answer?"
"That you're dangerous. That you're not what the Orders expect. That killing Zarek might break something they can't control." Lyra's jaw tightened. "They want Zarek dead. The cult wants Zarek dead. The only ones who don't want him dead are the people paying him to stay alive."
"The Whispering King."
Lyra nodded slowly. "He's the one giving the orders. The one orchestrating everything. The Rift-Touched wasn't lying about that part. He controls the cult cells, the rifts, the entire operation. And he controls information flowing to every Order contact in the city."
"Including Dorian."
"Dorian's been compromised for six months." Lyra's voice dropped. "He didn't betray us deliberately. The Shadow Court has leverage on his family. They do what the Court demands, or their daughter dies. It's not complicated."
The pieces clicked together in Kael's mind. Dorian had fed them the foundry location. He'd confirmed the cult leadership's presence. He'd made it sound like a golden opportunity.
A trap. Bait.
"The rifts," Kael said. "The Rift-Touched said twelve seals break tonight. Twelve rifts open before dawn. If that's true—"
"Then the cult's not trying to weaken the network." Gwen finished the thought. "They're trying to collapse it. All at once. Overwhelming force instead of slow erosion."
"Which means—"
A sound cut through the foundry's silence. Low at first, like distant thunder. Then rising. Building. The sound of reality tearing at its seams.
Lyra's eyes went wide. "No. That's not possible. The seals should hold for—"
The ground shook.
Somewhere across the city, glass shattered in a chain reaction. People screamed. Kael felt the spiritual energy in the air spike, then stabilize at a new, higher baseline.
A rift had opened.
Not twelve. One. But one meant the others were following.
"The Whispering King changed the plan." Lyra's voice shook. "He's not waiting anymore. He's opening them all tonight."
Dawn was still hours away. But the world Kael had known was already ending.
Kael grabbed Lyra by the collar. "Take me to Dorian. Now."
"He won't tell you anything. The Shadow Court—"
"Then I'll make him." Kael released her. "The cult's moving. The Orders are compromised. If the rifts open fully, there won't be anyone left to fight the Abyss. We're done playing politics."
He walked toward the door, not waiting to see if the others followed. Behind him, Gwen fell into step. After a moment, Lyra did too.
They emerged into a city transformed. The gaslights flickered against an unnatural sky, the stars blotted out by something darker moving at the edges of vision. The air tasted of ozone and copper.
And from somewhere to the east, that sound rose again—lower now, resonant, like the heartbeat of something vast and hungry.
The rift was spreading.
