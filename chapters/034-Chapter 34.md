Kael pressed his back against the damp brick, breath shallow, fingers curled around the cold iron of his blade. Three men blocked the alley's mouth. Steam hissed from a ruptured pipe overhead, shrouding the narrow passage in white mist. One carried a serrated blade that caught the gaslight. Another twirled a length of chain with practiced ease.
"You're the corpse-walker," the tallest one said. Not a question. His grin showed two missing teeth. "The Veiled Market's been waiting for you."
"Disappointed I don't have horns?"
The chain-wielder lunged. Kael pivoted, steel screaming against steel as he deflected the first strike. His free hand shot out, catching the attacker's wrist and wrenching him off balance. A brutal knee to the gut. The man folded with a grunt.
The tall one moved faster than expected, closing distance with a speed that spoke of Awakened blood. His blade carved an arc toward Kael's throat. Kael dropped beneath it, rolled across the slick cobblestones, and came up with his knife buried in the man's shoulder.
Pain flared. The tall one's free hand grabbed Kael's collar, slamming him against the wall. Breath stank of cheap ale.
"Orders above you," the man hissed. "Dead or alive, corpse-walker. Your choice."
Kael's fingers found the wound on the man's arm. He pushed, and the stranger screamed.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 3 fragments of Awakened skill (Bladework, Intermediate) from the remains. 12 spiritual energy recovered."
Kael wiped his hand on his coat and checked the alley. Empty now. The third man had fled the moment his companions fell.
The Veiled Market lay three streets deeper into Thornwick's underbelly, a bazaar of stolen goods and forbidden knowledge that the city Watch pretended didn't exist. Kael had heard whispers about it in his cell, from men who would never see daylight again. The market dealt in soul-essence, in artifacts pulled from sealed tombs, in information that could topple noble houses.
Most importantly, it dealt in secrets about the cult.
Kael moved through the evening crowds, keeping his hood low. Thornwick's streets choked with smoke and the thunder of steam-carts. Gas lamps flickered against buildings blackened by decades of industrial soot. A church bell tolled somewhere distant—the Order of Dawn's cathedral, likely, calling the faithful to evening prayers.
The faithful. Kael had been faithful once, before they put him on the executioner's block for crimes he didn't commit.
Before the blade fell and he woke up with his heart beating again, hungry for something he couldn't name.
The entrance to the Veiled Market announced itself through an unmarked door flanked by two guards whose eyes glowed faintly in the dimness. Awakened, both of them. They looked at Kael with the bored assessment of predators deciding whether prey was worth the effort.
"Reference," the left one said.
"I don't have one."
"Then you don't get in."
Kael reached into his coat and produced a small glass vial filled with swirling silver light. Soul-essence. Enough to purchase a minor weapon or feed a family for a year.
The guard's eyes widened slightly. Then narrowed.
"Where'd you get that?"
"Harvested it."
Understanding flickered across the guard's face. He stepped aside, gesturing toward the descending staircase.
"Down. Don't cause trouble, and you'll leave in one piece. Cause trouble, and you'll leave as a corpse. Your choice which kind."
Kael took the stairs.
The Veiled Market sprawled beneath the city's streets like a fungal growth, beautiful in its wrongness. Stalls carved from the tunnel walls sold everything imaginable and several things that shouldn't exist. A woman offered "genuine Entity-touched artifacts" from a table draped in black velvet. A one-eyed man whispered about souls for sale—willing donors, he claimed, though Kael doubted that.
The air tasted of copper and ozone. Voices echoed off stone, a constant murmur that built into a dull roar as Kael moved deeper. Vendors called out their wares in clipped, careful tones. The customers—whoever they were beneath hoods and masks—bargained with the intensity of those who knew that every transaction carried consequences.
Kael's fingers brushed against a stall selling information. Maps, documents, bound journals with covers stained in suspicious brown. The vendor sat behind the table like a spider at the center of its web: thin, patient, his fingers twitching as he tracked every customer who passed.
"You seek something specific," the thin man said. Not a question.
"Maybe."
"Everyone here seeks something. Most find disappointment. Some find death. A few find what they need." He smiled, revealing teeth filed to points. "Which will you be?"
"Information about the cult."
The vendor's twitching stopped. His eyes—pale gray, almost colorless—studied Kael with new interest.
"Which cult?"
"Prime Zarek's people. The ones working on the Great Rift."
"You speak loudly for someone who values his throat."
Kael placed another vial of soul-essence on the table. Then another. The vendor's gaze never left Kael's face.
"I'm told there's a bounty on cult members. Thought I might collect."
"You're told wrong." The vendor swept the vials into a hidden drawer with practiced speed. "The bounty was withdrawn three weeks ago. Official policy. The Council of Six has decided that... Acknowledging the cult's existence constitutes a greater threat than allowing it to operate unopposed."
"That's insane."
"That's politics." The vendor leaned forward, lowering his voice. "But information flows even when official channels dry up. The cult operates from locations in the city's industrial quarter, specifically near the steamworks. They've been purchasing large quantities of sanctified iron—enough to construct something significant. A device, maybe. Or a weapon."
"Any names? Faces?"
"I have a contact inside their operation. A woman who sells me information in exchange for protection from certain... Interested parties." The vendor's pointed smile returned. "She can provide more details. Locations, schedules, leadership structure. All of it."
"What's the price?"
"More than three vials. Much more." The vendor studied Kael's face. "But maybe we can negotiate an alternative arrangement. You have skills that interest me, corpse-walker. The ability to harvest abilities from the dead—I've heard stories. Imagine what I could do with such a talent."
"I don't work for free."
"No. You'd harvest the souls of my rivals, and I would pay you in information, protection, and currency. A mutually beneficial partnership."
Kael considered the offer. The vendor was connected—he had access to intelligence networks that extended throughout Thornwick's criminal underworld. That kind of access could prove invaluable in the days ahead.
But partnerships with spider-like figures rarely ended well for the smaller party.
"I want to meet your contact first. Prove she has what you claim."
"Reasonable." The vendor rose, bones creaking. "Follow me."
They moved through the market's winding passages, past stalls selling weapons and stolen art and things Kael couldn't identify. The thin man led him to a section of the tunnels that felt older, the stonework rougher, the air thick with dust and old magic.
The contact waited in a room lit by a single lantern. She sat with her back to the wall, hands visible on the table, a scar running from her left temple to her jaw. Her eyes—sharp, intelligent, carrying the weight of secrets—fixed on Kael the moment he entered.
"This is him?" she asked.
"This is him," the vendor confirmed.
The woman studied Kael for a long moment. Then she nodded.
"Sit."
Kael sat.
"I'm called Sera," she said. "Former member of the Order of Dawn, current employee of the Veiled Market's information division. I left the Order when I discovered that some of my brothers and sisters were actively sabotaging the seal network. Deliberately weakening the barriers that keep the Abyss contained."
"Why?"
"Because they're cultists in all but name. They believe that the Entities should be released—that humanity has grown weak and corrupt, and that only through total destruction can the world be reborn." Sera's voice carried no emotion, but her hands tightened slightly on the table's edge. "The cult calls itself the Children of the Unbound. Prime Zarek is their leader—a former member of the Order of Dawn who went rogue decades ago. He's been building toward this moment for longer than most people have been alive."
Kael's jaw clenched. Zarek. The same Zarek who had framed him for murder, who had orchestrated his arrest and conviction. The same Zarek who walked free while Kael rotted in a cell awaiting execution.
"Tell me about the Great Rift," he said.
"The Great Rift is one of seventy-two sealed rifts that separate the Material from the Abyss. Each rift contains an Entity—a being of immense power that predates human civilization. The seals were constructed during the Abyssal Wars, a thousand years ago, by the first Awakened in cooperation with certain Entities who chose to ally with humanity." Sera paused, letting the information settle. "The Great Rift's seal is the oldest and the most unstable. It sits beneath Thornwick's eastern quarter, directly below the cathedral district. If it breaks, the Entity within—a being the old texts call the Whisper King—will be unleashed upon the world."
"How long until that happens?"
"The seal degrades naturally over time. But the cult has been actively weakening it through some kind of ritual. My sources suggest they have weeks, maybe less."
"Weeks. Not days?"
"Their work is painstaking. They need to accumulate enough sanctified iron to construct a key—a device that will unlock the seal without triggering the failsafes that would otherwise destroy the city." Sera reached into her coat and produced a folded document. "This is a map of their current operation. Locations, personnel counts, security protocols. Everything I've gathered over the past six months."
Kael took the document, spreading it across the table. His eyes moved across the detailed markings, noting the positions of guard posts and entry points. The cult operated from a series of warehouses near the city's steamworks, hidden in plain sight among legitimate businesses.
"The seal's location," he said. "Can you show me?"
Sera hesitated. "The seal chamber is beneath the cathedral. Only the highest-ranking members of the Order of Dawn have access. I could get you inside, but once you're there, you're on your own."
"And the cult's leadership? Where are they?"
"Scattered throughout the city. Prime Zarek himself operates from a base in the industrial quarter, but he moves frequently. His lieutenants are easier to locate." She traced a finger across the map. "There's a woman called the Pale Mother who handles recruitment. A man called Corvin who manages their supply network. And a figure known only as the Architect who designed their key-device."
Kael memorized the layout. His mind absorbed details with a precision that felt almost mechanical—the result of the reavings he had performed, each one adding to his store of skills and knowledge.
"The cult's goal," he said. "Beyond releasing the Whisper King. What do they want?"
Sera's expression flickered with something like pity. "You don't know, do you? You don't know what you're dealing with."
"Tell me."
"The Entities aren't demons. They're not monsters. They're something older and stranger—beings of pure spiritual energy that existed before the material world took shape. The seventy-two rifts contain the oldest and most powerful of them, beings who could reshape reality itself if they crossed into our realm." Sera's voice dropped. "The cult doesn't want to destroy the world. They want to transform it. They believe that releasing the Entities will trigger an evolution in humanity—those who survive will become something more than human. Something divine."
"And those who don't survive?"
"They'll be recycled. Their souls will return to the Abyss, feeding the Entities that consume and are consumed in an endless cycle of spiritual renewal." Sera met his gaze. "The cult isn't evil. It's idealistic. They genuinely believe they're saving humanity by destroying most of it."
The lantern flickered, casting dancing shadows across the walls. Kael's mind processed the information, fitting it into the framework of what he already knew. The cult operated from hidden bases. They had a timeline. They had leadership that could be identified and targeted.
And somewhere in that leadership sat the man who had destroyed Kael's life.
"The Order of Dawn," he said. "They know about this?"
"They know enough to maintain plausible deniability. The Council of Six has decided that acknowledging the cult's existence would cause more harm than good—that panic would weaken the seal network faster than the cult ever could." Sera's laugh held no humor. "Politics. Always politics."
"What about the other Orders? The Iron Vanguard? The Arcane Spire?"
"The Iron Vanguard focuses on military matters and seal maintenance. They're aware of the cult's existence but consider it a problem for the Order of Dawn to handle. The Arcane Spire studies the Abyss from a safe academic distance. The Conclave of Whispers monitors spiritual anomalies but lacks the authority to act. The Veiled Tribunal punishes those who violate the Concordat, but the cult operates outside the Concordat's jurisdiction." Sera spread her hands. "Six Orders. Seventy-two rifts. One enemy. And no one willing to pull together long enough to make a difference."
Kael stood, folding the map and tucking it into his coat. "Your contact within the cult. How reliable is she?"
"Completely. She joined the Children of the Unbound believing their promises of transcendence. After six months inside, she realized the truth—that transcendence meant annihilation for everyone she loved. She wants out, and she wants the cult to burn."
"Can you arrange a meeting?"
"I already have." Sera rose, moving toward the door. "She'll meet you tomorrow night, at the old cemetery on Ashmore Street. The cult conducts rituals there twice a month, using the dead as fuel for their weakening rituals. She'll have more information by then—maybe even a way into their inner circle."
"Tomorrow night." Kael nodded. "I'll be there."
"Be careful." Sera paused at the threshold. "The cult has agents everywhere. The Order of Dawn has eyes throughout the city. And there are others—powers that don't announce themselves—who have taken an interest in the corpse-walker who walks free. One wrong step, and you'll find yourself surrounded by enemies with no way out."
"I've been surrounded before."
"Yes." Sera's gaze held something unreadable. "I know."
She left. The vendor remained, his thin smile fixed in place like a mask.
"A productive meeting," he said. "I trust you'll consider my proposal?"
"I'll think about it."
"Thinking is dangerous. Action is profitable." The vendor's fingers twitched toward the hidden drawer where Kael's soul-essence had disappeared. "The market will close in two hours. I suggest you make use of that time. There are items here that might prove useful in the nights ahead."
Kael moved through the market's final circuit, evaluating wares with a critical eye. Weapons caught his attention first—a blade forged from sanctified iron that hummed with contained energy, a set of throwing knives balanced for silent work, a coat lined with lead threading that would mask his spiritual signature from casual detection.
He purchased what he could afford. The rest would have to wait.
The exit stairs brought him back to street level, into Thornwick's evening chill. Gas lamps lined the avenue, their flames guttering in a wind that carried the scent of coal smoke and coming rain. Kael pulled his collar higher and began the walk back to his current hiding place—a abandoned tenement in the city's lower quarter, claimed through violence and occupied through necessity.
Halfway there, he heard the footsteps behind him.
They matched his pace. Patient. Professional. Two sets, maybe three, spread across the street to cut off escape routes.
Kael's hand found his knife. He kept walking, keeping his breathing steady, his stride relaxed. A trap was forming, and he needed to know who had sprung it.
The first attacker came from a side alley—a man in a dark coat with a blade aimed at Kael's kidney. Kael twisted, taking the cut across his ribs instead of through his vitals, and drove his elbow into the man's throat. The second attacker came from ahead, a woman with Awakened speed and eyes that glowed with killing intent.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 7 fragments of Awakened skill (Stealth, Intermediate) from the remains. 24 spiritual energy recovered."
Kael didn't pause to admire his handiwork. The third attacker—a massive figure wielding a war-hammer—loomed out of the darkness, and Kael barely rolled aside in time to avoid having his skull crushed.
His knife found the man's wrist. The war-hammer clattered to the cobblestones. Kael followed with a thrust that should have ended the fight.
The massive man caught his wrist. Surprised, Kael looked up into eyes that held no light at all—pits of darkness that seemed to drink in the gaslight.
"You fight well, corpse-walker." The voice came from everywhere and nowhere, a whisper that bypassed Kael's ears and settled directly into his mind. "But you cannot kill what was never truly alive."
The massive man's face rippled. The features shifted, rearranged, became something else—something with too many angles and not enough substance. Something that Kael's mind refused to fully process.
"Run," it said. "Run, and tell the Whisper King that his servant will come for him soon."
The creature dissolved into shadow, leaving Kael alone in the street with three corpses and a wound that seeped blood through his coat. He stood for a moment, processing what he had just seen. An Entity? A servant of the Entity? Something else entirely?
The answer would have to wait. The Watch would arrive soon, drawn by the sound of combat, and Kael needed to be gone before they did.
He gathered his knife and moved into the darkness, his mind racing with new questions and old fury. The cult. The seal. The Whisper King. All threads in a tapestry he was only beginning to glimpse.
And somewhere in Thornwick, Prime Zarek was planning the end of the world.
Kael would find him. He would make Zarek pay for every false accusation, every sleepless night, every moment of degradation in the cell that should have been his grave.
He would harvest the truth from Zarek's dying soul, and then he would harvest everything else.
The footsteps behind him had stopped. But Kael knew—with the certainty that came from being hunted by powers beyond mortal comprehension—that this was only the beginning.
Something was watching from the shadows now. Something patient. Something old.
Kael's hand tightened on his bloodied knife as he disappeared into Thornwick's winding streets, aware that every step brought him closer to answers—and further from safety.
