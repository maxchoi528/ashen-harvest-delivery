The blade came down. Kael's hand caught the cultist's wrist an inch from a dying merchant's throat, twisting bone until a scream cut through the alley's fog. The sound bounced off damp brick and died in the heavy mist.
Three more figures emerged from the steam-shrouded shadows, robed in black, sigils glowing faintly on their chests. The Veil-touched marks. Kael had seen their handiwork in the Ashenmoor — bodies twisted into shapes that defied nature, villages erased from maps. The stench of ozone preceded them, crawling into his sinuses.
"You shouldn't be here, Reaver." The cultist's voice rattled like gravel in a jar. Blood flecked his lips, spittle catching the gaslight. "The Prime's ritual begins at midnight. No living soul will interrupt."
Kael's grip tightened. "The Prime?"
Laughter bubbled from another cultist, a woman with silver streaks through her hair and madness dancing in her eyes. "Zarek didn't tell you? Prime Zarek has been preparing this for decades. Seventy-two rifts, opened simultaneously. The Entities will flood this world like water through a broken dam."
Kael released the cultist's wrist and drove his palm forward. The man's sternum collapsed with a wet crack. Three bodies hit the cobblestones in the span of a heartbeat. The smell of voided bowels and copper filled the alley.
"Seventy-two," Kael breathed.
The woman smiled, raising a curved knife etched with spiraling script. "All of them. The seals are already failing. Your precious Six Orders can't stop what's coming."
Kael stepped over the corpse, boots squelching in the pooling blood. "Where?"
"Does it matter?" She lunged.
He sidestepped, grabbed her by the throat, and slammed her against the alley wall. The knife clattered away. Her feet dangled, toes scraping stone.
"Answer me."
"The cathedral," she choked. "Old Thornwick cathedral. Where the first seal was placed. Where it will break first."
Kael's eyes swept the fog. More robed figures moved in the distance, dozens of them, converging toward the city's ancient heart. The air tasted of copper and something else — ozone, the sharp tang of reality fraying.
*Seventy-two rifts. All at once. This isn't a raid. This is an apocalypse.*
The woman laughed again, blood bubbling at her lips. "You're too late. Even if you stop the cathedral, the others will open. The Prime has ensured it. Six Orders against seventy-two breaches. Math doesn't favor them, Reaver."
Kael dropped her. She crumpled, unconscious or dead — he didn't care which. His mind raced through possibilities, discarding them one by one. He couldn't be everywhere. Couldn't stop dozens of rituals across the continent with only his blade and his touch.
But he could start here.
He moved through the fog-shrouded streets, keeping to the shadows where the gaslight didn't reach. Thornwick's steam-belching factories roared in the distance, oblivious to the death gathering in their midst. Workers trudged homeward, faces drawn, eyes fixed on the ground. None of them could see what approached. None of them knew that the membrane between worlds was about to tear.
The fog thickened as he neared the city's ancient heart. Cobblestones gave way to older paving, cracked and uneven, treacherous underfoot. Kael's boots found steady purchase through instinct alone. His spiritual senses stretched outward, probing the darkness. What returned made his jaw clench — pressure, vast and oppressive, radiating from somewhere ahead like heat from a furnace.
*Zarek. Of course. Always at the center of everything rotten.*
Kael's boots found the cathedral steps. Ancient stone, carved with prayers in languages dead for a thousand years. The prayers had failed. Whatever faith had raised this edifice had long since bled away, leaving only the shell — and the horror growing within. The doors hung open, spilling sickly green light into the night. The light pulsed with a rhythm that matched his heartbeat, syncopating, trying to pull him in.
Inside, hundreds of cultists knelt in concentric circles. At the center, a figure stood before the massive altar, arms raised toward the vaulted ceiling where something pressed against reality itself — a bulge in the air, shimmering like heat off summer stone, waiting to burst.
Kael recognized the figure.
Zarek.
His jaw locked. The man who'd framed him for murder, who'd orchestrated his execution, who wore the title of Prime like a crown of thorns. He stood atop a ritual circle drawn in salt and ash, chanting words that made Kael's teeth ache.
"The seal weakens," Zarek intoned. "The Veil thins. Through death, we are reborn. Through blood, we are freed. The Entities await their children."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 47 spiritual energy from the remains. 12 acquired."
The notification pulsed in Kael's vision as he absorbed the dead cultists around him. His spiritual reserves swelled, warmth spreading through limbs that had grown cold in the fog. Three kills. Minimal energy, but every drop mattered now.
He descended the steps, keeping to the shadows between pillars. The chanting grew louder, more fervent. Cultists swayed, voices rising in terrible harmony. The bulge in the ceiling pulsed, and hairline cracks spider-webbed across ancient stone.
"Brothers and sisters," Zarek continued, "the time of waiting ends. The Sun God's puppets have held dominion over humanity for too long. The Six Orders cling to power while the Abyss offers true freedom. We need only tear down their barriers."
Kael's fingers found the dagger at his hip. Not his primary weapon — that was the black blade he'd taken from a dead Awakened months ago, serrated and hungry. But close enough for this.
He waited until the chanting reached its crescendo, until every cultist's eyes closed in shared ecstasy, until Zarek himself seemed lost in the ritual's pull. Then he moved.
Three steps. Five. Ten. The cultists nearest him opened eyes, mouths forming warnings that died when his blade opened their throats. Blood fountained, painting the ritual circle in crimson. The chanting shattered into screams.
Zarek spun, shock rippling across his features before hardening into rage. "Kael Ashborn. I should have let them kill you in the prison."
"You tried." Kael carved through a cultist rushing him, spun, and opened another from hip to shoulder. "Not hard enough."
"The ritual will continue." Zarek's hands wove patterns in the air, spiritual energy crackling between his fingers. "Kill me, and my disciples will finish what I've started. Spare me, and I might let you walk away."
Kael laughed — a harsh, broken sound. "You're bargaining? Now?"
"Self-preservation isn't weakness, Reaver."
"It's stupidity." Kael lunged.
Zarek's defensive ward flared, a wall of shimmering light that should have stopped the blade. It didn't. Kael's black sword carved through the barrier like paper, momentum carrying it toward Zarek's throat.
The Prime twisted, taking the blow across his shoulder instead of through his neck. Blood sprayed. He staggered back, chanting accelerating, fingers bloody but still weaving power.
"The seal breaks whether I live or die," Zarek snarled. "The Entities are coming. You can kill me and watch humanity drown in the Abyss, or you can let me finish — my way. Controlled. Selective. A few rifts instead of all seventy-two."
Kael's blade hesitated. "You'd release them on purpose?"
"Release some. Contain others. The Orders can't stop what they can't see." Zarek's eyes glittered with madness and calculation. "Imagine it, Ashborn. A world where the Abyss flows freely. Where spiritual power isn't hoarded by a select few. Where death doesn't mean oblivion but transformation."
"Transformation into what?"
"Servants. Soldiers. Gods." Zarek's smile stretched too wide. "The Entities don't destroy, Reaver. They *elevate*. The first Soul Reaver understood that. The one who came before you. The one whose power you carry."
The words hit like a physical blow. Kael's grip faltered for a heartbeat.
"You know nothing about me."
"I know everything." Zarek's ward pulsed brighter. "The Soul Reaver ability isn't a gift from the Abyss. It's a curse from the past. Someone like you was the reason the seals were created in the first place. Someone like you nearly broke the world open three hundred years ago."
The cathedral shuddered. Cracks raced across the ceiling, green light bleeding through. The bulge was growing, reality protesting against its own unraveling.
"Three hundred years ago," Kael repeated slowly. "The first Soul Reaver."
"The first. And the last. Until you." Zarek pressed a hand to his bleeding shoulder. "He wanted what you want. Freedom. Power. Control. He nearly succeeded. The Six Orders barely stopped him, barely sealed the rifts he'd opened. They killed him, or so they claimed. But death doesn't hold a Soul Reaver long."
Kael's mind raced. The Sepulcher tablets. The mystery of where his power originated. Here, in this moment, fragments of truth pressing against his consciousness.
"You're lying."
"Am I?" Zarek gestured toward the growing tear in reality. "The Entities recognize their own. Watch."
The bulge pulsed, and something ancient pressed against the Veil from the other side. Not an attack. Not a threat. Recognition. Welcome.
Kael's skin crawled.
"The ritual continues whether you stop it or not," Zarek said. "I can control the flow. Guide which rifts open and which remain sealed. Or you can kill me, watch everything collapse, and die knowing you doomed the world."
Kael sheathed his blade. Zarek's eyes widened — surprise flickering before triumph.
"Smart choice, Reaver."
Kael grabbed Zarek by the throat and slammed him into the altar. Stone cracked. The chanting disciples froze, watching their Prime being manhandled like a child's doll.
"You think I need you?" Kael's voice dropped to a growl. "I can read your memories. Harvest everything you know about the ritual, the seals, the other seventy-one locations. Then I don't need you alive."
Zarek's triumph curdled into fear. "You wouldn't. The harvest requires touch. You can't—"
Kael's hand pressed against Zarek's forehead. Spiritual energy surged, cold and invasive, sliding through the Prime's defenses like water through sand.
"Soul Harvest: Initiating extraction from Prime Zarek. Target spiritual density: Exceptional. Target memories: Encrypted. Extraction time: a few heartbeats."
The world narrowed to the connection between them. Zarek screamed, body convulsing as decades of knowledge, plans, and secrets ripped free. Kael's vision fractured into fragments — ritual diagrams, coordinates, names, the exact locations of sixty-three other sites across the continent, each tagged with preparation levels and cultist numbers.
Zarek's knees buckled. He collapsed against the altar, gasping, diminished. The ward around him flickered and died.
"Impossible," he whispered. "The encryption should have held."
Kael's mind filled with information he hadn't known existed. The other rifts. The timing. The contingencies. Zarek's network stretched across every major city, dozens of hidden cells, hundreds of devoted fanatics.
"Soul Harvest: Extraction complete. Memory fragments acquired: 2,847. Ritual knowledge acquired: 342 techniques. Target psychological profile: Deep integration. Warning: Subject retains core personality. Minor cognitive impairment detected."
The green light pulsed brighter. The seal cracked further, reality groaning under pressure it was never meant to contain.
"The ritual," Zarek rasped. "It's beyond me now. I can't stop it."
Kael looked up. The bulge had become a tear, a ragged wound in the fabric of existence. Something moved on the other side — vast, hungry, patient.
Through the connection, he felt it. Recognition. Hunger. Welcome.
*The first Reaver's fate.*
Kael wrenched himself away from the mental link, severing the last threads of Zarek's memories. His hands shook. His vision swam with images he didn't understand — the first Reaver standing before seventy-two open rifts, bathed in Entity light, choosing transformation over death.
The cultists were running now, fleeing the cathedral as the tear widened. Some prayed. Others laughed. A few stood, waiting to be consumed.
Kael grabbed Zarek's collar and hauled him upright. "The seal. Where's the anchor?"
"Center of the rift," Zarek coughed. "The original seal stone. It's what I'm channeling. Destroy it, and the tear stabilizes. Temporarily."
"And if I don't destroy it?"
"Then it opens fully. And whatever's waiting on the other side comes through."
Kael tossed Zarek aside and ran toward the altar. The tear pulsed above it, green light casting his shadow in grotesque shapes against the walls. The seal stone sat at its heart — a black monolith etched with the same script as the cathedral steps, ancient prayers in dead languages.
He could feel the Entities pressing against the boundary. Not hostile, not yet. Curious. Waiting to see what he would do.
Kael's blade rose.
"Wait." A voice, not Zarek's. New. Female. Cold.
He spun. A woman in grey robes stood at the cathedral doors, her face hidden beneath a deep hood. Spiritual energy radiated from her in waves that made Kael's teeth ache. An Awakened. High stage, judging by the pressure.
"The Order of Dawn dispatches you, Soul Reaver." Her hand rested on a sword that glowed with contained fire. "Lower your weapon. The seal is under our protection."
Kael's grip tightened. "Your protection? Your Order's been asleep while cults organized across the continent. While Zarek prepared to tear reality apart."
"Our resources are stretched across seventy-two potential sites." No defensiveness in her tone. Pure statement of fact. "We cannot be everywhere."
"Then maybe you need help."
The woman's head tilted. "You would assist the Orders? After everything they've done to Reavers like you?"
"I'd assist anyone who's willing to fight." Kael turned back to the seal stone. "But this is my choice. Not yours. Not theirs."
The woman drew her sword. Fire danced along the blade's edge. "The seal requires a specific dissolution ritual. Attempting to destroy it with force will accelerate the breach."
"Then guide me."
Silence stretched between them. The tear pulsed, impatient.
Finally, she lowered her blade. "On your knees. Hands flat. I will channel the dissolution through your touch."
Kael hesitated. This was a trap. Had to be. The Orders hunted Soul Reavers. They wouldn't offer help freely.
But the alternative was letting the rift open. Letting whatever waited on the other side through.
He knelt.
The woman moved behind him, her hands pressing against his shoulders. Spiritual energy flowed into him — not cold like the harvest, but burning, purifying, cleansing. The seal stone responded, its surface crumbling, the tear shrinking inch by agonizing inch.
"Forty-seven seconds," the woman said. "Then it seals. Then we deal with the aftermath."
"Deal with what?"
"The other seventy-one sites. The remaining cultists. And you, Soul Reaver." Her grip tightened. "The Orders will have questions about your abilities. Your origins. Your intentions."
The stone crumbled to dust. The tear collapsed inward with a sound like thunder suppressed. Silence fell over the cathedral, broken only by the groans of wounded stone and the distant screams of cultists fleeing into the night.
"Soul Harvest: Ritual disruption successful. Target rift: Sealed. Spiritual backlash: Minimal. Status: Mission continuing."
Kael stood, legs unsteady. The woman hadn't moved, her hands still on his shoulders.
"The other sites," he said. "How many can the Orders cover?"
"Eleven. Maybe fifteen, if we mobilize immediately."
"Then I take the rest."
"You'll die."
Kael turned to face her. Firelight played across her features — sharp cheekbones, grey eyes, a scar running from temple to jaw. She wasn't old, but something in her expression suggested depths of experience that transcended years.
"I've died before," he said. "Didn't stick."
Her lips twitched. Not a smile. "The Shadow Court has taken interest in you, Ashborn. We saw their agents in the crowd. They're watching."
"Let them watch."
He walked past her, toward the cathedral doors, toward the fog-shrouded streets and the fifty-seven other sites waiting across the continent. Fifty-seven rifts. Fifty-seven rituals. Fifty-seven chances to fail.
The woman called after him. "The original Soul Reaver. His name was Aldric Vane."
Kael stopped at the threshold. "How do you know that?"
"Because the Order of Dawn killed him three hundred years ago. And we've been watching for someone to take his place ever since." She sheathed her sword. "His tomb is in the Ashenmoor Wastes. Unmarked. Forgotten. If you want answers about what you are, that's where they rest."
The fog swallowed her before he could respond.
Kael stood in the cathedral doorway, the weight of revelation pressing against his chest. Aldric Vane. The first Soul Reaver. The one who'd nearly broken the world. Whose power now flowed through his veins.
*His tomb is in the Ashenmoor Wastes.*
Not tonight. Tonight, fifty-seven rifts waited. Fifty-seven rituals needed disrupting. Fifty-seven chances to prove that the Soul Reaver's legacy wouldn't end in catastrophe.
Kael descended the steps, boots echoing against ancient stone. Behind him, the sealed cathedral groaned and settled into uneasy silence. Ahead, the fog rolled thick and endless, hiding the city that didn't know how close it had come to annihilation.
He had names now. Coordinates. The layout of Zarek's network stretching across the continent like a disease on a map. The Shadow Court watched from the shadows. The Orders would demand answers he didn't have.
But first — the rituals. The rifts. The Entities pressing against a Veil that was fraying faster than anyone realized.
Kael pulled his black blade free and vanished into the night.
The fog closed behind him like a curtain falling on the first act of a war no one else knew had begun. Somewhere in the distance, church bells began to toll — not midnight yet, but close. The cult's coordinated timing was precise. They would strike when the Veil thinned naturally, at the witching hour, when reality's grip on existence slipped its weakest.
Fifty-seven sites. One night. And a Soul Reaver running toward the apocalypse because no one else could.
Kael's breath misted in the cold air as he navigated the empty streets. His spiritual senses stretched outward, searching for the familiar signature of cultist gatherings, Veil-touched energy, ritual preparations.
The fog replied with whispers. Distant screams. The clash of steel on steel — somewhere in the city's eastern district, the Orders had already engaged.
He marked the location mentally and moved on. His blade needed blood. The continent needed saving. And somewhere in the Ashenmoor Wastes, a dead man's tomb waited with answers Kael wasn't sure he wanted.
But that was tomorrow's problem.
Tonight, the war began.
