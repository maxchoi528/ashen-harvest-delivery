The rift in the Mortician's Quarter erupted without warning, a wound in reality vomiting shadows and bile.

A tendril of black mist lanced from the alley mouth and wrapped around his throat. The sound he made wasn't a scream. It was something wetter, shorter.
Kael's hand closed on the man's ankle as he fell. Contact. The body went slack in his grip as the mist retracted, leaving nothing but a hollow shell collapsing into the gutter.

The second cultist died with a prayer on her lips, her soul scattering before he could touch her. The third had the same problem—they'd pushed their bodies past limits for the ritual, and the death was violent enough to scatter everything.

"No!" Kael lunged.
The rift pulsed.

"Soul Reaver. The lineage persists. The paradox strengthens."

"Paradox," Kael muttered. "What paradox?"

[SOUL HARVEST: You extracted Memory Fragment from the remains. The Faceless One's whispers remain fragmented. Partial clarity achieved.]

Seventy-two seats around a table that existed outside reality. Seventy-two Entities, and something was calling them home.
Not all at once. One by one. Each rift opened deliberately, each Entity released just enough to weaken the whole structure. By the time the Orders noticed, it would be too late to stop.
"Coordinated," Kael breathed. "This is coordinated."
The rift pulsed again. He had seconds.
Kael ran.

The communication crystal in his pocket had been silent for three days. Lyra's crystal, keyed to frequencies the Shadow Court used for deniable operations. She'd given it to him before disappearing into whatever web she'd woven to protect him from her former masters.
He found an alley far enough from the rift to think. His hands shook—not from fear, but from spiritual feedback. The Entity's presence had ed marks on his senses, a resonance that made his Soul Reaver ability itch with unnatural hunger.
"Paradox," he muttered, pressing his back against damp brick. "Lineage."
The memory fragment offered no clarity, only impressions. The Faceless One knew about Soul Reavers. The Entities knew. Whatever secret he'd stumbled into, it was bigger than his survival, bigger than the Orders' politics, bigger than the individual rifts failing across the continent.
The crystal hummed in his palm. A message, encrypted in the old Shadow Court ciphers that Lyra had made him memorize.
*Council meets in Thornwick. Six Orders. Emergency session. The Whisper King has made contact.*
Kael stared at the words. The Whisper King—one of the original Entities, locked away in the first rift. If he was making contact, then the coordinated assault wasn't just weakening seals.
It was communication. Negotiation. The Entities wanted something, and they'd found someone willing to bargain.
*They're selling the world,* he thought. *Someone in the Council is going to sell it.*
The crystal hummed again. Different frequency. Older cipher.
*Thornwick is burning. Multiple rifts. The Council can't respond in time. If the rifts aren't sealed manually, seven Entities walk free.*
*Manual sealing requires Soul Reaver blood.*
The message ended. Kael's blood ran cold.
The Faceless One had known. Every step he'd taken since his execution had been anticipated. The Soul Reaver ability wasn't just a power—it was a key. And somewhere in the world, someone was gathering the keys.
"I need to find Lyra," Kael said to no one. "I need to find out what the Shadow Court knows."
The alley exit led to a street choked with refugees. Families clutching children, merchants abandoning carts, street vendors leaving their entire lives behind in favor of a few more seconds of movement. The Mortician's Quarter had been a maze of alleys and cheap housing, and now it was a river of humanity fleeing something they didn't understand.
Kael pushed upstream, searching for a familiar face. The Shadow Court's agents operated in Thornwick through layers of identity—he'd met Lyra's contacts before, people who wore masks even among their own kind.
"There." A woman in a hood stood at the corner of a collapsed building, her hand raised in a signal Kael recognized. She led him through a hidden passage, down stairs that smelled of copper and old magic, into a basement that shouldn't exist beneath a building that no longer stood.
"Lyra sent you," Kael said.
"Lyra's compromised." The woman's voice was flat, professional. "The Shadow Court has been compromised. We don't know by whom or how deeply, but the network is leaking. Everything. Including your location."
Kael's hand found his knife. "And you are?"
"Mira. I was Lyra's backup. She's activated me because she's running, and you're the only Soul Reaver in the city who might survive the night." Mira pulled back her hood, revealing a face that had seen too much violence to flinch from more. "The Thornwick Concordat is broken. The Orders are fighting each other instead of the rifts. Someone orchestrated this."
"Someone like the Whisper King."

"The central seal holds the Whisper King."
"If it fails, the others have no chance. The entire network depends on the anchor points." She tapped seven locations on the map. "We need to seal these rifts manually. It's possible for Awakened of sufficient power, but the cost is—"

"You knew."
"I figured it out." He studied the map, memorizing locations. "The Faceless One's cultists weren't trying to open all the rifts. They were trying to create a situation where Soul Reavers would have to act. Force our hand."
"Force your hand toward what?"
Kael didn't answer. The Faceless One's words echoed in his memory: *The paradox strengthens.* Whatever the Entities wanted from Soul Reavers, it wasn't simple destruction. They wanted something that required his kind alive and cooperative.
"The Shadow Court," he said. "What do they want from this?"
Mira hesitated. "The Council of Six is dead. All six representatives. Assassinated in the last hour by cultists wearing Order uniforms. The Shadow Court was monitoring the situation, and now they're moving."
"Moving how?"
"Seizing assets. Abandoning neutrality. They're choosing sides, and they're choosing the side that wins."
"And Lyra?"
"Lyra chose you." Mira's expression didn't change. "She burned her contacts and ran when she realized what was coming. She left a message for you: 'The Soul Reaver origin is in Thornwick. Find the Mirror Chamber. The Faceless One isn't just speaking through cultists. He's already here.'"
The words hit Kael like a physical blow. The Faceless One. In Thornwick. Not in the Abyss, not sealed in a rift, but walking the city's streets in a form that could pass for human.
"Mirror Chamber," he repeated. "Where?"
Mira pulled out another map, this one older, marked with symbols that predated the current city layout. "Beneath the old temple district. It was the site of the first seal, back when the Entities were first imprisoned. The Orders built Thornwick on top of it to maintain the anchor."
"And the Shadow Court knows this because?"
"Because we helped build it." Mira met his gaze without flinching. "The Shadow Court isn't just a deniable operations arm. We're the seventh Order. We were created to maintain secrets the other six couldn't be trusted with. The Mirror Chamber is our responsibility."
Kael absorbed this, fitting it into his understanding of the world. Seven Orders. Not six. The Shadow Court had been hiding in plain sight, watching, manipulating, maintaining power through information than force.
"The Faceless One in the Mirror Chamber," he said. "What does he want there?"
"To finish what he started two hundred years ago." Mira's voice dropped. "The original sealing didn't just trap the Entities. It fractured them. The 72 are pieces of something larger, something that existed before the Abyss and Material split. The Faceless One wants to reassemble. He wants to become whole."
"And he needs Soul Reavers for that."
"The Soul Reaver ability is the key. It can harvest from anything—living or dead, human or Entity. The lineage exists because the Faceless One planted it. You're not a mutation or an accident. You're a tool he designed."
Kael's hand closed around the knife. Every lesson he'd learned about Soul Reavers, every time he'd questioned why the ability existed, why it had chosen him—it was all manipulation. All design.
"The second Soul Reaver," he said slowly. "In the records. If only one can exist, why was there another?"
"Because the Faceless One needed redundancy. Two keys are better than one. If one fails, the other can still open the door." Mira paused. "Both of you are alive. The paradox isn't a problem—it's intentional. He's hedging his bets."
The basement shook. Dust rained from the ceiling. Somewhere above, glass shattered and people screamed.
"Another rift opening," Mira said. "The timeline's accelerating. If we don't move now, the Faceless One completes his assembly before dawn."
Kael looked at the map, at the seven marked locations, at the hidden path to the Mirror Chamber. He had hours at best, and the world was unraveling faster than anyone had predicted.
"Lyra," he said. "Where is she now?"
"She was heading for the Mirror Chamber. She said she'd buy you time."
"Buy me time to do what?"
Mira's expression flickered—something that might have been respect, or pity. "To choose, Soul Reaver. The Faceless One needs you to open the door. The Orders need you to seal the rifts. Lyra needs you to save yourself. Three paths, three costs. Choose."

Kael sheathed his knife and moved.

Awakened dueled Awakened, their spiritual attacks shattering buildings and turning cobblestones to glass. Kael descended through a basement that shouldn't exist, following passages that predated Thornwick's founding. The air grew cold, then warm, then cold again. His breath misted before him, and the mist moved in patterns that suggested intention.
The Faceless One was watching.
"The door is sealed," Kael called into the darkness. "I felt the locking mechanism. Whatever you're doing in there, you can't finish without me."
Silence. Then a voice that came from everywhere and nowhere:
"You're right, Soul Reaver. I designed the lock and the key. But I never said you were the only key."
Lyra stepped out of the shadows.
Her eyes were wrong—too many pupils, layered like reality itself. Her hands bore sigils that pulsed with stolen light. She stood before a door covered in mirrors, each reflection showing a different version of her.
"Kael." Her voice was hers and not hers. "I'm sorry. I tried to warn you."
"Lyra." He reached for his knife. "What's he doing to you?"

The shadows in the chamber began to move. Kael felt other presences gathering—not the Faceless One, but his servants. Cultists. Lost souls. The accumulated weight of two centuries of planning.
"You could have warned me," he said. "Before he took you. You could have run."

The word hung between them. Loving.
"I didn't want this," Lyra whispered. "But I'm tired of running. I want it to end."
Kael sheathed his knife. He stepped forward, past the point where she could strike, and wrapped his arms around her.
Lyra went rigid. "What are you doing?"
"Fighting." He pressed his palm against her back, against the sigil that pulsed with stolen power. "You said the Faceless One can't fully manifest without a host. That means he's not complete. He's vulnerable."
"You can't harvest from a living—"

[SOUL HARVEST: You extracted Faceless One Fragment from the host. Partial separation achieved. Warning: Entity resistance detected.]

[SOUL HARVEST: You extracted Memory of the First Sealing from the Entity. Partial truth revealed. Entity stability compromised.]

"The first Soul Reaver," the ancient voice whispered. "My creator. My vessel. My key."

"You designed the lineage," he said. "You designed me. But you made a mistake."

"Even you."
[SOUL HARVEST: You extracted Essential Fragment from the Faceless One. Warning: Entity destabilization critical. Rift cascade initiated.]

The Faceless One coalesced before him, massive and incomplete, missing the piece Kael carried.

They could wait a few more generations.

The Faceless One had given him leverage. Now he had to figure out how to use it.

Behind them, in the Mirror Chamber that existed between worlds, something ancient and patient began to stir. The Faceless One had made a deal. He would honor it.
For now.
The fragment in Kael's palm pulsed once, twice, three times—a rhythm that matched his heartbeat, a rhythm that felt almost like anticipation.
Something was coming.
He just didn't know what yet.
The night air carried screams and fire and the smell of a world ending. Somewhere in the distance, the seventh rift pulsed with weakening light. The seals held. The Entities waited. The Soul Reaver walked forward into uncertain darkness.
The game had changed. The pieces were in motion. And Kael, Divine-stage Awakened and bearer of stolen power, finally understood why he'd been born.
Not to save the world.
To choose what it became.
