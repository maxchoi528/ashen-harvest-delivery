The Ashwick Cemetery was a charnel house.
Kael had seen battlefields before — had walked through the aftermath of the Pinnacle Collapse, had harvested from the corpses left behind by the Ashwick Massacre. But this was different. The mausoleums had become fortifications. The trees had been stripped for firewood and barricades.
And everywhere, the dead.
Kael counted thirty-seven bodies in the first hundred yards — Vanguard soldiers, cult fanatics, and civilians who'd been caught in the crossfire. The Vanguard dead lay in neat rows, their insignia visible, their wounds precise. The cultists were scattered, their bodies marked with ritual tattoos that writhed faintly in the dim light. The civilians were... Everywhere. Men, women, children. Old. Young. Broken.
He started with the Vanguard.

*—saw them coming from the east, thought it was reinforcements until—*

*—they're not retreating, they're waiting—*
