The tunnel walls were older than Thornwick itself, carved from stone that predated human settlement. Symbols covered every surface—not the Blood God's crude sigils, but something more complex, more deliberate. Mathematical sequences. Spiritual formulas. The building blocks of reality itself, encoded in languages that humanity had forgotten.

They'd inherited them from a civilization that came before, one that had learned to imprison the Entities than coexist with them.
The Whisper King wanted to finish what that civilization started. He wanted to tear open the prison and let the prisoners decide humanity's fate.
Kael's boots echoed against the steps as he spiraled downward. The air grew colder with each level, carrying notes of something organic beneath the stone—moss, maybe, or fungus growing in absolute darkness. His flame guttered twice but held.

The Whisper King.

Something waiting to reshape the world in its own image.
The Whisper King fled through shadows that melted before the Entity-light flooding from Kael's transformed body. His throne room shattered around him. His carefully laid plans cracked along fault lines no one had known existed.

They just didn't know what yet.
