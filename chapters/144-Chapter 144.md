Kael's leg bent at an unnatural angle as he was dragged toward the alley's mouth, toward the main street where gaslight painted the fog orange and pedestrians walked oblivious to the execution being staged twenty feet away.
Think. Think faster.

Enough.

Two corpses. Two minutes. One more standing.

The Whisper King. A name he'd never heard before tonight. Someone powerful enough to command a network of Stage 3 operatives and order attacks on a Soul Reaver with casual precision.

Kael glanced at the corpses. Three potential harvests, each one carrying fragments of Choir strategy and leadership structure. But if he stopped to reap now, more enforcers would arrive, and the political consequences of a Soul Reaver caught extracting memories from Order-identified cultists might outweigh the intelligence gained.
"I need thirty seconds with the corpses."

Kael dropped to his knees beside the first cultist—the one whose throat he'd crushed. His fingers found the cooling flesh of the man's wrist, and he pressed his Soul Reaver mark against the contact point.

Kael stared at the manacles. The logical choice was to accept temporary captivity, gain access to Order intelligence networks, and extract himself once he'd learned enough to advance his own agenda.
