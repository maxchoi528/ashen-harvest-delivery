"Down!" Lyra's voice cut the chaos. She landed beside him, twin blades drawn, her Shadow Court training etched into every line of her stance.
The Flesh Colossus lurched through the wreckage. It moved wrong—joints buckling at angles that made Kael's stomach lurch. When it spoke, the voice boomed from everywhere and nowhere, a chorus of dying screams compressed into words.
"The vessel is marked. The vessel will deliver."
The weight of those words sank into Kael's bones. "It's hunting me specifically."
"Obviously." Lyra's blades caught the dim light. "Cult construct. Someone sent it."
Three days they'd spent tracking cult activity. Moving through Fogmire District's rotting underbelly, following threads of ritual sacrifice and Abyssal tampering. Kael had sensed something wrong that morning—a wrongness that clung to the back of his neck like cold fingers. He'd suggested they investigate.
He wished he'd kept his mouth shut.
The Colossus charged. The ground shuddered with each footfall. Kael dove right, rolled, came up swinging Soul Reaver in a silver arc. The blade bit into the construct's arm and stuck. He wrenched it free as the thing pivoted, its fist catching him across the chest.
Pain burst through his ribs. He flew backward, hit a stack of crates, and felt wood splinter around him. Coughing blood, he dragged himself upright in time to see Lyra dancing around the construct's legs, her blades trailing shadows.
Kael needed a plan. The Colossus wasn't a man—it wouldn't bleed, wouldn't tire, wouldn't surrender. Every strike he landed barely slowed it. But constructs like this, stitched together from dead flesh, they had anchors. Hearts. Control nodes. Something binding the stolen bodies into a unified whole.
He watched it move, searching. There—a knot of scarred tissue pulsing with a dim glow, embedded in its chest where a sternum should be.
"The core," he shouted. "Chest, center mass. I need to get close."

She couldn't. Already he could see her movements growing sluggish, her spiritual energy draining. The Shadow Court had trained her well, but she was still only Awakened. Against this thing—
Kael sprinted toward the Colossus. It swatted Lyra away—she hit the ground hard, rolled, stayed down, breathing but not fighting—and fixed those terrible multi-eyed gaze on him. The construct raised both fists overhead.
He didn't slow down. At the last second, he dropped into a slide, letting momentum carry him across the floor. The fists crashed down where his head had been. The impact shook dust from the rafters.
Kael came up inside its guard, close enough to smell the rot wafting from its patchwork skin. He drove Soul Reaver upward with both hands.
The blade punched through the knot of tissue. The Colossus screamed—a sound like a thousand voices crying out. Kael twisted the weapon, felt something inside rupture, and then—
He was falling.
Not from a blow. His body gave out. Soul Reaver had done something, channeled something, and now energy surged through him like a river breaking through a dam. He landed on his knees, gasping, vision swimming with colors that had no names.
