Chapter 137: First Night Teeth

The pump house floor bulged upward.

Not much. A shallow lift beneath the ash-crusted stone. The pressure of something large pressing its knuckles from below. Deciding not to break through yet.

Brendt's shield unfolded with a soft metallic snap.

Too loud.

Everything outside stopped.

For one full second, the Hollowed City held its breath.

Then the roof screamed.

A hooked claw punched through the stone above Ines and tore backward, ripping a jagged line across the ceiling. Ash and dust, plus splinters of old concrete rained down. Ines rolled away before the second claw followed, rifle still folded, knife in hand. Fast. Not panicked. Good.

Drogan moved before the ceiling finished falling.

His short blade came out and struck upward through the crack, not at the claw but beside it, into the joint shadow. Something shrieked outside, high and wet. The claw vanished.

"Positions," Drogan said.

No shouting. No wasted force.

Roan kicked the nearest camp anchor deeper into its bracket and dragged Brendt's spare shield frame toward the machinery line.

The first mistake in a night attack was defending the door because doors looked like entrances. The second mistake was forgetting walls were only doors with better marketing. In a Rift, every surface was a negotiation.

Roan preferred controlling the terms.

"Brendt, center brace," he said.

"I know."

"Then do it faster."

Brendt planted himself between the central pump casing and the door, shield raised, second frame angled to cover his left. The setup created a narrow kill lane through the room. Not perfect. Perfect required advance notice, fresh materials, and a world that respected architecture.

This would do.

Ines fired a marker dart into the far wall. It burst into pale light, revealing the chamber in cold slices.

The bulge under the floor split.

A pale hand emerged.

Not human. Too many joints. Fingers long enough to wrap around a skull. The skin had the color of boiled cartilage, stretched thin over black corded muscle. A second hand followed, then a narrow head without eyes pushed through broken stone.

Tier Three Husk.

Roan had seen sketches. The sketches had been optimistic.

The creature's mouth opened vertically from collarbone to brow.

Brendt made a sound like he had decided not to swear halfway through.

Roan threw his standard knife.

The blade entered the Husk's mouth and struck the back hinge. Not lethal. Disruptive. The creature jerked, buying half a second.

Drogan used it.

He stepped in and drove his blade down through the top of the skull. Vitalis pulsed once along the edge. The Husk collapsed before it finished emerging.

Clean.

Too clean.

Roan's attention went to the floor around it.

Three more bulges.

"Below," he said.

The door exploded inward.

Brendt caught it.

Not the whole door. The door itself held, reinforced by the ward line. But something hit from outside hard enough to drive the iron panel two inches off alignment. The camp ward shrieked blue. Brendt's shield took the pressure spill, his boots grinding back across the floor.

A claw slipped through the gap.

Roan cut it with Sidewound.

The blade met resistance, then found a seam.

Not flesh. Structure. A tension line running through the Husk's wrist as it flexed against the ward. Roan felt it for less than a heartbeat, a narrow invitation, almost polite.

He cut.

The claw dropped to the floor.

[Sidewound contact: successful.]

[No inversion triggered.]

[Husk tissue destabilized.]

The severed claw twitched twice and dissolved into gray-black sludge.

Outside, something slammed the door again.

Brendt grunted. "Many!"

"How many?" Ines asked, loading her rifle with one hand.

"Enough!"

"Technical."

The floor ruptured in three places.

Roan moved.

First emerging Husk: left side, too close to Ines. He stepped onto the rising stone, used his weight to slow the emergence, and drove Sidewound down through the shoulder seam. The blade entered shallowly but opened deep. The Husk's left side failed. Ines finished it with a shot through the exposed core-thread.

Second: center, under Brendt's shield line. Brendt smashed it back with the rim, not killing it but ruining its angle. Drogan stepped over the shield edge and stabbed through the spine.

Third: behind Roan.

He didn't turn fully. Turning fully cost time. He dropped his left shoulder, let the claw pass over it close enough to scrape his coat, and cut backward with Sidewound.

Bad angle.

The blade caught but didn't open.

The Husk's second claw struck his ribs.

The Vance Pendant went cold.

Impact spread across his chest and spine in a flat, ugly wave. Pain flashed white through his sternum, but the claw failed to penetrate. Without the pendant, it would have opened him from ribs to lung.

Roan filed that under Eckhart: useful, underpaid.

He let the pain register. A heartbeat.

[Invert impact trauma?]

No.

Not worth a quota.

He pivoted and drove his knee into the Husk's elbow joint. Not enough force to break it. Enough to change the seam. Sidewound followed.

This time, the blade opened the arm from wrist to shoulder.

The creature fell back, shrieking.

Drogan killed it without looking at Roan. "You hit?"

"Not open."

"That wasn't the question."

"Then ask better."

The door buckled again.

The ward line flickered.

Ines fired twice through the widening gap. The shots sounded wrong in the enclosed space, flat and hungry. Something outside stumbled away, clicking against stone.

Clicking.

One. Three. One.

The scout pattern from earlier.

Roan looked toward the ceiling.

Too late.

The roof caved in above the back wall.

Two Husks dropped through with the debris.

These were smaller, faster, their limbs folded tight until they hit the floor and sprang apart like broken umbrellas. One landed on the pump casing and launched toward Drogan. The other went for Ines.

Not random.

The pack had identified ranged threat and command threat.

Good. Intelligent enemies made fewer meaningless moves. Fewer meaningless moves meant patterns.

Ines ducked the first leap and slashed upward, but the Husk twisted midair. Its claw caught her rifle case strap and tore it free. The case skidded under the machinery.

Her expression sharpened.

Roan threw a flare stone at the creature's head.

It snapped its mouth open and swallowed the flare.

Unfortunate.

The flare ignited inside its throat.

More fortunate.

Pale light burst through the Husk's skull seams. It spasmed, blind or equivalent. Ines used the opening to drive both knives into the side of its neck and wrench opposite directions. Not enough to kill. Enough to expose the core line.

Roan would have respected the technique more if she hadn't immediately glanced toward the fallen rifle case.

"Ines," he said.

"I know."

"Do you?"

"Annoyingly, yes."

She abandoned the rifle and retreated behind Brendt's shield.

Good. Social penalty postponed.

Drogan's Husk was already dead. Of course it was. The old man had taken a shallow cut along his forearm, but the creature lay in two pieces near the wall, twitching.

The floor bulged again.

The door ward failed.

Not shattered. Failed.

The blue line went black, and the door burst inward.

Brendt took the first Husk head-on.

His shield frame screamed under the impact. He bent at the knees, shoulders locking, and redirected rather than stopped. Good. Very good. The Husk slid along the shield face and hit the pump casing. Roan stepped in and cut the exposed hip seam.

Sidewound opened it.

Brendt smashed the shield down on its skull.

Messy. Effective.

More shapes moved beyond the doorway.

Roan counted too many limbs in the gray dark.

Drogan retreated two steps, placing himself between the breach and the center of the room. "Fallback route."

Ines kicked aside a loose floor panel near the drainage channel. Beneath it, black space dropped into an old maintenance culvert.

"Found one," she said.

Brendt stared. "You knew that was there?"

"I suspected."

"That isn't the same thing."

"No time," Drogan said. "Ines first. Brendt second. Roan—"

"No," Roan said.

Drogan's glare could have cracked cheap glass. "Three seconds."

Roan pointed to the doorway. "They're not flooding. They're holding."

He was right.

The Husks beyond the door moved, clicked, scraped, but didn't all rush in. Several had bodies low to the ground, limbs splayed across walls and ceiling. Waiting.

For what?

Roan's gaze flicked to the floor bulges. The ceiling breach. Door. Below. Above. Front.

They weren't trying to overwhelm the camp.

They were shaping movement.

"They want us in the culvert," Roan said.

Ines froze halfway into lowering herself.

Brendt looked down at the black opening, then very slowly stepped back.

Drogan's face went flat.

A sound rose from the culvert.

Soft.

Wet.

Crowded.

Brendt whispered, "I vote not the culvert."

"Agreed," Ines said.

The Husks at the door clicked.

One. Three. One.

The pattern came from below too.

Roan considered the room.

Entrances compromised: door, roof, floor, false fallback. Camp ward down. Team intact but pressured. Enemy count unknown, coordination confirmed. Objective: force descent. Reason unknown. Possibilities: nest below, controller below, clean ruin access below, or digestion.

He disliked all options.

Available assets: Drogan high combat capability but conserving strength; Brendt shield functional but one frame damaged; Ines knives, rifle inaccessible but recoverable; Roan full quota, Sidewound tested, pendant absorbed one impact, Crimson Wake unused.

He knew timing.

Using Crimson Wake now would clear immediate pressure at a cost: blood loss, visible technique, possible overdraw if chained with Sidewound. But saving it risked team separation. Brendt in a culvert would die. Ines might survive long enough to make it expensive. Drogan would survive and hate himself.

Bad distribution.

Roan sheathed Sidewound.

Drogan saw the motion. "Roan."

"I know."

"No, you don't."

Roan drew a thin line across his left palm with his standard knife.

Blood welled black in the pale flare light.

The Codex Shard opened like an eye.

[Crimson Wake vector available.]

[Self-inflicted blood loss detected.]

[Invert?]

Not yet.

The Husks at the door surged.

Roan stepped forward, bloody hand closing around Sidewound's grip.

Pain threaded through palm, ribs and spine, plus the old wound where his father's ghost had left thirteen words behind. A heartbeat.

He smiled without warmth.

"Now," he said.
