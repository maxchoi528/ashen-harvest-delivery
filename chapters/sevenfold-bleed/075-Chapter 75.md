Chapter 75: A Cleaner Sent North

The rain on the window pressed a rhythm against the glass. Beep. Beep. Beep. The monitor array kept time. Brendt was still alive.

Roan stood at the bedside, watching the rise and fall of his friend's chest. The Rift contamination had been purged, but the body remembered. Vitalis channels inflamed, reacting to stimuli that no longer existed.

The Healer had said Brendt would wake in three hours. It had been two.

He turned to the narrow window. Ironholm sprawled below, lights cutting through the rain like scattered embers. Somewhere out there Selene Ardent was tracking the same absence of signal. Somewhere out there, the Cleaner dropped off the map the moment they'd entered the Hollow Reaches.

A soft groan behind him.

Brendt's eyes were open, unfocused, searching the ceiling before landing on Roan.

"Roan." Cracked, dry.

Roan poured water, held out the cup. Brendt drank with shaking hands.

"Healer said three hours. It's been two."

"Couldn't sleep." Brendt pushed himself upright. "Kept feeling something. Like falling. Like someone was calling."

"Calling?"

"I don't... it sounds stupid." Brendt's brow furrowed. "When I was falling, there was a moment where the Rift opened differently. Not like a wound. Like a door. And something on the other side looked back. I felt it recognize me. Not as a threat. As something... Familiar."

The monitor array beeped. Steady.

"The Hollow Reaches are deeper than we thought," Roan said. "Not news. What's new is that something down there might have a reason to keep you alive."

Brendt absorbed that. "That's either very good or very bad."

"Both. Usually both."

Silence. Then: "What happened after I fell? You came after me. I saw you jump."

Roan gave the clinical version. Extraction, medical team, two days under. He omitted the Codex Shard's pull, the Aldric Echo, the edge he'd danced along.

Brendt processed it. Then: "House Greel?"

"Unknown. Envoy's signal dropped in the Reaches."

"The Greel envoy." Brendt tested the name. "You're sure about him?"

"About as sure as I can be about someone who's been dead fifteen years and is now walking around Ironholm with a family grudge."

Brendt almost laughed. Almost.

"There's something else." Roan's voice shifted. "The Healer who treated you said the contamination purge was 'unusually clean.' Standard method, but execution above her level. She assumed a senior Healer stepped in." Pause. "There was no senior Healer."

Brendt's eyes narrowed. "You think someone intervened without being there."

"I think something used the Rift's own energies to scrub you clean. Not the Healer team. Something else."

The array beeped, steady. Brendt rubbed his temples.

"Roan. That technique you used—when you pulled me out—it felt like you were pushing against something. Like you were fighting to reach me. But the whole time I could sense... something moving with you. Like a hand on your shoulder."

Roan didn't answer immediately. His hand brushed his collarbone, where the Codex Shard rested beneath his shirt. It had been quiet since they left the Reaches. But Brendt's words made it pulse once, a fraction of warmth, then still.

"When I learned the Sevenfold Bleed," he said carefully, "the instructor said it wasn't meant for solo use. Designed for pairs. Meant to reach someone trapped in the Rift's pull."

"Sevenfold Bleed is forbidden."

"It works." That was all Roan gave. No Conclave history, no risk breakdown. The less Brendt knew directly, the less could be taken from him.

Brendt studied him. "That thing that guided you—was it part of the technique, or something else?"

"It's something my father left." Roan's tone closed the door. "That's all I'm telling you."

"Understood." Brendt didn't push. He swung his legs off the bed, tested his balance. The room swayed, but he steadied. "I can walk."

"Selene's waiting."

"Now?"

"The longer we wait, the more questions pile up."

Brendt stood, wobbled, caught himself on the bed frame. Roan didn't move to help. Brendt's pride wouldn't take it, and the stubbornness meant he was recovering faster than predicted.

"The Conclave will want a full debrief."

"They'll want what we choose to give them."

"And if what we give them raises more questions?"

Roan opened the door. "Then we deal with those too."

They walked the corridor. Healers passed without acknowledgment. The doors slid open automatically, a small efficiency that felt mocking.

Outside, Ironholm's rain had softened to drizzle. Sky the same grey as the buildings, nothing distinct, nothing certain.

Brendt fell into step beside Roan, matching pace despite the weakness. "One more thing. When I was falling, I thought I saw someone else. Not House Greel. Not the thing that called to me. Someone else standing at the edge of the light."

"Describe them."

"Couldn't. Too fast. But they were watching. Just... keeping track."

The word landed. *Track*. Someone keeping track.

"Man or woman?"

"Don't know."

"Clothing?"

"Just the impression of presence."

Another unknown. The Cleaner wasn't the only one in the Reaches. House Greel had been there. Selene had been tracking. And now a third.

"A presence that recognized you and a third party watching from the edge." Roan's voice was flat. "The Reaches are keeping a ledger."

"Let's hope it takes notes," Brendt muttered.

That almost earned a smirk. Almost.

They walked toward the transit hub, toward Selene, toward whatever answers the archives might hold.

Roan's pocket buzzed. A short encrypted message from Selene's relay:

*Cleaner signal just resurfaced. Northern quadrant, moving fast. Meet me at the archive annex. Bring Brendt.*

He read it twice, then pocketed the device.

"Change of plan?"

"Cleaner's still alive. Selene wants us at the annex now."

Brendt's jaw tightened. "He's heading north."

"It's what cleaners do."

They picked up the pace. The rain kept falling, a steady indifferent rhythm.

Roan's thumb brushed his collarbone again. The Shard had gone cold. But Brendt's words—*familiar*—still sat in his chest, a weight he'd have to measure later.

The transit hub doors slid open. Warm light spilled out.

"Brendt. When we talk to Selene, let me lead. She responds better to calculation than sympathy."

"You think she'll give us access?"

"She'll give us something. Everyone always gives something when the ledger's incomplete."

They stepped inside. The doors sealed behind them.

The Cleaner was moving. The Reaches were watching. And Selene Ardent had just put herself in play.

They had an hour to get to the annex. An hour to decide how much they were willing to lose.
