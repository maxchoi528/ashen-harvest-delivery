Chapter 180: Inverting Ice

He had turned one rule. Kael still had a blade, a functional body, superior formal training, and enough pride to keep spending damage as currency. More importantly, the oath seam had opened but not discharged. It pulsed beneath Kael's wrist in an ugly gold-red rhythm, visible only when frost thinned around the glove.

An opened seam wasn't the same as a confession.

Roan needed Kael conscious. Angry. Hurt enough for the oath to stutter, not damaged enough for the wards to intervene. Publicly pressured, but not publicly cornered so hard that silence became heroic.

A narrow lane.

Good.

Wide lanes made people lazy.

Kael stood with his left hand flexing, frost cracking between his fingers. The wound at his oath wrist hadn't broken skin, but black lines spread beneath it like ink under ice. He looked down once, then lifted his gaze.

"You planned that," he said.

Roan's ribs hurt when he breathed. "Yes."

Kael laughed once.

It wasn't a pleasant sound. "Do you plan everything?"

"No."

"Liar."

"Sometimes people are stupid without assistance."

Kael attacked on the last word.

Good. Dialogue as timing, not expression. He was recovering.

His blade came in three cuts: wrist, neck, inner thigh. Roan gave the wrist, protected the neck, moved the thigh out of line by less than an inch. The wrist cut opened skin. Cold entered. He didn't invert. Too small. The neck cut shaved hair. The thigh strike missed and cracked frost across the floor.

Roan countered with Sidewound toward Kael's ribs.

Kael parried with the flat of his blade and shifted the frost field around the contact point, trying to lock the knife again. Roan let the lock begin, then rotated the hilt in the direction the frost wanted instead of against it. The ice overcommitted. The seam opened along Kael's own parry angle.

Sidewound kissed the outer coat and split one of the reinforcing threads.

No injury.

But the audience saw Kael's coat open.

Kael saw the audience see it.

His jaw tightened.

Roan stepped back.

Prestige damage mattered. Not because Roan cared what the crowd thought, but because Kael did. House doctrine had made reputation part of his nervous system. Cut reputation and the body twitched.

Kael lifted his free hand. Frost gathered in a ring around Roan's feet.

Roan moved before it closed.

Too slow.

Ice locked around his left boot and climbed.

[Localized immobilization detected.]

He didn't need the Shard to tell him.

Kael advanced.

Roan had three options. Cut the ice with Sidewound, obvious and wasteful. Burn it with Pyrebrand, effective but noisy. Let Kael think he had anchored him, then use the anchor.

He chose the third.

Kael's blade thrust toward his shoulder.

Roan didn't dodge fully. He pivoted around the trapped foot, using the ice as a fixed point. The blade entered his shoulder weave, bit shallow flesh, and stopped short of the joint. Pain flared. Cold followed. A heartbeat.

[Invert frost-laced wound?]

No.

Not yet.

Roan brought Sidewound up under Kael's extended arm and struck the oath wrist again, not with the edge, but with the pommel.

Kael hissed and retreated.

The ice around Roan's boot cracked from the recoil tremor.

Roan freed his foot.

The crowd sound shifted. Not louder. Smarter. They were starting to understand that every exchange had two fights inside it: one with blades, and one with whatever Roan kept touching beneath Kael's glove.

That was acceptable. Curiosity made witnesses attentive.

Kael's eyes flicked toward the arbitration dais.

Hale watched without expression. The duel remained within bounds. Barely. Roan liked Hale more with every second she refused to be frightened into intervention.

Kael saw the same thing.

His next decision came from pressure.

He dropped the polite domain.

The frost around him withdrew into a tight mantle, white and silver layers wrapping his arms and shoulders, plus blade. The arena temperature rose by a fraction as the external field collapsed. Students in the first rows exhaled, thinking the worst pressure had passed.

Wrong.

Kael had stopped trying to control the ring.

Now he intended to control Roan.

The mantle technique was faster. Less area, more density. Every movement left afterimages of frost. His first dash crossed ten meters before the floor finished cracking under his launch.

For the benefit of spectators, the collapsed domain looked like Kael had reduced his range because Roan had forced him to. That was pleasant and wrong. The truth was more dangerous. Wide domains punished armies and cowards. Mantles punished individuals.

Drogan had once described close-range elemental mantles as "wearing a knife storm and hoping your skin has better lawyers than the other man's." It had sounded stupid at the time. Standing two meters from Kael as the frost thickened around his sleeves, Roan revised the judgment. It was still stupid, but accurately stupid.

The mantle didn't make Kael faster. It shortened the delay between thought and frost. Every twitch of his wrist created a localized pressure drop. Every footstep laid a slick patch exactly where Roan's next step wanted to be. The technique forced the opponent to solve three problems at once: the blade, the terrain, and the body losing small amounts of heat faster than it could report them.

A clean RoyalRoad rule, if one wanted to be abstract about it.

Kael gained speed by paying recoil. Roan gained information by making him spend it.

That was the trade.

Roan let the first mantle pass take a line from his sleeve. Cloth froze and shattered. Let the second pass nick his forearm. Blood rose and darkened, then crystallized along the cut. Let the third pass drive him half a step toward the center, where the ward telemetry was cleanest and the overhead microphones best.

Kael noticed the direction on the fourth exchange.

His eyes flicked up.

Good.

He knew Roan wanted record quality. That meant he would try to drive the fight back toward the west shadow or the broken pylon hum where readings blurred. A clever choice. Unfortunately, clever choices made through anger were still visible.

Kael changed angle.

Roan was already moving.

Their blades met twice, then not at all. Roan ducked under a mantle-laced slash and felt frost scrape across the top of his hair. A strand snapped off white. He marked the height. Kael's shoulder had locked for a fraction before the slash; recoil at the deltoid. Secondary seam. Not useful enough for confession, useful enough for survival.

Sidewound tapped the shoulder seam on the next pass.

Kael's left arm hesitated.

Only once.

But once was enough to prove the read.

Roan barely caught the blade.

Barely was a polite word for it.

Sidewound met the pale sword at an angle that saved his face and punished his wrist. Force drove through his elbow into his shoulder. The earlier cut tore wider. Frost mantle pressure crawled along his sleeve.

Kael's knee hit his stomach.

The Vance Pendant spread the impact but couldn't remove it. Air left Roan's lungs in an undignified burst.

Kael followed with an elbow toward the temple.

Roan dropped.

The elbow missed by a finger. Roan cut at Kael's ankle seam. Kael lifted his foot, avoiding the edge, and brought the heel down toward Roan's hand.

Roan withdrew.

Too late.

The heel clipped two fingers.

Something cracked.

[Minor fracture detected.]

[Invert?]

No.

Pain was information. Also, he was running out of polite quotas.

The first time Kael's mantle caught him cleanly, Roan learned another rule.

It didn't freeze everything equally.

The frost chased intention. A normal cold field spread through exposed surface and available moisture. Kael's mantle followed commitment. If Roan began a dodge and abandoned it, frost thickened along the abandoned line as though punishing the lie. If he feinted without committing muscle, the mantle ignored it. If he committed fully, it arrived ahead of the blade.

That made ordinary deception expensive.

Interesting.

Roan tested it three times.

First, a shoulder feint with no weight. The mantle did nothing.

Second, a real step left aborted halfway. Frost snapped around the empty space where his ankle would've been and shaved a layer from his boot.

Third, a full attack toward Kael's ribs, pulled only at the final instant. Frost locked along his forearm hard enough to burn skin.

[Localized frost adhesion detected.]

Roan ignored the prompt and tore free. Skin came with it.

Kael's eyes narrowed. "You are experimenting."

"Yes."

"During the duel."

"Best available laboratory."

Kael struck at his mouth.

Apparently even Greel heirs disliked being treated as equipment.

Roan leaned back. The blade missed his lips by less than a finger and cut the air cold enough to numb his teeth. He logged the reaction anyway. Taunting Kael didn't produce loss of form. Diminishing Kael's role from warrior to subject did. Pride had categories. Good to know.

He would use that carefully. Too much and Kael became reckless before the oath was ready. Too little and the confession never came.

He needed to bleed Kael's technique without making the audience think he was losing control.

That was harder than not dying.

The crowd understood obvious dominance. Blade at throat. Body on floor. Big number on a display. It didn't understand why Roan deliberately took a shallow rib cut instead of avoiding it cleanly, or why he let Kael's frost push him into a worse-looking stance because the worse stance exposed the oath wrist to the north camera. Spectators saw retreat. Hale saw legal contact. Vorath, if he was still behind the glass, saw pattern recognition.

Roan couldn't hide the pattern forever.

He didn't need forever.

He needed one question.

Between exchanges, the ward display flickered again, trying to summarize a fight that had stopped being polite to measurement.

KAEL GREEL

THERMAL RECOIL: 62%

SELF-HARM PROFILE ACTIVE

ROAN VANCE

PATHWAY INSTABILITY: 41%

RECOVERY METHOD: UNREGISTERED RESPONSE DETECTED

The last line caused a ripple in the faculty box.

Roan saw Cole lean toward Hale. Drogan's mouth moved in what was almost certainly a curse. Selene looked from the display to Roan and understood too much, too quickly.

The line was bad, but not fatal.

Unregistered response didn't prove Inversion. It proved only that Roan had recovered from internal frost in a way the arena's diagnostic matrix didn't possess a clean template for. Kael's mantle saturation had flooded the thermal channels with competing recoil signals. The arena was a dueling system, not a medical audit tool. It flagged the unexpected. It never truly understood the flagged.

Kael saw the line too. His expression sharpened.

"Hidden recovery after all," he said.

"Apparently."

"You lied to the baseline."

"No. It asked bad questions."

Kael's frost mantle flared with a sound like cracking teeth.

The temperature at the center of the ring dropped another degree. The old west pylon answered late again, its hum lagging behind the others. Kael either didn't notice or decided the delay was irrelevant. That was the problem with superior training: it taught people which anomalies to ignore. Most of the time that saved attention. Sometimes it gave an opponent a hinge.

Roan shifted his next retreat three inches closer to that hinge.

Kael's fifth mantle burst came with a feint toward the injured shoulder. Roan believed it just enough to sell the block. The true strike went low—a sweep of frost and steel meant to take his balance and pin him for a finishing freeze. Good. Kael had adjusted from killing immediately to disabling publicly. He was learning the arena even while losing the legal war.

Roan stepped onto the sweep.

The edge cut through his boot sole and opened a line along the outside of his foot. The frost tried to seize the ankle. He let it climb halfway, then drove his weight down. Ice fixed his foot to the floor.

Kael's eyes lit.

Roan rotated around the anchored foot and slammed his elbow into Kael's sternum.

Not hard enough to injure. Hard enough to interrupt the mantle breath.

Kael coughed once. The oath mark pulsed. Wrist spike early.

There.

Sidewound flashed toward it.

Kael jerked away in time, sacrificing the sleeve and a layer of skin along his forearm. First blood from him. Thin. Red. Freezing at the edges.

The arena display marked the strike as legal minor injury.

That mattered more than the injury. Legal minor injury meant the system had seen Sidewound touch Kael's arm and classified it as blade contact, not mental interference, not oath manipulation, not hidden technique. It was the wrong category for the right reason. Roan needed the record to keep making that mistake until the final question.

He could almost hear Ines saving the line for later.

Somewhere in the stands, someone shouted Roan's name and was immediately swallowed by louder noise.

Kael looked at the blood on his arm.

The old House polish returned like a door slamming shut.

"Enough," he said.

Roan freed his foot from the ice. "Usually not."

Kael pressed.

This was the fight he had wanted from the beginning: fast, close, too much pressure for Roan to stage legal theater. Kael's technique improved as the domain shrank. He had trained for duels, not battles. He understood distance and timing, plus how to make pain arrive before fear.

Roan respected it.

Then he used it.

Kael's mantle required constant recoil cycling. Every burst froze his channels and forced them open again under Greel discipline. The self-damage pattern had changed from three small pulses and one deep contraction to a faster rhythm: two surface burns, one wrist spike, one shoulder lock, reset.

The oath mark reacted to the wrist spike every time.

There was the rule.

Not Kael's weakness. His metronome.

Roan retreated in a spiral toward the ring center, taking small cuts and refusing larger ones. One across the ribs. One along the forearm. One glancing strike to the jaw that made his teeth click. He inverted none of them.

Kael's expression hardened with each refused reaction.

He expected desperation. Everyone did, eventually. Pain was supposed to simplify a person.

Roan found pain clarifying, provided it was catalogued properly.

Shoulder: bleeding, functional.

Left fingers: two fractured, grip compromised.

Ribs: bruised, pendant holding.

Quota: three confirmed remaining if oath inversion counted as one; maybe two if the Shard was being creative about math.

Exposure risk: present, mild visual ghosting at arena edge.

Vorath reflection: still in north glass. Watching.

Kael's wrist spike: due on next mantle burst.

Roan stepped into range.

Kael took the offer.

Of course he did.

He lunged, mantle flaring white around his blade.

Roan raised Sidewound too late.

The pale sword punched through his defense and struck his chest over the pendant. Not a stab; a freezing impact. The pendant spread force, but the frost tunneled around it, looking for softer pathways.

Internal cold again. A heartbeat.

[Invert thermal intrusion?]

Not yet.

Kael's wrist spiked.

Now.

[Invert thermal intrusion?]

Yes.

Roan's chest opened to heatless force. Vitalis surged outward, not enough for power, enough for motion. He trapped Kael's blade under his arm, stepped inside the mantle, and slammed his broken left fingers against the oath wrist.

Pain detonated up his hand.

[Fracture aggravated.]

Ignore.

Sidewound came across, shallow and precise.

This time it cut the glove.

White fabric split. Gold-red light spilled out.

The entire arena saw it.

Kael's eyes went wide.

Roan spoke quietly, but the ring microphones caught everything.

"Your grandfather should have used a better seal."

Kael snarled.

There it was. Not a word he had chosen. A sound dragged out from beneath training.

He drove his forehead into Roan's face.

The impact broke Roan's nose.

[Facial trauma detected.]

[Invert?]

No.

Blood ran hot over Roan's mouth, then cooled as frost touched it.

The crowd surged to its feet.

Kael shoved him back and raised both hands.

Third Stillness.

Too much.

Even Greel channels weren't built for three full compressions under mantle stress. The oath mark flared bright enough to paint Kael's glove red from beneath. The ward pylons shifted from blue to amber to warning orange.

Hale stood. "Combatant Greel, thermal recoil approaching unsafe threshold."

Kael didn't look at her.

"Continue," he said.

His voice had rough edges now.

Hale's gaze moved to Roan. "Combatant Vance?"

Roan wiped blood from his mouth. "Continue."

Drogan, high in the faculty box, moved one step forward.

Cole placed one hand on the rail.

Selene's face remained still, but her witness tablet had cracked under her grip.

Useful detail. Later.

The third Stillness formed.

This one wasn't elegant.

It was stronger.

The air screamed as motion left it. Frost leapt from the floor up the ward pylons, climbed the arena barrier, whitened the first row's protective shield. The death-stop system sounded one warning tone. Kael's sword vanished inside the white field.

Roan stood still.

Not because he wanted drama. Moving would waste blood pressure. His body had three useful motions left before the freeze reached the heart channels. He needed all three.

The cold took his feet.

Then knees.

Then hands.

Kael appeared out of the white, blade angled toward Roan's sternum.

Not throat. Not head.

Chest.

He was aiming for the pendant.

Smart.

Roan almost appreciated it.

The blade struck.

The Vance Pendant cracked.

Not visibly to the crowd, perhaps, but Roan felt it: a sharp internal sound beneath his shirt, a defensive anchor splitting under force it hadn't been made to take. Cold punched through the crack and into his central channels.

[External anchor damage detected.]

[Critical intrusion.]

[Invert?]

Yes.

The inversion hit like a door opening in a burning house.

All the cold Kael had forced into him became expansion. Not healing. Not warmth. Capacity. Roan's channels widened under brutal pressure, pulling the frost recoil into usable movement.

Vitalis climbed.

The west display, still reading through ward interference, flickered.

9,940.

14,200.

18,700.

Warning lines flashed. The crowd saw numbers and reacted like numbers were the point.

They weren't.

The point was Kael's oath wrist, glowing through torn fabric, directly above Roan's left hand.

Roan moved.

First motion: he turned his torso, letting the blade slide along the cracked pendant instead of through it.

Second motion: he stepped on Kael's lead foot, pinning it.

Third motion: he drove Sidewound into the oath seam and pulled.

Not deep. Not flesh. The smallest cut that could open a legal truth.

[Oath seam destabilized.]

[Exposure risk spike.]

[Recognition risk: severe.]

The reflection in the northern glass brightened.

Roan didn't look.

Looking would acknowledge the observer as part of the exchange. It would also invite whatever pressure traveled through the reflection to find a better handhold. Roan had enough problems in the ring without making eye contact with a Throne-tier murderer through illegal glass.

So he kept his attention on Kael's wrist, on the exact place where family command and personal pain had become the same line.

Kael's Stillness collapsed inward, not outward. The cold snapped back into its owner. His shoulders arched. Frost cracks ran up his neck. The wards screamed but didn't intervene; the injury was self-recoil, registered and accepted.

Exactly as Kael had filed it.

Thank you again.

Roan released the wrist and stepped back.

Kael fell to both knees.

The display stuttered, then stabilized long enough to show Roan's peak before crashing back down.

30,180.

Then 11,400.

Then error.

The arena lost its mind.

For most of them, the number was the revelation. Thirty thousand looked like victory wearing a decimal point. Roan knew better. It was a temporary peak bought with damage, quota ambiguity, and a cracked anchor. The display showed the receipt total, not the debt schedule. And Roan had always cared far more about debt schedules.

Roan barely heard it. His ears rang. His chest hurt around the cracked pendant. His broken fingers had gone numb in a way that suggested later consequences. The Codex Shard burned and hummed against his spine.

[Temporary peak achieved.]

[Quota remaining: 1?]

[Exposure risk: elevated.]

[External anchor integrity: compromised.]

Question mark after quota.

Annoying.

Kael tried to stand.

Failed.

Roan walked toward him.

Slowly, because walking quickly would've been medically optimistic.

Hale watched with one hand raised near the ward control, ready to stop lethal continuation.

Roan sheathed Sidewound.

The gesture traveled through the arena like a thrown stone.

Kael looked up.

Blood ran from his nose. Frost clung to his lashes. The torn glove exposed the gold-red oath mark, now pulsing out of rhythm.

Roan crouched in front of him.

"Kael Greel," he said.

The microphones drank the words.

Kael's lips pulled back. "Go to hell."

"Already scheduled."

Roan placed his broken left hand on Kael's exposed wrist.

The oath mark flared.

Kael tried to jerk away.

Couldn't.

Roan didn't squeeze. He didn't twist. No pain beyond what the oath had already made. Torture was crude and legally inconvenient.

He asked one question.

"Why did you come to Black Spire?"

Kael's mouth closed.

For one second, nothing happened.

Then the oath stuttered.
