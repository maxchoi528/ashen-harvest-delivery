Chapter 25: The Threshold

Roan found Brendt in the library's eastern annex. Dust motes drifted through the lamplight. The air smelled of binding glue and slow decay. Brendt was hunched over a logbook whose spine cracked when he turned the pages—paper that hadn't seen air in decades.

"You're brooding," Roan said.

"I'm calculating." Brendt pushed the book across the table. "There's a difference."

Roan sat. The wood creaked. "What am I looking at?"

"The Hollow Reaches. Tier-Four. Before the seal."

"The official logs say the Reaches were evacuated."

"The official logs are a lie." Brendt's voice was flat, but his knuckles were white where he gripped the table edge. "A survey expedition went down thirty-two years ago. Forty-seven Hunters. They mapped a city. Estimated population of intelligent Hollowkin, eleven thousand."

Roan's fingers drummed once on the wood. "Intelligent enough to organize."

"Intelligent enough to build." Brendt pulled a second sheet from beneath the pile. "A follow-up expedition found the city empty. No bodies. No combat damage. The Bureau sealed every file."

Roan studied his face. "How do you have access to sealed Bureau files?"

Brendt didn't answer immediately. The lamp flickered. Somewhere in the stacks, a page turned in a book no one was reading.

"The lead surveyor was my mother's cousin. He sent her a personal letter before the expedition. Described the buildings. The layout. What he called 'precision construction.'" Brendt paused. "He never came back. She spent the rest of her life trying to find out what happened."

"You never told me."

"I don't talk about it." Brendt's jaw tightened. "The drone sweeps from the last six months show *expansion*. The tunnels are getting wider. Longer. Someone is building down there. The Bureau is suppressing the data."

Roan absorbed it. A city that should be empty, actively expanding. A Bureau that classified evidence instead of investigating it. A family wound Brendt had carried without showing.

"What's your angle?"

Brendt pulled a small vial from his pocket and set it on the table. Dark liquid caught the lamplight. "The Spire has a research expedition planned. Tier-Three survey. Instructor Maren. She needs Hollow Cores for funding."

"And?"

"And Drogan is coming as an off-the-books navigator. Maren specifically requested him."

That was new. Drogan hadn't mentioned it.

"She also has a daughter with a degenerative nerve condition. The Bureau has the treatment synthesis. They gate it behind extraction quotas." Brendt tapped the vial. "Maren needs a big find to access the medicine. She's competent. Careful. And desperate enough to take a chance on a fight she can't fully vet."

Roan looked at the vial, then at Brendt. "You want me to use a twelve-year-old's illness as leverage to get into a restricted zone."

"I want you in the Reaches." Brendt met his gaze without flinching. "You need field experience. I need someone who trusts my intel enough to look where the Bureau doesn't want anyone looking. Maren is the door. The girl is why I found the crack in her armor. You decide how to walk through it."

Roan turned the arithmetic. A desperate mother. A hidden city. Drogan in the background, ready to guide. A Bureau sitting on a thirty-year lie.

"I need a sponsor for the application."

"Selene Ardent. House Ardent. She owes me. I already spoke to her."

"Faculty sponsors don't take students without a personal stake."

"Selene has her own reasons." Brendt gathered the papers and slid them back into the pile. "The expedition leaves in eighteen days. Application deadline is in three. Maren reviews them herself."

Roan stood. "I'll think about it."

"You have three days to file. Eighteen to decide if you want to walk into something that might not let you back out." Brendt moved toward the door. "I'll be in the training hall."

Roan sat alone with the vial, the silence, and the shape of something he couldn't see yet.

---

The next morning, he went to the third floor.

The administrative corridor was beige and fluorescent. A poster warned against unauthorized Rift access. Another advertised the exchange rate between Spire Credit and Black Market Tokens.

Selene's office was at the end. A small placard read: *S. Ardent. Student Affairs Liaison (Junior Division).*

He knocked.

"Enter."

The office was aggressively organized. Color-coded tabs lined the shelves. A single stack of forms sat on the desk beside a cup of tea that was still steaming. Selene Ardent was already watching the door.

"Roan Vance." She didn't gesture for him to sit. She just assessed.

"You were expecting me."

"Brendt explained the situation." She tapped the application form. "Standard expedition excuse. Seeking field experience in advanced Rift navigation and hazard assessment."

"It's accurate."

"No, it's boring." She leaned forward. "Boring is good. Boring keeps you alive down there. But I want you to understand something. I'm not sponsoring you because Brendt asked. I'm sponsoring you because I want something in return."

Roan waited.

"There's a structure in the lower reaches that doesn't appear on any Bureau map. A sealed room. House Ardent's archives predate the Spire by three generations. They mention a temple at these coordinates." She slid a sealed envelope across the desk. "I don't know what's in it. But I want to know why the Bureau erased it from every official record."

Roan picked up the envelope. "And if I find nothing?"

"Then you had a boring expedition, and I was wrong." Her smile was cold. "Either way, your records will be clean enough to survive a Sentinel audit. As long as you *look*."

"I'll look."

"I know. That's what concerns me." She returned to her tea. "The expedition leaves in eighteen days. Maren interviews on day three. Be punctual. Be forgettable. I don't sponsor instability, and I don't sponsor corpses."

Roan left the office with the envelope in his pocket and the shape of the equation solidifying in his mind.

Brendt wanted the truth about the Reaches—and about what took his mother's cousin.

Selene wanted reconnaissance on a temple the Bureau had buried.

Maren wanted a cure for her daughter.

Drogan was waiting in the wings, ready to guide.

Everyone was pulling toward the same dark door, each for their own reasons.

Eighteen days.

He could work with that.
