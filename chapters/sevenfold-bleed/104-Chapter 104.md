Chapter 104: The Unbroken

The room was dark. He had not turned on the light. The only illumination was a thin strip of amber beneath the door. He had been sitting long enough for the stiffness in his back to mark the time. He was processing what Drogan had told him.

Kaelen the Unbroken. Third member of the Black Squad. Disappeared after Aldric's death. Possibly alive. Possibly in Suthmere.

His father had never mentioned the Black Squad. Not in the journal, not in the data embedded in the Codex Shard. Aldric had buried that secret, and Drogan had carried it for six years.

Kaelen. He had seen the name in Aldric's margins, always a warning. *Kaelen doesn't forgive. Kaelen doesn't forget. If he finds you before you're ready, he will break you.*

But Drogan said Kaelen was the best combatant he had ever seen. Those two descriptions did not align. Unless Aldric's death had broken something in Kaelen, too.

Roan reached for the Codex Shard, pressed for the data. Nothing. Aldric had hidden Kaelen from the record as carefully as he had hidden the rest of the truth.

It didn't matter whether Kaelen was friend or enemy. It mattered whether Kaelen had information. And if Kaelen had seen the execution order, he was the closest thing to a living witness Roan could reach.

He stood.

The Suthmere address was still in his jacket. The timing was too precise. Someone in Suthmere knew he was coming. Someone wanted him to arrive.

He pulled it out. *Get to Suthmere.* An address. A date. Eleven days.

He took inventory, not on paper, but in the same mental ledger he used for everything. Drogan would need to know. Brendt would need to cover the supply lines. And Iris was holding the Maddox folder like a live grenade. If she confronted her grandfather before Roan left, the blast would hit the wrong target.

He folded the paper. Eleven days. It would have to be enough.

- break -

Brendt found him at 2 AM, still packing.

"You're leaving."

It wasn't a question.

"Suthmere. Eleven days."

Brendt leaned against the doorframe. "How long?"

"I don't know. Depends on what I find."

"Kaelen. Drogan told me. The Unbroken. Sounds like a ghost story."

"If he is, he's one who sent an address."

Brendt snorted. "Or one who wants to bury you next to your father." He crossed the room and sat on the cot, watching Roan pack in silence for a long moment.

"Iris is going to do something stupid if you don't talk to her. She's locked herself in the archive."

"I know."

"And the supply lines. Old Pell can't cover the hellhound venom alone. I'll handle it."

Roan stopped packing. "You don't have to."

"I know." Brendt picked up a loose strap from the pack and tightened it. "You owe me. Figure out what you owe me when you get back."

"If I get back."

Brendt shook his head. "You'll come back. You're too stubborn to die in a mountain. Besides, who else is going to explain your death to your mother?"

He stood and left. The door clicked shut behind him.

Roan stood in the middle of the room, half-packed, the name Kaelen still burning behind his eyes.

*Unbroken.* It sounded like an achievement. In practice, it probably meant he had just gotten good at not looking down.

Eleven days. Then the mountain.
