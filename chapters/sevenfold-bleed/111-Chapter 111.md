Chapter 111: The Bracket Narrows

The corridor stank of copper and ozone.

Roan pressed himself against the stone wall, counting heartbeats. Three. Four. The Husks had stopped moving at the junction thirty meters ahead, their clicking mandibles fell silent in unison, a sound like a hundred locks clicking shut.

Of course. They weren't animals. They were following orders.

His Mental Radius stretched forward, testing the darkness. Five signatures. Four in the corridor ahead, one flanking left through the service duct. The Hollowed Sovereign's servants had finally realized a Tempered-ranked intruder wasn't going to die to their first wave of cannon fodder.

Roan exhaled slowly through his nose. The ledger hadn't changed, but the variables had shifted.

[The Inversion Capacity: 3/5.]

Not full. He'd burned two uses clearing the first checkpoint, the Crimson Wake technique on a Husk that had gotten too close, its acid-spray killing another two behind it. Clean. Efficient. The Codex Shard had converted the blood loss into forty-three Vitalis, which was about forty-three Vitalis more than bleeding out in a Rift.

Problem was, that left him three inversions for the next four hours of descent.

Problem within the problem: the Hollowed Sovereign had stationed five Husks here instead of two. Which meant it knew.

It knew he was coming.

Roan's jaw tightened. The logical conclusion was obvious. Someone had talked. The Junior Hunters' Hall's mission briefing had been specific enough, target location, estimated Rift age, projected Hollowed Sovereign tier. Someone had sold that information.

Cyrus Ashford's face surfaced in his mind. Then Selene Ardent's. Then Warden Drogan's, which he'd discarded immediately because Drogan didn't betray and didn't make mistakes, and the intersection of those two facts meant the leak was elsewhere.

The Husks shifted. One of them made a sound, a low clicking that resonated through the stone.

They were repositioning. The flanking one had stopped moving, waiting for the corridor group to pin him in place.

Roan ran the figures again. Five Husks. Tempered-tier creatures, each with Vitalis readings between six hundred and eight hundred on a good day. He was at roughly three thousand, which was a five-to-one advantage in raw output. The Crimson Wake would let him cut through two before the other three closed the gap. The Hollowstrike Doctrine would let him turn the third's momentum against it.

But three simultaneous melee engagements at Tempered-tier speed wasn't math he liked.

The service duct. That was the variable.

He could hear it now, the faint scrape of chitin against metal as the flanking Husk navigated the narrow passage. It would emerge behind him in approximately ninety seconds if it maintained current pace. The corridor group was holding position, waiting for the pincer to complete.

Roan made his decision.

He stepped into the corridor. Directly into the kill zone. The four Husks surged forward as one.

They were all wrong, in the way that Hollowkin were wrong. Their bodies were close enough to human to trigger pattern recognition, close enough to make the lizard brain scream. Six limbs. Exoskeletons that shifted between carapace-black and grey, like oil on water. Faces that almost had features, eye sockets where eyes should be, a mouth-slit that opened too wide and too far.

Roan didn't let them get close.

Crimson Wake: First Cut.

The blade opened his left forearm from wrist to elbow in a single precise line. Blood sprayed. The Husks flinched, instinct, prey behavior they'd inherited from whatever humans they'd been before the Rift had hollowed them out.

The Inversion activated in the space between heartbeats.

[Sidewound absorbed.]

[Conversion successful: +67 Vitalis.]

[Capacity: 2/5.]

The blood loss reversed. Veins sealed. The wound closed to a thin pink line that would be invisible under his sleeve in an hour.

The first Husk died before it registered the reversal.

Sidewound caught it under the mandible, angled up, severing the nerve cluster that connected the creature's brain to its motor functions. It dropped mid-leap, legs still twitching.

The second Husk adjusted trajectory, a slight course correction that Roan had predicted. He'd seen the shift in its forelimbs, the subtle redistribution of weight. It expected him to follow through on the kill.

He didn't. He pivoted, letting momentum carry the blade in a controlled arc, and caught the third Husk across its thorax. Not a kill shot. A disable. The creature staggered, exoskeleton cracking along the impact line.

The fourth Husk was already past him.

Roan twisted, dropped, rolled, brutal economy of movement that threw him under the creature's leap. Its forelimbs raked stone where his spine had been. He came up inside its guard, drove Sidewound through the gap in its cervical plating, and felt the satisfying crunch as the blade found the central nerve cluster.

One left. The damaged third, trying to retreat.

Roan let it run. Sixty seconds until the flanking fifth arrived. The damaged Husk would slow it down, but not by much.

He cleaned the blade on the nearest corpse and started moving toward the service duct junction. The flanking Husk emerged exactly when he expected it to. It came through the access grate low and fast, mandibles spread wide for a grapple. Which meant it expected him to be standing in the corridor where the other four had been waiting.

It didn't expect him to be already past the junction, Sidewound waiting in a high guard position.

The creature had exactly one second of free air before it died.

[Capacity: 1/5.]

Roan stepped over the corpse and kept walking.

The mission briefing had been specific: descend three levels, locate the Hollow Core, mark it for extraction. The Sovereign would be somewhere in the final descent, guarding the Core itself. The briefing had estimated Tier 3 to Tier 4 based on Rift age and expansion rate.

Had estimated. Past tense. Past tense meant the estimate might be wrong.

The stairwell opened into a massive chamber, cavernous, natural, the stone walls scarred by what looked like crystal growths that pulsed with faint bioluminescence. The Hollow Reaches did that to places. The Rift energy got into the rock, the water, the air, and everything grew wrong.

Or was grown wrong. The distinction mattered when you were trying to survive.

The Hollow Core was visible at the chamber's center. It was a sphere of crystallized corruption, roughly two meters in diameter, pulsing with light that was too slow and too bright for any natural process. Beautiful, in the way a tumor was beautiful under the right lighting.

But it wasn't unguarded.

The Hollowed Sovereign stood between Roan and the Core.

It had been human once. That was the worst part, it was impossible not to see the ghost of the person underneath the Hollowing. Female, or what had been female before the Rift had stretched the bones and reinforced the joints and replaced the soft tissue with chitinous plating. Its height had doubled, maybe tripled. The face still had something like features, cheekbones, a nose, a mouth that opened in four directions instead of two.

It wore armor made of its own exoskeleton. It carried a weapon that might've been a spear, might've been a limb that had been modified into something longer and sharper.

And it was looking directly at him.

"You are the Vance boy."

The voice came from everywhere and nowhere, the creature's throat had been restructured into something that wasn't vocal cords.

Roan kept walking. Slow. Deliberate. The balance was running in his head, numbers flickering across his mental screen.

"Correct."

"The one who kills our kind without fear." The Sovereign tilted its head, a gesture that was almost human, almost curious. "House Greel has offered bounty for your capture. Live. Not dead. Lord Greel was specific."

House Greel. Founding Seven. The Northern Crown.

Roan's expression didn't change. His mind did, the tally recalculating, new variables entering the equation.

"The bounty is irrelevant," he said. "Move."

"You misunderstand." The Sovereign stepped forward, and the chamber seemed to contract around it. "I am not here to collect the bounty. I am here to offer an alternative."

Right.

"I have no interest in alternatives."

"Everyone has interest in survival." The Sovereign's four-part mouth opened in what might've been a smile. "Your Inversion is impressive. The Codex Shard's signature is distinctive. Lord Greel recognized it. From seventy years ago. When the Seven burned your father's archive."

Roan stopped walking.

The Inversion. The Codex Shard. His father's technology.

House Greel knew. The Northern Crown had encountered Aldric Vance, and now he knew the son was following in the father's footsteps.

This mission had never been about the Hollow Core. Someone had known exactly where Roan would go and why, and the Hollowed Sovereign was the bait.

"Here's the alternative," Roan said. His voice was flat. "I kill you. I take the Core. I report to the Guild that the Sovereign was higher-tier than estimated, and I get a commendation for surviving."

"Or?"

"Or I take the information about my father that you're carrying and leave."

The Sovereign's smile widened. "House Greel is correct. You are clever. More clever than your father was at your age."

"I'm not interested in your assessments."

"The Seven don't share their history," the Sovereign continued. "But the Hollowed remember everything. We were there when the archive burned. We watched."

Roan's hand didn't move toward Sidewound. His posture didn't shift. Nothing changed externally.

Internally, the calculations were running at a speed he'd never reached before.

The archive. His father's home. Something the Founding Seven had destroyed.

"You're offering to trade information about my father for my compliance."

"I am offering survival. Both of ours." The Sovereign lowered its weapon, not a threat display, something more deliberate. "Lord Greel knows the Vance bloodline still exists. He's sending hunters. You won't survive their attention. But I can hide you. Here, in the Hollow Reaches, where the Seven's eyes can't reach."

"And in exchange?"

"Stay. Become one of us. Hollowing isn't death, it's transformation. Your Inversion would persist. Grow. You would be stronger than House Greel's hunters, stronger than the Seven themselves."

Silence.

Roan considered the offer. The arithmetic. The variables. The likely outcomes.

"Declined."

The Sovereign's face fell. Loss, maybe, or disappointment. It was hard to read expressions that had been stretched and restructured beyond recognition.

"A pity. I had hoped the son would be more pragmatic than the father."

"My father was pragmatic. That's why he built the Codex Shard. That's why he sent it forward in time instead of staying to die with the rest of you."

Something shifted in the Sovereign's posture. A tension. A readiness.

"You know more than you should, boy."

"I know enough."

The chamber exploded into motion. The fight lasted eleven seconds.

Roan moved first, not toward the Sovereign but toward the wall, using a crystal formation as cover while he activated every tactical option he'd been calculating since the conversation started.

The Sovereign had been stalling. Its posture had been wrong for combat, too relaxed, too open. It expected to talk longer. It expected him to consider the offer, weigh the variables, make a careful choice.

It hadn't expected him to reject the offer and immediately pivot to violence while its guard was down.

Sevenfold Bleed: Third Cycle.

He'd only driven the technique this far twice before. The strain on his Vitalis channels, on his body, was immense. Seven pathways burning simultaneously, each one sending white-hot fire through his nervous system.

[Sidewound absorbed. Seven wounds logged.]

[Mortal-grade Sidewound detected.]

[Conversion capacity exceeded. Efficiency: 20%.]

[Residual Sidewound stress: active.]

[Warning: Exposure risk.]

[Capacity: 0/5.]

Pain. Seven points of it, radiating outward from his core. The Inversion was converting what it could, the burning, the tissue damage, the blood, but the technique had outpaced the system. He was taking real strain.

He didn't care.

The Sovereign's spear thrust found empty space. Roan was already past its guard, Sidewound carving a line through chitinous plating that had resisted Tempered-tier weapons for decades.

The creature screamed, a sound that shook dust from the cavern ceiling, and twisted, its secondary limbs sweeping toward him in a pincer attack.

Hollowstrike Doctrine. Inversion-compatible.

The impact should have shattered his arm. Instead, the force redirected through the technique's pathway, converted, added to his momentum. He twisted in mid-air, used the Sovereign's own strength to accelerate his return pass.

Sidewound found the joint where neck met spine.

The Sovereign didn't die cleanly. Hollowed Sovereigns didn't have clean deaths, the corruption ran too deep, the transformation too complete. Instead, it collapsed, limbs still twitching, and its voice came out as a wet rasp.

"The hunters... Are already... Here."

Roan stepped back, breathing hard. The pain was receding as the Inversion finished its work, but the exposure risk remained, a weight at the back of his mind that would take days to fade. The incomplete conversion meant the Sevenfold Bleed's stress was still present in his channels, raw and unredeemed.

He checked his Mental Radius. Expanded it past the chamber walls.

Three signatures. Coming down the stairwell he'd used. Moving with the confidence of people who knew exactly where they were going.

Tempered-tier, all three. Maybe Hardened. Hard to tell at this distance.

"Already here," he murmured. "Which means the Sovereign was the secondary trap."

The mission briefing had been compromised. The Hollow Core had been bait. And whoever had set this up had known exactly how Roan would respond to the Sovereign's offer, which meant they knew about the Inversion, about his father, about the Codex Shard.

They'd wanted him to reject the Sovereign. They'd wanted him to kill it.

And now they were here to kill him.

Roan looked at the Hollow Core. At the Sovereign's twitching corpse. At the three signatures growing closer.

He had maybe ninety seconds before they reached the chamber.

The math didn't work. Three versus one, two of them likely Hardened, with zero left in the Inversion tank and a body full of stress from an overloaded Third Cycle.

He could run. The service ducts would get him out, eventually, with enough luck and enough speed.

But they knew the layout. They knew the mission briefing. They knew his escape routes.

Running wasn't math. Running was hope, and hope was for people who couldn't calculate.

He looked at the Hollow Core.

It pulsed, slow and bright, like a heart that didn't know it was dying.

Or maybe like a bomb that did know.

Roan made his decision.

He picked up the Sovereign's discarded spear, too long for his preferred style, but the weight was right, and he walked toward the chamber's only other exit.

The one that led deeper into the Hollow Reaches.

The one no one would expect him to take.

The footsteps in the stairwell were quiet. Professional.

Roan disappeared into the dark, and the Hollow Reaches swallowed him whole.
