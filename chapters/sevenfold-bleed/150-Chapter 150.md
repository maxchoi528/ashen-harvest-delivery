Chapter 150: A Name Behind the Glass

The temple door opened before they reached it.

Not all the way. Not even enough for a body to pass. The two black stone panels parted by the width of a hand, releasing a thin blade of gray city-light into the corridor. The pressure outside came with it.

Brendt raised his shield.

Ines drew both knives.

Drogan's breathing changed.

Roan watched the gap.

Nothing lunged through.

That was polite of it.

Also suspicious.

The guardian stopped three paces from the threshold. "Blood-marked heir must depart. Companions may depart. Archive will close after exit. Re-entry requires proof not currently possessed."

"Define proof," Roan said.

"Return with wound named by choice, not inheritance."

Roan filed that under later problems.

"Can you fight outside?" Brendt asked the guardian.

"No."

"Can you fight inside?"

"Yes."

"Can we stay inside forever?"

"No."

"I knew I wouldn't like the sequence."

Ines glanced at the guardian. "Why can't you fight outside?"

"I am remnant-bound. Temple law defines my body. City law disputes it."

"If you cross the threshold?"

"I become argument. Hungry corrections resolve arguments."

Brendt looked at Roan. "I don't understand the metaphysics, but I understood the outcome."

"Good enough," Roan said.

Drogan stepped beside him. "Plan."

Roan looked through the narrow gap.

The exterior stairs were visible in slices: black stone, gray ash, the broken plaza beyond the temple entrance. No immediate claws. No visible front line. Too clean.

The enemy had learned.

The first night attack had used pressure from multiple surfaces to force them into a prepared culvert. Now they had the temple door, a single exit, and an army choosing not to rush. That meant the trap wasn't at the threshold. It was beyond visible range.

Likely formations: outer ring of Husks in cover, Hollowkin command nodes elevated or buried, bone-wire constructs targeting route markers and exposed ankles, possible clean objects as observation anchors. Unknown elite presence. Guardian called them hungry corrections, not Husks. That suggested they were a response mechanism, more than predators.

Response to what?

Blood-marked heir exiting with archive contact.

The city considered him an error.

Roan disliked being categorized by urban metaphysics.

"We don't run straight," he said. "We exit in wedge, turn left toward the broken colonnade, not the main stairs. They expect the stairs. Brendt leads shield for first three seconds, then Drogan passes left and cuts command line if formation reveals. Ines marks only hard turns. I rear until contact, then I move wherever the line opens."

"And if no line opens?" Ines asked.

"We make one."

"With Crimson Wake?"

"No."

Drogan looked at him.

Roan met his eyes. "Not first. They saw the first night clear. If something commands them, it knows blood-wave output. We hold it until they commit to a counter-pattern."

Drogan nodded. "Agreed."

Brendt adjusted his shield grip. "I love being part of plans where the phrase counter-pattern appears."

"Your job is simple," Roan said.

"You keep saying that before terrible things."

"Stand where being hit helps."

"That isn't simple. That's philosophy with bruises."

The door opened another inch.

Outside, the clicking stopped.

All at once.

Everyone heard it.

The silence was worse.

Roan lifted the Black Rite manuscript and strapped it inside his chest harness beneath the Vance Pendant. It sat cold against him. The Codex Shard pulsed once but offered no prompt.

Either it approved or had decided commentary would be redundant.

He preferred neither.

Drogan spoke without looking back. "Guardian."

The remnant inclined its head.

"If Aldric returns as echo?"

The corridor became very still.

Roan didn't look at Drogan.

The guardian answered after a delay. "Echo of blood-marked predecessor unlikely. Archive room preserves intention, not ghost. City may imitate. Don't answer."

Drogan nodded once.

Roan kept the question, the timing, and the specific word predecessor.

Later.

The door opened enough.

Gray light filled the corridor.

"Move," Drogan said.

Brendt went first.

The shield crossed the threshold and the world attacked.

Not with claws. With sound.

Every clean object in the plaza rang at once.

The cup they had seen on the first day. The folded coat in the glass storefront. The polished statue heads. The clean circle in the ruined substation. Objects Roan couldn't see but knew, instantly, belonged to the same category. Their ringing struck the Vance Pendant and passed through it into his sternum.

The Codex Shard flared.

[Recognition net activated. Hostile categorization attempt suppressed.]

Roan stepped through the door.

For half a second, the Hollowed City knew him.

Not as Roan Vance. Not as student, killer, brother, son, debtor, or heir.

As continuation.

The Black Rite answered before the Shard could.

First Refusal drew a clean black line through the pressure.

No.

The categorization cracked.

Pain hit behind Roan's eyes, sharp and sterile.

He accepted the pain without inversion.

Measurement, not waste.

The plaza resolved.

They were surrounded.

For the first ten paces, nothing touched them.

That was the proof of command. Hunger didn't wait this well. Animals lunged when prey emerged from a hole. Husks, even the smarter Tier Three variants, still carried that basic impatience under the pack logic. The ranks in the plaza held themselves back with visible strain. Pale fingers dug into stone. Vertical mouths flexed open and shut without sound. Masked Hollowkin tilted their heads in tiny corrections whenever a Husk leaned too far forward.

The crowned node didn't move at all.

Its four arms hung at four different angles, each hand forming a sign Roan couldn't read but could feel pressing against the formation. The black antlers rising through its mask weren't bone. Not exactly. They branched like frozen command lines, each point connected to the army by threads his new Black Rite awareness could almost see.

Almost was dangerous.

Almost tempted assumption.

Roan deliberately looked away from the antlers and counted the things that could kill them immediately.

Seventeen Husks in front arc. Nine on visible walls. At least twelve above. Four masked Hollowkin with blades. Two relay bodies marked by thicker gray armor and no weapons, likely sacrificial command anchors. Bone-wire constructs too numerous to count and therefore better treated as terrain. Clean objects in sight: six statue heads, one cup on a window ledge, three polished stones placed in a triangle near the fountain, a hanging child's mask under the colonnade.

He preferred fights that resisted being counted. Short lists meant the surprise was still somewhere off the list.

The mask bothered him most.

It was too small.

Fourteen and under mapped too easily onto Lyra. Even when the object wasn't a child, even when it was likely bait, the association had teeth. Roan saw the mask, kept the reaction, and let First Refusal keep it from deciding his route.

The city noticed anyway.

The mask turned toward him.

Brendt saw it and went rigid. "Roan."

"Don't look at it."

"I already looked at it."

"Then stop having eyes."

"Working on it."

Ines moved half a step to block Brendt's line of sight. Not kind. Practical. He was the shield. If the clean object hooked him, everyone paid.

The crowned node clicked once.

The child's mask smiled.

Roan didn't cut it.

He wanted to. That was the point.

Instead, he flicked a pebble with his boot into a different clean object, the triangle of polished stones near the fountain. Not hard. Not an attack. A disturbance. The pebble tapped the nearest stone and bounced away.

The recognition net twitched toward the contact point.

For a fraction of a second, the child's mask stopped smiling.

"Useful," Ines murmured.

"Expensive if repeated," Roan said.

Drogan understood. "It learns attention."

"Yes."

"Then don't teach it too much."

"That was the plan."

Brendt shifted his shield. "The plan being irritate the haunted objects just enough?"

"Exactly."

"I preferred not understanding."

Roan didn't answer. He was counting how quickly the formation repaired itself after each disruption. First break: three seconds. Second break: a little under two. Recognition disturbance: half a breath. The army wasn't recovering. It was learning recovery, shortening its own confusion with every exchange. That meant every repeated tactic depreciated immediately.

A useful lesson, if they survived long enough to invoice it.

The plaza rewarded repetition with punishment. That was almost comforting. It meant the enemy obeyed a rule, and rules could be abused once named.

He told himself that twice, not because he needed reassurance, but because the first rule of staying alive was making useful observations louder than fear.

Brendt would have called that grim. Ines would have called it exploitable. Drogan would have said nothing and done it anyway. Roan preferred the shortest version: found rule, cut here.

He changed his grip on Sidewound and marked the command node's relays again. No identical route. No identical cut. No identical joke twice, despite Brendt's preferences.

Hollowkin filled the ruins in layered rings. Not Husks alone. Husks crouched along walls and roofs, pale limbs folded, mouths shut. Bone-wire constructs skittered through ash near the ground in glittering streams. Taller figures stood behind them. Hollowkin remnants or something wearing the idea of soldiers badly. Their bodies were wrapped in gray strips of old armor, faces hidden behind cracked masks, each mask marked with lines that didn't match the guardian's seven cuts.

At the far end of the plaza, on the dry fountain where statue heads faced inward, stood a larger figure with four arms and a crown of black antlers grown through its mask.

Command node.

Brendt took the first impact.

A Husk launched from the left colonnade. It hit his shield and bounced off at a wrong angle because Brendt had finally learned not to stop everything head-on. Good. He redirected it into the stone steps. Ines cut its rear tendon as it slid. Roan severed the exposed seam at the base of the skull with Sidewound.

The creature died silently.

The army didn't react.

That was unfortunate.

The first masked soldier to reach them after the recognition net broke carried no weapon.

That made it the most dangerous one in the immediate arc.

Roan had learned early in Greycross that empty hands in a knife fight meant one of three things: arrogance, hidden weapon, or someone else doing the stabbing. The soldier moved with neither arrogance nor fear. Its palms were open, fingers spread, gray cords running from wrist to elbow like exposed tendons. Command conduit. Maybe relay. Maybe trap.

"Don't let it touch skin," he said.

Brendt shifted his shield to block it. The soldier placed both palms against the metal. For a heartbeat, nothing happened. Then the shield frame's ward line flickered, and Brendt's left arm jerked as if someone had pulled strings through the elbow.

Roan cut the soldier's wrist seam before the effect climbed higher.

The hands fell away.

Brendt inhaled sharply. "It tried to move me."

"Noted."

"I would like it noted angrily."

"Later."

Drogan's expression had gone murderous in the quiet way. Mental command, physical override, oath-bind, whatever category the Hollowkin used, it touched one of his old wounds. The Black Rite platform hadn't removed that. It had given the wound a fence.

"Focus," Roan said.

Drogan's eyes cut to him.

"The conduit is bait," Roan said. "They want you angry enough to sever wrong."

The old man looked back at the advancing ranks. His blade lowered half an inch. "I know."

"Now you do."

"Left," Drogan said.

They turned toward the broken colonnade as planned.

The ground opened.

Not beneath them. Ahead. Stone tiles lifted like trapdoors, revealing a lattice of pale arms underneath. The route was blocked before they committed.

Roan changed the plan.

"Right."

Brendt shifted without asking. Very good.

Ines threw a marker into the left route anyway, decoy-bright. Three bone-wire swarms converged on it instantly, giving the team a two-second clear lane to the right.

Useful.

They moved.

A tall Hollowkin stepped into their path.

Not a Husk. Too controlled. Its mask bore a single vertical crack, and a long hooked blade extended from one forearm. It didn't rush. It waited until Brendt's shield angled toward a roof-leaper, then struck at the gap between shield and thigh.

Roan intercepted with Sidewound.

Blade met blade.

The impact traveled up his arm and into his ribs. Stronger than expected. Hardened peak could contest for a heartbeat. Not longer.

The Hollowkin turned its mask toward him.

It spoke in Veyrnese.

The Codex translated late.

"Unfiled heir."

Roan cut toward its elbow seam.

It withdrew before contact.

Smart.

He hated that.

Drogan passed on Roan's left, blood still wet on his palm. The old man raised his short blade and spoke through clenched teeth.

"Hollow-borne external command line. Bound to hostile pack continuity. Severance authorized under unfinished breach oath."

The crowned figure by the fountain snapped its head toward him.

Drogan cut.

For three seconds, the army became individuals.

It wasn't dramatic from far away. No explosion, no wave without light. The formation lost the invisible grammar that made it one sentence. Husks lunged too early. Bone-wire constructs collided. Two masked Hollowkin turned toward different targets and blocked each other's path.

Roan exploited the disorder.

"Through," he said.

Brendt charged.

Not fast, but heavy. Shield forward, pack low, boots grinding ash. Ines stayed behind his right shoulder and cut anything that reached around the shield. Drogan staggered once after the severance, cost noted, but recovered. Roan moved rear-left, Sidewound opening seams only when they mattered.

First cut: a Husk's shoulder as it leapt from above. It spun into a second Husk instead of landing on Ines.

Second cut: the knee of a masked soldier. Not lethal. It collapsed in Brendt's path and became terrain.

Third cut: a bone-wire cluster trying to reach Drogan's bleeding palm. Sidewound disliked small targets but tolerated them when Roan used the flat to shear rather than pierce.

The three-second window ended.

The army remembered itself.

The crowned command node clicked once.

The plaza changed.

Every Husk stopped attacking Roan.

They went for Brendt.

Of course.

Not because Brendt was weakest. Because Brendt was structure. Remove the shield, and the group became four individuals in a city designed to eat individuals. The command node understood formation value.

Roan updated threat assessment from dangerous to professionally irritating.

"Brendt brace," he said.

"Noted," Brendt grunted.

The first two Husks hit the shield together. Brendt held. The third came low, crawling under the rim toward his ankle. Ines killed it with both knives, but a fourth hooked over the top and raked claws across Brendt's shoulder plate.

Metal shrieked.

Brendt stayed upright.

Roan cut the fourth's wrist seam and kicked it away.

A masked soldier used that moment to stab at Roan's ribs.

The Vance Pendant took part of the impact. Not all.

The blade slid under his coat and opened a shallow line across his side before he twisted away. Pain flared hot.

[Invert laceration?]

No.

Not worth quota.

He drove Sidewound into the soldier's forearm seam and used his left hand to shove the elbow the wrong way. The limb folded. The soldier didn't scream. It headbutted him.

Roan's vision flashed white.

He stepped back two paces, which prevented the follow-up blade from entering his throat.

Good. Pain had done its job.

Drogan killed the soldier with a cut through the mask crack.

"You're hit," the old man said.

"Shallow."

"You always say that."

"Frequently true."

The crowned command node raised one of its four arms.

The statue heads in the fountain turned outward.

Ines swore.

The heads opened their mouths.

Sound hit.

This wasn't the recognition net. This was command pressure sharpened into a weapon. The note vibrated through bone, shield, blade, tooth, pendant. Brendt's shield dipped. Ines stumbled. Drogan's severance wound reopened across his palm.

[Hostile empathy bleed detected. Black Rite stabilization active.]

First Refusal stopped the sound from becoming identity.

It didn't stop it from hurting.

The army advanced.

The right-side lane collapsed.

They were being pushed toward the dry canal.

Not their chosen route. The city's.

Roan looked past Brendt's shield, past the oncoming Husks, toward the canal mouth. Clean stones lined the descent. Too clean.

Trap.

"Not the canal," Roan said.

Drogan's eyes flicked that way. "Agreed."

"Alternative?" Ines snapped.

Roan looked up.

Broken colonnade. Leaning tower. Half-collapsed bridge above the plaza, not on the original route, too narrow for the larger Hollowkin. High risk of fall. Lower risk of prebuilt trap. Brendt could cross if shield folded. Drogan could. Ines easily. Roan yes.

"Up," he said.

Brendt looked at the bridge. "I hate up."

"Hate later."

They moved toward the nearest column.

The bridge wasn't their only upward path.

It was merely the least bad.

Roan saw two alternatives as they fought toward the column: a cracked exterior stair on the left side of the plaza and a hanging maintenance ladder behind the fountain. The stair had clean ash gathered on every third step, too regular to be natural. The ladder swayed despite no wind and had three bone-wire constructs pretending to be broken rungs. The bridge, by contrast, was honestly damaged. It might collapse because stone obeyed gravity, not because the city had written a joke into it.

Honest collapse beat malicious stability.

A Husk came low at his right knee. Roan let it come, stepped inside the arc, and used Sidewound not to cut the creature but to cut the seam in the cracked paving under its weight. The stone dipped. The Husk's lunge became a stumble. Brendt's shield finished the conversion from threat to obstacle.

"I like that one," Brendt said through clenched teeth.

"Don't get attached."

"To the technique or the living?"

"Yes."

Ines threw another marker, not bright this time but heat-active. It landed near the malicious ladder and pulsed once. Three hidden constructs revealed themselves by turning toward it. Drogan killed the nearest with a downward stab and kicked the twitching body into the others.

The crowned command node raised two arms.

The relays shifted again.

Roan felt the formation preparing not to stop their climb but to punish the moment after it. That was thing competent commanders did. Don't prevent every enemy plan. Let them pay full price for reaching the wrong success.

He almost respected it.

Almost.

"It wants us on the bridge," he said.

Brendt made a strangled sound. "You just said up."

"I said up. I didn't say safe."

"I am beginning to hate distinctions."

Drogan's blade took a masked Hollowkin across the throat seam. "Can we use that?"

"Yes."

"How?"

Roan cut his palm again and felt Crimson Wake answer beneath the Black Rite line. This time he inverted only the leakage, not the wound. Black Rite held the mind. Crimson Wake took the blood.

"By making it commit to being right."

The army moved to stop them.

Drogan lifted his blade for another severance.

The crowned command node clicked twice.

Two masked soldiers threw themselves into the command line, not physically but structurally. Roan felt the change: sacrificial relays. If Drogan cut now, the severance would spend itself on them and the pack would remain coherent.

"Don't," Roan said.

Drogan's blade stopped.

"Relays," Roan said.

Drogan saw them and cursed.

The command node had adapted after one use.

Good enemy.

Bad timing.

Roan considered Crimson Wake.

The pack expected it. But expectation could be used. If he shaped the blood wave toward the relays instead of the mass, the command node might reinforce incorrectly. Cost: blood loss, attention, likely triggering whatever counter-pattern they had prepared. Benefit: open vertical route.

He let blood run over Sidewound's grip.

Brendt saw it. "That usually means my day gets worse."

"Briefly."

"Roan."

"Jump when I say."

The crowned node lowered its arm.

The Husks surged.

Roan cut low.

The wave that left Sidewound wasn't the broad red crescent he had used on the first-night pack. It was thinner and darker, plus meaner. A line rather than a tide. It passed under the front Husks, ignored them, and struck the two sacrificial relays behind the formation.

They split at the knees.

Drogan cut immediately.

"Hollow-borne external command line. Bound to hostile pack continuity. Severance authorized under unfinished breach oath."

His blade fell.

The army lost grammar again.

"Jump," Roan said.

Brendt jumped badly but high enough.

Roan boosted him with one hand under the pack, spending more Vitalis than he liked. Ines went up the column like she had been waiting for architecture to become relevant. Drogan followed, slower but sure. Roan came last, cutting a Husk's reaching arm and using its collapsing body as a step.

They reached the broken bridge.

For three breaths, they had height.

Then the crowned command node looked up and spoke one word.

The Codex translated it as:

Descend.

The bridge cracked beneath them.
