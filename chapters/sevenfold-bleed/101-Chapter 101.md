Chapter 101: Hardened Threshold

Drogan caught him in the corridor before dawn.

Roan saw the Iron Hand first, glinting under the gaslights, the rest of the warden emerging from the shadows like a wall of worn stone. The training hall was still locked. The hallways were empty. Drogan had come looking for him specifically.

“Walk with me.”

Not a request.

Roan fell into step beside him. They moved down past the armory checkpoint, through a utility door into a stairwell that smelled of rust and old coolant. Drogan didn’t speak until the door sealed behind them.

“Iris Vell’s grandfather has made inquiries,” Drogan said. “Three separate channels in the last twelve hours. He’s asking about a male student matching your description.”

Roan kept walking. “Does he have a name?”

“Not yet. But he’s a retired military intelligence officer with enough rank to access Spire personnel records if he pushes the right buttons.” Drogan’s voice carried no inflection. “The Vell family is small, but they have reach where it matters.”

They reached the bottom of the stairwell. A maintenance corridor stretched ahead, dim and empty.

“Iris withdrew cleanly,” Roan said. “No evidence. No trace.”

“She doesn’t need evidence to talk to her grandfather. She needs a suspicion.” Drogan turned to face him. “You let her walk. That was the right call. But now you’ve got a retired hound sniffing at the edge of your trail, and you’re still at Tempered peak.”

Roan felt the weight of the statement settle across his shoulders. Not a reprimand. A fact.

“How long before he finds something?”

“If you stay where you are?” Drogan’s gaze held. “He won’t need to find anything. He’ll just watch long enough to see something you don’t want seen.”

The words landed colder than any threat.

Roan nodded once and turned back toward the stairwell. He didn’t go to breakfast. He didn’t go to the library. He went to his dorm room, locked the door, and sat cross-legged on the floor with his back against the wall.

The Codex Shard hummed against his spine, warm and familiar.

He closed his eyes and pressed inward.

The internal landscape formed around him: a gray plain under a vault of dark sky. The wound-map of his cultivation, every channel, every bottleneck, every shallow scar from the failed medicines he’d inverted. He’d mapped it a hundred times, but tonight he looked for something specific.

The threshold.

It sat at the base of his core, a seam of denser tissue where Tempered ended and Hardened began. He’d felt it for weeks now, a kind of pressure behind his sternum, a sense that the next step required more than accumulation. It required compression. The Inversion had given him speed, but the body still had to hold what the system poured through it.

He reached for the last dose of failed refining agent, a sample he’d never inverted—a batch of Marrowforge catalyst that had gone unstable. The side effects were tissue brittleness and micro-fracture across the skeletal frame.

Perfect.

[Failed Marrowforge catalyst absorbed.]

[Sidewound detected: skeletal fragility cascade.]

[Inversion queue ready.]

Roan let the Inversion roll through him. Heat spread from the Codex Shard, a current that threaded into his ribs, his spine, the long bones of his arms and legs. The brittleness inverted into density. The micro-fracture lines filled with new crystallization, a scaffold of compressed strength that pushed against the threshold.

The pressure behind his sternum intensified. He held it.

[Conversion expanding.]

[Threshold contact established.]

[Hardened alignment: in progress.]

The gray plain in his internal landscape cracked. A new layer opened beneath it, darker, denser, a stratum of compacted vitality that had been there all along, waiting for enough weight to press through. Roan breathed through the sensation, let the Inversion finish its work without forcing the pace.

Minutes passed. Or hours. He wasn’t tracking.

When the heat subsided, the pressure behind his sternum was gone. In its place was a new kind of stillness, a body that felt heavier in the bones and lighter in the breath.

[Conversion complete.]

[Hardened threshold crossed.]

[Vitalis baseline: 2,100 (resting) / 4,800 (combat peak).]

[You’ve reached the first real floor, son. Now build the walls. — A.V.]

Roan opened his eyes. The room looked the same, but his hands felt different. Steadier. The bones in his forearm caught the light differently when he turned his wrist.

He stood, tested the weight distribution, and found his center of gravity had settled half a finger lower. Every movement felt like it started from a deeper anchor.

Hardened.

Not the end of anything. A floor, his father had called it. A foundation.

But it was enough. Enough to make a retired hound think twice before pushing too hard. Enough to give him breathing room while he figured out what Iris Vell’s grandfather actually wanted.

He reached for his coat and opened the door.

Dawn was breaking through the hall windows. Students were starting to stir. The world hadn’t changed, but Roan had.

And that was the only kind of leverage that couldn’t be taken away.
