Chapter 175: The First Knife

The assassination attempt happened because Brendt wanted noodles.

That wasn't the cause in any meaningful strategic sense, but Roan found himself returning to the detail afterward with unreasonable irritation. Noodles were too small a hinge for that much blood. People liked grand causes for violence: family hatred, ancient wars, revenge, political necessity, secret inheritance. Those existed here. They had built the room, stocked the weapons, hired the hand, selected the route.

But the reason Roan and Brendt left campus through the south market gate at 8:12 p.m. instead of eating in the refectory was that Brendt had spent the entire day being medically compliant, academically functional, and only moderately annoying, and had decided this entitled him to broth from a street stall run by a woman who hated students but respected cash.

Roan had agreed because the route offered three useful side benefits: off-campus movement to test tails, access to a dead-drop box near the tramline, and noodles that were, unfortunately, good.

That was how traps worked when designed by competent people. They didn't lure with implausible rewards. They attached themselves to things the target already wanted.

The south market district pressed against Black Spire's lower wall in a crowded belt of food stalls, supply shops, student bars, pawn counters, and temporary vendor tents that appeared before exam periods like fungus after rain. Steam curled under hanging lamps. Vitalis heaters hissed. Students moved in clusters, loud with relief or panic depending on exam performance. Merchants shouted prices. A busker played a cracked violin near the gate and earned more sympathy than coin.

Brendt held a paper bowl in his good hand and looked almost content.

Almost, because contentment near Roan had developed trust issues.

"You're scanning rooftops again," Brendt said.

"Yes."

"If an assassin kills me while I'm eating, I'm haunting your notebook."

"Eat faster."

"That isn't the lesson."

"It's one lesson."

Roan stood with his back to a wall between a shuttered charm repair shop and the noodle stall's side awning. Sidewound rested under his jacket. Standard knife left hip. Vance Pendant cold but not alarmed. Shard quiet. No visible tail from campus. Two possible watchers near the tramline. One drunk House student pretending not to stare at Selene's last message on Roan's wrist slate, which was either coincidence or poor operational discipline.

Selene had sent three lines after leaving the archive.

*Father received notice. He hasn't confronted me. That's worse.*

*Greel vessel terminology appears in one older Ardent file. I need time.*

*Don't engage Kael privately.*

Good advice.

Roan had no intention of engaging Kael privately.

Publicly was becoming more attractive.

Brendt slurped noodles and made a noise of appreciation. "Worth the risk."

"No."

"You haven't tried them."

"I assessed the risk."

"Your loss."

Roan accepted the second bowl the vendor shoved at him with the resentment of someone forced to participate in commerce. He paid exact cash and placed the bowl on the narrow counter beside him.

He didn't eat immediately.

Brendt noticed. "Poison?"

"not."

"Comforting range."

Roan lifted the bowl, smelled broth, spice, cheap oil, scallion, meat of uncertain origin. No obvious toxin. He touched one thread of Vitalis to the surface. Nothing reacted.

He ate.

Brendt looked vindicated.

The noodles were good.

Annoying.

The first sign wasn't movement.

It was absence.

The violin stopped.

Not at the end of a phrase. Mid-note. The broken sound scraped once and cut away. Market noise continued for half a second longer, then thinned as people turned toward the busker.

Roan's bowl dropped before the first scream.

His left hand shoved Brendt backward. His right went for Sidewound.

The busker's violin case had opened.

Inside wasn't a rifle. Too obvious. Not a bomb. Too indiscriminate under Spire-adjacent wards.

A folded throwing rig snapped upright, five narrow blades launching in a fan of dull silver.

Roan counted angles.

Three for him. One for Brendt. One wide, crowd control or second target.

He moved right, because the obvious dodge left would place Brendt in line with two blades. Sidewound cleared leather. The first blade struck the awning post where his throat had been. The second met Sidewound and split along its own stress seam, halves spinning into the wall.

The third was wrong.

Too slow.

Bait.

Roan twisted instead of cutting.

A man stepped out of the steam behind the noodle stall with a knife already inside striking range.

Not student. Not market thug. Professional. Plain coat. Calm eyes. Forged baseline, suppressed. Right-hand knife aimed low under Roan's ribs, where the Vance Pendant didn't protect fully. Left hand carrying a charm spike designed to disrupt healing circulation.

Good plan.

Roan liked it less because he was inside it.

He caught the knife wrist with his left forearm and let the edge cut through coat and skin rather than give the attacker his abdomen. Pain opened hot. A heartbeat. No inversion. Not yet.

Sidewound came up toward the attacker's throat.

The man smiled.

Second bait.

Roan saw the real strike one heartbeat too late.

Not from the man.

From the vendor.

The noodle stall woman, too resentful, too ordinary, too well placed, moved with a short black blade hidden under the ladle cloth. She didn't aim for Roan's body.

She aimed for the back of his neck.

The Codex Shard flared cold against his chest, issuing a hazard pulse.

Roan began to turn.

Brendt hit him from the side.

Not a tackle. A shove. Shoulder-first, using the injured side because that was where he stood, because there was no time to choose the good arm, because Brendt Holloway had a talent for being inconvenient in ways that saved lives.

The black blade missed Roan's spine.

It entered Brendt under the left ribs.

For a moment, the market didn't exist.

Not because Roan lost awareness. He didn't. Awareness narrowed, became violently clean.

Blade depth: severe.

Angle: upward, toward lung and heart line.

Brendt's mouth opened, but no sound came out.

Vendor-assassin tried to withdraw the blade.

Roan caught her wrist.

He didn't think about whether she was old, whether she had served noodles, whether she had a family, whether killing her in a market would complicate legal standing.

He broke her wrist in three places and drove Sidewound through the seam under her jaw.

Clean.

Fast.

No cruelty.

Not enough.

[Sidewound contact: fatal seam opened.]

[Yield: 412 Vitalis.]

The male assassin moved to disengage.

Roan threw the dying vendor into his path and stepped through the falling body.

The world became positions.

Male assassin: knife high, charm spike ready, weight shifting back.

Brendt: collapsing, blade still lodged, blood spreading too fast.

Crowd: screaming outward, creating space and witnesses.

Rooftop: second shooter? Unknown.

Market wards: waking, eight seconds to suppression.

Roan: left forearm cut, right hand functional, Sidewound active, Crimson Wake possible, public exposure high, Brendt dying.

Priority: stop immediate threat.

The assassin threw the charm spike.

Not at Roan.

At Brendt.

Roan intercepted it with his left hand.

The spike punched through his palm and unfolded hooks.

Pain white. A heartbeat.

[Invert foreign charm trauma?]

No.

The hooks locked around metacarpal bone.

Roan closed his impaled hand around the spike to keep it from activating fully and moved.

The assassin's eyes changed.

Good. Professionals disliked clients who failed to specify abnormal targets.

Sidewound cut once at the man's lead knee. Not deep. Enough to open structure. The assassin corrected, retreating diagonally.

Roan followed with Crimson Wake.

He didn't open the full technique. Too many civilians. Too much public risk. Instead he used a narrow vector through the blood pouring down his left palm, reversing pressure into a short burst along his right side.

The world jumped.

He appeared inside the assassin's guard.

Three steps. Two cuts. One pause to confirm.

Knee seam. Wrist seam. Throat.

The man died with surprise still negotiating the terms.

[Yield: 689 Vitalis.]

[Exposure risk: elevated.]

[Foreign charm trauma unresolved.]

Roan ignored it and dropped beside Brendt.

The market noise returned in pieces. Screams. Running feet. Someone vomiting. The noodle vendor's heater hissing. A child crying behind an overturned table. Spire alarm bells starting above the gate.

Brendt lay on his back, eyes unfocused, one hand pressed weakly against the knife in his ribs as if politeness might keep the blood inside.

"Don't pull it," Roan said.

Brendt blinked. "Wasn't planning to."

His voice was wet.

Bad.

Roan cut Brendt's coat open. Blood soaked the shirt beneath. Dark arterial pulses mixed with froth. Lung involvement confirmed. Heart line uncertain. The blade's material shimmered faintly black along the exposed hilt.

Not ordinary steel.

The Vance Pendant had protected Roan from a similar strike before.

Brendt had no pendant.

Roan pressed both hands around the wound, ignoring the charm spike still through his palm. "Stay awake."

"Trying."

"You're bad at it."

"That's motivating."

"Good."

Brendt's eyes shifted. "Did I get noodles on your coat?"

Roan's hands tightened.

Not on purpose.

"Focus."

"Sorry."

"Don't apologize. Breathe shallow."

"Hurts."

"That means you're alive."

"Your bedside manner..." Brendt coughed blood. "Still crime."

Roan looked at the blade.

Black edge. Barbed channel. Vitalis disruption. If removed improperly, fatal bleed. If left, poison spread. Medical wing eight minutes if security cleared path. Brendt had perhaps three.

No full inversion quota prepared. Daily load still recovering from prior experiments. He could force one, but the blade carried a foreign charm designed to disrupt healing. Inverting wrong might amplify poison, seal damage around the blade, or bond the weapon to the wound.

Bad options.

He had used worse.

Drogan arrived like a thrown wall.

One moment absent. The next, kneeling across from Roan with his blade drawn and eyes taking in the scene faster than speech.

"Status."

"Left thoracic penetration. Poisoned barbed blade. Lung hit. Heart uncertain. Three minutes or less."

Drogan's face didn't change. "Can you invert?"

"Maybe."

"That means no."

"It means maybe."

Security bells grew louder.

Selene appeared behind Drogan, pale but controlled, already shouting at students to clear a corridor. Ines came from the alley side with a knife in each hand and blood on one sleeve that was not hers.

Roan looked at Brendt.

Brendt looked back, barely.

There were moments when calculation failed not because there were no numbers, but because every number pointed to a conclusion Roan refused to accept.

He had chosen Brendt in the Rift with one quota left.

He would choose him again.

The difference was that this time, the arithmetic didn't feel cold.

It felt like something in his chest had opened and found teeth.

The Codex Shard pulsed.

[Forced inversion possible.]

[Risk: hostile charm / exposure spike.]

[Proceed?]

Roan's voice came out flat.

"Yes."

Drogan grabbed his wrist. "Roan."

Roan looked at him.

Drogan let go.

The world narrowed to blood, black steel, and Brendt trying to breathe.

Roan placed his impaled left hand around the blade hilt and his right over Brendt's wound.

Pain answered from both sides. A heartbeat.

The Inversion engaged. The Codex Shard pulsed flat affirmation—no qualifiers, no ledger.

[Forced external inversion engaged.]

[Target stabilization established. External sidewound channel closed.]

The black blade's poison tried to crawl up the inversion channel, seeking a host. Roan let it. The charm flooded his system, and for a moment language centers misfired against the foreign code. The word for rain was simply absent. Then his own circulation burned the trace out, and the fog cleared. The rage that followed was clean, precise, and entirely his own.

The blade came free in Drogan's hand.

Blood followed, then slowed.

Brendt's eyes closed.

Roan checked pulse.

Present.

Weak.

Coma likely.

Alive.

The market blurred at the edges, not from weakness. Roan had stopped that reflex years ago. His body had decided vision was less important than murder.

Drogan said, "Medical wing. Now."

Roan stood.

The charm spike remained through his left hand.

Ines reached toward it. "Let me—"

"No."

His voice made her stop.

Selene stared at him.

Security arrived in black uniforms, shouting orders.

Roan looked down at the two dead assassins and the vendor's body.

Not enough.

The people who had sent them were still breathing.

That was an administrative problem.

He intended to correct it.

The healers tried to move Brendt on a floating stretcher. Roan walked beside it until one of them told him to step back. He looked at the woman, catalogued her badge, rank, tremor pattern, and the fact that she was pressing one hand against the stretcher's stabilizer because it was drifting under the weight of Brendt's damaged Vitalis field.

Then he stepped back.

Not because she had authority. Because she was doing useful work and his proximity was making it harder.

Useful work received more patience than authority.

Security tried to question him in the market. That went poorly. Captain Marren asked for a statement while Brendt's blood was still on Roan's sleeves. Roan gave her three sentences: number of attackers, weapon types, direction of likely third party body recovery. When she asked whether he recognized the assailants, he said no. When she asked whether he had reason to suspect House involvement, he looked at the stretcher disappearing through the south gate and said, "Ask me when your evidence room is locked."

Marren didn't like that.

Marren wasn't the current problem.

Selene took witness names. Not softly. She stood in the middle of the market with her Ardent posture wrapped around her like armor and asked questions in a tone that made frightened students answer before remembering they were rich enough to refuse. Ines disappeared for four minutes and returned with the busker's broken launch rig wrapped in oilcloth, two charm fragments, and a street camera node that technically belonged to municipal security.

"Borrowed," she said.

Roan didn't ask.

Drogan carried the black blade hilt in a sealed case and looked at the crowd as if deciding which witnesses had become liabilities. No one approached him. Good. Black Spire students possessed at least one shared survival instinct.

At the gate, a first-year stood frozen beside an overturned table, hands shaking, eyes fixed on the blood trail. Fourteen? Fifteen? Hard to tell under fear. Roan stopped long enough to kick a dropped coin pouch toward him.

"Go inside," he said.

The boy stared.

Roan pointed at the gate. "Now."

The boy ran.

Selene saw. She said nothing.

Good.

Roan didn't need commentary on the few lines he refused to cross. Children left the board when possible. That wasn't kindness. It was structure. Without structure, rage became weather. Weather killed randomly. Roan had no interest in random.

By the time they reached the medical wing, the hole in his left hand had stopped bleeding around the embedded spike. Not healed. Clamped. The charm hooks still bit bone. A healer saw it and made a sound professionals made when manners failed.

"Mr. Vance, that has to come out."

"After Brendt."

"If it activates—"

"It won't."

"You can't know that."

Roan looked at the spike. Cold fire crawled along its runes, searching for a healing channel to poison. He tightened his hand and crushed one of the smaller hooks against bone. Pain climbed his arm and cleared the last fog from his head.

"Now I can."

The healer went pale. Drogan muttered something that sounded like a prayer and an insult sharing rent.

They took Brendt through double doors.

Roan remained outside with blood drying on his coat and the market still arranged behind his eyes in angles.

Three attackers. One body missing. One charm spike designed for a secondary victim. One black blade meant for the back of his neck. One friend unconscious because he had moved faster than calculation.

The numbers formed a neat column.

The column didn't help. Columns were for later, when blood had dried and men pretended reason had arrived first.

\- break -

Brendt didn't wake in the medical wing.

The healers stabilized him after not long after, two emergency graft spells, one blood replacement cycle, and a surgical extraction of black charm residue from tissue that kept trying to reject ordinary repair. They didn't let Roan inside the operating room after the first healer saw the charm spike through his hand and realized he hadn't removed it.

Three staff members attempted to treat him.

Roan declined.

The fourth threatened sedation.

Drogan quietly removed her from the room before Roan had to decide whether hospital property counted as an enemy asset.

Eventually Cole arrived.

The Headmaster wore no ceremonial coat, only a dark shirt and the expression of a man whose school had become legally inconvenient again. He looked at Roan's hand. Then at the blood on his clothes. Then at Drogan.

"Student Holloway?" Cole asked.

"Alive," Drogan said. "Unconscious."

Cole's jaw tightened. "Assailants?"

"Dead," Roan said.

"You killed both?"

"Three."

Cole closed his eyes briefly. "Of course there were three."

"One posed as a vendor."

Security Captain Marren, standing near the door, looked up from her slate. "We recovered two bodies."

Roan turned his head slowly.

Marren swallowed.

Drogan's hand went still near his blade.

Cole said, very carefully, "Explain."

"Busker rig. Male knife specialist. Vendor with black blade."

Marren checked her slate again, face draining. "Vendor body wasn't listed among recovered deceased. The stall burned during containment." She hesitated. "There was a twelve-second window before our teams secured the perimeter. The market wards showed a Vitalis waveform suppression starting five minutes before the attack."

"The window was prepared," Cole said.

Roan looked at the window.

His reflection looked calm.

That was inaccurate.

"Someone removed her," he said. "And someone opened the door."

All three options made the room colder.

Cole dismissed Marren with a glance. "Full lockdown on south gate evidence. No public statement beyond attempted off-campus assault. No mention of student Holloway's condition."

Marren left quickly.

Roan waited until the door closed. "Greel."

Cole didn't deny it. "Likely."

"Likely is for reports."

"Reports are what keep schools open."

"Brendt is in a coma."

The room quieted.

Cole looked at him then, really looked.

Roan had heard anger described as heat. That was childish. Heat wavered. Heat distorted. This was colder than the Vance Pendant, colder than the Hollowed City, colder than Kael Greel's polite smile across a classroom.

It didn't want to burn.

It wanted to preserve the moment forever so the correct people could be made to understand it slowly.

Drogan said, "Roan."

"No."

"You don't know what I'm saying."

"Yes, I do."

Cole's voice sharpened. "You can't kill Kael Greel on suspicion."

Roan looked at him. "Watch me not."

Cole frowned.

Drogan understood first. "You're not going for Kael tonight."

"No."

"Then who?"

Roan pulled the charm spike from his left hand.

Blood ran down his wrist and hit the floor.

He placed the spike on Cole's desk tray.

"The supply chain," he said.

Cole's eyes narrowed. "You won't conduct a private purge through Ironholm."

"No."

That made everyone look at him again.

Roan held up his injured hand. Blood ran in a steady line to his cuff. "A purge kills people. This was procurement. Procurement has invoices."

Ines, who had arrived at the doorway with the stolen camera node tucked under one arm, smiled like someone hearing a favorite song begin. "Invoices have names."

"Names have accounts," Selene said quietly.

"Accounts have owners," Roan said.

Drogan looked at the three of them and seemed to age in real time. "I hate all of this."

Cole rubbed the bridge of his nose. "So do I. Continue."

Roan stepped toward the glass window of the operating theater. Through the frosted charm layer, he could see only blurred movement: healers in white, blue pulses of stabilization light, the dark shape of Brendt on the table. The sight should've been useless. It provided no data beyond activity. Still, he watched for six seconds.

Then he turned away.

"Lock the evidence," he said. "Seal the market recordings. Pull freight permits for cryo-stabilized medical imports, black charm precursors, and barbed surgical alloys entering through Greel or Vell intermediaries in the last thirty days. Freeze deletion rights on student visitor logs and south gate security channels."

Cole stared at him.

"You are issuing instructions to the Headmaster."

"Yes."

"Bold."

"Timed."

Cole's mouth flattened. After a moment, he looked at Marren's empty place by the door. "I will secure official channels. You won't leave campus without informing Drogan."

Roan considered lying. Too many witnesses.

"Fine."

Drogan snorted. "That means nothing."

"It means I will inform you creatively."

Cole pointed at the spike in Roan's hand. "And you will let a healer remove that before your hand becomes decorative."

Roan looked at the operating room again.

The blue light around Brendt flickered once, then steadied. The tissue monitor beside him showed a regeneration gradient climbing above standard curve.

"After the first data pull," he said.

No one liked that answer.

No one had a better one.
