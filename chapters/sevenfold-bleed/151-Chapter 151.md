Chapter 151: Forged Pressure

The bridge didn't collapse all at once.

That would've been too generous.

It fractured in decisions.

First, the central support split with a sound like ice breaking under a boot. Then the left rail dropped away into gray mist. Then the stones beneath Brendt's rear foot tilted downward, forcing him to choose between keeping the shield raised and not falling.

He chose not falling.

Correct.

A shield did little for a man already airborne over a plaza full of teeth.

Roan caught the back of Brendt's harness and drove Vitalis through his right arm. Tendons protested. Shoulder joint warned of future litigation. He ignored both and pulled Brendt forward onto the remaining spine of the bridge.

The action cost half a second.

A Husk used it.

It launched from the wall below, climbing not by claws but by hooking its elbows through cracks too small for its body. Its mouth opened vertically. Its target wasn't Roan's throat.

It went for his abdomen.

Roan twisted, but the bridge shifted underfoot.

The Husk's claw punched into his side just below the ribs.

The Vance Pendant spread part of the impact. Not enough. Pain opened deep under the ribs. The claw scraped along something inside that shouldn't have been touched.

Roan's body assessed the damage for him. Internal. Organ level. The Forged gap Drogan had warned about. The Husk had found it.

That could be fatal.

It could also be leverage.

Roan drove Sidewound down into the creature's shoulder seam. Bad angle. Too shallow. The blade caught, opened a partial line, and stuck for a fraction of a second.

The Husk pulled.

Roan went with it.

Better than resisting. Resistance would tear the wound wider and cost the knife. He stepped into the pull, brought his knee up, and struck the creature's throat hinge. The hinge flexed. Sidewound freed. Drogan's blade came from the left and split the skull.

The Husk fell.

So did three more stones.

Ines had already crossed to the far end of the bridge, where the broken span met the second floor of a leaning archive building. She drove two anchor spikes into the stone and threw a rope back.

"Move," she said.

Brendt clipped first and crossed hand over hand, shield folded against his back. Drogan followed, one eye on the command node below. Roan waited rear because the rear was where problems accumulated.

The crowned Hollowkin raised all four arms.

The plaza below reorganized.

No. Not reorganized.

Reassigned.

Husks began climbing the columns, not toward the bridge but toward the building where Ines had anchored the line. Bone-wire constructs streamed up the walls in pale threads, aiming for the anchor spikes. Masked soldiers formed rings around the base of the columns, ready to catch anyone falling or force a descent path toward the clean canal.

The command node was turning the vertical route into another trap.

Good.

Predictable. Still useful.

Roan clipped onto the rope.

A bone-wire construct reached the first anchor spike.

Ines crushed it under her boot.

A second reached the rope itself and began chewing through the outer fiber.

Roan crossed faster.

Halfway across, the bridge behind him collapsed.

The rope snapped taut.

His wounded side tore wider.

He reached the far ledge and rolled into the archive building as the rope gave way behind him. Ines cut the remaining strand before constructs could use it as a path.

Brendt dragged Roan upright.

"You're bleeding," Brendt said.

"Observed."

"Badly."

"Less useful observation."

Drogan looked at the wound and his face hardened. "You invert that now."

"No."

"Roan."

"Not as healing."

The old man's eyes narrowed.

Roan pressed one hand against the wound and looked around.

The archive building's second floor had once held rows of shelves. Not books. Stone tablets, metal strips, bone-white cylinders, most shattered or missing. The floor slanted toward the outer wall. Two staircases: one collapsed, one descending into darkness. Windows without glass opened toward the plaza. The air was thick with ash and old paper that had never been paper.

Temporary shelter. Poor defensibility. Good line of sight.

Below, the Hollowkin army flowed toward the building.

They had less than a minute.

Roan's wound pulsed.

Pain spread inward, hot around the organs, cold along the spine. Hardened advancement had strengthened bone, muscle, tendon. It hadn't fully remade the organs. That was the gap between Hardened and Forged. Forged didn't mean more Vitalis. It meant the body could circulate power through the organs without tearing them, could survive pressure changes, poison, internal shock, and sustained field contamination.

Tier Three environments exploited the gap.

Drogan had said that.

The Husk had just found it.

Roan looked at the blood on his hand.

Not ideal.

Useful.

"You are about to do something stupid," Brendt said.

Roan glanced at him. "Define stupid."

"Something that makes Drogan look like that."

Drogan did, in fact, look displeased.

"It's the right wound," Roan said.

Ines's eyes sharpened. "For the Black Rite?"

"For Forged."

The silence after that was very short because the first Husk hit the outer wall below them.

The building shook.

Drogan grabbed Roan by the front of his coat. "You don't break through mid-siege unless the alternative is death."

Roan looked past him to the window.

Husks climbed the wall in pale clusters. Bone-wire constructs spread across the stone like frost. The command node remained in the plaza, watching.

"Yes," Roan said.

Drogan's grip tightened.

"The alternative," Roan clarified, "is death."

Brendt said, "I hate when he's right."

Drogan released him with a curse. "What do you need?"

"Twenty seconds without interruption."

"Impossible."

"Then twelve."

"Maybe."

"Good."

Roan sat against a tilted shelf, back to stone, wound open under his palm. He didn't close his eyes. Closing eyes in a siege was theatrical nonsense. He needed the room.

The Black Rite line inside him steadied.

First Refusal: don't let the wound decide the self.

Sevenfold Bleed: seven channels forced open under pain.

Marrowforge Sutra: bones as anchors, not cages.

Pyrebrand Full Cycle: blood heat without blood waste.

Crimson Wake: output through sacrifice.

Sidewound: seams are events.

The Forged threshold wasn't a number. The number would come later because people liked to measure things after surviving them. The threshold itself was structural. Hardened made the body a weapon. Forged made the body a furnace that didn't cook its owner.

Roan had been avoiding the threshold because his organs remained the weakest link and because forcing it early risked permanent damage.

The risk had just become a cost.

Costs could be paid.

[Breakthrough vector detected: Forged initiation possible.]

Roan didn't answer immediately.

He watched Brendt plant himself at the window, shield unfolded across the opening. A Husk hit the shield from outside and nearly drove him back, but Ines hooked a knife through its wrist and levered it off. Drogan stood near the stairwell, blade low, waiting for the first masked soldier to come up.

Twelve seconds was generous if everyone was competent.

They were.

Roan accepted.

The wound inverted.

Not into healing.

Healing would close the line and waste the catalyst. Instead the Black Rite named the injury: organ vulnerability under hostile field pressure. The Codex Shard inverted the damage vector. Sevenfold channels opened around the wound and drove pain inward in controlled arcs.

Roan's liver burned.

His lungs locked.

His heart stuttered once, then slammed hard enough to shake his vision.

The world narrowed to red-black lines and the sound of Brendt shouting something that didn't matter because the body had become a room full of alarms.

The Black Rite drew a line.

*This is wound.*

*This is vessel.*

*This isn't self.*

The Hollow residue pushed.

Roan refused.

The first window shield cracked.

Brendt grunted.

"Left brace," Roan said.

His voice sounded distant.

Brendt moved left without thinking. A Husk claw missed the gap where his thigh had been.

Ines glanced back at Roan. "Still conscious?"

"Unfortunately."

"Then warn before exploding."

"No promises."

His lungs unlocked.

Air entered like broken glass.

Vitalis surged. The command node already knew. That cost was obsolete. Roan let the surge happen. Vitalis moved through his organs for the first time without reinforcing tissue from the outside. It threaded through membranes, wrapped vessels, hardened the soft places without making them brittle. The sensation was intimate and unpleasant, like being rebuilt by a surgeon who disliked anesthesia and had excellent reasons.

The building shook again.

A section of ceiling collapsed near Ines. She rolled under it, came up with one shoulder bleeding, and threw a knife into the eye slit of a climbing Husk.

Drogan killed another masked soldier at the stairwell but took a cut across the thigh.

Brendt's shield frame bent.

Twelve seconds had passed.

They were buying more on credit.

Roan hated credit unless he wrote the terms.

The room flickered.

For a moment, Roan wasn't in the archive building.

He stood under a sky on fire beside someone with Aldric's hands and not Aldric's face. A child sobbed behind a door. A woman said, in Veyrnese, "Don't answer when the sky speaks."

The memory folded.

It wasn't his. It had no right to occupy the main ledger.

First Refusal cut it away.

*No.*

Roan's heart steadied.

Pain didn't vanish. It reorganized around a new center.

His organs, previously protected passengers inside a Hardened frame, became participants. Vitalis circulated through them with a low, dense rhythm. Not faster. Deeper.

[Breakthrough achieved.]

[Rank: Forged — initial.]

The Codex Shard offered no celebratory flourish. No trumpets. No golden frame around the new rank. Just the cold line and silence.

Roan appreciated that. Celebration in the middle of combat was how people got stabbed while admiring themselves.

He didn't feel chosen.

He felt maintained under worse operating conditions. Maintenance was better. Chosen people waited for destiny. Maintained people checked their bleeding and moved.

Also, maintained people didn't pause to admire metaphors while a roof full of Husks tried to solve them permanently.

He marked the breakthrough as an active asset, marked the pain as unresolved cost, and stood because sitting was the slowest available decision.

A Husk burst through the window over Brendt's shield.

Roan stood.

The movement felt wrong for one step, then very right. His body had changed its weight distribution by fractions. Organs reinforced meant balance under impact improved. The wound in his side remained open, but it no longer threatened collapse.

The Husk came at his face.

Forged didn't make him immune to fear, either.

That was worth noting because stories lied about thresholds. They made rank advancement sound like a door closing behind weakness. In practice, it was closer to moving into a larger room where the windows were harder to break and the rent was higher.

The improvement wasn't safety.

It was margin.

One more breath before collapse. One more step after impact. One more category of pain that could be used rather than merely endured.

Margin wasn't glamorous.

Margin was how people survived long enough to look glamorous later.

Roan didn't use Crimson Wake.

He stepped half a pace inside the lunge and cut with Sidewound.

The blade entered the seam under the sternum and found the core line. Roan felt the moment the creature's structure committed to the attack. He cut there.

The Husk folded in half and died at his feet.

Brendt stared. "That was new."

"Yes."

Drogan looked at Roan once, fast and assessing. "Stable?"

"Enough."

"Not an answer."

"The only available one."

The command node in the plaza clicked.

This time, the sound carried something like irritation.

Good.

Roan stepped to the broken window and looked down at the army.

Before the breakthrough, he had seen bodies, lines, threats. Now he saw pressure gradients. The Hollowkin formation circulated command like Vitalis through a body: node to relay, relay to pack, pack to strike. Drogan could sever the line briefly. Roan could now see where the severance would hurt most.

That wasn't enough to win.

It might be enough to leave.

"New plan," he said.

Brendt groaned. "I missed the old terrible one."

Roan pointed to the far side of the archive building, where a collapsed walkway sloped toward a neighboring roof and away from the canal trap.

"Across roofs. Drogan cuts command at the second relay, not the node. Brendt shields rear. Ines marks false descent points. I break the pursuit line."

Drogan nodded. "You can see relays?"

"Now, yes."

Ines's eyes glittered despite the blood on her shoulder. "Forged senses."

"Initial," Drogan said sharply. "Not stable enough for heroics."

Roan stepped over the dead Husk.

"Then we avoid heroics," he said, and cut the nearest command relay through the window before the army adjusted.

The plaza below lost rhythm for one breath.

They ran.

- break -

Roof movement inside the Hollowed City was worse than street movement in every way except the one that mattered.

Forged senses didn't sharpen the world. They sharpened his reading of it.

A roof tile cracked differently under Brendt's weight. Roan heard the specific pitch and shoved him right before the tile gave way.

A command thread thickened behind a masked soldier's movement. Roan cut the relay instead of the soldier and watched three Husks lose timing.

Ines's shoulder wound bled with a rhythm he could almost anticipate. He caught her sleeve before she overreached and opened it fully.

"Stop doing that," she snapped.

"Stop telegraphing bad decisions through blood flow."

"I hate Forged senses already."

"So do I."

The information load was significant. Without Black Rite stabilization, it might have become intoxicating. Every second offered ten new details, and details bred confidence. Confidence bred mistakes. Roan forced himself to choose only three inputs at a time: team integrity, command pressure, footing. Everything else went into peripheral accounting.

The Codex Shard approved by not commenting. Or it was busy recalibrating. Either way, silence was acceptable.

They reached a roof garden that hadn't been a garden for a long time. Waist-high planters held ash instead of soil. Black vines, dead or pretending, wrapped around broken railings. At the far end, a narrow plank of fused stone connected to the next building. Too narrow for Brendt's unfolded shield.

"Fold it," Roan said.

Brendt looked at the gap. "I hate this city."

"Get in line," Ines said.

Husks reached the roof behind them.

Drogan turned, palm bleeding again, and began the shortened severance phrase. The crowned node countered from below; Roan felt relays move into place. Not enough time to cut them all.

He selected the one with the highest pressure value: a masked soldier climbing over the left parapet, body marked with three gray bands.

Sidewound left his hand.

Throwing it was a bad idea. The weapon was balanced for held precision, not flight. But the distance was short, the target was structurally committed, and Roan's Forged senses gave him the seam event half a breath before it happened.

The knife struck hilt-first, not blade-first.

Exactly as intended.

The impact knocked the relay's head sideways at the moment Drogan cut. The command line shifted. Severance passed through the gap.

The pack staggered.

Roan retrieved Sidewound with the cord loop he had tied to the pommel that morning because trusting expensive weapons to stay in hand was for people with family estates. The blade skittered back across the roof and into his grip.

Brendt stared. "You put a string on the legendary knife."

"Utility cord."

"Eckhart would kill you."

"Only if he survives the invoice."

Ines crossed the narrow plank first, laughing once despite herself.

The army had prepared the streets. The roofs merely hated everyone.

They crossed a slanted archive roof, jumped a gap filled with gray mist, climbed along a bridge of exposed rebar, and slid down the spine of a building whose surface had fused into black glass. Brendt nearly fell twice. Ines fell once, caught herself by driving a knife into a wall, and made a sound that suggested the wall had offended her family.

Roan moved last.

Forged initiation hadn't made him tireless. It had made fatigue more negotiable. Each breath carried Vitalis deeper. Each impact spread through organs rather than around them. His side wound continued bleeding at a reduced rate, but the bleeding no longer weakened him proportionally.

Useful.

Dangerous.

The body liked new power. The mind liked new options. The Black Rite kept both from writing checks the situation couldn't cash.

Behind them, the command node adapted again.

The Husks stopped climbing in packs and began launching bone-wire constructs ahead of the team, not to attack them but to weaken roof edges before they arrived. Twice Roan had to alter route mid-stride because a ledge collapsed a second before Brendt stepped on it.

The third time, he used the collapse.

"Drop," he said.

Brendt didn't question. He dropped through the roof into the room below. Ines followed. Drogan landed badly but upright. Roan cut the weakened roof seam with Sidewound and brought a three-meter section down behind them, crushing the first wave of pursuing constructs.

They emerged into an old residential floor.

Clean table.

Clean chairs.

A child's toy horse on the table, polished white.

Everyone stopped.

The toy horse turned its head.

Brendt whispered, "Absolutely not."

"City lord trap," Drogan said flatly. "Don't engage the objects. Move through."

The walls opened.

Not doors. Walls.

Gray hands reached through plaster, stone, and old wallpaper as if all surfaces had become water. Brendt slammed his shield into the nearest cluster. Ines kicked the clean table over without touching the toy. Drogan dragged her back before one of the chairs folded inward like a mouth.

Roan felt the recognition net try to form again.

First Refusal drew the line.

*No.*

He drove Sidewound into the floor seam under the table.

The clean objects shuddered.

Not destroyed. Disrupted.

"Out," Roan said.

They went through the far window.

No bridge this time. Just a three-story drop into an alley filled with ash.

Brendt landed with a grunt and rolled. Ines landed better. Drogan landed like a man whose knee had opinions. Roan landed last and felt the new Forged circulation take the shock through lungs, spine, and legs.

Painful.

Manageable.

A masked Hollowkin waited in the alley.

It hadn't climbed. It had predicted.

Four arms. Smaller than the command node, but marked with two antler stubs and a mask split into seven uneven lines.

Field commander.

It spoke Veyrnese.

"Continuation should descend."

Roan drew Sidewound fully.

"No," he said.

The Black Rite liked the answer.

The Hollowkin attacked.
