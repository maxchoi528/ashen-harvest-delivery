Chapter 60: The Unauthorized Procedure

The clinic smelled like copper and antiseptic. Roan stood in the doorway, watching Brendt's back rise and fall on the cot. Not rise and fall. Shudder. Involuntary muscle spasm that came from nervous system damage, not sleep.

He checked the clock on the wall. Four minutes until the infirmary nurse returned from her break. Four minutes was enough to do what he came here to do and get out before anyone asked questions he didn't want to answer.

The chart at the foot of Brendt's bed listed the injuries in clean, clinical language. Three cracked ribs. Hairline fracture left ulna. Mild concussion. Second-degree burns across the forearms where the spire's energy feedback had passed through his defensive stance. The attending physician had noted "patient stabilized, expected full recovery within six weeks."

Six weeks. The global tournament was in five.

Roan pulled the chair closer to the cot and sat. Brendt's face was swollen on one side, purple-yellow bruising spreading from his jaw to his temple. Someone had cleaned the blood off, but they hadn't done a thorough job. There was still a streak along his hairline, dried to a dark brown that didn't match his hair.

The burns were worse than the chart suggested. Roan could see where the sleeves of Brendt's training shirt had been cut away to access the wounds. The skin wasn't just burned, it was translucent in places, the subsurface layers visible through the damage. Whatever technique had hit him, it had been calibrated to cause maximum tissue damage while keeping the victim alive and conscious.

Someone had wanted Brendt to suffer.

He'd prepped the three vials last night, after Ines tipped him off about the accident. A gamble on the injury type, but Spire feedback always left the same electrical signature in the meridians. He reached into his bag.

The nurse would be back in three minutes now.

Roan opened his bag and pulled out three vials. Wraithmilk Tincture, he'd bought the batch off a third-year who needed credits fast and didn't care about the minor psychotic episodes that came with it. Ironbile Resin, a discount stock from the campus pharmacy that everyone knew was sub-potent. Ash-Veined Lotus, you couldn't pay people to take it because of the skin-peeling side effects during cycling.

Three vials. Three side effects he could invert. Three chances to convert pain into power for Brendt's recovery timeline.

He uncapped the first vial.

"You know," Brendt said without opening his eyes, "most people just send flowers."

Roan set the Wraithmilk Tincture down on the side table. "Flowers don't fix nerve damage."

Brendt's eyes opened. They were unfocused for a moment, then found Roan's face and stayed there. "How bad?"

"Bad enough that standard healing accelerators won't cut it. The feedback went into your meridian network. Your body's trying to flush the residual energy, but it's doing more damage than the energy itself."

"Translation: I'm screwed."

"Translation: you need outside intervention." Roan picked up the second vial, held it up to the light. The Ironbile Resin had separated slightly, still usable, but the active compounds would need to be re-suspended before injection. "The Ash-Veined Lotus accelerates cellular regeneration at the cost of surface tissue. If we can manage the skin damage separately—"

"Roan."

He stopped.

"You're talking about an inversion cycle. On me. Without authorization. And without telling me what it's going to cost you."

The Ironbile Resin went back on the table. "It doesn't cost me anything."

"Like hell." Brendt pushed himself up on his good arm, wincing. "I've seen you run those conversions. The way your breathing changes. The way you get quiet for about thirty seconds after, like you're listening to something I can't hear. That's not nothing."

The clock on the wall showed two minutes.

"Classified." Roan's voice was flat. "I can't tell you."

"Then I'm saying no."

The word hung in the antiseptic air.

Roan didn't move. His jaw tightened, just slightly, just enough for someone who knew him to notice. Brendt knew him. They'd been classmates for three years. Roommates for two. Brendt knew exactly what that tightness meant.

"I'm not asking," Roan said.

"I know. That's why I'm refusing." Brendt lay back on the cot, careful of his ribs. "You show up at my bedside with contraband pharmaceuticals and a plan that apparently involves some kind of classified medical technique, and you expect me to just lie here and let you—"

"It's not medical." Roan's hand was already reaching for the Ash-Veined Lotus. "It's training."

"Training doesn't fix nerve damage."

"Correct. So I'm going to fix it differently." He uncapped the vial. The smell hit him immediately, sharp, acidic, the kind that made your eyes water if you got too close. "The Lotus accelerates your cellular turnover. The Ironbile Resin flushes the meridian blockage. The Wraithmilk Tincture stabilizes your nervous system while the other two work. Layered approach. Three catalysts, three conversion cycles on my end."

Brendt stared at him. "You're going to burn through your daily capacity for this."

"Probably."

"You have limits. I know you do. You just hide them better than other people."

One minute. The nurse would be back in one minute.

Roan tilted the Ash-Veined Lotus. The liquid inside was dark green, almost black. "The tournament is in five weeks. Your team needs you. You can't afford six weeks of recovery."

"I can't afford you burning yourself out either."

"That's not your calculation to make."

The sentence came out harder than he intended. Brendt flinched, not from pain, from something else. Something in the tone. Roan remembered that and put it with the other problems he had no time to solve.

"I'm doing this anyway," Roan said. "The only variable is whether you cooperate or I do it while you're unconscious. Your choice."

Footsteps in the hallway. The nurse was early.

Brendt's eyes went to the door, then back to Roan. His jaw worked. He knew Roan wouldn't ask twice. Fighting now just meant wasting the time he couldn't afford. He let his head fall back in resignation.

"Fine," Brendt said. "But I want to know the cost."

"You won't like it."

"I'm going to like it even less if you don't tell me."

Roan set the Ash-Veined Lotus down. He picked up the Wraithmilk Tincture instead, clear liquid, faintly luminescent. Thing that looked like it belonged in a different world.

"The conversion process works by inverting side effects," he said. "The Lotus makes your skin peel. I'm going to turn that peeling into accelerated healing for your nerve tissue. The Ironbile Resin damages your organs slightly to trigger an immune response. I'm going to convert that damage into meridian flush. The Wraithmilk makes you hear things that aren't there for about forty-eight hours. I'm going to convert that into nervous system stabilization."

"That's—" Brendt stopped. Swallowed. "That's not how medicine works."

"No. It's how I work."

The door opened.

Roan moved. Fast. Faster than a normal Tempered should be. The Wraithmilk Tincture went into his coat pocket. The Ironbile Resin went into his other pocket. He was standing, chair pushed back, hands empty by the time the nurse walked through the doorway.

She was older. Grey hair in a bun. Face that had seen enough students pull enough stupid stunts to develop a permanent expression of mild suspicion.

"Visiting hours ended twenty minutes ago," she said.

Roan nodded. "I was leaving."

He walked past her. He didn't look back. He could feel Brendt's eyes on him through the closing door, the weight of unspoken questions, the calculations being run in a mind that was good at mathematics but not as good as Roan's.

Not good enough to know what was coming.

Roan reached the end of the corridor. Took the stairs down. Made it to the exterior exit before he let himself stop.

His hand went to his chest. Not his heart. The space between his shoulders where the Codex Shard had embedded itself after the first Bloodscourge conversion. The place where he felt the system respond when he pushed it.

He had three vials in his pockets. Three side effects waiting to be inverted. Three conversion cycles that would cost him Vitalis and capacity, plus a measurable drain on his reserve that he could already feel settling into his bones.

He was going to do it anyway.

The door behind him opened.

Roan turned.

Selene Ardent emerged from the infirmary door, a small bandage on her forearm. She stopped when she saw Roan. Her expression was hard to read, but she didn't look surprised.

"You're going to burn yourself out, Vance," she said. "I've seen the kind of contraband you carry. People notice."

Roan didn't ask how she knew. Selene had sources. The rumor mill said she fed information to House Ardent's network. He didn't care if it was true.

"That's my risk."

"It's also a risk to everyone connected to you. The academy has rules about unsanctioned pharmaceutical experiments."

"Who would report me?"

Selene's lips pressed into a thin line. "I might."

"Why?"

She studied him for a moment. "Because there are people watching you. Not all of them mean you harm. But you're not as careful as you think."

She turned and walked back into the building.

Roan stood in the stairwell. The vials in his pockets felt heavier than they should. He thought about her words. He thought about Brendt's eyes. He thought about the sound of his father's voice saying *don't get caught*.

He was going to do this anyway.

He pushed off the wall and walked back toward the dormitory. The vials clinked softly in his pocket. He had three cycles to run and one friend who needed them. The door to the stairwell clicked shut behind him.
