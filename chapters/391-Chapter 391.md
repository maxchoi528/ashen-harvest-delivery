The seal cracked beneath Kael's boot heel, and the sound echoed through the ruined nave like a gunshot.
He stumbled forward, catching himself against a shattered pillar. Frost crept up the broken stone where his hand touched it. The cathedral of Saint Aldric had been a fortress of faith three centuries ago. Now it was a wound in the city, a place where the boundary between worlds had worn so thin that the air itself tasted of copper and decay.
"You're late, Soul Reaver."
The voice came from everywhere and nowhere, a chorus of whispers that seemed to emanate from the darkness pooling between the broken pews. Kael's hand dropped to Soul Reaver's hilt. The blade thrummed against his palm, hungry.
"I had to cut through a patrol of Iron Vanguard." Kael kept his voice flat, controlled. "They're sweeping the district. Something's got them spooked."
"Something should have them terrified." The shadows coalesced, twisting into a shape that defied geometry—a figure of angles and absence, humanoid in suggestion but wrong in every proportion. Two points of pale light served as eyes, fixed on Kael with ancient hunger. "They know the seal is failing. They do not know *why*."
Kael's breath misted in air that had grown colder by the second. His spirit sense told him everything his eyes confirmed: this was not a cultist, not a possessed vessel. This was an avatar, a fragment of something that existed beyond the Veil, pressed into the shape of a man through sheer will.
An Abyssal Entity.
"You summoned me here." Kael's fingers tightened around Soul Reaver's grip. "I assume you didn't do it just to deliver a lecture on local politics."
The avatar's non-face split into something that might have been a smile. "Direct. Good. The last Reaver wasted breath on prayers before I consumed him." It drifted closer, and Kael felt the pressure of its presence like a hand closing around his throat. "You are different. The blade recognizes you. It has not tasted a true Reaver in centuries."
"Soul Reaver isn't a blade." Kael shifted his stance, weight balanced on the balls of his feet. "It's a tool. I decide what it cuts."
"Such confidence." The avatar circled him now, and Kael tracked the movement without turning his head. Every instinct screamed warnings—this creature was playing with him, savoring the moment before violence. "The tool remembers what its wielder forgets. You are not the first to carry that weapon. You will not be the last. But you could be the one who breaks the cycle, if you chose correctly."
"And what choice is that?"
The avatar stopped directly before him, close enough that Kael could see the way shadows writhed beneath its skin. The pale eyes blazed brighter.
"Join us. Not as a servant—as an ally. The seals are failing. The Entities will return regardless of what the Orders do. You could position yourself among the new powers, or you could die with the old world."
Kael's laugh came out harsher than he intended. "You came all this way to offer me a job?"
"I came to assess you." The avatar's form flickered, and for a moment Kael saw something else beneath it—a vast darkness, an absence so complete it hurt to perceive. Then it was gone, replaced by that twisted human mask. "The Order of Dawn believes they control the seal network. The Iron Vanguard believes they control the martial strength of the realm. The Shadow Court believes they control the hidden war. None of them understand what is coming."
"Then enlighten me."
"Knowledge has a price." The avatar extended one hand, fingers elongated into points of solidified shadow. "I offer you a gift—a fragment of understanding. In return, you will permit me to observe you. Not interfere. Just observe. You will not feel my presence, and I will learn how the latest Soul Reaver conducts himself."
Kael's mind raced. This was a trap—of course it was a trap. But the information it promised might be worth the risk. He thought of the Sepulcher tablets, of the incomplete truths about Soul Reaver's origin. He thought of the seal network, degrading faster than anyone admitted. He thought of Lyra's warning about the Shadow Court's interest in him.
The avatar waited, patient as only something immortal could be.
"Show me."
Kael barely had time to register the error before the avatar's hand touched his chest. Cold exploded through his body—not the cold of winter, but the cold of the void between stars. His vision went white, then flooded with images that burned themselves into his memory.
He saw the seal network as it existed in its prime: seventy-two points of golden light arranged across the continent, each one a fortress of faith and power, each one guarded by the greatest Awakened of their generation. He saw them weaken, one by one, century by century, not from attack but from simple entropy. He saw the current state: forty-three seals functioning at full strength, nineteen operating at diminished capacity, ten on the verge of collapse.
And he saw why the seals were failing faster than projected.
The Entities were feeding on each other.
Not randomly—strategically. The stronger ones consumed the weaker when their seals grew thin enough, absorbing their essence and growing more powerful. The avatar showed him a glimpse of what waited beyond the Veil: three massive presences that had grown fat on their fallen kindred, their combined power threatening to overwhelm the weakened barriers between worlds.
"Soul Reaver." The avatar withdrew its hand, and Kael found himself on his knees, gasping. His lungs burned with cold. "The blade you carry was forged specifically to counter what you have just witnessed. It can disrupt Entity feeding patterns. It can stabilize seals temporarily. It can even—" The avatar paused, and something like hesitation crossed its inhuman features. "It can end the threat permanently, if wielded by one willing to pay the price."
"What price?"
"That is not knowledge I can give." The avatar's form began to dissolve, shadows bleeding into the darkness between pillars. "But I have shown you enough to understand that this war is not what the Orders believe. They fight to maintain a system already lost. You must choose your own path."
"Wait—"
But the avatar was gone, leaving only a chill that lingered long after its departure.
Kael forced himself to stand, his legs unsteady. The encounter had lasted maybe ten minutes, but he felt as though days had passed. Soul Reaver pulsed against his hip, warm now where it had been cold before.
"System notification pending," he muttered, and the familiar interface flickered into existence before his eyes.
[Information Asymmetry] The avatar's gift has provided insight into the seal network's true weakness. Kael now understands: the Entities are not only escaping through failing seals—they are consuming each other to grow stronger, and three dominant presences have emerged beyond the Veil. The seal network is not just degrading; it is becoming a feeding ground.
Kael dismissed the notification and looked around the ruined cathedral. Dawn light crept through the broken windows now, painting the frost in shades of amber and rose. The Entity's presence had faded, but something else remained—a feeling of being watched that had nothing to do with the avatar.
He moved through the nave toward the altar, where a familiar figure waited.
Lyra emerged from behind a pillar, her dark coat still bearing traces of ash from the street fighting she'd mentioned. Her sword hung at her hip, and she carried a crossbow that looked like it had seen heavy use.
"You're alive." She kept her distance, eyes scanning the shadows. "What happened? I felt something when I crossed the threshold—a presence that made every hair on my body stand up."
"The avatar showed itself." Kael stopped at the altar, turning to face her. "It offered me information. I accepted."
"Accepted?" Lyra's hand drifted to her sword. "You dealt with an Entity willingly?"
"I dealt with a messenger. There's a difference." He caught her skeptical look and sighed. "It told me about the seals. The reason they're failing faster than projected is that the Entities are feeding on each other. Three of them have grown strong enough to threaten the entire network."
Lyra's expression shifted from suspicion to genuine alarm. "Three dominant Entities. That would explain the increased cultist activity. They've always been proxy forces, but if the true powers are consolidating..." She trailed off, calculating. "The Orders need to know."
"The Orders know." Kael's voice hardened. "They've known for at least a decade. They just haven't done anything about it because admitting the truth would mean admitting the Concordat is worthless. Six Orders can't hold back three Entities that are stronger than all of them combined."
"Then what do we do?"
The question hung between them. Kael looked at Lyra—former Shadow Court operative, current outcast, woman who had every reason to distrust him and every reason to want the old powers burned down. She was asking what *they* should do, as if they were a team. As if her fate was tied to his choices.
It might be. If the avatar's words held truth, the coming war would consume everyone, Awakened and inert alike.
"We start by surviving the week." Kael moved toward the cathedral's main entrance. "The avatar said it would observe me. That means the Entities are watching. The Shadow Court is watching. The Orders are watching. We need to figure out what they want before we can decide what we need."
He pushed through the massive doors and emerged into a Thornwick morning thick with coal smoke. The street beyond was quiet—too quiet for a district that should have been waking to commerce and industry. A single cart sat abandoned in the middle of the road, its horse long since fled.
Lyra followed him out, her eyes scanning rooftops and alleyways. "There's a safehouse in the Merchant Quarter. Off the grid, away from Order patrols."
"Good. We should talk about the avatar's information and what it means for our next moves." Kael started walking, and Lyra fell into step beside him. "But first, I need to check something."
"Check what?"
He didn't answer immediately. Instead, he focused on the residual energy in the area—plentiful after an Entity manifestation, even a brief one. The Law of Soul Conservation meant that spiritual power couldn't be created from nothing, but it could be transferred, and Entity avatars left traces wherever they manifested.
"There." Kael pointed to a section of cobblestone near the cathedral steps, where the stone had been darkened by some spilled substance. Lyra saw it a moment later: a shape pressed into the rock, a handprint that seemed to absorb light fairly than reflect it.
"That's residue from the avatar. You can harvest that?"
"I can harvest anything that once held spiritual energy." Kael knelt beside the mark, ignoring the cold that seeped through his trousers. "It's not a corpse, but it's close enough to the source. The quality should be decent."
He placed his palm against the darkened stone and triggered Soul Reaver's harvesting function.
The sensation was different from harvesting from the dead. There was no warmth, no echo of personality or memory. Instead, he felt a vast emptiness, a hunger so profound it seemed to reach into his own soul and try to pull something out. For a moment, he was afraid the Entity had inexplicably poisoned the harvest, taken something from him in exchange for the information.
Then the system notification appeared, and Kael's fear transformed into something else entirely.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Shadow Essence (Entity-touched) from residual manifestation. Quality: Rare. Acquired: Abyssal Resonance (Passive) — You can sense Entity presence within 100 meters. Effect intensified when near active manifestations or weakened seals.]
Lyra watched his face and frowned. "What happened? You look like you've seen a ghost."
"Better than a ghost." Kael stood, flexing his fingers. The new ability pulsed at the edge of his awareness, a constant low-level awareness of the hidden layer of reality. "The avatar left something behind when it manifested. I harvested it."
"And?"
"I can sense Entities now. Not just through their manifestations—through their presence in general. It's like..." He struggled for words. "It's like having a sixth sense for wrongness. The Abyss itself has a flavor, and this lets me taste it."
Lyra absorbed this information with a nod. "That could be useful. Dangerous, but useful. The question is what else the avatar left behind."
"Meaning?"
"Entities don't give gifts without strings." Lyra started walking again, and Kael followed. "The observation clause it mentioned—letting it watch you. That's not nothing. If they're studying you, they want to understand how you work. How Soul Reaver works. They might be planning to counter you specifically."
Kael considered this as they moved through the empty streets. The coal smoke had thickened, obscuring the upper stories of buildings and muffling the distant sounds of the city. A fog, natural or otherwise, had rolled in.
"You're saying the avatar might have compromised my abilities inexplicably."
"I'm saying we should assume it tried. The fact that you harvested something useful doesn't mean there isn't a trap waiting to spring." Lyra ducked into an alley, and Kael followed. "The Shadow Court uses similar tactics. Present something valuable, let the target get comfortable, then exploit the attachment they develop. If an Entity is smart enough to manifest an avatar, it's smart enough to play psychological games."
"You're speaking from experience."
Lyra's jaw tightened. "I'm speaking from survival. The Court doesn't keep operatives who make stupid mistakes. I watched three of them die because they trusted the wrong source of information. I won't be the fourth."
They emerged from the alley onto a wider street, lined with shuttered storefronts and darkened windows. The Merchant Quarter was supposed to be active at this hour—merchants opening their shops, customers beginning their purchases. The silence felt wrong, deliberate.
"Something's happened." Kael's new sense tingled at the edge of perception. "There's presence here. Multiple sources."
"I feel it too." Lyra raised her crossbow, scanning the street ahead. "Order soldiers, if I had to guess. This is Iron Vanguard territory."
"Not just soldiers." Kael pointed to a figure standing in the middle of the road, perfectly visible despite the fog. A man in the white and gold tabard of the Order of Dawn, sword drawn, eyes fixed on their approach. "That's not standard patrol behavior."
"He's bait."
"Or an advance scout." Kael kept walking, hand on Soul Reaver's hilt. "Either way, we're committed now. If we run, they know we're avoiding confrontation. If we fight, we're escalating."
The Dawn knight didn't move as they approached, but two more figures emerged from doorways behind him—more Dawn soldiers, weapons ready. Kael counted at least four more in the fog, vague shapes that resolved into armed men as they drew closer.
"Kael Ashborn." The lead knight's voice carried easily through the stillness. "By order of the Order of Dawn, you are to surrender your weapon and submit to questioning regarding your connection to Abyssal activity in Thornwick."
"Connection?" Lyra stepped forward, placing herself between Kael and the knight. "The connection being that he's been killing cultists? The same cultists you're apparently too incompetent to find?"
"Careful, former Shadow Court operative." The knight's eyes never left Kael. "The Order of Dawn has no quarrel with you. Stand aside and you may leave freely."
"He stands with me." Lyra's hand rested on her sword hilt now. "Whatever you're planning, it won't work."
The knight sighed, a gesture that seemed almost rehearsed. "We had hoped to resolve this peacefully. Lord Commander Varen was entirely specific about avoiding casualties." He raised his sword, and the Dawn knights behind him moved into combat positions. "Kael Ashborn, you are charged with conspiracy to weaken the seal network, collaboration with Abyssal Entities, and unauthorized use of a Soul Reaver artifact. You will come with us, or you will die here."
Kael's grip tightened on Soul Reaver. The new ability showed him the spiritual energy flowing through the knights—moderate power, well-trained, coordinated. They weren't elite, but they weren't weak either. Six of them, all told, with more approaching from the streets behind.
Six versus two. Bad odds, but not impossible.
The knight waited, and Kael realized he was genuinely considering the offer. Surrender meant interrogation, probably torture, definitely imprisonment. The Order of Dawn didn't release prisoners once they had them. But fighting meant establishing himself as an enemy of the Orders, which complicated every future interaction.
The wrong choice here could doom everything.
Lyra's voice came low, meant only for him. "Whatever you decide, I'm with you. But we should move. There's an exit through the tannery three streets over—my safehouse route."
"I heard you the first time." Kael's eyes moved between the knights, calculating angles and distances. "The lead one is Sovereign rank. I can take him."
"And the other five?"
"I'll handle what I can." Soul Reaver slid free of its sheath, and the blade seemed to drink the light around it. "The rest is up to you."
The lead knight saw the movement and reacted instantly, raising his sword to strike. But Kael was faster—not faster than physical reflexes, but faster than conscious thought, his body moving on instinct honed through dozens of encounters with the dead.
Soul Reaver met the descending blade and turned it aside. The knight stumbled forward, off-balance, and Kael drove an elbow into his face. Cartilage crunched. The knight dropped, and Kael was already moving to the next target.
Lyra's crossbow sang. One of the flanking soldiers fell with a bolt in his throat. She dropped the weapon and drew her sword in one smooth motion, cutting at another knight who tried to press past Kael's position.
The fog swirled around them, and Kael felt his new sense flare with each flash of spiritual energy. These knights were Awakened, but their power came from training and discipline, not raw ability. Soul Reaver could drain them if he touched them long enough, but combat wasn't the time for harvesting. Combat was about survival.
Two knights came at him together, blades crossing. Kael parried one, ducked the other, and slammed Soul Reaver's pommel into the nearest attacker's temple. He dropped, and Kael used his momentum to spin, kicking the second soldier in the knee. The joint bent wrong, and the man screamed before collapsing.
"Kael!" Lyra's warning came a half-second before he felt the spiritual pressure.
He threw himself sideways as a massive force slammed into the space he'd occupied. A third knight—larger than the others, wearing heavier armor—had entered the fray, and his weapon wasn't a sword. It was a war hammer wreathed in golden light, the kind of weapon that could crush bone through plate armor.
Sovereign rank. Not just the leader—multiple Sovereigns. The Order of Dawn had sent real fighters.
The hammer came again, and Kael barely deflected it with Soul Reaver. The impact numbed his arm to the elbow. This wasn't a soldier; this was a champion, someone who had earned the right to carry blessed weapons.
"You should have surrendered when you had the chance." The champion pressed forward, hammer swinging in devastating arcs. "The Order of Dawn doesn't send only six to make an arrest."
"Lyra!"
"I see them!" She was already moving, cutting down a knight who'd tried to flank while Kael dealt with the champion. "More coming from the south! We need to—"
The fog exploded with golden light. Kael felt it before he saw it—a surge of spiritual energy that knocked him off his feet and sent the champion staggering. When his vision cleared, he saw a new figure standing in the middle of the street.
A woman in robes of pale blue, silver hair bound in elaborate braids, eyes that glowed with contained power. Her presence alone was enough to make Kael's skin crawl.
Transcendent. At minimum.
"The Order of Dawn's business is concluded here." Her voice carried an authority that made even the champion lower his weapon. "I will handle the Soul Reaver."
"Commander Sera." The champion's voice held grudging respect. "Lord Commander Varen ordered—"
"Lord Commander Varen is not present. I am." The woman—Commander Sera—turned her glowing eyes toward Kael, and he felt the burden of her attention like a physical force. "You have caused considerable disruption, Soul Reaver. The Council has questions. You will answer them."
Lyra appeared at his side, sword ready but clearly recognizing the futility of resistance. "We can't fight a Transcendent."
"No." Kael stood slowly, keeping Soul Reaver in hand. "But we can talk."
"Can you?" Sera's smile held no warmth. "The last Soul Reaver who claimed he could negotiate ended up on a pike outside the Dawn Citadel. I am not interested in games. You will come with me, and you will provide information about the Entities' activities in Thornwick. In exchange, I will ensure you are not executed immediately."
"And if I refuse?"
"Then I will take you by force, and Commander Aldric will demonstrate what happens to those who resist the Order of Dawn." She gestured, and the champion raised his hammer. "Choose quickly. My patience is limited."
Kael looked at Lyra. She shook her head slightly—not in refusal, but in warning. Whatever he decided, fighting here meant dying here.
But surrender meant the Order of Dawn's dungeons, and he knew what happened to Soul Reavers who fell into Order custody. The records from the Abyssal Wars were clear. They didn't interrogate; they executed.
"Soul Reaver." Sera's voice cut through his thoughts. "I know what you're considering. The avatar you met in the cathedral—it told you the seals are failing faster than projected. It told you about the Entities feeding on each other. It offered you a choice."
Kael's blood went cold. "You were watching."
"I was monitoring. There is a difference." Sera's eyes narrowed. "The Order of Dawn has known about the accelerated degradation for decades. We have also known that the avatar's kind is not unified—some Entities want to return to the Material, others want to continue feeding in the Abyss, and a few want something else entirely. The one who spoke with you belongs to the third category."
"And what do they want?"
"That is what I intend to determine." Sera stepped closer, and Kael felt the pressure of her spiritual presence increase. "You are the latest in a long line of Soul Reavers. The blade you carry was created for a specific purpose, one that even the original bearers did not fully understand. The Entities know this. They are watching you because you represent a variable in an equation that has remained stable for centuries."
"So I'm a threat to their plans."
"You are a potential asset or a potential weapon. The difference lies in who controls you." Sera stopped an arm's length away, close enough that Kael could see the lines of strain around her eyes. "The Order of Dawn can offer protection. The Iron Vanguard can offer power. The Shadow Court offers secrecy. All of them want to use you. I am offering something different."
"Which is?"
"Information. The truth about Soul Reaver's origin, the purpose it was designed to serve, and why the Entities fear what it represents." Sera's voice dropped, meant only for him now. "The avatar showed you fragments of reality. I can show you the whole picture. But only if you come with me willingly."
Kael's mind raced through possibilities. Every option led to compromise, to someone else controlling his choices. But Sera was offering something the others hadn't—the truth about the blade he carried, the power he used.
The truth might be worth the risk.
"Lyra comes with me."
Sera raised an eyebrow. "The Shadow Court operative? She is not part of this negotiation."
"She's with me. Where I go, she goes. Take it or find out if you can truly take Soul Reaver from a Demigod fighting for his life."
The silence stretched. Commander Sera studied him, and Kael felt the burden of her assessment like a physical thing. Finally, she nodded.
"Agreed. But understand—this is not alliance. This is examination. I will determine whether you are an asset or a threat, and act accordingly."
"And Lyra?"
"She remains as collateral. If you prove false, she dies. If you prove true, she goes free." Sera turned, robes swirling. "We leave now. The Iron Vanguard will arrive within minutes, and I would prefer not to explain your presence to their Commander."
Kael looked at Lyra. She gave him a slight nod—cautious acceptance, not enthusiasm. They had walked into a situation where choices were limited, and this was the least terrible option available.
"Lead the way, Commander."
Sera started walking, and they followed. Behind them, the champion gathered his soldiers, but they didn't pursue. Kael's new sense picked up the spiritual signatures fading as they moved—Sovereign-level power receding, to be replaced by something else. Something vast and patient and waiting.
The Entities were watching. They had seen what happened in the street, and they would adjust their plans accordingly.
Kael fell into step beside Lyra as they followed Sera into the fog.
"That went better than expected," Lyra murmured.
"It went exactly as expected for someone planning to use me." Kael kept his voice low. "The question is whether we can turn their plan to our advantage."
"Can we?"
"The avatar gave me a way to sense Entities. Sera wants to give me information about Soul Reaver's purpose. The Order of Dawn knows the seals are failing and hasn't told anyone else." Kael glanced back at the receding street. "Someone is lying. I intend to find out who."
They emerged from the fog into a courtyard dominated by a building of white stone and stained glass. The Order of Dawn's Thornwick chapter house—larger than Kael had expected, and far more heavily guarded. Sentries watched from every position, and the spiritual pressure of ward stones hummed along the perimeter.
Sera led them through a side entrance, away from the main doors. The interior was a maze of corridors and stairs, designed to confuse intruders. Kael memorized the route as they walked, filing it away for potential future use.
"This way." Sera stopped before a heavy door bound in iron. "The archives are below ground. What I have to show you cannot leave this room."
The door swung open to reveal a staircase descending into darkness. Sera entered first, and Kael followed, with Lyra close behind.
The stairs went down further than should have been possible, past foundations, past sewers, past the natural stone of the city's bedrock. When they finally emerged into a chamber lit by floating spheres of pale light, Kael understood why.
This wasn't a basement. This was a vault, carved from the living rock and reinforced with materials that predated the city above. The walls were covered in writing—not just words, but diagrams, maps, and images that seemed to shift when viewed directly.
"The truth about Soul Reaver begins here." Sera gestured to the nearest wall, where a massive illustration dominated the surface. It depicted a figure holding a blade similar to Soul Reaver, facing a wall of shadows that stretched from horizon to horizon. "One thousand years ago, during the Abyssal Wars, humanity faced extinction. The Entities had breached the Veil in seventy-two locations simultaneously. The Orders were new, undisciplined, and losing."
"We know this history," Lyra said.
"You know the sanitized version." Sera's voice hardened. "The truth is that the Orders were failing. Not because they lacked power, but because they lacked a weapon capable of truly destroying Entities fairly than only driving them back. The seventy-two seals were a desperate measure, a way to buy time fairly than achieve victory."
"And Soul Reaver?" Kael's eyes were fixed on the illustration.
"Was not created by the Orders. It was given to them—by an Entity." Sera let that revelation settle before continuing. "The Sun God, in his mercy, provided humanity with a weapon against his wayward siblings. Soul Reaver was forged in the heart of a dying star and tempered in the blood of a god. It was designed to do what no other weapon could: sever the connection between an Entity and its power source, making them vulnerable to destruction."
"But the Entities weren't destroyed. The seals were built instead."
"Because killing an Entity is not the same as killing its power. The Abyss is infinite. Destroying an Entity only frees its essence to be absorbed by others." Sera traced a line on the illustration, showing the seals and their arrangement. "The Orders chose containment over victory. They believed the seals could hold indefinitely. They were wrong."
Kael studied the diagrams, seeing patterns he recognized from his own observations. "The seals are connected. Not just spiritually—physically. They're a network."
"Very good." Sera's approval was grudging but genuine. "The seventy-two seals form a circuit, each one reinforcing the others. When one fails, the entire network weakens. When enough fail, the remaining seals cannot hold the pressure of the Abyss."
"And the avatar was right about the Entities feeding on each other."
"Correct. With the seals weakening, the Entities closest to the Material have begun consuming their distant siblings. Three have grown powerful enough to threaten the entire network." Sera paused. "The Order of Dawn has known this for fifteen years. We have been attempting to develop countermeasures, but every solution requires something we do not possess."
"What's that?"
"A Soul Reaver capable of using the weapon's true purpose." Sera turned to face him directly. "The blade you carry was made for you—or fairly, for whoever carries it. It attunes to its wielder, amplifying their abilities and granting new ones as they grow stronger. But it has limits. In its current state, it can disrupt Entity feeding, stabilize seals temporarily, and harvest power from the dying. What it cannot do is destroy an Entity permanently."
"Then how do I unlock its true purpose?"
"That is what I intend to determine." Sera gestured to a smaller illustration on the adjacent wall, depicting a tomb. "Soul Reaver was not the only weapon of its kind. There were twelve forged in total, each given to a chosen bearer. All twelve were lost in the centuries after the Abyssal Wars, their bearers dead or disappeared. The tomb depicted here holds the original—sometimes called the First Reaver or the Prime Blade. It contains the knowledge needed to unlock Soul Reaver's full potential."
Kael stared at the illustration, and for a moment, the image seemed to shift. He saw the tomb—not as a drawing, but as a memory, something buried in the depths of his own mind.
"Where is this tomb?"
"That information is restricted to the highest levels of the Order of Dawn." Sera's expression gave nothing away. "Even I do not know its location. Only the Lord Commander possesses that knowledge, and he has shared it with no one."
"Then how do we find it?"
"We don't. Not yet." Sera moved toward a side chamber, and Kael followed. "But there are clues scattered throughout history, references to the First Reaver in obscure texts and artifact descriptions. The avatar you met possesses information about the tomb's location. It is one reason the Entities are watching you."
"You want me to bait the avatar into revealing the tomb's location."
"I want you to understand the situation." Sera stopped before a table covered in documents and artifacts. "You are not a weapon to be wielded. You are a key that may unlock a door humanity has been knocking on for a millennium. The question is what lies on the other side of that door—and whether we want to open it."
Kael examined the documents spread across the table. Maps, star charts, fragments of ancient texts. Someone had been researching the tomb for years, following every lead and dead end.
"You've been searching for the tomb."
