The air tasted of copper and burning. Kael's fingers pressed against the cooling flesh of an Iron Vanguard sergeant, drawing out the last dregs of spiritual energy before they dissipated into nothing. Around him, the Ashenmoor Wastes stretched endlessly—smoke and ash, shattered siege equipment, bodies scattered like broken toys across dead earth.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted ferrous essence from the remains. Essence: 3 vials acquired. Fragment: Iron Skin (Stage 1) acquired."
Three vials. Pathetic yield. The sergeant had been stage two at best—barely worth the effort of kneeling. But every drop mattered now. Every corpse brought him closer to something more.
Kael rose and surveyed the battlefield. Dead Awakened littered the ground—Dawn warriors in gold-trimmed armor, Vanguard soldiers in grey, even a few Arcane Spire mages in their distinctive blue robes now splashed with blood. The rift overhead pulsed with violet light, bleeding corruption into the sky.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted corrupted essence from the remains. Essence: 12 vials acquired. Fragment: Abyssal Corruption (Degraded) acquired."
Twelve vials. Better. The fragment carried taint, but taint could be filtered, refined, used. His own soul remained clean—the corruption settled into a distant corner of his consciousness like sediment in still water.
A horn sounded in the distance. Deep, resonant, wrong.
Kael turned toward the sound. Figures emerged from the smoke—not soldiers, not Awakened, but something else. Cultists. Dozens of them, pouring through a gap in the siege lines, moving with coordinated precision that spoke of organization, planning, purpose.
The Six Orders had been caught off-guard.
Kael's hands tightened into fists. His channels held maybe two more decent harvests before depletion, but the cultists carried weapons that gleamed with Abyssal resonance. They'd been Awakened once. They'd given themselves to something older, darker, more hungry than any natural power.
One of the cultists noticed him standing over the Dawn warrior's corpse. She pointed, barked an order, and the group split—half continuing toward the siege lines, half converging on his position.
"Another Reaper," she called out, voice carrying strange harmonics. "Fresh one. Kill him slow."
Kael assessed the odds. Twenty cultists, all carrying stolen Awakened power. His own reserves: depleted. His best fragments: barely stage three.
The system pulsed in his vision.
[Soul Harvest Available: 47 fresh corpses within harvesting range. Estimated yield: 200+ vials if fully harvested.]
The cultists charged.
Kael ran.
His boots pounded against ash and corpse-flesh as he sprinted toward the densest cluster of bodies—enemy dead, friend dead, it no longer mattered. The cultists followed, their movements too fast, too fluid, their eyes burning with stolen light.
He dove behind a collapsed siege engine, rolled, came up with his hands pressed against the first corpse he could reach.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted corrupted essence from the remains. Essence: 8 vials acquired. Fragment: Zealot's Resolve (Stage 2) acquired."
The essence flooded his channels—turbulent, violent, tasting of fanatical devotion. He pulled his hand free and threw the power outward without shaping it, without controlling it.
The resulting explosion tore through three cultists, scattering their burning bodies across the field.
The remaining seventeen didn't slow.
Kael harvested again. And again. Each corpse gave him another fragment, another vial, another moment of desperate survival. The cultists died, but they took soldiers with them—and those soldiers' bodies became his fuel.
When the last cultist fell, Kael stood alone in a field of corpses, breathing hard, hands trembling with exertion and channel strain.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted mixed essence from the remains. Essence: 34 vials acquired. Fragment: Dawn's Judgment (Stage 3) acquired. Fragment: Iron Skin (Stage 2) upgraded."
His channels burned. The corruption from the Abyssal corpses had spread further, now threading through his spiritual pathways like dark veins through marble. But his reserves had never been higher.
The system notification pulsed again.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted soul fragments from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired.]
Kael paused.
Memory Fragment. That was new. Or, that was rare—the kind of harvest that only happened when the dying soul held something precious enough to preserve itself.
He pressed his palm to the nearest corpse, pulling the fragment into his consciousness.
Images flooded him. Not skills, not essence—memories.
A woman in Silver Council robes, standing before a map of Thornwick. Red markers scattered across districts. "The unsealing proceeds on schedule," she said. "By the new moon, all seventy-two rifts will—"
The memory shattered.
Kael gasped, staggering back. His mind reeled with implications. Seventy-two rifts. All of them. Not random attacks, not isolated incidents—the cults were planning coordinated mass unsealing.
The Silver Council operative had died nearby. Her body lay ten feet away, throat cut, eyes staring at the violet sky.
Kael crossed to her, knelt, pressed his palm to her forehead.
The harvest hit him like a physical blow.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted soul essence from the remains. Essence: 7 vials acquired. Memory Fragment acquired."
The memories poured in. Organization charts. Rift locations. Names—including Zarek, Prime Cultist, the man who'd framed Kael for murder three years ago. Communication protocols. And beneath it all, a single burning imperative: the original Soul Reaver's tomb.
The original.
The first.
Kael's hands shook as the implications settled. Soul Reavers weren't natural. They were made—created by some ancient process, some forgotten ritual. And the original had been dead for three centuries.
Except the memories said otherwise.
The original Soul Reaver wasn't dead. They'd been imprisoned, contained, their soul crystallized and used as a power source for three hundred years. And they were located directly beneath Thornwick, in a chamber hidden beneath the old cathedral where the Orders held their council meetings.
The system notification pulsed.
[Fragment: Sepulcher Memory (Locked). Additional power required to access.]
Locked. He needed more strength to unlock whatever the original Soul Reaver had left behind.
But the information was enough.
Somewhere in the chaos of the battlefield, a new horn sounded—different from the cultists' signal. Kael looked up to see soldiers in grey-and-silver armor reforming their lines, pushing back against cultist advances. Iron Vanguard. They'd rallied faster than expected.
A scout patrol detached from the lines and ran toward his position. Five soldiers, led by a scarred woman with grey threading her temples.
"Soul Reaver," the leader said, not a question.
Kael stood slowly, hands visible at his sides. His palms still glowed faintly with residual essence.
"I'm not your enemy," he said.
The Vanguard soldiers exchanged glances. Trust didn't come easily—not to warriors who'd seen too many allies fall, not to soldiers fighting a war they were losing.
"The Dawn warrior," one of them said, gesturing to the corpse Kael had harvested. "That was Sergeant Aldric. He had a family."
"I know." Kael had felt it in the harvest—the man's memories of a wife, children, a cottage in the eastern provinces. "He died well. Protecting the lines."
"Protecting the lines," the scarred woman repeated. Her eyes were hard, calculating. "And you harvested him."
"The Abyss killed him. I collected what was left." Kael's voice came out flat, matter-of-fact. "I can do more. There are cultists dying in the eastern quadrant. Fresh corpses. High-quality essence."
The scarred woman studied him for a long moment. "You're offering to fight."
"I'm offering to harvest. Combat is your job." Kael gestured to the ongoing battle. "Give me access to the corpses, and I'll fuel your Awakened. Every vial I harvest, you get half. The rest goes to my personal reserves."
"That's against Order regulations."
"The Orders are losing." Kael's gaze swept the battlefield. Bodies everywhere. Essence dissipating into nothing. "They can have their regulations when they win. Until then, they need every advantage they can get."
The scarred woman exchanged another glance with her squad. Something passed between them—a decision made without words.
"The eastern quadrant," she said finally. "There's a Dawn knight dying near the rift breach. Stage four. If you can reach him before he expires, you can have whatever's left."
Kael was already moving.
The rift breach glowed violet against the horizon, a wound in reality bleeding corruption into the world. Around it, the fighting had devolved into chaos—soldiers and cultists locked in desperate combat, Awakened on both sides throwing everything they had at each other.
Kael ran through the carnage, leaping over fallen bodies, ducking under swinging blades, harvesting from every corpse he passed. By the time he reached the Dawn knight, his channels had refilled to nearly full capacity.
The knight lay against a shattered pillar, his golden armor cracked, his white cloak soaked in blood. His hand still gripped his sword, knuckles white with determination.
"Hold on," Kael said, dropping to his knees beside him. "Don't die yet."
The knight's eyes—faded blue, clouding with approaching death—focused on Kael's face. "Reaver," he breathed. "I can... I can feel you pulling."
"I know. I'm sorry." Kael pressed both palms to the knight's chest. "This is going to feel strange."
The harvest began.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted light essence from the remains. Essence: 24 vials acquired. Fragment: Sun's Judgment (Stage 4) acquired."
Twenty-four vials. Stage four ability fragment. The knight's power flowed into Kael like liquid sunlight—warm, clean, infinitely pure. He felt the man's memories alongside the essence: decades of service, countless battles, faith in the Order's cause that had never wavered until this final moment, when the Abyss had proven too strong.
"Soul Harvest: Memory Fragment acquired."
The knight's final memory crashed into Kael's consciousness. The rift breach. The creature that had emerged—not one of the lesser Entities, but something vast and terrible, something that had torn through the Dawn knight's defenses like paper. A Prime. One of the original Abyssal Entities, freed from its seal for the first time in centuries.
The knight had died buying time. Buying enough minutes for the evacuation to complete, for the non-Awakened civilians to flee, for the other Orders to arrive with reinforcements.
His sacrifice had worked.
Kael pulled back, the knight's body going slack beneath his hands. The man had been stage four. Demigod class. One step below true Divinity, and he had thrown his life away to save strangers.
"Rest," Kael murmured. "I'll make sure it counts."
He turned back toward the main battle.
The Iron Vanguard had pushed forward, establishing a perimeter around the rift breach. The cultists still fought, but their numbers had thinned, their coordinated advance broken by the desperate stand of exhausted soldiers.
Kael waded into the killing ground.
For the next hour, he harvested. Enemy dead, friend dead, it no longer mattered. Every corpse became fuel. Every fragment became power. The system notifications scrolled continuously, his reserves swelling with each extraction.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted corrupted essence from the remains. Essence: 18 vials acquired. Fragment: Shadow Step (Stage 2) acquired.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted mixed essence from the remains. Essence: 22 vials acquired. Fragment: Dawn's Judgment (Stage 3) upgraded. Fragment: Iron Skin (Stage 2) upgraded to Stage 3.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted zealot essence from the remains. Essence: 6 vials acquired. Fragment: Zealot's Resolve (Stage 2) upgraded to Stage 3.]
By the time the battle ended, Kael had harvested over two hundred vials of essence and accumulated fragments from stage one through stage four. His channels burned with accumulated power, corruption threading through his spiritual pathways like dark rivers through fertile soil.
The system notification pulsed with an alert.
[Warning: Spiritual corruption levels elevated. Extended exposure to Abyssal essence may result in permanent contamination.]
Kael dismissed the warning. Contamination was a concern for the future. Right now, he needed to survive the present.
Soldiers began clearing the battlefield, dragging bodies to designated collection points. The Mortician's Guild would handle the official harvesting—legal, regulated, providing essence to the Orders through proper channels. What they collected would be a fraction of what Kael had taken.
The scarred woman from the Vanguard found him near the rift breach. The breach itself had been contained—sealed by a combination of Dawn magic and Arcane Spire techniques—but the violet light still pulsed through the cracks.
"You did well," she said, something like grudging respect in her voice.
"I did what was necessary."
"The Dawn knight. Aldric. He spoke of you, before the end." The scarred woman's jaw tightened. "He said you gave him peace. Said he felt his soul moving toward something... Better."
Kael remembered the man's final moments. The light pouring out of him, warm and clean. The memories of a life well-lived, preserved in fragments that would serve Kael until he himself died.
"I took what he no longer needed."
"You took everything." The scarred woman's eyes were hard. "His power. His memories. His final breath. And you call that taking what he no longer needed?"
Kael met her gaze without flinching. "Would you his soul have dissipated into nothing? Returned to the Abyss, recycled into spiritual energy for some future Awakened to draw upon? At least this way, something of him survives. His skills. His memories. His determination."
"In you."
"In whoever carries the fragments." Kael turned back toward the rift breach. "When I die, those fragments will pass to someone else. And eventually, the chain will break, and his essence will return to the world. That's how it works. Soul Reaving doesn't destroy—it transfers."
The scarred woman was silent for a long moment. "The Order of Dawn would call that heresy."
"The Order of Dawn is welcome to their opinion." Kael's voice hardened. "They can also welcome their own deaths when the next major rift breach occurs and they don't have enough essence to seal it."
He walked away before she could respond.
The battlefield stretched endlessly around him. Smoke rose from burning equipment. The violet light of the rift pulsed overhead. Soldiers moved among the corpses, collecting their dead, preparing for the next battle.
Somewhere in the distance, another horn sounded. Another cultist force, another coordinated attack.
Kael walked toward the sound.
He was halfway to the new engagement when he felt it—a presence behind him. Not hostile, not threatening, but watching. Tracking. Following at a distance that suggested either great confidence or great patience.
He didn't look back. Didn't slow down. Just kept walking toward the sound of combat, the presence trailing behind him like a shadow.
The system notification pulsed.
[Alert: Shadow Court operative detected. Distance: 47 meters. Threat level: Unknown.]
Kael's hands tightened into fists.
The Shadow Court. Deniable assets for Orders that wanted things done without evidence. They operated in the space between legitimate warfare and assassination, and they were very, very good at what they did.
He had a choice. Turn and confront them. Run and see if they pursued. Or ignore them entirely, hoping they were content to watch.
None of the options were appealing.
The presence remained at his back as he reached the edge of the new engagement—a smaller skirmish, cultists attacking a supply convoy that had wandered too close to the front lines. Soldiers in Dawn colors fought desperately against masked figures in black.
Kael waded into the battle without hesitation.
Three cultists fell to his enhanced abilities. Two soldiers went down, their corpses joining the harvestable pile. By the time the engagement ended, Kael had added another thirty vials to his reserves and accumulated fragments from two Dawn soldiers and a Silver Council operative who had apparently been embedded with the convoy.
The Silver Council fragment proved most valuable. Not abilities—information.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted soul essence from the remains. Essence: 4 vials acquired. Memory Fragment acquired.]
The memories painted a picture. Organization charts. Communication protocols. The Silver Council's structure—a network of operatives embedded in every Order, every major city, every significant political faction. They'd been planning this for years. Decades, maybe.
And they'd been watching Kael since before his execution.
The memories showed his file. His abilities. His potential. Recommendations for acquisition or elimination, depending on circumstances.
Current status: Acquisition recommended.
Kael's hands trembled as the implications settled.
He'd been noticed. Flagged. Marked for either recruitment or death. And the Shadow Court operative following him now was probably the one assigned to make that decision.
He turned.
The operative stood thirty meters away, half-hidden by smoke. Humanoid. Weaponless. Watching him with an intensity that felt like cold iron pressed against his throat.
"You've been following me since the battlefield," Kael said.
The operative didn't deny it. Didn't move. Just stood there, patient as stone.
"The Silver Council file mentioned you," Kael continued. "Asset designation: Shadow Step. Specialization: tracking, acquisition, and elimination. You've been assigned to evaluate me for the past three years."
A slight inclination of the head. Confirmation without words.
"And?" Kael demanded. "What's your conclusion?"
The operative stepped forward, emerging fully from the smoke. Their face was hidden beneath a mask of black cloth, but their eyes—pale blue, ancient, infinitely patient—held Kael's gaze without wavering.
"Interesting," the operative said. Their voice was soft, controlled, carrying harmonics that suggested training in psychological warfare. "You've harvested over three hundred vials today. Killed cultists, reinforced Order soldiers, tipped the balance of at least two engagements. And yet you refuse to join any faction. Refuse to be controlled."
"I'm not interested in being anyone's tool."
"Everyone is someone's tool." The operative tilted their head. "The question is whether the tool chooses its wielder or is chosen by them."
Kael felt the corruption spreading through his channels, the accumulated power demanding release. His hands tingled with potential energy.
"I don't have time for philosophy," he said. "If you're here to kill me, do it. If you're here to recruit me, save your breath. I'm not interested."
"I'm here to observe." The operative's lips curved beneath the mask—not a smile, but close. "The Soul Reaver phenomenon is... Unprecedented. The original was contained three centuries ago, their knowledge extracted, their soul used as a power source. The fact that new Reavers can emerge independently suggests either residual echoes in the spiritual ecosystem or deliberate cultivation by forces unknown."
Kael went still.
The original. Contained. Used as a power source.
"What do you know about the original?" he demanded.
"More than you. Less than I need." The operative reached into their cloak and withdrew a small crystal vial, filled with swirling silver light. "This contains coordinates. A location beneath Thornwick where the original Soul Reaver's tomb is located. Their crystallized essence, their preserved memories, their power—all of it waiting for someone capable of accessing it."
"Why would you give this to me?"
"Because I want to see what happens." The operative tossed the vial. Kael caught it reflexively, the crystal cold against his palm. "The cults are accelerating their timeline. The seventy-two rifts will unseal within the month, not the year as originally projected. When that happens, everything changes—including the nature of Soul Reaving."
"You think the original can help stop them."
"I think the original is a weapon that has never been fired." The operative stepped back, melting partially into the smoke. "What you do with that weapon is your choice. But know this: the Shadow Court is watching. The Silver Council is watching. The cults want you dead, and the Orders want you controlled. You have maybe seventy-two hours before someone decisive acts."
The operative's form began to fade, consumed by smoke and shadow.
"The tomb is beneath the old cathedral," they said, their voice growing distant. "Guarded by things that were once human. Bring offerings if you want to survive the approach. The dead have appetites that transcend death."
Then they were gone.
Kael stood alone in the smoke, the vial cold in his hand, the weight of impossible choices pressing down on him.
Seventy-two hours. Seventy-two rifts. The numbers swirled in his mind, a countdown he couldn't stop.
He looked at the vial. The coordinates pulsed with faint light, promising answers, power, knowledge.
He looked at the battlefield. The corpses. The essence still dissipating from bodies that would never be harvested.
He looked at the sky, where the rift still bled violet light, where the Abyss pressed against reality with growing strength.
The system notification pulsed.
[Fragment: Sepulcher Memory (Locked). Location identified. Access possible within three days.]
Kael tucked the vial into his coat, turned toward Thornwick, and began to walk.
Behind him, the presence returned—faint, distant, but definitely there. The Shadow Court operative, watching, waiting, following.
He had seventy-two hours to reach the original Soul Reaver's tomb. Seventy-two hours to unlock whatever secrets lay within. Seventy-two hours before the cults unleashed their coordinated assault on seventy-two rifts across the continent.
And somewhere in that time, he would have to decide what kind of weapon he wanted to be.
The dead lay waiting. The living would soon join them.
Kael walked into the smoke, and the shadows followed.
