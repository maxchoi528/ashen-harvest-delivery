A crossbow bolt hissed past his ear and buried itself in a wooden beam.
Kael twisted down a side passage barely wide enough for his shoulders. The buildings here leaned together like drunks, their upper stories nearly touching. He pressed his back against wet brick and forced his breathing to slow.
Voices echoed off the walls.
"He went this way, Sergeant."

Kael's blood chilled.
Cultists. Staging ground.
The Order knew about the cult's presence and was herding him toward it. Whether he died at the hands of the zealots or got caught fleeing their lair, the result was the same. The Order won either way.
He pressed deeper into the passage, following the fetid trickle of drainage water. The tunnel narrowed, then opened into a forgotten courtyard. Moonlight filtered through gaps in the cloud cover. Dead vines clung to a collapsed stable. Water dripped from a broken gutter.
And there, piled against the far wall, lay the bodies.
Kael counted seven shapes in the darkness. His Reaver senses tingled, reaching out, tasting the spiritual residue clinging to cooling flesh. These had died recently. Within hours. The energy signatures were still fresh.
He approached slowly, aware that anything this convenient could be a trap.
The corpses wore mismatched clothing. Peasant garb, workers' clothes, but their positioning told a different story. They lay in a deliberate semicircle, facing the stable doors. Someone had arranged them with care.
"Amateur," Kael muttered.
A professional kill-squad would have hidden the bodies. These had been left as a message, or maybe bait.
Kael knelt beside the nearest corpse. A woman, middle-aged, her throat opened by a blade so precise it looked like a surgical incision. Her eyes stared at nothing. Blood had pooled beneath her, black in the moonlight, already beginning to thicken.
[System Notification] Corpse detected. Estimated time since death: several hours. Spiritual residue: Strong. Harvest potential: High.
He reached out, hesitated.
The Order of Dawn was somewhere behind him, closing in. Cultists were somewhere ahead, waiting to kill him or worse. And here he knelt in a courtyard full of fresh corpses, about to do something that would confirm every horror story the Orders told about Soul Reavers.
*You're already damned*, he reminded himself. *Might as well get paid.*
His palm pressed against the dead woman's forehead.
The Reaver woke.

It felt like plunging his hand into ice water, then fire, then something older and colder than either. The woman's soul-glow flickered at the edge of perception, a fading ember of identity and power. Kael's fingers closed around it, and the harvest began.

Instructors shouting corrections. The woman had been an Iron Vanguard initiate before she defected, or so the scattered images suggested. She'd run from something. Joined the cult as sanctuary. Died in this courtyard without ever finding safety.
Kael released her and moved to the next body.
A young man, maybe twenty. Bullet wound to the chest, the kind that shattered ribs and pulmonary tissue. He'd been running when he died. Kael could see it in the tension of his muscles, preserved by rigor mortis.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 8 spiritual essence from the remains.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Skill Shard (Keen Hearing) from the remains.]
Another woman, older. Knife wounds. Many of them. Someone had worked her over slowly.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 6 spiritual essence from the remains.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Memory Fragment (Poison Resistance) from the remains.]
Kael worked through the bodies methodically, taking what he needed and leaving what he didn't. The Order's footsteps grew louder in the distance. Time was bleeding away.
The sixth corpse was a man, heavily built, his face a ruin of contusions and broken teeth. He'd put up a fight. His knuckles were skinned raw, and the dirt beneath his nails suggested he'd clawed at stonework before dying.
Kael touched his forehead.
The harvest hit differently.

Images slammed into Kael's mind with the force of a physical blow. He staggered, gasped, and found himself somewhere else entirely.
*A chamber lit by candles that burned without shadows. Figures in robes, their faces hooded. An altar at the center, black stone veined with something that pulsed with sickly light.*
*"The seal weakens," a voice intoned. "The Veil grows thin."*

[Soul Harvest: You extracted Rare Skill Shard (Thousand Cuts) from the remains.]

They knew about him. Not just as a Soul Reaver, but specifically. They were looking for something he carried. Something connected to the Sepulcher tablets.
Kael's hand went to the pouch at his belt, where he kept the fragments he'd collected over months of careful harvesting. Most were useless—memory debris, emotional residue, the detritus of extinguished lives. But one fragment was different. A sliver of black stone, no larger than his thumbnail, that radiated a cold that had nothing to do with temperature.
He'd harvested it from a dying cultist three weeks ago. The man had clutched it like a talisman, dying words forming a single name: *Sepulcher*.
Kael had kept it because it was unusual. Because Soul Reavers collected unusual things.
Now he understood. The cult wanted it back. The Order might not know what it was, but the cult did.
Footsteps at the courtyard entrance.
Kael moved to the seventh and final corpse, a woman who might have been beautiful before death twisted her features. Her death had been violent—a deep gash across her abdomen, her intestines half-spilled from the wound. She'd fought hard before bleeding out.

[Soul Harvest: You extracted Memory Fragment (Organization Structure) from the remains.]

*The squad had scattered. She'd tried to run. Something had caught her, opened her up, left her dying in this courtyard while her squad bled out around her.*

Thousand Cuts. The name alone suggested damage over time, bleeding effects, sustained assault. Kael had picked up skill shards before. Most were minor, useless without a complete set. *Integrate.*
The skill poured into him like ice water finding cracks in stone. His muscles remembered movements they had never made. His fingers understood angles of attack that defied anatomical logic. A technique designed for blades, but adaptable to claws, to broken glass, to anything sharp enough to draw blood.
Thousand Cuts was exactly what it sounded like. One cut meant nothing. Five cuts meant pain. Ten cuts meant bleeding out. A hundred cuts meant slow, agonizing death. The skill didn't add power to individual strikes. It multiplied them. Each wound opened fed the next, channeled energy through the wound-track, ensuring maximum damage from minimal contact.
Kael's hands trembled as the integration completed.

Rank: I (of X)
Effect: Each successive strike against the same target within a few heartbeats increases bleed damage by 15%. Stacks up to 10 times.
Note: This technique was developed by a executioner known only as "The Surgeon." Victims reportedly survived for hours before bleeding out.

Cultists. Kael recognized the robes, the symbols stitched into the fabric. The ones dragging their companion looked young, panicked. The one being dragged was bleeding from a dozen superficial cuts across his arms and face.

From the stable's dark interior came a sound like wet cloth tearing. A shape unfolded from the shadows, impossibly tall, its limbs bending at wrong angles. It moved with a stuttering, insect-like gait, its head rotating in full circle before fixing on the fleeing cultists.
