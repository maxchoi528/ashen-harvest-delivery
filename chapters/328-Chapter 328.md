"Fall back! Fall back to the second line!"
The command dissolved into the roar of cannon fire and the screams of wounded men. Kael pressed his back against a shattered stone wall, breath ragged, fingers slick with blood that wasn't entirely his own. The Ashenmoor Wastes stretched before him like a wound in the world—a landscape of churned mud, burning debris, and corpses that would feed his hunger for days.
Three hours ago, the order had come through: all available Awakened were to reinforce the eastern perimeter. The cult offensive had broken through the Iron Vanguard's lines at dawn, and now waves of Entity-touched fanatics crashed against Thornwick's defenses like a tide of teeth and claws. Kael had answered the call, not out of loyalty to the city or its Orders, but because the battlefield offered what the streets never could.
The dead. So many dead.
"Ashborn!" A voice cut through the chaos. Sergeant Mira Vex, a grizzled woman with iron-grey hair and a scar that bisected her left eyebrow, slid to a stop beside him. Her armor was dented, her spiritual signature flickering with depletion. "We need you on the left flank. The Spire's mages are holding, but barely."
"What's the threat?" Kael asked, already pushing himself upright. His coat hung in tatters, and beneath it, his skin bore the telltale marks of recent combat—cuts that had already closed, bruises that had already faded.
"Three Aspect-class cultists. Awakened, all of them. They're carving through our infantry like wet parchment." Mira's jaw tightened. "Standard Abyssal worshippers, nothing fancy. But there's something else with them. A presence. My gut says Transcendent-class at minimum."
Kael absorbed this. Three Awakened cultists meant three potential harvests, assuming he could get close enough to touch their corpses. The "presence" Mira mentioned was more concerning—a Transcendent-class enemy would be beyond his current ability to harvest meaningfully. Soul Reaver abilities worked best on the dead, and fighting a living Transcendent was a recipe for a quick death.
"I'll handle the Awakened," he said. "You and your people draw the big one away from the infantry."
"Ashborn, those cultists aren't playing games. They'll have wards, protections—"
"I know what they'll have." Kael drew his blade, a simple steel sword that looked laughably inadequate against the spiritual weapons most Awakened carried. Looks were deceiving. His Soul Reaver blade had claimed enough lives to know how to take more.
Mira stared at him for a long moment, then nodded sharply. "Two minutes. Don't be late."
She sprinted away, barking orders to her squad as she went. Kael moved in the opposite direction, picking his way through the rubble and corpses toward the sounds of combat.
The left flank was a charnel house. Infantry bodies lay scattered across the muddy ground, their corpses still twitching in the death-throes of spiritual depletion. A cluster of Order soldiers—Iron Vanguard, from their armor—held a makeshift barricade of overturned wagons and stacked crates, their spiritual signatures flaring with desperate defensive measures. Beyond them, cutting through the defenders with brutal efficiency, three figures in black robes advanced.
Kael counted the corpses as he approached. Seven Iron Vanguard soldiers dead. Two more wounded badly enough that they'd be dead within the hour. The cultists hadn't just killed them—they'd fed on the dying, their Abyssal powers drinking the released spiritual energy like parasites. The ground around them shimmered with residual power, energy that would dissipate within minutes if someone didn't claim it.
Not that anyone else could claim it. Only a Soul Reaver could harvest spiritual energy from the dead, and the Orders would sooner execute a Reaver than let them operate openly.
Kael circled around the combat zone, using the rubble as cover. The three cultists were focused on the Iron Vanguard defenders, their attention fixed on breaking through the barricade. None of them noticed him as he slipped into position behind them.
One of the corpses near the barricade had been an Awakened—a woman in Lieutenant's insignia, her spiritual signature barely fading. Her body lay in a pool of her own blood, eyes staring at nothing, throat cut by what looked like a curved ceremonial blade. Her death had been recent, her spiritual energy still churning within her corpse.
Kael dropped to one knee beside her, ignoring the combat happening just meters away. His fingers touched her cold forehead, and he reached inward with his Reaver senses.
The harvesting sensation was always the same—a hollow ache in his chest, a moment of vertigo as his consciousness expanded to encompass another soul's remnants. He felt the Lieutenant's final moments: terror, defiance, a flash of pain as the blade found her throat. Her skills began to flow into him, her spiritual signature merging with his own.
**[Soul Harvest: You extracted a few hours of residual spiritual energy from the remains of Lieutenant Cira Marsh. Energy acquired: 0.7 standard units. Skill fragment acquired: Iron Wall (Defensive stance technique, degraded quality due to time since death).]**
The notification flickered across his vision, but Kael's attention was already moving. Three harvests per corpse was the limit—the spiritual energy degraded rapidly after the initial extraction, and trying for a fourth would yield nothing useful. He filed away the Iron Wall technique, knowing it would take practice to integrate into his combat style, and rose to his feet.
"Die, heretic!"
The nearest cultist had spotted him. The man lunged forward, his black robe billowing, a curved sword crackling with Abyssal energy materialized in his grip. Kael sidestepped the strike, letting the blade whistle past his ear, and drove his Reaver blade into the cultist's chest.
The man screamed—not in pain, but in horror. The Soul Reaver's touch was a violation that transcended the physical. Kael felt the man's spiritual energy spike in panic, felt the desperate struggle as the cultist tried to pull away from the connection. He held on, letting the harvesting do its work.
**[Soul Harvest: You extracted a few hours of residual spiritual energy from the remains of Cultist Vorn. Energy acquired: 0.9 standard units. Skill acquired: Abyssal Claw (Corrupted strike technique, corrupted quality).]**
The cultist collapsed, his body going limp as Kael withdrew his blade. The man's spiritual signature had been substantial for an Awakened—his death would leave a mark on the local ley lines for days.
The other two cultists broke off their attack on the barricade, spinning to face this new threat. One of them, a woman with wild red hair and eyes that burned with Abyssal light, raised her hands. Shadows coiled around her fingers, forming into shapes that might have been claws or might have been serpents.
"Another Reaver," she hissed. "The Court warned us you might show your face here."
Kael filed that information away. The Shadow Court was monitoring him—the Shadow Court, that shadow organization that operated between Orders, feeding on the gaps in the Concordat. He made a mental note to investigate later, assuming he survived the next thirty seconds.
The red-haired cultist launched her attack. The shadows streaked toward him like living things, seeking to wrap around his limbs and drain his spiritual energy. Kael twisted aside, feeling the wrongness of the Abyssal power as it barely missed him. Corrupted energy always felt wrong, like static electricity crawling across his skin.
He countered with the Iron Wall technique he'd just harvested, dropping into a defensive stance that seemed to materialize a barrier of spiritual energy around him. The shadows struck the barrier and dissipated, their force broken. The cultist's eyes widened in surprise.
"Where did you—" she started.
Kael closed the distance in two strides, his Reaver blade singing through the air. The woman twisted aside, but not fast enough—a line of red opened across her shoulder, and she stumbled back with a cry of pain. Her companion, a heavyset man with a scarred face, moved to flank Kael.
"Stay back, Reaver," the man growled. "We're not like these Order dogs. We know what you are."
"And what am I?" Kael asked, keeping both cultists in his peripheral vision. His Reaver senses reached out, probing the spiritual signatures of the wounded woman and her companion. The woman was still dangerous despite her injury; the man was stronger but slower.
"A parasite," the man spat. "A thief of souls. You think you're above the natural order, taking what should return to the Abyss?"
"I think you're stalling," Kael replied. "Your friend there is bleeding out, and you're talking instead of fighting."
The man's face contorted with rage. He charged, his spiritual signature flaring with power that exceeded what Kael had expected. Not Awakened—Transcendent. Mira had been right about a stronger presence, just wrong about its location.
Kael had maybe two seconds before a Transcendent-class cultist tore him apart.
He didn't waste them on words.
The wounded woman was the weaker target, and she was bleeding. Kael lunged past the charging man, ducking under a swing that would have taken his head off, and slammed his palm against the red-haired cultist's chest. Her eyes went wide as he began to harvest.
**[Soul Harvest: You extracted a few hours of residual spiritual energy from the remains of Cultist Senna. Energy acquired: 1.2 standard units. Skill acquired: Shadow Step (Short-range teleport technique, standard quality).]**
The harvesting took only a heartbeat, but it was a heartbeat too long. The Transcendent cultist's fist caught Kael in the side, driving the air from his lungs and sending him flying backward. He hit a pile of crates hard enough to crack several of them, pain lancing through his ribs.
"Clever," the man said, advancing slowly. Blood dripped from a cut on his forearm where Kael's blade had grazed him. "But not clever enough. The Court wants you alive, Reaver. You're worth more as a specimen than a corpse."
Kael pushed himself upright, tasting copper in his mouth. His ribs screamed protest, but nothing was broken. The harvesting had given him a boost, spiritual energy flowing through his system and accelerating his natural recovery.
Shadow Step. The technique pulsed in his newly expanded skill set, promising a short-range teleport that could close distances or escape threats. He could feel the corruption of Senna's harvested power, but it was manageable—the Soul Reaver's abilities filtered out the worst of the Abyssal taint, leaving something usable.
"Did the Shadow Court also tell you that Soul Reavers don't die easily?" Kael asked, rising to his feet.
The cultist smiled, a horrible expression that showed too many teeth. "They told me you're dangerous. They told me you've killed dozens of Awakened, that you've harvested abilities that should be impossible. They didn't tell me you were arrogant."
"Arrogance implies confidence without cause." Kael settled into a fighting stance. "I have cause."
He activated Shadow Step.
The world blurred, spatial coordinates folding in on themselves for an instant that felt like eternity and a heartbeat simultaneously. Kael reappeared behind the Transcendent cultist, his Reaver blade already in motion. The man was fast—faster than Kael had expected—but fast wasn't fast enough to counter a teleportation strike.
The blade bit deep, severing the man's spinal column at the base of the skull. The cultist's spiritual signature flared with shock and rage, then began to fade as consciousness fled.
**[Soul Harvest: You extracted several hours of residual spiritual energy from the remains of Cultist Draven. Energy acquired: 2.1 standard units. Skill acquired: Blood Fury (Berserker technique, standard quality). Memory fragment acquired: Fragment of Draven's last mission—coordinates, names, objectives. Warning: Fragment is corrupted and incomplete.]**
Kael let the body fall, then stepped back as Iron Vanguard soldiers rushed forward to secure the area. The barricade defenders were cheering, but Kael barely heard them. His attention was focused on the memory fragment he'd just acquired—the first meaningful information Draven had carried.
Coordinates. Thornwick's eastern district, near the old canal network. Names—several cultist operatives he didn't recognize. Objectives—something about "the Third Seal" and "preparation for the Great Unsealing."
The Great Unsealing. Kael's stomach tightened. The 72 sealed rifts between the Material and the Abyss, each containing an Entity that could tear reality apart if released. The cultists weren't just attacking the city—they were working toward something bigger.
"Ashborn!" Mira's voice cut through his thoughts. She ran toward him, her squad following close behind. "The presence I mentioned—it's moving. Toward the city center."
"What kind of presence?"
"Can't tell. My senses aren't strong enough." Mira's face was grim. "But whatever it is, it's big. The kind of big that makes you want to run."
Kael looked toward the center of Thornwick, where smoke rose from a dozen fires and the clash of spiritual weapons echoed through the streets. The Ashenmoor Wastes surrounded the city on three sides, but the battle had spilled inside the walls now. Casualties were mounting. The dead were piling up.
And somewhere in that chaos, something powerful was moving.
"Show me," he said.
Mira led him through streets that had become a warzone. Buildings burned. Citizens fled or fought or died in their doorways. The Six Orders' forces were scattered, responding to multiple threats across the city. The coordinated cult assault had achieved exactly what it was meant to—overwhelming the defenses, stretching the Awakened thin, creating chaos.
Kael's Reaver senses extended outward, probing the spiritual signatures in the area. He felt the exhausted flickers of Order soldiers, the corrupted pulses of cultist forces, the fading echoes of the dead. And beyond all of that, something else. Something vast.
They emerged into a plaza near the city's central fountain, and Kael stopped dead.
The presence was an Entity-class manifestation. Not a full Entity—the Abyssal Entity itself remained sealed in its rift—but a projection of power so concentrated that reality bent around it. The thing looked like a mockery of a human form, a silhouette of darkness and teeth that towered three stories tall. Its eyes burned with cold light, and where it walked, the cobblestones cracked and blackened.
Three Awakened from the Order of Dawn had engaged it. They fought with light-based techniques, their spiritual signatures blazing like beacons against the Abyssal corruption. But they were losing. The Entity-thing moved with terrible speed for its size, its attacks shattering their defenses and sending them flying.
"How is that possible?" Mira whispered, her voice tight with fear. "Entity manifestations require a rift nearby. The nearest sealed rift is miles from here."
"It's not a true manifestation," Kael said, his Reaver senses continuing to probe. What he felt made his blood run cold. "It's a convergence. Concentrated Abyssal energy, pulled from multiple sources and shaped into a form. The cultists are feeding it with every death in the city."
The implications hit him like a physical blow. Every cultist killed, every defender who fell, every citizen who died in the crossfire—they weren't just casualties. They were fuel. The cultists had designed this attack to feed their creation, to give it enough power to accomplish whatever objective they were pursuing.
And Kael couldn't harvest from an Entity-class manifestation. The power was too concentrated, too corrupted. Attempting to harvest from it would burn out his Reaver abilities entirely.
The Entity-thing's gaze swept across the plaza, found the cluster of Order soldiers and Awakened who had gathered to watch, and opened a mouth that shouldn't have existed. Sound emerged—not a roar, but a whisper that filled the entire plaza.
"**Sssssoul Reaver.**"
Every head turned toward Kael.
"**We have been waiting for you.**"
The Entity-thing abandoned its attack on the Order of Dawn fighters, its massive form pivoting to face Kael directly. The pressure of its attention was crushing, a weight that made his knees want to buckle. But Kael had faced worse. He had stood against Zarek's machinations, had walked through Order territory with a price on his head, had harvested from the dying and the dead while the world tried to kill him.
He didn't run.
"What do you want?" he asked, his voice steady despite the fear crawling through his gut.
"**The Reaver's purpose. The original purpose.**" The Entity's whisper carried harmonics that resonated in Kael's bones. "**You carry a fragment of what was. We offer completion. The True Name. The power to unmake the seals. To free those who wait.**"
The True Name. The Sepulcher tablets, recorded by the first Soul Reaver, contained the True Name of something—or someone. Something that could unmake the seals. Something that the Abyssal Entities wanted released.
Kael's mind raced. The cultists weren't just attacking Thornwick for territory or resources. They were sending a message. They were demonstrating power. And they were recruiting—or threatening—him.
"You think I want to free your kind?" he said. "I harvest the dead because it lets me survive. I don't care about your seals or your Entities or your Great Unsealing."
"**Then why do you carry the blade?**" The Entity's form shifted, becoming more defined, more solid. Features emerged from the darkness—features that Kael recognized from his own reflection. "**Why do you walk the path of the Reaver? You did not choose this. You were chosen. By something older than the seals. Older than the Orders. Older than humanity itself.**"
The memory fragment from Draven pulsed in Kael's mind. Coordinates in the eastern district. Names. Objectives. The Third Seal. The Great Unsealing. The cultists weren't just attacking—they were preparing. And this Entity manifestation was part of that preparation.
Mira's hand found his arm, her grip tight with fear. "Ashborn, we need to retreat. Now. Before that thing finishes what it started."
The Order of Dawn fighters were regrouping, preparing for another assault on the Entity. Behind Kael, more soldiers and Awakened were arriving, drawn by the spectacle of the manifestation. If the Entity attacked again, this plaza would become a killing field.
But if Kael ran, the Entity would likely pursue. It had marked him. Wanted him for something.
And somewhere in this city, cultists were executing their true objective—whatever the Great Unsealing meant, whatever the Third Seal represented. Kael had information now. Fragments of a plan that he could piece together.
The Entity waited, its burning eyes fixed on Kael's face.
"**Choose, Reaver. Join the rising tide, or drown beneath it. There is no third path.**"
Kael's hand tightened on his Reaver blade. His ribs still ached from Draven's punch. His spiritual energy reserves were depleted from the harvests. And facing an Entity-class manifestation with whatever power remained in his body was probably suicide.
But the Shadow Court was monitoring him. The cultists were organized, not random. The Ashenmoor War was accelerating, and somewhere in the chaos, answers waited.
He had made choices before that seemed like suicide. He was still standing.
"I'm not joining you," Kael said. "And I'm not running. But I'm going to find out what you're really planning. And when I do, I'm going to stop it."
The Entity laughed—a sound like breaking glass and distant thunder. "**So certain. So naive. The first Reaver thought the same thing, once. Before the burden broke him. Before he became what you now carry.**"
The manifestation began to dissolve, its form losing cohesion as the energy that sustained it dispersed. In moments, it was gone entirely, leaving only scorched cobblestones and the lingering smell of corruption.
But its words remained, echoing in Kael's mind.
The first Reaver. The burden. What he now carried.
He looked down at his Reaver blade, the simple steel weapon that held so much death. Where had it come from? Why did it choose him? The questions had plagued him since his awakening after execution, but they'd taken on new urgency.
"Ashborn." Mira's voice was careful, her tactical mind clearly working through what she'd just witnessed. "The entity—it spoke to you. About the Reaver. About something called the True Name."
"Don't," Kael said, not harshly, but firmly. "I know what you're thinking. Soul Reavers are monsters, right? We steal from the dead, we consort with Entities, we're one step away from joining the cultists ourselves."
"That's not—"
"It is. And it's what everyone thinks. Which means if you report this conversation, I become a target. For the Orders, for the Shadow Court, for whoever else is watching." He met her eyes. "I saved your people on that flank. I'm fighting in this war because the cultists are the enemy. But if you make me an enemy too, things get complicated."
Mira was silent for a long moment. Then she nodded slowly. "I didn't see an Entity manifestation. I didn't hear a Reaver bargaining with Abyssal forces. I saw you engage three Awakened cultists and eliminate them efficiently. That's all."
"That's all," Kael agreed.
He turned away from the plaza, from the gathered soldiers and Awakened who were already spreading rumors about what they'd witnessed. The battle for Thornwick was far from over—the cult offensive continued on multiple fronts, and the coordinated assault had achieved at least some of its objectives.
But Kael had new information. A location in the eastern district. Names of cultist operatives. References to the Third Seal and the Great Unsealing. And a conversation with an Entity-class manifestation that had revealed more about his own nature than he'd learned in the months since his execution.
The first Reaver had carried the same blade. Had faced similar choices. Had been broken by the burden of whatever the Reaver truly was.
Kael wouldn't be broken. He would use the power he'd been given to survive, to fight, and to find the answers that the Entity had dangled before him like bait.
The eastern district. The canal network. Draven's coordinates.
Time to see what the cultists were really planning.
"Ashborn!" Mira called after him. "Where are you going?"
"To finish this war," he said without turning around. "One way or another."
The streets of Thornwick burned around him as he walked toward the next battle.
The question that haunted him, beneath the tactical calculations and the fury of combat: What had the first Reaver become? And was Kael following the same path to destruction?
The weight of his blade felt heavier than it had that morning.
