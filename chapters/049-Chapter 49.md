The cell door clanged open with a sound like a tomb sealing shut. Kael rolled to his feet, muscles coiled, as two armored figures filled the narrow corridor beyond. Torchlight from the wall sconces carved harsh lines across their iron masks.
"Prisoner Ashborn." The taller guard's voice emerged flat, mechanical. "You are summoned."
"By whom?" Kael kept his hands loose at his sides. Three days in Blackgate's deepest pit had taught him nothing except that questions earned silence. But silence killed slowly, and he wasn't interested in either.
"The Warden." The shorter guard checked something on a rusted clipboard. "There's been a... Development. An executioner's error requires correction."
The words hit Kael's gut like cold iron. He'd woken in this cell three days ago with no memory of how he'd arrived, no memory of the trial that supposedly condemned him, just the brand on his wrist marking him as Awakened-proven and the ache in his chest where his soul still trembled from whatever had killed him.
"An error." Kael tested the word. "You mean the first one didn't take."
Neither guard answered. They never did.
Kael followed them through corridors that stank of mildew and old blood, past cells containing shapes he refused to look at closely. Blackgate Prison sat on the bones of a collapsed cathedral, its foundations sunk so deep that the air tasted of burial earth. He'd been processed through the main level—the intake desk, the spiritual assessment chamber, the branding iron that had burned the execution mark into his flesh—but this descent took him somewhere else entirely.
The walls changed from dressed stone to rough-hewn rock. No more torches. Bioluminescent fungi clustered in the cracks, casting everything in sickly green. The temperature dropped with each step, and Kael felt his breath misting despite the exertion of walking.
"Here." The taller guard stopped before a door reinforced with bands of cold iron. "You will enter alone. The Warden waits."
The door swung inward before Kael could decide whether to demand answers. Darkness gaped beyond, deep and absolute. Then something moved in that darkness—a scrape of stone against stone, and a voice like dry leaves blowing across a grave.
"Enter, Soul Reaver."
Kael's blood froze. He hadn't told anyone what he was. He'd barely admitted it to himself, even after waking from death with the ability still burning in his veins.
"Cat got your tongue?" The voice carried a note of amusement. "I've been watching you since you arrived. The way you looked at the bodies in the transfer corridor. The way you touched the wall when they led you past the execution chamber. You weren't mourning, child. You were hungry."
"Who are you?" Kael stepped across the threshold. His eyes adjusted slowly, revealing a circular chamber carved from living rock. At its center sat a figure on a throne of fused bones—an old woman, impossibly old, her skin like parchment stretched over kindling. But her eyes blazed with something that had nothing to do with physical sight.
"I am Warden Morvaine. Seventeen years I've sat in this hole, keeping the King's inconvenient dead. Do you know what Blackgate holds, boy?" She leaned forward, and Kael caught a whiff of something ancient, something that predated the church that had built this place. "Not just prisoners. The bodies of everyone the Crown wishes forgotten. Executed Awakened. Failed assassination targets. Anyone whose soul might be... Repurposed."
"Repurposed for what?"
"The Soul Trade." Morvaine's laugh cracked like breaking pottery. "You think essence just appears in the markets? Someone has to harvest it. Someone has to touch the dead and take what they carry. The Orders pretend their hands are clean, but I've been here long enough to know the truth. The Mortician's Guild takes what they need with permission and ceremony. The rest?" She gestured around the chamber. "The rest come here."
Kael's gaze swept the room. Bodies lined the walls—not displayed, but arranged with careful precision, each one preserved by techniques he didn't recognize. Some wore the tattered remains of fine clothing. Others were naked, marked with ritual symbols that seemed to writhe in the fungal light.
"You're telling me the Crown runs a soul harvest operation out of a prison."
"I'm telling you," Morvaine said, "that you're standing in the largest soul-essence repository in the kingdom. And you, Soul Reaver, are going to help me solve a problem."
"Help you." Kael kept his voice flat. "You kidnapped me, dragged me down here, and now you want cooperation."
"I saved you." The old woman's eyes flared brighter. "The execution order for your second death came through an hour after they threw you in that pit. Someone wanted you dead permanently, boy. No resurrection, no second chances. Just oblivion." She let that word hang in the air. "I delayed the flow. Bought you time."
"For what?"
"To prove you're useful." Morvaine rose from her throne with a sound of ancient joints protesting. She moved to the nearest body—a man in the remains of an Iron Vanguard uniform, his throat a mass of scar tissue. "This one was an Awakened. Third stage, specializing in close-quarters combat. He knew a skill called Blade Storm—one of the rarest in the kingdom. A technique that lets its user strike with weapons imbued with spiritual resonance, bypassing most conventional defenses."
Kael felt something stir in his chest. The Soul Reaver ability pulsed, recognizing what she was offering.
"I can harvest skills?" He kept his voice carefully neutral. "I thought I could only take essence."
"Lesser Reavers take essence. You—" Morvaine's cracked lips split in something like a smile. "You're something different. I've read the old texts. First-generation Reavers could do more than drain. They could extract, preserve, inherit. But the knowledge was lost. Buried with the original practitioners."
"The original Reavers."
"Died out centuries ago. Or so the Orders claim." She pulled a thin blade from her robes and approached the Iron Vanguard corpse. "But you exist. Which means either the Orders lied about their extinction, or someone managed to recreate what was lost." She held the blade out to Kael. "I don't care which. What I care about is that skill. Blade Storm could turn the tide in a conflict that's been brewing for decades. The old rifts are destabilizing. The seals are failing. And when they go, we'll need every advantage we can muster."
"You're asking me to harvest from a corpse. Just like that."
"I'm asking you to harvest from *this* corpse, specifically, because there's a complication." Morvaine moved to a second body—this one fresh, less than a day dead, laid out on a stone table at the chamber's far end. A woman, young, wearing the insignia of the Order of Dawn on her shattered armor. "She's been prepared for standard harvesting. The Mortician's Guild expects her essence within the week. What they don't know—what *no one* knows—is that she was carrying something when she died. Something that shouldn't exist outside of sealed archives."
Kael approached the table. The woman's face was peaceful, almost serene, but her chest bore a wound that spoke of violence. Spiritual combat. Someone had killed her with abilities than steel.
"What is it?"
"Knowledge." Morvaine's voice dropped. "The location of the original Soul Reaver tomb. Supposedly lost for three hundred years. The Orders have been hunting for it since before I was born, and this woman—Agent Celine of the Dawn's Hand—she found it. Or found enough to narrow the search to a single region."
Kael stared at the corpse. His Soul Reaver ability thrummed in his chest, eager, hungry. But something held him back.
"Why tell me this? If that information is so valuable, why not just harvest it yourself?"
"Because I can't." Morvaine's admission came with bitter resignation. "I have sensitivity but not Awakening. I can see the spiritual residue, but I can't touch it. The harvesting itself requires a true Reaver. And until you woke up in my prison with that mark burning on your wrist, I hadn't seen a Reaver in forty years of service."
"You've been watching me."
"I've been *waiting* for you." The old woman moved closer, and Kael caught something in her expression—desperation, maybe, or the weight of secrets carried too long. "The rifts are failing. The Entities are stirring. The Six Orders can barely hold things together, and the Concordat between them is fraying at the seams. When the seals finally break, we won't just face the Abyss—we'll face a war between those who want to control the Entities and those who want to destroy them. Both sides will need Reavers."
"You think I'm going to work for the Crown."
"I think you're going to work for yourself, like every other Awakened who ever lived. But in doing so, you might accidentally help the rest of us survive." Morvaine stepped back. "Harvest the Vanguard soldier. Take Blade Storm. Then harvest the Dawn agent and extract whatever she knew about the tomb. After that, we'll discuss your... Employment options."
Kael looked at the Iron Vanguard corpse, then back at Morvaine. The decision wasn't really a decision at all. He'd been marked for death by forces he didn't understand. He needed power, information, anything that would give him an edge in whatever game had consigned him to this pit.
He approached the soldier's body and knelt beside it. The flesh was cold, waxy, three days dead at least. His fingers hesitated above the chest wound that had killed him.
"Touch the corpse," Morvaine instructed. "Direct contact. The ability won't activate otherwise."
Kael placed his palm flat against the dead man's sternum. The cold bit into his skin, shocking and absolute. For a moment, nothing happened. Then the familiar sensation flooded through him—not the gentle pull of essence extraction he'd experienced before, but something deeper, more complex. Threads of spiritual energy unwound from the corpse's remains, visible now that he knew to look. They carried fragments of memory, emotion, skill.
**Soul Harvest Initiated.**
**Target: Iron Vanguard Corpse (Stage 3 Awakened).**
**Corpse Age: three days. Quality: Moderate.**
**Extracting: Spiritual Essence... Complete. (12 units acquired.)**
**Extracting: Combat Memories... Complete. (Fragmentary.)**
**Extracting: Skill Matrix... Processing...**
The sensation intensified. Kael's vision fractured, showing him flashes of another man's life—the pressure of a sword hilt, the smell of smoke and blood, a face twisted in rage. Then something clicked into place inside his mind, and a new ability unfurled like a flower blooming in darkness.
**Skill Acquired: Blade Storm (Intermediate).**
**Cost: 8 spiritual essence per activation.**
**Effect: Imbue weapons with resonant spiritual energy. Each strike carries a 15% armor-piercing modifier. Critical hits trigger secondary explosive damage.**
Kael withdrew his hand, gasping. His fingers trembled, but not from cold.
"Incredible." Morvaine watched with hungry fascination. "You did it. The texts said it was possible, but to see—"
"How does it work?" Kael flexed his new awareness. The ability sat in his mind like a loaded weapon, waiting. "I can feel it, but—"
"You'll need to practice. Like any skill, it takes time to master." The Warden moved toward the Dawn agent's body. "But you have two more harvests ahead. This one will be... Different."
Kael knelt beside the second corpse. This woman had died more recently—within the last day, judging by the residual warmth still clinging to her skin. Her features were young, barely older than his own, and her expression held none of the peace he'd seen in the Vanguard soldier. Her jaw was clenched. Her hands were positioned as if she'd been fighting even in death.
"She's fresh," Kael noted. "Better harvest quality."
"Yes. But she's also protected." Morvaine gestured to symbols carved into the stone table around the body. "Standard preservation wards, but layered. Someone wanted to make sure her essence couldn't be extracted by conventional means."
"Conventional meaning—"
"Meaning the Mortician's Guild methods. Ritual harvesting. Spiritual resonance at a distance. They're good at what they do, but they're limited by the Law of Conservation. They have to take essence slowly, carefully, or risk corrupting the harvest." Morvaine's smile turned sharp. "You don't have those limitations."
Kael placed both hands on the woman's chest, directly over her heart. The wards flared in response, a cascade of light that washed over his skin without burning. The Soul Reaver ability surged forward, and he felt the protections shatter like glass under a hammer.
**Soul Harvest Initiated.**
**Target: Order of Dawn Agent (Stage 4 Awakened).**
**Corpse Age: several hours. Quality: High.**
**Extracting: Spiritual Essence... Complete. (24 units acquired.)**
**Extracting: Combat Memories... Complete. (Detailed.)**
**Extracting: Skill Matrix... Processing...**
**Extracting: Knowledge Fragments... Processing...**
The harvest hit him harder than the first. Images flooded his consciousness—the woman's last mission, her squad moving through darkness, the ambush that hadn't been an ambush because no one had expected the attack. Kael saw the fight through her eyes, felt her fear when her teammates fell, her rage when the enemy revealed themselves. The Order of Dawn hadn't been fighting cultists or criminals. They'd been fighting *other Awakened*—wearing no insignia, using techniques she didn't recognize.
Then the killing blow, and the strange sensation of a fragmented soul clinging to existence long enough to deliver a final message—
*The tomb is real. Three days' ride from Thornwick, into the Ashfall Mountains. Look for the monastery where the Sun Mother's priests died. The entrance is marked by—*
The message shattered, incomplete. Kael's eyes snapped open.
"What did you see?" Morvaine demanded.
"Something's wrong." Kael pulled back from the corpse, his hands shaking. "She was killed by other Awakened. Not cultists, not criminals. Someone trained in Order techniques."
"The Dawn and the Vanguard have been sniping at each other for years—"
"Not the Vanguard. The techniques were different. I could feel them." Kael struggled to organize what he'd seen. "There was something else in the memory. A voice giving orders. I couldn't see who, but the speaker knew exactly where the squad would be. They were betrayed."
Morvaine's face went pale. "A traitor within the Orders. That's... That's not possible. The security protocols alone—"
"The protocols failed." Kael stood, his mind racing. The Blade Storm skill hummed in the back of his consciousness, but it was this new information that demanded attention. A traitor. A betrayal that led to Agent Celine's death and the loss of crucial intelligence about the original Reaver's tomb. "Someone doesn't want that tomb found."
"Or someone wants to find it first." Morvaine sank back onto her throne, looking every one of her countless years. "The implications are... Staggering. If the original Reaver's tomb exists, if it contains the techniques that were supposedly lost—"
"What kind of techniques?"
"The kind that could break the seal network entirely." Morvaine's voice dropped to a whisper. "First-generation Reavers didn't just harvest skills, boy. They harvested *Entities*. They pulled the Abyssal beings through the rifts and consumed them, growing powerful enough to challenge the Entities directly. That's how the original war was won—not by sealing the rifts, but by hunting the things that came through them."
Kael felt ice crystallize in his stomach. "The Orders don't seal the rifts to protect people. They seal them because—"
"Because if someone learned how to harvest Entities, the balance of power would shatter overnight." Morvaine nodded grimly. "The Six Orders maintain their dominance by controlling spiritual advancement. The Mortician's Guild controls the soul trade. Every faction in this world has a stake in keeping the Abyss contained and the seals intact. But a Reaver who could harvest Entities directly?" She shook her head. "That would make the current power structure meaningless."
Footsteps echoed from the corridor outside. Both Kael and Morvaine froze.
"That was faster than expected." The Warden rose with visible effort. "The guards shouldn't be back for another hour."
The footsteps grew louder. Multiple sets, moving with purpose. Kael counted at least four, maybe five. And something else—a presence he recognized from his harvest, a spiritual signature that carried the unmistakable flavor of Order training.
"We have company," Kael said.
"I see them." Morvaine moved to a panel in the wall and pressed her palm against a hidden mechanism. Stone ground against stone as a section of the floor shifted, revealing a narrow passage descending into deeper darkness. "This leads to the old drainage system. From there, you can reach the river gate and escape the compound."
"You're helping me run?"
"I'm ensuring that the information Agent Celine died for doesn't die with her." The old woman pushed Kael toward the opening. "Someone in the Orders betrayed her. Someone with enough authority to coordinate an ambush and enough knowledge to use Order techniques. That information is more dangerous than any skill you could harvest."
Kael hesitated at the passage's edge. The footsteps were nearly at the door now. He could hear voices—commands barked in military cadence, the scrape of weapons being drawn.
"The Blade Storm skill," he said. "How do I—"
"Focus your spiritual energy through the weapon's edge. The rest will come naturally." Morvaine's hand pressed against his back, forcing him forward. "Go. Now. The passage will seal behind you in thirty seconds."
"And you?"
"I'll buy you time." Something in her voice made him turn. The old woman was smiling, but the expression held no warmth. "I've been dying in this hole for seventeen years, boy. It's about time I did something meaningful with my death."
The door exploded inward.
Kael didn't see what happened next. He was already falling, dropping into the passage as Morvaine's voice rose in words that made his ears ring and his soul shudder. Spiritual energy flooded the chamber above—raw, uncontrolled, the kind of discharge that came from someone burning out their reserves in a single devastating act.
**Soul Harvest: Spiritual Resonance Detected.**
**Warning: Massive energy discharge in progress.**
**Warning: Unknown combat capabilities detected.**
Kael hit the ground running. The passage sealed behind him with a grinding finality, cutting off the sounds of combat and the screams that followed. He ran blind through absolute darkness, one hand trailing along the damp stone wall, the Blade Storm ability pulsing at the edge of his awareness like a heartbeat.
The drainage system was ancient. Pre-cathedral, pre-church, maybe even pre-settlement. The tunnels narrowed and widened unpredictably, forcing him to duck under collapsed sections and climb over debris that might have been bones. Water trickled from somewhere above, cold enough to make his skin prickle.
Twenty minutes. Thirty. He lost track of time in the darkness, driven only by the need to keep moving, to put distance between himself and whatever had followed him into Blackgate's depths.
The passage ended at a rusted grate overlooking a rushing black river. Moonlight filtered through cracks in the ceiling, painting silver stripes across the water. Beyond the grate, the tunnel continued—but the iron bars were old, corroded by decades of moisture.
Kael examined the grate. The metal was weak, eaten through in places. He could force it, but the noise would carry. And somewhere behind him, pursuit might still be coming.
He reached through the bars and touched the river water. Cold. Fast-moving. The current would carry him downstream, toward whatever awaited beyond Blackgate's walls.
A sound echoed from the tunnel behind him. Distant, but unmistakable. Boots on stone.
Kael grabbed the grate and pulled. Metal screamed in protest, but the rust won. The bars came away in his hands, leaving an opening just wide enough to squeeze through. He dropped into the river without a sound.
The current grabbed him immediately, spinning him, pulling him under. Kael fought to surface, but the tunnel was low here, forcing him to swim flat against the water. Stone scraped his back, his arms, his face. He tasted blood and river water and something else—something that made the Soul Reaver ability flare in sudden recognition.
Soul essence. Trace amounts, but present. Someone had died in this river recently.
Kael burst through the tunnel's end into open air. Stars wheeled overhead, unfamiliar constellations in a sky he'd never seen from below. The current carried him toward a distant glow—Thornwick's lights, the city's heart maybe a mile downstream.
He dragged himself onto the riverbank, gasping, shivering. His clothes were soaked, his skin numb, but he was alive. He'd escaped Blackgate Prison.
And he was carrying more secrets than he'd left with.
The location of the original Soul Reaver tomb. The existence of a traitor within the Orders. The knowledge that someone was hunting the techniques that could harvest Abyssal Entities. And now, rising from the water like a half-remembered dream, the implications of what Morvaine had told him.
First-generation Reavers had hunted the Entities themselves. They'd pulled beings from the Abyss and consumed them, growing strong enough to end the war that had nearly destroyed humanity. And somewhere in those techniques lay the power to break every seal, shatter every barrier, unleash everything the Six Orders had spent centuries containing.
Kael stood, water streaming from his clothes. The Blade Storm skill hummed in his chest, eager for use. The harvested memories pulsed with Agent Celine's final mission, her betrayal, her death. And somewhere in the darkness behind him, Blackgate Prison burned with spiritual fire.
The old woman had kept her promise. She'd given him time.
Now Kael had to figure out what to do with it.
He started walking toward the distant lights of Thornwick. He had a tomb to find, a traitor to identify, and a skill to master. And somewhere in the city that never slept, someone was waiting for news of his permanent death.
They were going to be disappointed.
The shadows at the river's edge rippled. Not water. Not wind. Something moved between the darkness and the moonlight, tracking his progress with eyes that held no light of their own. It didn't follow—instead, it fell into step alongside him, matching his pace, keeping perfect distance.
Kael stopped walking.
The shape stopped too. A figure, man-shaped but wrong in proportions, limbs too long, head tilted at an angle that suggested curiosity. Or hunger.
"You've made a mess," it said. The voice came from everywhere and nowhere, sliding through the darkness like oil on water. "The Warden. The agent. The prison. All those little deaths, just to acquire a skill and a rumor."
"Who are you?"
"Someone who's been watching you for a very long time, Kael Ashborn." The figure stepped closer, and moonlight finally caught its features—or the absence of them. A face like smooth stone, featureless except for a mouth that split too wide when it smiled. "Someone who knows what you really are. What you could become."
"I'm just a Reaver."
"You're *the* Reaver. The first in three centuries. The heir to techniques that could reshape this world." The smile widened, impossibly wide, showing teeth that weren't teeth at all. "And I have an offer for you."
Kael's hand drifted toward his belt, searching for a weapon. Any weapon. The Blade Storm skill pulsed again, offering itself, but using it here might bring more attention than he wanted.
"An offer from whom?"
"From those who remember what the world was before the seals. Before the Orders decided humanity needed protecting from its own potential." The figure extended a hand—an invitation, not a threat. "We know where the tomb is. We've known for decades. What we lacked was a key. Someone who could open what we cannot touch."
"And you think I'm that key."
"I think you're the only one who matters." The smile faded, replaced by something almost like respect. "The traitor in the Orders, the one who killed Agent Celine? That's a thread. The tomb in the Ashfall Mountains? That's another. But you're the center, Kael. Everything connects to you now."
"Why tell me this? Why not just grab me and drag me to your master?"
"Because that's not how the game is played. Not anymore." The figure began to retreat, dissolving into the darkness like smoke in wind. "You're being hunted, Reaver. By the Orders who fear what you represent. By the cultists who want to control what you carry. By factions you haven't even learned to name. The only way to survive is to choose your allies carefully."
"And you're claiming to be one of those allies."
"I'm claiming to be an option." The voice came from everywhere now, surrounding him, pressing against his mind. "Find the tomb. Learn what's inside. Then decide whether the Orders deserve your loyalty, or whether you're meant for something greater."
The presence faded. The night fell silent. Kael stood alone on the riverbank, surrounded by shadows and moonlight and the distant glow of a city that held answers he didn't yet know how to find.
Someone had known he was coming. Someone had prepared. And now he had a choice that went beyond escape, beyond survival, beyond simple revenge against whoever had framed him for execution.
The tomb. The traitor. The Entities.
All of it led somewhere. All of it connected to him.
Kael Ashborn, Soul Reaver, started walking toward Thornwick. The night was far from over, and he had the feeling that his troubles were just beginning.
