The Hollow Knight's blade whistled past Kael's ear as he ducked, boots scraping against the slick cobblestones. Three weeks underground in Thornwick's Undercroft, and the stench of rot still caught in his throat.
"Drop the reaver, filth." The cultist's voice echoed through the tunnel, distorted by the tattered mask over his face. Black veins crawled up his exposed neck, pulsing with stolen Abyssal energy. "The Prime wants your tongue for the ritual."
Kael rolled beneath another strike, counting his options. The Knight moved with mechanical precision, each swing a fraction too slow, a fraction too predictable. Enhancement without refinement. Whoever built this thing hadn't understood the soul beneath the armor.
His fingers found the edge of his reaver. The weapon responded instantly, hungry.
"The Orders teach unity," Kael said, circling left. "Wonder what they'll teach you when they find out you've gone hollow."
The Knight snarled. Kael had touched a nerve.
Information asymmetry worked both ways. The cult didn't know he could harvest from the living—not yet. They thought him limited to corpses, a sanitation duty than a weapon.
He'd keep that advantage a while longer.
The tunnel branched ahead. Kael caught a glimpse of symbols etched into the walls, freshly painted in something dark. Warning markers. Territory boundaries. The cult wasn't random scattered victims; they were building something down here, section by section, tunnel by tunnel.
The Shadow Court's presence flickered at the edge of his awareness, a cold pressure he was learning to recognize. His watchers wanted him alive, at least for now. That meant options.
Two more Hollow Knights emerged from the shadows behind the first, masks identical, movements synchronized. A hunting pack. Someone had trained them well.
Kael smiled.
"Big mistake, showing me your numbers."
He lunged not at the lead Knight but past it, shoulder-checking the nearest into its partner. Bodies collided. Swords tangled. In the chaos, his reaver found flesh—a slash across the lead Knight's thigh, deep enough to draw black blood.
No harvest yet. He needed a corpse.
The lead Knight stumbled but didn't fall. Its mask cracked down the middle, revealing milky eyes beneath. Not blind—seeing something else entirely. Something from beyond the Veil.
Kael's soul-sense screamed a half-second before the attack came.
The Knight's hand shot forward, fingers elongated into, each tip glowing with concentrated Abyssal power. Spiritual strike, not physical. Direct soul attack.
Kael twisted. Thesliced across his ribs instead of his chest, tearing through fabric and flesh. Pain lanced through his side, hot and immediate. Blood soaked his shirt.
"Soul Reaver," the Knight whispered, voice shifting to something layered, resonant. Not speaking alone anymore. "We know what you are. The First found your kind once. The original tomb yielded its secrets."
Kael gritted his teeth against the pain. The wound wasn't fatal, but it bled freely. Every second of combat cost him blood, and blood meant weakness if this stretched too long.
He needed to end this quickly.
The two other Knights disentangled themselves, flanking wide. Trapping him. The tunnel behind him offered no retreat— he'd counted six dead ends on his way in. The only exit lay past these three.
The lead Knight pressed forward, claws retracting, preparing another strike. The milky eyes never blinked.
"The Prime has questions," the cultist said. "The Shadow Court will deliver you eventually. Better it ends here, reaver. The Abyss is hungry, but it is not cruel. The Orders—"
Kael's reaver took the Knight's head before the sentence finished.
Black ichor sprayed from the severed neck. The body stood for a heartbeat, then crumpled, mask clattering against stone. The head rolled several feet, mouth still moving, words emerging as wet gurgles before fading to silence.
The other two Knights hesitated. Their synchronized formation broke, uncertainty creeping into their movements. Pack tactics required a leader.
Kael vaulted the collapsing body, reaver raised, and buried the blade in the second Knight's chest. No elegance. Pure momentum and rage.
The third Knight turned to flee.
Kael's reaver spun end over end, a throwing weapon now, and caught the cultist between the shoulder blades. The Knight face-planted into the tunnel's grime, sliding several feet before stopping.
Silence settled over the Undercroft. Water dripped somewhere distant. Kael's blood dripped into the pooling ichor beneath the first body, two fluids mixing in ways that probably meant something terrible if he thought about it.
Three corpses. Fresh, all of them, kills within minutes. Excellent harvest conditions.
He retrieved his reaver from the third body, wiping ichor off on his thigh. The wound in his side throbbed with each heartbeat, a metronome counting down to infection or worse. He needed to close it soon, needed to get out of these tunnels, but first—
Kael knelt beside the lead Knight's headless corpse. The body still twitched occasionally, nerve endings firing in the darkness. Soul energy radiated from the remains, thick and potent, unlike the faded remnants he'd harvested from days-old victims in the upper slums.
His fingers touched the cooling flesh.
The harvest began.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 47 spiritual essence from the remains. +1 Ability Acquired: Soul Sight (Fragmented).]
Kael's vision fractured.
Not physically—his eyes remained open, staring at the corpse beneath him. But something else opened within him, a third sense blooming behind his familiar sight. The tunnel walls shimmered with overlaid images: past layouts, older passages, things that had died here decades ago leaving spiritual residue. He saw the Abyss bleeding through hairline cracks in reality, thin threads of wrongness connecting to each body, to him, to everything.
Soul Sight. The Hollow Knight had possessed a fragment of the ability to perceive beyond the physical. Abyssal enhancement left marks, traces that could be harvested if you knew how to look.
Kael gasped as the vision intensified. The tunnel's history poured through him—thousands of souls passing through these passages, their final moments crystallized in spiritual memory. A woman weeping. A child running. Soldiers marching, uniforms suggesting the Iron Vanguard, hunting something through these passages decades past.
Then: a room. Deep below. Symbols he recognized from the Sepulcher tablets, carved into obsidian pillars. A throne of bones. A figure seated upon it, cloaked in shadows that moved independently of any light source.
The Whisper King.
Kael's heart hammered. The vision showed him only glimpses—nothing concrete, nothing actionable—but the presence was unmistakable. A soul so saturated with stolen power that it left afterimages even in the spiritual residue of an underground tunnel.
The figure turned toward where Kael stood, though in reality Kael stood nowhere near, separated by decades and dimensions. Those eyes—if they were eyes—found him.
"You are not the first," a voice whispered, layered and ancient, speaking directly into Kael's mind. "The Reaver walked these halls. Found the tomb. Found me. Ask the Shadow Court about your predecessor. Ask them why they made you."
The vision shattered.
Kael stumbled backward, free hand pressed against his skull. His nose dripped blood—spiritual overload, too much information channeled too quickly. The wound in his side had opened wider during the vision, fresh crimson joining the dried stains on his shirt.
The other two corpses still lay nearby, cooling. He needed to harvest them too, needed the essence at minimum, but his hands shook too badly to touch them immediately. The vision had drained something from him beyond the spiritual.
He allowed himself thirty seconds of breathing room, back against the tunnel wall, reaver clutched in white-knuckled hands. The Soul Sight lingered at the edges of his perception, flickering like a candle in wind. He could dismiss it—barely—or he could embrace it and see more.
Embrace it. Definitely.
The ability was fragmented, incomplete. The Hollow Knight had possessed a pale imitation, Abyssal energy substituting for genuine spiritual perception. But Kael's Soul Reaver had absorbed the core technique, and with practice, he could develop it further.
Footsteps echoed from the tunnel ahead. Multiple sets, moving with purpose. The pack had sent reinforcements, or maybe this was a second group responding to the combat sounds.
Kael moved.
He harvested the second Knight quickly, roughly, more essence extraction than finesse. The body convulsed under his touch as spiritual energy flooded into him, mending some of the damage from the vision, restoring his reserves.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 31 spiritual essence from the remains. +0 Abilities.]
Thirty-one essence wasn't much, but it stopped the trembling in his hands. The third corpse yielded another forty or so essence, along with something unexpected—a fragment of memory, half-coherent.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 39 spiritual essence from the remains. +1 Memory Fragment acquired: Cultist Deployment Orders.]
Kael paused, the fragment rippling through his consciousness. Orders. Deployment schedules. A map of Thornwick's underground with locations circled in red ink—supply caches, ritual sites, defensive positions.
And a name. Prime Zarek.
The name he'd been chasing since his resurrection. Zarek had framed him for crimes he hadn't committed, had him executed, had turned the entire city against him. The debt between them was blood-deep and unpaid.
But the memory fragment showed something else. Zarek's orders came from above, transmitted through intermediaries Kael didn't recognize. The Shadow Court insignia appeared twice, burned into parchment seals, unmistakable to anyone who'd studied Order hierarchy.
Shadow Court. The hidden hand moving pieces across the board. They monitored him because they had reasons to—reasons connected to his predecessor, to the original Soul Reaver, to whatever had happened in that tomb beneath Thornwick.
Kael filed the information away. The cult activity in Thornwick was organized, not random. Someone coordinated attacks, deployments, ritual sites. Zarek served as a front man, but the real power remained shadowed.
He needed to find the source.
The footsteps grew louder. Kael sheathed his reaver and moved toward a side passage he'd noticed during the vision. The Soul Sight flickered active, showing him the tunnel's true layout beneath the physical appearance—wooden supports rotted decades ago, collapsed sections sealed with rubble, alternative routes the physical eye couldn't perceive.
He chose a path that avoided the approaching group and headed upward.
Twenty minutes later, Kael emerged through a drainage grate into an alley behind a steamwork factory. The night air hit his lungs, thick with coal smoke and the copper tang of distant rain. Above ground, Thornwick's gas lamps cast orange pools of light across wet cobblestones.
His wound had slowed bleeding but hadn't stopped entirely. He needed treatment, rest, time to process what he'd learned. The Undercroft would wait. The cult wouldn't disappear overnight.
A figure stepped from the shadows between two buildings.
"Impressive performance down there." The voice belonged to a woman, sharp and amused. She wore leather armor that absorbed the lamplight, and her face was partially hidden by a hood. "Three Hollow Knights in under five minutes. Most Awakened would have fled."
Kael's hand drifted toward his reaver. "You were watching."
"We always watch, Soul Reaver." She stepped forward, letting the light catch her features. Young—maybe twenty—with silver threading through dark hair. "The Shadow Court appreciates your... Initiative. The cult has become a problem. Too bold, too visible. They're disrupting carefully maintained equilibrium."
"Equilibrium." Kael kept his voice flat. "You mean the balance that keeps you in power while people die in alleys."
"I mean the balance that keeps the seals intact and the Abyss contained." No defensiveness, just fact. "The cult wants to tear everything down. The Orders are too busy fighting each other to notice. You're the only variable they didn't predict—a Reaver who walks his own path."
Kael studied her. Soul-sense showed him a powerful aura, firmly in Ascendant territory, possibly approaching the threshold to Transcendent. She could kill him if she wanted. She hadn't attacked.
"What do you want from me?"
"Nothing tonight. Consider this an introduction. The Court will reach out when we need something specific." She smiled, teeth catching the lamplight. "You need things too, Reaver. Resources, information, allies who won't betray you to the nearest Inquisitor. We're not your enemies. We're... Interested parties."
"Interested in what?"
"In what you might become." Her smile widened. "The original Soul Reaver walked these streets five hundred years ago. The Court has been waiting for a successor ever since. You're late, but the timing could be worse. The seals are failing. The cult grows bolder. The Orders squabble while the Abyss seeps through."
She turned to leave, then paused.
"Three more Hollow Knights patrol the eastern section of the Undercroft. They're protecting a ritual site—something the cult calls the Gateway Preparation Chamber. Prime Zarek visits weekly. If you wanted information about your frame-up, about who really ordered your execution..." She glanced back over her shoulder. "The answers might be worth more than revenge."
She vanished into the darkness between buildings, footsteps silent, presence fading like she'd never existed.
Kael stood alone in the alley, blood cooling on his side, Soul Sight still flickering at the edges of his perception. The Shadow Court had offered him bait—information, alliance, answers to questions he'd carried since his resurrection. Too generous for a simple introduction.
They wanted something. Everyone wanted something.
He checked his wound again. Still bleeding, but slower. He could make it to a safehouse if he moved carefully, could treat himself with supplies he'd cached weeks ago. The next steps required planning, not panic.
Three more Hollow Knights stood between him and the Gateway Preparation Chamber. Zarek visited weekly—this might be his chance to confront the man who'd destroyed his life. Or it might be a trap designed to eliminate him.
The Soul Sight pulsed, showing him ghost-images of what lay beneath the city. The cult had been digging for years, possibly decades. Whatever they prepared in that chamber, it connected to the Great Rift's degradation, to the seals failing across the continent.
The Six Orders existed to prevent that. The 72 sealed rifts held the Abyssal Entities at bay. The cult wanted to unseal them, wanted to tear down the walls between worlds. And somewhere in that chaos, Kael had been given a role he hadn't auditioned for.
Reaver. Successor to someone who'd walked these streets centuries ago.
He needed to understand what that meant. The Sepulcher tablets might hold answers, if he could find someone to translate them. The Shadow Court claimed knowledge about his predecessor. The cult built gates in darkness.
And Inquisitor Vane hunted him through Thornwick's streets, one step behind, patient as erosion.
Kael climbed out of the alley onto the main street, moving with the late-night crowds—drunks, workers heading home, the occasional Awakened passing through with casual arrogance. No one looked twice at a bloodied man. Thornwick saw worse every night.
He headed toward the warehouse district, toward the safehouse he'd established after his third resurrection. Privacy, supplies, walls thick enough to muffle screams if necessary.
Behind him, invisible to normal eyes, the Soul Sight showed him watchers. Not the Shadow Court woman—she'd already faded—but others. Inquisitor's agents, possibly, or cult observers tracking his movements from the Undercroft. Someone had followed him to the surface.
He had maybe two hours before they reported his location to someone who could act on it. Two hours to treat his wound, analyze his harvests, and plan his next move.
The Hollow Knight's ability sat in his mind, half-understood, fragmentary but potent. Soul Sight would let him perceive spiritual energy, see through illusions, navigate the hidden layer of reality that most people couldn't access. With practice, he might perceive the Abyss bleeding through weakened seals, might sense cult rituals before they completed, might even track the Whisper King's movements through the city's spiritual residue.
But the ability was incomplete. The fragment the Hollow Knight possessed was Abyssal-touched, corrupted by contact with the wrong side of the Veil. Kael would need to refine it, cleanse it, make it his own.
He could harvest more Hollow Knights. He could hunt through the Undercroft for better sources. He could follow the Shadow Court's bait and investigate the Gateway Preparation Chamber.
Or he could rest, recover, and approach the situation strategically. Rushing got people killed. He knew that better than most, having died twice already.
The safehouse door opened at his touch, wards recognizing his spiritual signature. Inside, dust motes swirled in the light from a single lantern. The space held a cot, a workbench, medical supplies, and several crates of materials he'd collected over weeks of preparation.
Kael locked the door behind him and began treating his wound.
The flesh wound was ugly but manageable—clean cuts than tearing damage, no signs of Abyssal infection despite the Knight's soul attacks. He stitched it closed with practiced efficiency, ignoring the pain, and bandaged it with strips torn from a spare shirt.
With the physical damage addressed, he turned to what mattered more.
The Soul Sight ability pulsed in his awareness, waiting to be examined. Kael closed his eyes and reached inward, examining the harvest's structure. The Hollow Knight's spiritual pathways had been altered by Abyssal exposure, channels widened and strengthened through contact with raw otherworldly energy. Those pathways were now partially integrated into his own soul architecture.
He could activate the ability at will. The world would shimmer, revealing layers invisible to ordinary sight. Spiritual residue, Abyssal bleed-through, the hidden movements of powerful souls.
But it would cost him. The ability drew on his spiritual reserves, not much, but enough that sustained use would drain him. And the corruption in the original source meant the ability came with occasional intrusions—whispers from the Abyss, half-glimpsed visions of what lay beyond the Veil.
Kael chose to keep it active anyway. Information was survival in Thornwick. Better to see threats coming than to stumble blind into ambushes.
The memory fragment from the third Knight proved more immediately useful. He examined it carefully, parsing the deployment orders for patterns, identifying ritual sites and supply caches. Zarek appeared in three separate communications, always receiving orders than giving them. Someone above the Prime directed cult operations.
The Shadow Court woman had hinted at answers regarding his frame-up. The information existed, scattered through harvests and hidden locations. He just had to find it.
Tomorrow, maybe. Or the day after. He needed rest first, needed to let his body recover from blood loss and spiritual strain. Pushing too hard invited mistakes, and mistakes in Thornwick meant death.
Kael lay down on the cot, reaver within arm's reach, Soul Sight flickering as a passive background process. The safehouse walls blocked most external spiritual energy, but he caught fragments of movement in the streets outside—patrols, watchers, the ordinary chaos of a city that never truly slept.
His mind drifted to the vision of the Whisper King. The tomb beneath Thornwick. The original Soul Reaver who'd walked these streets five hundred years ago. The Shadow Court had been waiting for a successor. The cult sought something in their underground chambers.
Questions multiplied faster than answers. Kael had learned to live with uncertainty—it came standard with resurrection, with the Soul Reaver's curse and its gifts. But tonight, the questions felt heavier than usual.
The wound throbbed dully as he drifted toward sleep. His last conscious thought was of the third Hollow Knight, fleeing before his thrown blade. Fear. The cultists weren't fanatics—they were afraid of something. Afraid enough to enhance themselves with Abyssal power, to attack a Soul Reaver in his own territory.
Afraid of what was coming.
Kael slept.
He woke to pounding on the door.
Not the safehouse door—something distant, muffled, coming from beneath him. The cellar below the warehouse, areas he hadn't accessed since establishing this bolt-hole. Someone had found the hidden entrance, had forced the lock, was currently descending toward whatever he'd left stored in the darkness below.
His reaver was in his hand before his eyes opened fully. Soul Sight activated, showing him the building's spiritual layout—three figures in the cellar, their auras burning with cold determination. Awakened, all three, stages difficult to determine through the floorboards but definitely not common criminals.
And a fourth figure, waiting outside. Stage three, possibly approaching four. Aura sharp and hunting, the particular signature he'd learned to recognize from reports and rumors.
Inquisitor Vane.
Kael's blood ran cold. The Inquisitor had found him. Not through cult reports or Shadow Court intelligence—through his own investigation, his own trail of spiritual residue and careful deduction. The man hunting him across Thornwick had finally caught up.
The pounding continued. Not at his door—at the cellar entrance, systematic, professional. They'd cleared the first chamber and were working toward the second.
He had maybe two minutes before they reached the main storage area, before they found the evidence of his presence and traced it upward to this room. Running meant abandoning supplies he'd spent weeks collecting. Fighting meant facing an Inquisitor far above his current stage, in a building with limited exits and no backup.
The window offered escape. Three stories down to the alley, dangerous but survivable if he landed correctly. Behind him, the Inquisitor waited with the patience of someone who'd tracked prey across continents.
Kael moved toward the window.
The glass shattered outward. He jumped, tucking into a roll, and hit the alley cobblestones hard enough to drive breath from his lungs. Pain lanced through his stitched wound. He kept moving anyway, rolling to his feet and sprinting into the maze of warehouse alleys.
Behind him, a voice called out—calm, measured, terrifyingly close.
"The Soul Reaver escapes again. You'd think he'd learn."
Kael ran faster.
The night swallowed him, gaslight and shadow, steam and smoke, the heartbeat of a city that fed on the desperate. His wound screamed with each stride. The Soul Sight flickered, showing him pursuit patterns, the Inquisitor's agents moving to cut off exits, Vane himself descending from the roof with impossible grace.
The trap closed.
Kael skidded to a halt at the alley's end, confronted by two Awakened agents in Inquisitor grays. Behind him, Vane's footsteps approached with the unhurried confidence of a predator that knew its prey had nowhere left to run.
"Resisting arrest this time, Ashborn?" One of the agents drew a blade wreathed in Order-crafted fire. "The charges are mounting."
Kael's mind raced through options. Fighting three Awakened including a stage-three Inquisitor would be suicide. Running would delay but not escape. The Soul Sight showed him a narrow gap between the two agents, a moment of vulnerability in their formation.
He took it.
Kael's reaver carved a silver arc through the fire-blade, shattered the enchantment, and sent the agent stumbling backward. He ducked under the second agent's counterstrike, shoulder-checked the man into the wall, and burst through the gap before either could react.
Behind him, Inquisitor Vane laughed.
"Interesting. You learn faster than expected."
Kael didn't look back. He ran.
