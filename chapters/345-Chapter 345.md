The siege lines at Greymoor fell in the third hour.
Kael heard the collapse from the ridgeline where he'd positioned himself, a sound like the world tearing at its seams. Stone and mortar gave way in a cascade of dust, and the Abyssal roar that followed drowned out the screaming of men still trapped within the fortress walls.
"Pull back!" Commander Vex of the Iron Vanguard bellowed, her voice cutting through the chaos. "Second and Third Companies, withdraw to the secondary defensive line! The rift's widening!"
Kael didn't move. He stood among the skeletal oaks that had witnessed a hundred such sieges, watching the green-black wound in reality pulse above the ruined fortress. The Veil was tearing. He could feel it in his marrow—that wrongness that had haunted him since he'd first Awakened to this nightmare.
Three months ago, he'd been a death-row prisoner awaiting execution in Thornwick's deepest dungeon. Now he stood on the front lines of a war that spanned the entire continent, the air thick with the copper tang of blood and the ozone sting of fractured spiritual energy.
The notifications flickered at the edge of his vision, as they always did when Entities drew close to the Material plane.
[WARNING: Spiritual anomaly detected. Rift stability at 34%. Entity designation: CLASSIFIED. Recommend evacuation.]
Kael dismissed the warning with a thought. The Orders could issue all the alerts they wanted. They couldn't evacuate the dead.
Bodies littered the approach to Greymoor—soldiers from both sides, their blood soaking into soil that had drunk deep of war for generations. The Ashenmoor Wastes earned their name from more than ash. Every step Kael took forward churned up fragments of bone and corroded metal.
"Soul Reaver!" A voice, ragged with exhaustion and fear, cut through the battlefield's din. "You mad bastard, get clear!"
Kael glanced back. A squad of Iron Vanguard soldiers had formed a defensive perimeter around a mortar team, their spiritual energy flaring in defensive formations. One of them—a young sergeant with sergeant's stripes burned onto his collar—stared at Kael with naked terror.
The Soul Reaver designation. Word had spread fast after Kael had carved his way through the Thornwick riots, harvesting abilities from cultists and leaving their hollow shells for the Orders to find. They called him a monster. They weren't entirely wrong.
"The rift will tear regardless of where I stand," Kael called back. "Save your fear for what comes through."
He turned back to the fortress. The breach pulsed again, and this time something pushed through—a limb of shadow and malice, jointed wrong, fingers too long and too many. The Entity was forcing itself into the Material, disregarding the membrane's resistance.
Across the battlefield, bugles sounded. The Horned Order's flanking force had arrived, their cavalry charging from the eastern woods with lances of crystallized spiritual energy. They struck the cultists who'd been maintaining the rift—a coordinated assault designed to collapse the breach by eliminating its anchors.
Kael watched the battle unfold. The cultists fought back with the desperate fury of the faithful, their bodies contorting as they channeled Abyssal power they barely understood. Two Horned Order knights went down in the first exchange, their armor breached by corrupted blades.
More bodies for the harvest.
He moved forward, keeping low as spiritual projectiles arced overhead. The battlefield was a maze of corpses and debris, each step taking him closer to the rift and the slaughter surrounding it. A fallen Iron Vanguard soldier lay twenty paces ahead, her hand still clutching a shattered shield. Kael's boots squelched in mud that was more blood than water.
The soldier had died with her eyes open. Kael knelt beside her, ignoring the way her blood soaked through his trousers, and pressed his palm flat against her chest.
Physical contact. Always physical contact. The Soul Reaver ability didn't work otherwise.
Cold flowed into him. Not temperature—something deeper. The last moments of the soldier's life cascaded through his consciousness: the cultist blade punching through her shield, the surprise that it hadn't hurt as much as she'd expected, the ground rushing up to meet her face.
And beneath that, buried in her dying breath, the fragments of who she'd been. Her name had been Mira. She'd been twenty-six. She'd joined the Iron Vanguard to escape a marriage her father had arranged, and she'd spent eleven years climbing from common soldier to squad leader.
Kael held those fragments as they dissolved, letting them pass through him while the Soul Reaver's hunger pulled at what mattered.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Physical Conditioning (Awakened-tier) from the remains. Corpse freshness: a few hours. Harvest quality: 78%.]
[Soul Harvest: Residual spiritual energy absorbed. +3 to personal reserves.]
The ability settled into his mind like a new tool in a workshop, waiting to be used. Physical Conditioning wouldn't make him stronger or faster, but it would let him push his body further before exhaustion set in. On a battlefield like this, that meant more chances to harvest before he had to withdraw.
Mira's eyes stared at the grey sky. Kael closed them with his free hand, a gesture that meant nothing and everything.
"Keep moving, Ashborn!" Commander Vex's voice again. She was somewhere behind him, coordinating the retreat. "The rift's destabilizing! Everyone clear!"
Kael rose. The Horned Order cavalry had broken through the cultist lines, and their knights were now hacking at the ritual circles that held the rift open. Each circle they destroyed sent pulses of reflected energy washing across the battlefield, staggering soldiers on both sides.
The ground trembled.
Kael staggered, catching himself on Mira's corpse. The tremor wasn't from the cavalry's assault—it came from beneath, from the deep places where the Veil was thinnest. Something else was stirring.
A second rift opened two hundred meters to his left, a vertical tear in reality that belched forth shadows and the shriek of things that had no names in human tongues. Cultists had been hiding in the ruins of an old watchtower, and they scrambled to flee as the breach expanded.
But the rift wasn't stabilizing. It was tearing wider, and faster than the one above Greymoor.
[CRITICAL WARNING: Multiple rift formation detected. Spiritual energy signatures indicate coordinated ritual activity. Seal network integrity: compromised.]
Kael understood then. The attack on Greymoor wasn't just about the fortress. The cultists had used the battle as cover, drawing the Orders' attention while they worked to destabilize the seal network itself.
Seventy-two sealed rifts. Seventy-two Abyssal Entities, each one a threat to reality if they broke free. The Orders had spent centuries maintaining the seals, and the cultists were trying to collapse them all at once.
"Fall back!" someone screamed. "The seals are failing!"
Chaos erupted across the battlefield. The Iron Vanguard soldiers abandoned their defensive positions, running toward the ridgeline. The Horned Order cavalry wheeled their mounts, abandoning the assault on Greymoor to cover the retreat. And above it all, the rifts continued to widen, their edges crackling with energy that made Kael's teeth ache.
He didn't run. Running meant abandoning the corpses still scattered across the Ashenmoor Wastes, and corpses were opportunity.
The watchtower's ruins collapsed as the rift there reached critical mass. Stone and timber fell inward, crushing the cultists who'd been too slow to flee. Kael moved toward the rubble, his boots finding purchase on debris that shifted under his weight.
A cultist had been pinned beneath a fallen beam. She was alive, barely—her legs crushed, her robes soaked with blood that steamed in the cold air. Her eyes found Kael's, and he saw recognition there. Not of him specifically, but of what he was.
"Soul Reaver," she spat. "The Orders will hunt you. The Court will—"
Kael pressed his palm to her forehead. Her spiritual energy flared in protest, but she was too injured to resist, and he was too hungry.
The harvest was different this time. The cultist had been deeply connected to Abyssal power, and that corruption flowed into him alongside her memories and abilities. He saw ritual chambers filled with candles and blood. He saw meetings where hooded figures discussed timelines and coordinates. He saw a map marked with seventy-two points, each one pulsing with malevolent light.
The cult's mass unsealing. It was real, and it was happening now.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Ritual Binding (Entity-tier) from the remains. Corpse freshness: several minutes. Harvest quality: 94%.]
[Soul Harvest: Memory fragment acquired. Warning: Information may be corrupted by Abyssal influence.]
The ability that settled into his mind was dangerous—not because of what it could do, but because of what it meant. Ritual Binding let him create and maintain spiritual connections, anchors that could hold reality in place or tear it apart. With seventy-two rifts threatening to open, such an ability could prove invaluable.
Or catastrophically lethal, if he used it wrong.
The cultist died beneath his hand, her last breath a wheeze that carried no words. Kael rose, scanning the battlefield for his next target.
The Horned Order cavalry had reached the ridgeline, their commander rallying survivors into defensive formation. Commander Vex was visible among them, her spiritual energy blazing as she organized a rear guard. They were abandoning the field, leaving the dead and dying to whatever fate awaited them.
Leaving Kael alone among the corpses.
The rifts continued to pulse, but the cultists' assault had lost coordination. Without the ritual circles to stabilize them, the breaches were expanding erratically—some tearing wider, others beginning to collapse. The Entities trying to push through found themselves caught in dimensional currents that spun them back toward the Abyss.
A temporary reprieve. The Orders would send more forces, seal what they could, and hunt down the remaining cultists. By morning, the battle would be written up in reports as a victory—the rifts contained, the Entity incursion repelled.
Kael knew better. The cultists had proven their capability. They could crack the seals. They had the resources and the personnel to target all seventy-two rifts simultaneously. What had happened at Greymoor was just the beginning.
The notification pulsed again, and this time it carried information Kael hadn't expected.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION: Six Orders coordination detected. Order of Dawn reinforcements en route. Iron Vanguard defensive protocol activated. Horned Order cavalry providing rear guard. Arcane Spire containment specialists deploying. Veil Watchers establishing observation posts. Silver Covenant medical corps responding.]
Six Orders. Working together, But imperfectly, to respond to the crisis. Kael had known intellectually that the Orders existed, that they were supposed to maintain the seal network and fight the Abyssal Entities. But seeing them move in concert, coordinating across miles of battlefield through means he couldn't perceive—it drove home the scale of what was happening.
This wasn't a skirmish. This was a war, and the front lines stretched across the entire continent.
A shout drew his attention to the eastern woods. A figure emerged from the treeline—armored, moving with the deliberate grace of an Awakened combatant. The armor was black, marked with silver sigils that Kael recognized from Order insignia. But the symbol on the breastplate wasn't one he knew: a serpent eating its own tail, encircling an eye that wept tears of blood.
The Shadow Court.
Kael's hand moved to his belt, where a blade of blackened steel hung in its sheath. The weapon wasn't much—standard issue for death-row prisoners in Thornwick's dungeons—but it had served him well since his Awakening.
The figure stopped fifty paces distant, raising one hand in a gesture of parley. The armor's helm retracted, revealing a face that was startlingly human beneath the ceremonial metal. Young, maybe twenty-five, with close-cropped dark hair and eyes that held the weight of too many secrets.
"Kael Ashborn," the figure said. The voice was female, cultured, carrying the accent of Thornwick's upper districts. "The Soul Reaver. I've been looking for you."
"Congratulations on your success," Kael replied. "State your business."
The woman's lips curved in something that wasn't a smile. "Direct. Good. The Shadow Court has an offer for you."
"I don't work for Orders."
"This isn't the Orders. The Shadow Court operates outside the Concordat, unbound by their treaties and their hypocrisy. We know what you are—what you can do. The cultists who attacked today, they're amateurs. True believers channeling power they don't understand. But you..." She spread her hands. "You could tip the balance. Tip it toward survival, if you're willing."
Kael studied her, reading the subtle tells that most people missed. The way she held her weight favored her left leg. The slight tremor in her extended hand that suggested she'd recently expended significant spiritual energy. The sigils on her armor pulsed with defensive formations that were already half-depleted.
She was alone, which meant this was either genuine negotiation or an assassination attempt that she didn't expect to survive.
"Survival for whom?" Kael asked.
"Everyone. The cultists want to open the rifts and let the Entities through. They believe they can control what emerges. They can't. The Orders want to seal everything and pretend the Abyss isn't leaking into our world. They can't do that either. The Shadow Court stands between, offering alternatives that neither side wants to consider."
"Alternatives like harvesting the Entities themselves."
The woman's expression flickered—surprise, quickly masked. "You know about that?"
"I know the Law of Soul Conservation. Energy can't be created or destroyed. If the Entities are made of spiritual energy, and the seals are weakening because the Veil is draining, then the solution isn't to push the energy back. It's to take it." Kael stepped forward, closing the distance between them by ten paces. "That's what you're really offering. A chance to harvest Abyssal Entities before they fully manifest. Turn the crisis into an opportunity."
"You're not what the reports suggested." The woman's voice had lost some of its certainty. "They said you were a brute. A scavenger working from instinct."
"Reports are written by people who've never faced me."
Silence stretched between them. Behind the woman, the rifts continued their chaotic dance, bleeding shadows into the Material plane. The Horned Order cavalry had completed their withdrawal, and the ridgeline stood empty except for the fires still burning among the abandoned siege equipment.
"The Shadow Court can offer you resources," the woman said finally. "Safe houses. Information about cult movements. Access to soul-essence markets that don't ask questions about provenance. In exchange, we ask only for cooperation when our interests align."
"Define 'cooperation.'"
"Occasional assistance. Intelligence sharing. Nothing that would require you to betray genuine allies." The woman's eyes flickered to the corpses still scattered across the battlefield. "Nothing that would deny you access to your... Source material."
Kael considered. The Shadow Court operated in shadows, yes. They were deniable assets for Orders that wanted to violate the Concordat without leaving evidence. But they also had information he needed—information about the cult's structure, their targets, their ultimate goals.
And he needed allies. The Soul Reaver ability made him powerful, but it also made him a target. The Orders would hunt him as long as they believed he was a threat to their monopoly on spiritual energy. The cultists would try to control him or destroy him. Only the Shadow Court seemed willing to treat him as a resource than a monster.
"I'll consider your offer," Kael said. "But I have conditions."
"Name them."
"Full access to your intelligence network regarding the cult. I want to know where they're striking next, what their ritual protocols are, how they're bypassing the seal network. And I want a contact—someone who can be reached when time is critical."
The woman nodded slowly. "Those terms are... Aggressive. But not unreasonable. I'll need to consult with my superiors."
"Then consult quickly. The cult won't wait."
A new rift tore open to their right, close enough that Kael felt the spiritual pressure like a hand closing around his throat. Shadows poured forth, coalescing into a shape that hurt to look at—a mockery of human form, limbs that bent in directions they shouldn't, a face that was no face at all.
The Entity had fully manifested.
Kael moved without thought, his body flowing into combat stances he'd learned from harvested memories. The blackened blade sang free of its sheath, spiritual energy channeling through metal that had never been designed to carry such power.
The Entity lunged.
Kael sidestepped, his harvested Physical Conditioning keeping his body responsive despite the exhaustion of the battle. The blade sliced across the creature's flank, drawing a sound like tearing metal. The Entity shrieked, its voice frequencies that scraped against his sanity.
[Soul Harvest: Opportunity detected. Entity in weakened state. Harvest viable with direct contact.]
He didn't have the luxury of hesitation. Kael closed the distance, driving his blade into the Entity's center mass while pressing his free hand against its churning surface. Cold burned through him—the Entity's spiritual energy was alien, corrupted, nothing like the human corpses he'd harvested before.
But it was still energy. And energy could be taken.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Abyssal Corruption (Entity-tier) from the remains. Entity stability: collapsing. Harvest quality: 67%.]
[Soul Harvest: Warning: Entity-tier corruption absorbed. Spiritual contamination risk elevated.]
The Entity dissolved around him, its form unraveling as its stolen energy fled. Kael stumbled back, his hand still burning with the contact. The corruption he'd absorbed sat in his mind like a splinter, foreign and wrong, waiting to be either purged or integrated.
"That was reckless." The Shadow Court woman's voice cut through his disorientation. She'd drawn her own weapon during the fight, though she hadn't intervened. "Entity-tier energy isn't meant for human vessels."
"Few things about me are meant for human vessels." Kael straightened, testing his balance. The corruption pulsed in his chest, neither spreading nor retreating. "Did you learn anything from that display?"
"I learned that the reports undersold you significantly." The woman sheathed her blade. "I also learned that your offer of conditions may need revision. If you can harvest Entities directly, your value to the Shadow Court increases considerably."
"Is that why you didn't help? To assess my capabilities?"
"That, and because interference in Entity manifestation is inadvisable without proper preparation." She produced a small crystal from her belt pouch, holding it up to display the rift still pulsing in the distance. "But I recorded what I could. Our analysts will extract useful data."
Data. Always data, in this war of information and power. Kael sheathed his own blade, acknowledging the tactical reality.
"The offer stands," the woman continued. "Shadow Court contact will reach you within three days. Until then, I suggest you evacuate the area. The Orders' containment specialists will arrive soon, and their response to a Soul Reaver harvesting Entities on an open battlefield is... Unpredictable."
She turned to leave, then paused. "One more thing. The cult's next target is likely the Thornwick Seal—the oldest and most stable of the seventy-two. If it falls, the entire network destabilizes. You have friends in that city. Consider whether their safety matters to you."
The woman walked into the eastern woods, her armored form vanishing among the skeletal trees. Kael watched until he couldn't see her anymore, then turned his attention to the battlefield.
The rifts were collapsing, one by one, as the cultists who'd maintained them were killed or driven off. The Entities that had partially manifested were retreating back through the breaches, their grip on the Material plane failing without ritual support. By dawn, the Orders' seal-keepers would arrive to reinforce what remained.
But the cult had proven their point. The seals could be broken. The Veil could be torn. And seventy-two Abyssal Entities were waiting for their chance to pour through.
Kael surveyed the corpses scattered across the Ashenmoor Wastes. The battle had been costly for both sides—hundreds dead, maybe thousands if the Iron Vanguard's field hospitals were overwhelmed. All that potential energy, dissipating into the earth.
He knelt beside the nearest body, a young soldier whose face was peaceful in death. His hand pressed against the cooling flesh.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Blade Mastery (Awakened-tier) from the remains. Corpse freshness: several hours. Harvest quality: 61%.]
The ability slotted into place alongside the others he'd collected. Physical Conditioning. Ritual Binding. Abyssal Corruption. Blade Mastery. Each one a tool, each one a risk, each one bringing him closer to whatever power he was building toward.
The Soul Reaver had given him this gift—or curse. He would use it as long as he drew breath, harvesting from the dead and taking what he needed to survive. The Orders could call him a monster. The cult could call him an abomination. The Shadow Court could call him an asset.
None of it changed what he was. A dead man walking, surrounded by the freshly killed, building an arsenal of stolen power one corpse at a time.
He stood, moving to the next body, then the next. The night wore on, and the harvest continued.
* * *
Dawn found Kael on the ridgeline, watching the Orders' containment specialists work below. They moved in coordinated patterns, their spiritual energy weaving nets that would stabilize the damaged seals. Observers from the Veil Watchers recorded everything, their eyes glowing with the power to perceive spiritual phenomena invisible to ordinary sight.
The Shadow Court woman had been right about one thing. The Orders' response to a Soul Reaver harvesting on an open battlefield would be hostile at best. He could already see the looks being cast in his direction—suspicion, fear, the calculation of threat assessment.
Time to leave.
Kael descended the ridgeline's far slope, away from the containment zone and the watchful eyes of the Orders. The Ashenmoor Wastes stretched before him, a wasteland of old battles and older bones. Somewhere beyond them lay Thornwick, and beyond Thornwick lay the Thornwick Seal—the oldest of the seventy-two.
The cult would strike there next. He had three days to prepare, three days to gather what intelligence he could, three days to decide whether the Shadow Court's offer was worth accepting.
His hand still tingled with the corruption he'd absorbed. The Entity's energy sat in his chest like a second heartbeat, alien and patient. He would need to deal with that eventually—either purge it through some means, or learn to wield it alongside his other harvested abilities.
The notification pulsed.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION: Soul Reaver advancement available. Current stage: Demigod (Stage 5). Progression to next stage requires harvesting of Demigod-tier or higher remains. Estimated requirement: 3 additional Demigod-tier harvests, or 1 Sovereign-tier harvest.]
Kael absorbed the information. Stage 5 of 6, with only the Divine standing between him and whatever lay beyond. The growth curve that governed his advancement had been laid out since his Awakening, each stage bringing new capabilities and new costs.
He wasn't ready to advance. Not yet. The corruption in his system needed to be addressed first, and he needed more information about what the higher stages entailed. The Soul Reaver's origin remained a mystery, one that the Sepulcher tablets might eventually solve.
But soon. The war was accelerating, the seals cracking faster than the Orders could repair them. When the crisis reached its peak, he would need every advantage he could harvest.
Kael walked toward the rising sun, leaving the battlefield and its dead behind. The Ashenmoor Wastes stretched endless before him, scarred by centuries of conflict, soaked with the blood of generations. Somewhere ahead, the cult was preparing their next strike. Somewhere behind, the Orders were scrambling to contain damage they didn't fully understand.
And somewhere in the shadows, the Shadow Court was watching, waiting to see which way he would fall.
He would give them an answer. When the time came.
But not yet.
The notification pulsed one final time, a reminder of what mattered most.
[Soul Harvest: 17 corpses processed. Total energy acquired: 847 units. Abilities integrated: 4. Corruption absorbed: 1 (Entity-tier). Status: Stable.]
Seventeen corpses. Four abilities. One piece of Entity corruption that would either kill him or make him stronger.
Kael smiled, though the expression held no warmth. The war was just beginning. And he was already hungry for more.
Behind him, the Ashenmoor Wastes fell silent, the dead waiting for the next harvest, the living oblivious to what was coming.
Ahead, the road stretched toward Thornwick and the battle yet to come.
