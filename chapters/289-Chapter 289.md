*Kael Ashborn*, they'd screamed. *The Soul Reaver walks!*

Seven locations. Seven marks.

*THE SEVENTY-TWO WAIT.*

Five thousand sovereigns. Enough to buy a small estate. Enough to fund an army.
"I'm aware the Orders want me dead."

"Nothing you aren't already doing. Continue hunting the cult. Continue breaking their operations. But understand this—" She leaned closer, rain dripping from her dark hair. "The cult isn't just random fanatics. They're organized. Funded. Protected by people inside the Orders themselves."

"Collaborators. Idealogues. People who believe the Entities should be released, that humanity has been shackled by false gods and weaker beings." Sera's expression flickered—something that might have been hatred, might have been fear. "The Shadow Court has existed for two hundred years specifically to combat this threat. When those seven seals break simultaneously, the Veil will tear. The Entities will pour through. And Thornwick—along with everyone in it—will cease to exist."

"Because the Shadow Court has watched you for two years, Ashborn. We've studied your methods, analyzed your capabilities, debated your potential value." She glanced back over her shoulder. "We know what you are. What you can become. And we know that if the Entities break free, nothing else will matter."

*Seven seals. Six days. The world ending.*

Instead, his gaze drifted back to the dead man.
The Soul Reaver's hunger stirred—not demanding, but patient. There might be more to harvest. Deeper memories. The cultist's handlers, his contacts, the identity of whoever coordinated this vast conspiracy.
Kael knelt once more. Pressed his palm against the cooling flesh.
And let the darkness take him.
