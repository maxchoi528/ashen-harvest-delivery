Kael's blade sang through the air, shearing through the shadow-thing's throat. It dissolved into oily smoke before hitting the cobblestones.
"Two down," Lyra whispered from behind him, her daggers slick with something that wasn't blood. "But there are more in the warehouse. I can feel them gathering."
The Fogmire District had become a killing field overnight. Three days ago, the Abyssal creatures had been lone predators, hunting in secret. Now they moved in packs, coordinated, almost military in their formations. Something had changed. Something had organized them.
"We take what we can and we leave," Kael said. He stepped over the dissolving remains of the creature he'd killed, heading for the warehouse entrance. The smell hit him first—rot and copper and something chemical, like alchemical reagents left too long in the sun.
The door hung crooked on its hinges. Inside, moonlight filtered through gaps in the roof, illuminating a scene that made Kael's stomach clench.
Bodies. A dozen of them, maybe more. Gang members by their tattoos, but they hadn't died from the shadow creatures outside. These wounds were precise. Surgical. Someone had executed them with professional efficiency, then arranged the corpses in a rough circle around a symbol painted on the floor in the victims' own blood.
"Soul anchor," Lyra breathed. "They're using death energy to power something."
Kael crouched beside the nearest corpse. The man's eyes stared at nothing, his throat opened in a single clean stroke. Kael placed his hand on the cooling flesh and reached with his awareness.
The Soul Reaver stirred.
This was the part that never got easier. The sensation of his ability unfurling felt like reaching into cold water, like plunging his hand into a fire that didn't burn. The man's residual essence responded to Kael's touch, drawn upward through his palm, into his own being where it belonged.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 23% spiritual energy and a Memory Fragment from the remains. Energy absorbed.]
The memory came in fragments. Running through fog-thick streets. A voice giving orders. Faces painted white, wearing masks that looked like bleached skulls. They had come without warning, these pale-faced killers, and they had killed everything in their path—gang members, civilians, even the shadow creatures that tried to intervene.
Kael pulled his hand back, leaving the corpse empty.
"The cultists did this," he said. "Not the gangs. Not the shadow things."
Lyra nodded slowly. "Using the gang war as cover. Killing witnesses. Making it look like our kind of violence so nobody asks questions."
"Smart."
"Efficient," she corrected. "Cultists are never smart. They're organized."
She was right, and that bothered Kael more than he wanted to admit. The Abyssal cults he'd encountered before were fanatics—violent, yes, but undisciplined, prone to sacrificing themselves in waves against superior opponents. This was different. This was tactical.
The symbol on the floor drew his attention again. He'd seen similar designs in the records he'd studied, theoretical texts about Abyssal summoning circles. This one was incomplete, a fragment of something larger. The cultists had been interrupted before they could finish their working.
Interrupted by what, though?
A crash from the back of the warehouse answered his question. Lyra's daggers were in her hands before Kael finished turning. The shadows there were too dark, too solid, and they were moving toward the moonlight.
"The shadow creatures followed us inside," Lyra said.
"Then we make a stand."
The first one emerged from the darkness fully formed—a hulking mass of shadow and hunger, vaguely humanoid but wrong in every proportion. Its limbs were too long, its head too small, and where its face should have been there was only a smooth expanse of darkness with two pinpricks of sickly light for eyes.
Kael met its gaze. "You want us that badly?"
It lunged.
He sidestepped, feeling the air move as claws scraped through the space where his head had been. His blade came up in a counter-strike, biting into the creature's side. Shadow-stuff poured from the wound, evaporating before it hit the ground. The creature didn't slow.
Lyra engaged the second one, her daggers flashing silver as she carved precise wounds into its form. They fought back to back, covering each other's blind spots the way they had practiced a hundred times in the past weeks. The creatures pressed them but couldn't overwhelm them.
But something was different this time.
Kael felt it in his bones before he saw it—a wrongness that had nothing to do with the shadow creatures. The temperature dropped. Frost formed on the walls. A figure stepped out of the warehouse's interior darkness, and the shadow creatures stopped attacking.
The newcomer wore armor that looked like it had been forged from solidified night. Black metal with faint purple veins of light pulsing through it, covering everything except a face that was all sharp angles and hollow sockets. It moved like it was still in shadow even when standing in full moonlight.
"Hollow Knight," Lyra whispered. "Third-Ascension. One of the six."
One of the six. That meant it was powerful. That meant it had been sent deliberately.
"You are Kael Ashborn," the Hollow Knight said. Its voice was the sound of wind through dead trees, dry and hollow. "The Soul Reaver."
Kael didn't bother asking how it knew. "And you work for the cult?"
"I serve the purpose." The Hollow Knight drew a sword that seemed to drink the light around it. "The purpose requires your abilities. You will come with me."
"That's not really how this works."
"The alternative is death." It stepped forward, and the shadow creatures moved with it, surrounding them in a loose ring. "I am authorized to accept either outcome."
Lyra shifted her stance, putting herself slightly ahead of Kael. "I'll hold them. You run."
"No."
"Kael—"
"I don't leave people behind. We fight together or not at all."
The Hollow Knight tilted its head, a gesture that might have been curiosity on something that still had humanity in it. "Interesting. The records suggested you were selfish. Willing to sacrifice anyone for your own advancement."
"The records were wrong."
"Or deliberately misleading." The Hollow Knight raised its sword. "We shall discover which."
It moved.
Kael had fought Awakened before. He'd fought creatures from the Abyss, cultists, gang enforcers, and one memorable encounter with a corrupt Order knight who'd thought Kael was easy prey. None of them had moved like this.
The Hollow Knight crossed the distance between them in a heartbeat, its sword coming down in a strike that would have split Kael from collarbone to hip if it connected. Kael barely rolled clear, feeling the displaced air ruffle his hair. The sword struck the floor and cracked the stone.
"That's new," he muttered, coming up with his blade raised.
Lyra engaged the shadow creatures while Kael faced their leader. He couldn't let it get past him to her. If the Hollow Knight focused on her instead, she'd last seconds. Third-Ascension was too powerful for her to handle alone.
The Hollow Knight pressed its attack, a flurry of strikes that came from angles that shouldn't be possible. Kael blocked, parried, dodged, and still took three wounds before he managed to create enough space to counterattack. His blade bit into the Hollow Knight's armor and stuck there.
He tried to pull it free. The armor held.
"Interesting technique," the Hollow Knight said, driving its elbow into Kael's face. Stars exploded across his vision. "But you lack the strength to exploit it."
Kael dropped his weapon and grabbed the Hollow Knight's arm instead. If he couldn't cut through the armor, he'd try something else.
The Soul Reaver activated.
He felt the Hollow Knight's essence like a wall of ice, dense and cold and resistant. This wasn't a normal Awakened being. This was something that had been fundamentally altered, remade by Abyssal energy into a weapon of a different kind. But it still had a soul. And that soul was still subject to Kael's ability.
[Soul Harvest: Attempting extraction from Hollow Knight. WARNING: Target soul structure is non-standard. Extraction may cause permanent damage to target.]
Kael pushed through the warning. The Soul Reaver had never failed him yet.
The Hollow Knight screamed—a sound like tearing metal—and tried to pull away. But Kael held on, pouring his will into the connection between them, forcing the extraction even as the Hollow Knight's defenses fought back. The sensation was like dragging a hooked fish through rapids, the resistance immense and the payoff uncertain.
[Soul Harvest: Partial extraction complete. You acquired Teleport Blink (fragmented). Ability is unstable. Use with caution.]
Information flooded Kael's mind. Spatial positioning. Dimensional anchoring. The precise spiritual frequencies required to shift from one location to another without traveling through the space between. The knowledge was incomplete, half-formed, but it was enough.
Enough to try something stupid.
Kael released the Hollow Knight and reached for the spatial frequencies he'd just learned. The world twisted. His stomach lurched. For one horrible moment he felt like he was being pulled in seventeen directions at once, stretched across dimensions that human minds weren't meant to perceive.
Then he was standing three feet to the left, exactly where he'd intended to be.
The Hollow Knight stared at him. "Impossible. That ability was—"
"Mine now." Kael retrieved his sword from where it had fallen. "Thanks for the gift."
Behind them, Lyra had finished off the last of the shadow creatures. She was bleeding from a dozen small wounds, but she was standing. She joined Kael, presenting a united front against the Hollow Knight.
The creature—the person inside the armor—laughed. It was a hollow sound, devoid of humor. "You think this changes anything? I am the least of what is coming. The Whisper King has awakened. The Seals will break. And when the Seventy-Two walk free, this world will finally know peace."
"The Seventy-Two," Kael repeated. The phrase triggered something in the memory he'd harvested earlier—cultists talking about rifts, about seals, about bringing their masters through. "The entities. The things locked behind the Veil."
"There is no Veil," the Hollow Knight said. "There is only the illusion of separation. And that illusion is dying."
It raised its sword again. But this time, Kael was ready.
He dodged the first strike, blocked the second, and when the third came he used the Teleport Blink again—this time for combat, shifting five feet to the side to appear behind the Hollow Knight and drive his blade through the gap in its armor at the hip joint.
The Hollow Knight stumbled. Ichor that looked like liquid shadow poured from the wound.
"Lyra. The head."
She understood immediately. While Kael kept the Hollow Knight off-balance, she circled behind it. The creature tried to turn, to face this new threat, but Kael pressed it from the front, driving it toward Lyra with attacks it couldn't fully block.
She struck once, her daggers finding the gap between the armor's helm and gorget. The blade sank deep. The Hollow Knight froze, its sword dropping from nerveless fingers.
"Whisper King," it whispered, its voice fading. "The King... Sees everything..."
It collapsed.
Kael stood over the body, breathing hard. His new ability pulsed in his mind, a half-understood technique that he knew would require practice to master. But it was his now, harvested from an enemy who had tried to kill him and claim him for their master.
[Soul Harvest: Target eliminated. Full extraction possible. Would you like to harvest the remaining essence?]
He looked at Lyra. She was watching him, her expression unreadable.
"Take it," she said. "We need every advantage we can get."
Kael knelt beside the Hollow Knight's body. The creature was already dissolving, its Abyssal nature causing it to evaporate as the animating force fled. He had minutes at most.
He placed his hand on the dissolving armor and pulled.
The extraction was cleaner this time. The Hollow Knight's core essence came freely, as if it wanted to be claimed. Kael took everything he could—energy, technique, fragmentary memories of training and purpose and the cold dark place where it had been remade.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 89% spiritual energy and Combat Memories from the remains. Energy absorbed. Combat Memories integrated.]
The combat memories were valuable. They showed him how the Hollow Knight had been created, the rituals involved, the preparation required to make an Awakened into something more and less than human. They also showed him where this particular Hollow Knight had come from.
A compound in the Ashwick slums. Underground chambers. Other creatures in various stages of transformation.
"We need to go there," Kael said, rising. "The compound. They're making more of these things."
"We need to survive until we can go there," Lyra corrected. "And right now, we need to disappear before more arrive."
She was right. Kael could feel it too—the spiritual disturbance from their battle would draw attention. Other cultists, other Abyssal creatures, maybe even Order investigators who'd been tracking the cult activity.
They left through a back entrance, moving through Fogmire's maze of alleys and abandoned buildings. The district was quieter now, the gangs having retreated to lick their wounds after the night's violence. Nobody stopped them. Nobody wanted to be seen.
It wasn't until they reached the safe house that Kael allowed himself to stop moving. Lyra locked the door behind them while he collapsed into a chair, his body finally registering the accumulated damage from the fight.
"You're getting stronger," Lyra observed, tending to her own wounds. "That technique you used. Teleportation. Where did that come from?"
"The Hollow Knight." He flexed his awareness, testing the boundaries of his new ability. It felt different from his other powers—more external, more like a tool he was borrowing than a part of himself. "The Soul Reaver took it from them. Stuffed it into my head."
"Does it hurt?"
"It's unstable. I can feel it wanting to pull me apart every time I use it." He met her eyes. "But it works."
Lyra nodded slowly. She had seen what Kael could do, what the Soul Reaver allowed him to become. She had chosen to stay with him anyway, knowing what he was, what he did. That said something about her, about them, about whatever this partnership had become.
"There's something else," Kael said. "Something I got from the Hollow Knight's memories."
"The Whisper King?"
"You know about that?"
"I know the name. The cultists whisper it. The Shadow Court fears it. Even the Orders pretend it doesn't exist." Lyra sat across from him, her wounds properly bandaged now. "Who is it?"
"I don't know. But they're organizing the cults. The attacks, the production of creatures like the Hollow Knight, the disruption of the gangs—it's all coordinated. Someone is building an army."
"An army for what?"
"The Hollow Knight said the Seals would break. That the Seventy-Two would walk free." Kael had seen the symbol in the warehouse, had felt the spiritual distortion in the air. "I think they're trying to open the rifts. All of them."
"All seventy-two?" Lyra's voice was flat. "That's impossible. The Orders maintain the Seals. They're reinforced every decade. They've held for centuries."
"The Hollow Knight said the Seals are dying. That the Veil is an illusion." Kael pulled out the memory fragment he'd harvested from the warehouse corpses, examining it again. "I think they have a way to accelerate the degradation. A ritual, maybe. Something that uses death energy to corrupt the anchoring points."
Lyra was quiet for a long moment. When she spoke, her voice was careful, measured. "If that's true—if the cults have a method to break the Seals—the Orders need to know."
"The Orders are the problem." Kael didn't mean to say it out loud, but the words came anyway. "The Hollow Knight mentioned them. Someone inside the Orders is helping the cult. Supplying information. Covering tracks."
"The corruption goes all the way to the top?"
"I don't know how far. But someone is selling out the world to the Abyss." Kael looked at Lyra, seeing the conflict in her eyes. She had been Shadow Court. She had her own reasons to distrust institutional power. "Can you get a message to your contacts? The ones who might care if reality collapses?"
"I can try." Lyra rose, moving toward the window to check the street. "But it will take time. And there's no guarantee they'll believe me. Or that they won't just use the information for political advantage."
"Politics later. Survival first."
"Funny how that never works out the way you plan."
She was right. Kael had been playing the survival game since his Awakening, since his execution, since the Soul Reaver had given him a second chance at life. Every choice he made was tactical, every alliance calculated. He didn't have the luxury of long-term thinking.
But if the cults succeeded, there wouldn't be a long-term for anyone.
"We rest until dawn," Kael decided. "Then we move on the compound. The Hollow Knight's memories show me where it is, how to get in. We hit them before they can create more soldiers."
"Us against a fortified position with unknown defenses." Lyra's tone was dry. "What could go wrong?"
"Nothing ever does." Kael closed his eyes, letting his body begin the slow process of healing. His mind stayed active, processing the combat memories, the fragments of intelligence he'd harvested. The Hollow Knight had known things. Orders things. Cult things. The overlapping circles of conspiracy that had brought them to this moment.
One name kept surfacing in the memories. A handler, maybe. Someone who gave the Hollow Knight its missions.
Dorian.
Kael didn't recognize the name, but the memories attached to it were clear. A man in Order robes, speaking with authority. Coordinating cult activities with Hollow Knight operations. Discussing the timeline for the Great Seal's failure.
Someone inside the Orders was feeding information to the enemy. Someone with access to Seal maintenance schedules, patrol routes, operational details. Someone trusted.
Lyra had noticed his tension. "What is it?"
"The Hollow Knight had a handler. Someone inside the Orders feeding them intelligence." Kael opened his eyes. "Dorian. Does that name mean anything to you?"
Lyra's expression shifted. Something like recognition, quickly masked. "Maybe. Where did you hear it?"
"The memories. Why?"
She hesitated. The silence stretched between them, filled with the sounds of Fogmire outside—distant shouts, a dog barking, the eternal fog horns of the river barges.
"Lyra."
"Dorian Valdris," she said finally. "He's high-ranking in the Order of Dawn. Third circle or higher. I've seen his name on operational briefings." She met Kael's gaze. "He's also supposed to be dead. Killed in an Abyssal incursion six months ago."
"Except he's not dead. He's working with the cults."
"Or he's been compromised. Infected by Abyssal influence. It happens sometimes. The Entities can corrupt even Awakened if they're exposed to enough of the raw stuff."
"Or he's been cult all along."
"That's darker."
"It's also more likely." Kael thought about it. The Orders were political organizations. They had internal factions, competing interests, long histories of betrayal and alliance. Someone deciding to sell out humanity to the Abyss wasn't beyond belief.
"We tell Gwen," Kael decided. "She needs to know. And if she can't act on it—"
"Then we deal with it ourselves." Lyra's voice was hard. "I know how you operate."
"I'm not the only one anymore?" Kael almost smiled. "You've changed, Lyra."
"Survival changes people." She looked away. "So does seeing the alternative. The Shadow Court taught me a lot about what the world really is. It's not about good and evil. It's about power and the people too weak to hold it."
"And you want to hold it?"
"I want to survive. That's all anyone wants when you strip away the lies." She stood, moving to the window again. "Get some rest. I'll take first watch. We have a long day tomorrow."
Kael closed his eyes. The Teleport Blink ability pulsed in his mind, unstable but present. He would need to practice it, to refine the technique until it stopped threatening to tear him apart. The Soul Reaver had given him a gift from the Hollow Knight, but gifts weren't skills. Skills required work.
As he drifted toward sleep, his mind kept returning to one image from the Hollow Knight's memories. A chamber underground, filled with vats of shadow-stuff. Figures floating in the vats, human shapes slowly being transformed. Soldiers being made. An army being built.
And at the center of it all, a throne of black crystal.
On that throne sat someone wrapped in shadows so deep they seemed to be made of pure darkness. The Whisper King. The mastermind. The one orchestrating the end of the world.
The memories were fragmented, incomplete, but they showed Kael enough. The cult wasn't random. The attacks weren't opportunistic. Everything was part of a plan, a timeline that had been building for years, maybe decades.
And now they knew. The enemy had a face, a location, a goal.
Now Kael had to stop them before it was too late.
Sleep took him before he could finish the thought. In his dreams, he saw the Hollow Knight again, standing over his body, sword raised. And behind the Hollow Knight, the shadows whispered a name.
Kael Ashborn.
The Soul Reaver.
They knew who he was. They knew what he could do.
And they were coming for him.
The sound of footsteps outside the safe house door pulled him from sleep. Lyra was already awake, her daggers in her hands, her eyes fixed on the entrance.
"We're not alone," she breathed.
Kael reached for his blade, his body tensing for another fight. The footsteps stopped outside the door. A moment of silence, broken only by the fog dripping from the eaves.
Then a voice, muffled but clear. "Kael Ashborn. The Whisper King requests your presence."
Kael and Lyra exchanged glances. This was wrong. This was a trap. They should run, should flee into Fogmire's maze and disappear until they were strong enough to fight back.
But the voice continued. "He has information you require. About your power. About the Soul Reaver's origin. About why you were chosen."
Kael's hand tightened on his blade. The mention of the Soul Reaver's origin hit him like a physical blow. That was the one mystery he hadn't been able to solve—the question of where his ability came from, why it had appeared in him, what it truly meant.
The door handle turned.
"If you refuse, the compound burns," the voice said. "And every soul within it dies. Your choice, Soul Reaver."
Kael looked at Lyra. She shook her head, mouthing the word no. He knew she was right. This was exactly the kind of manipulation the cultists would use. A false choice designed to lure him into a trap.
But if there was even a chance...
The door opened.
