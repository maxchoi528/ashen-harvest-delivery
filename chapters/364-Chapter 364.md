The ritual circle pulsed with sickly green light, and Kael pressed himself against the crumbling stone archway, breath shallow. Three robed figures stood at cardinal points around the formation, their chanting reverberating through the abandoned warehouse like a stomach growl before plague.
"Soul anchor is stable," one of them called out, voice muffled by the hood. "Begin the secondary inscription."
Kael counted the bodies. Seven corpses lay arranged in a precise pattern around the circle, their chest cavities cut open and hollowed. Fresh kills, no more than a few hours old. The blood pooling beneath them still glistened.
This wasn't random cultist violence. The precision of the formation, the quality of the victims—these were Awakened who had been hunted down. Someone had orchestrated this, and the seal-work glowing beneath the blood suggested they were attempting to unseal a rift.
The Order of Dawn would have sent a full strike team for this. The Iron Vanguard would have buried it under political maneuvering. But Kael wasn't here on behalf of any faction. He was here because the soul-energy signature of this ritual had registered on his awareness three blocks away, and that kind of power gathering couldn't be ignored.
"Contact on the upper walkway," one cultist said, head turning toward the shadows where Kael had been about to position himself. "We have an intruder."
Kael dropped from the archway, landing in a crouch fifteen feet from the nearest cultist. His coat flared, and the air tasted of copper and ozone. The three robed figures broke formation immediately, surrounding him with practiced efficiency.
"Soul Reaver," the largest one said, and Kael caught the tremor in their voice despite the bravado. They knew what he was. They recognized the hollow-eyed pallor that came with consuming too many harvested souls. "The Faceless One warned us you might come."
"Then your Faceless One has good intelligence." Kael straightened slowly, letting them see his hands were empty. Not that it mattered. His power didn't require weapons. "I'm curious about what you're trying to accomplish here. Unsealing a rift takes more than seven dead Awakened."
The largest cultist smiled beneath their hood, and Kael caught the flash of metal as they drew a ritual dagger. "It takes a Soul Reaver."
The warehouse erupted into motion.
Kael didn't wait for the first strike. He moved left, ducking beneath a sweeping blade that would have taken his head, and drove his palm into the nearest cultist's solar plexus. The man doubled over, and Kael followed up with an elbow to the temple, dropping him to the blood-slicked floor.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted combat proficiency fragments from the remains. A tiny fraction skill enhancement acquired.]
The notification flickered across his awareness like a bitter aftertaste. He hadn't killed the man—just rendered him unconscious—but the harvesting had triggered anyway, drawn from the residual life-force. That was the danger of his ability. It operated on proximity and contact, not intent.
The large cultist came at him with the ritual dagger, thrusting for his heart. Kael sidestepped, grabbed the wrist, and twisted. Bone cracked. The cultist screamed, but instead of releasing the blade, they lunged forward, driving it toward Kael's throat with desperate strength.
Kael twisted harder, felt the shoulder socket separate with a wet pop, and used the cultist's momentum to throw them over his hip. They landed hard on the ritual circle, disrupting the inscription work.
"Third anchor destabilizing," the remaining cultist shouted. "We need to—"
Kael didn't let them finish. He crossed the distance in three strides, caught the cultist by the throat, and slammed them against the warehouse wall. The man's feet dangled, and his hood fell back to reveal a face Kael didn't recognize. Young. Too young for this work. Scared.
"The ritual," Kael said, keeping his voice level. "What's it for? What are you unsealing?"
The young cultist laughed, a broken sound. "You think we'd tell you? The Orders have tried torture. The Shadow Court has tried bribery. You're just another weapon, Reaver. Another tool the Entities will use when the seals break."
"The Faceless One sent you."
"Everyone serves the Faceless One. You just don't know it yet."
Kael dropped the cultist and turned back to the ritual circle. The disrupted anchor was bleeding spiritual energy, the carefully drawn inscriptions flickering like dying candles. The seven corpses remained motionless, their chest cavities empty.
One of them moved.
Kael's hand came up, spiritual energy condensing into a blade-shape at his fingertips. The corpse that had moved was a woman, her throat cut and her eyes now open, staring at him with pupils that had been replaced by swirling voids.
"You're not one of them," the corpse said with a voice that sounded like wind through dead leaves. "Your soul doesn't carry the flavor of the Entities. But you harvest from the dead. You carry the old power. The first Reaver's gift."
"I'm carrying nothing that belongs to you." Kael kept his stance ready, watching the other six corpses. They remained still, but he could feel spiritual energy building in the room, responding to the disrupted ritual.
"The first Reaver walked the same path you walk. They harvested. They grew strong. And when the seals began to crack, they made a choice." The corpse's head tilted at an unnatural angle. "The same choice you're going to make. Join the Entities, or be consumed by them."
"I don't make deals with corpses."
"No. You make deals with the dead. There's a difference." The corpse's mouth opened, and something black and formless began to emerge from its throat. "The Faceless One wanted you here. Wanted you to see what we're building. The Great Rift isn't the only seal that's weakening. Seventy-two rifts. Seventy-two Entities. One by one, they're all going to open. And when they do, you'll need to choose a side."
The black entity finished emerging and launched itself at Kael like a spear of shadow. He dodged, feeling the spiritual cold that radiated from it, the wrongness of an Abyssal presence in the Material world. This wasn't possession or corruption. This was direct manifestation, and it shouldn't have been possible without a fully open rift.
Kael's blade sliced through the shadow-entity, and it dispersed into wisps of dark energy. But it reformed immediately, larger than before, surrounding him in a sphere of consuming darkness.
"The old power protects you," the entity said from all directions at once. "But it won't save you from what's coming. The Shadow Court is watching. The cultists are preparing. The Orders are too busy fighting each other to notice the real threat. In sixty days, the Great Rift will open completely. The Whisper King will rise. And you, last Reaver, will kneel or die."
"Interesting timing," Kael said, searching for a weakness in the formation. "You expect me to believe you're here to negotiate?"
"We're here to recruit. The Entities don't want to destroy humanity. That was always a lie the Orders told to justify their existence. We want to transform it. To bring the Material and the Abyss together. To end the war between the realms." The darkness pressed closer, and Kael felt his spiritual energy beginning to drain. "Join us willingly, and you'll be part of that transformation. Resist, and you'll be harvested like the weakest of your victims."
Kael smiled, and the entity paused.
"You made a mistake," he said. "You told me there's a timeline. Sixty days. That means you're not ready. You're bluffing."
He reached into his coat and pulled out a small vial of silver liquid—soul-essence, purchased from the Veiled Market at considerable cost. High-grade Awakened essence, enough to fuel a significant spiritual technique. He uncorked it and drank it down in one motion.
The energy hit his system like lightning through his veins. His Soul Reaver ability activated at maximum output, and instead of trying to destroy the shadow-entity, he grabbed it with both hands and pulled.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted residual Entity fragments from Abyssal manifestation. Unique energy signature acquired. Warning: This material is spiritually corrosive. Handle with extreme caution.]
The entity screamed, a sound that shook dust from the warehouse ceiling, and began to unravel. Kael pulled harder, drawing the dark energy into himself, feeling the wrongness of it trying to bond with his soul. It resisted, fought, but his Reaver power was designed for exactly this—for taking what the dead carried, for consuming the energy that remained after death.
"Impossible," the entity hissed. "Reavers can't harvest from the Abyss. The Entities are beyond—"
"Nothing is beyond harvesting," Kael said through gritted teeth. "Everything dies. Everything leaves remains. Even things from the Abyss."
The entity finished unraveling, and Kael released the dark energy into the air, where it dissipated harmlessly. His hands were shaking, and his vision swam with afterimages of shadow and void. The soul-essence had given him the fuel to attempt something that should have been impossible, but the cost was already showing. His spiritual reserves felt hollow, scoured clean by the technique.
He turned back to the ritual circle. The seven corpses had gone completely still, whatever connection that had powered them severed when the entity was destroyed. The young cultist Kael had dropped was trying to crawl toward the exit, dragging their broken arm.
Kael crossed the room in three strides and grabbed them by the collar, dragging them upright. "The Great Rift. Where?"
"I don't know," the cultist whimpered. "Nobody knows exactly. It's sealed somewhere in the northern territories. The Whisper King has agents working on—"
"The Whisper King." Kael repeated the name. He'd heard it before, in fragments of harvested memories. "Who is he?"
"I don't know. Nobody does. He's a title, not a name. The leader of the Abyssal-aligned faction among the Entities. The one who wants to open the rifts permanently."
Kael dropped the cultist and stepped back. His mind was racing, trying to process what he'd learned. The cultists were trying to unseal a rift here in Thornwick. The Shadow Court was monitoring him specifically. The Entities were aware of Soul Reavers and wanted to recruit or destroy him. And somewhere in the northern territories, a rift that could end the world was about to break open.
The Orders needed to know about this. All of them. The coordinated attack on Awakened, the ritual work, the timeline—all of it suggested a level of organization that the individual cults shouldn't have been capable of. Someone was coordinating them. Someone with access to information about the seal network and the spiritual hierarchy.
Someone like the Shadow Court.
Kael walked to the nearest corpse—the woman whose body had been used to speak for the entity—and knelt beside her. The harvesting would work even on remains that had been partially animated by Abyssal energy. The system notification appeared immediately.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted fragmented memories from remains. Information partially corrupted. Recovered data: Coordinates to secondary ritual site. Reference to "Crimson Covenant." Name: Theron Ashworth, former member of the Order of Dawn.]
Theron Ashworth. The name triggered a flash of recognition. Kael had heard it mentioned in previous harvests—a disgraced Dawn initiate who'd been expelled for unauthorized experimentation with seal-work. According to the rumors, he'd been trying to find a way to strengthen the seals than break them. But if he was involved with this cult, either the rumors were wrong or he'd changed sides.
Or he was playing a deeper game than anyone realized.
Kael stood, checking the positions of the unconscious cultists. The large one with the dislocated shoulder was starting to stir. The young one had stopped trying to crawl and was now just lying on the floor, staring at the ceiling with hollow eyes. The third was still unconscious.
He had maybe ten minutes before local authorities responded to the spiritual disturbance. The city watch wasn't a threat—Kael could handle anything they might send—but their involvement would mean official reports, which meant the Orders would get involved, which meant his presence at a cult ritual would be documented and analyzed.
He needed to disappear. And he needed to find out more about the Crimson Covenant, the secondary ritual site, and whatever Theron Ashworth was really doing.
Kael walked to the warehouse's loading door, tested the rusted lock, and pushed through into the alley beyond. The fog that perpetually shrouded Thornwick's lower districts had thickened while he'd been inside, turning the gaslights into fuzzy orange spheres and muffling the sounds of the city. Steam vents hissed from nearby buildings, and somewhere in the distance, a factory whistle marked the shift change.
He made it three blocks before he felt the presence.
It wasn't hostile, exactly. More like being watched by someone who expected you to notice them. Kael slowed his pace, letting his spiritual awareness expand outward, and caught a faint signature hovering on the edge of his perception. Someone was following him, but they were doing it from the hidden layer of reality—the realm where only stage two and higher beings could perceive.
A stage twoAwakened, then. Or something worse.
Kael turned down a narrow side street, one that would force the follower to either close the distance or reveal themselves. The buildings here were older, their foundations dating back to before the steam age, when Thornwick had been a fishing village on the edge of the Vastmere Sea. The cobblestones were uneven, and the streetlights were spaced too far apart, creating pools of darkness between each one.
The presence followed.
Kael stopped in the middle of a particularly dark stretch and turned to face the shadows. "You can come out. I'm not going to pretend I don't know you're there."
A figure detached itself from the darkness between two buildings. Female, dressed in practical traveling clothes than any faction uniform. Her eyes caught the distant gaslight and reflected it back with an odd metallic shimmer.
"Kael Ashborn," she said. Her voice carried the clipped precision of someone trained in formal speech. "The Soul Reaver. The Orders have been looking for you."
"The Orders can keep looking." Kael kept his hands loose at his sides, ready for violence. "I'm not interested in whatever recruitment pitch you're about to deliver."
"This isn't recruitment." The woman took a step forward, and Kael tensed. "This is a warning. The Shadow Court has marked you as a priority target. Not for capture. For elimination. Whatever you did in that warehouse tonight, it drew their attention."
"Everyone keeps telling me about the Shadow Court. What exactly do they want?"
"The same thing everyone wants. Control. The Shadow Court operates as a deniable arm for several of the Orders, but it has its own agenda. Its own leadership. It believes that a Soul Reaver who refuses to serve any faction is the greatest threat to the current balance of power."
Kael laughed, a harsh sound. "The current balance of power. You mean the system where Awakened nobles oppress commoners, where the Orders fight proxy wars instead of addressing the real threat, where the seals are failing and everyone's too busy playing politics to notice?"
"I'm not here to debate philosophy." The woman reached into her coat and pulled out a small object. She threw it to Kael, who caught it automatically. A bronze medallion, stamped with the symbol of a broken seal. "The Crimson Covenant is meeting tomorrow night at the old foundry on Cutter's Wharf. If you want answers about what's really happening with the seals, that's where you'll find them."
"Why would you help me? Who are you?"
"My name is Mira Ashford. I'm a former member of the Order of Dawn, currently operating as an independent contractor. I help people who are trying to do the right thing, even when the Orders won't support them." She stepped back into the shadows. "The Reaver before you—your predecessor, if you want to think of it that way—tried to fight the Entities alone. He failed. Don't make the same mistake. Find allies. Find information. And whatever you do, don't trust anyone who claims to have all the answers."
Kael looked at the medallion, then back at the shadows where Mira Ashford had been standing. She was gone, vanished into the hidden layer of reality like she'd never existed.
He pocketed the medallion and continued walking. The fog was thicker now, and he could hear the distant sound of bells—city watch bells, announcing some kind of emergency. Probably responding to the warehouse. They'd find the unconscious cultists and the ritual circle, and then the Orders would start asking questions.
Kael needed to move faster. He needed to find the secondary ritual site, learn about the Crimson Covenant, and figure out what Theron Ashworth was really planning. The timeline the entity had mentioned—sixty days until the Great Rift opened completely—was too short for mistakes.
He also needed to understand more about his own abilities. The harvest from the shadow-entity had worked, but it had cost him. His spiritual reserves were depleted, and the corrosive nature of Abyssal energy meant he'd need time to recover, time he didn't have.
The Soul Reaver power was supposed to be free—no spiritual cost, no corruption, no limits except the physical act of touching corpses. But tonight had proven that there were other costs. Social costs. Political costs. The cost of drawing attention from forces he didn't fully understand.
Kael ducked into a shadowed doorway and pressed his back against the cold stone, letting the fog curl around him. His breath came in short gasps, and his hands wouldn't stop shaking. The soul-essence crash was hitting harder than he'd expected.
[Soul Harvest: Residual Entity energy detected in spiritual system. Recommend purification protocol. Estimated recovery time: 6-several hours if standard rest is employed. Accelerated recovery possible through additional soul-essence consumption.]
Six to eight hours. During which the Shadow Court might send assassins, the cultists might regroup, and the Orders might finally pin down his location. He didn't have that kind of time.
But he did have one advantage. The cultists had been trying to unseal a rift here, which meant they had resources, personnel, and organizational infrastructure. If he could find their secondary site before they completed whatever ritual they were planning, he could disrupt their work and buy himself more time.
The medallion in his pocket felt heavier than it should.
Tomorrow night. The old foundry on Cutter's Wharf. The Crimson Covenant would be meeting, and somewhere in that meeting would be answers about the seals, about Theron Ashworth, and about what the Shadow Court was really planning.
Kael pushed off from the wall and started walking toward the Thornwick docks, where he had a safehouse that the city watch didn't know about. He needed to rest, recover, and prepare. Tomorrow night would require everything he had.
The fog swallowed him whole, and the bells continued to toll in the distance.
Behind him, in the shadows he had just vacated, a pair of eyes watched him go. The figure they belonged to was tall, thin, wrapped in a cloak that seemed to drink the light around it. They made no move to follow. They didn't need to.
They already knew where Kael would be tomorrow night.
And they were going to make sure he never left the foundry alive.
