The body of a Silverguard captain lay half-buried in ash, her sigil still glowing faintly against the gray sky. Kael crouched beside her, pressing his palm to her cooling skin. The Soul Reaver hummed in his chest, hungry.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 47 spiritual essence from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired: 'The third seal resonates differently. Sera, the wards are screaming.'"
Kael stood, ash grinding between his teeth. The message burned in his mind—someone named Sera, someone still alive, still fighting. He didn't know her. But the memory felt personal, intimate, like a secret shouted into a storm.
The Ashenmoor Wastes stretched endlessly before him, a scarred landscape of blackened earth and crumbling fortifications. Three weeks ago, this had been farmland. Now it resembled something from the old histories, the descriptions of the Abyssal Wars etched into children's primers. Smoke rose from a dozen directions. The thunder of artillery echoed across the valley where the Iron Vanguard made their last stand against the emerging horde.
He moved toward the sound.
A Silverguard patrol spotted him before he reached the ridge. Four soldiers, their armor scorched and dented, their eyes hollow with the particular exhaustion of those who'd watched friends die. The lead soldier raised his spear, the blade trembling.
"Soul Reaver." The word came out like a curse. "The Orders have a price on your head."
"I'm not here for you," Kael said. His voice sounded flat even to his own ears. Three months of harvesting, three months of eating the dead, had scraped something essential from him. He couldn't remember the last time he'd spoken to someone living.
"The Orders say you're a threat to the seal network. That you destabilize the Veil."
"The Orders say a lot of things." He stepped closer, and the soldiers tensed. One of them, barely old enough to shave, whimpered. "I've been fighting cultists for three weeks. Harvested forty-seven corpses between here and the Thornwick border. Every single one of them was carrying something useful."
"Your kind destabilizes the balance," the captain spat. "The Law of Conservation—"
"The Law of Conservation says power can't be created." Kael pulled back his sleeve, revealing the black veins spreading up his forearm. Soul Reaver marks, visible now. "It doesn't say it can't be redistributed by someone willing to get his hands dirty."
Silence stretched between them. In the distance, another explosion ripped through the morning air, followed by the inhuman shriek that Kael had learned to associate with Entity-touched cultists.
The young soldier looked toward the sound. "We're supposed to hold this ridge."
"Against what's coming?" Kael followed his gaze. A dark mass moved through the smoke—shapes too large to be human, moving with the coordinated purpose of a coordinated attack. "How many do you have?"
"Enough." But the captain's voice wavered.
Kael considered his options. The patrol was hostile but not immediately aggressive. They were also positioned between him and the main battle, which meant getting past them meant either fighting or convincing them he wasn't an enemy.
"The cultists are using coordinated tactics," he said. "I've seen their patterns. They're not random raiders. Someone's directing them, someone who understands the seal network."
The captain's expression shifted. "How would you know that?"
"Because I've been killing their commanders." Kael reached into his pack and produced a collection of identification tags—small metal discs worn by cultist lieutenants. "Three from the Thornwick cell, two from the river settlements, one from the mountain passes. All carrying the same sigil." He held one up. "The Sepulcher Mark. You've seen it before."
More silence. The captain's jaw worked. "The Council denied the reports. Said it was scattered resistance, not coordinated—"
"The Council's full of shit." Kael tucked the tags away. "I've been watching the seal network for months. Every major rift in this region has cultist activity within a day's march. That's not coincidence. That's planning."
Another explosion, closer this time. The ridge trembled. The young soldier grabbed his spear with both hands.
"We're supposed to hold this position," the captain repeated, but the conviction had drained from her voice. "Orders from the Iron Vanguard command."
"Your command's three miles behind the front line," Kael said. "By the time you get orders, the cultists will be eating your children."
The captain stared at him. Something passed across her face—not trust, not respect, but maybe the desperate recognition of someone who'd run out of better options. "What are you suggesting?"
"Fall back to the secondary fortifications. Use the terrain. Make them fight for every inch instead of streaming through your lines like water through a broken dam." Kael pointed toward a rocky outcropping to the east. "That ridge has natural choke points. You can funnel them."
"And you?"
"I'm going to find out who's coordinating them." Kael turned toward the smoke. "They've been leaving commanders alive on purpose. I can trace the signal."
"Soul Reaver." The captain's voice stopped him. "If you're lying—if this is some kind of trap—"
"Then you'll have a good story to tell the executioner." Kael didn't look back. "But I don't think you're going to get that chance. The way they're moving, you won't survive the day."
He walked into the smoke.
The battle opened around him in fragments. To his left, a Silverguard swordsman went down under a wave of Entity-touched cultists, their bodies twisted and bloated with stolen power. To his right, an Iron Vanguard artillery crew fired desperately at a massive shape that Kael recognized as a Siege Brute—a corpse animated with enough spiritual energy to punch through castle walls.
Kael moved through it all like a ghost. He wasn't here to fight. He was here to harvest.
A cultist leapt from behind a collapsed wall, a serrated blade raised. Kael sidestepped, grabbed the man's wrist, and drove his palm into the cultist's chest. The body went rigid, then limp.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 31 spiritual essence from the remains. Skill acquired: Entity Detection (Minor)."
The cultist had been able to sense Entity energy. Useful, though the range was limited. Kael dropped the corpse and kept moving.
Another cultist, this one wielding a spear crackling with corrupted lightning. The man thrust, Kael deflected, and in the confusion of combat, his hand found the cultist's throat. The harvesting took three seconds.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 28 spiritual essence from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired: 'The convergence point is ready. When the seal breaks, He will rise.'"
Kael's blood ran cold. The convergence point. The seal breaking. He didn't know what it meant, but the certainty in the cultist's dying thought carried weight.
He pushed deeper into the battlefield, following the pattern he'd identified over weeks of observation. The cultists weren't fighting to win—they were fighting to distract. Their true objective lay somewhere else, somewhere behind the chaos.
A massive explosion ripped through the center of the battlefield. Kael staggered, ears ringing, and looked up to see the Siege Brute collapse in on itself, its stolen energy finally depleted. Around it, Silverguard and Iron Vanguard soldiers cheered, momentarily emboldened.
But Kael saw what they couldn't. A group of figures moving through the smoke, not toward the enemy but away from it, carrying something large and covered in cloth.
He followed.
The cultists moved with purpose, navigating the battlefield as if they'd memorized every trench and crater. They passed through a gap in the fortifications, crossed a stream choked with bodies, and began climbing the slopes of a hill that Kael recognized from maps of the region.
Cairn Hill. An ancient burial ground from the Abyssal Wars.
Kael's skin prickled. The Soul Reaver in his chest hummed with an intensity he'd never felt before, almost painful, like a tuning fork struck against his ribs.
The cultists reached the hill's summit and began to work. They weren't building—they were uncovering. Removing centuries of packed earth and stone to reveal what lay beneath.
A door. Massive, carved from black stone, covered in sigils that glowed with sickly light.
Kael watched from behind a boulder, counting heads. Eight cultists, including two who radiated the distinctive aura of Awakened power. Not powerful—probably Transcendent at most—but enough to be dangerous.
One of the Awakened cultists stepped forward and placed his palm against the door. Light flared. The sigils rearranged themselves, forming a pattern that Kael's new Entity Detection ability read as an access sequence.
The door groaned open.
Beyond it, darkness. But not empty darkness—Kael could feel the spiritual pressure emanating from the opening, a weight that pressed against his senses like deep water.
"The First Reaver sleeps within," one of the cultists said. "We bring the key."
Kael's mind raced. The First Reaver. The original Soul Reaver—the one whose tablets had started everything, whose techniques Kael had inherited through the black market artifact that had first awakened his abilities.
The tomb wasn't supposed to exist. The histories said the First Reaver had been destroyed, his remains scattered to prevent anyone from replicating his power. But here it was, hidden beneath a burial ground that had sat untouched for centuries.
And the cultists were opening it.
He had to act. If they took whatever was inside—if they used the First Reaver's techniques for their own purposes—the results would be catastrophic. He'd seen what happened when cultists accessed power beyond their understanding. The Siege Brute had been a pale example.
But eight cultists, including two Awakened. And he didn't know what waited in the darkness beyond the door.
The Entity Detection ability pulsed in his mind. Fresh information: the pressure coming from the tomb wasn't just spiritual. It was alive. Something was waiting inside, something that registered on scales his ability couldn't fully measure.
Kael reached for the harvest on his belt. He'd collected essence from seventeen different cultists over the past three weeks, enough to fuel several major techniques. But using them would drain his reserves, leave him vulnerable.
Or he could do what Soul Reavers did best.
He moved.
The first cultist died without understanding what happened. Kael's hand found his throat, the harvesting took hold, and the body dropped. Two more cultists turned, raising weapons, but Kael was already among them, a shadow moving faster than thought.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 34 spiritual essence from the remains. Skill acquired: Coordinated Strike (Minor)."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 29 spiritual essence from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired: 'Prime Zarek will be pleased. The convergence proceeds on schedule.'"
Zarek. The name hit Kael like a physical blow. The same Zarek who'd framed him for murder back in Thornwick, who'd orchestrated his execution, who'd vanished into the shadows when the Soul Reaver had been revealed.
Zarek was behind this. Zarek was coordinating the entire campaign.
The two Awakened cultists reacted faster than their companions. One raised his hands, gathering spiritual energy for an attack. The other produced a blade that glowed with Entity corruption.
"Soul Reaver!" the first one snarled. "You dare to desecrate this ground?"
Kael didn't answer. He lunged, and the Awakened cultist released his attack—a wave of corrupted fire that scorched the air where Kael had been a moment before. But Kael was already past the flames, closing the distance, his hand reaching for exposed skin.
The cultist anticipated him. The glowing blade swept across, forcing Kael to retreat or lose an arm. They circled each other, the second Awakened cultist flanking to cut off escape routes.
"You don't understand what's happening," the first cultist said. "The Reaver's legacy isn't meant for the Orders. It's meant for those who can use it properly."
"The First Reaver was a monster," the second cultist added. "He harvested without restraint, without morality. The cult seeks to complete his work."
"And you're doing that by opening sealed tombs and coordinating attacks across the continent?" Kael kept his voice steady, though his mind worked frantically. Two Awakened at once. He could take one if he was fast, but the second would capitalize on his vulnerability.
"Order or chaos," the first cultist said. "Those are the only choices. The seals are failing whether we act or not. Better to control the change than be crushed by it."
Kael's Entity Detection pulsed. The pressure from the tomb was building, the something inside stirring. Time was running out.
He made his choice.
One of the harvested skills activated—the Coordinated Strike he'd taken from the cultist corpse. His body moved with preternatural precision, flowing around the glowing blade, closing the distance. His palm struck the second cultist's chest, and the harvesting began.
The first cultist's fire attack caught Kael in the side. Pain exploded through his torso. But he didn't stop. The second cultist died in his grip, and Kael used the body as a shield, spinning to face the first.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 52 spiritual essence from the remains. Ability acquired: Soul Shield (Minor)."
The fire attack had done damage, but the new ability flared to life, a barrier of spiritual energy that absorbed the next blast. Kael laughed—a harsh, broken sound—and threw the corpse at the surviving cultist.
The cultist stumbled back, giving Kael time to close the distance. They grappled, traded blows that crackled with spiritual energy, and in the end, Kael's hand found the man's throat.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 61 spiritual essence from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired: 'The tomb contains his vessel. When the convergence completes, the First Reaver will walk again.'"
The implications crashed over Kael like a wave. The vessel. The First Reaver's body, preserved, waiting for resurrection. The cult wasn't just opening a tomb—they were preparing for something far worse.
But he didn't have time to process. The tomb was still open. The pressure was still building.
Kael approached the doorway, every instinct screaming at him to turn back. The darkness beyond seemed to breathe, pulsing with the rhythm of something vast and ancient.
He stepped inside.
The tomb stretched before him, carved from living rock, lit by phosphorescent fungi that cast everything in sickly green. And at the center, on a platform of black stone, lay the body.
Not decayed. Not preserved artificially. still, as if the First Reaver had lain down to sleep and never woken. He looked almost peaceful—middle-aged, weathered, his hands folded over his chest. But the spiritual pressure emanating from him was immense, the accumulated power of someone who'd spent a lifetime harvesting the dead.
Kael's Soul Reaver ability screamed at him. The connection between him and the body was electric, magnetic, pulling him forward with an intensity that bordered on compulsion. He could harvest this. Right now. The power contained in these remains would eclipse everything he'd taken before.
But the Memory Fragment echoed in his mind. The First Reaver would walk again. If Kael harvested this body, would he be stopping a resurrection—or completing one?
A sound echoed from the corridor behind him. Voices. More cultists, arriving too late to stop him but in time to witness what he did next.
Kael made his decision.
He pressed his palm against the First Reaver's chest and began to harvest.
The sensation was unlike anything he'd experienced. Power flooded into him, not the steady trickle of normal corpses but a torrent, a river breaking through a dam. His vision whited out. His body convulsed. And in the chaos, he heard a voice—cold, ancient, amused.
"Finally. A successor worthy of my legacy."
When Kael's vision cleared, he was on his knees, gasping, his body burning with new power. The Soul Reaver notification appeared unbidden.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 847 spiritual essence from the remains. Legendary Skill acquired: Soul Dominion. Memory Fragment acquired: 'I am the First. I was the last. The cycle ends with you.'"
Kael stared at his hands. The black veins had spread up his arms, across his chest, reaching toward his face. The Soul Reaver marks were evolving, becoming something more.
He didn't know what Soul Dominion meant. He didn't know what the First Reaver's Memory Fragment meant by "the cycle." But he knew one thing with absolute certainty.
Zarek was planning to use the First Reaver's resurrection to accomplish something far worse than coordinated attacks. And now Kael had the power—and the knowledge—to stop him.
But the cultists had seen. The news would spread. Every faction that feared Soul Reavers would descend on him now.
The voices in the corridor grew louder.
Kael stood, his legs unsteady, and faced the darkness ahead. He'd wanted answers about the Soul Reaver's origin. He'd gotten them. But the cost was about to become very clear.
Footsteps echoed in the tunnel. Dozens of them. Moving fast.
And somewhere in the depths of his newly expanded consciousness, the First Reaver's voice whispered: "They are coming for you, successor. What will you do?"
Kael smiled—a grim, terrible expression that felt foreign on his face. He knew exactly what he would do.
The tomb's entrance filled with cultists, their weapons raised, their eyes blazing with righteous fury. Behind them, visible through the doorway, the battlefield continued its chaos.
Kael raised his hands, and the new power answered. Soul Dominion. The ability to command the harvested, to bend their remnants to his will. Around him, the bodies of the cultists he'd killed stirred, rising as puppets of spiritual energy.
"Soul Harvest: Soul Dominion activated. Controlling 5 corpse-puppets."
The cultists hesitated.
"You want the First Reaver's legacy?" Kael's voice carried through the tomb, cold and certain. "Come and take it."
They charged.
Kael let his corpse-puppets meet them, drawing on skills harvested from a dozen different bodies. The battle became a whirlwind of death and rebirth, each corpse he claimed adding to his army, each harvest strengthening his hold.
But even as he fought, questions burned in his mind. The cycle. The convergence. What did the First Reaver know that Kael didn't?
The cultists fell back, regrouping at the entrance. Their leader—a woman with silver hair and the bearing of someone accustomed to command—raised her hand for a pause.
"Soul Reaver." Her voice was calm, almost respectful. "We are not your enemies. We seek the same goal."
"Zarek sent you."
"Zarek serves a greater purpose. As do you, now." The woman gestured toward the platform where the First Reaver's body had lain. "You have taken his power. You have become the new vessel. The question is—will you embrace your destiny, or will you fight it?"
Kael said nothing. The corpse-puppets stood ready, waiting for his command.
"The convergence cannot be stopped," the woman continued. "The seals are failing across the continent. In six months, in a year, in five years—the Abyss will break through regardless. The First Reaver understood this. He prepared a way to survive, to guide humanity through the transition."
"By resurrecting him?"
"By becoming him." The woman's eyes glistened. "Your body, his knowledge. Your power, his experience. Together, you could reshape the world before it ends."
The First Reaver's voice whispered again: "She speaks partial truth. I did prepare for the convergence. But my methods were... Extreme."
"What happened to the other Reavers?" Kael asked. "The ones before me?"
Silence. The woman's expression flickered.
"The cycle," Kael pressed. "The Memory Fragment mentioned a cycle. What did it mean?"
The woman's composure cracked. "How did you—you shouldn't have access to that knowledge yet."
"Tell me."
"The First Reaver discovered the truth about the seal network. About what happens when the Abyss finally breaks through. And he made a choice—to pass his power and memories to a successor, over and over, ensuring that someone would always exist to maintain the balance." The woman took a breath. "But the transfers were imperfect. Information was lost. Each new Reaver started with less knowledge, more instability. The cycle was designed to preserve humanity. Instead, it's been destroying it."
"And now you want to restart it."
"Now we want to complete it." The woman stepped forward. "You are the last chance. The final Reaver. Take the First Reaver's full legacy, integrate it completely, and you will have the power to save what remains of this world."
Kael's mind raced. The First Reaver's voice remained silent, waiting.
The woman's proposal made a terrible kind of sense. The seal network was failing. The cultists had been coordinating attacks across the continent, weakening the barriers deliberately. The convergence was coming whether anyone was ready or not.
But trusting cultists, trusting Zarek, trusting the First Reaver's methods—Kael had survived this long by trusting no one.
"You're lying," he said. "About something. I just haven't figured out what yet."
The woman smiled. "Maybe. But the convergence is coming regardless. Choose your allies carefully, Soul Reaver. The war is about to get much larger."
She turned and walked away, her cultists following. They disappeared into the tunnel, leaving Kael alone in the tomb.
The First Reaver's body remained on the platform, now truly empty, a husk of flesh and residual energy. Kael stared at it for a long moment.
"What aren't you telling me?" he asked the silence.
The voice came again, weaker now, fading. "The woman is a servant of Prime Zarek. She believes what she says. But Zarek serves the Blood God, who sees the convergence as an opportunity, not a crisis. If you integrate fully, you become a weapon of the Abyss, not humanity."
"Then why didn't you tell me before I harvested?"
"Because you would not have believed. Because you needed to see the power, to understand what you could become." A pause. "The woman is correct about one thing. The seals cannot hold. But there is another way—a path the First Reaver rejected because it required sacrifice beyond his willingness to give."
"What sacrifice?"
But the voice had faded, leaving Kael alone with his questions and his newly acquired power.
He stood in the tomb of the First Reaver, surrounded by the bodies of fallen cultists, and felt the weight of centuries pressing down on him. The convergence. The cycle. The choice that would determine the fate of the world.
And somewhere beyond the tomb, beyond the battlefield, Zarek was waiting.
Kael closed his eyes, accessing his new Soul Dominion ability. The corpses around him could move independently, follow commands, fight. But the real power lay in something else—a connection to every Soul Reaver who had come before, a lineage of death and rebirth stretching back to the first harvest.
He could feel them. Dozens of voices, hundreds, thousands, all the dead whose power had been harvested and passed down through generations. They whispered in his mind, fragments of knowledge and experience that would take years to fully integrate.
But one voice cut through the noise. Familiar. Impossible.
"You found it." Zarek's voice, speaking directly into his consciousness. "I knew you would. The First Reaver's tomb was always meant for you, Kael. The question is—will you use the power to stop me, or will you finally understand why I did what I did?"
Kael's hands clenched. The connection felt invasive, intimate, like someone reading his diary while he slept.
"The convergence cannot be stopped," Zarek continued. "But it can be shaped. The First Reaver understood that. The Orders understand nothing. They cling to dying ideals while the world burns around them."
"You're insane."
"I'm the only one who's sane." Zarek's voice hardened. "Six months. That's how long the seals will hold. Six months until the Abyss breaks through and everything humanity has built collapses. The only question is what rises from the ashes."
"And you think you can control that."
"I think I can give humanity a chance to survive. But only if the Soul Reaver's power is used properly. Only if someone with your abilities joins me instead of fighting me."
Kael opened his eyes. The tomb felt smaller now, the walls pressing in. He needed to get out. He needed to think.
"I'm not joining you," he said aloud.
"Then you doom us all." Zarek's voice faded. "But maybe that's inevitable. The First Reaver tried to save humanity. He failed. Each Reaver has failed. Maybe the cycle must complete itself in destruction."
The connection severed.
Kael stood in silence. Around him, the corpse-puppets waited for orders. Beyond the tomb, the battle continued. And in his mind, the whispers of a thousand dead Soul Reavers pressed against his consciousness.
Six months. The convergence was six months away.
He had six months to figure out whether Zarek was right. Whether the world was doomed regardless of what he did. Whether the First Reaver's legacy was salvation or damnation.
And he had six months to decide what kind of Reaver he wanted to be.
Kael walked out of the tomb, his army of corpses following. The cultists had vanished, fleeing into the smoke. The battlefield still raged beyond the hill, but Kael saw it differently now. Not a war between Orders and cultists, but a desperate struggle against an inevitable end.
He descended the hill, heading toward the sound of combat. The Silverguard patrol was still holding the secondary fortifications, their defensive line bolstered by his earlier advice. The Iron Vanguard had regrouped, their artillery finding new targets.
Kael moved through the chaos like a ghost, harvesting from the fallen, building his power. But his mind wasn't on the battle. It was on the tomb he'd left behind, on the choice he'd made, on the future that stretched before him.
Somewhere in the smoke, Zarek was watching. Waiting.
And in six months, the world would find out which of them was right.
Kael raised his hand, and three corpse-puppets surged forward to intercept a group of Entity-touched cultists emerging from the smoke. The battle continued.
But the real war was just beginning.
