The Blademaster moved.
Steel met steel with a sound like breaking ice. Time compressed.

Because the Cultist's coat had torn during the exchange, and visible on his chest, branded into the flesh itself, was a mark Kael recognized from stolen documents and whispered rumors in the Veiled Market. A sigil of interlocking triangles surrounding a single eye.
The Shadow Court's internal enforcers. Not hired killers. Not proxy soldiers.
Shadow Court's own operatives.

Which faction had sent tonight's team? And why?

The Shadow Court wanted to stop him. Their Silver Council had authorized his elimination. They were monitoring his movements closely enough to anticipate his next action. This represented a serious escalation from the scattered Cult cells he'd been picking apart over the past months.

The last possibility was the most disturbing. It suggested that the Shadow Court's infiltration of Thornwick's power structures had reached the point where they could direct the actions of middle-ranked Order officers. How long had the Shadow Court been building their network? And what were they building it toward?

Ancient machinery. Pre-Concordat construction. The same era when the Soul Reaver had first appeared in Kael's cell.

The Soul Reaver.

The Soul Reaver hummed against his hip like a living thing, eager for the hunt that was coming.

The Silver Council had mobilized at least six Shadow Court cells for his elimination, coordinating their efforts through encrypted channels that even Vex's network struggled to intercept. Dawn-trained operatives had been spotted in the Ashveil District, presumably continuing their search for the Knight of the Third Radiance who had fled the failed assassination attempt.

Human bones. Thousands of them, possibly tens of thousands, arranged in patterns that suggested ritual significance. Skulls faced upward with empty eye sockets. Femurs crossed like kindling. Ribcages formed geometric shapes that repeated with mathematical precision across the chamber's expanse.
This was not a burial site. This was a monument.
