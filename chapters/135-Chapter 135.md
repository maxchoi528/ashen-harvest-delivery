*Show me what you were.*

"Kill it!" The Dawn soldier with the shattered arm threw her mace. It tumbled end over end, a desperate arc that bounced uselessly off the Hollow Knight's back. The creature's head snapped toward her—eye sockets flaring brighter, attention caught for half a heartbeat.

Kael stood over the Stage 3 Hollow Knight, chest heaving, blood dripping from his wounds. Each breath sent fresh agony lancing through his ribs. If that soldier hadn't distracted it for even a second—

He dropped beside the Stage 3 corpse. This one was fresher. Steam still rose from the wound in its neck, curling into the cold tunnel air like the last exhalation of a dying man. Whatever had animated it was fighting to escape, to dissipate back into the Abyss from which it came. He could feel the energy already beginning to fray at the edges, dissolving into the ambient spiritual background like salt into water.
He didn't have long.
His palm hit cold flesh. The contact point buzzed with residual power, and his skin puckered with gooseflesh. He reached.

The world inverted.
Kael stood in darkness. Not the darkness of the tunnel—the darkness of a soul, vast and empty and hungry. This Hollow Knight's inner landscape was a void, stripped bare by whatever process had created it. No memories. No identity. Nothing but the Abyssal energy that kept it moving, pulsing in slow, tidal rhythms that made his own heartbeat sync against his will.
But beneath the void, something waited.
*Who are you?* The voice came from everywhere and nowhere—ancient, tired, broken. It resonated through his chest cavity and hummed behind his teeth. *Who walks in my grave?*
Kael's consciousness recoiled, spiritual form drawing back like a hand snatched from a flame. *A fragment. Something survived the Hollowing.*
*I survived nothing.* The voice grew louder, angrier, and the void around him shuddered with each word. *I was taken. Stolen. They hollowed me out and filled me with this—this hunger—*
A face materialized in the darkness. Young. Haunted. Eyes that had once been brown, now filmed with something that looked like tears made of shadow. The face hung in the void like a mask suspended in black water, features shifting between expressions as if cycling through the memories that had been stripped away.
*My name was Edric. Edric Vane. I was supposed to become an Inquisitor. Instead, they made me into this.*
"Inquisitor?" Kael's thought-voice echoed through the void. "The Order of Dawn's Inquisitorate?"
*They call it the Hollowing of the Vessel. A "necessary sacrifice" for the war effort.* Edric's ghost—because that's what it was, Kael realized, a ghost trapped inside its own murdered body—laughed bitterly. The sound cracked and splintered, each fragment carrying a different emotional frequency. *The Orders don't just seal the rifts. They create soldiers. Weapons. They take Awakened with potential and hollow them out, fill the empty vessels with Abyssal essence, and point them at the enemy. Sometimes the enemy is the cultists. Sometimes it's anyone who asks too many questions.*
Kael processed this. The Orders. The righteous defenders of humanity. Creating weapons by torturing Awakened into something less than human. The weight of the revelation settled into his stomach like swallowed stones.

*Because you're going to take something from me.* Edric's face drifted closer, his hollow eyes meeting Kael's with desperate intensity. The darkness around them compressed, the void pushing inward as if trying to crush them both into nothing. *The Hollowing didn't just corrupt me. It gave me a gift. A technique from when I still served the Sun God, before they took it away. I don't know if you can use it. I don't know if anyone can use it. The scarred woman's blade trembled in her grip, and her companions had gone rigid, weapons raised against an enemy they couldn't begin to comprehend. And on the altar, the thing that was not yet awake finished its final breath and opened eyes that burned with the light of the Abyss.

But the chains held. The cultists closed in. And somewhere in the chaos that was about to unfold, Zarek's smile never wavered.
"I wonder," the Prime said softly, "how much of your soul you can harvest before you have nothing left."
