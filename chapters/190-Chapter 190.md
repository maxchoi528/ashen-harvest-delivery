But first, he needed to lose them.

"The Reaver's heading for the drains!" Someone's voice echoed from behind. "Cut him off at Mercer's Junction!"

The Law of Soul Conservation. Every Expenditure a calculated risk.
He reached the roof just as the first of his pursuers caught up. A man in the black leathers of a Shadow Court operative, twin daggers already drawn.
"Stop running." The operative's voice was bored, confident. "You're just prolonging the inevitable."
Kael's hand found a loose chimney piece. "So's talking."

"Stupid." The operative pressed the advantage, blade dancing. "You're a Transcendent playing with Ascendants. The Court wants you alive. That doesn't mean intact."

A third figure appeared at the skylight above, dropping down behind Kael.
No. A fourth from the loading door.
They had him boxed in.

Better than nothing.

Not random cultist activity. Organized preparation for something larger.

And the Shadow Court worked in the spaces between, preparing for a world where the Veil no longer held.

He descended.
Level one: homeless populations, thieves, fugitives from surface law. They watched him pass with hollow eyes, too broken or too clever to interfere.
Level two: abandoned infrastructure. Steam pipes that no longer hissed, maintenance corridors sealed for decades. Ghosts of the city's industrial past.
Level three: the Undercroft.

And bodies.

The Whisper King was planning something far larger.

Unless the Whisper King had found one. Or made one.
