Two words, written in ash that still smelled faintly of corpse-fat and incense: WAREHOUSE DISTRICT. He pocketed the note and moved.
Thornwick's warehouse district sprawled along the city's eastern edge where the river split into industrial tributaries, feeding steam forges and rendering plants that never slept. Fog rolled thick between the buildings, carrying the chemical bite of alchemical runoff and something else beneath it—something that made Kael's newly awakened senses prickle like static before lightning. Something was wrong with the air itself.
[SOUL REAVER: Passive resonance detected. Spiritual disturbance in proximity.]

He moved.
Dorian had left no other instructions, which meant Kael was expected to find the correct warehouse through instinct or luck—whichever ran out first. The fog grew denser as he progressed, turning the industrial landscape into a maze of shapes that might have been machinery or men or something else entirely. [SOUL REAVER: Warning. Hostile intent detected. Multiple sources.]

He had been Awakened for three days now, long enough to understand his new abilities in theory but short on practice. The Soul Reaver ability had manifested during his execution—the hanging that should have killed him—and ever since then, the dead had whispered in a language he was still learning to translate.

The fog swirled around it in patterns that suggested wind, but Kael felt no breeze against his skin. The temperature here had dropped enough that frost crept along the chain links, glittering faintly in the dim light from a distant streetlamp.
A symbol had been painted on the warehouse wall in something dark and flaking. "Soul Reaver."

"I can try."

[SOUL REAVER: Active resonance detected. Potential harvest target beyond barrier.]

A consciousness pressed against the barrier from the other side, clawing at the chains with fingers that had long since worn to bone.

"You're stronger than the reports suggested."

The air tasted of copper and decay, thick enough that Kael could feel it coating his lungs with each breath. Shapes moved in the shadows at the far end—human shapes, dozens of them, standing in concentric circles around a central point that pulsed with sickly light.

"Gareth." The name tore from his throat before he could stop it.
The Order of Dawn's inquisitor stared back at him with eyes that had been carved out, leaving hollow sockets that wept clear fluid down his gaunt cheeks. His mouth moved, shaping words that produced no sound. His chest rose and fell with mechanical regularity, kept alive by tubes that ran from his arms into the floor, feeding his blood into channels carved into the stone.
The ritual circles were filled with that blood.
"Soul Reaver." The voice echoed from the shadows, and this time Kael saw its source—a figure robed in black, standing apart from the gathered cultists like a shepherd among sheep. The hood obscured the face, but the voice itself was feminine, cultured, laced with an accent Kael couldn't place. "You were not expected so soon. Dorian's information network has grown more efficient than we credited."

"I am the Voice of the Veil. I am the Herald of Return. I am the one who will watch the world burn so that something better may rise from the ashes." "You're insane."

Kael's blood went cold. The last battle, the one that had earned him his Awakening—that was supposed to be a secret. No witnesses. No records. Just Kael and the thing that had tried to devour him, and the strange new power that had risen from its death.
"Who sent you?"
"No one sent me. I serve a purpose older than your comprehension." The cultists had formed a loose perimeter now, their empty eyes fixed on Kael with hunger that transcended the only physical. "The seals are weakening. Seventy-two fractures in the fabric of reality, waiting to be widened. The Orders believe they can hold them forever, but forever is a concept that belongs to the Abyss, not to mortal minds." She stepped forward, and the shadows around her seemed to deepen, reaching for her like loyal hounds. "We will tear those seals down. We will let the Entities return to their rightful domain. And in the chaos that follows, something new will be born."
"You'll destroy everything."
"We will destroy what must die so that something greater may live." Her hand emerged from the shadows, holding a blade of black glass that seemed to drink the light around it. "But first, you will give me your power, Soul Reaver. The first Reaver was one of us, long ago. His abilities were never meant to leave our hands."
Kael understood then. The whispers in his skull, the abilities that had manifested from nothing, the way the dead had always seemed to call to him since his childhood—it wasn't random chance. It wasn't a miracle. Someone had planned this. Someone had set him on this path.
And they had been watching him ever since.
"No."
The word came out harder than he intended, edged with a fury that surprised him. He had spent so long being pushed and pulled by forces beyond his control, surviving from one crisis to the next, never knowing why the world seemed to hate him so consistently. Now he knew the reason. He had been a pawn, moved across a board he couldn't see by hands he couldn't identify.
Not anymore.
"No?" The Herald sounded amused. "You think you have a choice?"
"I think you've made a mistake."
Kael lunged.
The cultists moved to intercept him, hands outstretched, mouths open in silent screams. They were slow, weakened by whatever ritual they'd been performing, and Kael's knife found flesh before they could close their fingers around his throat. Blood sprayed hot across his face as he cut through the first line of defense, carving a path toward the Herald with all the grace of a hammer through glass.
[SOUL REAVER: Harvest available. Target: Cultist Adept. Potential extraction: Unknown skill fragment.]

She was faster than she looked, the black glass blade singing through the air as she parried his strike. Sparks flew where the two weapons met, and Kael felt the impact shudder through his arm like a physical blow. This wasn't some zealot playing at power. This was someone who had fought Awakened before and survived.
"You've killed your way here before." She circled him, blade held in a guard he didn't recognize. "The reports mentioned that. Twelve kills in the shadowrealm. Fourteen more since your Awakening. You move like someone who knows exactly how fragile the body is."
"I've died once. It didn't take."
"Then you're already halfway to understanding." She struck, a feint that forced him back a step. "Death is a door, Soul Reaver. Most people walk through it and are lost forever. But you—you walked back out. That makes you something unprecedented. Something that shouldn't exist."
"Funny. I feel pretty normal most of the time."
"You won't, for much longer."
She twisted, and Kael caught a glimpse of something moving beneath her robe—something with too many limbs, wrapped in shadow that seemed to writhe and shift with its own malevolent intelligence. The Herald wasn't just a cultist. She was something else, something that had been wearing human skin long enough to forget what it originally was.

[SOUL REAVER: Injury detected. Spiritual energy not required for healing. Recommend immediate harvest.]

[SOUL REAVER: Harvest initiated. Target: Cultist Adept. Extracting skill fragment... Acquired: Shadow Step (lesser). Movement speed increased by 15% for a few heartbeats. Cooldown: a few minutes.]

Voices. Thousands of them, screaming in languages that had died before humanity learned to speak.

He wouldn't let this happen.

The Shadow Step ability burned through his reserves, not spiritual energy but something else, something that felt like borrowed time, and Kael pushed it to its limit.

Someone was pushing from the other side. Someone who knew exactly how weak the barriers had become.

Something else.

What had the first Soul Reaver become, after centuries on the other side of the Veil?
And what would he do when he found out?
