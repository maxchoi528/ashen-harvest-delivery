Night fell quickly in the Ashenmoor Wastes. The sun descended behind the jagged peaks of the Greymarch, plunging the landscape into darkness punctuated by the ghostly glow of fractured seals. Kael made camp in the remains of a watchtower, its upper levels collapsed but its foundation still intact. He built a small fire using scavenged wood and whatever would burn, warming his hands while the cold seeped through the stones.
Three corpses lay nearby. The Corruptor, the fanatic, and a third cultist Kael had dispatched the previous night. He'd harvested from all of them already, but the night offered time for analysis. He needed to understand what he'd taken.
Corrupted Blade technique pulsed in his awareness like a second heartbeat. The ability allowed the user to infuse their weapon with corrupted spiritual energy, causing additional damage and potentially triggering Entity-infection in wounds. The corruption aspect was concerning—abilities extracted from Entity-touched carried contamination that could spread if used carelessly. But the core technique was valuable. Bladed combat was his specialty.
Fragmented Combat Instinct was simpler. Enhanced reflexes, faster reaction time, improved muscle coordination. Useful but not remarkable. He'd absorbed similar fragments from a dozen different corpses over the years. The combination of multiple fragments often created synergies he hadn't anticipated.
Vance's revelation about the Shadow Court troubled him more than any combat encounter. The Court was watching. The Court knew what he was. The Court was connected to multiple Orders, which meant the political web surrounding him was even more tangled than he'd assumed.
Kael pulled out a battered journal, its pages filled with his cramped handwriting. Notes on abilities harvested, locations visited, names of contacts and enemies. He'd been keeping records since Thornwick, building a map of the world as he experienced it.
New information joined the others:
- Shadow Court: deniable operations arm for multiple Orders, monitors region, aware of Reaver presence
- Council of Six: meeting in Thornwick, three weeks
- Great Rift seal degradation: six months projected before containment failure
- Cultist attacks: coordinated across multiple fronts, not random
He closed the journal and fed another piece of wood to the fire. Sparks rose toward the broken ceiling, disappearing into the darkness above.
A sound interrupted his thoughts. Footsteps on frozen ground, approaching from the east.
Kael extinguished the fire with a gesture, plunging the tower into darkness. He moved to a narrow window slot, peering out at the moonlit wasteland. Figures emerged from the treeline—five, no, seven people moving in a loose formation. They carried no torches, navigating by whatever spiritual sense Awakened possessed.
Not cultists. Cultists moved in larger groups and made no effort to conceal their approach. These figures moved like soldiers on patrol, efficient and coordinated.
Or like assassins.
Kael counted them again. Seven. Coordinated enough to be Order-trained. The Shadow Court wouldn't send such a small team for an assassination—they'd want deniability, which meant accidents and disappearances, not visible confrontations.
The group stopped fifty yards from the watchtower. One figure separated from the formation, walking toward the ruined structure with hands raised in a gesture of peace.
"Kael Ashborn." A woman's voice, calm and authoritative. "I know you're watching. We need to talk."
Kael remained silent, calculating angles and exits. The watchtower had one functional door and three window slots. Against seven Awakened, escape was possible but not guaranteed. Fighting was possible but costly. Dialogue was the third option, and the most dangerous.
"Who are you?" he called out.
"My name is Mira Vance. Commander Vance is my father."
Vance's daughter. Kael filed the name away, then considered the implications. The Commander had left an hour ago. His daughter arrived at night with an armed team. Coincidence was unlikely.

Kael emerged from the watchtower, positioning himself in the doorway where he could retreat if necessary. Mira Vance was tall, her dark hair pulled back in a practical braid. She wore the grey and silver of the Order of Dawn, but her insignia marked her as something other than standard military. A black eye within a silver sun—the symbol of the Dawn's intelligence division.
"Six Orders," Kael said. "Iron, Dawn, Spire, Flame, Tide, Verdant. The Dawn's intelligence division doesn't officially exist."

Corrupted Blade techniques are rare, and the Court has been hunting for practitioners of that school for decades."
