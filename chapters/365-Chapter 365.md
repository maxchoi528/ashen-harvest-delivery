The stone door groaned, ancient hinges protesting after centuries of silence. Kael pressed his palm against the cold surface, feeling the tremor of residual energy thrumming beneath his skin. Something waited on the other side. Something that had waited for him.
Three months of tracking cultists through Thornwick's underbelly had led here—to a forgotten catacomb beneath the Ironworks District, where steam vents created perpetual fog and the Mortician's Guild buried its most dangerous acquisitions. The symbols carved into these walls matched the ones burned into his palms when he'd first Awakened in his cell. The same sigils the executioner had tried to cut from his flesh before he rose from the gallows.
Behind him, Lyra's breath fogged in the chill air. Her daggers remained sheathed—she'd stopped asking why he came here, stopped pretending she wasn't following him. Two weeks of watching her watch him. Two weeks of wondering which version of her loyalty would surface when the moment came.
"Whatever's inside," she said, "the seals on this place are older than the Concordat."
Kael pushed the door wider. Darkness yawned beyond, thick and textured, swallowing the light from their phosphorescent lanterns. The air tasted of copper and something sweeter—rot aged past its worst, preserved by whatever force kept this tomb sealed.
"The Concordat is three hundred years old." He stepped across the threshold. "This place is older than the Orders themselves."
The tunnel stretched forty paces before opening into a circular chamber. Pillars carved with spiraling script ringed the perimeter. At the center stood a sarcophagus of black stone, its lid sculpted into the shape of a man kneeling, hands clasped around the hilt of a sword driven point-first into the floor.
Kael's Soul Reaver pulse surged without his command. The ability recognized something here—resonance, memory, blood.
"That's not a grave." Lyra's voice dropped. "That's a binding."
She was right. The figure wasn't praying. The pose was submission—the pose of someone pinned, held down, contained. The sword through the stone wasn't decoration. It was the lock.
"The first Reaver." Kael approached the sarcophagus. "The one who started everything."
"How do you know?"
"Because I can feel it." He pressed both hands to the stone lid. The cold bit through his gloves, sharp as teeth. "It's calling me. It's been calling me since I killed that cultist in the Ashworth cellar."
Lyra moved to his left, positioning herself between him and the tunnel mouth. Old habits. She still hadn't decided if she was guarding him or keeping him contained. "The Shadow Court's been asking about you. They know you took something from that cultist. Something that led you here."
"They can keep asking."
"They won't." She sheathed one dagger, keeping the other loose in her grip. "When they stop asking, they start taking. You know how they operate."
Kael knew. He'd seen their work in the eastern provinces—bodies left as messages, families erased to protect an operation, assets disappear into unmarked vans. The Shadow Court didn't negotiate. They assessed, positioned, and struck when least expected.
But the cold beneath his palms felt like the answer to a question he'd been asking since the gallows. Since before that, if he was honest. The power thrumming through his veins, the ability to touch the dead and pull their essence into himself—it had to come from somewhere. It had to mean something.
The Soul Reaver pulse intensified. His vision fractured, seeing double—the chamber as it was, and the chamber as it had been. Torchlight. Figures in robes. Seven of them, standing at the pillar positions, chanting in a language that predated modern Common.
Seven pillars. Seven founding members. Seven who bound the first Reaver because he had become something they feared.
The vision faded. Kael's hands trembled against the stone.
"Kael." Lyra's tone sharpened. "Your nose is bleeding."
He wiped his upper lip. Crimson smeared across his fingers. The Soul Reaver ability was reacting to something—a test, a challenge, a response from beyond the grave. The original Reaver's consciousness had been preserved, trapped in this binding, waiting.
Waiting for a successor.
"The seal on this binding," he said slowly, "is the same design as the seals on the rifts. The ones holding the Entities back."
Lyra went still. "That's not possible. The rift seals were constructed by the Six Orders during the Abyssal Wars. That was a thousand years ago."
"The Orders claim that." Kael traced the spiraling patterns on the sarcophagus lid. "But these symbols don't match any Order heraldry I've seen. They're older. Primal."
The pulse from his Soul Reaver ability changed—no longer a steady throb but a rhythmic beat, almost like a heartbeat. Something inside the sarcophagus responded.
"Whatever you're thinking," Lyra said, "don't. Breaking that seal could release more than memories. If the original Reaver is still conscious in there—"
"Conscious enough to answer questions." Kael pulled his hands back. "The cultists want the Soul Reaver ability for themselves. They've been conducting rituals, harvesting Awakened, trying to replicate what I do naturally. But they don't understand the cost."
"They don't care about cost. They care about results."
"Then they need to understand what results look like when the source is angry."
He stepped back from the sarcophagus, studying the chamber. The pillars held more than decorative script—each one contained a hollow channel running from floor to ceiling, designed to channel energy into the central binding. Someone had built this place as a containment cell, then a shrine, then forgotten it entirely.
"The cultists didn't lead me here." The realization struck him cold. "Someone wanted me to find this. Someone who knew I'd come."
"Zarek," Lyra said immediately. "He's been tracking your movements since Thornwick. He knows every location you've investigated."
"Zarek is a weapon dealer playing politics. He doesn't have access to pre-Order artifacts." Kael circled the chamber, examining each pillar. The script varied between them—seven different hands, seven different perspectives. "This was a collaborative binding. Seven practitioners, each contributing their own binding methodology. The kind of knowledge that doesn't exist in any Order archive."
"Because it's forbidden."
"Forbidden things still exist. They just change names and locations." He paused at the northern pillar, where the carving showed a figure holding a sword made of light. The same pose as the sarcophagus, but inverted—the wielder standing while shadows writhed at his feet. "The first Reaver wasn't just a practitioner. He was a war hero. Someone the Orders trusted with their secrets."
"Until he became a threat."
"Until they decided he was." Kael ran his fingers along the channel carved into the pillar's face. The stone was warm here, warmer than the surrounding rock. "The Abyssal Wars lasted two centuries. That's two hundred years of Entities breaking through, of Awakened dying in quantities that would collapse modern society. The Orders needed something that didn't run out of power, something that could harvest strength from the fallen instead of depleting itself."
"They created the Soul Reaver ability."
"They stole it. From the Entities themselves." The warmth beneath his fingers intensified. "The Entities don't die—they're fragments of the Abyss, manifestations of pure spiritual energy. When you kill one, its essence disperses back through the Veil and reconstitutes. But the first Reaver found a way to intercept that cycle. To capture the dispersed energy before it recycled."
Lyra's expression tightened. "That would make him the most powerful Awakened in history."
"That made him dangerous." Kael released the pillar. "Not to the Entities. To the people who discovered what he could do. Seven Orders, each built on the foundation of containing Abyssal power. What happens when someone develops the ability to contain Abyssal power within himself?"
The heartbeat pulse from the sarcophagus grew stronger. Kael could almost hear it now—deep, resonant, thrumming through the stone floor and into his bones.
"He becomes the template," Lyra said quietly. "The Orders can't control him. They can't replicate him. They can only fear him."
"So they bound him. Contained him. Built the Soul Reaver ability into their own practitioners as a controlled echo of what he'd been, then erased his name from history." Kael turned to face the sarcophagus. "But they couldn't destroy him. Because the ability doesn't work that way. Soul energy can't be created or destroyed. The same power that made him a Reaver is the same power that kept his binding intact."
"The binding is failing." Lyra moved to his side, daggers drawn. "Is that what you're saying?"
"I'm saying someone sent me here for a reason. Either to finish what the Orders started, or to give me a choice they never gave him." Kael studied the sword embedded in the stone floor—the hilt, the crossguard, the faded inscription running down the blade. "I need to know what I'm dealing with."
"You open that seal, and you might not be able to close it again."
"I know."
"The Shadow Court will know the moment you do. They'll come for you."
"They already know I'm here. Lyra, I've known since the Ashworth cellar. Someone sold my position. Someone who knew exactly where I'd end up." He met her eyes. "The question is whether you're here because you're tracking me for them, or because you want to see this through."
The silence stretched. Lantern light flickered across her face, shadows pooling in the hollows of her cheeks. She had the look of someone calculating angles, weighing outcomes, running through scenarios that ended in blood.
"I've been Shadow Court since I was fourteen," she said finally. "They made me. Broke me. Remade me into something useful. Every order I've followed, every body I've left behind—they own that. They think they own me."
"And now?"
"Now I'm standing in a room with a man who eats souls and asks questions later." The corner of her mouth twitched—not a smile. "Let's see what the dead have to say."
Kael pressed his palm to the sarcophagus lid. The stone recognized him—the Soul Reaver ability recognized itself—and the binding responded. The sword in the floor began to rise, pulled upward by invisible forces, inch by inch, each movement accompanied by the grinding of stone against stone.
The air pressure shifted. The lanterns dimmed. And when the blade cleared the floor, when it hung suspended above the sarcophagus like a pendulum waiting to fall, the figure sculpted on the lid opened its eyes.
Not flesh. Not stone. Something between—light and shadow woven together, a consciousness that had endured centuries of imprisonment.
**BINDING FRAGMENT DETECTED.**
**ORIGINAL SOULREAVER SIGNATURE IDENTIFIED.**
**INITIATING RESONANCE COMPATIBILITY CHECK...**
Kael staggered as information flooded through him. Not memories—the original Reaver's consciousness was too fragmented for that—but data. Battle records. Ability specifications. A complete technical understanding of the Soul Reaver methodology, from initial Awakening through the transference protocols that allowed successive Reavers to inherit the power.
The binding hadn't just contained the first Reaver. It had catalogued him. Encoded his knowledge into the seal itself, waiting for someone with compatible resonance to download.
"Compatible," the figure spoke. The voice came from everywhere and nowhere, vibrating through Kael's chest. "You carry my blood. My line. They thought they severed us. They were wrong."
Lyra's daggers clattered against the floor. She stood frozen, eyes fixed on the luminous figure, her breath coming in short gasps.
"The Orders," Kael said. "They bound you because they were afraid."
"They bound me because I refused to become their weapon." The figure's gaze—hollow points of concentrated darkness—fixed on Kael. "They wanted the ability. Controlled. Contained. Useful for sealing rifts, harvesting Entity essence, maintaining their precious network of prisons. But they didn't want what the ability truly offered."
"Enlighten me."
"Power without limit. The Entities are infinite. The Abyss regenerates what we destroy. Every war we fight, we lose a little more. The Veil weakens. The seals crack. In a thousand years, there will be nothing left but entities walking freely, and humanity reduced to cattle." The figure drifted higher, the sword following its ascent. "But a Reaver doesn't fight the Abyss. A Reaver consumes it. Every Entity killed becomes strength for the Reaver. Every death feeds the cycle instead of breaking it. The Abyss cannot regenerate what we take."
Kael's Soul Reaver ability pulsed in response, hungry, eager. He'd always known the power felt right, felt natural, as if it belonged inside him. Hearing the truth behind it—
"They feared you would become something beyond control."
"I was beyond control. I killed fourteen Order masters in a single night when they tried to bind me. Not because I was evil. Because they were small." The figure's form flickered, destabilizing. "The binding has lasted longer than it should. I preserved myself through will, through spite, through the knowledge that someone would eventually find this place. Someone carrying my lineage. Someone who could finish what I started."
Lyra recovered her daggers, positioning herself at Kael's flank. Her face had gone pale, but her hands were steady. "If the binding fails completely, what happens to you?"
"I disperse. My consciousness fragments, my knowledge encoded into whoever inherits the resonance." The figure turned toward her. "But the girl asks the wrong question. The binding isn't failing. Someone is actively weakening it. Someone who wants what I carry, and is willing to destroy me to obtain it."
The lantern nearest the tunnel mouth exploded. Glass sprayed across the stone floor. In the sudden darkness, Kael heard footsteps—heavy, purposeful, more than one set.
"Lyra." His voice dropped to a whisper. "The entrance."
"I sealed it behind us. Warded it."
"Then they found another way in."
The footsteps grew louder. Kael activated his Soul Reaver sense, reaching out into the darkness. Four presences. Five. All of them Awakened, all of them carrying the faint taint of Entity corruption that marked cultists who had worked too closely with Abyssal power.
The original Reaver's consciousness flickered again. "They cannot enter the inner chamber while the binding holds. But they will try to break it. My fragments are the key to their ritual—the method they've been searching for since they discovered my tomb existed."
"How long can you hold?"
"Minutes. Maybe less. The binding was designed to contain spiritual energy, not resist physical assault. If they bring enough force—"
Lyra moved before he finished speaking. She sprinted toward the northern tunnel, where the footsteps concentrated. Two daggers appeared in her hands, their edges gleaming with spiritual reinforcement.
"What are you doing?" Kael called after her.
"Buying time!" She vanished into the darkness. A moment later, the sound of steel meeting steel echoed through the chamber.
The original Reaver's form solidified, burning brighter. "You have a choice, inheritor. I can transfer my remaining knowledge to you now—the complete Soul Reaver methodology, every technique I've developed, every countermeasure against the Orders' binding protocols. But the transfer will leave me vulnerable. The cultists will break through. And you will have seconds to escape before they claim what's left."
"Or?"
"Or I hold the binding until you leave. You take the knowledge you've already gained, the resonance compatibility you've established, and you develop the rest yourself. It will take years. Decades. But you walk out of here alive, and the choice of what to do with this power remains entirely yours."
Kael looked toward the tunnel where Lyra had disappeared. The sounds of combat intensified—metal clashing, bodies falling, her voice shouting words he couldn't make out. She was Shadow Court. She knew how to survive ambushes. But five Awakened cultists against one rogue agent—
The binding pulsed. The sword, waiting.
"You asked me what the ability truly offered," Kael said. "You said it was power without limit."
"Truth. Not comfort."
"I didn't ask for comfort." He stepped closer to the sarcophagus, close enough to feel the heat radiating from the original Reaver's form. "Transfer everything. Now."
"Even knowing what it will cost?"
"I already know the cost. I've been paying it since the gallows." Kael extended his hand toward the luminous figure. "I'm done running from this. Whatever I'm becoming—I'm becoming it on my terms."
The original Reaver's form dissolved. Light poured from the empty shell, streaming into Kael's outstretched palm, racing up his arm, flooding his consciousness with a thousand years of accumulated knowledge. Battle techniques. Ritual configurations. The location of every rift seal the Orders had constructed, and the weakness in each one. Names. Faces. Betrayals stretching back to the founding of the first Order.
**SOUL HARVEST: TRANSMISSION INCOMING.**
**ORIGINAL SOULREAVER MEMORY ARCHIVE UNLOCKING...**
**WARNING: COMPATIBILITY THRESHOLD EXCEEDED.**
**PARTIAL INTEGRATION IN PROGRESS.**
Pain. Absolute, blinding, threatening to tear his mind apart. Kael screamed—a raw, guttural sound that echoed off the chamber walls—and collapsed to his knees.
The binding shattered. The sword clattered to the floor. And from the tunnel mouths, figures emerged, robed in black, faces hidden behind masks carved to resemble screaming faces.
Kael struggled to his feet. The knowledge burned inside him, half-integrated, a library on fire. He could see things now—things he shouldn't be able to see. The spiritual cores of the cultists blazing like beacons. The Entity corruption threading through their bodies like parasitic vines. The true shape of the chamber beneath the physical architecture, all those centuries of binding energy.
And he could see Lyra, bleeding from a wound on her shoulder, holding a corridor against three assailants with nothing but fury and precision.
"Get away from him!" One of the cultists threw something—a glass vial that shattered against Kael's chest, releasing a cloud of purple smoke that stank of sulfur and rotting flowers.
Entity essence. Concentrated, distilled, weaponized. The same substance the cultists had been harvesting in the Ashworth cellar, multiplied a thousandfold.
**CONTAMINATION DETECTED.**
**ABYSSAL CORRUPTION SPREADING.**
**INITIATING SOULREAVER PURGE PROTOCOL...**
Kael felt the corruption trying to take root—cold tendrils wrapping around his spiritual core, preparing to rewrite his identity. He'd seen what happened to Awakened who absorbed too much Entity essence. They'd become hollow shells, puppets animated by Abyssal will.
But the Soul Reaver ability didn't care about contamination. It cared about consumption.
The corruption ignited. Kael drew it in, pulled it apart, converted its structure into raw energy that flooded his depleted reserves. The cultists' faces twisted behind their masks—shock, fear, the dawning recognition that they'd just made a terrible mistake.
"You wanted to know what the Soul Reaver truly is?" Kael's voice came out wrong, doubled, layered with echoes of the original Reaver's bass undertone. "It's this. It's everything. The Abyss feeds us whether we want it to or not. The only question is whether we choose what we consume."
He moved. The first cultist died before realizing he'd been touched—Kael's hand closing around his throat, the Soul Reaver ability screaming as it extracted decades of accumulated spiritual energy in seconds.
**SOUL HARVEST: You extracted 847 units of spiritual essence from the remains. ABILITY FRAGMENT ACQUIRED: Entity Sight (Lesser).**
The second cultist swung a ritual blade inscribed with binding sigils. Kael caught the weapon by the blade, ignored the cuts opening his palm, and headbutted the man so hard the cultist's mask cracked in three places. He dropped the blade, reversed his grip, and drove it upward through the cultist's sternum.
**SOUL HARVEST: You extracted 612 units of spiritual essence from the remains. MEMORY FRAGMENT ACQUIRED: Cult ritual configuration (partial).**
Two left. They hesitated, reassessing, calculating whether the target was worth the cost. Kael gave them no time to decide. He sprinted toward the northern corridor, toward Lyra's position, toward the three remaining cultists who were already starting to break when they saw their companions fall.
The original Reaver's knowledge made him lethal in ways he hadn't been minutes ago. He saw the flow of spiritual energy through bodies, predicted attacks before they launched, identified the structural weaknesses in every stance. When the first cultist thrust a spear reinforced with Entity essence, Kael sidestepped, deflected the shaft with his forearm, and drove his bleeding palm directly into the man's solar plexus.
The extraction took three seconds. The body dropped.
**SOUL HARVEST: You extracted 1,203 units of spiritual essence from the remains. ABILITY FRAGMENT ACQUIRED: Void Step (Lesser).**
Lyra finished the second cultist with a dagger across the throat, then kicked the body away and pressed her back against the corridor wall. Blood soaked her left sleeve, dripping from the wound on her shoulder. Her breathing came ragged, but her eyes were sharp.
"The main chamber," she gasped. "There are more coming. I heard them coordinate before I engaged."
"How many?"
"Ten. Maybe more. They had this place surrounded before we even entered." She pushed off the wall, swaying slightly. "This was a trap. They knew exactly where we'd be."
"They knew where I'd be. You were supposed to be their backup." Kael grabbed her arm, steadying her. "The Shadow Court sold you out."
"I know." Her expression didn't change. "I knew before I walked in here. But I wanted to see this through anyway."
Three cultists appeared at the far end of the corridor. Kael raised his hand, felt the new abilities stirring—Entity Sight showing him the corruption threading through their bodies, Void Step promising escape if things went wrong. But the knowledge from the original Reaver offered other options. Better options.
"The chamber behind us connects to an older tunnel system," he said. "Pre-Order construction. The cultists won't know about it."
"How do you know that?"
"Because the original Reaver built it himself. As an escape route, in case the binding ever weakened enough for him to act." Kael pulled Lyra toward the chamber. "He spent centuries planning his survival. I intend to benefit from his paranoia."
They retreated through the sarcophagus chamber, now filled with cultists streaming in through multiple tunnel mouths. Kael didn't stop to fight—every moment spent here was a moment more for reinforcements to arrive. He pulled Lyra toward the eastern pillar, where the original Reaver's consciousness had shown him a hidden seam in the stone.
"Press here," he instructed. "When I tell you."
The cultists charged. Ten of them, maybe twelve, all carrying weapons inscribed with binding sigils and corruption-infused edges. They spread out to flank, professional formation, the kind of coordination that came from months of training together.
Kael's hand found the seam. A button, carved to look like decorative stonework, hidden for centuries.
"Now."
Lyra slammed her palm against the stone. The pillar section slid aside, revealing a passage barely wide enough for one person. Kael shoved her through, then followed, pulling the door shut behind him. The sound of grinding stone masked the cultists' shouts of frustration.
"Move," he ordered. "Don't stop until we reach the surface."
They ran. The passage spiraled downward, then up, then sideways through geological formations that predated human construction. Kael's Entity Sight showed him the path—a faint residual trace left by the original Reaver's spiritual signature, still active after all these centuries.
"Will they follow?" Lyra's voice echoed in the confined space.
"Some will try. But this tunnel system wasn't built to be found. Even if they search, they'll need days to map it properly." Kael ducked under a low-hanging rock formation. "We have hours."
"Hours before what?"
"Before the Shadow Court realizes I've taken something they wanted. And decides to take it back by force."
The passage opened into a larger cavern, where phosphorescent moss coated the walls and a underground stream carved a path toward distant light. Dawn. Or dusk. Kael couldn't tell without seeing the sky.
Lyra stopped, pressing her hand against the wound on her shoulder. The bleeding had slowed, but the spiritual contamination from the cultist blades would spread if not treated. She needed a healer. Proper medical attention. Things she wouldn't get if the Shadow Court caught them first.
"Kael." Her voice came out quieter now, exhausted. "What you did in there. The transfer. You took on the original Reaver's knowledge."
"Part of it. Most of it." He turned to face her. "The integration isn't complete. There's still gaps, corrupted files, things I can't access without more power."
"But you know what he was. What he wanted."
"I know he was betrayed by the people who should have protected him. I know the Orders have been lying about the Abyssal Wars for a thousand years. And I know there are seventy-two rift seals holding back the Entities, and every single one of them is failing faster than the Orders admit." Kael's expression hardened. "I know the Shadow Court has been preparing for that failure. And I know they think I'm the key to their survival or their domination."
"Which is it?"
"That's what I'm going to find out." He reached into his coat, pulling out the sword that had hung suspended above the sarcophagus. It had come with him through the passage, —shrunk to a manageable size, tucked against his ribs without him remembering grabbing it. The original Reaver's final gift. "But first, we need to disappear. The cultists will report what happened. The Shadow Court will mobilize. Everyone who wants the Soul Reaver ability will know exactly where I am."
"Where do we go?"
Kael considered the map burning in his mind—seventy-two rifts, each one a wound in reality, each one sealed by techniques the Orders had stolen from the original Reaver's methodology. He could see their locations now. Their weaknesses. Their connections to the Entity corruption spreading through Thornwick's population.
"There's a place in the northern marshes," he said slowly. "Ancient. Pre-Order. The cultists have been operating there for months, conducting rituals that require proximity to a weakening seal. If we can reach it before they realize we're coming—"
"You want to investigate a cultist stronghold. With no backup, no extraction plan, and a wounded agent who may or may not still be loyal to the organization trying to kill us."
"I want to understand what we're fighting." Kael met her eyes. "The original Reaver said the Soul Reaver ability was designed to consume the Abyss. To turn the Entities' strength against them. But the cultists aren't trying to consume—they're trying to amplify. They're not feeding on the Entities. They're feeding the Entities."
Lyra stared at him for a long moment. The underground stream gurgled past, carrying silt and organic debris toward the distant light. Somewhere above them, the sun was rising or setting. Somewhere above them, the Shadow Court was already mobilizing.
"You've changed," she said finally. "Since the transfer."
"I've learned what I'm capable of." Kael sheathed the ancient sword. "The question is whether you're going to help me prove it."
Lyra laughed—a short, harsh sound that echoed off the cavern walls. She pulled a small vial from her belt, uncorked it, and drank the contents in a single swallow. The wound on her shoulder stopped bleeding immediately, spiritual energy knitting the flesh back together.
"I told you," she said. "They made me. But they don't own me." She tucked the empty vial away and drew her daggers. "Northern marshes. Let's see what the cultists are hiding."
They climbed toward the light, leaving the original Reaver's tomb behind. The knowledge he'd gained burned inside Kael—partial, powerful, dangerous. He could feel the Entities now, more clearly than ever before. Their presence in the world, their influence seeping through cracks in the Veil, their patient hunger waiting for the seals to fail completely.
Seventy-two rifts. Seventy-two prisons holding back absolute destruction.
And somewhere in Thornwick, a man named Zarek was planning something that required the Soul Reaver ability.
Kael emerged from the underground stream into grey morning light. The marshes stretched before him, fog-shrouded and treacherous, dotted with the skeletal remains of ancient trees. Somewhere in that fog, cultists were preparing rituals designed to accelerate the world's end.
He had seventy-two hours before the Shadow Court tracked him down. Seventy-two hours to find answers. Seventy-two hours to decide whether he was going to save this broken world or consume it entirely.
Lyra appeared beside him, water streaming from her dark hair. "Which direction?"
Kael extended his senses, feeling the currents of spiritual energy flowing through the marshland. There—a disturbance. A concentration of corrupted essence, centered around something that pulsed with Abyssal resonance.
"East," he said. "Half a day's walk. Maybe less if we move fast."
"And when we find what they're hiding?"
Kael touched the sword at his hip. The original Reaver's knowledge hummed in response, eager and hungry.
"Then we take it from them. Whatever it is. Whatever it costs." He stepped into the fog. "The Soul Reaver was never meant to be controlled. Time the Orders learned that again."
They walked east, into the mist, toward the place where the Veil was thinnest and the cultists' plans were closest to fruition. Behind them, the tomb of the original Reaver fell silent, its purpose finally fulfilled after a thousand years of waiting.
Ahead, the war for reality's survival was about to begin.
And Kael Ashborn—latest in a hidden lineage of Soul Reavers, carrier of abilities the Orders had tried to bury, inheritor of a dead man's vengeance—was ready to end it his way.
