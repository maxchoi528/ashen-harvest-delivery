Kael said nothing.
"It means you're not just a Soul Reaver anymore." Thorne leaned forward, his elbows on his knees. "You're a weapon. The only weapon that can maintain the seal network without rituals, without sacrifice, without centuries of infrastructure. The Orders will want to use you. The cultists will want to destroy you. And the Shadow Court—"
He paused.
"What about the Shadow Court?" Kael asked.
"They've been in contact with us. They want you released." Thorne's expression was unreadable. "We've refused. The question is what to do with you now."
"Let me go."
"That's not going to happen." Thorne stood, pushing the chair back. "Not until we understand what you are. Not until we know whether the original Soul Reaver's madness is something that can be... Inherited."
The word hung in the air between them. Kael's pulse quickened. His fingers closed around Soul Reaver's hilt—the blade was still at his hip, the Order hadn't removed it. They'd left him armed in a locked room.
Thorne noticed the direction of his gaze. "We can't remove it. The weapon bonds to its bearer. Anyone who tries to take it dies." He moved toward the door. "We'll be watching, Ashborn. Every moment. If you show any signs of the original's instability..."
He didn't finish the sentence. He didn't need to.
The door closed behind him. The lock engaged.
Kael sat in the silence, bound to the bed, Soul Reaver heavy at his hip. Through the window, the guards watched without blinking.
He tested the restraints again. They didn't budge. Spiritual energy laced through the bindings, suppressing whatever reserves he might have regenerated. He wasn't going anywhere.
He closed his eyes and reached for the darkness inside himself. Shadow Step. The technique he'd harvested from the Hollow Knight—the ability to fold through the Veil between shadows. In this room, in this light, it was useless.
But the sun was setting. He'd seen it through the gauze curtains before Thorne had entered. The shadows in the room's corners were growing longer. Deeper.
And somewhere beyond the iron door, someone was whispering.
