The first explosion tore through Thornwick's Market District at dawn.
Kael threw himself behind a steam-powered merchant's cart as glass shards sang through the morning air. A second detonation followed—closer, near the Ironbridge. The cobblestones trembled beneath his boots.
Three breaches in the city's wards. Three almost simultaneously.
That meant organization. That meant intent.
He rose from cover, scanning the skyline. Smoke billowed from three distinct points—the Market District, the Ironbridge, and somewhere near the Cathedral of Dawn. Each pillar of flame aligned with the positions he'd memorized from Thornwick's sealed rifts.
Eleven rifts. Eleven prisons holding back the Abyss. The Six Orders had spent centuries maintaining the seals, rotating guards, performing the rituals that kept the membrane between worlds intact.
Someone had just declared war on that entire system.
A woman sprinted past him, clutching a child to her chest. The child's face was slack with terror. Neither spared Kael a glance.
Another explosion shook the street. Closer.
Kael moved against the flow of fleeing citizens, heading toward the Ironbridge. If the attacks were coordinated, they'd strike the weakest points first—the rifts already showing stress fractures. The Ironbridge seal had degraded fastest over the past month. He didn't know why. He'd hoped no one else had noticed.
Clearly, someone had.
A figure in silver-plated armor materialized from the smoke ahead—the insignia of the Order of Dawn burned bright on her pauldron. She spotted him and skidded to a halt.
"Soul Reaver." Her voice carried the flat certainty of someone who'd trained herself not to show fear. "The wards are failing across the city. We need every Awakened."
"You're asking a Reaver to fight for the Orders?"
"I'm informing you that every able body fights or dies. The choice is yours." She thrust a blade toward him, hilt-first. The steel bore Dawn Knight runes—consecrated weapons meant for hunting Abyssal entities. "But if you won't fight beside us, get out of the city before the Enforcers arrive. They'll assume you're part of this."
Kael studied the blade. Spiritual energy hummed along its edge, bright enough to leave afterimages in his vision.
"And if I take this and walk away?"
"Then I know where to find you." She didn't blink. "We always find Reavers who consort with the enemy."
He caught the blade by its edge, letting the runes sear his palm. Blood welled, black as pitch. The woman flinched but held her ground.
"I'll fight," Kael said. "Not for the Orders. Not for your laws. But the things coming through those rifts kill everyone equally."
"Acceptable." She was already turning. "The Ironbridge. The seal is failing. Three cultists breached the outer ward and are working on the inner barrier."
She vanished into the smoke like she'd never existed.
Kael followed the trail of destruction toward the Ironbridge. Bodies littered the street—common folk caught in the crossfire of spiritual warfare. Some still twitched, their spiritual energy leaking away like sand through fingers. He passed a dead Iron Vanguard soldier, throat torn open by something with claws. The soldier's eyes stared at nothing. Residual power clung to his corpse, dim but present.
Kael knelt. His fingers found the cooling flesh.
The Soul Reaver stirred in his chest, a cold hunger that never asked permission. He let it rise.
