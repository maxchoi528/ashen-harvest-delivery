The patrol leader's head rolled across the mud, and Kael retrieved the corpse before it finished falling.

[Soul Harvest: You extracted Sentinel's Instinct from the remains. Memory fragment acquired.]

Vera Blackthorn stepped forward, her scarred face unreadable. "Three Order of Dawn scouts. They weren't supposed to be this far south."

The canyon led to an abandoned mining settlement called Ashford Hollow. Decades ago, the site had produced iron ore for Thornwick's foundries. Now it served as a forward operating base for the combined forces that would push into the Ashenmoor Wastes.
Kael's team assembled in what had once been the mine foreman's office. Four wooden walls, a single window overlooking the valley below, and a cast-iron stove that someone had lit despite the mild weather. Smoke curled from the chimney.
"The Order of Dawn isn't the only force operating in this sector," Vera said. She spread a map across the table—a patchwork of intelligence reports, hand-drawn routes, and red X marks indicating confirmed cultist activity. "The Iron Vanguard has a company at Miller's Crossing, eight miles east. The Arcane Spire maintains a research station near the old cathedral ruins."

"For the past few months, cultist activity has increased across every sector. Coordinated attacks on Order patrols. Assassinations of field commanders. Sabotage of supply lines."
"The Great Rift," Kael said.
"The seal is failing." Vera's voice dropped. "And the cultists know it too. They're not just harassing the Orders—they're preparing for something."
Kael studied the map. Red X marks clustered around three locations: the old cathedral ruins, Miller's Crossing, and a point marked only as "Site 7" in faded ink.
"Site 7," he said. "What is it?"
"We don't know." Vera's jaw tightened. "But the cultists have been excavating there for weeks. Whatever they're looking for, they expect to find it before the Great Rift fails."
Dorin spoke up from the corner. "You trust her?"

"You're suggesting," Kael said slowly, "that the organization responsible for the continent's soul-essence supply is funneling resources to the cults trying to break the seals."
"I'm suggesting," Vera replied, "that someone inside the Mortician's Guild is working with the cultists. Someone high up. Someone with access to the supply chain."
Kael studied the map. The Mortician's Guild controlled the largest legal source of soul-essence in the region. If they were compromised—if even a faction within the organization was supporting the cults—then the entire supply chain was tainted.
"The Order of Dawn," he said. "They're buying soul-essence from the Mortician's Guild. If the Guild is compromised—"
"Then the Order of Dawn might be funding their own destruction without knowing it." Vera nodded grimly. "The Iron Vanguard too. The Arcane Spire. All of them purchase from the same sources. All of them blind to what's happening behind the scenes."
Dorin's hand moved away from his sword hilt. His expression had shifted from hostility to something closer to confusion.
"How do you know all this?" he asked Vera.
"Because my sister worked for the Guild." Vera's voice hardened. "She discovered the discrepancy in the supply chain months ago. She tried to report it. She disappeared soon after."
Kael watched Vera's composure crack—a twitch at the corner of her mouth, her fingers curling against her palm. Vera Blackthorn wasn't just an intelligence source. She was a woman with a personal vendetta—and that made her either the most reliable or the most dangerous member of this team.
"Where is your sister now?" Kael asked.
"I don't know." Vera's gaze dropped to the map. "But I intend to find out."
Night fell over Ashford Hollow. The team rested in shifts, two hours on, four hours off. Kael took the first watch alone, standing on the roof of the foreman's office with a clear view of the valley below.
The Soul Reaver talent hummed in his chest—a constant presence since his execution and resurrection. He still didn't understand where the power came from. The notifications appeared in his vision like clockwork. The harvesting worked without fail. But the origin remained a mystery.
Somewhere in the world, another Soul Reaver had existed before him. Someone had developed this technique, refined it, left traces of their knowledge in ancient tablets and forgotten tombs. That person's fate was unknown. Dead, probably. Soul Reavers didn't live long once the Orders learned what they could do.
Kael had killed three Order of Dawn scouts today. That number would grow. The Orders would respond. Eventually, they would send hunters.
Let them come.
A rustling sound from the valley below. Kael dropped into a crouch, his hand finding the hilt of his blade. The noise came again—footsteps on loose stone, deliberate and quiet.
He waited.
A figure emerged from the treeline twenty yards distant. Female. Cloaked in black. She moved with the confidence of someone who knew the terrain and didn't fear what might be waiting.
"Shadow Court operative," Kael called out. "State your business."
The figure stopped. After a moment, she pulled back her hood, revealing a face marked by old scars and sharper eyes.
"Kael Ashborn," Lyra Vex said. "I heard you were in Thornwick. Didn't expect to find you here."
"Lyra." Kael's grip on his blade relaxed a fraction. "What brings you to Ashford Hollow?"
"The same thing that brings you, I imagine." Lyra stepped closer, her boots crunching on frost-hardened earth. "The cultist excavation at Site 7. The Order of Dawn patrol you left bleeding in that canyon. The fact that something big is happening in the Ashenmoor Wastes, and everyone with eyes can see it except the people who should be stopping it."
"You're Shadow Court. You don't work with the Orders."
"The Shadow Court works for whoever pays." Lyra's smile was thin and humorless. "But we're not stupid. If the Great Rift fails, there won't be anyone left to pay us. The Abyssal Entities don't negotiate. They don't hire intermediaries. They just consume."
Kael studied her. Lyra Vex had been a thorn in his side since his resurrection—a spy, an assassin, and possibly the only person in Thornwick who knew what he truly was and hadn't tried to kill him on sight.
"You want to talk about Site 7," he said.
"I want to propose an alliance." Lyra produced a small object from her cloak—a glass vial filled with swirling silver light. "Soul-essence. High-grade. Stolen from a Mortician's Guild shipment days ago. Enough to fund a small army."

"I want access to whatever the cultists find at Site 7. And I want you to use your... Unique talents... To extract information from their leaders."
The offer hung in the cold air between them. Kael considered.
The Soul Reaver ability worked on corpses. But it could also extract fragments from the dying—the final moments before death, when the soul was still tethered to the body. The cultist leaders at Site 7 would be valuable sources of information. And corpses, once killed, could be harvested for their skills and memories.
"What guarantees do I have?" Kael asked. "The Shadow Court has a reputation for betrayal."
"Everyone has a reputation." Lyra pocketed the vial. "But I'm not offering you the Shadow Court's word. I'm offering you mine. And I have my own reasons for wanting the cultists stopped."
"Reasons?"
"My former handlers in the Shadow Court have been compromised. Someone inside the organization is feeding information to the cultists. I've been cut off from my network, disavowed, marked for termination." Lyra's expression hardened. "Someone in the shadows is playing a game that involves destroying the seals. I want to know who."
Kael made his decision. "We'll move on Site 7 at dawn. If your information is valuable, you can accompany us. If not—"
"Then you'll harvest me for parts." Lyra nodded. "Fair enough."
The team assembled before sunrise. Vera, Dorin, and three others Kael had recruited from the margins of Thornwick society—a former smuggler named Colm, a disgraced Order of Dawn initiate named Thessaly, and an aging mercenary who went by the single name of Rook.
Lyra arrived separately, emerging from the treeline as the first light crept over the eastern hills. She wore practical traveling clothes and carried a pair of daggers that gleamed with faint enchantment.
"The canyon pass," Vera said, pointing to a narrow defile on the map. "It leads directly to Site 7. But the cultists will have guards."

"Zarek," he repeated.
"You know him?" Lyra's eyes narrowed.
"We've met." Kael's voice came out flat, stripped of inflection.
"Zarek is dangerous," Lyra said. "He's been leading the cult's expansion in Thornwick for the past year. Assassinations, sabotage, recruiting Awakened who are desperate or corrupt. Whatever they're building at Site 7, he's at the center of it."

Kael sensed it before he saw it—a distortion in the spiritual energy around them, like heat shimmer rising from sun-baked sand. The 72 sealed rifts. The Abyssal Entities. The fabric of reality itself, wearing thin.

[Soul Harvest: You extracted Shadow Weaving technique from the remains. Minor spiritual corruption detected.]
