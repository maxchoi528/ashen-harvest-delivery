Two hundred years.

Harvested from the remains of an Order commander killed during last month's border skirmish."
Stage 5. Demigod-class. The energy contained within those bone fragments could fuel a small city's spiritual defenses for a year. Someone had killed an Order commander and harvested the corpse for this moment.

Kael felt the woman's Demigod pressure pressing against his senses. Stage 5. Demigod. The same realm he occupied. But she had been Demigod for decades. He had only reached this level weeks ago through desperate harvests from fallen enemies.
She would destroy him in a direct confrontation.
But she wasn't fighting him. She was recruiting him.

The soldier's hand froze on his blade. The other cultists exchanged uncertain glances.
"Go on," the woman said. Her voice carried new interest.
"If the King wanted me dead, I'd be dead. If he wanted me captured, he'd have sent a team strong enough to guarantee compliance. Instead, he sent you—a Demigod who can restrain but not necessarily defeat another Demigod. He wanted to see what I'd do. How I'd react." Kael gestured at the chamber. "This entire encounter was a test."

If the Whisper King has watched me since my resurrection, he knows what questions I'd ask. He knows I'd want to verify his claims." Kael spread his hands. "Give me access to the Oracle. The Oracle waited at the passage's end.
