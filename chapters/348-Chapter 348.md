It swung again, a backhand that caught Kael across the chest. He flew backward into stacked crates, splintering wood, tasting blood. His ribs screamed. Something cracked—hopefully not anything vital.
*Too strong. Direct combat won't work.*
Kael scrambled behind a support pillar as the sentinel's fist demolished the crates he'd used as cover. Each impact shook the entire chamber. Dust rained from the ceiling.
He needed another approach.
The construct was made from corpses—hundreds of them, bound together through dark ritual. Each body contributed fragments of will, memory, skill. The sentinel existed because someone had harvested all those souls and stitched them into a single weapon.
*If I can separate the components...*
Kael extended his Reaver senses.
