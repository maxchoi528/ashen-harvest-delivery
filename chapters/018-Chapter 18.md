The Church of the Sun occupied a fortified compound three blocks from Thornwick's administrative district, its walls built from stone quarried in the eastern highlands, its towers topped with mirrors that focused sunlight into weapons capable of incinerating spiritual threats. The building had served as both fortress and temple for four centuries, surviving wars, plagues, and three attempted coups by rival factions.
Kael crouched in an alley opposite the compound's eastern gate, watching patrol patterns with the attention of someone who'd spent years learning how bodies moved in formations. Two guards at the entrance. Two more visible on the parapets. Spiritual sensors mounted at regular intervals along the walls, their detection fields overlapping to create comprehensive coverage. He'd dampened his spiritual signature to a whisper while climbing, drawing his Soul Reaver abilities inward like a fist closing around a dying flame.
Impenetrable to conventional Awakened. But Kael wasn't conventional.
The perimeter raid Madame Vex had mentioned arrived twenty minutes later—a group of hooded figures emerging from the harbor district, their movements accompanied by the crackle of corrupt spiritual energy. They carried crude weapons and simpler tools, but their conviction blazed like beacons in the night.
The Church's defenders responded immediately. Bells rang from the fortress towers. Light gathered along the walls, transforming the compound into a blazing beacon. The cultists charged, and for the first time, Kael saw the chaos of a spiritual assault in close proximity.
Screams. Burning flesh. The sickening sound of bodies hitting stone. The cultists fought with desperation and fury, but the Church's defenders held the walls with professional efficiency, cutting down attackers while minimizing their own exposure.
Kael counted the bodies as they fell. Four cultists dead within the first minute, their corpses left where they collapsed. Two Church soldiers wounded, dragged back behind defensive positions. The fighting continued for several minutes before the surviving cultists retreated, leaving their fallen behind.
Church burial details emerged twenty minutes later, moving with ceremonial precision to collect the dead. They gathered cultist bodies first, stacking them on carts for disposal in the harbor—standard protocol for enemies of the faith. Church casualties received different treatment, their fallen carried reverently toward the compound's interior, toward the graveyards that occupied the fortress's central courtyard.
Kael tracked the flowion as it passed through the eastern gate. Six bodies. Two carried on stretchers by dedicated bearers, four dragged on sleds toward the cremation facilities. The bearers moved slowly, their burden demanding respect, their route taking them through a checkpoint where another guard verified their authorization before permitting entry.
Not the main burial detail Madame Vex had described. These were front-line soldiers, their deaths requiring immediate ceremony before spiritual contamination could spread. The graveyard crews worked later, after the violence ended and cooler heads prevailed.
Kael waited until the compound settled into its post-battle rhythm before moving. The cultist corpses had already been transported to the harbor, their remains lost to harvesting opportunities. But the Church soldiers who'd fallen were processed separately, their bodies preserved through spiritual means that might improve harvest quality.
He needed access to the courtyard graveyard. He needed one of those bodies before cremation.
The compound's defenses focused outward, designed to repel assaults from beyond the walls. Inward-facing security concentrated on the living—verification protocols, authorization checks, spiritual monitoring for hostile signatures. But a dead body carried no aura, projected no threat, registered on no sensor.
Unless someone specifically scanned for post-mortem spiritual residue.
Kael approached the eastern wall during the next shift change, when guard attention focused on internal logistics than external observation. He carried a cultist cloak recovered from the earlier battle, its corrupt energy already fading but still carrying enough signature to confuse basic detection.
The wall's base featured drainage channels that prevented flooding during heavy rains. The channels were too narrow for an adult body, but Kael wasn't trying to pass through them. He was trying to pass something through them—a rope with a weighted end, launched using techniques harvested from a dead soldier three weeks ago.
The weighted end cleared the drainage grate on the courtyard side. Kael secured the rope to a nearby post and climbed, his movements silent, his breathing controlled. The spiritual sensors on this section of wall were damaged during the earlier assault, their repair not yet scheduled.
He dropped into the courtyard as a funeral procession emerged from the inner buildings, bearing another body toward the cremation pyres. The graveyard occupied a sacred space, its headstones and monuments arranged according to liturgical significance. A small building at the courtyard's edge served as preparation area, where bodies were washed and dressed before their final ceremonies.
Two attendants worked inside, their attention focused on the body before them. Kael moved along the graveyard's perimeter, using monuments for cover, until he reached a position adjacent to the preparation building. Through a window, he could see the attendants' backs as they performed their duties.
A third body lay on a table near the room's rear—already prepared, dressed in ceremonial robes, awaiting transport to the pyre. Fresh. Recent. The harvest window wouldn't last long.
Kael waited until one attendant left to retrieve additional supplies. Then he moved.
The remaining attendant didn't hear him enter. Didn't hear the soft footsteps or the displacement of air as Kael crossed the room. The Reaver's hand closed on the corpse's forehead, and the harvest began.
The sensation was intimate in ways Kael had never fully articulated—contact with another soul's final moments, a last impression of personality and purpose captured before complete dissolution. This soldier had been young, barely twenty, his spiritual pathways still developing. He'd joined the Church's military wing seeking purpose, found brotherhood among his fellows, and died defending walls he'd come to consider home.

[Soul Harvest: You extracted [Basic Infantry Training] and [Church Compound Navigation Data] from the remains. Authorization Fragment acquired.]
The authorization fragment was new—a sliver of spiritual signature that the Church used to identify legitimate personnel. It wouldn't grant full access, but it might confuse sensor systems expecting specific patterns.
Footsteps in the corridor. The returning attendant, humming a hymn.
Kael released the corpse and moved toward the building's rear exit. The attendant entered the preparation room just as he slipped through the door, their paths crossing without contact. For one frozen moment, Kael stood in the corridor beside the attendant's peripheral vision, his body pressed against the wall, his breathing suspended.

The climb down was faster than the climb up. He dropped into the alley, rolled to absorb impact, and was running before his feet fully stabilized. Behind him, bells began ringing again—not the alarm of assault, but something else. Commotion within the compound, voices raised in confusion or discovery.
They'd found the harvested body. They wouldn't know what happened immediately—the Reaver's touch left no obvious signs—but eventually someone would piece together the evidence.
Kael had hours before the compound lock-down, hours before his description circulated through Thornwick's security networks with new details added. He intended to use every one of them.

The data harvested from the young soldier painted a picture of Ironhold Fortress that matched Madame Vex's description and exceeded it in troubling ways. Something was waking beneath Ironhold Fortress. Something that the cultists wanted free and the Church wanted contained. Something that was whispering to its followers through cracks in reality that no one had noticed until now.
The final document in Lady Vivienne's folder carried a header that made Kael pause. It was a list of personnel assigned to the fortress during his squad's final mission, cross-referenced with commendations and promotions received in the months since. Most names meant nothing to him. Dorian Ashford. Gwen's second-in-command. The man who'd survived the ambush with injuries severe enough to warrant immediate evacuation. The man who'd provided testimony that confirmed Kael's guilt at his trial.

Dorian had been promoted twice since the mission. Awarded the Sunburst Medal for valor. Currently assigned to a strategic planning position within the Church's military command structure.
The man who'd survived when no one else should have. The man whose testimony had sent Kael to the execution block. The man who'd been on-site during the ambush, supposedly evacuated with critical wounds.
Kael's hands trembled as implications crystallized. Gwen hadn't been the only one with command access. Dorian had served as her tactical officer, her intelligence coordinator, her direct subordinate. If anyone had provided targeting information to the cultists, it could have been him.
But motive. What would Dorian gain from eliminating his own squad?

The priests who went to investigate haven't reported back. The containment wards are fluctuating, and the garrison commander has declared emergency protocols."
The prisoners in the lower cells. Cultists, probably—followers of the Whisper King who'd been captured during raids and imprisoned in the fortress's detention facilities. If they'd all started screaming at once, it meant something had activated their connection to their master.
Something was happening beneath the fortress. Something that the seal network was struggling to contain.
"Madame Vex's message," Kael prompted.
The woman nodded toward the envelope. "She says the tomb operation is postponed. She says there's a better opportunity developing. She says you should prepare for extraction within the next twenty-four hours, because Thornwick is about to become very hostile territory for anyone the Church considers a threat."

He broke the seal and read the contents. Madame Vex's handwriting was precise, economical, and alarming.

*Recent intelligence confirms accelerated timeline for the Great Rift's degradation. The Whisper King has moved his primary operations into position. Ironhold Fortress is the focal point of an imminent assault that the Church will not survive without external assistance.*
