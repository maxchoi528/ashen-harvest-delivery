"This one won't."
The cultist lunged, and Kael met him with a sweeping parry that knocked the second strike wide. Steel shrieked against steel in the narrow passage between tenement buildings. Steam hissed from a broken pipe overhead, adding to the oppressive fog that clung to Thornwick's lower districts like a living thing. Kael's blade sang through the murk, seeking openings the cultist quickly closed.
Three exchanges. The cultist fought with the desperate precision of someone who had sacrificed pieces of his humanity for power. Every strike carried the weight of something inhuman behind it. Kael felt the impact shudder through his arms, felt his muscles strain against the relentless assault.
A feint. A thrust. The cultist's knife opened a gash across Kael's forearm.
Blood dripped onto the cobblestones, and Kael gritted his teeth. His vision narrowed to the essential: blade, movement, breathing. The world beyond his enemy's silhouette faded to irrelevance. He had learned long ago that fighting above his capacity meant dying above his capacity. The stages existed for reasons, and Ascendant strength had limits.
The cultist pressed forward, driving Kael toward the dead end of the alley. His eyes held nothing — no fear, no rage, just the empty certainty of a puppet following its strings. Whatever he had been before the Hollow Crown claimed him, that person was already gone.
Kael twisted, letting momentum carry the cultist past him. His free hand caught the man's collar and yanked backward. The cultist stumbled, off-balance for half a heartbeat. That fraction of an instant was all Kael needed. His blade plunged upward, finding the gap between the cultist's ribs and piercing straight through to his heart.
The man stiffened. His mouth opened, but no sound emerged. The dark veins on his skin flickered once, twice, then went still.
Kael withdrew his blade and let the body collapse onto the blood-slicked stones. His chest heaved. The wound at his ribs pulsed with heat, and he pressed his coat tighter against it, slowing the flow. The tang of copper filled his nostrils, mixing with the ever-present coal smoke and canal stench of Thornwick's depths.
"Soul Harvest available," the system whispered in the space behind his thoughts. "Corpse within range. Proximity confirmed. Awaiting command."
The Soul Reaver ability stirred in his core, hungry as always. Kael wiped his blade on the cultist's coat and crouched beside the body. The flesh was still warm — maybe ten minutes dead at most. The fresher the corpse, the richer the harvest. This one might yield something worth the risk of lingering in an alley where more Hollow Crown fanatics could appear.
He placed his palm flat against the cultist's chest.
The world inverted.
Kael's consciousness plunged into the grey territory between life and death, the realm where Soul Reaver abilities operated with terrible clarity. The cultist's memories cascaded past him in fragments: a village burning, children screaming, the cold comfort of a voice promising power in exchange for service. The Hollow Crown had found him after tragedy stripped him bare. They had filled the emptiness with purpose and poison in equal measure.
Then the useful memories ended, and the abilities began to separate themselves like threads from a torn tapestry.
**[Soul Harvest: You extracted Spiritual Essence from the remains. 12 essence acquired.]**
**[Soul Harvest: You extracted Combat Memory fragments. Fragment quality: Low. No usable skill acquired.]**
**[Soul Harvest: You extracted Ability: Hollow Step. Quality: Rare. Absorption successful.]**
Kael's eyes snapped open. His hand jerked back from the corpse as new information bloomed through his mind, foreign yet instantly comprehensible. Hollow Step — a short-range displacement technique that drew energy from the space between moments. The cultist had used it to close distances faster than should have been possible, blurring through the gaps in perception that most Awakened couldn't perceive.
Not a revelation about the Whisper King or the tomb of the first Soul Reaver. No forbidden knowledge about unsealing timelines. Just a combat technique, useful for the fights that would come.
Kael stood slowly, testing his weight on legs that threatened to shake. The wound at his ribs had slowed its bleeding, though the fabric around it was soaked through. He would need to treat it properly once he reached somewhere safe. Until then, the pain was manageable. Pain was always manageable. Pain meant alive.
He searched the cultist's body with practiced efficiency. A small vial of clouded glass rolled from the man's coat pocket — soul-essence, probably harvested from some poor victim before being traded to the Hollow Crown. The sigil of the Mortician's Guild was stamped on the bottom, which meant legal channels had been compromised, or the cult had found ways to launder stolen essence through legitimate facilities. Either option pointed toward problems that extended far beyond a few fanatics bleeding in alleyways.
More concerning was the folded paper tucked into the cultist's boot. Kael unfolded it with bloodstained fingers, angling it toward the sputtering gaslight.
Maps. Crude diagrams of Thornwick's underground, marking locations with symbols he didn't recognize. Tunnel networks. Sewer systems. The old catacombs beneath the Cathedral of Dawn. And at the center of several intersecting lines, a single word: RESONANCE.
Kael's stomach dropped.
Resonance was the term the Orders used to describe locations where the Veil grew thin — where the barrier between the Material and the Abyss thinned to translucence. If the Hollow Crown was establishing resonance points throughout Thornwick's foundations, they weren't conducting random rituals. They were building something. Something coordinated.
He thought of the 72 sealed rifts, each containing an Entity that could unbalance the world if freed. The seals degraded over time — everyone who studied such matters knew that. But deliberate weakening? Using resonance to accelerate the decay? That required resources, organization, and a goal that went beyond mere worship of the Abyss.
The cultist had mentioned the Shadow Court. Kael filed that information away with the cold precision of someone who had learned long ago that enemies came in many shapes, and some of them wore friendly faces.
Footsteps echoed from the mouth of the alley. Multiple sets, moving with purpose than the aimless shuffle of night-soaked laborers.
Kael pocketed the vial and the map, then melted into the shadows between two dumpsters. His new ability stirred in his awareness, waiting to be tested. Hollow Step could carry him past ordinary observers, blurring his presence for the span of a heartbeat. Against trained Awakened, it might buy him seconds at best. Against whoever was approaching now, he needed to know which category they fell into.
The footsteps grew louder. A voice called out — female, commanding, with the clipped diction of someone accustomed to authority.
"Seeker squad, confirm the signature. We're reading residual spiritual activity consistent with recent combat."
Another voice, male, lower: "Blood trail leads into this passage. Whoever did this hasn't gone far."
Kael pressed himself deeper into the shadows. His eyes found a rusted ladder bolted to the alley wall, leading up toward a roof access that might offer escape. But climbing with his injuries would leave a trail of bloody handholds. The wound at his ribs was slowing, but not stopped.
The female voice again: "This signature isn't Hollow Crown. The resonance is wrong. Someone else is operating in this sector."
A third voice, younger, uncertain: "Orders are to report any non-affiliated Awakened to Inquisitor Vane's office."
Kael's jaw tightened. Inquisitor Vane. The name had surfaced in whispers for weeks — a hunter from the Order of Dawn, stage three, known for tracking Soul Reavers among other transgressions. If Vane was already building files on non-affiliated Awakened in Thornwick, then Kael's anonymity was eroding faster than he'd feared.
He couldn't afford to be caught. Not now, with the Hollow Crown organizing something that smelled like a coordinated assault on the seal network. Not when Lyra was still navigating her complicated past with the Shadow Court. Not when Zarek's schemes had set events in motion that wouldn't unwind for another hundred chapters.
Kael moved.
His hand found the ladder's lowest rung, and he pulled himself up hand over hand, ignoring the scream of torn muscle in his side. Each movement left fresh blood on the metal, but he climbed anyway. The voices below continued their investigation, oblivious to the shadow sliding upward through the darkness.
Three rungs from the roof, his grip slipped. The wetness from his palms betrayed him. Kael caught himself, teeth clenched against the spike of agony that shot through his ribs, and forced his fingers to hold fast. He couldn't fall. Couldn't make noise. Couldn't let them know he had been there.
He reached the roof and pulled himself over the edge, sprawling onto cold tar paper. The night air hit his damp skin and sent shivers wracking through his frame, but he rolled onto his stomach immediately, pressing his wounded side against the rooftop to slow the bleeding. Above him, the sky was a bruise of orange and black — Thornwick's perpetual smog reflecting the city's endless industrial glow.
The voices below faded as the seeker squad moved deeper into the alley. Kael allowed himself one ragged breath, then another.
**[Ability Activated: Hollow Step. Duration: 0.a few heartbeats. Displacement distance: 6 feet. Energy cost: 0 essence. Cooldown: a few heartbeats.]**
The system notification confirmed what he already sensed. The ability was now part of him, as natural as breathing. He had used it instinctively during the climb without consciously triggering it — a dodge of reflex when his footing faltered, a micro-adjustment that had prevented a catastrophic fall. That meant the integration was complete. The harvest had taken.
Kael rose to a crouch and surveyed the rooftop. Chimney stacks punched silhouettes against the smog-choked sky. Water tanks loomed like sleeping giants. In the distance, the Cathedral of Dawn's spires rose above the surrounding buildings, their blessed bells silent at this hour.
He had a choice to make. The map suggested the Hollow Crown was building toward something significant. The seeker squad suggested the Orders were already aware something was wrong. And the Shadow Court's mention of a bounty on his head suggested that his enemies were coordinating across channels that should have been impossible.
Every path forward was dangerous. Staying still was more dangerous still.
Kael moved across the rooftops, heading east toward the canal district where Sera had established her network of informants. The Mortician's Guild connection on the soul-essence vial needed investigation. The map needed to reach someone who could interpret its full implications. And his wounds needed treatment before infection set in.
The Underworld had its own rules, its own hierarchies, its own wars. Kael walked among them like a ghost, taking what he needed from the fallen and giving nothing back but the cold certainty of death to those who threatened him. The Hollow Crown wanted him dead. The Shadow Court wanted him controlled. The Orders wanted him erased.
Let them want. Wanting had never killed anyone.
Kael's foot caught on a loose tile, and he stumbled, catching himself against a chimney stack. The impact sent fresh pain flaring through his side, and he pressed his forehead against the soot-stained brick, breathing through the sensation. His coat was heavy with blood now. The wound had reopened during his rooftop escape, and every movement cost him more than the last.
He had maybe twenty minutes before blood loss would start affecting his judgment. Less if he pushed harder.
The canal district materialized through the smog — a sprawl of warehouses, boarding houses, and establishments that served clientele who preferred their business conducted away from the gaze of city watchmen. Sera's safehouse sat on the third floor of a building that had once been a textile factory. The owner was a former thief who had aged into discretion, asking no questions and remembering nothing.
Kael descended via a fire escape that groaned under his weight but held. His boots found the cobblestones of the alley behind the building, and he pressed himself into the shadows, scanning for observers. Nothing stirred except rats investigating refuse heaps.
He entered through the back door, climbing stairs that creaked with every step. The hallway smelled of boiled cabbage and old cooking grease. A lamp burned beneath one door — Sera's signal that she was present and the space was secure.
Kael pushed inside without knocking.
Sera looked up from a desk cluttered with papers and empty teacups. Her grey-streaked hair was pulled back in a severe bun, and her eyes — always sharp — widened at the sight of him. She was at his side in an instant, guiding him toward a chair near the stove where warmth radiated from cast iron.
"The coat. Off. Now."
Kael obliged, wincing as fabric tore away from the wound beneath. The gash was uglier than he'd estimated — deep enough to have nicked muscle, wide enough to have bled profusely. Sera's fingers probed the edges with clinical detachment, and she nodded once.
"Stitches. You'll take them." She wasn't asking. "Lie back."
Kael leaned against the chair's worn wood as Sera gathered supplies from a cabinet — thread, needle, a bottle of clear alcohol that would serve as antiseptic. The first splash of alcohol sent fire coursing through his side, and he gripped the chair's arms hard enough to crack the finish.
"You were reckless," Sera said, threading her needle with practiced efficiency. "The Hollow Crown has been moving through this district for weeks. Any competent operative would have avoided direct engagement."
"The cultist gave me no choice. He knew I was coming."
Sera's hands stilled for a fraction of a second. "Knew you were coming."
"The Shadow Court has a bounty on my head. Apparently information about my movements has been circulating."
"That information shouldn't exist. I control the flow of data regarding your operations. No one outside my network should know where you are at any given moment."
"Then your network has a leak."
Sera resumed stitching. The needle passed through flesh with the precise rhythm of someone who had performed this task many times before. Kael watched her face, searching for tells, but found only concentration.
"The Shadow Court operates differently than other factions," Sera said finally. "They don't acquire information through conventional channels. They have... Methods. Preternatural methods. If they've set a bounty on you, it's because someone within their hierarchy believes you represent a threat to their interests."
"I represent a threat to everyone's interests. I'm a Soul Reaver."
"You represent a particular threat to interests that require secrecy." Sera tied off the final stitch and reached for a bandage. "The Hollow Crown is a cult. The Shadow Court is something else entirely. They've existed longer than any of the current Orders, operating in the spaces between power. If they're moving against you, it's not for the reasons the bounty suggests."
Kael accepted the bandage, pressing it firmly against his newly-closed wound. "Then why?"
"That's the question you should be asking." Sera cleaned her hands in a basin of water, the motion deliberate, unhurried. "Soul Reavers have appeared throughout history. Most are destroyed quickly — the Orders hunt them with particular vigor. But the Shadow Court doesn't destroy. They collect. They study. They wait."
"Waiting for what?"
Sera met his gaze. In the lamplight, her eyes held depths that reminded Kael she had survived decades in a world that chewed up the unwary and spat out their bones. "The first Soul Reaver wasn't a man. He was a door. A way through the membrane between realms that shouldn't exist. Every Soul Reaver since has been a shadow of that original opening. The Shadow Court knows this. They've been waiting for someone with enough power to become the door again."
Kael's blood ran cold. "The Whisper King."
The name hung in the air between them. Sera's expression didn't change, but something shifted in the quality of her silence.
"That name is forbidden in most circles," she said. "Even here."
"The bounty. The cult movements. Everything that's been happening since I awakened." Kael's mind raced, pieces connecting in ways that should have been obvious. "It's all connected to him. Isn't it?"
"Everything in Thornwick connects to the Whisper King eventually. He's been here longer than the Orders. Older than the seals. Some say he was here before the Abyssal Entities were imprisoned, before the membrane between realms was established." Sera sat down across from him, her movements weary. "But no one knows for certain. The Shadow Court guards that knowledge jealously."
"And Zarek? The framing? Where does he fit?"
"Zarek is a piece on someone else's board. He believes he's working toward his own advancement, but the pieces he's been moving have been part of a larger pattern for a long time." Sera's voice carried the resignation of someone who had watched patterns repeat for too long. "You'll have your reckoning with him. But not yet. The time isn't right."
Kael wanted to argue. The anger he carried toward Zarek — the man who had destroyed his old life, who had set in motion everything that had led to this moment — demanded resolution. But Sera had never steered him wrong before, and her instincts for the currents beneath surface events were keener than his own.
"The cult map," he said, pulling the folded paper from his coat. "The Hollow Crown is marking resonance points throughout the city. They're building something."
Sera unfolded the paper, and her face went pale. "This isn't just Thornwick. These coordinates extend to the surrounding provinces. The cathedral. The old fortress. The quarry." Her finger traced the lines connecting the points. "They're not weakening existing seals. They're creating new anchors. Something to counter the existing network."
"Counter how?"
"If you wanted to release an Entity, you'd weaken the seals that hold it. But if you wanted to bring something through that the seals couldn't stop..." Sera trailed off, her expression troubled. "You'd need to create your own passage. A resonance path that exists outside the existing framework."
Kael thought of the 72 sealed rifts. The Entities imprisoned beyond the membrane. The Whisper King, who might have been here before any of it began.
"They're building a highway," he said. "For something to walk through."
Sera nodded slowly. "The question is what. And when."
The clock on her wall chimed the hour. Outside, Thornwick continued its eternal industrial grind — engines roaring, factories belching smoke, the masses of spiritually inert commoners going about their lives unaware of the machinations unfolding in the shadows of their world. The Three-Tier Hierarchy placed Awakened above them all, but it was the Awakened who were trapped in the most dangerous game of all.
"I need to speak with someone from the Orders," Kael said. "They need to know about this."
Sera's laugh was bitter. "The Orders are fractured. The Council of Six hasn't agreed on anything meaningful in decades. And Inquisitor Vane — the hunter you've encountered — is investigating corruption within the Order of Dawn itself. If you approach any official channel, you'll be arrested before you can explain the threat."

"The Six Orders all have factions that operate outside official structures. The Order of Dawn has the Vigil. The Iron Vanguard has the Anvil. The Arcane Spire has the Invisible College." Sera ticked them off on her fingers. "The Veiled Market has information brokers. The Mortician's Guild has its inner circles. The Shadow Court has... Whatever it is they do."
"The Shadow Court is watching me. Approaching them would be suicide."
"Approaching the Vigil might be possible. They're fighting the Abyss cults with more commitment than politics. If anyone would believe your intelligence, it's them."
Kael considered the risks. The Vigil operated within the Order of Dawn, but their loyalty was to the cause than the hierarchy. If Sera vouched for a contact, they might listen long enough to hear him out. Or they might capture him and deliver him to Inquisitor Vane, who would execute him without trial for the crime of being a Soul Reaver.
His options were narrowing. The Hollow Crown was building something catastrophic. The Shadow Court had marked him for purposes he didn't understand. The Orders were too fractured to respond effectively. And his own wounds — while treated — would take days to fully heal.
A knock echoed through the safehouse.
Sera's hand moved to a knife hidden beneath the table. Kael tensed, his newly-acquired Hollow Step ability stirring in his awareness. Three sharp knocks, followed by two slower ones. A code.
"It's Lysa," Sera said, relaxing slightly. She rose and crossed to the door, opening it to reveal a young woman with close-cropped dark hair and nervous eyes. Lysa was one of her runners — spiritually inert but quick and observant, perfect for gathering information in places where Awakened attention might be dangerous.
"Message came through the drop," Lysa said, her voice hushed. "From the contact at the Cathedral. They're moving tonight. The Vigil is staging a raid on one of the Hollow Crown's ritual sites in the old quarter."
Sera and Kael exchanged glances.
"Where exactly?" Kael asked.
"The condemned church near the Iron Bridge. They're timing it to coincide with a scheduled cult ceremony."
Kael calculated distances. The old quarter was twenty minutes away by rooftop, less if he pushed through the pain. If the Vigil was raiding a Hollow Crown site, there would be corpses afterward — cultists and potentially Vigil soldiers caught in the crossfire. Fresh bodies meant quality harvests. And if he arrived during the chaos, he might be able to harvest from both sides without anyone noticing in the confusion.
"You're not thinking clearly," Sera said, reading his expression. "You can barely stand."
"I can stand well enough. And I need information about what the Hollow Crown is doing. The map alone isn't enough. I need to see their rituals, understand what they're channeling."
"The Vigil won't let you walk into their operation."
"Then I don't walk. I observe from a distance and harvest what I can from the aftermath."
Sera's jaw tightened. She wanted to argue, but she understood necessity as well as anyone. The information Kael could gather from a Hollow Crown ritual site — even scraps — might be the key to understanding the resonance map's full implications.
"Take the back routes," she said finally. "Stay out of sight. And if the Vigil catches you, you were never there. Do you understand?"
"Understood."
Kael rose from the chair, testing his weight on legs that held despite their trembling. The stitches pulled at his side, a constant reminder of how close the cultist's blade had come to killing him. But pain was familiar. Pain was manageable. Pain meant he was still breathing in a world that wanted him dead.
He pulled his coat back on, concealing the bloodstains as best he could, and moved toward the door. Lysa stepped aside, her eyes widening slightly as she took in his pallor. She was too well-trained to comment.
"Kael." Sera's voice stopped him. "The Whisper King. The Shadow Court. Everything connected to them. Be careful what you learn. Some knowledge has teeth."
Kael nodded once and slipped into the hallway, descending the stairs with careful steps. The night swallowed him as he emerged onto the streets, and he moved through Thornwick's shadows like the reaver he had become.
The condemned church near the Iron Bridge was thirty minutes away. By the time he arrived, the Vigil's raid would be underway or just concluding. The smoke from burning buildings would cover his approach. The screams of dying cultists would mask his presence. And afterward, when the Vigil withdrew to count their wounded and report their victory, Kael would move through the corpses like a professional.
The soul-essence in his pocket was worth a hundred gold sovereigns. The map in his coat might save thousands of lives. And the Hollow Step ability thrumming through his nerves would keep him alive long enough to use both.
Thornwick burned in its own slow apocalypse of industry and corruption. The 72 sealed rifts groaned against their bindings. The Hollow Crown prepared for an arrival that had been promised for centuries. And somewhere in the shadows, the Shadow Court watched, and waited, and wondered if Kael Ashborn would be the key they had spent generations seeking.
The condemned church emerged from the smog ahead of him, its broken steeple a black finger pointing accusingly at the poisoned sky. Flames licked at the stained glass windows. Shouts echoed across the empty square. Steel clashed against steel.

The Vigil fought in tight formations, their blessed weapons carving through cultists who screamed and burned with borrowed Abyssal fire. Three Hollow Crown fanatics fell to one Vigil soldier's coordinated strikes. A cultist priest raised his hands toward the sky, summoning something that tore at the fabric of reality itself—

Not Vigil. Someone else. Someone fighting from an angle that suggested they wanted the same thing Kael did — chaos, confusion, and access to the aftermath.

The harvest would have to wait. Whoever that figure was, they had seen him. They knew he was here. And in a world where Soul Reavers were hunted and knowledge had teeth, being seen by unknowns was the most dangerous thing of all.

The fire crackled overhead. The smoke stung his eyes and throat. And somewhere in the burning darkness, something laughed.

He wouldn't know until he crossed the threshold.
