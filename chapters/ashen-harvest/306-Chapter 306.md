"Move! They know we're here!"
Kael dropped low as a bolt of crimson light scorched the air where his skull had been a heartbeat before. Stone dust rained down from the ruined archway. Behind him, three soldiers from the Iron Vanguard scrambled through the collapsed merchant gate, their armor clanking against rubble.
The Thornwick district of Ashcroft had been a prosperous quarter once. That was before the cultists came. Now, gutted storefronts gaped like open wounds. Gas lamps flickered with unnatural violet flame. Something hummed beneath the cobblestones—a low vibration that made Kael's teeth ache.
"Captain Ashborn!" Sergeant Petra Brennick skidded to a halt beside him, her weathered face streaked with grime and blood. A gash ran across her cheek, still weeping. "We've got four wounded. Vance took a hit to the shoulder. The southern route is compromised—they've got wards everywhere."
Kael pressed his back against a shattered pillar. The stone radiated cold that had nothing to do with the autumn chill. Beyond the ruins, he could hear them moving—soft footsteps, the whisper of robes, the click of something mechanical.
"Number of hostiles?"
"At least twenty between us and the safehouse. Could be more inside the building."
Twenty cultists wasn't the problem. The problem was what those cultists could do. Kael had seen their work in the Ashenmoor Wastes—soldiers ripped apart by shadows that moved without sources, bodies twisted into shapes that defied anatomy. The Abyssal Entities were waking, and their servants were preparing the way.
One of the Iron Vanguard soldiers—a young man barely past twenty—pressed himself against the wall, breathing hard. His hands shook around a rifle that looked too big for him. "Captain, what are those things? I saw one of them tear through a full squad in the Wastes. It didn't even stop when we shot it."
"Fodder," Kael said. The word came out flat. "Low-tier cultists channeling minor Entity power. They burn out quickly but hit hard. Stay mobile, aim for the head if you can, and don't let them touch you with bare skin."
He could feel the spiritual energy pulsing from the ruined district—a thick, oily presence that clung to everything like a sickness. The cultists had been here for weeks, drawing power from something below the streets. The seal network in this region had been failing for months. Everyone knew it. The Orders had been scrambling to contain the damage, but there were too many rifts, too few Awakened to defend them all.
Which was why they were here. Kael's team wasn't official Order business. They were a extraction unit—four Iron Vanguard soldiers, two scouts from the Order of Dawn, and Kael himself. Their mission: locate the cult's spiritual focal point and destroy it before they could complete whatever ritual they were preparing.
The briefing had been clear: this was a priority strike. Intelligence suggested the Thornwick cult was coordinating with cells across multiple cities. They weren't random fanatics anymore. They were organized. Strategic. Moving like an army than scattered insurgents.
That last detail had made Kael's blood run cold.
"Ashborn." Petra's voice cut through his thoughts. "The scouts reported movement on the upper floors. They're preparing something. Could be an ambush."
"Could be the focal point."
"Either way, we need to move. Another minute and they'll have us surrounded."
Kael took a breath, tasting ash and copper on his tongue. The district stretched before them—a maze of collapsed buildings, narrow alleys, and blind corners. Perfect killing ground for an enemy with supernatural senses. The cultists knew these streets better than the soldiers did. They'd had weeks to prepare.
"Change of plans," Kael said. "We go through, not around. Brennick, take your people and fix them in place. Make noise, draw attention. The scouts and I will circle to the east side and hit them from the flank."
Petra's eyes narrowed. "That's a suicide run. They'll cut us to pieces."
"They'll focus on you, not the flankers. By the time they realize we're not the main threat, we'll already be inside."
"And if you're not fast enough?"
"Then you run." Kael checked his blades—both of them, standard Iron Vanguard issue, unenchanted steel. The Order of Dawn scouts watched him with wary expressions. They knew what he was, even if they didn't understand it. "Get moving. Radio silence unless you're dying."
Petra spat on the ground, a gesture Kael had come to recognize as her version of acceptance. "Don't get yourself killed, Ashborn. I hate filing reports."
She signaled her squad, and they moved out—boots crunching on broken glass, weapons ready. Within seconds, they engaged. The sound of gunfire echoed through the ruins, punctuated by the crack of spiritual discharge. Crimson light bloomed in the darkness like diseased stars.
Kael waited three heartbeats, then gestured to the scouts. "With me."
They ran.
---
The eastern approach was a nightmare of collapsed scaffolding and rusted pipes. Kael moved through the debris like water through cracks, his body flowing around obstacles before his conscious mind registered them. The scouts struggled to keep pace—a reminder that he was no longer fighting at the level of normal humans.
Sovereign. The word sat in his chest like a coal, burning with potential. He could feel the boundaries of his power now, the edges of what he could do. The Soul Reaver ability had grown with him, evolving from simple harvesting into something more sophisticated. He could sense corpses from a distance now, feel the lingering spiritual residue of the dead like heat from a fire.
Right now, he could feel several of them nearby—recent deaths from the engagement Petra's squad had started. The dead hadn't begun to cool yet.
"Kael." One of the scouts—a woman named Sera—grabbed his arm. Her face was pale, eyes tracking something he couldn't see. Sensitive, not Awakened. "There's something ahead. In that building. It's... Wrong."
"Wrong how?"
"I don't know. It feels like a hole in the world."
A rift. Not a full breach, but a weakened point in the seal network. The cultists had been using it to fuel their rituals.
"Stay close," Kael said. "And stay behind me."
They entered through a shattered window, glass grinding underfoot. The interior was dark, lit only by the violet glow seeping through gaps in the boarded windows. The air tasted metallic, tinged with something that made Kael's skin crawl.
The building had been a warehouse once. Crates lay scattered across the floor, some torn open and emptied. Chains hung from the ceiling, rusted but still functional. And in the center of the space, carved into the concrete floor, was a massive sigil—lines of glowing violet energy tracing patterns that hurt to look at directly.
A summoning circle. Crude, but effective.
"The focal point," Sera whispered. "They've anchored it to the rift."
"Which means destroying it won't be simple."
"No. When this goes, the whole district will feel it."
Kael studied the sigil, committing its layout to memory. The cultists had chosen this location for a reason—the natural weakness in the Veil amplified their power. Destroying the focal point would require more than just physical damage. Someone would need to disrupt the spiritual flow at its source.
That meant going into the rift itself, or at least close enough to disrupt its connection to the Material.
"Sir." The second scout, a man named Dren, pointed toward the back of the warehouse. A door hung open, leading to a set of stairs that descended into darkness. "Movement downstairs. At least six heat signatures."
Six cultists guarding the descent. Probably more beyond.
"How long until Petra's distraction reaches us?"
"Two minutes, maybe less. They'll realize the flankers any second."
Two minutes. Not enough time to clear the guards and reach the focal point. Unless—
"Stay here," Kael said. "If I'm not back in five, leave. Get word to Command about the rift."
"Kael, you can't—"
He was already moving.
---
The stairs led down into a basement that had been converted into a ritual chamber. Torches burned with violet flame, casting dancing shadows on walls covered in painted sigils. Six cultists stood guard—robed figures with masks depicting eyeless faces, hands crackling with Abyssal energy.
They saw him immediately.
"Halt!" The lead cultist raised a hand, energy coiling around his fingers. "In the name of the Voidbringer, you are—"
Kael's blade took him through the throat.
There was no conversation with cultists. No warnings, no ultimatums. They served Entities that wanted to unmake reality. Negotiation was meaningless.
The second cultist lunged forward, energy arcing from his palms. Kael pivoted, letting the attack pass inches from his face, and drove his second blade up through the man's sternum. Blood sprayed across the stone floor. The body convulsed once, then went limp.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 12 units of spiritual essence from the remains. Energy retained.]
A notification flickered in the corner of Kael's vision, visible only to him. The Soul Reaver ability had activated automatically, drawing power from the dying cultist. The energy flowed into him like ice water—not corrupting, not costly, there. Available.
The other four cultists scattered, forming a semicircle around him. They weren't amateurs. They'd trained for this, prepared for opposition. Energy gathered in their hands, crackling and hungry.
"Stand aside," Kael said. "This doesn't have to end with all of you dead."
One of them laughed—a broken, wet sound. "The Reaver thinks he can threaten us. Brothers, witness the beast that fears death!"
They attacked as one.
Kael moved.
The first strike came from his left—a blast of energy that would have vaporized normal flesh. He dropped beneath it, rolling across the stone floor, and came up inside the cultist's guard. His blade opened the man from hip to shoulder. The second cultist tried to flank him, but Kael was faster, ducking under a sweeping kick and driving steel through an unprotected knee. The cultist screamed and fell.
Two left. They exchanged glances, then split, trying to divide his attention.
Kael let them.
He rushed the one on the right, overwhelming her with speed. She managed to raise a barrier of energy—thin, flickering, desperate. His blade shattered it like glass and buried itself in her chest. She collapsed without a sound.
The last cultist turned to run.
Kael's thrown knife took him in the back of the neck.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 8 units of spiritual essence from the remains. Energy retained.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 15 units of spiritual essence from the remains. Energy retained.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 6 units of spiritual essence from the remains. Energy retained.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 11 units of spiritual essence from the remains. Energy retained.]
Four harvests. The spiritual energy settled into Kael's reserves, reinforcing what he already had. He could feel the power building—not overwhelming, but present. Available for use.
The basement opened into a larger chamber beyond. This was the heart of the operation.
Kael stepped through.
The room was circular, maybe fifty feet across, with a ceiling that vanished into darkness above. In the center, a column of violet light pulsed and writhed—the rift, partially manifested, bleeding Abyssal energy into the Material world. Chains of spiritual energy connected the column to four points around the room, each marked with a different symbol.
The symbols of the Six Orders.
Kael's blood turned to ice.
The cultists weren't just using the rift. They were drawing power from the seal network itself, siphoning energy meant to maintain the barriers between worlds. And they were using the Orders' own spiritual signatures to do it.
That was impossible. The seals were protected by layers of encryption, keyed to each Order's specific spiritual frequency. Only someone with inside knowledge could bypass them.
Someone from within the Orders themselves.
The realization hit Kael like a physical blow. The intelligence reports had mentioned organized activity, coordinated strikes across multiple cities. He'd assumed the cultists were growing more sophisticated. But this—this was something else entirely.
Someone was feeding them information. Someone high up.
"Impressive, isn't it?"
The voice came from behind him. Kael spun, blades raised, but the speaker had already positioned themselves at the room's only exit. Tall, gaunt, wrapped in robes that seemed to drink the light around them. A mask covered their face—white porcelain, featureless except for a single symbol carved into the forehead.
The symbol of the Shadow Court.
"You're earlier than expected, Soul Reaver." The figure's voice was modulated, stripped of identifying characteristics. "We calculated another two hours before your team would breach the perimeter."
"Who are you?"
"A concerned party." The figure gestured to the rift column. "You see what we've built here. Sixty-three active points across the continent, each drawing power from the seal network. The Entities grow stronger with each passing day. Soon, the Veil will thin enough for true manifestation."
"You want to destroy the world."
"We want to free it." The figure stepped forward, hands clasped behind their back. "The Entities are not enemies, Reaver. They are alternatives. The Orders have kept humanity shackled for millennia, feeding us lies about the afterlife, convincing us that death is an ending than a transformation. We offer a different path."
"You offer annihilation."
"I offer evolution. The Abyss is not empty. It teems with life, with possibility. When the Veil falls, both worlds will merge. What emerges will be greater than either."
Kael's grip tightened on his blades. "You're insane."
"Maybe. But I'm also right." The figure tilted their head. "You feel it, don't you? The power flowing through this place. The potential. You could take it, Reaver. Harvest the rift itself if you were capable. But you're not. Not yet. You're still limited by your own understanding of what you are."
"And what am I?"
"The first of a new order. The Soul Reavers were never meant to serve the dead. They were meant to harvest from the source—to drink from the Abyss itself." The figure spread their arms. "Join us. Take what's being offered. In exchange, we ask only one thing."
"What?"
"Your service. When the Veil falls, when the merge begins, you will be our instrument. Your ability will let us control the flow of souls between worlds. You will be the arbiter of who survives the transition and who... Doesn't."
The words hung in the air, heavy with implication.
Kael said nothing.
The figure waited a moment longer, then sighed. "A shame. We could have done great things together."
They moved—not physically, but spiritually. Kael felt the attack before he saw it: a lance of pure Abyssal energy aimed at his core, designed to unmake his soul than his body.
He twisted aside, letting the attack graze his shoulder. Pain lanced through him, sharp and cold, but he was still standing. Still fighting.
The figure's head tilted. "Interesting. You resisted that."
"I resist a lot of things."
"Then let's see how much."
More figures emerged from the shadows—eight, ten, a dozen cultists pouring into the chamber from hidden passages. They carried weapons that pulsed with hungry light, armor that seemed to shift and reform with each movement.
And behind them all, something massive stirred. A shape that defied geometry, a presence that made Kael's soul recoil in instinctive terror.
An Entity. Partial manifestation, but still powerful enough to kill them all.
"Run," Kael said to himself. "Run now."
He didn't run.
Instead, he moved toward the rift column.
The cultists shouted, breaking formation to intercept him. Energy blasts filled the air, forcing him to weave and dodge, to sacrifice ground for speed. One strike caught him across the ribs, sending fire racing through his nerves. He stumbled but kept moving.
The column loomed before him, pulsing with violet light. Up close, he could see the chains of energy connecting it to the seal points—the stolen power flowing into this central reservoir. If he could disrupt the flow, collapse the circuit...
He reached out, pressing his palm against the column's surface.
The world went white.
[Soul Harvest: WARNING - Target exceeds normal parameters. Attempting extraction...]
[System Alert: Rift energy detected. Compatibility uncertain. Proceeding with harvest...]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 47 units of mixed spiritual essence from the rift. Energy retained. Warning: foreign contamination detected.]
Power flooded into him—not just energy, but something else. Information. Fragments of knowledge that didn't belong to any human mind. Images of vast geometries, of Entities that dwarfed cities, of a war that had shaped the world before humanity existed.
Kael's vision fractured. He saw the Entity clearly now, its form a storm of malevolent intelligence, reaching for him with tendrils of pure will. He saw the cultists recoiling, their masks cracking, their faith uncertain. He saw the figure from the Shadow Court standing motionless, watching with something that might have been fear.
And beneath it all, he felt the rift itself—torn, bleeding, dying.
The column shattered.
Energy exploded outward in a wave that threw everyone backward. Kael felt his body lift off the ground, felt stone and metal and flesh cascade around him. The last thing he saw before impact was the Entity's manifestation collapsing in on itself, the partial form unraveling like smoke in a gale.
Then darkness.
---
Kael woke to the sound of rain.
He lay on rubble, staring up at a sky torn open by clouds. Blood dripped from somewhere on his face, warm against his skin. His ribs ached with every breath. His left arm wouldn't respond when he tried to move it.
The warehouse was gone. In its place, a crater twenty feet deep and fifty wide. Cultists lay scattered across the debris—some dead, some groaning, some not moving but still breathing. The rift column was a memory. The chain of stolen power had been severed.
And the figure from the Shadow Court was nowhere to be seen.
Kael forced himself to sit up. His body protested, joints screaming, muscles refusing to cooperate. But he managed to rise, to survey the damage he'd wrought.
Petra's voice crackled through a communicator he'd retained. "Ashborn! Ashborn, respond! We saw the explosion—what happened?"
"Situation resolved," Kael said. His voice came out rough, scraped raw. "Gathering survivors. Send medical."
"Sir, the scouts said there was something else down there. Someone with them. They escaped during the blast."
Kael's eyes swept the crater, looking for a shape that matched the Shadow Court figure's robes. Nothing. They'd gotten away, which meant the information they'd gathered would spread. The cult would know a Soul Reaver had disrupted their operation.
They would be hunting him now. Not just as an enemy, but as a resource.
"Understood," Kael said. "I need time to assess. Stand by."
He ended the transmission and looked at his hands. They trembled, fine vibrations running through muscle and bone. The rift energy still hummed beneath his skin, foreign and vast and hungry.
He'd taken something from that column. Something that didn't belong to him.
And it was starting to wake up.
---
Footsteps approached from behind. Kael turned, blades rising on instinct, but it was only Sera and Dren, emerging from the crater's edge with weapons drawn.
"Kael." Sera's voice shook. "We found something. In the debris. You need to see this."
She held out a leather satchel, recovered from the wreckage. Inside were documents—ledgers, correspondence, maps marked with symbols Kael recognized as rift locations. Sixty-three points across the continent, just as the figure had said.
But that wasn't what made his blood run cold.
The documents were addressed to a single recipient. A name written in code, but with a clear identifier attached.
Order of Dawn. High Inquisitor. Security clearance level seven.
Someone inside the most powerful military Order in the realm was working with the cultists.
"Petra," Kael said into the communicator. "Change of plans. We need to extract immediately. And I need a secure channel to Command."
"What happened?"
Kael looked at the documents in his hands, at the evidence of betrayal that could tear the realm apart.
"I've found something," he said. "Something that changes everything."
The rain fell harder, washing blood and ash into the cracks of the ruined district. Somewhere in the distance, sirens wailed—a response to the explosion, to the spiritual discharge that had lit up every sensor in the city.
Kael Ashborn stood in the wreckage he'd created, holding evidence of a conspiracy that reached to the highest levels of power.
The war had just become far more complicated.
