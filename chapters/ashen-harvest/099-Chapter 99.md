Too many. Plural. This wasn't an isolated incident.
"Retreat," the hybrid hissed, its broken jaw knitting back together with wet sounds. Cartilage and bone grinding, snapping, realigning. "Not worth... The cost."

"And if I'm not smart?"
She smiled without warmth. "Then the Order of Dawn has a bounty on Soul Reavers. Third-highest in the Council's registry, right after Entity-touched and deserters. Someone already claimed you, Ashborn. Whoever sold your location wants you dead or contained."
The fog swirled around his ankles. Somewhere distant, more of those creatures howled. The sound scraped at his spiritual senses like broken glass dragged across raw skin.
"Who?" Kael asked.
Voss shook her head. "That's above my pay grade. But I'd start looking at people who knew you before your execution. People who might want you silenced before you remember something they need forgotten."
She turned and vanished into the steam, leaving him alone with the bodies.
Kael crouched beside the hybrid's remains. The flesh had stopped regenerating—whatever link it had to the Abyss severed with the creature's death. He reached out and pressed his palm against its chest. The skin was cold. Colder than the air, colder than the cobblestones beneath his knees. Like touching a window in deep winter. His spiritual senses probed deeper, past the dead flesh, past the ruptured channels where energy had flowed. What remained was thin. Scattered. But clean.
Soul Harvest: You extracted residual Abyssal essence from the remains. Energy acquired: 47 units. Spiritual corruption detected: NONE.

He moved to the cultist corpse. Older, colder. The skin had already taken on the waxy pallor of the dead, and the smell hit him—copper and rot and something chemical beneath. Ritual sites. Locations across Thornwick—abandoned churches with broken pews and stained glass replaced by boards. Flooded basements where black water lapped at stone walls. Factory floors with rusted machinery and salt circles drawn in the dust. Each site had a circle drawn in salt and blood. And at every site, a rift crackling with unstable energy, the seal barely holding. The memories carried sensation with them—the taste of copper on the tongue, the hum of energy through bone, the acrid smoke of burning herbs.
Not random attacks. Systematic. Someone was testing the seals, measuring their strength.
Soul Harvest: You extracted combat memory fragment from the remains. Ability acquired: Abyssal Resistance (lesser). Quality: DEGRADED.
Lesser quality. Expected, given the age. But the information made his blood run cold. His hands trembled as the last of the memory fragments settled into his mind. He pressed his palms flat against the cobblestones, grounding himself. The stone was cold and rough. Real. Present. Not the flickering nightmare of someone else's recollection.
He stood, processing what he'd learned. The hybrid's message. The Iron Vanguard sergeant's warning. The ritual sites burned into his new memory. His chest wounds throbbed with each heartbeat, the three parallel lines still seeping through his shirt.
The Abyss was pushing back. Testing the cage. And somewhere in Thornwick, someone was helping it.
Kael headed back toward the safehouse, moving through alleys he now recognized as surveillance points. The Shadow Court had eyes everywhere—he counted three watchers as he walked, their spiritual signatures flickering at the edge of his perception. One crouched on a rooftop, motionless. Another leaned in a doorway, face hidden beneath a hood. A third walked parallel to his path, matching his pace through the adjacent street.
They didn't approach. Just watched.
When he reached the safehouse, Lyra was waiting on the roof, her pale hair almost invisible against the moonlight. She had her blades drawn. The steel caught the pale light and threw it back in thin lines across the rooftop.
"You're hurt," she said, not moving to help. "And you're carrying something. Something wrong."
"Hybrid creature. Abyssal-touched, I think." He showed her his palm, where residual essence still glowed faintly. The light was pale blue, almost white, pulsing in time with his heartbeat. "Got some answers. Got more questions."
"The Shadow Court is asking about you. Specifically about what you can do." Her eyes narrowed. "Someone's been talking."
"Someone's always talking in this city." He moved past her toward the trapdoor. "Get some rest. Tomorrow we start hunting."
"Hunting what?"
He paused at the opening. "Whatever's feeding those hybrids. Whatever's cracking the seals. And whoever sold my location to the Iron Vanguard."
Lyra's expression didn't change, but something flickered in her eyes. "You can't fight the entire city, Kael."
"I don't need to fight the city." He descended into darkness. "I just need to find the ones responsible and take what I need from them."
The trapdoor closed behind him. Above, he heard Lyra's blade slide free of its sheath, then silence as she resumed her watch.
In the safehouse below, Gwen lay on a cot, her breathing shallow. Feverish. Whatever the cultists had done to her, it was still working through her system. Sweat beaded on her forehead, and her fingers twitched against the thin blanket. The room smelled of damp and medicinal herbs and the sour undertone of illness.
Kael sat beside her, studying the faint pulse in her throat. If she died, he'd lose access to whatever information she carried. If she lived, she might remember something useful.
His fingers hovered over her forehead. Should he try to harvest now, while she was vulnerable? The temptation gnawed at him. His hand trembled. He could feel the residual warmth of her skin from inches away.
But he didn't know what she'd been through. Didn't know what she'd seen. And she hadn't betrayed him yet.
Not worth the cost.
He withdrew his hand and lay back on his own cot, staring at the water-stained ceiling. The plaster was cracked in places, dark stains spreading like maps to nowhere. Above him, the city churned with secrets. Cultists. Abyssal incursions. Political maneuvering among the Orders. Someone wanted him dead. Someone else wanted him found.
And in the depths of his mind, the hybrid's voice echoed: The Old Dreamer wakes. The seal breaks.
Tomorrow, he'd start putting the pieces together.
Tonight, he'd rest.

"You're still here," she said. "I thought you'd leave."
"You've got information I need." Kael tossed her a wrapped bundle—stale bread and dried meat from the corner market. "Eat. Then talk."
She caught the bundle one-handed, the other arm still bandaged. "About what?"
"Everything you remember. The cultists. The woman who took you. What they were looking for." He settled into a chair across from her. "Start from the beginning."

"They knew about you," she said finally. "Before they took me. They were asking questions in the gutter taverns—asking about the Reaver who walked the Fogmire. Someone had given them your description."
"Who?"
"I don't know. But they had money. Real money, not gutter scraps." She took a bite of bread, chewing mechanically. "The woman—her name was Mira. She wasn't Awakened, but she gave orders to people who were. That doesn't happen unless you know something they want."
Mira. A name. He filed it away.
"What did they want?"
"To recruit you, at first. Mira said the cult believed Reavers were gifts from the Abyss. That you could be turned." Gwen's hands trembled slightly. "When they realized you wouldn't come willingly, they decided to take what they needed. Your soul, specifically. They said a Reaver's soul was worth more than a hundred normal Awakened."
Worth more. Of course. His ability made him valuable to anyone who collected soul-essence.
"They had a ritual prepared," she continued. "A circle that was supposed to trap your soul when you died. But something went wrong. The energy feedback—it killed three people instantly. Burned them from the inside out."

"She walked away. Unharmed. She looked... Disappointed, but not surprised." Gwen met his eyes. "Like she'd expected it to fail and was only following procedure."
Not a fanatic, then. A professional. Someone running an operation according to specifications she hadn't written.
"The woman you mentioned—the one who took you—did she say anything about the cult's leadership? Names, locations?"

Seven pupils. Seven Orders? Or something else entirely?
"Could you draw it?"
Gwen nodded slowly. "If you have paper. But Kael—those sites, they weren't random. They formed a pattern. A shape, maybe. Like the symbols on the seal stones the Orders use."
The seal stones. The markers that maintained the Veil between worlds. If the cult was positioning themselves at locations that matched the seal network...
They weren't just testing the seals. They were mapping them.
Lyra appeared in the doorway, blade drawn. "We have company."

The Shadow Court.

Gwen. They knew about Gwen.
