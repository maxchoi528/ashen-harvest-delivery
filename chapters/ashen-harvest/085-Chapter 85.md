"Brokers deal in information," Lyra said. "Information flows both directions."
"Then we give her something valuable." Kael rose, leaving his blade on the table—a statement. *I'm not afraid to fight disarmed.* Lyra followed without question.
The Broker didn't look up as they approached. "Sit."
Kael took the chair across from her. Lyra remained standing, one hand resting on her dagger hilt.
"You want something," the Broker said. Not a question. Her voice carried the texture of old gravel and older regrets. "Everyone here wants something. The interesting question is what you're willing to trade."
"Information about the cults. Cells, schedules, targets."
"Such information costs more than coins." The Broker's head tilted, revealing a fraction of her face—sharp cheekbone, thin lips, eyes the color of tarnished silver. "Soul Reavers carry different currencies."
"I'm not selling soul-essence."
"No. But you carry something else." Her silver gaze fixed on him, and Kael felt the weight of assessment behind her words. "The whispers say you've been collecting. Skills. Techniques. Abilities torn from the dying and stitched into your own arsenal."
Lyra's hand tightened on her dagger.
"And?" Kael kept his voice level.
"And I have a proposition." The Broker produced a small crystal vial from her sleeve, setting it on the table between them. Inside, something luminous swirled—pure, concentrated soul-essence, the kind that sold for small fortunes. "One hundred gold sovereigns' worth of Awakened essence. Yours, if you do me a service."
"What's the service?"

A thin smile crossed her lips. "This man—his name is Aldric Voss—he carries something valuable. A defensive ability. Arcane Shield, fourth-stage enhancement. Rare, even among Order-trained mages."

"Where do I find him?"
"He's been hiding in the Undercroft. Subterranean tunnels beneath the old cathedral. My people tracked him to a safehouse there two nights ago." The Broker pushed the vial across the table. "Take this as advance payment. Bring me proof of harvest, and I'll double it."
Kael studied the vial, then her face. "Why me? You've got contacts in every guild in Thornwick."
"Because killing Aldric isn't the problem. Stopping him is." The Broker's silver eyes hardened. "He's survived three assassination attempts in the last month. Whatever he's carrying, it's more than a simple shield technique. He's layered defenses, traps, contingencies. My people die before they get close."
"And you think a Soul Reaver can do better?"
"I think you're hungry enough to take the risk." She rose, her form dissolving into the tavern's gloom. "Find me in three days. Don't disappoint me."

The walk to the Undercroft took them through Fogmire District's winding guts—past shuttered factories vomiting steam into the night, through streets where lampposts flickered with irregular gas-flow, around clusters of desperate citizens huddled around barrel-fires for warmth.
"The Broker works for someone," Lyra said as they descended a crumbling staircase into darkness. "Information networks that size don't answer to freelancers."
"Doesn't matter." Kael conjured a pale wisp of spiritual light, enough to see by without alerting every hostile in the tunnels. "The payment's real. The target's real. Everything else is politics."

"Then we don't die." He reached the bottom of the stairs and paused, letting his senses extend. The tunnels stretched in branching darkness, carved generations ago as sewer access and since claimed by those who preferred to live unseen. His Ascendant perception painted the spiritual landscape in shades of residue and flow—recent foot traffic, older bloodstains, and ahead, maybe two hundred meters, a signature that blazed like a signal fire.
"Awakened," he confirmed. "Fourth-stage, maybe higher. Defensive orientation."

Because six hundred years of 'maintaining the balance' has only prolonged humanity's chains." He rose to his feet, the barrier expanding with his movement. "The Abyss isn't our enemy. Why do you instinctively know how to extract skills, how to parse soul-fragments, how to feed without losing yourself?" His voice dropped to a whisper. Drain every fragment of knowledge, every scrap of power. But understand—what you take changes you. Every soul has weight. Every skill carries obsession. The Arcane Shield was forged in blood and sacrifice. Its original owner died screaming as the Entities tried to claim her."
