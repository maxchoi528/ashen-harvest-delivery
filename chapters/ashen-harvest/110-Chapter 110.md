"Soul Harvest: You extracted Spiritual Essence from the remains. 127 essence units acquired."
The notification pulsed through Kael's consciousness like cold water, washing away the last echoes of the dying cultist's terror. He withdrew his hand from the corpse's forehead, watching the faint luminescence fade from his fingertips. Three cultists dead in this tunnel junction, their bodies cooling against the damp stone walls of Thornwick's Undercroft. Fresh kills. Prime material.
Above him, boots hammered against metal grating. The Iron Vanguard patrol had finally caught up, their shouted commands echoing down through the ventilation shafts. Kael counted six distinct voices—too many for a direct confrontation at his current reserves. His soul energy hovered at a quarter capacity after the earlier skirmish.
He moved.
Shadow and steam, that was Thornwick's Undercroft. Pipes threaded the ceiling like metal vines, sweating condensation that dripped into stagnant pools along the drainage channels. The gas lamps here had burned out decades ago, leaving only the faint phosphorescence of fungal growth to paint the darkness in sickly greens. Kael navigated by memory and instinct, his Awakened senses painting the world in layers invisible to ordinary men.
The tunnel branched ahead. Left descended toward the old sewer network—civilized territory, patrolled by Mortician's Guild sanitation crews. Right climbed upward toward the Foundation Stones, the ancient monuments that anchored Thornwick's seal network. Straight ahead lay the Hollow District, and beyond it, the place where three of the 72 rifts had been sealed nearly eight hundred years ago.
Kael turned right.
He needed to reach the Foundation Stones before the Vanguard cornered him. Those ancient pillars radiated enough residual spiritual energy to mask his signature from casual detection. A tactical retreat, nothing more. The cultists' deaths had been necessary—he'd learned that much from the first harvest. Prime Zarek's network extended deeper into the Undercroft than anyone outside the Shadow Court knew.
A fragment of memory surfaced from the harvest: maps, coordinates, a timeline marked in symbols Kael didn't recognize. The cult was moving something. Something important.
The tunnel opened into a vaulted chamber dominated by three massive pillars of black stone, each one carved with geometric patterns that seemed to shift when viewed directly. The Foundation Stones. Kael felt the spiritual pressure the moment he crossed the threshold—the accumulated weight of centuries pressing against his consciousness like deep water.
He pressed his palm against the nearest pillar, drawing on its ambient energy to replenish his reserves. The cold seeped into his bones.
"You move like you belong here."
The voice came from everywhere and nowhere, bouncing off walls that shouldn't have existed in a chamber this size. Kael spun, placing his back against the stone, scanning the shadows between the pillars. His hand found the grip of his knife.
A figure detached from the darkness—tall, gaunt, draped in a coat that seemed woven from night itself. No spiritual signature. No warmth radiating from living flesh. Just a void in the shape of a man, watching Kael with eyes that reflected no light.
"Stage-three Hollow Knight," Kael said. "I've heard rumors."
The figure smiled. It wasn't a pleasant expression.
"Not a Knight anymore. Too many chains broke when I climbed out." The man—or thing that had been a man—tilted his head at an angle that suggested his neck contained too many vertebrae. "The Shadow Court sends its regards, Soul Reaver. They've been watching your work. Impressive efficiency. Three kills in under an hour."
"I'm not interested in Court business."
"Everyone is interested in Court business, boy. They make the business." The Hollow shifted, circling Kael with movements that didn't follow the laws of physics. "I have a proposition. Help me reach the Sealed Rift in the Deep Hollows, and the Court will forget about the four Awakened you've reaped this month. They know. They've always known."
Kael's grip tightened on his blade. Four Awakened. Word traveled faster than he liked. "What's in the Deep Hollows?"
"Answers. The kind that cost more than you're currently worth." The Hollow's smile widened. "I was like you once. A Reaver, I mean. Before they hollowed me out and filled the empty space with something useful."
The words hit Kael like a physical blow. A former Soul Reaver. The hidden traces—vague hints about the original Reaver's fate, about the Whisper King's true nature— felt less abstract. This creature wore the shape of a man, but beneath that coating of darkness, something ancient stirred.
"Why tell me this?"
"Because knowledge is the only currency that matters in the Underworld. And because you're running out of time." The Hollow gestured toward the chamber's far wall, where a crack had opened in the ancient stone. Through it, Kael glimpsed movement—dozens of figures in robed garments, chanting in unison. "Zarek's people are preparing something. A mass ritual at the rift site. You want to know what's really happening in Thornwick? Then follow me."
The Hollow slipped through the crack without waiting for an answer.
Kael hesitated. Every instinct screamed trap. The Shadow Court didn't offer help—they collected debts, and their interest rates were measured in souls. But the mention of a former Soul Reaver, the mass ritual, the organized cult activity that contradicted everything the Orders claimed about random Abyssal incursions—
The crack in the wall began to widen.
Kael sheathed his knife and followed.
The passage beyond led downward through carved stone that predated Thornwick's founding by centuries. Symbols lined the walls—not the geometric patterns of the Foundation Stones, but something older, more organic. They pulsed with faint light as Kael passed, tracking his movement like eyes.
The Hollow moved ahead, never more than a shadow's width ahead, never turning to check if Kael followed. The tunnel widened into a natural cavern dominated by a fissure in the floor—a vertical wound in reality, edges shimmering with wrong-colored light. The Abyssal Rift.
Around its perimeter, cultists in black robes knelt in concentric circles. At the center, bound to the rift's edge by chains of solidified spirit-energy, hung a figure Kael recognized.
Vesper.
The young scout from his earlier encounter. The one who'd helped him navigate the Undercroft in exchange for information about the cult's movements. Her eyes were open, glazed with pain, mouth moving in silent prayer.
"Zarek's masterwork," the Hollow said, materializing at Kael's side. "A living anchor. She's been fed enough soul-essence to bond with the rift itself. Once the ritual completes, she'll tear the seal apart from the inside. The Entity trapped within will pour through, and Thornwick above will cease to exist."
"How long?"
"Hours. Maybe less." The Hollow's void-eyes fixed on Kael. "Your Soul Reaver ability can sever the bond. Touch the chains while they're still unhardened, and you can drain the spiritual energy anchoring her. The cultists won't like it."
"They'll fight."
"They'll die. Some of them, anyway." The Hollow produced a curved blade from somewhere within his coat. "The Court wants that rift sealed, boy. We don't feed Entities—we keep them contained. Help me, and I'll tell you everything I know about the original Soul Reaver. His tomb. His purpose. The reason the Court fears what you might become."
Kael studied the scene. Thirty cultists, most of them spiritually inert, easily dispatched. But six figures stood apart from the others—Awakened, stage uncertain, positioned at cardinal points around the rift. High-temperature spiritual energy radiated from their forms.
"I need to get to Vesper."
"Then we move now. Before Zarek arrives."
They descended together into the cavern.
The first cultist died without realizing he'd been touched. Kael's fingers brushed the man's throat, and the Soul Reaver did what it always did—reached into the dying spark of consciousness and pulled. A flash of memory, fragmentary, showing a face that might have been Zarek giving orders in a language that tasted like ash. Then nothing.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Fragmented Memory from the remains. Partial coordinates acquired.]
The second cultist screamed.
Steel sang as the Hollow's blade opened the man's chest, black ichor spraying across the stone floor. The creature moved with terrible efficiency, each strike precise, economical, stripping life from bodies like a butcher working a carcass.
"Contact left!" one of the Awakened cultists shouted. A bolt of spiritual energy streaked toward Kael's position—raw power, stage-two intensity, the kind that could melt bone if it connected.
Kael dodged right, feeling the heat sear past his shoulder. His mind raced. He'd never fought a stage-two Awakened in direct combat. His abilities focused on harvesting, not destruction. But the Soul Reaver offered options.
He closed the distance in three strides, ducking under a follow-up attack that shattered a stalagmite behind him. The cultist's eyes went wide as Kael's palm found his chest.
Soul energy flooded through the contact point.
The harvest hit differently this time. The cultist's spiritual reserves were substantial—years of accumulated power, carefully hoarded. Kael felt it pour into him, warming his core, refilling depleted reserves with liquid fire.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Spiritual Essence from the remains. 89 essence units acquired.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted... Something unusual. Skill Fragment acquired: Spell Disruption (Unstable).]
The notification pulsed bright in Kael's consciousness. A skill. He'd never harvested a complete ability before—only fragments, echoes, the residual impressions of talent than actual technique. But this felt different. The skill settled into his soul like a key finding its lock, and Kael understood something new about his own power.
Spell disruption. The ability to interfere with spiritual techniques mid-cast. Not by blocking or deflecting, but by severing the energy flow that powered them. The Law of Conservation applied to the caster as much as the target.
The remaining Awakened cultists recognized the danger. Two broke formation, charging toward Kael with murder in their eyes. The third raised both hands, beginning an incantation that made the air taste of copper and lightning.
Kael raised his palm.
"Disrupt."
The word came from somewhere deep in his newly acquired knowledge. Energy pulsed outward from his hand in a invisible wave, and the chanting cultist's spell collapsed. The gathered power dissipated harmlessly, leaving the Awakened staring at his empty palms in disbelief.
"Impossible," the man whispered.
The Hollow's blade answered him.
Chaos erupted as the remaining cultists realized their leaders were falling. Half fled toward the tunnel exits, scrambling over each other in their haste. The rest pressed forward, armed with crude weapons and desperate faith in their mission.
Kael ignored them. Vesper hung at the rift's edge, her chains glowing brighter with each passing second. He reached her as the Hollow carved through the last of the resistance, painting the cavern walls with expressions of terror frozen mid-scream.
"Don't touch the chains directly," the Hollow warned. "Your ability will drain them, but the feedback could kill you both."
Kael studied the bindings. Spirit-energy in solid form, anchored at six points to the rift itself. Touching them would be like grabbing a live wire with spiritual fingertips.
"Suggestions?"
"Find the anchor points. Six knots, hidden in the stone around the rift. Sever those, and the chains lose their power source." The Hollow gestured toward the ground. "I'll keep her alive until you're done. Go."
Kael ran.
The first anchor point hid in a crack in the floor, barely visible unless you knew to look. He pressed his palm against the smooth stone, felt the pulse of energy within, and harvested. The knot dimmed but held.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Spiritual Essence from the anchor. 23 essence units acquired.]
Second anchor, embedded in a stalactite overhead. Third, set into the wall at ankle height. Fourth, sunk into the stone bench that the cultists had used for their rituals. Each harvest drained power from the chains, weakened the bonds holding Vesper suspended.
The fifth anchor proved problematic. It sat in the center of a summoning circle, residual energy crackling in patterns that threatened to shock anyone who approached. The spiritual discharge alone could incapacitate an Ascendant.
Kael approached anyway.
"Disrupt."
His newly acquired skill pulsed outward, interfering with the circle's operation. The patterns scrambled, lost coherence, flickered and died. He harvested the anchor in three seconds of contact.
One knot remained.
A voice echoed through the cavern, deep and resonant, carrying harmonics that made Kael's teeth ache.
"Fascinating. The Soul Reaver in the flesh, making use of gifts he doesn't understand."
Kael turned.
The speaker stood at the cavern's entrance, silhouetted against the gaslight from the tunnels beyond. Tall, broad-shouldered, wearing the formal robes of an Order official. But the face—Kael recognized that face from a thousand nightmares. The same cruel eyes that had watched him dragged to the execution platform. The same thin lips curved in an expression of absolute contempt.
"Zarek," Kael breathed.
"The very same." The cult leader stepped into the cavern proper, and the remaining cultists parted before him like water before a ship's prow. "I've been waiting for this meeting, Ashborn. Ever since you crawled out of your grave and started collecting souls like a common thief."
"You framed me for your murders."
"I framed you for murders that needed framing, yes. Someone had to take the blame for the Seep's activity. You were convenient." Zarek's gaze moved past Kael to the rift, to Vesper, to the Hollow standing guard over the suspended scout. "And now you interfere with my ritual. Predictable. Sentiment clouds your judgment. It always does with your kind."
"My kind?"
"Soul Reavers." Zarek spread his arms, and spiritual pressure flooded the cavern—not the refined power of an Awakened, but something rawer, hungrier, touched by the Abyss itself. "Did you think you were unique? The first to discover how to harvest from the dead? We've been doing it for centuries. The difference is, we understand what we're dealing with. The Entities aren't monsters, boy. They're potential. And the seals that imprison them—"
Kael lunged.
Zarek caught him by the throat without looking, lifting him off the ground with casual contempt. Spiritual energy crackled around the cult leader's hand, burning through Kael's defenses like paper.
"The sixth anchor," Zarek said conversationally. "It's inside the rift itself. Touch it, and you'll be consumed. Leave it, and my sacrifice completes. Either way, you lose."
Vesper screamed.
Kael's hand found Zarek's wrist, and he harvested. Not essence, not skill—something deeper. The Soul Reaver reached into the cult leader's soul and pulled at the threads binding his power to the Abyss.
Zarek dropped him, stumbling backward with an expression of genuine shock. The spiritual energy around his hands guttered like a candle in a hurricane.
"What did you—"
"Disrupt."
The word tore from Kael's throat as he hit the ground, rolled, came up in a fighting stance. His newly acquired skill pulsed outward, severing Zarek's connection to whatever Entity had been feeding him power. The effect was immediate and devastating.
Zarek screamed.
Not a cultured cry of outrage, but something animal, broken, the sound of a man who'd built his entire existence on a foundation that had just crumbled beneath him. He collapsed against the wall, clutching his chest, spiritual energy leaking from wounds that shouldn't have been visible.
"You've doomed yourself," Zarek gasped. "The Whisper King will—"
The Hollow's blade took the cult leader's head before he could finish the sentence.
Silence fell over the cavern.
Kael stood over Zarek's corpse, breathing hard, the taste of copper and ozone coating his tongue. His reserves were nearly empty. His body ached with the exhaustion of spiritual combat. And somewhere in the back of his mind, a notification waited to be acknowledged.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Spiritual Essence from the remains. 312 essence units acquired.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Soul Fragment from the remains. Memory corruption detected. Partial data recovered.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Fragmented Skill from the remains. Skill Acquired: Soul Drain (Basic).]
Kael's hands trembled. Two skills in one fight. The Soul Reaver was evolving, adapting, feeding him the tools he needed to survive. But at what cost?
The chains binding Vesper dissolved. She fell, and the Hollow caught her before she hit the stone. Her eyes found Kael's, filled with gratitude and confusion and the dawning realization that nothing in her world would ever be the same.
"Thank you," she whispered.
"Don't thank me yet." Kael crossed to where the sixth anchor waited—an orb of pure spirit-energy hovering at the rift's edge, pulsing in rhythm with the Entity trapped beyond. "The rift is still open. Zarek's ritual is incomplete, not cancelled."
"Your Hollow friend says the Court will seal it."
"The Hollow isn't here anymore."
Kael spun. The creature that had called itself a former Soul Reaver was gone, vanished into shadows that shouldn't have existed in a cavern with no exits. His blade lay on the ground where Vesper had fallen, abandoned like a snake shedding its skin.
"Clever," a new voice said.
The speaker emerged from the rift itself.
Not an Entity—something worse. A woman in the black-and-silver robes of the Order of Dawn, face obscured by a hood, power radiating from her form in waves that made the cavern walls shake. Stage three. Maybe higher.
"Inquisitor Vane," Kael breathed.
The hunter's smile was visible even in shadow. "You've been busy, Soul Reaver. Cultists, Abyssal rifts, Shadow Court operatives. All within the span of a single night." She descended from the rift's edge like gravity was a suggestion than a law. "The Council will be very interested in your explanation."
"The Council can wait."
"They can't,. Your actions tonight have accelerated a timeline I've been tracking for three years." Vane drew a blade of crystallized light from somewhere within her robes. "Prime Zarek was my asset. His death means nothing if I can't determine who ordered him to accelerate the ritual."
Kael backed toward the tunnel exit. Vesper couldn't run, couldn't fight—Kael was the only one with spiritual reserves left, and they numbered in the single digits.
"You want the real enemy?" Vane's eyes glittered. "Then drop the defensive stance, Soul Reaver. Because I'm about to show you something the Orders have been hiding for eight hundred years."
The Inquisitor's blade traced a pattern in the air, and the cavern dissolved around them.
Kael found himself standing in a different place entirely—a vast chamber carved from living crystal, walls pulsing with light that shifted through colors human eyes weren't meant to perceive. In the center, suspended in columns of solidified spiritual energy, hung bodies.
Dozens of them.
All Awakened. All dead. All bearing marks that Kael recognized from his own reflection.
"Soul Reavers," Vane said. "Every one. The first generation, the ones who discovered how to harvest from the dead. The Orders didn't hunt them because they were dangerous." Her voice hardened. "They hunted them because the Reavers discovered the truth about the afterlife. And the truth about what the Entities really are."
Kael stared at the preserved corpses. At the records carved into the crystal walls. At the evidence of a conspiracy that predated the Concordat by centuries.
"The Entities aren't prisoners," he said slowly.
"No." Vane's blade lowered. "They're refugees. And the Orders have been committing genocide against an entire dimension to keep that secret."
The chamber trembled. Somewhere in the distance, alarms began to sound.
"You're a long way from home, Soul Reaver," Vane said. "And now you know too much to ever go back."
Footsteps echoed in the corridor beyond—multiple sets, approaching fast.
"We need to move," Vane added. "Unless you'd prefer to explain yourself to the Iron Vanguard patrol that just arrived."
Kael looked at the crystal chamber, at the preserved bodies of his predecessors, at the evidence of atrocities committed in the name of sealing the Abyss. His hands still trembled, but not from exhaustion anymore.
"Where?" he asked.
"Anywhere but here." Vane sheathed her blade. "The Underworld has layers, Ashborn. And we've only just scratched the surface of the truth."
The footsteps grew louder.
Kael followed the Inquisitor into the darkness, leaving behind everything he thought he knew about the war against the Abyss, the purpose of the Six Orders, and the nature of his own power.
Behind them, the crystal chamber began to collapse.
Above, in the city of Thornwick, dawn broke over gaslit streets and steam-belching factories. The common folk woke to another day of labor and struggle, unaware that the foundations of their world had just shifted beneath their feet.
And in the shadows, watching Kael's retreat through eyes that reflected no light, the Hollow smiled.
"The Reaver awakens," it whispered to no one. "The Whisper King will be pleased."
The darkness swallowed the words whole.
