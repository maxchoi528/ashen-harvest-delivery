The blade found the cultist's throat before the man could scream. Kael wrenched the steel free, hot blood spraying across the cobblestones as the body crumpled into the gutter. Three more waited in the alley's shadows, their robes marked with the sigil of the bleeding eye—Zarek's inner circle.
"Get him!" one shouted.
Kael was already moving, bare feet slapping against wet stone as he vaulted a stack of crates. Behind him, footsteps thundered in pursuit. The gas lamps sputtered overhead, casting fractured light across the canal district's narrow passages.
A bolt of spiritual energy singed past his ear. Kael spun, ducking beneath a second attack, and felt his newly harvested skill flare to life—a combat technique borrowed from a dead Iron Vanguard days ago. His body twisted with mechanical precision, blade carving through the attacker's extended arm.
The man howled, clutching the ruined limb. Kael didn't hesitate. He pressed forward, driving the cultist backward until the body met the canal's black water with a splash.
Two left. Both flanking now, trying to cut off escape routes.
Kael's heart hammered against his ribs. The Soul Reaver ability sat coiled in his chest, eager to be used, but these were fresh kills. Killing them would be simpler. Cleaner. And far less risky than harvesting in the middle of a chase.
An explosion ripped through the night air three streets away. Flames painted the underside of low-hanging clouds in orange and red.
That wasn't his pursuers. Something else was happening in Thornwick tonight.
The cultists hesitated, glances exchanged in the chaos. Kael used the moment. He dove toward the nearest, using the man's own shoulder as a pivot point to spin past, and then he was running again—toward the flames, toward the chaos, toward whatever disaster was unfolding in the city's heart.
Thornwick burned.
Not —not yet—but the fabric of the city itself seemed to be tearing apart. Kael burst from the alley onto Merchant's Bridge and froze. The canal below churned with unnatural energy, dark tendrils of something reaching upward from beneath the water's surface. Buildings along the quay sagged, their foundations compromised by forces beyond mortal comprehension.
A seal. One of the seventy-two seals that kept the Abyss at bay.
Kael's blood ran cold. He'd heard rumors about accelerating degradation, about cult activity near the old rifts, but seeing the evidence was something else entirely. If that seal failed completely—
"Move!" A hand grabbed his arm, yanking him sideways. "You're standing in the focal point, you fool!"
The woman who pulled him to safety wore the gray coat of the Mortician's Guild, her face half-hidden beneath a deep hood. Familiar. Sera, the informant who'd passed him details about Zarek's operations for the past month.
"Keep your hood down," Kael muttered, scanning the crowd that had gathered despite the danger. "We can't afford attention."
"We can't afford for you to die in the street." Sera dragged him toward a side street, away from the growing number of Awakened converging on the compromised seal. "The Orders are mobilizing. Order of Dawn, Iron Vanguard, both here in force. If anyone recognizes you—"
"Then we're already dead."
Sera's grip tightened on his arm. "You need to see something. My Guild contacts intercepted communications—cultist codes, but the encryption was weak. Deliberately weak."
Kael's stomach dropped. "What kind of communications?"
Before she could answer, a sound cut through the chaos. Not the crackle of unstable seals or the shouts of panicking civilians. Something else. A rhythm. a heartbeat of spiritual energy that pulsed through the district like a second pulse.
Every Soul Reaver in range would feel it.
Kael certainly did. The pull in his chest intensified, the familiar hunger that came with proximity to death. But this sensation carried something else. Recognition. As if another voice was singing in a frequency only he could hear.
"What is that?" Sera whispered.
Kael didn't answer. His eyes swept the rooftops, the windows, the shadows between gas lamps. Searching. Hoping he wouldn't find what he was looking for.
He found it.
A figure stood on the roof of the Broken Compass Tavern, fifty yards distant. Male, tall, dressed in dark leathers that blended with the night. The moonlight caught the edge of a blade at his hip, but that wasn't what held Kael's attention.
The figure was watching him. Not the seal. Not the chaos. Him.
And he was smiling.
The smile of someone who had found exactly what they were hunting.
"I need to go," Kael said.
Sera grabbed his sleeve. "The communications—"
"Later." He pulled free, already moving toward the rooftops. "Find somewhere safe. Don't return to the usual meeting points."
"Kael—"
But he was already gone, scaling a drainpipe with borrowed Agility, ascending into the maze of slate and chimney that made up Thornwick's upper reaches. Behind him, the other figure moved to intercept. Parallel paths, converging on a single point.
They met on the roof of an abandoned counting house, three streets from the compromised seal. Close enough to feel the spiritual pressure. Far enough to speak without shouting.
"You move well," the stranger said. His voice carried easily across the rooftop gap. "The Ashborn execution was a spectacle. Watching you climb out of your own grave was... Impressive."
Kael's hand drifted toward his blade. "Who are you?"
The stranger stepped forward, moonlight revealing more of his features. Young, maybe mid-twenties, with sharp cheekbones and eyes that gleamed like polished obsidian. A scar traced from his left temple to his jaw.
"I could ask you the same question, Kael Ashborn. Or should I use your true name? The one the Abyss carved into your soul when you died?"
The temperature on the rooftop dropped several degrees. Not. Just the sensation of danger pressing closer.
"I'm a collector," the stranger continued. "Like you. We have a term for people like us, you know. In the old tongue. Soul Reaver."
The word hit Kael like a physical blow. He'd only recently learned it himself, gleaned from fragments and rumors. To hear it spoken aloud, casually, by another person who shared his curse—
"There's no one else," Kael said. "I've looked."
"You looked in the wrong places." The stranger smiled wider. "The Orders have spent centuries erasing evidence of our kind. But information doesn't disappear. It hides. It waits." He spread his hands. "I've been watching you for months, Kael. Learning your patterns. Your methods. Your habits."
"Stalking me."
"Studying you. There's a difference." The stranger's eyes narrowed. "You've been sloppy. The Mortician's Guild noticed your activities. The Shadow Court noticed. Even some of the Six Orders have your name in their files. You're drawing attention, and in our world, attention is a death sentence."
Kael's grip tightened on his blade. "Is that a threat?"
"It's an observation. I'm here to offer you a choice." The stranger reached into his coat and withdrew a sealed document case. "The Shadow Court knows what you are. They've known since your execution. This case contains information—proof of Zarek's involvement with the cult, evidence that could clear your name and expose the conspiracy that's been hunting you."
"And what do you want in exchange?"
"The same thing you want. Answers." The stranger tossed the case. Kael caught it reflexively. "Your Soul Reaver ability—where did it come from? Who created it? Why does it work differently than any documented Awakened power in history?"
"I don't know."
"I believe you. But someone does." The stranger pointed toward the compromised seal in the distance. "The cult isn't random, Kael. They're organized, funded, directed by forces that have been planning this for decades. The seals weakening? That's not accident or natural decay. That's deliberate sabotage."
Kael stared at the distant flames. "You're saying Zarek's cult is working with someone inside the Orders?"
"More than that. The cult is a distraction. A cover for the real operation." The stranger's voice dropped. "Someone is trying to reopen the rifts. All of them. Seventy-two tears in the Veil, releasing the Entities that humanity imprisoned during the Abyssal Wars."
The weight of that statement settled over Kael like a shroud. Seventy-two Entities. The combined might of all Six Orders had barely contained them the first time.
"The Soul Reaver ability," the stranger continued, "is the key to everything. The original bearer of our power was connected to the Abyss in ways that shouldn't be possible. He created a method for harvesting soul energy that violates the fundamental laws of spiritual conservation. He was either the greatest genius in history or a servant of something far darker."
"You know who the original was?"
The stranger's smile faded. "I know he vanished. I know his tomb was sealed by the Orders two hundred years ago. I know that every Soul Reaver who has emerged since—including you—appeared without warning, without origin, without any connection to the previous bearer."
"That's not an answer."
"No. It's a clue." The stranger glanced toward the flames. "The cult has been searching for that tomb. If they find it, if they access what the original left behind..."
The implication hung unspoken between them.
"Why tell me this?" Kael demanded. "Why offer help? What do you gain?"
"Insurance." The stranger's expression hardened. "The Shadow Court wants to control you, not kill you. You're too valuable to destroy. But if you become a liability, if you refuse their offers, their protection disappears. Same with the cult. You have enemies on every side, Kael. I'm offering you a chance to understand why."
"And if I refuse?"
"Then you die alone. Or worse, you become a puppet." The stranger turned toward the edge of the rooftop. "I'll be in contact. The document case contains everything I've gathered. Read it carefully. Trust no one."
"Wait." Kael stepped forward. "If there are others like us, where are they? Why haven't I encountered any?"
The stranger paused at the rooftop's edge. "We've been hunted to extinction, Kael. Murdered by the Orders, corrupted by the Entities, destroyed by our own power. The survivors learned to hide. To pass as normal Awakened. To never reveal what we truly are." He looked back, his obsidian eyes reflecting the distant flames. "You're the youngest Soul Reaver I've encountered in thirty years. The fact that you've survived this long is remarkable. Don't waste it by being careless."
He dropped off the roof and vanished into the shadows below.
Kael stood alone on the rooftop, the sealed document case heavy in his hands. The sounds of the compromised seal filled the air—cracking stone, hissing energy, the distant shouts of Order members trying to contain the damage.
Below, Sera was waiting, her face pale in the gaslight.
"What did he want?" she asked.
Kael opened the document case and began reading by the flickering illumination of a nearby lamppost. His expression grew darker with each line.
"The conspiracy is bigger than we thought," he said finally. "Zarek is connected to the Shadow Court. To the cult. Possibly to someone inside the Orders themselves."
"That's impossible. The Orders are separate. The Concordat prevents—"
"The Concordat prevents open warfare. It says nothing about secret alliances." Kael folded the documents and stuffed them inside his coat. "We need to move. The cult activity tonight isn't random. It's a distraction. Something else is happening while everyone focuses on the seals."
"What about the other Soul Reaver? The one who gave you this?"
Kael looked toward the spot where the stranger had disappeared. "He called himself a collector. Said he's been watching me for months."
Sera's hand went to the knife at her belt. "That's not reassuring."
"No. But it's useful." Kael started walking, pulling his hood lower. "The Shadow Court knows about me. This stranger knows about me. Zarek definitely knows about me. We're running out of time and running out of options."
"We could leave Thornwick. Disappear."
"And go where? The cult operates across the continent. The Shadow Court has presence in every major city. The Orders control the rural territories." Kael shook his head. "Running isn't an option anymore. We need to stop Zarek before his plan succeeds."
"You don't even know what his plan is!"
"I know it's connected to the seals. I know it involves the original Soul Reaver. And I know that if we don't interfere, seventy-two Entities are going to tear this world apart." Kael's voice hardened. "That's enough to work with."
They moved through the secondary streets, avoiding the main thoroughfares where Order members patrolled. The night air carried the taste of smoke and something else—ozone, the residue of spiritual energy discharge. The compromised seal was being contained, but the damage had already been done. Word would spread. Panic would follow. The conspiracy would have to accelerate its timeline.
Which meant Kael needed to accelerate his.

Three hours later, they reached the safehouse in the tanner's district—a converted warehouse that smelled of curing hides and old secrets. Lyra was waiting inside, her blade already drawn.
"You took your time," she said.
"We had complications." Kael outlined what had happened, including the encounter with the rival Soul Reaver.
Lyra's expression remained neutral throughout, but her knuckles whitened around her sword's grip. "A rival Soul Reaver. And you let him leave."
"He had information I needed."
"Information he could have been lying about."
"Possibly. But if even half of what he said is true, we're in deeper trouble than we realized." Kael spread the documents across a makeshift table. "The cult isn't working alone. They're connected to the Shadow Court, possibly to elements inside the Orders. Someone is orchestrating everything—the framing, the seal sabotage, all of it."
Lyra examined the documents, her eyes scanning the coded messages and intelligence reports. "These intercepts are legitimate. My contacts confirmed the encryption keys weeks ago. The Shadow Court has been monitoring Zarek's movements, but they haven't moved to stop him."
"Why not?"
"Unknown. Either they're planning something we don't understand, or..." She trailed off.
"Or they're working with him."
Lyra nodded slowly. "The Shadow Court serves no single master. It's a network of spies, assassins, and information brokers operating across faction lines. If Zarek has something they want, they'd ally with him temporarily."
"What could he possibly have that they'd want?"
"The same thing everyone wants." Lyra met his eyes. "Power. The cult has been researching the original Soul Reaver for decades. If they found anything—an artifact, a technique, information about the Entities' imprisonment—that knowledge would be worth any price."
Kael thought about the stranger's warning. The tomb of the original Soul Reaver. The seventy-two sealed rifts. The conspiracy that spanned centuries.
"There's something else," Sera interrupted. "My Guild contacts heard rumors about movements in the eastern districts. Iron Vanguard troops being redeployed. Order of Dawn representatives recalled from their missions. Something big is happening inside the Orders themselves."
"Internal crisis?" Kael asked.
"Maybe. Or maybe they're preparing for something." Sera pulled out a folded paper. "I intercepted this too. A list of names. People marked for 'special attention' by the cult."
She handed the paper to Kael. His own name wasn't on it, but several others were. Names he recognized from his investigation. Informants. Witnesses. People who had seen Zarek's operations firsthand.
"They're cleaning house," Lyra said quietly. "Eliminating anyone who could connect them to the conspiracy."
"How many of these people are still alive?"
Sera's expression turned grim. "Based on what I've heard? Less than half."
Kael stared at the list. Each name represented a life, a piece of evidence, a potential ally. The cult was systematically erasing the trail.
"We can't save them all," Lyra said, reading his expression. "But we can use what they left behind."
"The Soul Reaver ability." Kael's jaw tightened. "Harvest from the dead. Extract their final moments. Find out what they knew before they died."
"It's risky. If the cult has people watching the bodies—"
"I know the risks." Kael stood, moving toward the door. "But doing nothing isn't an option anymore. The conspiracy is accelerating. The seals are failing. If we don't act now, there won't be anything left to save."
Lyra rose to follow. "I'll come with you."
"No. I need you here. Protect Sera while I'm gone. If the cult is cleaning house, they'll come for her next."
"And if they come for you?"
Kael paused at the doorway. "Then I'll give them something to remember."

The first body was easy to find. A dockworker named Thomas Greaves, discovered floating in the canal an hour after the seal incident. Official cause of death: accident, caught in the chaos of the seal's destabilization.
Kael knew better. The wound on Thomas's back was too precise for accident. Someone had killed him and dumped the body to look like collateral damage.
He waited until the Mortician's Guild retrieval team left, then slipped into the water and retrieved the corpse himself. The touch of dead flesh sent the familiar shiver through his system—the Soul Reaver ability awakening, hungry for harvest.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted memories from the remains. Fragmented images acquired. Subject witnessed cultists near the eastern docks. Heard mention of 'Prime Zarek' and 'the convergence.' Cause of death: single strike to the spine.]
Kael absorbed the fragments, pieces of Thomas's final moments playing through his consciousness. The cultist's face. The warehouse where they'd met. The words 'convergence' and 'night of the bleeding stars.'
Another piece of the puzzle fell into place.
He moved on to the next body. And the next. Each harvest provided more fragments, more connections, more evidence of the conspiracy's scope. By the time dawn painted the eastern sky gray, Kael had harvested from eight corpses and assembled a rough timeline of events.
The cult planned to move within days. Not weeks. Days.
A message arrived at the safehouse via courier: a single sheet of paper with elegant handwriting.
*Mr. Ashborn—*
*Your activities have drawn significant attention. The Order of Dawn has classified you as a primary threat. Iron Vanguard strike teams are being deployed to your last known location.*
*You have until sunset to surrender yourself for questioning, or you will be eliminated as a hostile element.*
*This is not a threat. This is a courtesy.*
*—The Council of Six*
Lyra read over his shoulder. "They know where we are."
"They know where we were. There's a difference." Kael crumpled the message. "We need to move again. But this time, we're not running. We're hunting."
"Zarek?"
"Zarek. The cult leadership. Everyone involved in the conspiracy." Kael's eyes hardened. "The Orders want to eliminate me before I can expose them. Fine. Let them try. But I'll bring down everyone responsible for this mess before I go down."
"That's suicide."
"Probably." Kael sheathed his blade. "But at least I'll take some of them with me."
Sera stepped forward, her expression conflicted. "There's something else. My Guild contacts just sent word. They've found Lyra's sister."
Lyra went rigid. "What?"
"The Mortician's Guild facility in the eastern quarter. She's being held there, alive. The records indicate she's scheduled for 'processing' in three days."
Lyra's hand flew to her blade. "We're going."
"That's a trap," Kael said. "The Shadow Court knows you're looking for her. They're using her as bait."
"Then I'll spring it." Lyra's voice carried the steel of absolute conviction. "She's my sister. Whatever it takes."
Kael studied her for a long moment. The tension in her shoulders. The fury barely contained behind her eyes. He understood that feeling—the desperate need to protect what remained of family, even when the cost was everything else.
"Three days," he said finally. "We use that time to prepare. To gather intelligence. To find allies."
"We don't have three days. The Orders are hunting us. The cult is cleaning house. Zarek is preparing his convergence."
"And rushing in blind will get us killed." Kael met her gaze. "Your sister is important. But so is stopping the conspiracy. We need both, or we need nothing."
Lyra's jaw worked silently. Then she nodded. "Three days. But if she dies because we waited—"
"She won't. I'll make sure of it."
The words surprised him as much as anyone. But they felt right. True.
Outside, the morning sun climbed higher, burning away the night's shadows. Somewhere in Thornwick, cultists prepared for convergence. Orders mobilized for war. And a rival Soul Reaver watched from the darkness, waiting to see what Kael would do next.
The game had changed. The pieces were moving. And Kael Ashborn was done being a pawn.
Time to become a player.
The document in his coat crinkled as he moved. Instructions for contacting the rival Soul Reaver. Coordinates for a meeting. A final warning: *The Whisper King knows what you are. He's been waiting two hundred years to meet you.*
Kael didn't know who the Whisper King was. Didn't know what he wanted. But he understood one thing with absolute clarity.
Someone had created the Soul Reaver ability. Someone had built the conspiracy that had framed him, hunted him, tried to destroy him. And that someone was still out there, pulling strings from the shadows.
It was time to cut those strings.
