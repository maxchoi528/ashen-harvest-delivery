Kael pressed his back against the alley wall, breath shallow. Three streets away, a church bell tolled midnight—and from the basement of a condemned tannery, candlelight flickered where no light should exist.

The tannery reeked of old hide and worse. Rotting beams groaned under his weight as he descended. At the bottom, symbols had been carved into the stone floor—spiraling patterns that made his skin crawl. A dozen robed figures knelt in a circle around a central pit. Their chanting rose and fell in discordant waves.
Not the Shadow Court. Different. Something hungrier.
One figure turned. "Another lost soul joins us."
Kael's hand found his knife hilt. "You're conducting a ritual near an active rift. You understand the consequences?"
Laughter rippled through the congregation. The speaker pushed back her hood, revealing a face marked by ritual scars. "Consequences? The Orders teach obedience to seals they cannot maintain. We offer another path."
The chanting intensified. Cold air pushed up from the pit. Kael felt it then—the wrongness, the thinning of reality itself. The Veil was tearing.
"Run," he said, not to them. To himself.
The floor erupted. Clawed hands burst from the pit, followed by a torso that defied human proportions. Fetid breath washed over the room. The cultists didn't flinch. They raised their arms in worship.
Kael sprinted for the stairs. A robe blocked his path. He ducked the blade meant for his throat, drove his elbow into the attacker's solar plexus, and kept moving. The stairs groaned. Behind him, the Entity pulled itself fully into the cellar, and its scream split the air like breaking glass.

"Reaver," one spat. No fear in his eyes. Faith, maybe. Or madness.

He didn't stop running until he reached the merchant district.
Dawn painted the rooftops gray when Kael finally allowed himself to breathe. He ducked into a covered market stall, its owner long fled or dead. His hands shook as he inventoried what he carried: knife, coins, a pouch of dried meat, and the crumpled note that had led him here.
The note had mentioned a cult meeting. It hadn't mentioned an active summoning.
