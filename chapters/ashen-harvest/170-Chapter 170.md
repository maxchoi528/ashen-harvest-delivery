Kael's boot splashed through a puddle of rainwater, the sound swallowed by the fog rolling off the Thornwick canals. Three bodies lay scattered across the warehouse floor behind him—cultists he'd found in the middle of some kind of ritual. Their blood still steamed in the cold night air.
Two more were trying to run.
He moved, not running exactly, but flowing forward with a speed that belonged to Transcendent bodies and stolen reflexes. The first cultist made it six steps before Kael's hand closed around his throat. The man's eyes went wide, then glazed as Kael pressed his palm flat against the corpse's forehead.
No spiritual cost. Just the wet click of a skill unlocking.
[SOUL HARVEST: You extracted 47 spiritual energy from the remains. Memory fragment acquired.]
The energy flowed into him like ice water, but it was the memory that made him pause. Fragments of plans. Coordinates. A map of organized cells across Thornwick, not random attacks but coordinated strikes aimed at specific locations.
*Cult activity in Thornwick is organized, not random.*
Kael dropped the body and turned. The fifth cultist had made it to the loading dock, his silhouette framed against the gaslight glow outside. He was screaming something about the Whisper King returning, about the seals breaking soon.
Kael crossed the distance in three seconds.
The man fought. He had a knife, some kind of Abyssal brand on his forearm that glowed faintly green. His strikes were wild, fueled by desperation and whatever corruption he'd absorbed from his masters.
Kael blocked, redirected, and brought his elbow down on the man's wrist. The knife clattered away. One more touch, palm to cooling skin.
[SOUL HARVEST: You extracted 89 spiritual energy from the remains. Memory fragment acquired. Ability acquired: DEATHLY STILLNESS (Grade B - Uncommon).]
Information flooded his mind—how to move without sound, how to freeze in place so completely that even other Awakened would overlook him. A useful skill. His collection kept growing, each death adding another tool to his arsenal.
The warehouse fell silent except for the drip of water from the rafters. Kael stood among the bodies, five corpses cooling in the fog, and sorted through the memories he'd stolen.
Organized cells. Coordinated strikes. The cult wasn't random anymore—it was planning something big.
He needed to find out what.
---
The Mortician's Guild facility loomed against the night sky, its chimneys pumping smoke into the clouds. Kael watched from across the canal, hidden in the shadow of a steam-powered crane that had been abandoned for repairs. The skill Deathly Stillness hummed in his awareness, ready to activate if needed.
From what he'd harvested, this place was important to the cult's operations. Not just another warehouse or hideout—a genuine facility with legitimate cover. The Morticians processed corpses for the city, but they'd been "losing" bodies lately. Bodies that ended up in cult rituals instead of proper burial.
The front gate had two guards, both Awakened but low-stage. Beyond them, the building's windows glowed with cold light. Somewhere inside, he knew he'd find answers about the cult's organization. Maybe even clues about Zarek and the Great Rift seal degradation.
Kael dropped from his perch and landed silently on the cobblestones. Deathly Stillness activated without conscious thought, his body becoming part of the fog, part of the shadows pooling in the alley's corners. When he moved, he made no sound at all.
The guards didn't notice him slip through the gate.
Inside, the facility smelled of chemicals and something else—something that made his Soul Reaver senses prickle with warning. Spiritual residue, heavy and old. Not just from dead bodies. From something that had been alive and then wasn't.
He followed the pull of that residue through corridors lined with cold storage units, past rooms filled with morticians working on bodies with respectful silence, down a staircase that shouldn't have existed according to the building's public blueprints.
The basement level was different. The walls here were older, stone instead of brick, sweating moisture in the cold. Symbols had been carved into the floor—seal-work, but corrupted, the careful geometry of the original Orders replaced with jagged approximations that hurt to look at.
Kael counted twelve people in the chamber. Six wore Mortician robes, their faces slack and empty, minds clearly elsewhere. The other six were cultists, armed and alert, standing around a massive circular pit that dominated the room's center.
The pit was full of bodies. Not processed corpses—fresh ones, still wearing the clothes of common folk, their faces frozen in expressions of terror.
"Three more rifts weakened this week," one cultist said. "The Whisper King will be pleased."
"The seals hold for now," another replied. "But our people at the Council say the degradation is accelerating. Another month, maybe two, and we'll have enough weakened points for the mass unsealing."
*Mass unsealing. The 72 rifts.*
Kael's stomach clenched. The cult wasn't trying to open one rift or even several—they were planning to unleash all 72 at once. The world would be overrun with Abyssal Entities, reality itself would crack under the pressure.
"The Thornwick cells are positioned," the first cultist continued. "We have people in the Iron Vanguard, the Arcane Spire. When the signal comes, they'll neutralize the Order response."
"What about the Soul Reaver?" someone asked. "The one the Shadow Court is watching?"
Kael's blood went cold.
"They're tracking him," the cult leader said. "He won't be a problem. No Reaver has survived once the Orders understand what they can do. The Whisper King wants him alive for the ritual, but if he interferes..."
The cultist shrugged. "The Orders will handle him for us."
Kael forced himself to stay still, to keep breathing, to keep his power contained. His mind raced through the implications. The Shadow Court was monitoring him, which meant someone high up wanted him captured than killed. The cult was embedded in the Orders themselves, and the timeline for the mass unsealing was much shorter than anyone outside this room knew.
He needed to get out. He needed to warn someone. But he also needed more information, and these cultists had it.
One of the Morticians straightened, his empty eyes focusing on something across the room. His head turned slowly, mechanically, until he was staring directly at the shadows where Kael hid.
"Someone's here," the Mortician said, his voice flat and inhuman.
The cultists spun toward the shadow, weapons drawn, spiritual energy flaring.
Kael moved.
Deathly Stillness released as he burst from cover, and he used the momentum to cover ground faster than their eyes could track. The first cultist fell with Kael's hands around his throat. The second tried to strike but found only air as Kael ducked under the blow and drove his palm into the man's solar plexus.
"Alert! We have a Reaver!"
The warning echoed through the facility. More footsteps thundered from above—reinforcements responding to the intrusion.
Kael finished the first cultist and immediately went for the second, dragging the body close and pressing his hand to the cooling face. Another harvest, another flood of information. Unit designations. Communication protocols. The location of three more cells across Thornwick.
"Surrender!" one cultist shouted, pressing a blade to the nearest Mortician's throat. "Surrender or I'll start killing hostages!"
Kael laughed, and the sound made the cultist flinch. "They're not hostages. They're bait. The Morticians don't know what's happening—the cult mind-controlled them." He gestured to the slack faces. "Look at them. No fear. No recognition. Just empty shells."
The cultist's blade trembled. For a moment, his confidence cracked, and Kael saw the uncertainty beneath.
That moment was enough.
Kael threw a knife he'd picked up from one of the corpses—nothing special, just steel—but thrown with Transcendent precision it became a killing stroke. The blade took the cultist through the eye socket, dropping him instantly.
The remaining cultists broke. Not toward Kael but toward a hidden door at the room's far end, their survival instincts overriding their orders. They made it three steps before Kael caught up.
He took two of them down with brutal efficiency, harvesting from each corpse before the bodies hit the ground. The third reached the door, slammed his hand against the seal, and the wall began to slide closed.
Kael dove for the gap, twisting his body through the narrowing space. He emerged into a narrow tunnel lit by sickly green light, chasing the sounds of retreating footsteps.
The tunnel wound downward, deeper into the earth than the basement should have allowed. The spiritual pressure increased with every step, making the air thick and hard to breathe. Kael's Soul Reaver senses screamed warnings, but also pulled him forward toward whatever was creating this resonance.
The tunnel opened into a cavern that stole the breath from his lungs.
Bodies covered the floor—not recent deaths but ancient ones, hundreds of them, maybe thousands, arranged in precise geometric patterns around a massive seal etched into the cavern's bedrock. The seal was black, shot through with cracks that pulsed with faint green light.
And at the center of the seal, chained with spiritual bindings that seemed to be failing, was a figure.
Kael approached slowly, his footsteps silent on the stone floor. The figure was humanoid but wrong, its proportions slightly off, its skin the color of old parchment stretched over too many bones. Eyes that should have been human reflected the green glow of the seal cracks.
"Soul Reaver," the figure said, and its voice was a chorus of whispers layered over each other. "You come to harvest me?"
Kael stopped ten feet away. His Soul Reaver ability pulsed in his awareness, ready to harvest, but something held him back. The figure's presence was familiar in a way he couldn't explain, like touching a memory he didn't know he had.
"You're one of the 72," Kael said. "An Abyssal Entity."
"I am what remains of what was once called the Keeper of the Dead." The figure's head tilted at an unnatural angle. "Before the First Orders bound us, before the seals were made, I was free. I walked among your kind and taught them how to harvest souls, how to preserve knowledge beyond death."
*Soul Reaver. The ability didn't come from nowhere.*
Kael's mind raced through the implications. "You're saying you're the origin. The first Soul Reaver."
"I am saying that your power comes from me. From what I was before the seal trapped me here, draining my essence to maintain the barrier between realms." The figure's cracked lips pulled back in something like a smile. "Every Soul Reaver since has carried a fragment of my power, passed down through bloodlines and rituals. You are not the first, and you will not be the last."
The green light pulsed brighter. Cracks spread across the seal with an audible grinding sound.
"But I am dying," the Entity continued. "The seal takes too much, and the cult works to accelerate the drain. Soon there will be nothing left to maintain this barrier, and when I die, so does the seal's anchor. The 72 will be free."
"Why tell me this?" Kael demanded.
"Because you can stop it." The figure raised one skeletal hand, pointing at Kael's chest. "Harvest me."
Kael's breath caught. "Harvest an Entity? That's not—"
"It has never been done, but you are different. You carry my essence. You can absorb what remains of my power, strengthen the seal, buy the world more time." The Entity's eyes burned with desperate intensity. "Or let me die, let the seal collapse, and watch as the Abyss pours through every crack in reality. Your choice, Soul Reaver. But understand—if you harvest me, you will carry my memories. My purpose. My hunger."
Kael stared at the creature that claimed to be the origin of his power. The whispers of the Abyss pressed against his mind, promising knowledge, promising strength, promising answers to questions he hadn't known to ask.
The cult would be coming. The Entity was dying. The seal was failing.
And he had seconds to decide.
---
Kael stepped forward and pressed both hands against the Entity's chest. The skin was cold, like touching a corpse that had been frozen for years, and beneath it he felt the massive spiritual pressure contained in this single being.
"No," he said quietly.
The Entity's eyes widened. "What?"
"I said no." Kael pulled his hands back. "You want me to carry your memories, your purpose, your hunger. You want me to become something else wearing a human mask." He shook his head. "I've seen what happens to people who give themselves to power without asking the cost. I've been that person. I'm not going to be it again."
"Then you doom us all," the Entity whispered.
"I doom you," Kael corrected. "The cult is killing you to break the seal. If I kill you to harvest your power, I'm doing their work for them. But if I find another way—if I stop the cult, if I reinforce the seal properly instead of using you as a battery—"
"There is no other way. The Orders don't know—"
"Then I'll make them learn." Kael turned toward the tunnel. "I'm getting out of here. I'm finding the Order leadership. I'm telling them exactly what's happening in their city, in their facilities, under their noses."
"They won't listen to a Reaver."
"They will when I show them the bodies upstairs." Kael paused at the tunnel entrance. "And if they don't listen to me, maybe they'll listen to the evidence I'll harvest from every cultist I find between here and the Council chamber."
The Entity laughed, a sound like bones rattling in a crypt. "You would fight the 72 with nothing but stolen skills and corpse-mutilation. You are either very brave or very foolish."
Kael grinned over his shoulder. "I've been called both. Usually by the same people, usually at the same time."
He ran.
The tunnel shook as he fled, dust and debris falling from the ceiling as the seal's integrity continued to degrade. Behind him, he heard the Entity's voice rising in some kind of chant, its words in a language that predated human civilization.
The cultists who'd escaped were waiting at the tunnel's end, joined by reinforcements that had come from deeper in the facility. Six Awakened, all of them Transcendent or higher, their spiritual energy blazing in the darkness.
"Die here, Reaver," one of them snarled. "The Whisper King has use for your corpse, but not for you breathing."
Kael skidded to a halt, his mind calculating odds and possibilities. Six against one. All of them at least his level, some possibly higher. He had Deathly Stillness, he had raw spiritual energy from the harvests, he had the Entity's knowledge still echoing in his memory fragments.
He didn't have enough.
"Think about what you're doing," Kael said, stalling for time he didn't have. "The Morticians upstairs are evidence. The bodies in the pit are evidence. I've already harvested enough cult memories to know your cell locations, your communication codes, your chain of command. Killing me won't bury this."
"Killing you will send a message," the lead cultist replied. "The Shadow Court wants you alive, but accidents happen in combat. A knife in the back, a fatal wound that takes too long to heal—"
A massive explosion tore through the facility above. The floor buckled, walls cracking, and for one chaotic moment everyone lost their balance.
Kael didn't waste the opportunity. He threw himself at the nearest cultist, not attacking but using the man's shoulder as a pivot point to vault over the group entirely. His feet hit the wall, pushed off, and he was through the gap before any of them could react.
More cultivators appeared from the ruined corridor ahead. These weren't cultists—these wore the insignia of the Iron Vanguard, their armor scarred and their weapons drawn.
"Stand down!" one of them shouted. "Iron Vanguard! We've received reports of unauthorized spiritual activity in this facility!"
The cultists exchanged glances. Kael kept moving, ignoring them, heading for the exits. Behind him, he heard weapons clashing as the Vanguard engaged the cultists. More explosions, more shouting, the sounds of a battle that might buy him the time he needed.
Kael burst out of the facility's front entrance and into the cold night air. The fog had thickened, wrapping Thornwick in grey obscurity that muffled the sounds of fighting from within. He made it three steps before a figure stepped out of the shadows directly in his path.
Lyra.
She looked different than the last time he'd seen her—thinner, harder, with shadows under her eyes that spoke of sleepless nights. She wore clothes that marked her as unaffiliated, neither cult nor Order, and her hand rested on the hilt of a sword that glowed faintly with spiritual energy.
"Kael," she said. "We need to talk."
He kept his distance. "Lyra. I thought you were—"
"In the Underworld? I was. I'm not anymore." She raised one hand, showing him a symbol carved into her palm. "I got out. I found something. Something that involves you."
The sounds of fighting from inside the facility grew louder. More Vanguard soldiers were arriving, their boots thundering on the cobblestones.
"Whatever you have to say, say it fast," Kael told her.
Lyra stepped closer, her voice dropping to a whisper. "The Shadow Court isn't one organization. It's three. Each one believes they're the real power, and each one is playing the cult and the Orders against each other."
"And you know this how?"
"Because I was Court. Before." Her eyes met his, and for a moment he saw the pain beneath the steel. "I'm still Court. They think I'm loyal, but I'm not. I'm playing both sides to figure out who betrayed my old unit, who killed my friends, who set you up for execution."
*The Whisper King. Zarek. The framing.*
Kael's instincts screamed at him to run, to trust no one, to remember that everyone had an agenda. But Lyra had saved his life once, back when they were both just pawns in someone else's game.
"Talk," he said.
Lyra grabbed his arm and pulled him deeper into the fog. They moved through back alleys and across rooftops, always staying ahead of whatever pursuit might be following. Twice they passed groups of cultists, but Lyra navigated around them with the confidence of someone who knew this city's dark places intimately.
Finally, they stopped in the shadow of a clock tower, its gears grinding overhead marking the hour.
"The cult's mass unsealing plan," Lyra said without preamble. "It's real. But it's not just the Abyssal Entities they're after. The ritual requires something else. Something that can only be provided by a Soul Reaver."
Kael's blood chilled. "They're using me. Using my ability."
"Not using. Consuming." Lyra's expression was grim. "The ritual requires the complete essence of a Reaver who's reached Transcendent. The power that lets you harvest skills, memories, fate—it can be channeled to crack the seals from the inside. You're not just a target, Kael. You're the key."
"Who knows about this?"
"The Whisper King. His inner circle. And now me." Lyra's hand tightened on his arm. "The Shadow Court is divided on how to handle you. Half want you captured for the ritual. The other half want you dead before the cult can use you. I'm in the minority faction that thinks you should be told the truth."
"Why?"
"Because I'm tired of watching good people die for secrets I could have shared." Lyra released him and stepped back. "The Whisper King is a former Soul Reaver. He knows what you can do because he could do it too, once. Until something went wrong."
*The Whisper King's true identity.*
Kael's mind reeled with implications. The Shadow Court's interest in him made sense—not just fear or manipulation, but personal history. The Whisper King knew exactly what Kael could become because he'd walked the same path.
"Where is he?" Kael demanded. "Where can I find the Whisper King?"
"You can't. Not yet. He's protected by layers of seals and cultists that would take an army to breach." Lyra pulled a folded paper from her coat. "But I can give you this. Names. Locations. The cult's supply lines, their communication networks, their hidden caches. Everything I learned before I left the Court."
Kael took the paper. His fingers trembled slightly as he unfolded it, scanning the information within. Addresses, schedules, key figures. This was enough to cripple the Thornwick cell operations if used correctly.
"Why help me?" he asked again.
Lyra smiled, but it didn't reach her eyes. "Because the cult killed my squad. The Whisper King ordered it. And I'm going to make him pay, even if I have to use you to do it."
"That's honest, at least."
"Honey, I haven't been honest with anyone in years. But I'm trying." She turned to go, then paused. "There's a cell meeting tomorrow night. Warehouse district, north end. Twelve cultists, including one of the Whisper King's lieutenants. If you're as good as people say you are, you could harvest enough information to map their entire network."
"How do I know this isn't a trap?"
Lyra shrugged. "You don't. But if it were a trap, I wouldn't have bothered with all this talk. I'd just have cut your throat while you were listening."
She melted into the fog, leaving Kael alone with the weight of revelation pressing down on his shoulders.
The cult wanted to use him as a key. The Shadow Court was hunting him from multiple directions. The seals were failing. And somewhere in Thornwick, Zarek was orchestrating it all, the man who'd framed him for crimes he didn't commit, the man who'd set the wheels of this nightmare in motion.
Kael looked down at the paper in his hands, then up at the gaslit towers of Thornwick rising against the clouded sky. Somewhere in that city, answers waited. Somewhere in that city, enemies gathered.
And somewhere, in the depths beneath the streets, the last fragment of the original Soul Reaver was dying, taking the world's best hope for survival with it.
He folded the paper carefully, tucked it inside his coat, and started walking. Tomorrow night, the warehouse district. Twelve cultists. One lieutenant. A chance to map the enemy's network and find a path toward Zarek.
Behind him, the clock tower chimed midnight, its bells echoing across the fog-shrouded city.
He didn't look back.
Footsteps. Dozens of them. Coming from the fog ahead.
Kael's hand moved to his knife as the silhouettes resolved from the grey: cultists, their faces hidden behind masks, their spiritual energy flaring in the cold night air. Not the six from the facility. More. Many more.
"Mr. Ashborn," one of them said, his voice distorted by some kind of amplification technique. "The Whisper King requests your presence. It would be... Advisable... To accept."
Kael counted fifteen, eighteen, twenty. More still emerging from the shadows, surrounding him completely, cutting off every escape route.
Twenty-five. Thirty.
The trap was real after all.
He dropped into a combat stance, Deathly Stillness humming in his awareness, spiritual energy gathering in his palms.
"Requests," he said. "That's a funny word for an ambush."
The lead cultist raised one hand. Spiritual pressure crashed down like a physical weight, driving Kael to one knee. Not one Transcendent. Not even a Sovereign.
A Demigod.
"This is not a request," the cultist said. "This is a demonstration of our resources. You can come with us willingly, or we can break you and take you anyway. The Whisper King prefers you conscious. I do not care either way."
Thirty-five cultists. One Demigod. And Kael, alone in the fog, with no way out.
His fingers tightened on his knife.
The fog seemed to press closer, as if the city itself was waiting to see what he'd do.
