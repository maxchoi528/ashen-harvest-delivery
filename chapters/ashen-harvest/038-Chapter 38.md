The iron gate screamed as Kael wrenched it open, the sound swallowed by the fog rolling off the Thornwick harbor.
Patrol lanterns bobbed in the distance. Two of them. Moving in a grid pattern toward the warehouse district. He had maybe forty seconds before they swept past the alley where he crouched, rain-slick cobblestones reflecting light like scattered coins.
"Soul Sight activated," he whispered. The world bleached to monochrome, spiritual energy blazing like heat signatures. The patrols burned orange-white, their life forces bright and steady. Behind them, a cluster of dim embers marked the harbor watch station. To the east, something else pulsed—faint, stuttering, the light of someone badly wounded or recently dead.
Kael pulled his collar higher and moved.
Three weeks since the Gallows. Three weeks of sleeping in drainage tunnels and eating whatever didn't sleep first. The Order of Dawn had posted his face on every broadsheet from here to the Ashwick copper mines, but the reward they'd attached told him more than any wanted poster could: one thousand gold sovereigns. They weren't offering that kind of money for a common criminal.
They were afraid of what he could do.
The fog thickened as he worked deeper into the Dockside slums, past shuttered gambling houses and the skeletal remains of processing plants that had closed when the fishing stocks died. Shadowmere Asylum sat at the tip of Widow's Point, a finger of black stone reaching into the harbor. The locals called it the Fingerbone. They said you went in mad and came out compliant, or you didn't come out at all.
Kael needed to know if Sera had been moved there.
The Order had taken her the night of the Gallows, dragging her away before he could reach her. She'd been trying to pull him off the execution platform when their soldiers flooded the square. Her face, contorted with fury, remained burned into his memory—fury at the soldiers, at the crowd, at the injustice of it all. Fury at him for getting caught in the first place.
His contact at the Veiled Market, a half-feral information broker named Crow, had paid good coin for the rumor that Sera was being held at Shadowmere. "Pre-trial detention," he'd called it. "Standard procedure for witnesses connected to Reaver activity."
The term sat in Kael's stomach like swallowed glass.
He found the maintenance tunnel where Crow had promised an access point—a corroded drainage pipe that ran beneath the asylum's western wall and emptied into the harbor shallows. The grate was rusted but not locked, pried open countless times by whoever had used this route before him.
Kael slipped inside, the smell of brine and rust thick enough to taste.
Darkness swallowed him. He activated Soul Sight again, the monochrome vision bleeding back into focus. The tunnel stretched forty meters before opening into a larger chamber—a pumping station, if the rusted machinery was any indication. Spiritual energy signatures dotted the space. Three of them, clustered near the far wall.
Living people. Or recently living.
He crept forward, keeping his footsteps light on the wet stone. The signatures resolved into shapes as he approached—bodies on the ground, arranged in a rough semicircle around something. The spiritual light emanating from them was dim, fading, the characteristic signature of souls recently departed.
Blood. The copper tang of it reached him a half-second before his eyes adjusted.
Three guards lay in their own entrails, their Order of Dawn tabards soaked through with something darker than rain. The wounds were precise—throat, armpit, femoral artery. Someone had killed them quickly and efficiently, the work of a professional.
A fourth figure slumped against the wall, still breathing. Kael recognized the uniform before he recognized the face.
Brother Aldric. The priest who'd been overseeing his execution. Who'd offered him water the night before, whispering about mercy and forgiveness while the noose bit into his neck.
Blood bubbled from a wound in his chest. His eyes found Kael in the darkness, widened, then fluttered with something like relief.
"You're alive," Aldric coughed. "They said—the noose—"
"What happened here?" Kael crouched beside him, hands hovering over the wound. The priest wouldn't survive without immediate medical attention. With immediate spiritual intervention he might last another ten minutes. "Who did this?"
"The Cult." Aldric's hand shot out, gripping Kael's wrist with surprising strength. "They came through the tunnels. Twenty of them, maybe more. They're here for something in the lower levels. Something they want to wake up."
Kael's blood chilled. "The Asylum holds prisoners. Political dissidents. Cult suspects. What could they—"
"The Black Sepulcher." Aldric's voice dropped to a rasp. "The first Soul Reaver's tomb. It's beneath Shadowmere. The Order discovered it decades ago, built the asylum on top of it to keep it secret. And now—" Another cough, wet and rattling. "Now they're going to open it."
The first Soul Reaver.
Kael's mind raced through implications. The Sepulcher tablets, the recordings left by the original Soul Reaver—they were here? Beneath this building? The same building where Sera might be imprisoned?
"Brother Aldric." He gripped the priest's hand. "Sera Vance. The woman taken during my execution. Is she in the asylum?"
"I don't—" Aldric's eyes rolled back. His grip went slack.
Kael checked for a pulse. Nothing. The priest was gone.
He should feel something, probably. Guilt, gratitude, the weight of unfinished business. Instead, there was only the hollow recognition that another body had joined the count, and the certainty that he couldn't afford to waste time on sentiment.
The Soul Reaver ability pulsed in his chest, ready. Zero spiritual cost. Touch required. The information he needed might be locked inside this man's memories—everything the Order knew about the asylum's layout, its guards, its prisoners.
Kael placed his palm on Aldric's forehead.
Cool skin. Stiffening already. Death had taken him three minutes ago, maybe four. Not ideal. The harvest would be degraded, fragments than full recollections.
The notification bloomed in his vision:
[Soul Harvest: You extracted faint memories from the remains. Consciousness degrading. Acquired: Fragmented map of Shadowmere Asylum (Level 1-3). Fragmented knowledge of Order patrol rotations.]
The information slotted into his mind like puzzle pieces—corridors and checkpoints, guard schedules and prisoner locations. Third level held the high-value detainees. Cells 37 through 42. One of them carried a signature that matched the spiritual resonance pattern he'd memorized from Sera.
She was here.
The cultists had created a chaos window. Perfect timing for extraction or disaster, depending on how he played it.
Kael stood. The pumping station had two exits—one led up into the asylum's service levels, the other continued deeper, toward the harbor outflow. The cultists had come from somewhere in that direction. Their bodies would be up ahead, probably, cut down by the guards they'd surprised.
Or the cultists were still in the building.
He checked his mental map. The service levels connected to the main structure through a series of maintenance corridors. From there, he could reach the third-level detention wing without passing through the heavily guarded main entrance.
Footsteps echoed from deeper in the tunnel. Multiple sets, moving with purpose.
Kael killed his lantern and slipped into the shadows along the tunnel's western wall. The footsteps grew louder, accompanied by the wet sounds of argument.
"—told you, the seal requires blood from a Reaver." A woman's voice, sharp with impatience. "We don't have time to hunt one down."
"We have one." A second voice, male, trembling with something between fear and excitement. "The Order brought him in last night. The one they executed. He's supposed to be dead, but Brother Marik says his soul signature appeared on the monitors. He's here. In the building."
Kael's grip tightened on his knife.
The footsteps passed within meters of his position. Three figures moved past, their spiritual signatures blazing with the sickly yellow-orange of cultist corruption—Entity-touched, all of them, their souls marked by whatever bargains they'd struck with the powers beyond the Veil.
They didn't see him. They kept moving, heading toward the pumping station's upper exit.
One of them carried a case of some kind. Metal, covered in sigils that hurt to look at even secondhand. The Sepulcher opener, maybe. The tool they'd need to breach whatever seal the Order had placed on the first Reaver's tomb.
Kael waited until their footsteps faded, then followed.
The service corridors were a labyrinth of pipes and conduits, steam venting from cracked seals, the walls sweating with condensation. He tracked the cultists by sound and spiritual signature, moving through maintenance passages that connected to the main building's infrastructure.
Shouts erupted somewhere above him. Glass breaking. The distinctive crack of spiritual discharge—weapons firing, abilities activating. The infiltration had been discovered.
The third level would be in lockdown soon, if it wasn't already. Prisoner cells could seal automatically during emergencies, locking everyone inside until manual overrides engaged.
If Sera was in there, he had minutes at most.
He found a service ladder that matched his mental map—a vertical shaft connecting the service levels to the detention wing. The ladder was narrow, slick with moisture, but it went up three flights before terminating in a sealed hatch.
Kael climbed.
Halfway up, the screaming started.
Not the sharp yelp of combat or the guttural roar of abilities. This was something worse—a prolonged, rising note of pure terror that seemed to scrape against the inside of his skull. Other voices joined it, dozens of them, a chorus of people confronting something their minds couldn't process.
The cultists had reached the tomb.
He climbed faster, arms burning, the screaming driving spikes into his temples. When he reached the hatch, he pressed his ear against the cold metal and listened.
Silence. Then footsteps, retreating, moving away from the tomb chamber.
The screaming stopped as abruptly as it had begun, replaced by the wet sounds of people dying.
Kael pushed open the hatch and emerged into a corridor of reinforced steel and flickering emergency lights. The detention wing's aesthetic was deliberately oppressive—low ceilings, narrow passages, doors thick enough to stop a runaway carriage. Cells lined both walls, their windows Observation slots designed for guards to peer through, not prisoners to see out.
Bodies lay scattered across the floor. Order of Dawn guards, their tabards torn, their throats opened. Cultists mixed among them, killed in turn by something that had come from below. The spiritual residue was overwhelming—dozens of signatures compressed into a space designed for maximum psychological impact.
Cell 39 stood open. Empty.
Cell 40 stood open. Empty.
Cell 41—
Sera Vance crouched in the corner, her blonde hair matted with blood, her eyes wild. She'd torn her prison uniform fighting something, and her hands were slick with someone else's blood. A dead cultist lay at her feet, his neck bent at an angle that suggested she'd done the bending.
"Kael?" Her voice cracked. "They said you were dead. They showed us the execution—"
"Later." He crossed the cell in three strides, catching her arm and pulling her upright. "We need to move. Now."
"The screaming—"
"I know. Cultists breached the tomb beneath us. Something woke up." He checked the corridor. More bodies, more blood, but no movement. The cultists who had penetrated this far were dead. "Can you walk?"
"I can fight." She grabbed the cultist's fallen blade—a short sword etched with protective sigils that would probably burn anyone but her. "There's more of them coming. They want something from the cells down there. Something they were willing to die for."
"The first Soul Reaver's tomb. It's beneath us." Kael pulled her toward the service corridor. "Brother Aldric told me before he died. The Order discovered it decades ago and built this place to keep it secret."
"And the cult wants it open."
"The cult wants whatever's inside." His mind raced through possibilities. The Sepulcher tablets contained the True Name, the ultimate power that could control or destroy the Abyssal Entities. If the cult got to it first—if they used it to amplify the weakening seals—
They'd have everything they needed to finish what the Abyssal Wars had started.
The service corridor stretched ahead, a gauntlet of corpses and emergency lighting. Kael led them through, Sera matching his pace stride for stride, her breathing harsh but steady. Whatever she'd survived in the past three weeks had forged her into something harder than the idealistic investigator who'd tried to save him from the gallows.
"The Order will have the building locked down," she said. "Standard protocol for a breach. We can't go out the front."
"There's a drainage system that runs to the harbor. It's how I got in." He glanced back at her. "But someone needs to warn the city. The cult didn't come here to steal artifacts. They came to accelerate the unsealing."
"Accelerate?" Sera's expression hardened. "You mean they're working with the Entities themselves."
"That's what the Soul Reaver tablets say. The first one knew about the 72 sealed rifts, the Abyssal Entities, everything. If that knowledge gets into the wrong hands—" He didn't finish the sentence. He didn't need to.
They reached the pumping station just as the first explosion tore through the asylum above them. The floor trembled, dust cascading from cracks in the ceiling. Somewhere in the building, structural supports were failing.
"The tomb," Sera breathed. "Whatever they woke up—"
Footsteps behind them. Heavy, purposeful, accompanied by the rhythmic pulse of spiritual energy being gathered and compressed.
Kael spun, placing himself between the threat and Sera. A figure emerged from the corridor they'd just traveled—tall, wrapped in cultist robes, but different from the others. This one radiated power like heat from a forge. The spiritual signature blazed with sickly light, twisted and amplified by whatever corruption had taken root.
"The Reaver." The figure's voice echoed strangely, as if multiple mouths were speaking in harmony. "You've been difficult to find. The Order buried your signature so deep we almost missed you."
"Who are you?"
"My name is of no consequence. What matters is what you carry." The cultist's hand rose, fingers crackling with energy that shouldn't exist outside the Abyss. "The True Name. The tablets say you can extract it. The tablets say you're the only one who can."
"The tablets." Kael's mind worked frantically. The Sepulcher tablets. The recordings left by the first Soul Reaver. They'd mentioned the True Name, but he'd never seen them—he didn't know where they were—
Unless they were in the tomb below. Unless the cult had found them and learned that a later Reaver might be able to extract what they needed.
"You can't have it." The words came automatically. "Even if I knew where it was."
"You'll tell us." The cultist stepped forward, power building around him like a gathering storm. "Or we'll take it from what's left of your allies."
The blade in Sera's hand trembled. Kael could feel her fear—the adrenaline spike, the way her breath shortened—but she didn't retreat. She'd watched him die once. She wouldn't run now.
"There might be another way." The words left his mouth before he'd fully formed the thought. "The tomb. Whatever you woke up down there—it's connected to the first Soul Reaver, isn't it? His remains. His power."
"His soul," the cultist corrected. "Preserved for three thousand years, waiting for this moment."
"And you need a Reaver to harvest it properly." Kael took a step forward, his hands raised in a gesture of negotiation. "That's why you're talking to me instead of just killing me. The soul fragments degrade without someone who can handle the extraction."
The cultist smiled. It was not a pleasant expression.
"You're smarter than the reports suggested. Yes. We need you functional, not dead. Your ability is the key."
"Then let her go." Kael gestured toward Sera. "She's not part of this. Take me to the tomb. I'll harvest whatever you need."
"Kael, no—"
"Quiet." He didn't look at Sera. His focus remained fixed on the cultist, reading the power fluctuations, calculating distances and angles. "My friend leaves. Then we negotiate."
The cultist considered. The building groaned around them, another section collapsing somewhere in the depths. Time was not on their side.
"Your friend can wait outside the asylum," the cultist said finally. "Once we've secured the tomb and verified your cooperation, we'll release her. Those are my terms."
"Agreed." Kael turned to Sera, meeting her eyes for the first time since the cell. "Go. Find the harbor outflow. Get out of the building."
"I'm not leaving you."
"You have to." He gripped her shoulders, fingers digging in hard enough to bruise. "Someone needs to warn the city. The Order of Dawn, the Council—anyone who can mobilize against the cult before they finish what they started. I'll hold them off as long as I can, but you need to run."
Her jaw tightened. Tears gathered in her eyes, but she didn't look away. "You better not be dead again. I didn't survive three weeks in this place just to watch you get yourself killed a second time."
"Deal."
She kissed him—hard, desperate, her blood-stained hands cupping his face—and then she was running, sprinting down the corridor toward the harbor access. The cultist watched her go with an expression of contemptuous amusement.
"Romantic. Foolish. It won't save her if you fail us."
"I know." Kael turned back to face the cultist. "Show me the tomb."
The cultist led him down through collapsing corridors and shattered stairwells, past the bodies of guards and cultists and prisoners who'd been caught in the crossfire. The deeper they went, the stronger the spiritual pressure became—a weight on Kael's mind that grew heavier with every step.
The tomb of the first Soul Reaver.
The foundation beneath Shadowmere Asylum was ancient, predating the Order's occupation by millennia. The walls transitioned from reinforced steel to carved stone covered in script that Kael couldn't read but understood—a language of power, of binding, of souls harvested and contained.
The cultist stopped at a massive door covered in seal after seal after seal. Most were broken now, shattered from within, but the final seal still held—a complex matrix of spiritual energy that pulsed with the rhythm of a heartbeat.
"The Reaver's soul is beyond that door," the cultist said. "We cannot breach it without triggering the containment failsafes. But you—you can walk through."
Kael studied the seal. His Soul Sight showed him the underlying structure—a lattice of binding spells designed to contain something that didn't want to be contained. The first Soul Reaver hadn't just died. He'd imprisoned himself, locked his own soul away to prevent anyone from using his power.
"Why?" he asked. "If he was the first Reaver, why would he seal himself?"
"Does it matter?"
"It might." Kael approached the door, letting his ability probe the seal's edges. The energy responded to him, recognizing something in his spiritual signature. "He left the tablets. He recorded everything he knew about the Entities and the rifts. If he wanted his power lost, why preserve that knowledge?"
"Guilt, maybe." The cultist's voice carried a note of genuine curiosity. "The first Reaver harvested too much. Took powers that should never have been taken. By the end, he understood that his ability was more dangerous than any Entity."
"And the True Name?"
"His final harvest. The name of the greatest Entity, the one that could unmake the seal network. He took it from something that should have destroyed him, and he's kept it locked away ever since."
Kael's hand pressed against the door. The seal recognized him, tested him, found him acceptable—and the door swung open.
Beyond lay a chamber of darkness and power, the air thick with spiritual energy so concentrated it felt solid. At the center of the room, suspended in a column of light that cast no shadows, hung a figure.
Not a body. A soul. Preserved in impossible clarity, frozen in the moment of its own sealing.
The first Soul Reaver.
He looked like Kael might have looked, thirty centuries from now—worn down by power, hollowed out by the weight of what he'd carried. His eyes were closed. His expression held something that might have been peace or might have been the absence of everything else.
"The soul fragments," the cultist prompted. "Harvest them. Take what we need."
Kael stepped closer. The soul pulsed in response to his presence, recognizing the connection between them—the same ability, the same hunger, separated by millennia of history.
And then something unexpected happened.
The first Soul Reaver's eyes opened.
Not physically. There was no physical body to support eyelids. But the soul's attention focused on Kael with sudden, terrible clarity, and he felt words pressing against the inside of his skull:
*You came. I've been waiting.*
Kael stumbled backward, his heart hammering. The cultist moved to grab him, but the soul's power flared, pushing him away.
*Not yet. This one is mine to judge.*
The chamber filled with light—golden, ancient, carrying the weight of three thousand years. Kael found himself unable to move, pinned in place by a will far stronger than his own.
*You carry my gift,* the voice continued. *The Reaver ability. Passed down through channels I never intended, into hands I never chose. Tell me, inheritor—have you used it well?*
"I don't know," Kael managed. "I've used it to survive."
*Survival is not purpose. I sealed myself because I could not control what I had become. The harvest drove me mad, and madness would have destroyed everything. Do you understand now why I left the tablets? Why I recorded everything?*
"To warn us?"
*To give you a choice.* The soul's form flickered, destabilizing. *I could not control my hunger. I took and took until there was nothing left of who I was. But you—you have something I lacked. Something the tablets cannot teach.*
"What?"
*Limits.* The word hung in the air like a verdict. *I killed without restraint. You do not. I consumed without conscience. You hesitate. The ability is the same, but the wielder determines whether it becomes salvation or damnation.*
Behind him, the cultist was growing impatient. Kael could feel the pressure of gathered power, ready to strike if this didn't proceed according to plan.
"The True Name," Kael said. "You have it. The cult wants it to break the seals."
*Yes.* The first Soul Reaver's soul pulsed with something that might have been regret. *And I will give it to you. But understand—once you know the Name, you become the seal. The knowledge binds you to this purpose. You cannot share it. You cannot use it. You can only guard it, forever, until someone stronger comes to take your place.*
"And if I refuse?"
*Then the cult takes it by force. They lack the subtlety to extract cleanly. They will shatter what remains of my soul and scatter the fragments across the Abyss. The Name will still exist, but it will be lost to any hope of control.* A pause. *I would trust you than trust chaos.*
Kael thought of Sera, running through collapsing corridors. Of the city beyond, millions of people with no idea what was awakening beneath their feet. Of the 72 sealed rifts and the Entities waiting behind them.
He thought of the power he'd already taken. The abilities he'd harvested from the dead. The path he'd walked since waking on the execution platform.
"What happens if I accept?"
*You carry my burden. My knowledge, my guilt, my purpose. The soul fragments will merge with yours—not overpowering, but informing. You will understand the seals as I understood them. You will know the Entities as I knew them. And you will know the Name.*
"And the cult?"
*Will receive nothing. My final act will be to deny them.*
The cultist's power flared. "This is not what we agreed—"
The first Soul Reaver's soul exploded into light.
Kael felt it pouring into him—memories and power and three thousand years of accumulated purpose. The sensation was overwhelming, a flood of information that threatened to drown his consciousness.
But beneath the flood, something crystallized. A single point of awareness that remained his, that anchored him to himself even as the ancient power reshaped his understanding of the world.
When the light faded, he was kneeling in an empty chamber. The column of suspension had collapsed. The first Soul Reaver was gone, his soul finally released after millennia of imprisonment.
The cultist lay crumpled against the far wall, dead—killed by the backlash of power he'd tried to harness.
Kael stood on unsteady legs and examined his hands. They looked the same. But beneath his skin, something had fundamentally changed.
The True Name was his now. Sealed away in a part of his mind he couldn't access, couldn't examine, couldn't share. He felt its weight—a responsibility he hadn't asked for and couldn't refuse.
The asylum was collapsing around him. He needed to move.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted the soul of the First Reaver. Consciousness stable. Acquired: Complete knowledge of the 72 Sealed Rifts. Complete knowledge of the Six Orders. Complete knowledge of the True Name.]
[Level unchanged: Awakened]

Kael stumbled toward the exit, the floor tilting beneath his feet. The building was dying, the tomb's destruction triggering structural failures that would bring the entire asylum down.
Somewhere in the chaos, he heard footsteps. Running. Getting closer.
"Kael!" Sera's voice echoed down the corridor. "Kael, where are you?"
He found the strength to call back. "Here!"
She appeared a moment later, her face streaked with soot and tears. She grabbed his arm and pulled him toward the harbor access, speaking rapidly about collapsed passages and flooding and the boats waiting in the shallows.
But Kael's attention kept drifting to the weight in his mind. The Name. The knowledge. The burden he now carried.
The first Soul Reaver had trusted him with everything.
Now Kael had to figure out what to do with it.
They burst out of the drainage tunnel and into the harbor's pre-dawn darkness, gasping, soaked, alive. Behind them, Shadowmere Asylum groaned and collapsed into the sea, taking the tomb and the cultists and three thousand years of secrets with it.
But not all the secrets.
Kael looked back at the destruction, at the fires beginning to light the sky, and felt the weight of what he carried settling into place.
He had learned more than he ever wanted to know. The Six Orders. The 72 rifts. The true nature of the Entities beyond the Veil.
And somewhere in the back of his mind, locked away where no one could reach it, the True Name waited.
The footsteps behind them were no longer alone. Multiple sets, converging from different directions, accompanied by the crackle of spiritual energy being gathered for pursuit.
The Order of Dawn had found them.
Sera's grip on his arm tightened. "What now?"
Kael scanned the harbor, calculating routes and resources and enemies. The fishing platforms to the north. The smuggler caves to the east. The open water to the south, where anything could be waiting.
And beneath it all, the weight of the True Name, demanding a choice he wasn't ready to make.
The footsteps grew louder.
The sun began to rise over Thornwick, painting the harbor in shades of gold and blood.
