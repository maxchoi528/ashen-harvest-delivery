The door to Kael's rented room exploded inward, the cheap wood splintering like kindling.
He rolled from the narrow bed, fingers closing around the knife beneath his pillow. Moonlight cut through the smoke and debris, illuminating three figures in the doorway—each wearing the black leather coats of the Iron Vanguard.
"You Kael Ashborn?" The lead figure stepped through the ruined doorframe, gauntleted hands raised.
Kael counted heartbeats. Three intruders. The window sat four paces behind him. "Depends on who's asking."
"Iron Vanguard Strike Team Seven." A wiry man with a scarred jaw produced a badge. "You're wanted for questioning regarding cult activity in the Ashworth District. Come quietly, no one gets hurt."
No one gets hurt. Kael's mouth twisted. The Iron Vanguard had a reputation for "questioning" that left witnesses catatonic. "What cult activity?"
"That's for the interrogators to—"
A wet gurgle interrupted him. The scarred man's eyes bulged. His hands flew to his throat, clawing at something invisible. Blood seeped between his fingers.
The other two Vanguard soldiers spun, weapons drawn, searching the shadows behind them. One fired into the darkness. The crack of the pistol shattered the quiet.
Kael moved.
His blade found the second soldier's kidney as the man turned. The third spun, rifle swinging. Kael ducked beneath the blow, driving his elbow into the man's solar plexus. The soldier doubled over, and Kael brought the knife around in a precise arc across the throat.
Silence fell, broken only by the gurgling of the first soldier, who had collapsed against the doorframe.
Kael crouched beside him, pressing his palm to the man's chest. The soldier's eyes widened with recognition—and terror.

Kael rifled through the dead soldiers' belongings. Two soul-essence vials—standard grade, maybe worth two hundred sovereigns combined. A folded document bearing the Iron Vanguard seal. A small journal filled with codes he couldn't decipher.
The document confirmed what he already suspected: the Vanguard had been conducting sweeps through the Ashworth District for weeks. Civilian casualties were listed as "acceptable parameters." No mention of what they were hunting.
He pulled on one of the black leather coats, retrieved the Vanguard badge, and checked the magazine in the rifle. Three rounds remaining. Not much, but better than nothing.
The stairwell of the boarding house was empty when he descended. Mrs. Thornbury, the proprietor, had fled to the country three days ago after Kael had politely explained that her building might become a target. He felt a flicker of guilt—genuine allies never.
Outside, the gaslights of Thornwick's lower districts cast amber pools across rain-slicked cobblestones. Steam rose from grated vents, obscuring the upper stories of crooked tenements. The distant clang of factory bells announced the shift change at the ironworks.
Kael oriented himself. The Ashworth District lay northeast, maybe forty minutes on foot through streets he knew better from necessity than preference. The old cathedral would be there, waiting.
He moved into the maze of alleys and side streets, each step deliberate. The harvested patrol routes painted a mental map in his mind—Vanguard checkpoints at intervals, blind spots between their patrol lines, the timing of their rotations. Information that had cost a man his life.
The Shadow Court's involvement nagged at him. The dying soldier had mentioned the Whisper King, and everyone in Thornwick's underworld knew that name. She was a puppet master, a voice in the darkness that directed cults, criminal syndicates, and worse. The Whisper King's identity remained unknown—some claimed she was an ancient Entity wearing human flesh, others whispered she was a former member of all six Orders who had transcended the mortal coil.
None of those theories explained why the Whisper King would care about him.
Two hours before dawn, Kael reached the perimeter of the Ashworth District. The streets here were narrower, the buildings older, their facades cracked and weathered. Most windows were dark. A few showed the faint glow of candles or oil lamps—survivors who couldn't afford to flee, or those too stubborn to leave their homes.
The old cathedral rose at the district's heart, its spire a black finger pointing at the clouded sky. Once, its bells had called the faithful to prayer. Now the doors hung from broken hinges, and the windows were boarded over with planks that had rotted in the damp.
Kael circled the building, keeping to the shadows. The Vanguard patrol routes the soldier had provided showed no current presence, but that meant little. Whoever controlled this operation could have pulled them back for all he knew.
Movement caught his eye. A figure on the cathedral's roof, barely visible against the darker sky. Someone was watching.
He pressed himself into a recessed doorway, controlling his breathing. The figure remained motionless, a sentinel scanning the streets below. Not a soldier—the posture was too fluid, too predatory. An Awakened.
A soft whistle came from behind him. Kael spun, knife already in motion.
"Easy." The voice belonged to a woman stepping from the deeper shadows of the alley. She was maybe thirty, dressed in the practical leathers of a street operative, her dark hair pulled back in a severe knot. Kael kept his knife ready. "The Order of Dawn disbanded its intelligence operations in Thornwick after the Concordat violations."

Destroying their leadership. That explained the chaos he'd sensed in the city—the power vacuum, the competing factions. Someone had been cutting heads off the hydra.

The connection crystallized in Kael's mind. The cult weakening the seals, the Whisper King funding them, the sudden interest in Soul Reavers. Someone wanted to break the barrier between worlds, and Soul Reavers were key to that goal.

A trap. He recognized it for what it was—the Order using him as bait. But the information she'd provided painted a picture he needed to understand. The conspiracy was larger than cult activity in a single district. It involved the seals, the Whisper King, maybe the fundamental structure of reality itself.

"Name it."
"Access to the Order's archives. Specifically, anything on Soul Reavers—historical records, documented cases, theories about the origin of the ability."

"I'm risking my neck for your intelligence. Consider it payment."
"I'll need to consult with my superiors." She extended the parchment. "For now, take the plans. Enter through the north transept—the main doors are likely trapped. Report what you find to me at the Meridian Café on Copper Street at midday."

"Then the Order will mourn a useful asset and adjust accordingly." She turned to leave, then paused. "One more thing. The cultists you've encountered—they're not the threat. The Whisper King has Awakened under her control, beings who've transcended the normal stages of spiritual development. Be very careful inside."
She vanished into the shadows as silently as she'd appeared, leaving Kael alone with the cathedral's dark silhouette.
