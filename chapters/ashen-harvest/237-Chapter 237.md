The window shattered inward. Kael dropped through smoke and broken glass, boots striking the warehouse floor with a muffled thud. Two cultists spun toward the breach, their robes still smoldering from the flashpowder charges he'd used to mask his entry.
"Contact! The Reaver comes!"
Kael didn't waste breath on reply. He lunged left, shoulder-checking a cultist into a support pillar while his free hand found the man's throat. One twist. A wet crack. The body slumped, but Kael was already moving, rolling beneath a sweeping blade and coming up with the dead man's ceremonial knife.
The warehouse stretched before him—a cathedral of industrial decay. Conveyor belts frozen mid-operation. Rusted machinery draped in chains that rattled in the draft from shattered skylights. And everywhere, the symbols: spiraling glyphs carved into every surface, pulsing with sickly luminescence that painted the space in shades of infection.
Forty paces to the central platform. That's what the intelligence had promised. Forty paces and he'd find the rift-anchor the cult was using to bleed power from Seal Delta-12.
He covered twenty before three more cultists materialized from behind a massive boiler. Their movements weren't the uncoordinated flailing of zealots. They flanked. They advanced in formation. One carried a net weighted with iron filings, another a blade coated in something that ate at the light around it.
Trained. Coordinated. Organized.
*Not random scavengers*, Kael realized with a chill that had nothing to do with the warehouse's damp air. *Someone's been teaching them.*
The iron-net cultist lunged. Kael pivoted, deflected the throw, but the weighted edges still caught his sleeve, dragging fabric and flesh. He tore free, leaving a strip of skin behind. The wound burned with spiritual venom—not lethal, but enough to slow him.
"Kill him slowly," a voice commanded from the platform. Female. Cultured. Familiar.
Kael's head snapped up. Through the haze of pain, he caught sight of her: tall, gaunt, draped in robes of deep violet. A mask covered her face—white porcelain, expressionless, with a single tear carved into each cheek.
He knew that mask. Knew it from wanted posters and whispered warnings in the underground.
The Widow's Tears. One of the Shadow Court's three enforcement phylacteries.
*They knew I'd come.*
The two remaining cultists pressed their attack. Kael gave ground, buying seconds to assess. The poison in his arm throbbed with each heartbeat. The Widow hadn't moved from her platform, but her presence alone seemed to bend the air around her—a pressure that made breathing harder, thinking slower.
"Impressive," the Widow said, her voice carrying effortlessly across the chaos. "The death-row monster, picking through Abyssal dregs like a common grave-robber. Tell me, Kael Ashborn—does it taste like victory? Taking from those too weak to resist?"
Her mockery cut precisely because it wasn't wrong. Kael had carved his path through corpses, harvesting skills and power from the fallen. He'd told himself it was survival. He'd told himself the world was better without its worst. He'd told himself many things.
The knife-wielding cultist overextended. Kael opened his throat with a backhanded slash, spraying arterial black across the ancient machinery. The iron-net wielder tried to retreat, but Kael was faster now, closing the gap, driving the ceremonial blade up beneath the ribs and twisting.
Two down. One wounded, crawling toward the exit.
The Widow descended from her platform, each step deliberate. Her hands emerged from her sleeves—long-fingered, scarred, stained with something that wasn't ink.
"You're confused," she observed. "Wondering why the Shadow Court cares about a cult's pet project. Why we'd station an Enforcer in a backwater warehouse in Thornwick's lowest quarter."
Kael wiped his blade on a fallen cultist's robe. "Enlighten me."
"Because this isn't a cult anymore." The Widow stopped ten paces distant. "It became something else eighteen months ago. When the Seeker found the first tablet."
*Tablet.* The word hit Kael like a physical blow. His mind raced through implications, discarding each as fast as it formed.
The Sepulcher tablets. The recordings of the first Soul Reaver.
"Zarek's been busy," Kael said, keeping his voice flat.
"Zarek is a tool. A useful one, but nothing more." The Widow tilted her head, studying him like a specimen. "The true architect has larger ambitions. You could have been part of that vision, Ashborn. Your ability is... Rare. Valuable. The old orders would never have understood what you represent."
"And you do?"
"We created your kind once before. We know the potential. The limitations." Her fingers traced a pattern in the air—spiraling, almost hypnotic. "The first Soul Reaver walked these same paths. Harvested from the fallen. Grew strong on death's bounty. And when he transcended mortality, do you know what he became?"
Kael's blood chilled. "You're baiting me."
"I'm offering context. Something you'll desperately need when the next hours unfold." She gestured toward the far wall, where shadows had begun to coalesce into doorways that shouldn't exist. "Your intelligence was accurate. There is a rift-anchor here. But it's not bleeding Seal Delta-12. It's bleeding something far more valuable."
More shadows. More doorways. Kael counted six, seven, eight—each one vomiting forth more cultists in disciplined formations.
"Eight Cult Cells," the Widow continued. "Forty combatants. And you, wounded and alone, with poison already reaching your heart." She spread her arms. "Or you could come with us. Accept what you're becoming instead of fighting it. The Seeker—or, the one who directs him—has use for a Soul Reaver who understands his purpose."
"Your boss wants to recruit me?"
"Our master wants to restore balance. The Seals weaken. The Entities stir. The Six Orders squabble while the world crumbles." The Widow's masked face betrayed nothing, but her voice hardened. "We offer purpose. Strength. A place in the architecture of what's coming."
Kael flexed his wounded arm. The spiritual venom had spread to his elbow now, a cold fire racing beneath his skin. She wasn't bluffing about the timeline.
"You mention the first Soul Reaver," he said slowly. "What happened to him?"
Something shifted in the Widow's posture. A microsecond pause, quickly masked.
"He discovered truth. He made a choice. And then he became what we now serve."
"What you serve—"
"I offer you the same discovery. The same choice." The Widow extended one scarred hand. "Come with me, and learn what the Orders have hidden. What the Seals truly are. Why your ability exists."
The cultists fanned wider, cutting off his retreat. Eight cells. Forty fighters. And behind them, the Widow—a woman whose reputation included the systematic dismantling of three Order installations and the complete annihilation of a Silver Dawn strike team.
Kael's options narrowed to a razor's edge.
He could fight. Die slowly from the poison while cutting through dozens of enemies who knew exactly how to counter his abilities. The Soul Reaver power was formidable, but it required corpses—and everyone here was still breathing.
Or he could run. But the Shadow Court didn't send Enforcers for quarry that escaped.
Or...
Kael looked at the bodies at his feet. Three dead cultists. Cold. Still bleeding.
*Physical touch required.*
He dropped the ceremonial knife. Before the Widow could react, Kael knelt beside the first corpse—one of the flanking specialists—and pressed both palms flat against the cooling flesh.
[System Notification] Soul Harvest initiated. Target: Cultist (Stage 2 Awakened). Corpse freshness: a few minutes. Extraction in progress...
The familiar sensation flooded through him. Not painful—not anymore—but intense. Like drowning in ice water that burned. He felt the cultist's life force pouring into channels he'd never known he possessed. Memories, skills, fragments of identity: all shredded into raw material that his ability could shape.
The Widow lunged forward. "Stop him!"
But she was too late. The harvest completed.
[System Notification] Soul Harvest complete. Extracted: Enhanced Endurance (Lesser), Combat Reflexes (Minor), Spiritual Resistance I. Acquired essence: 47 units. Physical recovery accelerated. Toxin purge engaged.
Power surged through Kael's poisoned arm. The spiritual venom recoiled from his renewed flesh, burning away like morning fog. He rose to his feet, feeling the stolen endurance settling into his bones like iron reinforcements.
"Better," the Widow observed. No surprise in her voice. Only cold calculation. "You understand faster than expected."
"I understand that you're stalling." Kael's eyes swept the warehouse, counting exits, measuring distances. "Why?"
"Because the ritual needs another six minutes to complete. And you—" She smiled behind her mask. "—you were always going to be the final component."
The warehouse shuddered. Not from combat. From something beneath it—something that pulsed through the floor in rhythms that matched Kael's heartbeat.
*Spiritual resonance.*
"What did you bring here?" Kael demanded.
"Not what. Who." The Widow's hand traced another pattern. "The cult's been collecting bodies for weeks. Unawakened. Awakened. Anyone who died in Thornwick's lower quarter, regardless of cause. Dozens of corpses, harvested for spiritual essence, fed into this facility's foundation."
"That's hundreds of people—"
"That will become a gate." The Widow's voice rose, sharp with fervor. "Not a rift-anchor bleeding power from a Seal. A proper gate. A door wide enough for what waits on the other side to step through."
Kael's stomach dropped. The 72 sealed rifts. The Abyssal Entities. And the cult wasn't trying to weaken the Seals—they were trying to *create new openings.*
"The Shadow Court wouldn't risk this," he said, but doubt crept into his voice. "The Seals protect everyone. If they collapse—"
"The Seals protect the Orders' power. Not humanity. Not you." The Widow stepped closer, her formation of cultists tightening. "The Abyss isn't our enemy, Ashborn. It's our heritage. Our birthright. And you—you could be the key to unlocking it properly. The way the first Reaver intended."
The first Reaver. The one who'd discovered truth and made a choice.
*The original Soul Reaver's fate.*
Kael's mind raced through fragments. What did the Widow know? What had she revealed without revealing?
"The first Soul Reaver," he said slowly. "He didn't fail. He didn't die. He *chose* the Abyss."
"Chose to become something more than human. Something that could bridge the gap between realms." The Widow spread her arms to encompass the spiraling glyphs, the pulsing lights, the shadows bleeding from impossible doorways. "We serve his vision. We continue his work. And now, with the tablets recovered, with the ritual preparations complete, we are *weeks* from success."
Six minutes. She needed six more minutes.
Kael assessed the cultists. Forty fighters, armed with spiritual poisons and binding nets. An Enforcer of the Shadow Court. And beneath his feet, hundreds of harvested souls being burned as fuel for something catastrophic.
*Too many variables. Not enough time.*
He needed to reach the ritual site. Destroy whatever anchor they'd constructed. And if possible, harvest something from the Widow herself—though Enforcers weren't typically left as corpses.
The poison cults charged first. Iron nets flew. Blades descended in coordinated strikes. Kael moved through them like water through stones, his newly harvested reflexes compensating for gaps in his experience. He deflected a net, used the deflection to blind its thrower, then drove his elbow into the man's throat.
Another cultist fell. Kael harvested mid-combat, pressing his palm against the dying man's chest.
[System Notification] Soul Harvest: Target expired. Extracted: Spiritual Sense (Minor). Acquired essence: 12 units. Combat efficiency improved.
The spiritual sense expanded his awareness. He could feel the ritual's pull now—a downward pressure that tugged at his own essence. Could feel the corpses stacked in the basement below, their residual energy siphoned into a central reservoir. Could feel the Widow watching, measuring, *waiting*.
*She's not fighting because she doesn't need to*, he realized. *The ritual will do her work for her.*
Two more cultists died under his blade. He harvested again, gaining fragments of their training—how to move in formation, how to coordinate attacks, how to recognize and counter Soul Reaver abilities.
*They've fought Reavers before. Studied us. Prepared for us.*
The realization settled like lead in his gut. The Shadow Court hadn't sent an Enforcer for a simple cultist operation. They'd sent her for *him* specifically. Which meant someone had known he would come. Someone had fed him this intelligence.
*Double agent. Or triple.*
Kael's borrowed knowledge let him predict the next attack pattern. He sidestepped, used a dead cultist's body as a shield against the iron net, then launched himself toward the platform. The Widow hadn't moved, but two new figures emerged from the shadows flanking her.
Not cultists. Shadow Court operatives. Stage 3 Awakened at least, their spiritual pressure radiating outward like heat from a forge.
"You adapt quickly," the Widow acknowledged. "But adaptation won't save you. The ritual will complete. The gate will open. And you will stand at its threshold, making the same choice the first Reaver made."
"Which is?"
"Whether to remain human. Or become something that transcends humanity's limitations entirely."
Kael planted his feet on the platform's edge, surrounded by glyphs that pulsed faster now, matching his racing heartbeat. Below him, he could feel the accumulated essence of hundreds of dead—raw power waiting to be shaped.
"The first Reaver chose wrong," he said.
"You assume you know his choice." The Widow's voice carried something new. Something that might have been pity. "You've only heard the Orders' version. The lies they tell to keep you compliant."
"Then tell me the truth."
"Join us. Complete the ritual. Discover truth for yourself."
The operatives circled closer. The ritual's pull intensified. And in the distance, Kael heard something else—boots on cobblestone, shouts in coordinated patterns, the distinctive crack of spiritual discharge weapons.
*The Orders. Someone called them.*
"Reinforcements arrive," the Widow observed. "How convenient. How predictable." She turned toward the warehouse entrance, where torchlight now flickered through the smoke. "But they won't reach us in time. And when they do, they'll find only bodies. Your body. And the gate, already open."
She raised her hand. The glyphs blazed white-hot.
"Last chance, Ashborn. Join what made you. Or be harvested like all the others."
Kael's hand found the cold metal of a cultist's beside him. The body was hours dead—degraded, drained by the ritual's preliminary stages. But it was still a corpse. Still harvestable.
*Physical touch required.*
He knelt, pressed both palms against the corpse's chest, and pulled.
[System Notification] Soul Harvest: Target significantly degraded. Corpse freshness: several hours. Extraction compromised. Extracted: Fragmented Memory (corrupted). Acquired essence: 8 units.
The memory fragment was damaged, but fragments could still reveal. Kael forced himself to process it—images flickering through his mind like shattered mirrors. A woman's face, older, scarred. A child crying in the darkness. A voice saying *it's for the greater good, you'll understand someday*.
And beneath it all, a name. A symbol. A truth that the Shadow Court had buried.
*The first Soul Reaver didn't choose the Abyss.*
*He was betrayed by those who served it.*
Kael's eyes snapped open. The Widow had frozen mid-gesture, her masked face tilted at an angle that suggested surprise.
"You saw something," she said. "The harvest. You saw—"
"I saw enough."
The operatives attacked. Kael moved, but not toward them—toward the ritual's heart. He drove his hand into the central glyph, where power pooled like water in a basin. The energy burned, but Soul Reaver harvesting wasn't about conservation. It was about *taking*.
[System Notification] Soul Harvest: Ritual energy reservoir. Extracting hostile essence. WARNING: Spiritual incompatibility detected. WARNING: Entity interference likely.
Pain exploded through Kael's nervous system. Not physical pain—something deeper. Something that tugged at the core of his being, trying to reshape it, redirect it, *become* something other than human.
The Abyss was reaching for him through the connection.
The ritual wanted to make him its vessel.
Kael pulled harder. The reservoir cracked. Energy discharged upward through the warehouse ceiling, shattering what remained of the skylights in a cascade of glass and light. The glyphs flickered, died, flared one final time.
And the ritual *stopped*.
"No." The Widow's composure shattered. "Sixteen seconds. I needed sixteen more seconds—"
"You needed a Reaver," Kael said, his voice raw from the spiritual feedback. "You needed someone who could interface with the ritual's core. Someone whose ability let them take from anything, including Abyssal energy."
He rose from the broken platform, his body trembling from the extraction's strain. Around him, the cultists had stopped moving. They'd felt the ritual fail. They'd felt their purpose torn away.
"This operation is finished," Kael continued. "Your gate isn't opening. Your grand design just collapsed."
The Widow stood motionless for three heartbeats. Then she smiled—a cold, knowing expression that chilled Kael more than the failed ritual.
"Collapsed?" She laughed, a brittle sound that echoed through the dying warehouse. "You stopped one ritual. One gate. One small piece of a much larger design."
She stepped backward into shadow, her form already dissolving at the edges.
"The Seeker has three more tablets. We have seventeen more sites operational across the continent. And you—" Her masked face turned toward him one final time. "—you just proved that Reavers can channel Abyssal energy without immediate corruption. That's data we needed. Information your harvesting provided."
Kael's blood turned to ice. "You wanted me to interfere."
"We wanted to see what you'd do. What you *could* do." The Widow's voice faded to a whisper. "The first Reaver faced this same choice. The same moment. And he chose correctly—for himself. You'll face the choice again, Ashborn. The Abyss remembers those who touch it. It will reach for you again."
"Tell me who the first Reaver was."
But the shadow had consumed her entirely. She was gone.
The warehouse fell silent. Cultists scattered into the darkness, their coordinated formations dissolving into individual panic. The Order forces closed in from multiple directions now, their shouts echoing off the industrial ruins.
Kael stood alone on the shattered platform, his hand still pressed against the dead glyph, his mind reeling from everything he'd learned.
*The cult isn't random. It's organized, funded, directed by the Shadow Court.*
*The Shadow Court serves the first Soul Reaver—or what he became.*
*Soul Reavers can channel Abyssal energy. The ability isn't just about harvesting from the dead.*
*And somewhere, the true architect is preparing seventeen more rituals across the continent.*
An Order strike team breached the warehouse entrance. Six Awakened in matching armor, their weapons trained on him.
"Kael Ashborn." The lead operative's voice carried authority and suspicion in equal measure. "Stand down. You're under arrest for unauthorized harvesting, trespassing on sealed territory, and suspected collaboration with Abyssal elements."
Kael raised his hands slowly, careful not to make any sudden movements. His body ached from the ritual extraction. His mind buzzed with fragmented revelations.
*Seventeen more sites. Three more tablets. The Seeker—Zarek—still out there, planning something worse.*
*And the first Soul Reaver's fate remains a mystery. Was he betrayed? Did he choose the Abyss? Or is the truth something else entirely?*
The Order operatives advanced, binding wards gleaming in the torchlight. Kael let them approach, too exhausted to fight, too confused to run.
*They'll want answers. The Shadow Court will want me dead or recruited. And somewhere in the darkness, the Widow is preparing her next move.*
One of the operatives noticed the bodies. Her expression hardened.
"How many did you kill?"
"Enough to survive." Kael's voice came out flat, factual. "Enough to stop the ritual."
The lead operative studied him for a long moment. Whatever she saw in Kael's face made her pause.
"You'll explain everything. The cult, the ritual, what you harvested." It wasn't a request. "And if we discover you've been working with the Shadow Court—"
"I'm not working with anyone." Kael met her gaze without flinching. "I'm trying to stop whatever's coming. Same as you."
She didn't believe him. He could see that clearly. But she also couldn't deny that he'd been here, fighting cultists, disrupting what appeared to be a major Abyssal operation.
"Take him in," she ordered. "Separate containment. Full interrogation. And someone get Archmagister Vane. Now."
*Vane. The Iron Vanguard commander who'd been hunting him for months.*
Kael allowed himself to be led from the warehouse, his thoughts churning through implications. The Shadow Court wanted him. The Orders wanted him imprisoned or dead. And somewhere, the true architect of this conspiracy was moving pieces Kael couldn't yet see.
The night air hit his face as they exited. Cold. Sharp. Carrying the scent of smoke and blood and something else—something that smelled like ozone before a storm.
Something that smelled like the Abyss reaching through broken Seals.
*Seventeen more sites. Seventeen more chances for the gate to open.*
*And I'm the only one who knows how to stop it.*
Kael's jaw tightened. Whatever came next, whatever trials awaited him in Order custody, one truth had become inescapable:
The conspiracy had revealed itself. And the war for the world's survival had just begun in earnest.
Footsteps echoed behind him in the darkness. Not the Order operatives—they'd already taken position ahead.
Closer. Steadier. The measured pace of someone who'd been waiting for this moment.
Kael twisted, trying to see past the torchlight, past the shadows, to whoever approached from the ruined warehouse's far corner.
The footsteps stopped. A figure emerged from the darkness—a woman, young, her face half-hidden by a hood. She carried no weapon. Her hands were raised in a gesture of surrender.
But her eyes...
Her eyes burned with spiritual light that shouldn't exist outside of late-stage Awakened.
"You're harder to find than the whispers suggested," she said, her voice low, urgent. "I need to speak with you. About the first Reaver. About what the Shadow Court is really building."
Kael's restraints hummed with binding energy. He couldn't reach her even if he tried.
"Who are you?"
She hesitated. Then, slowly, she lowered her hood.
Kael's breath caught.
He recognized that face. That precise arrangement of features. The same face he'd seen in a fragmented, corrupted memory barely minutes ago.
"My name is Sera," she said. "And I'm looking for the truth about my mother. The first Soul Reaver."
The question hung in the air between them, unanswered, pregnant with implications that could reshape everything Kael thought he understood about his power, his purpose, and the enemy he faced.
Behind him, the Order operatives shouted for him to stop, to remain still, to submit to custody.
Ahead, a woman claiming impossible lineage offered answers that might help—or might be another trap designed to lead him deeper into the Shadow Court's web.
And in the distance, echoing through Thornwick's narrow streets, came the sound of more footsteps. More figures emerging from shadow.
The Shadow Court had found him first. Or maybe they'd never lost him at all.
