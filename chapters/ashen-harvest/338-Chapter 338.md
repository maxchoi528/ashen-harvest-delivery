The safehouse smelled of lamp oil and unwashed bodies. Kael sat in the corner, cataloging the night's harvest while Commander Vasrick debriefed the team. Twelve cultists eliminated. Four civilian casualties. Two of her Iron Vanguard soldiers wounded—one critically.
Not a victory. Not a defeat. Just another day in the war that had swallowed the continent whole.
"Four raids in one week," Vasrick said, her scarred face grim in the flickering light. "All coordinated. All hitting different districts simultaneously." She spread a map across the makeshift table, pins marking the attack sites. "They're not random. Someone's orchestrating this."
Kael studied the pattern. Four points forming a rough circle around the city's industrial quarter. "They're testing defenses. Looking for weaknesses."
"Looking for something," Serra corrected. The pale woman from the Order of Dawn traced her finger along the map's edge, her eyes distant. "The attacks started three days after the Council's emergency session. Whatever they're doing, it requires resources. Manpower." She looked up. "Or distraction."
The other team members—a mixed group of Order soldiers and freelance Awakened pressed into service—exchanged uneasy glances. Kael understood their fear. The cults had always been a nuisance, isolated lunatics preaching about the "Great Unsealing" and trying to sacrifice villagers to their Entities. Now they moved like an army.
"How many cultists total?" Kael asked.
"Estimates range from three hundred to one thousand," Vasrick replied. "We're fighting blind. The Mortician's Guild is overwhelmed with the dead. The Arcane Spire is stretched thin protecting the seal network. And the Shadow Court—" She stopped, something dark crossing her expression.
"What about them?"
"Nothing." Vasrick's voice hardened. "They're doing nothing. Orders from on high."
Kael filed that away. The Shadow Court operated in shadows, yes, but they always had their fingers in every pie worth watching. Their silence meant either paralysis or complicity. Neither option sat well.
"Tomorrow's raid," Kael said, changing the subject. "What are we walking into?"
Vasrick's jaw tightened. "Intelligence suggests a staging ground in the Ashworth district. Warehouse complex, underground sections. We've got maybe thirty hostiles, possibly Awakened-class. The goal is destruction of supplies and capture of any documents we can find."
"Just us?"
"The Spire is providing magical support remotely. The Dawn sent two scouts ahead for reconnaissance. But on the ground?" She gestured at their ragtag group. "We're it. Everyone else is holding the northern wall."
Kael nodded slowly. Six Orders. Thousands of Awakened. And the war effort reduced to scattered teams defending a single city from coordinated assault.
The numbers didn't add up.

The Ashworth district had been a manufacturing hub once, back when the steam engines still ran and the factories belched smoke into Thornwick's grey sky. Now the buildings stood silent, their windows like empty eye sockets staring at nothing. The machines had stopped three weeks ago when the cult attacks began targeting the industrial infrastructure.
Kael moved through the pre-dawn shadows, his senses extended to their limits. Sovereign stage granted him perception beyond what normal Awakened could achieve—subtle gradations of spiritual energy, the lingering resonance of violence, the faint pulse of corrupted life force that marked cultist presence.
Twenty meters ahead. Basement level. Twenty-eight signatures, all faintly glowing with that sickly purple taint.
He signaled to Vasrick, who held up three fingers. Three entry points. Standard pincer formation.
Serra and her Dawn partner would breach from the east. Two of Vasrick's soldiers from the north. Kael and the Commander would hit the main entrance head-on.
"Takeshi, Orin, hold position at the secondary exit," Vasrick ordered through the communication stones. "No one leaves without passing through us."
Kael checked his blade. Standard iron, nothing special—against Awakened-grade enemies, spiritual weapons mattered less than positioning and timing. His true weapon hung at his side in a sheath that had never known a formal name, only the cold certainty that it would find its mark when called upon.
The doors were wooden. Old wood, weakened by neglect. Kael's boot connected once, twice, and they burst inward like a broken jaw.
Flash-bang talismans sailed through the darkness. Screams erupted. Kael was already moving, his body a blur of controlled violence as he plunged into the chaos.
A cultist lunged at him with a rusted halberd, spiritual energy crackling along its blade. Kael ducked under the swing, his own blade carving upward in a devastating counter-stroke. The cultist staggered, blood spraying from a wound that opened from hip to shoulder.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 12 units of spiritual essence from the remains. Fragment acquired: Pain Resistance I.]
Kael was already spinning, parrying a strike from his left, driving his elbow into the attacker's throat. The cultist crumpled, choking.
"Contact! Contact! They're in the—"
The voice cut off as Sera emerged from a side corridor, her twin blades wet with blood. She gave Kael a curt nod. "Cleared the eastern wing. Six down."
"Same north," one of Vasrick's soldiers reported through the stone. "Commander, we have movement below. Heavy resistance."
Kael felt it then—a pulse of spiritual energy so dense it made his teeth ache. Something in the basement was radiating power on a scale he hadn't encountered outside of Order Masters.
"Vasrick," he said quietly. "We need to accelerate the timeline."
She must have sensed it too. Her face had gone pale beneath the grime. "Serra, take your partner and hold the main floor. Kael, with me. We finish this now."
They descended into darkness.

The basement stretched before them like the throat of some vast beast. Support pillars of rusted iron ran at irregular intervals, casting grotesque shadows across the stone floor. And in the center, arranged in a precise geometric pattern, stood rows of glass cylinders.
Kael stopped cold.
Each cylinder contained a body. Human bodies, suspended in viscous fluid that glowed with that same purple corruption. Tubes connected them to a central apparatus—a massive device of brass and bone that pulsed with sickly light.
"What in the Sun God's name," Vasrick whispered.
Kael's mind raced through possibilities. Soul harvesting. No—wrong setup. Soul storage? But storage for what purpose?
"The Sealed Rifts," he said, the realization hitting like a physical blow. "They're not trying to break them open. They're trying to strengthen them."
Vasrick's expression went from confused to horrified in an instant. "That would require—"
"Direct interface with the Abyss. Soul-quality interfaces." Kael gestured at the cylinders. "They're using human souls as conduits. The stronger the Awakened, the better the connection."
The device in the center pulsed brighter. A voice echoed from somewhere within its brass heart—not words, but a presence, vast and hungry and aware.
*You are too late, Son of Ash.*
Kael's blood ran cold. That voice. He'd heard it once before, in a nightmare he'd never been able to shake. The voice of the First Rift, the Entity that had nearly broken through during the siege of Ironhold.
"The Abyssal Prime," Vasrick breathed. "They've established direct communion."
Two cultists in elaborate robes stepped from the shadows, their eyes blazing with purple fire. Behind them, three more figures emerged— Awakened-class soldiers, their spiritual energy stretched thin but lethal.
"Kael Ashborn," the taller cultist intoned. "The Soul Reaver. We have been waiting."
Vasrick moved to engage, but Kael grabbed her arm. "Wait. These aren't standard cultists."
"No," the tall cultist agreed, his smile revealing teeth filed to points. "We are the Keepers of the Unsealing. The Shadow Court's... Estranged children." His eyes glittered with something ancient and alien. "You carry a gift that was never meant for mortal hands. The First Soul Reaver tried to control the Abyss. He failed. You will fail as well."
Kael's hand tightened on his blade. "The First Soul Reaver?"
"The one who created your precious weapon. The one whose tomb lies hidden beneath the bones of a thousand failed attempts." The cultist's smile widened. "But that is a story for another time. Tonight, we take what is owed."
They attacked as one.
Kael moved like water, his blade a silver arc through the darkness. The first cultist fell, then the second. But the Awakened soldiers were faster, stronger, their techniques refined through years of combat training that matched his own.
Vasrick engaged the tall cultist, her spiritual energy flaring bright as they traded blows that shook dust from the ceiling. Kael found himself facing all three Awakened, his blade arm already aching from the sustained fighting.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 89 units of spiritual essence from the remains. Technique acquired: Void Step (requires high-stage compatibility).]
The notification pulsed as another cultist fell to Sera's blades above. But the three soldiers pressed forward, their coordination perfect, their movements synchronized by techniques Kael didn't recognize.
"You're not Order trained," he said, deflecting a thrust that would have opened his throat. "Where did you learn to fight like this?"
One of the soldiers answered by driving a knee toward his midsection. Kael twisted, taking the blow on his hip instead of his stomach, and responded with an elbow strike that shattered the man's nose. Blood sprayed, but the soldier barely flinched.
"Shadow Court technique," Kael realized. "But modified. Improved." His mind worked furiously. The Shadow Court was watching him. Had been watching him for months. This wasn't just surveillance—it was evaluation.
A blast of spiritual energy slammed into his back, sending him sprawling across the stone floor. Pain exploded through his nervous system as corrupted energy tried to burrow into his soul.
[Soul Reaver Protocol Activated: Corruption detected. Purge initiated. Cost: 12% harvested essence.]
The corruption burned away, replaced by a hollow cold that left Kael gasping. He rolled, barely avoiding a descending blade, and kicked his attacker's legs out from under him.
Vasrick screamed in fury somewhere to his left, her blade locked with the tall cultist's. But Kael could see the strain on her face—her spiritual reserves were depleting faster than she could regenerate.
The device pulsed again, brighter this time. The presence within grew stronger.
*Give us your soul, Reaver. It would be a mercy compared to what comes next.*
Kael understood then. This wasn't just a staging ground or a research facility. This was a ritual site, a place where the cults had established a direct connection to the Abyss itself. And they had summoned something through it.
The basement floor began to crack. Purple light erupted from the fissures, and through the gaps, Kael glimpsed something moving—vast, formless, hungry.
"No," he said, pushing himself to his feet. "Not today."
He charged.
The three Awakened soldiers tried to intercept, but Kael was faster now, his movements guided by instincts honed through countless harvests and a survival drive that bordered on the pathological. He ducked under one blade, shouldered through another, and buried his steel in the third's throat.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 156 units of spiritual essence from the remains. Technique acquired: Shadow Court Blade Dance III. Memory fragment acquired: Combat protocols, unknown origin.]
Information flooded his mind—tactical patterns, spiritual manipulation techniques, and fragments of memory that didn't belong to him. A training facility in the mountains. Children raised to be weapons. A master who wore masks and spoke of "necessary sacrifices."
The Shadow Court had created child soldiers. And these three had been among them.
Kael pushed the knowledge aside. Later. There would be time for horror later.
The tall cultist turned as Kael approached, his face twisted with surprise. "Impossible. You're Sovereign-stage. You shouldn't be able to—"
Kael's blade took his head before he finished the sentence.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 201 units of spiritual essence from the remains. Technique acquired: Abyssal Resistance II. Memory fragment acquired: Ritual parameters, partial.]
The device screamed—a sound that existed somewhere between mechanical and organic. Kael didn't hesitate. He reached for the brass and bone apparatus, his hands pressing against its pulsing surface.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 74 units of spiritual essence from the remains. Abyssal connection disrupted. Residual energy acquired.]
The presence in the Abyss recoiled. For a moment, Kael felt its attention lock onto him with an intensity that made his soul ache. Then it was gone, the connection severed, the ritual collapsed.
The purple light died. The cracks in the floor sealed themselves. Silence fell like a shroud.
Vasrick limped toward him, clutching her side. Blood soaked through her fingers, but her eyes were alive with fierce determination. "Kael. Did you—"
"Disrupted it." He surveyed the destruction around them. "The device is dead. The connection is severed. For now."
Serra appeared at the top of the stairs, her pale face even paler than usual. "We need to move. Fast. Whatever that was, it won't stay quiet forever. And the spiritual backlash..." She pointed upward. "The whole district just felt that."
Kael nodded, already calculating. "Vasrick, can you walk?"
"I'll manage." She looked at him with something between respect and unease. "That thing in the device. You stopped it. How?"
"I don't know," Kael admitted. And he didn't—not fully. The Soul Reaver's abilities had reacted instinctively, reaching into the ritual and siphoning the energy that powered it. But the deeper question remained: why had the Abyss responded to him? Why had it called him "Son of Ash"?
What had the First Soul Reaver done that still echoed through the ages?
The questions would have to wait. Right now, they needed to extract before whatever attention they'd attracted arrived.

The safehouse was chaos and relief in equal measure. Vasrick's wounds were serious but not fatal—Sera's healing techniques could only do so much, and the Order's medical supplies were rationed to the point of cruelty. The two wounded soldiers had stabilized. The civilians they'd rescued were being processed by Dawn representatives, their traumatized faces a reminder that the war touched everyone.
Kael sat in the corner, his mind churning through the night's events. The Keepers of the Unsealing. Shadow Court child soldiers. A ritual device connected directly to the Abyss. And that voice—the Prime Entity that had spoken to him as if it knew him personally.
"Soul Reaver," Sera said, settling beside him. "You look like someone who's seen a ghost."
"Something like that." He didn't elaborate. Sera was Dawn, and while she hadn't shown herself to be an enemy, the information he'd absorbed tonight was too dangerous to share casually.
"The device you destroyed," she continued, her voice dropping. "I've never seen anything like it. The energy signatures were... Wrong. Like they were pulling from somewhere outside normal spiritual circulation."
"The Abyss," Kael said quietly.

"I've read the basics." He chose his words carefully. "The Veil. The seal network. The Entities."

Kael absorbed that. The Six Orders—the Dawn, the Iron Vanguard, the Arcane Spire, the Mortician's Guild, the Veiled Watch, and the Shadow Court—all bound by the Concordat, all supposedly fighting to maintain the seal network. But the cults were multiplying, the attacks were coordinated, and now direct communion with Abyssal Primes was possible.
Something had changed. Something fundamental.
"The Keepers of the Unsealing," he said. "You heard them. They're Shadow Court's estranged children."

"They're not the leadership. They're the weapons. The ones who were created to be used and discarded." Her eyes met his. "Like you, maybe. The Soul Reaver abilities didn't come from nowhere. Someone made them. Someone trained you. And I don't think it was the Orders."
The words hung in the air between them. Kael thought about the fragments of memory he'd harvested—the training facilities, the masked masters, the children raised to kill. Shadow Court techniques, modified and improved. And the voice in the device, calling him "Son of Ash."
He thought about the First Soul Reaver. The one whose tomb was hidden. The one who had tried to control the Abyss and failed.
What if the Soul Reaver ability wasn't just a power? What if it was a legacy?
"Commander Vasrick," a voice called from across the room. "The Dawn's messenger has arrived. They're requesting a full debrief."
Vasrick, pale but standing, nodded. "Tell them we're coming. Sera, you're with me. Kael—" She paused, something calculating in her gaze. "Stay here. Rest. You've done enough tonight."
It wasn't a suggestion. It was an order from a woman who understood that what Kael had done in that basement would raise questions the Orders weren't ready to answer.
He nodded. "I'll be here."

The safehouse grew quiet as the others left. Kael sat alone in the darkness, his mind cataloging the night's acquisitions. Techniques and fragments, spiritual essence and corrupted memories—all of it pieces to a puzzle he couldn't yet see.
The Keepers had mentioned the First Soul Reaver's tomb. Had mentioned that Kael's abilities came from that source. But the location was hidden, protected by "the bones of a thousand failed attempts." Whatever secrets lay buried there, they weren't accessible to him now.
The cult's mass unsealing timeline. The Keepers had been conducting rituals, establishing connections, building toward something. But the specific timeline—the when and how—remained beyond his grasp.
And yet the signs were everywhere. Coordinated attacks across multiple cities. Direct communion with Abyssal Primes. Children raised as weapons. The Shadow Court's silence.
The war wasn't just escalating. It was changing.
Kael reached for the cold presence at the edge of his awareness—the Soul Reaver ability that had saved his life more times than he could count. It responded to his touch like an old friend, familiar and strange in equal measure. Zero spiritual cost. Harvesting from corpses. Notifications in his mind that tracked his gains with mechanical precision.
A gift, the Keepers had called it. Or a curse.
Outside, the first light of dawn crept over Thornwick's battered skyline. Somewhere in the city, cultists were regrouping, planning their next attack. Somewhere, the Shadow Court was watching, evaluating, waiting. And somewhere in the depths of the Abyss, a Prime Entity had taken notice of a Sovereign-stage Reaver who had disrupted its ritual and walked away.
Kael closed his eyes and let exhaustion take him. Tomorrow, the war would continue. Tomorrow, he would lead more missions into hostile territory, harvest more abilities from the fallen, and dig deeper into mysteries that might destroy him.
But tonight, he had survived. And for now, that was enough.

A sound.
Kael's eyes snapped open. His hand found his blade before conscious thought caught up—instinct honed by months of sleeping in hostile territory.
Silence. Just the settling of old wood, the distant rumble of the city's morning traffic.
Then, from somewhere outside, a single footstep on gravel. Followed by another. Deliberate. Unhurried. The gait of someone who knew exactly where they were going and didn't care if they were heard.
Kael rose, moving to the window. The street below was empty except for the grey light of dawn. But he could sense something—a presence that didn't belong. Spiritual energy, faint but unmistakable, wrapped around itself like a cloak.
Watching.
Waiting.
The figure emerged from the shadows between two buildings. A woman, tall and angular, dressed in clothes that seemed to absorb the light around them. Her face was hidden by a hood, but her posture radiated confidence that bordered on arrogance.
She looked up at the window where Kael stood.
And smiled.
Then she turned and walked away, disappearing into the morning mist as if she had never existed at all.
Kael's fingers tightened on the windowsill. The spiritual signature she'd carried—it was familiar. Shadow Court, but different. Refined. And something else, something that tugged at the edges of the memories he'd harvested.
The Keepers of the Unsealing. The estranged children of the Shadow Court.
They weren't done with him yet.
The war had just found a new front.
