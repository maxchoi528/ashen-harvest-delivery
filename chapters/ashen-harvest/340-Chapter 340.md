The ground split open three streets from the Ashborn tenement.
Kael felt it before he saw it—a wrongness in the air, a vibration that bypassed his ears and settled directly into his bones. Steam hissed from ruptured pipes. Glass shattered somewhere above. A woman's scream cut short, replaced by a sound like tearing silk.
He was moving before conscious thought caught up, boots pounding against cobblestones slick with condensation. The gas lamps flickered, their flames bending toward something invisible, something pulling.
The rift manifested as a wound in reality—a vertical tear filled with colors that human eyes refused to process. No light escaped it. The darkness ended, replaced by a deeper darkness that breathed.
Fourteen cultists knelt in a circle around the rift's edge, their robes marked with sigils that writhed like living things. At the center of their formation stood a figure in bone-white armor, arms raised toward the tear.
"Back!" Kael's voice tore from his throat. "Clear the area now!"
None of the cultists turned. They kept their chanting, a droning ululation that set his teeth on edge. The rift pulsed, and the wound widened by another inch.
Kael counted three heartbeats. The bone-armored figure still hadn't moved. The cultists still chanted. The rift still grew.
He didn't wait for them to notice him.
His blade sang free of its sheath as he closed the distance, weaving between bodies that shifted just enough to avoid his strikes. The first cultist died with a wet gurgle. The second twisted away, her eyes—those eyes were wrong, pupils like burning holes—meeting his for a single instant before his steel found her throat.
Two cultists broke from the circle, hands raised, fingers crackling with sickly green light. Abyssal energy, pure and corrupted. Kael ducked under the first blast, felt heat sear across his scalp. He rolled, came up swinging, opened the second cultist from hip to shoulder.
The chanting faltered. Three more cultists abandoned their positions, surrounding him in a loose semicircle. Their lips moved in words that hurt to hear, syllables that belonged to no human language.
"Stop the ritual!" Kael demanded, positioning himself between the rift and a cluster of fleeing civilians. "Whatever you're doing, stop it now!"
The bone-armored figure finally turned.
Beneath the helm, Kael glimpsed a face that was more scar than skin, features melted and reformed into something barely human. The voice that emerged sounded like stones grinding together.
"The seal fractures. The return begins." The figure gestured toward the pulsing rift. "You cannot stop what was always meant to be."
"Watch me."
Kael lunged.
The bone-armored cultist moved faster than anything human had a right to move, intercepting his strike with a forearm guard that rang like a struck bell. The impact sent shockwaves up Kael's arm. He disengaged, spun, slashed at the figure's exposed side.
Steel scraped against bone.
The cultist caught his blade with bare fingers, blood welling from the grip but showing no pain. "The Veil thins. The Sleepers wake. Your kind has held us back for millennia." Those wrong-eyed pupils fixed on him. "No longer."
A pulse of energy exploded outward, throwing Kael backward into a merchant's cart. Wood splintered. His shoulder screamed. He tasted copper.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION] You have sustained injuries. Current status: Left shoulder contusion, minor lacerations. Spiritual reserves: Unaffected (Soul Reaver ability has zero cost).
Kael hauled himself upright as the remaining cultists completed their reformation around the rift. The wound in reality had grown to the size of a doorway, and something was moving within—a shape that defied perspective, limbs that bent in directions that hurt to track.
"Retreat!" The bone-armored figure's command echoed across the street. "The Vessel must not fall! Fall back!"
The cultists scattered into the fog-choked alleys, their robes blending with the shadows. Kael started after them, then stopped.
The rift pulsed again.
A hand emerged—four-fingered, jointed backward, skin like wet leather. It gripped the edge of the tear and pulled, widening the opening by another foot.
Kael's blood ran cold.
The entity that clawed its way through the rift stood seven feet tall, its body a mockery of human proportion. Six arms sprouted from its torso, each ending in fingers that dripped with something luminescent and foul. Its face—if it could be called a face—consisted of a mass of writhing tendrils where features should be.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION] Entity classification: Abyssal Scout. Threat level: Sovereign-stage equivalent. Recommendation: Do not engage directly.
"Beautiful, isn't it?"
Kael spun, blade raised.
A woman stood in the mouth of an alley, her posture casual despite the apocalypse unfolding behind him. She wore the practical leathers of someone accustomed to violence, her dark hair cropped short. A sigil marked her collar—the sun-in-triangle of the Order of Dawn.
"Order of Dawn?" Kael kept his blade ready. "Where have you been? This started ten minutes ago!"
"Longer than that." The woman stepped forward, her eyes never leaving the entity that was still pulling itself through the rift. "The cultists have been working toward this moment for months. We intercepted their communications three weeks ago. This—" she gestured at the rift, "—was anticipated."
"Anticipated." Kael's grip tightened. "And you let it happen?"
"We let them believe they had the element of surprise." Her voice carried the weariness of someone who had repeated this explanation too many times. "Better to catch them mid-ritual than to scatter them and hunt down every cell. The Orders have been playing defense for too long. Sometimes you need to let the enemy commit before you strike."
"While civilians die?"
"Fewer civilians die this way. Total war against the cults would be bloodier." She met his gaze, her expression unreadable. "You fought well. Better than most Awakened I've seen at your stage. Who trained you?"
"No one." Kael turned back to the rift. The entity had fully emerged now, its six-armed form looming against the gaslight. "I need to know what's happening. All of it."
The woman studied him for a long moment. "The Sealed Rifts. You know about them?"
"Seventy-two tears in reality, each containing an Abyssal Entity. The Orders maintain seals that keep them contained." Kael recited the basics he'd learned from stolen documents and whispered rumors. "The seals are weakening."
"They're not just weakening." She moved to stand beside him, both of them watching the entity orient itself toward the street where civilians still fled. "They're being systematically sabotaged. The cults have found a way to accelerate the degradation. This rift—" she pointed at the tear, which had stabilized but continued to pulse with that wrongness, "—was sealed at the end of the Abyssal Wars. It should have lasted another three centuries. It's been open for six hours and it's already at forty percent manifestation."
"What happens when it reaches full manifestation?"
"The entity fully crosses over. Once that happens, the seal can't be restored. The rift becomes permanent. Another wound in the Veil that we can't close."
The entity moved, its six arms sweeping through the air. A building's corner came apart as if struck by an invisible hammer. People screamed in the distance.
"Can you kill it?"
"Kill it?" She laughed, though there was no humor in it. "That thing is Sovereign-stage equivalent. The only reason it hasn't wiped out this entire district is because it's still partially—still transitioning between realms. In another hour, it will be fully manifested. Then it will be beyond any single Awakened."
"Two Awakened?"
"Illustrator Vance, at your service." She inclined her head slightly. "Sovereign-stage, Order of Dawn. And no, even together we can't kill that thing. Not without a full warband and specialized binding equipment."
Kael watched the entity tear through another building. "Then what do we do?"
"We contain it until reinforcements arrive. Three hours, maybe four." She drew her own blade—a shorter weapon, curved for thrust work. "Stay on its left flank. It favors that side. I'll keep its attention. You protect the evacuation routes."
"That's not fighting. That's babysitting."
"That's survival." She met his eyes. "Welcome to the war, kid. It's not about glory. It's about buying time until someone with more power shows up."
Kael wanted to argue. Every instinct screamed at him to charge, to prove that he could handle what others couldn't. But he had seen what happened when pride met reality. The entity was beyond him—for now.
"Fine." He repositioned himself toward the main street. "But I'm not running away if there's an opening."
"We never run." Illustrator Vance smiled grimly. "We retreat tactically to regroup for a more advantageous engagement."
"That's running."
"That's strategy."
The entity roared—a sound that existed more in the spiritual spectrum than the audible—and charged toward a cluster of fleeing civilians. Illustrator Vance sprinted to intercept, her blade blazing with golden light.
Kael moved in the opposite direction, herding panicked citizens away from the kill zone. The entity's attention fixed on Vance like a predator tracking movement. It swung two arms at her simultaneously. She ducked under one strike, deflected the other with a burst of spiritual energy that cracked the cobblestones.
For twenty minutes, Kael watched Illustrator Vance dance around the entity, never engaging directly, always staying just out of reach. The technique was masterful—a fighter's ballet performed against something that could kill her with a careless swipe.
But she couldn't hold it forever.
"Help is coming!" she shouted over the entity's bellowing. "Thirty minutes!"
The entity's tendril-face twisted. It had learned her pattern. When she dodged left, two arms swept right. When she retreated, it advanced faster.
Kael saw the moment she would be hit.
He didn't think.
He moved.
His blade caught the entity's strike, absorbing enough force to shatter his wrist and most of his arm. Pain exploded up his limb. He felt bones splinter. But the blow that would have taken Vance's head instead glanced off his steel.
"What are you—" Vance stared at him.
"Get up." Kael's voice came out strangled. "I can't do this twice."
The entity recovered, its attention shifting to this new threat. Kael stood between it and the woman, his broken arm hanging useless at his side, his good hand white-knuckled around his blade.
The entity swung.
Kael dropped, rolled, came up inside its guard. His blade found one of its many joints, driving deep. Corruption spurted, thick and black. The entity shrieked, all six arms reaching for him.
He retreated, leading with his blade, buying seconds with blood and luck.
[Kael! Kael, can you hear me?] The voice exploded in his mind like a thunderclap. [I need you to listen. The entity is still partially. Its form is maintained by the rift's energy. Sever the connection and it will destabilize.]
"Zara?" Kael gasped the name aloud.
[Focus! Your Soul Reaver ability can disrupt spiritual energy. The entity's connection to the rift is spiritual. You need to touch it and activate your harvest.]
"I can't touch it! I'd be killed before I got close!"
[You don't need to touch the rift. Touch the entity. Use the connection it already has. Your ability will do the rest.]
The entity loomed over him, its tendril-face inches from his own. Kael could smell it—ozone and rot and something older, something that had no name in human language.
He raised his good hand.
The entity's arm descended.
Kael met the blow with his blade, felt his steel buckle, felt his arm shatter in three more places. But he didn't die. Because in that instant of contact, his palm pressed against the entity's wet-leather skin, and he activated the ability that had made him what he was.
The Soul Reaver awakened.
Power flooded through him—not his own, drawn from something vast and hungry and barely contained. The entity convulsed, its form flickering between solid and translucent. The rift behind it pulsed erratically, energy flowing backward into the tear.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Spiritual Anchoring from the remains. Entity connection to rift weakened.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 40% Abyssal Essence from the remains. Spiritual reserves significantly increased.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Fragment of the Void from the remains. New ability acquired.]
The entity screamed. Its form began to dissolve, pulled back toward the rift by the severed connection. Illustrator Vance's voice cut through the chaos: "What did you do?"
"Giving it a refund." Kael watched the creature disintegrate, its substance streaming back into the wound in reality. The rift convulsed, the edges collapsing inward. In seconds, nothing remained but a flickering scar in the air.
Kael's legs gave out. He collapsed onto the cracked cobblestones, his arm a ruin, his body wracked with aftershocks that felt like lightning running through his veins.
Vance crouched beside him, her expression a mixture of awe and concern. "That was Soul Reaver technique. I've only seen it described in old texts."
"Got it from a friend." Kael's voice came out ragged. "The, uh, the entity is gone?"
"Gone and the rift is resealing." She examined his arm, her touch gentle despite the calluses. "You need a healer. Now. This is beyond field treatment."
"Save the civilians first."
"The evacuation is complete. You bought the time we needed." She looked at him with those assessing eyes. "Where did you learn to fight like that? Your technique is crude but effective. Self-taught?"
"Something like that."
"There's a war coming." She said it matter-of-factly, as if discussing the weather. "The cults aren't just opening rifts randomly. They're targeting specific locations, weakening specific seals. Someone is coordinating them."
"Who?"
"We don't know. But whoever it is has access to information that should be impossible. Seal locations. Weakness patterns. Order movements." Vance's jaw tightened. "We think there's a traitor. Someone inside the Orders feeding information to the cults."
Kael thought of the documents he'd stolen, the fragments he'd pieced together. "The Shadow Court?"
"You know about the Shadow Court?" Her eyes sharpened.
"Everyone knows about the Shadow Court. Deniable operations arm for Orders that want to violate the Concordat." Kael forced himself to sit up, ignoring the protest of every nerve. "If someone is coordinating the cults, it would take resources beyond what any single organization could muster. Resources only the Orders have."
"Correct." Vance stood, offering him a hand up. "The Shadow Court is our primary suspect. But we can't investigate openly without exposing our own people who are feeding us information from inside."
"What do you need?"
She studied him for a long moment. "Right now? I need you to get that arm treated and stay alive. The fact that you survived a Soul Reaver activation against a Sovereign-equivalent entity is remarkable. Most Awakened would have been burned out from the inside."
"What about the other rifts? The other entities?"
"Each one will need to be handled similarly. The Orders are spreading thin, but we'll adapt." She paused. "You have a choice to make. The Order of Dawn could use someone with your abilities. We could train you properly, give you resources, connect you with others who fight this war."
"I'm not joining the Orders."
"Not even to save lives you'd otherwise lose?"
Kael thought of the bone-armored cultist, of the coordinated attack, of the coming war. He thought of Zarek, somewhere in the shadows, pulling strings. He thought of the Sepulcher tablets, the truth about his power, the enemy he didn't yet understand.
"I'm not joining the Orders," he repeated. "But I'll fight beside them when our goals align."
"Acceptable." Vance nodded. "For now. But the war will demand more than neutrality eventually. People will need to choose sides."
"Then I'll choose the side that lets me protect the people who can't protect themselves." Kael managed to stand, leaning heavily against a lamp post. "That's my line. Not yours. Not the Orders'. Mine."
"I can work with that." She smiled, just slightly. "Rest. Heal. When you're ready, find me at the Chapel of the First Light. Thornwick's eastern district, near the old market."
She turned to leave, then paused. "The entity you killed. The Soul Reaver technique. Where did you learn it?"
"Still figuring that out myself."
"Find out. The ability to sever an entity's connection to the Abyss could change everything. The Orders have spent centuries trying to develop techniques like that. If you can master it..."
"What?"
"Nothing. Just rest." She disappeared into the fog.
Kael stood alone in the ruined street, surrounded by the debris of a battle he shouldn't have survived. His arm throbbed with each heartbeat, a constant reminder of the price of intervention. But beneath the pain, he felt something else—a new power, still unfamiliar, still waiting to be understood.
The Fragment of the Void pulsed in his awareness, a fragment of something vast and alien, waiting to be understood.
The war Vance had promised was already here. The cults were moving. The seals were failing. And somewhere in the shadows, Zarek was watching, waiting for his moment.
But Kael had survived execution. He had survived the Soul Reaver awakening. He had survived a Sovereign-equivalent entity.
He would survive this too.
He limped toward the medical ward, already calculating his next move. The Sepulcher tablets held answers he needed. The Shadow Court held threats he needed to understand. And somewhere in Thornwick, the cult that had killed so many was regrouping, planning, preparing for whatever came next.
The rift in the street had sealed itself, leaving only a faint scar in the air. In seventy-two other locations across the continent, similar rifts pulsed and threatened. The Abyssal Entities stirred in their prisons. The war had truly begun.
And Kael Ashborn, reluctant symbol, killer of the innocent and the damned alike, walked into the dawn with his enemies at his back and his purpose burning in his chest.
The footsteps behind him stopped.
Kael turned, hand dropping to his blade.
Zarek stood at the end of the alley, his robes pristine despite the chaos, his smile as empty as a hollow grave. The man who had framed him for murder. The architect of his execution. The shadow that had haunted every moment since his resurrection.
"Kael." The name fell like a stone into still water. "We need to talk."
"Talk?" Kael's voice came out raw, dangerous. "You murdered innocent people and blamed me for it. You had me executed. You—"
"I saved your life." Zarek stepped closer, his movements unhurried. "The execution was the only way to sever your connection to the Order of Dawn's tracking systems. The Soul Reaver ability needed to awaken without their interference. Everything I've done has been to prepare you for what's coming."
"I don't believe you."
"You don't have to believe me. You just have to understand what you did tonight." Zarek's gaze drifted to the sealed rift, to the subtle scar in the air that marked where reality had been torn open. "That entity was the first of many. The cults have been working toward this moment for decades. The seals are failing faster than the Orders can repair them. Within a year, maybe two, there will be so many rifts open that the Veil itself will collapse."
"And you want me to stop it?"
"I want you to understand what's at stake." Zarek reached into his robes and withdrew a small object—a tablet, carved with symbols that made Kael's eyes water. "The Sepulcher tablets. The original Soul Reaver's journal. Everything you've been searching for is contained in these pages."
Kael stared at the tablet. His fingers twitched toward it, then stopped.
"Why?"
"Because the original Soul Reaver died trying to stop what the cults are now attempting. And because you're the only one who can finish what he started." Zarek extended the tablet. "Unless you prefer to keep stumbling in the dark while the world burns around you."
The tablet gleamed in the early light. Kael's mind raced, calculating risks and rewards, truths and lies. Every instinct screamed that this was a trap. But the information in that tablet could change everything.
Zarek smiled, that empty smile that revealed nothing.
Behind Kael, the sealed rift pulsed once more—a final warning, a reminder that the war had only begun.
