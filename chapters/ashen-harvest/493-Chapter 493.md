The cultist's head twisted backward at an impossible angle, vertebrae grinding wetly before the body collapsed at Kael's feet.
Kael didn't flinch. He'd learned that much.
"Three more," he muttered, wiping black ichor from his blade. The alley stank of copper and something older—brine and rot that made his eyes water. Thornwick's underbelly never failed to deliver.
Two cultists came at him from the left, robes torn, carrying curved knives that pulsed with sickly violet light. Entity-touched weapons. Rare. Expensive. Worth killing for.
He moved before they finished their battle cry, his body flowing through motions he'd stolen from a dozen dead warriors. The first cultist fell with a knife in his throat. The second managed to slash Kael's arm before a palm strike caved in his sternum.
[System Notification: Soul Harvest. You extracted residual soul-energy (Entity-touched) from the remains. Spiritual essence (+12) acquired. Corpse freshness: a few hours. Harvest quality: High.]
The notification pulsed behind his eyes, pleasant and cold. That was the thing about being Divine—no spiritual cost meant he could harvest every corpse he passed without consequence. The Law of Soul Conservation didn't apply to Soul Reavers. His soul didn't deplete.
It was why the Orders feared him.
The fourth cultist tried to run. Kael caught him by the collar and slammed him into the alley wall hard enough to crack the brickwork.
"Prime Zarek," Kael said, not a question. "Where?"
The cultist's eyes went wide with terror. His gaze dropped to Kael's chest—to the faint black lines that traced visible beneath his shirt, pulsing with each heartbeat. Soul Reaver marks. Impossible to hide at his stage.
"You're him," the cultist whispered. "The one they talk about. The dead man walking."
Kael applied pressure to the man's windpipe. "Zarek. Now."
"He's not—I don't know where he—" The cultist's hand shot into his robe. Kael crushed his wrist before he could draw whatever talisman waited there. The man screamed.
"The Shadow Court is watching," he gasped. "They'll—"
Kael dropped him. The cultist crumpled, cradling his shattered wrist, and Kael stepped over his body to reach the fifth—the one who'd been running toward the street. The one who'd almost made it.
This one was already dead, throat opened by the same violet blade that marked him as one of their own. Someone inside the cult didn't want witnesses talking.
"Cleanup crews," Kael muttered. He crouched beside the body and pressed his palm flat against the cooling chest.
[System Notification: Soul Harvest. You extracted skill fragment (Shadow Step) from the remains. Memory fragment acquired. Emotional residue: fear, fanatical devotion, last sensation—betrayal. Corpse freshness: several minutes. Harvest quality: Exceptional.]
The skill slotted into Kael's mind like a key finding its lock. Shadow Step. Short-range teleport through spiritual shadows. Useful. Rare. Worth more than most Awakened would see in a lifetime.
The memory fragment was better.
Images flooded his consciousness—a hooded figure standing before a massive map of Thornwick, red markers clustered in specific districts. The Whisper King's symbol carved into a stone altar. A voice speaking words that made reality shiver: *"The seals are weakening faster than projected. We have six months. Maybe less."*
And then—Zarek's face, twisted with something between hatred and desperation. *"He thinks he can control this. The fool. The old fool. When the Rift opens, no one will be safe. Not even him."*
Kael released the corpse and stood.
Six months. Maybe less.
That was the first concrete timeline he'd heard since his resurrection. The Great Rift—the largest of the 72 sealed rifts—was failing. The seal network that kept the Abyss contained, that maintained the Veil between worlds, was crumbling faster than anyone in power wanted to admit.
He needed to find Zarek. The cult leader knew more than anyone else outside the Orders—and unlike the Orders, Zarek wasn't bound by politics or the Concordat. Zarek would tell him exactly what was happening.
If Kael could make him talk.
---
The Veiled Market operated out of a converted warehouse near the docks, accessible only through a series of coded doors and past guards who were all Awakened and all mercenary. No questions. No loyalty. Just coin.
Kael had been here twice before. Both times he'd left with new skills and new enemies. The third time might be his last.
The doorkeeper, a scarred woman with one clouded eye, recognized him immediately. Her hand moved to the blade at her hip.
"Ashborn." Her voice carried grudging respect and open fear. "Thought the Orders got you."
"They tried."
She studied him for a long moment, gaze tracing the black lines visible on his throat. Soul Reaver marks. The kind that made even hardened killers nervous.
"What do you want?"
"Information. Zarek's location. The Veiled Market has to know something."
She laughed, short and humorless. "The Shadow Court's attack dog? We're not stupid enough to—"
Kael placed his palm on the doorframe. A thin layer of frost spread from his fingers, crystallizing the wood. His stage. His power. All of it radiating pure threat.
"I'm not asking again."
The doorkeeper's remaining eye widened. She knew what he was capable of. What a Divine-stage Soul Reaver could do to a building full of people if he wanted.
"Back room," she said quickly. "Vex handles cult intel. But if you're going after Zarek, you're either very brave or very stupid."
"Which one keeps me alive longer?"
"We'll find out." She stepped aside. "Vex charges triple for Soul Reaver consultations. He doesn't like your kind."
"Neither does anyone else."
The back room smelled of pipe smoke and old leather. Vex was a thin man with silver hair and fingers stained permanently black from handling soul-essence vials. He'd been a fence, a broker, and reportedly an assassin before that. Now he sat behind a desk covered in papers and maps, looking up at Kael with the calm disinterest of someone who'd survived by never showing fear.
"Ashborn." Vex gestured to the chair across from him. "Sit. You want Zarek."
"I want to know what Zarek knows."
"Same thing, different words." Vex pulled a leather case from his desk, opened it, and extracted a small vial filled with swirling silver light. Soul-essence. High grade. Enough to fuel an Ascendant's advancement for months. "This is what information costs. Advance payment."
Kael didn't blink. "That's robbery."
"That's the market rate for someone who wants to die." Vex set the vial between them. "Zarek has been moving for three weeks. The cultists are organizing—really organizing, not the usual rabble-rousing. Real cells. Real training. Real supplies."
"The Shadow Court?"
"Nowhere near as organized as the cult." Vex tapped the desk. "That's what scares me. The Shadow Court plays politics. They don't go all-in on anything. But these cultists—they're preparing for something. And Zarek is at the center of it."
Kael pulled a vial from his own pocket—one he'd harvested from a dead Awakened two days ago. High-grade, Entity-touched. Worth twice Vex's asking price.
"This for everything you have. Location. Guards. Abilities. Weaknesses."
Vex's eyes glittered. Greed and calculation warred behind his expression. "You want to kill him."
"I want to talk to him."
"No one talks to Zarek. Not alive." But his hand was already reaching for the vial. "There's a church. Old. Abandoned since the Second Abyssal War. It's been converted into a temple of something that shouldn't exist. Forty cultists, minimum. Three Awakened lieutenants. And Zarek himself—he's Ascendant, but rumors say he's been touched by something from beyond the Veil. That he can draw power directly from the rifts."
"That's impossible."
"So is being resurrected. So is harvesting souls. So is everything you do." Vex leaned back. "The world stopped making sense two hundred years ago. We just stopped pretending otherwise."
Kael slid the vial across the desk. Vex palmed it with practiced speed.
"One more thing." Vex hesitated, and for the first time, genuine uncertainty crossed his face. "The Whisper King. You know the name?"
"I've heard it."
"It's not a person. It's a title. Seven hundred years old. Passed down through whoever controls the cult's inner circle." He paused. "Current holder has been in position for two centuries. No one knows who they are. No one knows where they came from. But they orchestrated the Shadow Court's creation. They brokered the Concordat's first fractures. They've been playing every Order against each other since before your grandfather was born."
"And Zarek?"
"Zarek is a true believer. He thinks he's serving something greater." Vex smiled thinly. "He's not. He's a pawn being moved across a board he can't see. Just like everyone else in this city."
Kael stood. "The church. Where?"
"Merchant Quarter. The old Cathedral of the Ashen Sun. Take the eastern entrance. The cult uses the front, but there's a crypt beneath that's been expanded. Zarek lives underground now. Doesn't come up during daylight."
"Why tell me this? If the Shadow Court is watching, they won't like what you're doing."
Vex laughed again. "Because I'm not the Shadow Court. I'm not affiliated with anyone except coin. And because—" He paused, choosing his words carefully. "Because whatever happens with those rifts, I want to be on the side that survives. You're Divine-stage, Ashborn. Soul Reaver. You're not supposed to exist anymore, but here you are. Maybe you're the variable no one's accounted for."
"Or maybe I'm just another pawn."
"Everyone's a pawn until they're not."

The Cathedral of the Ashen Sun hadn't seen services in two centuries. Its stained glass windows were cracked and dark, its stone walls scarred by old battles, its bell tower a nesting ground for crows. But tonight, lights burned in its depths. Candles. Torches. Something that pulsed with sickly violet energy.
Kael entered through the eastern crypt, just as Vex instructed. The passage was narrow, damp, and lined with niches that once held the bones of honored dead. Now the niches were empty, and the walls were marked with symbols that made his skin crawl.
Entity-script. The language of the Abyss.
He descended three flights of stairs before reaching the converted temple. A vast underground chamber, lit by floating orbs of violet flame. Rows of stone benches faced an altar carved from a single piece of obsidian. Behind the altar, a massive seal had been painted on the wall—identical to the markings Kael had seen on cultists' weapons.
The seal of the Great Rift.
Forty cultists knelt in prayer, their voices a low murmur that vibrated in Kael's chest. Three figures stood apart from the congregation, robed in black, their hands crackling with power. The Awakened lieutenants. All Ascendant. All dangerous.
And there, at the altar's base, a man Kael had never met but recognized immediately.
Zarek.
He was tall. Gaunt. His robes hung on him like a shroud, and his face was a mask of cold certainty. But it was his eyes that held Kael's attention. They weren't entirely human anymore. Something moved in their depths. Something that watched from beyond the Veil.
"So." Zarek's voice cut through the prayer-murmur like a blade. "The dead man returns."
The cultists didn't move. Didn't turn. They remained kneeling, as if programmed to ignore intruders.
"You knew I was coming," Kael said.
"I knew someone would come. The Shadow Court has been watching you for weeks. They know about your little visits to the Veiled Market. Your conversations with Vex." Zarek spread his hands. "Did you think you were being subtle? A Divine-stage Soul Reaver, resurrecting in a world that burned your soul at the stake? Every Order in Thornwick has a file on you. Every cult. Every power broker who matters."
"Then why let me get this far?"
"Because I wanted to meet you." Zarek descended the altar steps, his lieutenants flanking him. "Soul Reaver. The only human ability that can harvest directly from the Abyss without destroying the harvester. Do you know how rare that is? How valuable?"
"I've heard the lectures."
"Then you know the Orders consider you an abomination. The Shadow Court considers you a weapon. The cult—" Zarek smiled, and there was nothing human in it. "The cult considers you a god."
Kael didn't move. "I'm not interested in worship."
"No. You're interested in answers. I can see it in you—the hunger. You want to understand what's happening. Why the seals are failing. Why reality is breaking down. Why you were brought back from the dead." Zarek stopped ten feet away, close enough to strike, far enough to talk. "I can give you those answers."
"In exchange for what?"
"Your service. Your abilities. Your willingness to do what the Orders never could—harvest directly from the Entities themselves." Zarek's eyes pulsed with violet light. "The seals are failing. We've known for decades. The Orders pretend otherwise, but they know too. And when the Great Rift opens—when the Whisper King's gambit reaches its end—we'll need someone who can take from the Abyss without being consumed by it."
"The Whisper King." Kael stepped forward. "Who is he?"
"Another pawn. Like you. Like me." Zarek's smile faded. "The only difference is that his pawns are centuries old. His plans span generations. And his endgame is nothing less than the complete dissolution of the Veil."
The words hung in the air. Around them, the cultists continued their prayers, oblivious to the conversation unfolding at the altar's base.
"Why tell me this?"
"Because you're the only one who can stop it." Zarek reached into his robes and withdrew a small object—a sphere of crystal containing what appeared to be a frozen flame. "This is a fragment of Entity-essence. Harvested from a rift that opened three years ago. Stable. Contained. But alive."
Kael stared at the sphere. The flame inside seemed to move, reaching toward him, hungry and aware.
"Take it," Zarek said. "Use your Soul Reaver ability on it. See what happens. See if you can harvest something from a being that exists between life and death, between the Material and the Abyss. If you can—if you prove the theory correct—then you'll have the power to challenge the Whisper King. To harvest the Entities themselves and become something the world has never seen."
"And if I can't?"
"Then you'll be dead. But you'll be dead trying to save a world that burned you alive." Zarek extended the sphere. "The choice is yours, Kael Ashborn. It always has been."
Kael's hand hovered over the sphere. He could feel the Entity's presence—alien, vast, terrifyingly patient. It was like touching a void that thought.
He thought of Thornwick burning. Of the seals failing. Of the six months Vex had mentioned and the centuries the Whisper King had planned.
He thought of Lyra, wherever she was. Of Aldric and their unfinished business. Of the obligations still hanging over him—obligations he'd failed to resolve because he'd been too busy surviving.
And he thought of the dead cultist's memory. Zarek's face twisted with hatred and desperation. *"When the Rift opens, no one will be safe. Not even him."*
Not even Zarek. A true believer who'd sacrificed everything for a cause he didn't understand.
Kael took the sphere.
The Entity-essence was cold against his palm, colder than any natural ice. The flame inside pulsed once, twice, and then—
[System Notification: Soul Harvest ATTEMPT. Target: Entity-fragment (Class: Abyssal Remnant). Spiritual cost: ZERO. Corpse freshness: N/A. Harvest viability: UNKNOWN. Proceed? Y/N]
The notification appeared before him, and for the first time in his existence, Kael hesitated.
This wasn't harvesting a human. Wasn't taking from a corpse or a dying soul. This was reaching into the Abyss itself and pulling something back.
It was exactly what the cult wanted him to do.
It was exactly what he needed to do.
---
The three lieutenants moved before Kael could decide. Violet light erupted from their hands—attack spells, binding incantations, something that screamed like a dying star.
Kael dropped the sphere and drew his blade in one motion.
The first spell shattered against a barrier he'd erected without thinking—Divine-stage power flowing through channels he'd never known he had. The second and third spells hit simultaneously, and Kael staggered as something tried to tear his soul from his body.
Soul attacks. Designed specifically to counter Awakened. To kill anything with a spiritual presence.
The lieutenants hadn't come to watch. They'd come to test.
Kael answered with his own assault.
He moved through the temple like a ghost, his blade carving through defenses and flesh alike. The first lieutenant fell with a cut across his throat. The second lost both arms before Kael's palm struck his chest, and something dark and hungry ripped free from the man's core.
[System Notification: Soul Harvest. You extracted skill fragment (Void Step) from the remains. Spiritual essence (+35) acquired. Corpse freshness: Instantaneous. Harvest quality: Transcendent.]
The third lieutenant ran.
Kael let him go. Let him sprint up the crypt stairs and into the night, probably to warn whoever was waiting. Let him carry the story of what a Divine-stage Soul Reaver could do.
Behind him, Zarek hadn't moved. The cultists were screaming now, breaking their prayer-silence, pouring toward the exits in blind panic. But Zarek stood at the altar, watching Kael with those not--human eyes.
"You could have accepted," Zarek said quietly. "Taken the sphere. Walked away. Pretended none of this was your problem."
"And let the world burn?"
"The world burns regardless. The only question is who controls the ashes."
Kael sheathed his blade. The sphere of Entity-essence lay on the ground between them, still pulsing with that frozen flame. He could feel the Entity inside watching, waiting, curious about what he would do.
"The Whisper King," Kael said. "Where do I find him?"
Zarek laughed. "You think I'd tell you? After this?" He gestured at the bodies, the chaos, the shattered remains of his temple. "No. The Whisper King is beyond you, Ashborn. Beyond all of us. He's been playing this game for two hundred years. He knows every move. Every variable. Every—"
Kael's hand closed around Zarek's throat.
The cult leader didn't resist. His eyes went wide, then calm, then something else—something that looked like relief.
"Do it," Zarek whispered. "Harvest me. See what I know. See what I've seen." His lips twisted into a smile. "I'll give you everything. Freely. Because in the end, we're all just fuel for something greater."
[System Notification: Soul Harvest AVAILABLE. Target: Ascendant Awakened (Entity-touched). Corpse freshness: Living. WARNING: Harvesting from living subjects is HIGH RISK. Proceed? Y/N]
Kael's grip tightened.
He could feel Zarek's heartbeat against his palm. Could feel the Entity-touched energy thrumming beneath the man's skin, corrupted and changed by years of exposure to the Abyss. Could feel the man's memories, his knowledge, his secrets—everything the Soul Reaver could take, laid out like a feast.
Zarek wanted this. Wanted to be harvested. Wanted his knowledge to live on in Kael, even if it meant his own death.
The cult leader was insane. Fanatical. Willing to sacrifice anything for a cause Kael didn't understand.
But he'd also told the truth about the seals. About the Whisper King. About what was coming.
Kael released him.
Zarek staggered, gasping, one hand clutching his throat. "Why?"
"Because I don't kill people who surrender." Kael bent and picked up the Entity-essence sphere. "And because I want you alive to see what happens next."
He turned and walked toward the crypt stairs.
Behind him, Zarek laughed again—a broken, desperate sound that echoed off the stone walls.
"You'll be back," the cult leader called after him. "They always come back. The seals will fail. The Rift will open. And when it does, you'll need what I know. You'll need the Whisper King's weaknesses. And I'll be here. Waiting. Like I've always been waiting."
Kael didn't answer. He climbed the stairs, pushed through the crypt, and emerged into the cold night air of Thornwick.
The sphere pulsed in his hand. The Entity inside stirred, aware now. Curious. It had felt his touch. Had felt the Soul Reaver's potential.
And somewhere in the Abyss, something ancient and vast turned its attention toward the Material plane.
Something that had been waiting for two hundred years.
Something that was very, very interested in Kael Ashborn.
---
Kael made it three blocks before the first hunter found him.
Not a cultist. Not an Order operative. Something else.
It emerged from the shadows between two buildings—a figure that was almost human but moved wrong, its joints bending at angles that hurt to watch. Its face was a mask of smooth, featureless flesh, and when it spoke, its voice came from everywhere and nowhere.
"You touched the sphere."
Kael's blade was in his hand before the creature finished moving. "Who are you?"
"We are what waits. What has always waited." The hunter tilted its head, studying him. "The Whisper King has been expecting you, Soul Reaver. He knew you would come. He knew you would touch what was offered."
"And?"
"And now you are marked. The Entities know your scent. The Abyss knows your hunger." The hunter smiled—a terrible expression on its faceless face. "You wanted to harvest from beyond the Veil. Soon, you will have that chance. The Rift opens in six months. When it does, you will be drawn through, one way or another."
"Why tell me this?"
"Because the Whisper King wants you to understand. Wants you to see the shape of what's coming." The hunter began to fade, its form dissolving into the shadows. "You have a choice, Kael Ashborn. Join him. Fight him. Or become something else entirely. But whatever you choose, the Reckoning is coming."
It vanished.
Kael stood alone in the empty street, the Entity-essence sphere cold in one hand, his blade humming in the other.
The night air smelled of coal smoke and coming rain. Somewhere in the distance, a clock tower struck midnight. And beneath his feet, far below the city streets, the Great Rift pulsed—closer now than it had been yesterday.
Six months.
Kael looked up at the sky. The stars were hidden behind a thick layer of clouds, but he could feel them burning. Could feel the weight of the Abyss pressing against the Veil, weakening, thinning, preparing to break.
He had six months to become strong enough to survive what was coming.
Six months to find the truth about the Whisper King.
Six months to decide what kind of god he wanted to be.
The sphere pulsed again, and this time, the Entity inside whispered directly into his mind—a single word, ancient and alien and impossibly vast:
*Soon.*
Kael pocketed the sphere and sheathed his blade.
Then he walked into the darkness, toward whatever waited for him in the night.
The Reckoning had begun.
