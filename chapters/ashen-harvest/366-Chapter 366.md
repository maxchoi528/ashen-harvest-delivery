The blade took the cultist's throat before the man's scream could form. Steel scraped cartilage. Kael wrenched the weapon free, crimson arcing in the gaslight, splattering the brickwork in dark, glistening patterns. The body crumpled against the warehouse wall, sliding down the damp stone, leaving a wet smear behind it. The copper stench of fresh blood hit Kael's nostrils, mixing with the ever-present reek of coal smoke and brine that clung to Thornwick's eastern docks like a shroud.
Three more figures emerged from the shadows of the steamwork district's shipping district. Kael tracked their movements: coordinated footwork, balanced weight distribution, hands wrapped in stained leather. Professional. Nothing like the desperate flailing of true believers. These killers had trained for this exact moment.
"They knew we'd be here." Maren dropped from a fire escape above, her boots striking the cobblestones in a crouch beside him. Her twin daggers dripped the same crimson as his blade, the droplets pattering against the stone. "Second ambush tonight."
"Third." Kael wiped his hands on his coat, the fabric rough and sodden. The notification pulsed at the edge of his awareness, the Soul Reaver's calloused whisper already hungry. It scraped against his skull, an itch behind his eyes that demanded scratching. He crushed the impulse. These bodies weren't his to harvest. Not yet. The hunger could wait. "They hit the safehouse first."
Across the alley, a fourth cultist staggered into the open, hands raised, gibbering prayers to something that wasn't listening. Spittle flew from cracked lips. His eyes rolled back, showing white. Maren's throwing knife took him between the eyes before the man could finish his first syllable. The blade punched through bone with a wet crack. The cultist dropped like a puppet with cut strings.
"Move." Kael didn't wait to see if she followed. His boots splashed through puddles tainted with industrial runoff, each step carrying him deeper into the warehouse district. The Whisper King's information had been precise: a shipment of counterfeit soul-essence vials moving through Thornwick's eastern docks, funded by Order coin. If the six Orders were actively destabilizing their own war effort, someone needed to know. Someone needed to document it before the Great Rift swallowed everything.
The steam-fog rolled thick between the warehouse stacks, lit from below by the orange glow of industrial furnaces. It clung to Kael's skin, damp and warm, carrying traces of sulfur and iron filings. Somewhere distant, a factory whistle screamed the changing of shifts, the sound carrying through the damp air like a wounded animal. Kael navigated by memory and the faint pulse of spiritual energy that every Awakened carried like a second heartbeat. The cultists' signatures blazed wrong. Tainted. Corrupted. Threaded with something that tasted of ash and rot.
"They're Entity-touched." Maren caught up, her breath misting in the cold. Sweat glistened at her temples despite the chill. "Not just cultists. Full converts."
"That should be impossible." Kael vaulted a stack of crates, landing soft on the cobblestones beyond. His knees absorbed the impact. "The Entities can't manifest without a rift. The seals—"
"The seals are compromised." A new voice, cultured and cold, cut through the fog. "That's the point, isn't it?"
Kael spun, Soul Reaver awakening to full fury. The power surged through his veins, hot and electric, making his vision sharpen and his teeth ache. The man standing in the alley's mouth wore robes of deep crimson, the color of the Blood God's faithful. But his insignia marked him as Order of Dawn. A sunburst encircling a single eye, stitched in silver thread that caught the gaslight. Grand Inquisitor Varen, if the intelligence files were accurate. One of the six Orders' highest authorities.
"Grand Inquisitor." Maren's voice went flat. Her daggers stayed in her hands, a breach of every protocol governing Order interactions. "You weren't supposed to be here."
"No. I wasn't." Varen's smile didn't reach his eyes. The skin around his mouth crinkled, but his eyes stayed cold. Flat. Behind him, more figures materialized from the mist. Awakened soldiers in mismatched armor, their spiritual signatures a discordant chorus of power that made Kael's skull throb. They moved with the quiet precision of experienced killers. "You two have been remarkably persistent. Following threads I thought I'd buried decades ago."
Kael's grip tightened on his blade. His knuckles ached. "You sent the cultists."
"I funded a movement. The distinction matters, to those who enjoy lying to themselves." Varen spread his hands, a gesture of false openness. The sleeves of his crimson robes fell back, revealing forearms corded with muscle and old scars. "The Orders have grown soft, boy. Decadent. They've forgotten what it takes to survive an enemy that cannot be reasoned with, cannot be defeated, cannot be reasoned with. The Entities don't want destruction. They want integration. A merger of worlds. And I intend to give it to them."
"You're insane."
"I'm practical." Varen's soldiers fanned out, cutting off escape routes. Their boots scraped against the wet cobblestones. "The seals are failing. I've seen the projections. Fifty years, maybe less, before the Great Rift tears itself open and the Entities walk free. The Orders will fight, and die, preserving a status quo that serves no one. I've chosen a different path."
Maren moved. Her blade traced a silver arc toward Varen's throat. Fast. Professional. And stopped three inches from its goal, arrested by a wall of spiritual force that crackled audibly in the night air. The impact knocked her backward, her arms vibrating from the shock.
A fifth figure stepped from behind the soldiers, her form wreathed in shadow that clung like living cloth. The darkness around her moved wrong, sliding across the cobblestones like oil on water.
Lyra.
Kael's blood froze. His heart stuttered in his chest. The woman he'd trusted, fought beside, bled for. She stood in enemy colors, her face betraying nothing.
"Hello, Kael." Lyra's voice carried no warmth. "I imagine you have questions."
"You—" He stopped, recalibrated. His jaw clenched. Everything he'd believed about her came into sharp, painful focus. The late-night conversations, the shared rations, the way she'd covered his flank in the Ashfall ruins. All of it reframed. Repurposed. "The Shadow Court. You're still serving them."
"The Shadow Court serves the Orders, and the Orders serve me. Eventually." Lyra stepped forward, her shadow stretching wrong against the cobblestones. Too long. Too dark. "I've been watching you since the execution grounds. The Soul Reaver phenomenon is... Unprecedented. The Orders would kill you for what you are. The Shadow Court would use you. I chose the latter."
"She's lying." Maren strained against the invisible barrier, her face contorted with rage. Her veins stood out against her neck. "Lyra, what did they promise you?"
"Survival." The word landed like a slap. "The same thing I'm promising him. Look around you, Maren. The Orders are rotting from within. Varen's faction controls half the Council. The Iron Vanguard is funding cult operations across three provinces. The Arcane Spire has been selling seal-weakening research to the highest bidder for twenty years. Your noble Orders are a corpse that hasn't stopped twitching yet."
Varen chuckled. The sound echoed off the warehouse walls. "She's not wrong, you know. The Whisper King feeds you information like a fisherman baiting a hook. Do you think he's any cleaner than the rest of us? The man has his own agenda, his own plans. You're a piece on his board, just as you are on mine."
Kael's mind raced. The information asymmetry burned. He didn't know who to trust, couldn't parse truth from manipulation. His thoughts tangled and knotted. But he knew one thing with absolute certainty: everyone in this alley wanted something from him.
"What do you want?" He kept his blade raised, kept his stance loose. Soul Reaver power coiled in his core, ready to unleash. The energy hummed beneath his skin.
"Cooperation." Varen's smile widened. "Join us. Use your abilities to harvest the Entities themselves. We've developed techniques to bind their essences, contain their power. With a Soul Reaver's touch, we could drain the Great Rift dry before it fully opens. Save humanity through the very power that threatens it."
"And if I refuse?"
Lyra's hand moved, and Kael felt cold steel at his throat. He hadn't seen her draw. The motion had been that fast, that precise. The blade kissed his skin. Her breath warmed his ear as she spoke.
"Then we take what we need regardless. The Shadow Court has contained Soul Reavers before. It isn't pleasant, but it is possible." Her blade pressed harder. "Don't make me do this, Kael. I don't want to hurt you."
"Funny way of showing it." His voice came out steadier than he felt. His pulse hammered against the edge of her knife. "When did you decide to betray everything we fought for?"
"Everything we fought for was already dead." Lyra's voice cracked, just slightly. Pain or frustration, he couldn't tell. "I watched you nearly die in the Ashfall ruins. Watched you burn through your own soul trying to save people who would have sold you out in a heartbeat if they'd known what you really were. The Orders don't want a savior. They want a weapon. And weapons get used until they break."
Kael's hand twitched toward his Soul Reaver ability. The power hummed in his blood, eager to answer violence with violence. It pushed against his restraint. But Lyra was too close, too skilled. She'd killed Awakened before. Probably Awakened stronger than him.
"The Sepulcher tablets." Kael spoke the words like a gambit, watching their effect. "You've been hunting them. Why?"
Varen's expression flickered. A tell, quickly suppressed. Lyra's blade wavered almost imperceptibly.
"Because they contain the True Name," she said. "The name the first Soul Reaver used to bind the Entities. A name that could unmake every seal on the continent, or reinforce them beyond breaking. Power absolute. The kind of power that ends wars."
"Or starts them." Kael's mind worked furiously. The Sepulcher tablets. The key to understanding his own abilities, the origin of the Soul Reaver phenomenon. The Orders wanted them to stay buried. The cultists wanted them to rise. Someone had to get there first.
"How do I know you're not just going to use me and throw me away?" He addressed Varen directly now, buying time. His eyes tracked the soldiers. "The Shadow Court, the Orders. Everyone's playing angles. Why should I believe your offer is genuine?"
"Because I have no reason to lie." Varen spread his hands again, and this time Kael caught the glimmer of something beneath the crimson robes. A pendant, old bronze, inscribed with symbols that made his Soul Reaver pulse in recognition. The power in his chest lurched toward it. "I've studied the old records. I know what you are, what you represent. You're not a monster, boy. You're a legacy. The first Soul Reaver walked among the Orders a thousand years ago, and when he died, his power was supposed to pass to another. Instead, the Orders sealed it away, buried the knowledge, persecuted anyone who showed the gift. They feared what they couldn't control."
"And you're different?"
"I'm honest about what I want." Varen's soldiers had stopped their advance, watching the negotiation with predator patience. Their breathing stayed slow, controlled. "I want to survive. I want humanity to survive. The Entities cannot be defeated through conventional means. The seal network proves that. We can only contain them, delay them, pray that someone smarter than us finds a real solution. But containment requires sacrifice. Someone has to do the ugly work."
Kael's blade lowered, just an inch. The gesture was deliberate, calculated. A sign of considering the offer that Varen would read as weakness. Maren's protest died in her throat as she caught his eye.
"The cultists in Thornwick." Kael's voice stayed flat. "The Entity-touched soldiers. They're part of your 'ugly work'?"
"Recruitment. Experimentation. The Abyssal taint can be controlled in small doses. Used to enhance Awakened abilities, extend lifespan, sharpen senses. The Orders know this. They choose ignorance because acknowledging it would mean acknowledging that the war can't be won through prayer and protocol." Varen stepped closer, close enough that Kael could see the age lines around his eyes, the weight of decisions that had carved themselves into his face. The smell of old parchment and something sour clung to him. "I'm offering you a place at the table, boy. A chance to shape what comes next, than being shaped by it."
"And if I say no?"
"Then you walk away. Lyra withdraws her blade. You return to your Whisper King and tell him whatever you like." Varen's smile turned thin. "And in six months, when the Great Rift opens and the Entities pour through, you die knowing you had a chance to prevent it."
"That's not much of a choice."
"It's the only choice that matters." Varen turned away, gesturing for his soldiers to stand down. "You have until dawn to decide. The shipment we're intercepting will be at the eastern pier at first light. If you want answers about the Soul Reaver, about the tablets, about what's really happening to the seals, you'll be there. If not..." He shrugged. "Well. We'll meet again, I'm sure."
The soldiers melted into the fog, their spiritual signatures fading like dying embers. Lyra held her position for a long moment, blade still at Kael's throat, her eyes searching his face for something he couldn't name.
"Don't trust him," she whispered. "Don't trust any of them. But especially don't trust yourself. The Soul Reaver wants to consume. That's what it was made to do. The first one thought he could control it, and look what happened to him."
"What happened to him?" Kael caught the hesitation, the way her words snagged on unspoken grief.
Lyra's blade withdrew. She stepped back, her shadow stretching toward the darkness like a living thing.
"That's what we're all trying to figure out." She turned away. "Dawn, Kael. Don't be late."
She vanished into the steam-fog, and Kael stood alone in the alley with Maren, his mind churning with implications he couldn't fully parse.
"Can we trust any of that?" Maren demanded, finally released from the invisible barrier. Her voice shook with suppressed fury. Her hands trembled. "The seals are failing, the Orders are corrupt, the Shadow Court is watching. How much of it is true?"
"All of it." Kael sheathed his blade. The leather grip left his palm slick with sweat. "That's the worst part. It's all true. The question is what we do about it."
"We report to the Whisper King. We expose Varen's faction."
"And then what?" Kael started walking, not toward the exit but deeper into the warehouse district, where the air hung thick with smoke and old blood. "The Whisper King has his own agenda. Varen has his. Lyra has hers. Everyone wants to use the Soul Reaver for something, and nobody's going to tell me the whole truth because the truth is dangerous."
"Then what do we do?"
Kael stopped in front of a shuttered loading dock, where a body lay crumpled against the wooden slats. One of the cultists from the first ambush, throat opened by his blade. The blood had pooled beneath him, black and thick in the gaslight. The soul still clung to the flesh. Not yet departed. Not yet beyond saving.
He knelt. Pressed his palm to the cooling skin. The flesh was already growing stiff beneath his fingers.
The Soul Reaver stirred.
The power ripped through him. Cold fire. Burning ice. It tore into the corpse's spiritual remnants, shredding the thin membrane between life and death, between memory and oblivion. Fragments of the dead man's experiences slammed into Kael's consciousness.
Soul Harvest: You extracted [Fragmented Memory: Varen's Laboratory] from the remains. [Corrupted Talent: Abyssal Resistance Lv.2] acquired.
Kael gasped as information flooded his mind. Images. Sensations. Cold steel tables and screaming figures. The smell of formaldehyde and rot. Varen's operation. The scale of it. The number of bodies. Dozens. Hundreds. Rows of them, stacked like cordwood in a basement that stank of fear and excrement.
He'd found his answer.
Maren stared at him, horror dawning. Her face went pale. "Kael. What did you just take?"
"The truth." He rose, wiping his hand on his coat. The blood had already grown tacky. "Or part of it, anyway. Varen's not experimenting on cultists. He's experimenting on Order members. Awakened. The seals don't just weaken on their own. He's been feeding them corrupted soul-essence to accelerate the decay."
"That's—"
"Treason. Heresy. The kind of thing that gets you executed by all six Orders simultaneously." Kael's voice came out flat, emotionless. "But proof doesn't matter if the people who could act on it are the ones benefiting from the crime."
The warehouse around them groaned, old timber settling in the fog. Rust flaked from the iron brackets holding the walls together. Somewhere in the distance, a bell tolled. Dawn was a few hours away. A few hours to decide whether to walk into Varen's trap, expose a conspiracy that reached the highest levels of the very organizations sworn to protect humanity, or run.
"Kael." Maren grabbed his arm. Her grip tightened. "The first Soul Reaver. Lyra said he thought he could control the power. What does that mean? What happened to him?"
He didn't answer immediately. The harvested memory still pulsed at the edges of his awareness, fragments of a larger picture he couldn't assemble. But one image stood out, burned into his mind like a brand. A man on a stone altar, surrounded by the six Orders' founders, his body broken but his eyes still burning with defiance.
The inscription on his chest, carved in flesh: FRAIL.
"The first Soul Reaver wasn't sealed away," Kael said slowly. "He was betrayed. The Orders took his power and used it to build the seal network, then destroyed him so he couldn't take it back." He met Maren's eyes. "And Varen's trying to recreate the flow."
The fog parted around them, revealing a figure standing at the end of the alley. Hooded, cloaked in shadow, with eyes that glowed faint gold in the darkness.
"The Whisper King's messenger." Maren's hand went to her daggers. "How did he find us?"
"Because he wanted to be found." Kael stepped forward, positioning himself between the messenger and Maren. "What do you want?"
The figure lowered its hood, revealing a face Kael didn't recognize. Young, sharp-featured, with the look of someone who'd seen too much too fast.
"Lord Whisper sent me." The messenger's voice cracked. "There's been a breach at the Sanctuary. Someone triggered the old containment wards. The Sepulcher tablets—they're gone."
Kael's blood ran cold. "Varen."
"Unknown. But the wards were keyed to Order spiritual signatures. Three different signatures, moving in concert." The messenger's hands shook as he produced a scroll, sealed with black wax. "Lord Whisper says to tell you: the game has changed. The location he promised—it's compromised. But there's another way. The Soul Reaver's grave."
Maren inhaled sharply. Kael's mind raced through implications, calculating angles he couldn't see.
"The original Soul Reaver's tomb?"
"His prison." The messenger pressed the scroll into Kael's hands. "Where they kept him after they stripped his power. Where they kept the truth."
Kael broke the seal. The parchment inside bore a single symbol. The same one he'd glimpsed on Varen's pendant. The same one that had pulsed in the harvested memory.
"Where?"
"Below the Great Rift itself." The messenger stepped back into the shadows. "The Orders built the first seal over his cage. He's been there for a thousand years, waiting. And now someone wants to let him out."
The fog swallowed the messenger whole, leaving Kael alone with the scroll, the knowledge, and the weight of impossible choices.
Dawn approached. Varen's meeting loomed. The Sepulcher tablets had vanished. And somewhere beneath the continent's most dangerous wound, a god waited to rise.
"Kael." Maren's voice cut through his spiral. "What are you going to do?"
He stared at the symbol on the scroll, seeing connections he hadn't seen before. The Soul Reaver's prison. Varen's experiments. The seal network. Everything connected.
"Everything," he said. "I'm going to do everything."
The first light of dawn crept over Thornwick's rooftops, painting the steam-fog in shades of amber and rose. Somewhere in the city, the cultists regrouped. Somewhere else, Lyra reported his failure to her masters. And somewhere far below, in darkness that had lasted a millennium, something ancient stirred in its cage.
Kael walked toward the eastern pier, toward Varen's trap, toward answers that might kill him.
He didn't know if he was walking into salvation or damnation.
But he was walking.
The game had changed. And he intended to change it back.
