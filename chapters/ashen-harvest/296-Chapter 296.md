The corpse twitched beneath Kael's palm. Not the idle settling of cooling flesh, but a deliberate tremor that ran through severed fingers and stopped at the wrist. He jerked backward, boots scraping against the damp cobblestones, his Soul Reaver senses flaring wide open like a wound ripped raw. The wet film of the undercity slicked his skin, carrying the bite of copper and old mildew into his lungs. His heart slammed against his ribs.
Thornwick's undercity reeked of rot and coal smoke. Somewhere above, gas lamps hissed their perpetual twilight, casting a sickly amber glow that bled down through cracked grates. The tunnel stretched in both directions, its vaulted ceiling weeping moisture that pattered into puddles thick with iridescent scum. Rats chittered somewhere in the dark, their claws skittering over stone. The air hung thick and warm, trapped between layers of ancient masonry and the ever-churning machinery of the city above. The tunnel had been silent until Kael found this body—a cult enforcer, judging by the blackened robes and the brand seared into his throat. A sigil he recognized from the gaslit streets. From the killings. The flesh around the brand had turned the color of spoiled fruit, puckered and weeping fluid that smelled of burnt hair and something sweeter, more chemical. Kael crouched low, forcing his breathing to slow. Focus. The dead don't move. But this one had.
"You're the one they call Ashborn."
The voice came from everywhere and nowhere. Kael spun, planting himself between the corpse and the shadows, his right hand reaching for the blade he didn't carry. Three weeks on the run had taught him to sleep with one eye open, but nothing had prepared him for this—a presence that felt less like a person and more like an absence, a void shaped into words. The temperature dropped. His skin prickled with gooseflesh, the fine hairs on his arms rising beneath his sleeves. Kael's jaw clenched. His knuckles went white.
"What do you want?" Kael kept his voice flat. Unafraid. A lie he sold well enough to survive.
"Want?" The voice carried a note of amusement, cold and ancient as the stones surrounding them. "I want nothing, Kael Ashborn. I already have everything. You, But, have questions. The origin of your gift. The reason the Orders hunt you. The true name etched into the Sepulcher Tablets." A pause, heavy with implication. "I can offer answers."
The dead enforcer's hand spasmed again. Kael watched in horror as the fingers curled, forming a shape—a symbol pressed into the air by phantom flesh. The same sigil burned into the corpse's throat. The joints cracked with the sound of dry twigs snapping, each finger locking into place with grotesque precision.
"The Shadow Court sends their regards."
Kael lunged for the body, slamming both palms against cold skin. The contact sent a jolt through his arms—ice and static and something that tasted of ozone on his tongue. The harvest triggered automatically, his ability recognizing death's door even when the victim had already passed through. The skin beneath his hands grew colder still, the last vestiges of body heat draining into his palms as the connection solidified.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted fragmented memories from the remains. Cult Formation: Basic Unlocked.]
Information flooded his mind—a flash of hooded figures in a candlelit chamber, a map showing twelve locations across Thornwick, and a face. Pale. Gaunt. Eyes that held no light. The images came in jagged fragments, disjointed and violent, each one slamming into his consciousness with physical force. His temples throbbed. His vision blurred at the edges. The smell of blood filled his sinuses, though none flowed. The memory of the dead man's last moments pressed against his own, a suffocating weight that made his chest tighten until he forced it down, walled it off, packed it into the back of his skull.
The vision shattered. Kael staggered upright, tasting copper.
"Impressive," the voice said. "The Reaver's gift works even on the recently dead. Tell me, can you harvest the still-living?"
"Show yourself."
Laughter echoed through the tunnel, bouncing off walls until it seemed to come from every direction at once. The sound crawled along Kael's spine, each echo layered with something that wasn't sound—more like a pressure behind his eyes, a vibration in his teeth. Then the shadows in the far corner deepened, thickened, and a figure stepped forward.
He wore the robes of a common beggar, threadbare and patched. His face was hidden beneath a hood, but Kael caught glimpses—jowls like a hound, a scar running from temple to jaw, eyes that glittered with something beyond madness. This was no simple informant. The spiritual pressure radiating from the stranger pressed against Kael's senses like a thumb against a bruise. It took conscious effort not to step back. His calves tensed, ready to spring. The air between them felt thick, resistant, as though the stranger's presence had weight.
"Who are you?"
"I am a interested party." The figure gestured toward the dead cultist. "You found that one interesting, I assume? The brand indicates a cell leader. Twelve such cells operate in Thornwick, each containing roughly forty operatives. They answer to a man called Prime Zarek."
Kael's blood chilled. Zarek. The name from his old life, from the frame that sent him to the gallows. His shoulders drew tight. A muscle in his cheek twitched. "You know about Zarek?"
"I know about many things, young Reaver. The conspiracy that destroyed your family. The seal degrading beneath the Ashborn estate. The Whisper King who watches from his throne of shadows." The stranger tilted his head. "But I suspect you have more pressing concerns. Such as the six cultists currently approaching this tunnel from the north passage."
Kael heard it then—footsteps, dozens of them, synchronized in their rhythm. The sound bounced through the tunnel, distorted by distance and acoustics into something mechanical. His ears strained, sorting the patterns. Not six. Seven. He was certain of it.
"How do I know you're not working with them?"
The stranger smiled. Kael saw teeth filed to points. "Because I want you alive. The Whisper King requires a tool, and you, Kael Ashborn, are the finest instrument he has seen in two hundred years."
"Tool." The word tasted like bile. "You're saying the Whisper King sent you?"
"I am saying the Whisper King knows what you carry. That blade you don't possess—the one that cuts souls. The one that harvests abilities without cost. Do you think your gift is unique?" The stranger shook his head slowly. "You are the second Soul Reaver to walk this world. The first was my master. His tomb lies somewhere in the eastern wastes, sealed by the Six Orders two centuries past. Inside that tomb rests the knowledge you crave."
Kael's heart hammered against his ribs. The Soul Reaver origin. The mystery he'd carried since awakening on the gallows platform. "Why would the Whisper King share this?"
"Because the Whisper King is not your enemy, Ashborn. The Whisper King is the only reason you weren't executed in the first place."
The footsteps grew louder. Kael counted seven distinct patterns. Seven Awakened, if his senses weren't lying. More than he could handle in a confined space. The walls pressed in. The ceiling felt lower than it had moments before.
"What's your price?"
"Survive the night. Meet me at the Sepulcher Gate when the moon reaches its peak. Come alone." The stranger's form began to blur, shadows wrapping around him like a cloak. "Refuse, and the Whisper King withdraws his protection. The Shadow Court will learn your location. The Iron Vanguard will learn your scent. And the cult—" The smile widened. "The cult will feed you to the thing beneath Thornwick."
He vanished. The tunnel plunged into silence.
Kael stood over the dead enforcer, mind racing. Information. Danger. A choice that could define everything. The footsteps were close now, maybe two hundred yards, rounding the final corner.
He had seconds to decide.
Running would mean abandoning the only lead on Zarek he'd found since escaping the gallows. Fighting seven cultists alone in narrow tunnels was suicide. But there was a third option—the one the stranger had hinted at.
The Soul Reaver's gift.
Kael pressed his palm against the enforcer's chest one more time, pushing past the initial extraction, digging deeper into the residual spiritual signature. The corpse yielded. He felt the spiritual remnants, the fading echo of techniques learned and mastered over years of training. It was there, buried beneath layers of basic formation knowledge. Something finer. The dead man's spiritual signature flickered against Kael's awareness, cold and fragmented. He dug deeper, his fingers curling against the dead man's sternum. His vision swam. His stomach lurched.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted advanced technique from the remains. Veil Step: Movement ability acquired.]
The technique slotted into his consciousness like a key finding its lock. A short-range teleport, limited to line of sight, requiring fifteen seconds of stillness to activate. Dangerous. Impractical. Potentially lifesaving.
The cultists rounded the bend. Seven figures in black, faces hidden behind masks of hammered iron. They carried no weapons visible, but Kael sensed the spiritual energy crackling around their hands. Binding techniques. Suppression seals. They wanted him alive. The iron masks caught what little light the tunnel offered, reflecting it in dull flashes. Their robes whispered against the stone floor. The spiritual energy radiating from them pressed against Kael's skin like heat from a furnace.
"Run," one of them said. The voice was feminine, sharp with authority. "The Whisper King's protection ends tonight, Reaver. Stand down and face judgment."
Kael smiled. His hand found the wall behind him, fingers pressing against cold stone.
The Veil Step activated.
Reality folded. For an instant, Kael existed in two places at once—the tunnel with the cultists, and a shadow-drenched corner fifty feet further back. He chose the latter, wrenching his consciousness through the gap between worlds and reappearing in darkness. The transition ripped through him. Cold fire raced along his nerves. His vision fractured into prismatic shards before snapping back to whole. His knees buckled. He caught himself against the wall, fingers digging into slick stone, and forced himself to stay upright.
Behind him, the cultists shouted. Their footsteps scattered, pursuing in the wrong direction.
Kael ran.
The tunnels beneath Thornwick stretched for miles, a maze of old sewer lines, abandoned mining shafts, and passages that predated the city itself. He'd mapped several routes during his three weeks of hiding, memorizing the routes that led to safe houses and dead ends. His boots splashed through standing water that soaked through worn leather and chilled his feet. The walls changed as he moved—brick giving way to rough-hewn stone, then back to brick, each transition marking a different era of construction. The air shifted too. Sewer stench yielded to mineral tang, then to the dry dust of passages long sealed and recently broken open. His breath came hard. His lungs burned. The Veil Step had taken more out of him than he'd expected.
The Sepulcher Gate lay on the eastern edge of the undercity, where an ancient temple once stood before the Orders burned it during the Reformation. If the stranger wanted a meeting, that location made sense. Neutral ground. Symbolic weight.
But first, Kael needed to confirm his suspicions about the cult's organization.
He found another body ten minutes later—another enforcer, killed recently, slumped against a junction marker. Fresh enough for a quality harvest. The blood had not yet fully coagulated. When Kael pressed his palm to cooling skin, the warmth that remained beneath the surface told him the man had been alive an hour ago, maybe less.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted strategic information from the remains. Cell Network: Partial Map acquired.]
This time the information came clearer. Names. Meeting points. Supply routes linking the twelve cells to a central location in the merchant quarter. And something else—a warning passed down from Prime Zarek himself.
*The Reaver walks among us. Capture only. His gift will fuel the Great Work.*
Kael's stomach turned. They didn't want to kill him. They wanted to harvest him. His throat tightened. He swallowed hard, forcing the bile back down. The taste of the dead man's memories lingered on his tongue—ash and fear and the cold certainty of being used.
He pushed the thought aside and kept moving, following the tunnels toward the east. The air grew colder as he climbed, shifting from damp sewer stench to something cleaner, older. Dust and ancient stone. The passage narrowed, then widened into a corridor lined with pillars carved with symbols that made his eyes ache to focus on. The temperature dropped steadily. His breath began to mist.
The Sepulcher Gate emerged from the darkness like a wound in the earth. Four pillars of black granite, each twenty feet tall, formed a gateway that led nowhere. The stonework bore symbols Kael recognized from the Sepulcher Tablets—the true names of the Entities, recorded in a language that predated the Orders by millennia. The granite was cold to the touch when Kael brushed his fingers against it. Cold and faintly slick, as though the stone itself perspired. A low hum emanated from the pillars, felt more than heard—a vibration that settled into his molars and stayed there.
Someone waited at the center of the gate.
Not the stranger from before. A woman.
She stood with her back to him, dressed in the white robes of an Order of Dawn initiate. Her hair hung loose past her shoulders, silver-white in the dim light. When she turned, Kael saw a face he knew.
Lyra.
"You're late," she said.
"Lyra?" Kael's hand twitched toward a weapon he didn't have. "What are you doing here?"
"Saving your life." She walked toward him, each step deliberate. "The stranger who contacted you works for the Whisper King. His information is accurate, but his motives are not. The Whisper King doesn't want to help you, Kael. He wants to use you."
"I figured that part out."
"Then you're smarter than I gave you credit for." Lyra stopped ten feet away, close enough for conversation but far enough to react if he attacked. "I was sent to intercept you before the meeting. The Shadow Court has decided the Whisper King's interest in you represents an unacceptable risk."
Kael's blood ran cold. "The Shadow Court."
"My former employers." Lyra's jaw tightened. "Yes. I know you know about my past. I know what I did for them. But I'm not here to bring you in, Kael. I'm here to offer you an alternative."
"An alternative to what?"
"To becoming the Whisper King's puppet. To being harvested by the cult. To running until they corner you in some forgotten alley and put you down like a rabid dog." Lyra's eyes burned with intensity. "The Shadow Court has resources. Contacts. A network that spans the continent. We can hide you. Protect you. Help you find what you're really looking for."
"The Soul Reaver's origin."
"Among other things." She reached into her robe and withdrew a small vial, holding it up to the light. Inside, something glowed with soft luminescence—soul-essence, pure and concentrated. "This is a sample of Entity-touched essence. Worth more than a mansion in the Noble Quarter. The Shadow Court extracted it from a minor Entity during a raid on one of the sealed rifts. Such cooperation with the cult would be worth a king's ransom."
Kael stared at the vial, then at Lyra. "You want me to work for you."
"I want you to survive. How you do that is up to you." She pocketed the vial. "But you should know—the Whisper King has been playing this game for two hundred years. He doesn't make moves without purpose. Whatever he's planning for you, it's bigger than Thornwick. Bigger than the cult. Maybe bigger than the seal network itself."
"You know who he is?"
Lyra hesitated. For the first time, uncertainty flickered across her features. "No one knows the Whisper King's true identity. But I've seen the records. The shadow operations. The pattern of moves spanning generations." She met his gaze. "He's old, Kael. Old and patient. And he wants a Soul Reaver for something specific."
The wind shifted, carrying dust and the distant rumble of steam engines from the city above. Kael thought about the information he'd harvested. The organized cult cells. The map of locations. The phrase that kept echoing in his mind.
*The Reaver walks among us.*
They knew he existed. They'd been watching him, tracking his movements, waiting for the right moment to strike. The Whisper King had intervened tonight, but for what purpose?
Lyra was still watching him, waiting for an answer.
"I'll think about it," Kael said finally.
"That's not good enough." She stepped closer. "The cult is moving faster now. The cells are preparing for something—a mass ritual, maybe, or a coordinated strike on the seal network. Zarek has been meeting with representatives from all twelve cells. Something is happening in Thornwick, and it's happening soon."
"How soon?"
"Days. Maybe less." Lyra's expression hardened. "The Shadow Court has agents embedded in the cult, but they're going dark. Someone tipped off Zarek. Either the Whisper King is burning his own network, or there's a third player we haven't identified."
Kael considered this. A conspiracy within a conspiracy. The cult acting on Zarek's orders. The Whisper King manipulating from the shadows. The Shadow Court watching from the edges. And himself,, at the center of it all.
The Soul Reaver.
He'd thought his gift was a curse. A mark of exile that would follow him to the grave. But now he understood—it was a target. Everyone wanted what he could do.
"What happens if I refuse both offers?"
"Then you face the cult alone. The Whisper King withdraws his protection. The Shadow Court stops watching your back. And Zarek's people close in from every direction." Lyra's voice softened. "You have maybe seventy-two hours before they find you, Kael. Maybe less if you're unlucky. The only question is whether you want to spend those hours running, or doing something that matters."
The silence stretched between them, heavy with implication.
Kael thought about his family. The estate that had been his birthright, now a smoldering ruin. The seal beneath it, cracking and leaking Abyssal energy into the groundwater. The conspiracy that had framed him, killed his parents, and left him for dead.
Zarek. The name burned in his mind like a brand.
"If I go after Zarek," he said slowly, "I won't have backup."
"You'll have information. Resources. Maybe even an ally or two." Lyra smiled grimly. "But the final move is yours, Reaver. It's always been yours."
She extended her hand.
Kael stared at it for a long moment. Then he reached out and shook.
"Not a contract," he said. "Just a conversation."
"For now." Lyra's grip was stronger than he expected. "Meet me tomorrow at the Drowned Rat tavern, midnight. I'll have more to share. Maps of the cult's operations. Names of the cell leaders. And something else—a lead on where Zarek will be in forty-eight hours."
She released his hand and stepped back, fading into the shadows of the ancient gate.
Kael stood alone in the darkness, the weight of choices pressing down on him.
The cult was mobilizing. The Whisper King was watching. The Shadow Court was offering deals. And somewhere in Thornwick, Prime Zarek was planning something that could shake the foundations of the seal network itself.
He had two days. Maybe less.
The question was whether to run, hide, or fight.
Kael looked down at his hands. The hands of a Reaver. The hands of a dead man walking.
He started walking toward the surface.
Above, the gaslit city of Thornwick churned with secrets. Steam engines roared. Ancient seals cracked. And in the depths of Shadowmere Asylum, in a throne room no living person had ever seen, someone smiled.
The game had changed.
The pieces were moving.
And Kael Ashborn, death-row survivor, Soul Reaver, wanted man, had just become the most important pawn on the board.
Footsteps echoed behind him. Multiple sets, moving fast.
They'd found him already.
Kael broke into a run, dodging between the ancient pillars, his mind racing through options. The Veil Step could get him out, but it required stillness—impossible while being pursued. His harvested abilities were limited, combat-oriented but untested. His boots hammered the stone. His breath tore through his throat.
Behind him, voices shouted commands. Spiritual energy crackled in the air.
He needed to find cover. He needed to think.
He needed to survive the next five minutes.
A side passage opened to his left, narrow and dark. Kael dove into it without hesitation, letting the shadows swallow him whole. The footsteps continued past, the cultists following the wrong path.
Kael pressed himself against the wall, breathing hard, waiting. The stone bit into his shoulder blades through his shirt. His chest heaved. Sweat stung his eyes.
The darkness closed in. Somewhere in the distance, water dripped onto stone. The city above rumbled with the weight of a million lives, unaware of the war being waged beneath its streets.
Forty-eight hours.
He would find Zarek. He would get answers.
And if the Whisper King, the Shadow Court, and the cult wanted to use him, they would learn exactly what a Soul Reaver was capable of.
The passage stretched ahead, leading deeper into the undercity.
Kael moved forward, one step at a time.
Behind him, the footsteps faded.
Ahead, the future waited, uncertain and dangerous.
But for now, he was alive.
And that was enough.
