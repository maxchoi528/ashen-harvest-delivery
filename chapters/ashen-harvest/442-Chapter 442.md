The rift tore open three blocks from Kael's position, vomiting crimson light and the stench of burning copper into Thornwick's gaslit night. Screams followed.
Kael moved before his mind caught up, boots pounding cobblestones slick with rain. The seal's warning sigil burned against his awareness—a frequency he'd learned to dread in the months since his execution. The sigil meant Entity contact. The crimson meant something had come through.
He rounded the corner into Ashworth Square and found carnage. Market stalls lay overturned, their iron wheels bent like parchment. Bodies sprawled in positions that defied anatomy—limbs twisted backward, spines curved into impossible arcs. A child screamed somewhere among the wreckage, and Kael's stomach clenched.
Not a child. Never a child.
The thing that had emerged from the rift stood nine feet tall, its form a shifting mass of shadows and teeth. A lesser Entity, stage four at most, but still beyond anything the city guard could handle. Its eyeless head swept the square, and where it looked, people collapsed—souls torn loose by the sheer pressure of its presence.
"The Orders will send someone," a voice called from behind an overturned cart. A man in merchant's clothing clutched a bleeding arm, face pale with terror. "We just need to survive until—"
The Entity turned toward the voice.
Kael didn't think. He ran.
The creature's limb—foreleg? tentacle?—swung toward him in a blur that left afterimages. Kael dropped beneath it, rolling across wet stone, and his hand found something cold and yielding. A corpse. One of the market guards, throat torn out, blood still warm.
He pressed his palm flat against the cooling flesh.
The Soul Reaver activated.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted Spatial Rupture [Fragment] from the remains. Combat Utility acquired."
Power flooded through him—not spiritual energy but something stranger, something that tasted like hunger and sounded like whispered secrets. The guard had been Awakened, stage two, and his final moments had been spent fighting this thing. Fighting and failing. But his ability survived in the meat and bone, waiting for someone who could use it.
Kael felt the fragment integrate, felt his perception split along invisible axes. The world gained depth. Lines of force appeared in the air around him—the Entity's own spatial distortions, its attempts to tear reality further open.
"You're mine," Kael breathed.
He reached out with the stolen fragment and pulled.
The Entity shrieked as its own spatial anchor collapsed. Its form convulsed, limbs spasming as the fabric of space rejected its presence. It stumbled backward, clawing at nothing, and Kael was already moving.
He hit it shoulder-first, driving through the resistance of reality itself. The collision should have shattered his bones. Instead, the spatial fragment absorbed the impact, folding space around him like armor. He felt the Entity's essence through his reaver-sense—raw, mindless hunger, a void where thought should be.
Another corpse lay nearby. A woman in servant's dress, killed by debris. Kael's hand found her cooling forehead.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted Emergency Healing [Minor] from the remains. Sustainability acquired."
The fragment slotted into place alongside the spatial ability, and Kael felt his wounds from the night—bruises, cracked ribs, a gash across his shoulder he'd almost forgotten—all beginning to knit closed. Pain receded to a dull throb.
The Entity thrashed. Its shriek rose in pitch, tearing at the air, and the rift behind it pulsed wider. More crimson light. More of that copper-stink.
"Impossible," Kael muttered. "It shouldn't have enough power to maintain a rift and fight."
Unless something was feeding it.
The realization came too late. The merchant who'd been hiding behind the cart stood now, his wounded arm hanging limp, his face slack and empty. Dark veins crawled beneath his skin—not blood, but something darker, something that pulsed with stolen light.
The Entity wasn't an invader. It was a distraction.
Cultists.
Kael spun, searching the square for others, and found three more figures rising from bodies they'd been crouching beside. All showing the same signs—the dark veins, the empty eyes, the subtle glow of harvested soul-essence pooled somewhere beneath their skin.
"They're vessels," Kael said. "You're using them as batteries."
The merchant smiled with a mouth that held too many teeth. "The Reaver sees clearly. The Faceless One will be pleased."
The Faceless One. Not an Entity—a name Kael had heard whispered in the criminal quarters of Thornwick, in the back rooms where desperate people made desperate bargains. Someone connected to the Abyss, yes, but not an Entity itself. Something that worked through others.
A cult leader. The mastermind behind the rifts that had plagued the city for months.
The Entity, no longer needing to maintain the rift, turned its full attention to Kael. Its form solidified, darkness condensing into something almost physical, and this time when it struck, Kael felt the air scream around its blow.
He dodged. The fragment of spatial manipulation let him slip between folds in reality, but the Entity was adapting, reading his movements, matching his distortions with its own. Every attack came faster. Every dodge left less margin.
Kael needed more power.
The servant woman lay three feet away, her corpse still warm enough for good extraction. He dropped beside her, palm pressing against her sternum, and pulled.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted Shadow Step [Advanced] from the remains. Mobility acquired."
The fragment entered him like ice water, and he could see the shadows differently—not as absence of light but as pathways, as doorways between moments. The servant had been Awakened, stage three, and her ability had been refined through years of use.
Kael stepped sideways into darkness and emerged behind the Entity.
His fist connected with its spine. The creature's shriek became something almost like a word, almost like a name, and then Kael was already gone again, shadow-stepping to a new position, a new angle, striking from directions that shouldn't exist.
The Entity thrashed. Cultists moved to flank him, their stolen light burning in Kael's reaver-sense like beacons. But they were slow—vessels, not warriors—and Kael was faster than anything that hadn't reached stage five.
Stage five. Demigod-tier. The threshold where Awakened stopped being dangerous and started being apocalyptic.
Kael wasn't there yet. Stage four, Sovereign-grade, but his abilities came from corpses, from the harvested skills of others. In raw power, he couldn't match a true Demigod. In versatility, in adaptation, in the ability to pull new tools from every fallen enemy—
He circled the Entity, shadow-stepping from shadow to shadow, and it tried to track him but couldn't, because the shadows weren't consistent, weren't logical, weren't bound by the rules the creature understood.
One cultist broke formation, lunging toward him with a knife that glowed with stolen essence. Kael let him come. When the blade was inches from his throat, he shadow-stepped, leaving nothing but an afterimage, and the cultist's momentum carried him into the Entity's thrashing limb.
The impact didn't kill him. The soul-essence burning in his veins kept him alive for three precious seconds—seconds in which his eyes cleared, in which recognition flickered across his face, in which he whispered "I'm sorry" to someone who couldn't hear.
Then the Entity consumed him. The dark veins went incandescent, and the creature's form expanded, shadows bleeding outward, reality groaning under the strain.
Kael watched the soul-essence transfer happen. Saw it with his reaver-sense as clearly as seeing color with his eyes. The cultist's stolen energy—harvested from dozens of victims over months of ritual sacrifice—flowed into the Entity like water into a cracked vessel.
"Stupid," Kael said. "You just gave it fuel."
The Entity turned toward him. Its form had changed, become more solid, more defined. The stolen power had pushed it toward stage five. The rift behind it widened further, and through it, Kael glimpsed something vast and patient, something that had been waiting in the Abyss for exactly this opportunity.
The 72 Entities. Each sealed, each imprisoned, each waiting for the day the seals failed. This one had found a way to cheat—not breaking its seal directly, but sending pieces of itself through, pieces that could consume and grow and eventually reunite with the whole.
The great rifts. The small rifts. All connected. All part of a pattern.
Kael understood now why the cultists had been so organized, why they'd struck at specific times, why their attacks always seemed designed to cause maximum chaos with minimum permanent damage. They weren't trying to destroy Thornwick. They were trying to destabilize it, to create the conditions where a Demigod-tier Entity could manifest without the Orders noticing until too late.
And the Orders—they fought the Entities, yes, Kael had learned that much. Six Orders, each protecting a different aspect of the seal network. The Order of Dawn guarded the day-seals. The Iron Vanguard maintained the physical barriers. The Arcane Spire studied the Abyss itself. The Verdant Circle tended the living seals—creatures and plants bound to the network. The Silent Covenant hunted cultists. The Twilight Choir... Kael didn't know what the Twilight Choir did, but he'd heard their name in conjunction with the Faceless One.
Someone inside the Orders was helping the cult. Had to be. The pattern was too precise, the timing too perfect, for the cultists to be operating blind.
The Entity lunged.
Kael shadow-stepped through its strike, emerging above it, and drove his hand into the crown of its eyeless head. The reaver-sense opened fully, and he felt the creature's core—the fragment of the 72 Entities that it had been, before the seal, before the wars, when it had been something else entirely.
Soul Harvest: You extracted Void Pull [Basic] from the remains. Offensive Potential acquired.
Soul Harvest: You extracted Entity Memory [Fragmentary] from the remains. Knowledge acquired.
Soul Harvest: You extracted Abyssal Resonance [Active] from the remains. Threat Assessment upgraded.
Three fragments in one harvest. The Entity had been powerful, and its death released a cascade of abilities that flooded into Kael's repertoire. He gasped as new senses opened—the awareness of the rift behind him, of the vast thing waiting beyond, of the network of seals that kept reality from collapsing.
The Entity collapsed into shadow, then into nothing, leaving only a cooling patch of darkness on the cobblestones.
Kael landed beside it, legs shaking, mind reeling. The fragments were integrating, reshaping his understanding of the world. The rift still gaped behind him, but without the Entity to maintain it, it was shrinking, reality healing itself now that the foreign presence was gone.
He turned to face the remaining cultists.
Three of them, all with the dark veins, all carrying stolen essence that burned in his reaver-sense. They stood in a triangle formation, hands raised, and Kael realized with cold certainty that they were preparing something. Not an attack—rituals required time, required focus, required positions.
They were calling for reinforcements.
A new rift opened behind Kael, and he spun, but this one was different. No crimson light, no copper smell. Instead, the rift was silver and silent, and through it stepped a figure in grey robes, a figure whose face was hidden behind a mask of shifting shadows.
"Second Reaver," Kael breathed.
The figure tilted its head. When it spoke, its voice came from everywhere and nowhere, layered with harmonics that made Kael's teeth ache.
"You know the term. Good. That means the knowledge survives."
The cultists faltered. One of them turned to run, and the masked figure raised a hand, and the cultist... Stopped. Frozen in place, essence draining from the dark veins, flowing backward into the figure's palm.
"You are not supposed to exist," Kael said. "The Soul Reaver lineage enforces singularity. Only one at a time."
"The lineage," the figure said, "is a tool. Tools can be broken. Tools can be copied." It stepped closer, and Kael saw the way its shadow didn't match its form—too many angles, too few dimensions. "You are the current bearer. I was the previous. We are... Echoes of the same hunger."
"I would have known. I would have sensed—"
"You sense what you are capable of sensing. There are depths you have not yet plumbed."
The remaining cultists made a break for it in different directions. The masked figure gestured again, and one fell, then another, essence streaming toward it. The third one made it to the edge of the square before the shadow beneath its feet reached up and pulled it under.
Kael watched this happen, mind racing. The Second Soul Reaver Paradox—the narrative obligation he'd triggered. The lineage that shouldn't allow two Reavers. But here one stood, and it was older, more powerful, more certain of itself.
"You killed the others," Kael said. "The Reavers before me. You were supposed to be the last, but you survived."
"I am not the last. I am the first. The lineage you inherited was designed to produce me again, but the design failed. It produced you instead—different, younger, weaker, but still a Reaver." The figure tilted its head again, and this time Kael caught something beneath the shifting shadows of its mask. Not a face. Not any more. Something that had stopped being human centuries ago. "You have been harvesting well. You have been learning. But you do not understand yet what you are becoming."
"Then tell me."
"Why would I?"
"Because I'm standing right here, and you're talking, which means you want something."
The masked figure laughed—a sound like breaking glass, like distant thunder, like the death of stars. "Clever. Yes, I want something. The Faceless One has learned of you. The cultists you just killed were its, and now it knows what a Reaver can do in the open. It will send more. It will send worse. It will not stop until it has harvested you or destroyed you."
"Then help me stop it."
"I cannot act directly. The Concordat binds me as it binds you. But I can offer information. The Faceless One is not one person—it is a title, passed down through a bloodline that has served the Abyss for three hundred years. The current holder is named Aldric Vane, and he sits on the Council of Six as the representative of the Order of Dawn."
Kael's blood went cold. The Order of Dawn—one of the Six, the most respected, the most public face of the war against the Abyss. Their leader was a cultist. The man who'd probably ordered Kael's execution was a cultist.
"The Six Orders," Kael said. "They fight the Abyss, but there's corruption inside them."
"There is corruption in all things. The question is whether it can be purged." The masked figure moved closer, and Kael had to fight the urge to step backward. "You are stage four. Sovereign-grade. The breakthrough to Demigod requires a specific trigger—a moment of transcendence where you cease to be only human and become something more. Most Awakened never reach that point. They exhaust their spiritual reserves and die, never knowing what they could have been."
"You reached it."
"I did. Through the harvest of something that should not have been harvestable. A god was dying, and I was there, and I took what remained." The figure's shadow stretched toward Kael like a hand reaching for a throat. "You will face a similar choice. Soon. The Faceless One is accelerating the rifts, and when enough of them break open, the seals will fail entirely. Seventy-two Entities will manifest, and the world will end. Unless someone reaches Divine stage—true Divine, not the fragments that call themselves gods—and restarts the seal network."
"Divine stage," Kael repeated. Stage six. The realm beyond Demigod, where beings became something more than enhanced humans. "I can't—"
"You can. You have harvested from enough dead Awakened that your capacity has expanded beyond normal limits. You need only one more harvest. One more piece. And then you will face the same choice I faced: take the power of a dying god and become something that can save the world, or refuse and watch it burn."
The masked figure stepped back, toward the silver rift that still hung open behind it.
"The cultists will return. More Entities will come. The Shadow Court already watches you—they have since the day of your resurrection, when you first used the Soul Reaver on your executioner's corpse. You are not alone in this fight, Reaver. But you must decide what you are willing to become."
The rift closed, and the figure was gone, leaving Kael alone in Ashworth Square with the bodies of the cultists, the cooling patch of shadow where the Entity had been, and a mind full of impossible questions.
The Council of Six. The Faceless One. The dying god waiting to be harvested.
Kael crouched beside the merchant's corpse, the man who'd been a vessel, and pressed his palm to the cooling flesh. The harvest came easily now, his reaver-sense opening like a flower, and the fragments flowed into him.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted Vessel Link [Fragmentary] from the remains. Network Analysis acquired."
New knowledge. The cultists had been connected to each other, to the Entity, to something larger—a network that pulsed with stolen essence. Kael could feel it now, a web of dark light stretching across the city, converging on a point somewhere in the noble quarter.
Aldric Vane's estate.
He stood, legs unsteady, body exhausted but mind blazing with new purpose. The Six Orders fought the Abyss, but one of their own had betrayed them. The cult was organized, patient, centuries in the planning. And somewhere in this city, a man who called himself the Faceless One was preparing to end the world.
Kael walked toward the noble quarter, past the city guard who were finally arriving, past the medics who would tend the wounded, past the officials who would count the dead. None of them looked at him twice. A Sovereign-grade Awakened wasn't unusual in Thornwick, not anymore, not since the rifts had started opening with increasing frequency.
They didn't know how close they'd come to annihilation. They didn't know about the Entity that had been growing in their midst, fed by stolen souls, pushed toward manifestation.
They didn't know about the choice that awaited Kael at the end of this road.
A dying god. A throne of power. A chance to save everyone or become something that couldn't be called human anymore.
He would face that choice soon. The masked figure's words echoed in his mind: *You will face a similar choice. Soon.*
But first, he had a cult to destroy. A Council member to expose. A city to save.
The night pressed close around him, thick with rain and the lingering copper-stink of Abyssal passage. Somewhere in the darkness, the Shadow Court watched. Somewhere in the noble quarter, Aldric Vane planned his next move.
And somewhere between the material world and the Abyss, seventy-two seals creaked under the pressure of things that had waited millennia to return.
Kael stepped into the shadows and shadow-stepped toward his destination, the fragments of harvested power thrumming in his veins like a second heartbeat.
The reckoning was coming.
