The creature lunged from the shadows, its form twisting between solid flesh and writhing darkness. Kael's blade sang through the air, catching moonlight along its edge as he twisted aside from a strike that would have opened his throat.
"Contact!" he shouted, resetting his stance.
Two more shapes emerged from the alley's mouth. The first fell to a precise thrust through its chest, black ichor spraying across the cobblestones. The second circled wide, trying to flank him.
Kael planted his foot and pivoted, bringing his sword around in a horizontal arc. The blade connected with the creature's neck, severing its head in a spray of viscous fluid. The head rolled across the stones, its mouth still working soundlessly.
"Clear," he muttered, lowering his weapon.
A groan from the far end of the alley drew his attention. One of the creatures still lived, its torso twisted at an impossible angle. Blood pooled beneath it, dark and thick.
Kael approached carefully. In the months since his Awakening, he'd learned that dying things sometimes had one more strike to give. He reached down with his left hand, pressing his palm against the creature's cooling flesh.
The Soul Reaver stirred in his chest, that cold hunger that had awakened the night of his execution. Energy flooded up his arm—foreign, corrupt, tasting of ash and corruption.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Shadow Movement Lv.7 from the remains. Agility +3 acquired.]
The notification flickered across his vision, those strange system words that had appeared the moment his heart restarted in the execution chamber. He still didn't understand them. He didn't need to. The power was real, and it was his.
The creature's body went still, all remaining essence drained. Kael withdrew his hand, wiping the residue on his coat. His stomach churned at the act—always did—but he pushed the feeling down. The world didn't afford him the luxury of squeamishness.
Thornwick spread before him, a maze of gaslit streets and industrial smoke. Three months ago, he'd been a death-row prisoner awaiting execution for crimes he hadn't committed. Now he hunted things that made the gallows look like a mercy.
A carriage rattled past the alley's entrance, its lanterns casting long shadows across the wet stones. Kael pressed himself against the wall until it passed. Even at this hour, the city's streets weren't safe for a man with Soul Reaver blood on his hands.
The whisper came from everywhere and nowhere, sliding through his mind like oil across water.
*"Found you."*
Kael spun, blade raised. The alley behind him had transformed. Where there should have been brick walls and garbage bins, an impossible darkness now pressed against the boundaries of reality. Not shadow—true darkness, the absolute absence of light that his eyes refused to process.
From that void, something watched him.
He couldn't see it. Couldn't hear it. But he felt its attention like pressure against his skull, and the Soul Reaver in his chest screamed warnings he'd never experienced before.
"What are you?" he demanded, voice steady despite the ice crawling down his spine.
The darkness rippled. A shape began to coalesce at its center—not a creature, not a presence. It flickered between forms: humanoid one moment, a writhing mass of tendrils the next, occasionally resolving into something with too many eyes or a mouth that stretched across its entire width.
When it spoke, the words formed directly in his consciousness, bypassing his ears entirely.
*"Little Reaver. Stolen thief of dead men's power. The Veil bleeds, and we taste your kind across the membrane."*
Kael's grip tightened on his sword. "Abyssal," he breathed. The term the Orders used for the entities that dwelt beyond the barriers separating their world from the raw spiritual chaos of the Abyss. He'd heard rumors—seen bodies twisted beyond recognition—but he'd never confronted one directly.
Until now.
*"Seventy-two seals hold our prison,"* the entity continued, its voice like grinding stone. *"Yours is the seventy-third. The first seal made flesh. We have waited long for your kind to rise again."*
The darkness around the creature began to spread, consuming the alley's walls. Kael took a step backward and felt brick against his spine. No exit. The entity had sealed him in.
"Why tell me this?" he asked. "Why not just kill me?"
A sound that might have been laughter vibrated through his chest. *"Because knowledge is its own torture. Because you will carry our message to the others—to the Orders who think themselves our jailers. The seals weaken. The Great Rift awakens. And when the seventy-three open as one, your world will drown in our glory."*
The entity lunged.
Kael moved on instinct, throwing himself sideways as tendrils of absolute darkness speared through the space he'd occupied. His shoulder struck the wall, pain flaring, but he'd already rolled to his feet with his blade raised.
The creature flowed after him, its formless mass filling the alley with hungry void. He struck at it, his sword passing through darkness as if cutting smoke. But where the blade touched the entity's substance, it recoiled, leaving trails of brighter black in its wake.
*Physical weapons hurt it,* he realized. *But barely.*
The Soul Reaver pulsed in his chest, responding to his emotional spike. Energy gathered in his palm—the same power he'd used to harvest abilities from corpses. He'd never tested it against something that wasn't already dead.
He might only get one chance.
The entity struck again, a sweeping tendril catching his leg and sending him sprawling. The cold that touched his skin burned like frostbite, and where it touched, his leg went numb. Kael scrambled backward, dragging himself with his arms as the creature advanced.
*"Run, little Reaver,"* it hissed. *"Show us how prey struggles."*
Kael planted his back against the wall and raised his hand. Energy crackled between his fingers—not the gentle flow he'd used for harvesting, but something rawer, more violent. He didn't know if this would work. He didn't know if it would kill him.
He released the power directly into the entity's mass.
The effect was immediate and catastrophic. Light blazed from the point of impact—not natural light, but something brighter, harder, the pure energy of stolen souls compressed into destructive force. The entity screamed, a sound that shattered windows up and down the street, and its form began to unravel.
[Skill Unlocked: Soul Rend — Expend harvested essence to deal damage to spiritual entities. Cost: Variable. Effect: Direct soul energy attack.]
Kael stared at the notification, heart pounding. A combat skill. Something beyond mere harvesting. The Soul Reaver wasn't just about taking from the dead—it could also weaponize what he'd taken.
The entity's form continued to disintegrate, but it wasn't finished. A fragment of its mass coalesced, drawing itself together with desperate speed, and shot toward Kael like an arrow of pure shadow.
He tried to raise his blade, but his legs wouldn't respond. The numbness from its touch had spread up his thigh, and his muscles wouldn't obey his commands. The shadow-arrow filled his vision, and for a moment he saw nothing but endless black.
Then something tackled him from the side.
Kael hit the cobblestones hard, the air driven from his lungs, as a body rolled across him and came up in a crouch. A woman—hooded cloak, practical armor, a blade that glowed with soft spiritual light. She thrust her weapon toward the approaching shadow and spoke a single word that cracked the air like thunder.
"*Dispergo.*"
The spiritual light exploded outward, scattering the shadow-arrow into a thousand fragments that dissolved into nothing. The entity's main mass convulsed, its form destabilizing further, and then it collapsed in on itself, imploding into a point of absolute darkness that vanished with a sound like tearing fabric.
Silence fell over the alley.
Kael lay on his back, staring up at the woman who had just saved his life. She turned to look at him, pushing back her hood to reveal a sharp-featured face, dark hair cut short, and eyes the color of winter steel. A scar traced her left cheek, disappearing into her hairline.
"You're the one they call the Soul Reaver," she said. Not a question.
"And you're not with the Orders," Kael replied, managing to sit up despite the lingering numbness in his leg. "Their patrol would have arrested me on sight."
"Smart." She offered him a hand. "I'm Sera. And we need to move—the Iron Vanguard will have heard that discharge. They'll be here within minutes."
Kael hesitated. Trust was a luxury he couldn't afford, not with his blood still on the cobblestones and the mark of a Soul Reaver burned into his existence. But his leg wasn't working properly, and whoever this woman was, she'd just saved him from something that had been trying to consume his soul.
He took her hand.
They ran.
Sera led him through a maze of back alleys and service tunnels, paths that clearly weren't on any official map. Kael limped behind her, dragging his unresponsive leg, while the sounds of pursuit echoed in the distance. Boots on cobblestones. Shouted orders. The clang of metal.
"How did you find me?" he asked between breaths.
"I've been tracking Abyssal activity for three months," Sera replied without slowing. "When the Veil tears, even a little, it leaves traces. I followed one such trace to your little alley confrontation."
"That thing said something about seventy-three seals. Seventy-two plus me."
Sera's pace faltered for just a moment. "What exactly did it say?"
Kael repeated the entity's words as best he remembered them—the message about the seals weakening, the Great Rift awakening, the reference to his kind rising again. By the time he finished, they'd emerged into a large underground chamber, its ceiling lost in shadow, its walls lined with strange symbols that glowed faintly in the darkness.
Sera stopped in the center of the room and turned to face him. In the pale light, he could see the tension in her shoulders, the way her hand rested on her blade.
"The Six Orders were founded to maintain the seals," she said slowly. "Each Order guards a portion of the network—twelve seals per faction. The seals have held for three hundred years, since the end of the Abyssal Wars."

It was a scout—a fragment of something larger, something trying to push through from the other side of the Veil. The fact that it could manifest at all means the nearest seal is critically weakened."
"The Great Rift," Kael said, remembering something from the entity's words. "It mentioned the Great Rift."
Sera's expression darkened. "The Great Rift is one of the original seals, one of the twelve maintained jointly by all Orders. If it breaks..." She didn't finish the sentence.
"Everyone dies."
"Everyone dies."
Kael leaned against a pillar, testing his leg. The numbness was fading, replaced by a pins-and-needles sensation that told him the damage was superficial. Whatever that creature had done to him, it hadn't been permanent.
"Why help me?" he asked. "You said you've been tracking Abyssal activity. The Orders would pay well for that information. Hell, they might pay better for a live Soul Reaver."

He hoped he was right. He feared he wasn't.
