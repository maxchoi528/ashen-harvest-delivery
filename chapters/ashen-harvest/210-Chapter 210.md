The blade found bone. Kael twisted.
His harvest knife carved a wet path through the cultist's chest, parting ribs with the efficiency of long practice. The body crumpled against the alley wall, steam hissing from the wound where the gaslight caught it. Six dead cultists lay scattered across the cobblestones—a patrol unit, not scouts. Organized. That word kept surfacing in his mind, an itch he couldn't scratch.
"Finished playing with your food?" The voice came from above.
Kael looked up.
A woman crouched on the rooftop edge, silhouetted against the belching smokestacks of the Thornwick industrial quarter. She wore the patchwork armor of a freelance hunter—leather reinforced with steel plates, the kind sold in any dockside market. Her hair was shaved on one side, the other side hanging in a greasy curtain past her shoulder. Ordinary enough. Forgettable.
Nothing about her felt ordinary.
The air around her rippled. Not spiritual energy—that came from within, coiled tight like a serpent preparing to strike. This sensation crawled along Kael's skin from the outside, a pressure that made his eyes want to close, his limbs want to go still.
*Other.*
He knew that flavor. He'd tasted it in the moment his own power awakened, felt it in the dreams that had plagued him for months afterward. The lingering taste of something fundamentally different from normal Awakened abilities.
"You're him." The woman dropped from the rooftop, landing in a crouch three feet from the nearest corpse. Her boots didn't make a sound. "The Reaver. Kael Ashborn."
Kael straightened slowly, keeping the harvest knife loose in his grip. "You have me at a disadvantage."
"Sera Blackthorn." She straightened as well, and her eyes caught the gaslight—pupils blown wide, irises ringed with silver that shouldn't exist in human physiology. "Though I suppose that name doesn't mean anything to you. It shouldn't. I've been careful."
"Been hunting me?"
"Watching you." She crouched beside the nearest cultist, fingers hovering over the cooling flesh. "Same thing, different timing. You're sloppy, Ashborn. Leaving bodies where the Mortician's Guild can find them. The Orders have a file on you thick as my arm."
The pressure intensified. Kael felt his heartbeat stutter, felt the air thicken like walking through water. His Soul Reaver senses screamed—not danger exactly, but presence. Power. A void where another human being should be.
"The hell are you?" he breathed.
Sera smiled. It didn't reach her eyes. "The same."
She touched the cultist's forehead.
The body convulsed. Soul energy—visible now, a sickly green luminescence—rippled outward from the point of contact and flowed into Sera's outstretched palm. She pulled her hand back, fingers curling closed around nothing, and the light vanished into her skin.
[System Notification: Foreign Soul Extraction Detected. Target: Sera Blackthorn. Classification: Soul Reaver. Stage: Transcendent (Advanced).]
Kael's blood went cold.
Transcendent. Advanced. That meant she'd been doing this longer than him. Harvesting longer, learning longer, feeding whatever drove her deeper into abilities that made normal Awakened look like children playing at power.
"Surprised?" Sera wiped her hand on her thigh, casual as wiping dirt from a spade. "Most are. The Orders don't exactly advertise that there are more of us."
"More of us." The words tasted like ash.
"That's right, Reaver." She turned to face him fully, and the pressure spiked—Kael felt his lungs constrict, felt his hands tremble despite his iron will. "The question is whether you're competition or a resource. I've been watching you for three weeks. You waste so much. All those abilities, bleeding out of corpses because you don't know how to extract properly. Pathetic, really."
Kael's fingers tightened on the harvest knife. "You want to teach me?"
"I want to make you an offer." Sera took a step closer. The gaslight caught her face, and Kael saw it—the faint lines around her eyes, the premature gray at her temples. Harvesting had cost her something. The abilities were free, but the body remembered. "There are six of us left. Six Reavers still breathing on this continent. We work together sometimes. Share targets. Share information."
"And when targets don't want to be shared?"
"Then we handle it." Sera's smile sharpened. "But you're still new enough that you might be useful. You haven't learned yet how deep this goes. How many people want us dead. How many would pay any price to have one of us on a leash."
The words hit like physical blows. Kael thought of the Mortician's Guild's facility, of the samples they'd tried to take from him, of the Order agents who'd tracked him across three cities. The world had always been hostile to him. He'd assumed it was because of what he could do.
He hadn't considered that others might be doing the same thing.
"The cultists," he said slowly. "They're not random. The attacks in Thornwick—"
"Organized." Sera nodded. "Cult Prime Zarek has been moving pieces for months. Maybe longer. He's trying to unseal something. I don't know what, and neither do the others. But I know where he's focusing his efforts."
She reached into her coat and produced a folded paper. Kael didn't move to take it.
"Why tell me this?"
"Because we're stronger together. Because the Orders are closing in. Because—" Sera hesitated, and for a moment her mask cracked. Something desperate flickered in those silver-ringed eyes. "Because they're accelerating. Whatever Zarek is doing, whatever's behind it, the timeline is collapsing. Weeks, maybe. Not months."
Kael studied her face. The woman was terrified. Not of him—she'd made that clear enough—but of something else. Something she'd seen coming.
"The offer," he said. "What exactly are you proposing?"
"An alliance. Temporary, until the crisis passes. I have information you need. You have..." Sera's gaze dropped to the corpses around them. "Resources I could use. The cultists in particular. Fresh kills, Awakened-level spiritual signatures. I could teach you to extract more efficiently. You could feed me targets."
"You'd harvest the skills too."
"Of course." No shame in her voice. No hesitation. "That's the arrangement. I take what I want, you take what you want. Neither of us wastes anything."
[System Notification: Proposed Alliance Detected. Terms: Information exchange, resource sharing, mutual non-interference. Duration: Until crisis resolution. Accept/Reject?]
Kael stared at the notification hovering at the edge of his vision. The System had never offered him choices before. It recorded harvests, tracked levels, catalogued abilities. It didn't negotiate.
*Something is different about this. Something is wrong.*
"Ashborn." Sera's voice cut through his thoughts. "I don't have time to stand here all night. The cult patrol will notice these bodies are missing within the hour. Either we move together or we move separately. Your choice."
"What's on the paper?"
"Locations. Cult activity over the past month, mapped and analyzed. The pattern points to a specific target: the old Sepulcher ruins beneath the Cathedral of the First Dawn. Zarek's been sending teams down there for weeks."
The Sepulcher. The word snagged in Kael's mind like a fishhook catching on bone. He'd been hearing that name for months—in whispered conversations, in fragments of cultist ravings, in the dreams that had started the night his power awakened.
*Not a coincidence. Nothing is ever a coincidence.*
"The First Dawn is a ruin," he said. "Half the district is cordoned off. Collapsed buildings, unstable ground—"
"Which is exactly why no one is watching it closely." Sera tossed the paper at his feet. "Open it if you want. Study it. Then find me at the address on the back if you decide you want answers instead of questions."
She turned to leave.
"Wait." Kael's voice stopped her. "You said six of us. Six Reavers. What happened to the others?"
Sera's shoulders tensed. She didn't turn around.
"The Order of Dawn hunted three of us to extinction two centuries ago. The Whisper King took another, and I don't mean a quick death. The sixth..." Her voice went flat. "The sixth disappeared into the Abyss during the First Sealing. No body. No remains. No way to know if he's still alive in there or if he was consumed."
"Who's the Whisper King?"
"Someone you don't want to meet." Sera looked back over her shoulder. "That's why I'm offering this alliance, Ashborn. The Whisper King is collecting. Soul Reavers. Pieces of us. He has two already, sealed and stored like wine in a cellar. He'll come for the rest of us eventually. I'd face what's coming with allies than alone."
She jumped. The rooftops of Thornwick swallowed her, darkness and steam and the distant rumble of machinery. Within seconds, she was gone.
Kael stood in the alley for a long time.
The paper lay at his feet, catching the fitful gaslight. Six bodies cooled around him—cultists who'd died without knowing why, pawns in a game they'd never understood. His harvest knife hung heavy at his hip, slick with fluids he didn't want to name.
*The same.*
He wasn't alone anymore. Somewhere in this city, five other people carried the same curse, the same power, the same hunger. They worked together sometimes. They competed when they didn't.
And something was hunting them.
He bent and picked up the paper. Unfolded it with hands that only trembled slightly.
A map. Hand-drawn, precise, annotated with dates and symbols he didn't recognize. Cult activity in Thornwick over the past thirty days, marked in red ink. And there—a pattern. A constellation of violence leading toward the old Cathedral district.
The Sepulcher.
Kael committed the location to memory, then set the paper on fire. The flames consumed it in seconds, leaving only ash on the cobblestones. He ground the ash beneath his heel and started walking.
---
The Undercroft beneath the old Cathedral was supposed to be collapsed. Sealed. Unstable.
Someone had broken through anyway.
Kael found the entrance in a drainage tunnel that should have been blocked by three feet of rubble. The stones had been moved recently—mortared edges still fresh, tool marks visible in the torchlight. He counted seven different boot prints in the accumulated dust, sizes and weights suggesting men and women in heavy armor.
*Cultists. Organized.*
He descended.
The stairs wound downward in a spiral that felt older than Thornwick itself, carved from stone that predated the First Empire by centuries. Kael's footsteps echoed strangely, returning to his ears at intervals that didn't match his pace. The air grew colder with each step, not the cold of winter but something deeper. Spiritual. The chill of a place where the Veil had grown thin.
His Soul Reaver senses opened wider than they ever had before.
The Undercroft wasn't empty. It pulsed with residual energy—not fresh, not decaying, but locked. Frozen. Sleeping. Layers of spiritual pressure pressed against his mind like fingers testing a door, curious and hungry and far older than anything human.
*72 sealed rifts.*
This was one of them. Or the approach to one.
Kael pressed forward.
The tunnel opened into a chamber that stole his breath.
Vaulted ceilings soared overhead, lost in darkness. Columns ringed the space—twelve of them, each carved with symbols that seemed to shift when viewed directly. At the center stood an altar, black stone veined with something that glowed faint silver in the darkness. And on the altar—
"Ashborn."
Zarek's voice echoed from everywhere and nowhere.
Kael spun, harvest knife raised. The chamber had been empty. He'd checked. He'd been certain—
"There is no need for that." A figure detached from the shadows behind the central altar, robed in gray, face hidden beneath a hood that revealed nothing but the glitter of eyes like wet coal. "I expected you eventually. The others talk too much when they're frightened."
"Cult Prime." Kael kept his blade ready. "I've been looking for you."
"And now you've found me. How fortunate." Zarek stepped into the light, and Kael saw what the hood concealed: a face burned and scarred beyond recognition, features melted into something that was almost but not human. "Though I wonder if you understand what you've found."
"The Sepulcher. The original Soul Reaver."
Zarek laughed. The sound scraped against the stone walls, multiplying until it seemed like a chorus of demons was laughing along with him.
"The original Soul Reaver." Zarek shook his head, movements almost gentle. "You know nothing, boy. The first Soul Reaver didn't create this power. He stole it. From something that was here long before humanity built its first fire. Long before the 72 Entities. Long before the Abyss itself."
Kael's grip tightened on the knife. "You're lying."
"Am I?" Zarek spread his arms wide, gesturing at the chamber around them. "Feel it. This place. The pressure. The hunger. You've sensed it since you descended, haven't you? Something waiting. Something watching. Something that has been sleeping for so long that even its dreams have become geological."
The cold intensified. Kael felt it now—a presence at the edge of perception, vast and patient and utterly alien. It wasn't an Entity. It wasn't anything he had words for.
"What do you want?" he demanded.
"What I've always wanted." Zarek's ruined face twisted into something that might have been a smile. "The seal network is failing. The Veil grows thin. In fifty years—twenty, maybe less—the Abyss will breach. The Entities will pour through. And in that chaos, this—" He touched the altar. "This will wake. And it will take what was stolen."
"The Soul Reaver power."
"The Soul Reaver power is a fragment. A splinter of what sleeps here. The original wielder took it and fled, leaving us with only scraps of what could have been." Zarek's eyes glittered. "But fragments can be reunited. Pieces can be brought back together. You carry one of those pieces, Ashborn. And I have spent decades collecting the others."
Kael felt ice crystallize in his stomach. "You're hunting Reavers."
"Hunting is such an ugly word. I prefer... Gathering." Zarek reached into his robe and produced a glass vial, no larger than his thumb, filled with swirling silver light. "Two already. Tamed. Sealed. Waiting. Two more in the world. And now you, stumbling into my chamber like a moth to flame."
"You're insane."
"Maybe." Zarek corked the vial and returned it to his robes. "But I will be alive when the end comes. When this thing wakes and remakes the world. And so will you, Ashborn—if you join me. The alternative is simple. Pain. Extraction. The same fate as every Reaver who refused."
Kael's mind raced. Two Reavers sealed. Zarek's forces everywhere. The cult growing bolder. The seals failing faster.
And somewhere in this city, Sera Blackthorn was preparing to fight a war she didn't know had already been lost.
"I'll never join you."
"I know." Zarek's smile widened. "That's why I called the others."
Shadows moved at the chamber's edge. Six figures emerged from the darkness—cultists in gray robes, hands crackling with spiritual energy. Awakened-level power, every one of them. Coordinated. Trained.
Kael had walked into a trap.
"Last chance, Reaver." Zarek backed toward the altar. "Join willingly, or be harvested against your will. The result will be the same. Only the flow differs."
Kael dropped into a fighting stance.
The first cultist lunged.
---
They came at him in waves.
Kael met the first with his harvest knife, catching the blade on his own and twisting. Bone cracked. The cultist screamed and fell. Kael pressed forward, using the body as a shield against the two behind it. His free hand found flesh—warm, living, not yet cold—and he pulled.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 847 units of spiritual energy from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired.]
The energy flooded through him, erasing exhaustion, sharpening his senses. He threw the body aside and pivoted, ducking beneath a strike that would have taken his head off. His blade found ribs, slipped between them, opened the cultist's chest from sternum to spine.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 923 units of spiritual energy from the remains. Skill Fragment acquired: Bloodletting Lunge.]
More energy. More power. Kael felt his reserves swelling, felt his body responding with strength that wasn't entirely his own. The fight was brutal and fast and more than he could win alone.
*Six of them. Six cultists. All Awakened.*
Zarek watched from behind the altar, safe in the shadows. Not fighting. Not fleeing.
Waiting.
The fourth cultist caught Kael with a palm-strike that drove the air from his lungs. He staggered, gasped, felt hands grabbing at his arms. A blade bit into his side—shallow, but bleeding. The fifth cultist raised a serrated knife for the killing stroke.
The cold presence in the chamber pulsed.
Kael moved.
Not his own will—something older, something that had been sleeping in the back of his mind since the moment his power awakened. The Soul Reaver ability expanded beyond harvesting. Beyond extraction. It reached out and touched the spiritual energy of the cultist raising the knife, and it pulled.
The man screamed. His eyes rolled back. His knife clattered to the stone floor as his body seized, convulsed, collapsed. Spiritual energy—raw, unrefined, stolen directly from a living source—poured into Kael's frame.
[System Notification: Unstable Harvest Detected. Target: Living Awakened. Extraction: 1,247 units. WARNING: Physical backlash imminent.]
Kael staggered.
His vision blurred. His hands shook. The backlash hit like a hammer to the skull—pain, disorientation, the sensation of something fundamental being twisted out of shape. He'd never harvested from the living before. He'd been told it was possible.
He'd been wrong about how it would feel.
"Impressive." Zarek's voice cut through the agony. "Most new Reavers never learn to touch living souls. You might be worth capturing intact."
The surviving cultists circled. Two left now. They'd pulled back, faces pale beneath their hoods. They'd seen what Kael had done. They were afraid.
They should be.
Kael straightened, ignoring the blood streaming from his nose. "Your move, Zarek."
"I think not." The Cult Prime stepped forward, and the temperature in the chamber dropped twenty degrees. "You have exhausted yourself, boy. The unstable harvest will cripple you within minutes. Your reserves are draining faster than you can compensate."
He was right. Kael felt it—the backlash spreading through his system, unraveling the threads of his spiritual foundation. He'd pushed too hard. Taken too much.
*Stupid. Arrogant. You thought you were stronger than you are.*
The sixth cultist lunged.
Kael's vision exploded into white.
---
He woke on the stone floor, tasting copper and ash.
The chamber was silent. Empty. The bodies of the cultists lay where they'd fallen—six forms scattered around the altar like discarded puppets. Kael pushed himself upright, ignoring the screaming protest of every muscle in his body, and looked at his hands.
Blood. His own blood, mixed with something else. Something that glowed faintly silver where it had dried on his skin.
"What..." He stopped. Swallowed. Tried again. "What happened?"
A notification waited at the edge of his vision.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 3,847 units of spiritual energy from the remains. Skill Acquired: Aura of Sanctity. Corpse freshness: Optimal. Extraction quality: Exceptional.]
Aura of Sanctity. Kael tested the ability instinctively, and light bloomed around him—not visible light, but spiritual. A field of repulsive energy that pushed against hostile intent, against malevolent spiritual pressure, against anything that sought to harm him through supernatural means.
*Protection. Pure protection. The ability to make himself untouchable to hostile forces.*
He laughed. It came out broken, wet with something that might have been blood. "You bastard. You absolute bastard."
Zarek was gone. The altar stood empty, the silver veins in its surface dark and dormant. Whatever the Cult Prime had planned, it hadn't included dying here. He'd let Kael fight. Let him harvest. Let him break himself against enemies who were never the real threat.
*He wanted me to get stronger.*
The thought was sickening. Zarek had watched him kill six Awakened in a confined space, watched him learn to harvest from the living, watched him extract an ability that should have been impossible at his stage. And then he'd... Left.
Why?
Kael limped toward the chamber's edge, toward the stairs that led back to the surface. His body screamed at every step. The backlash from the living harvest was still working through his system, and he could feel the damage—cracked spiritual pathways, depleted reserves, a tremor in his hands that wouldn't stop.
But he was alive. And he was stronger.
*Two Reavers sealed. Two more in the world. And now me.*
Sera's words echoed in his mind. Six Reavers total. Zarek had already captured two. The Whisper King had taken a third. One had vanished into the Abyss.
That left two. Him and Sera.
*The alliance. She wanted to warn me. She knew something was coming.*
But she didn't know how deep it went. She didn't know about the Sepulcher, about the thing sleeping beneath Thornwick, about Zarek's plan to reunite the fragments of the original Soul Reaver's stolen power.
The stairs stretched upward into darkness. Kael climbed.
He emerged into the drainage tunnel just as dawn light began to filter through the rubble at its entrance. The industrial district was waking—steam whistles, distant shouts, the rumble of early-morning freight carts. Thornwick continued its rhythms, oblivious to what had happened beneath its streets.
Kael found a niche in the rubble and collapsed into it, back against stone, eyes on the tunnel mouth. His body needed rest. His spirit needed time to heal. But he couldn't stop thinking.
*The Six Orders exist. They fight the Abyss. I knew that.*
*The 72 sealed rifts exist. I suspected it.*
*Cult activity is organized. I've seen the evidence.*
*The Shadow Court is watching me. Sera mentioned them. Zarek too.*
Information. New pieces falling into place. The conspiracy he'd stumbled into—it was bigger than he'd imagined. More dangerous. And he had allies. Or potential allies. Or threats wearing the mask of allies.
Sera Blackthorn. The address on the back of her paper. The offer of alliance.
*Find me when you decide you want answers.*
He had the address. He had the map. He had Aura of Sanctity, a skill that would make him harder to kill.
But he also had questions. Questions that had no answers. Questions that might get him killed if he pursued them.
Zarek wanted to reunite the Soul Reaver fragments. The Whisper King was collecting Reavers. The cult was accelerating its timeline. And beneath Thornwick, something ancient was waking up.
Kael closed his eyes.
The tunnel remained silent. The morning sounds continued outside. Normal. Peaceful.
Then he heard footsteps.
Slow. Measured. Coming from the direction of the surface entrance.
Kael's hand found his harvest knife. He pressed himself deeper into the shadows, controlling his breathing, letting the new Aura of Sanctity wrap around him like a cloak.
The footsteps stopped at the tunnel mouth.
A figure stood silhouetted against the dawn light. Tall. Broad-shouldered. Wearing the gray robes of a cultist, but the posture was wrong. Too military. Too controlled.
"You survived." A man's voice, cold and familiar. "Impressive. Zarek said you would, but I had my doubts."
Kael didn't answer. He waited.
The figure stepped into the tunnel.
The light caught his face, and Kael felt ice crystallize in his veins. He knew that face. He'd seen it in reports, in wanted posters, in the nightmares that had plagued him since his awakening.
The Shadow Court's Emissary. The man they'd sent to kill him in Greyhaven. The man who had failed and disappeared.
"Hello, Kael Ashborn." The Emissary smiled, and there was nothing human in it. "The Court has questions for you. And I'm afraid they require... Detailed answers."
Behind Kael, in the deeper darkness of the tunnel, something else began to move.
Kael looked left. Looked right.
Trapped.
