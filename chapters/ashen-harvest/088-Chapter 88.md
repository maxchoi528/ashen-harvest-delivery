Blood dripped from Kael's blade onto the rain-slick cobblestones. Behind him, two cultists lay motionless in the alley's shadows, their breathing shallow, their mission incomplete.
The message in his pocket crinkled—Lyra's latest intelligence report, describing patterns of disappearances in the Ashwood district. Organized. Methodical. Whatever Zarek's cultists were hunting, they weren't acting alone.
"Move, Ashborn." The Iron Vanguard patrol would pass this street in four minutes. Kael sheathed his blade and slipped through a gap in the brickwork, landing in the courtyard of an abandoned textile mill. His new base needed supplies, information, and something more—a way to tip the scales against enemies who numbered in the dozens while he remained a single blade.
The Ashwood Wendigo had been spotted three nights running near the old growth forest's edge. Predators like that attracted cultists seeking power. Kael could use that. The creature's territory sat between two active cult compounds, and anything that cleared the path for him served double purpose.
He climbed through the mill's broken window, checking the rooftop vantage point he'd marked earlier. The fog rolling in from the harbor would mask his approach through the forest. A perfect night for hunting.
Or being hunted.
The Ashwood forest loomed at Thornwick's eastern boundary, where gaslight gave way to true darkness. Ancient oaks twisted toward the sky, their branches like skeletal fingers clutching at the mist. Kael moved beneath them, his footsteps silent on the carpet of dead leaves. His perception stretched outward, reading the spiritual residue that clung to everything living and dead.
Three distinct trails crossed near the forest's heart. Two he recognized as cultist signatures—cold, calculating, tinged with Abyssal corruption. The third made his breath catch.
Wendigo. Stage Two, maybe Stage Three. The spiritual pressure radiating from its trail pressed against his senses like a hand around his throat. This wasn't the mindless beast the city watch described in their bulletins. Whatever lived in these woods carried enough power to challenge a full Order patrol.
Kael adjusted his course, following the creature's path than approaching directly. He'd learned that lesson in the Mirehollow swamps—predators expected prey to run. Making them question whether he was prey at all required a different approach.
The forest grew quieter as he moved deeper. Birds fell silent. Insects stopped their chittering. The absence of sound pressed against his ears until he could hear his own heartbeat, steady and patient.
Ahead, through a gap in the trees, candlelight flickered.
Kael dropped to a crouch, pulling himself behind a fallen log. The light came from a clearing where five figures in gray robes knelt in a circle. Their chanting rose and fell in patterns that scraped against his spiritual senses. At the circle's center, a shallow pit held something that pulsed with sickly green luminescence.
A summoning circle. The cultists were trying to call something through.
He counted heads, measured distances, calculated angles. Five cultists, two of them Awakened based on their spiritual signatures. The third... Something else moved in the shadows beyond the circle's edge. Watching. Waiting.
The Wendigo.
It stood upright despite its name, seven feet of wiry muscle and bone, covered in pale hide stretched over an emaciated frame. Its antlers branched into deadly points, and its face—humanoid but wrong—snapped toward the trees where Kael hid.
One of the cultists turned, staff raised. "The forest knows you, intruder. Show yourself, or the Huntmaster will find you anyway."
Kael's fingers tightened on his blade. They knew he was here. The mist had been too thick, the approach too obvious. They'd been waiting.
The largest cultist—a woman with ritual scars covering her bald scalp—stepped forward. "The Whisper King will be pleased. Another Reaver walks into our hands."
Ice flooded Kael's veins. They knew what he was. They'd expected him.
The scarred woman smiled, teeth filed to points. "Did you think we wouldn't notice you harvesting our dead? Every soul you take leaves a trail. Every trail leads here."
Kael's mind raced through options. Five against one, with a Wendigo watching from the shadows. The cultists had prepared this ground. The pit at their center pulsed faster, feeding spiritual energy into some mechanism he couldn't identify.
"You're stalling," the scarred woman observed. "Trying to read our abilities. Don't bother." She raised her staff, and runes along its length flared with crimson light. "I am Sister Vex, Third Voice of the Ashwood Conclave. You will tell us where you learned to Reave, or the Huntmaster will pull your soul from your living body."
The Wendigo—Huntmaster—stepped forward, its antlers catching the candlelight. Kael could see now that its eyes held intelligence. Calculation. It wasn't a pet or a summoned creature. It was an ally, or maybe a partner in this trap.
"One more time," Sister Vex said. "Where did the Reaver come from? Who created you?"
The question struck Kael like a physical blow. He'd asked himself that same thing since waking on the execution platform. The power had been there, embedded in his soul like it had always belonged. No origin he could remember. No teacher. Just the ability, sudden and complete and terrifying.
"I don't know," he said, and meant it.
Sister Vex's eyes narrowed. "Lie to me, Reaver, and I will—"
"Ask him yourself, then." The voice came from behind the cultists, from the darkness beyond the candlelight. A figure emerged from the trees, tall and gaunt, wearing robes that seemed to drink the light. A mask covered his face—pale porcelain with features too smooth, too blank.
The Whisper King.
Kael's body tried to move, to run, to fight, but something held him in place. The same pressure he'd felt in the Mirehollow, when the Shadow Court had cornered him. Spiritual suppression, far stronger than anything he'd encountered.
The masked figure stopped beside Sister Vex, who immediately dropped to one knee. "Rise, Third Voice. This matter requires my direct attention."
"Yes, Your Whisper." She stood, stepping back into the circle.
The Whisper King turned toward Kael, and behind that featureless mask, Kael felt attention like a blade against his skin. "You are remarkably adaptable for one so young in your power. The Shadow Court underestimated you. The Order of Dawn dismissed you. Even my own people thought you."
The Chinese caught Kael off guard. A test? A taunt? The Whisper King's fingers—too long, too pale—traced patterns in the air, and Kael's body locked completely. He couldn't move. Couldn't speak. Could barely breathe.
"Ashborn Kael," the Whisper King said, his voice like dry leaves scraping stone. "Death-row prisoner. Soul Reaver. Ascendant stage at an age when most struggle to Awaken. You harvested skills from a Thousand-Faced Dancer, survived the Mirehollow, and continue to accumulate power at a rate that should be impossible."
Kael's lungs burned. The pressure intensified, and he felt his vision darkening at the edges.
"Tell me, child." The Whisper King leaned closer. "When did you first know you could Reave? What triggered the awakening? Memory is currency in my world. The price of your continued existence is information."
Blood dripped from Kael's nose. His spiritual energy churned against the suppression, but the Whisper King's power was absolute. This wasn't Stage Two or Stage Three. This was something else entirely—Sovereign at minimum, possibly Demigod.
The Wendigo moved closer, sniffing at Kael's feet. Its breath came in rattling gasps, and Kael caught fragments of its surface thoughts—hunger, confusion, something that might have been recognition. It knew what he was. The hunt had been real, but the trap had been bait.
"You resist," the Whisper King observed. "Fascinating. Most break within seconds of—"
The Huntmaster's head snapped toward the forest's edge. A new presence touched Kael's senses—familiar, achingly familiar. Spiritual energy that sang in harmonics he knew from somewhere deep in his soul.
Lyra stepped into the clearing, her twin blades drawn, her face a mask of cold fury. "Release him."
The Whisper King turned slowly. "Ah. The Shadow Court's fallen knight. I wondered when you would arrive."
"Third Voice Vex raised her staff. "My King, she is—"
"Infected with something I do not yet understand." The Whisper King's mask tilted, studying Lyra. "You carry fragments of the True Names, knight. Pieces of power that do not belong to you. How... Inconvenient."
Lyra's grip tightened on her blades. "I said release him."
"Or what? You will fight? You are one. We are many." The Whisper King gestured, and three more cultists emerged from the trees behind him. Kael's perception counted six Awakened in total, plus the Huntmaster, plus the masked figure whose power dwarfed them all.
This was suicide. Kael screamed against the pressure holding him, his spiritual energy finally breaking through enough to move his fingers, just his fingers.
Lyra smiled. It was the expression of someone who had stopped caring about consequences. "You want to know about his awakening? I'll show you something better." She reached into her coat and pulled out a vial filled with swirling darkness. Soul-essence, concentrated, powerful, worth more than most noble houses.
The Whisper King went very still. "Where did you get that?"
"From a friend." Lyra uncorked the vial. "The Mortician's Guild has been very generous since they learned what happened to their shipment. They want it back, along with the Shadow Court operative who stole it."
She drank the essence in one swallow.
The effect was immediate. Spiritual energy exploded outward from Lyra, her realm spiking upward in a cascade Kael could barely track. Ascendant. Transcendent. Sovereign. The power hung in the air between them like a physical presence, wild and unstable and absolutely lethal.
"This is temporary," Lyra said, her voice resonating with harmonics that made the Wendigo whine in pain. "Thirty seconds, maybe less. Enough time to kill everyone here, and then I die too." She raised her blades. "But I won't die alone."
Sister Vex's composure cracked. "Your Whisper, she cannot—"
"I can." Lyra moved.
The clearing erupted into chaos. Kael felt the suppression release as the Whisper King pivoted to face this new threat. His body dropped forward, catching himself on the log, gasping for air.
Lyra was a blur of steel and power. The first cultist died before Kael registered her movement, blade through the throat. The second managed to raise his arm before she bisected him at the waist. Sister Vex threw up a barrier of crimson light, and Lyra shattered it with a single strike, the shockwave sending the Third Voice flying into a tree.
The Wendigo charged. Lyra spun, blades catching its antlers, leveraging its momentum to slam it into the ground. It scrambled up, faster than anything that size should move, claws raking toward her face.
Kael's fingers found his own blade. Twenty seconds, maybe less.
He moved. Not toward the Whisper King—that would be suicide even with Lyra's distraction. Instead, he sprinted for the summoning pit at the circle's center. Whatever those cultists had been trying to summon, disrupting it might change the battlefield.
The pit's contents pulsed with sickly green light. Kael's perception plunged inward, and he saw what lay beneath—something waiting on the other side of reality, pressing against the membrane between worlds. A fragment of something larger, something that would pour through if the ritual completed.
He brought his blade down into the pit.
The effect was immediate and violent. Spiritual energy discharged in a cascade of green fire, and Kael felt the summoning collapse in on itself. Something screamed on the other side—rage, hunger, frustration—and then was gone.
A system notification flickered in his vision:
[Soul Harvest: Ritual disruption detected. Residual spiritual energy absorbed. +15 Spiritual Energy.]
[Acquired: Fragment of Abyssal Resonance (Passive: +5% resistance to summoning effects)]
Kael staggered back from the smoking pit. Behind him, the battle raged. Lyra had killed three more cultists, but her power was fading visibly. Her movements grew sluggish. Her strikes came slower.
The Whisper King stood untouched in the center of the carnage, watching, waiting.
"Fascinating," the masked figure said. "You truly would die for him. The irony of your situation is not lost on me, knight. Once, you served the very organization hunting him. Now you burn yourself alive to save—"
Lyra's blades stopped moving. She dropped to one knee, power guttering, the Sovereign-stage presence collapsing back to her true realm. Blood ran from her nose, her ears, her eyes. Soul depletion, accelerated by the essence she'd consumed.
The Whisper King raised one pale hand. "Kill them both. Keep the knight alive—she carries information I need. The Reaver..."
The Wendigo lunged toward Kael, antlers lowered.
Time compressed. Kael saw the creature's bulk filling his vision. Saw Lyra's broken form behind him. Saw the cultists closing in from all sides. Saw the Whisper King's mask reflecting the firelight.
His Soul Reaver stirred.
Not the ability to harvest—something else. A fragment of knowledge buried in his soul since the beginning, since his first awakening, since he woke up on the execution platform with power that shouldn't exist.
The True Name of the Wendigo.
It came to him like memory recalled from someone else's life. Three syllables that meant nothing and everything, sounds that resonated with the creature's true nature. The Wendigo was not just an animal. It had been something else once, someone else, transformed by hunger and Abyssal corruption into what it now became.
Hunta mortis vox. Huntmaster of the Dead Voice.
Kael spoke the Name.
The effect was immediate. The Wendigo's charge stuttered, its body convulsing as Kael's will overrode its instincts. The creature's eyes—intelligent, calculating—met Kael's own. Recognition flickered there. Confusion. A plea for something Kael couldn't identify.
"Your True Name," Kael said, the words burning his throat with power he didn't understand. "You are HUNTA MORTIS VOX. You were someone once. Someone who chose this path."
The Wendigo screamed—a sound that tore through the clearing and sent the remaining cultists scrambling backward. The Huntmaster fell to its knees, antlers drooping, powerful limbs trembling.
Behind his mask, the Whisper King went very still. "Impossible."
Lyra raised her head, blood dripping from her chin. "Kael... What did you..."
"I don't know." Kael's own nose bled freely now, the True Name costing him something he couldn't measure. "But it worked."
The Wendigo—the Huntmaster of the Dead Voice—looked up at him with eyes that held the ghost of whoever it had been before corruption claimed it. "Help... Me..."
One word, dragged up from beneath layers of hunger and madness and Abyssal taint. A plea. A request. A soul still fighting to be free.
Kael's hand moved before his mind could stop it. He pressed his palm against the Wendigo's forehead, feeling the creature's spiritual signature beneath his touch. The Soul Reaver stirred again, and this time it didn't just offer to harvest.
It offered to save.
The cost would be enormous. Extracting a soul from Abyssal corruption required spiritual energy beyond anything Kael currently possessed. It would leave him empty, drained, possibly dead.
But a True Name wasn't just power. It was responsibility. It was choice.
Kael activated the Reaver.
[Soul Harvest: True Name resonance detected. Soul extraction initiated...]
[Extracting: Huntmaster of the Dead Voice (Corrupted)]
[Warning: Soul corruption detected. Purification attempt in progress...]
[Soul Harvest INTERRUPTED: Insufficient spiritual energy to complete extraction.]
[Partial harvest achieved: +20 Spiritual Energy, +10 Awakened Vitality.]
[Acquired: Fragment of the Huntmaster's Memory (Incomplete)]
[New Ability Unlocked: Name's Burden (Passive: Can speak the True Names of corrupted beings. Each use drains 30% current spiritual energy.)]
The Wendigo collapsed, corruption writhing beneath its skin as the partial extraction disrupted the Abyssal hold. It wasn't free—not completely—but the thing inside it had been wounded. Driven back. Weakened.
Sister Vex's screamed orders barely registered as cultists surrounded them. Kael dropped to his knees beside Lyra, his spiritual reserves scraped nearly empty.
The Whisper King stepped forward through the carnage, robes pristine despite the blood splattering the clearing. "Remarkable. You carry a piece of the original. I had suspected, but to witness it..."
His mask tilted toward Kael's face.
"You do not know what you are, do you? What you carry? What you might become?" The pale hand reached toward him. "I could teach you. Show you the path your power was always meant to follow. Join me, and the secrets of the Soul Reaver will be yours. Refuse..."
Kael spat blood into the grass. "Refuse, and you kill me. Got it."
"Eventually. But there are fates worse than death for those who carry such power and refuse to use it wisely." The Whisper King withdrew his hand. "I will give you one week. One week to consider my offer, to learn what you truly possess. Then I will return, and you will give me an answer."
He turned to Sister Vex. "The knight lives. The Reaver walks free—for now. Kill the Wendigo if it survives its corruption. I have what I came for."
"The Ashwood Conclave will not—"
"Will follow my commands, as always." The Whisper King walked into the darkness between the trees. "One week, Ashborn Kael. Use it wisely."
The cultists melted into the forest, leaving Kael and Lyra alone beside the Wendigo's trembling form. The creature's breathing steadied slowly, its corruption fighting against whatever Kael had disrupted.
Lyra's hand found his arm. "You... You spoke its True Name. You shouldn't be able to do that."
"Neither should I be able to Reave." Kael slumped forward, exhaustion dragging at every muscle. "Apparently I do a lot of things I shouldn't."
"Fragments of the original," Lyra murmured. "He meant you carry a piece of whoever created the Soul Reaver ability. A memory. A legacy."
Kael looked at his hands, still trembling from the Name's drain. The system notification glowed faintly in his vision, confirming what he'd done. What he'd become capable of.
A new ability. One that cost him thirty percent of his spiritual energy just to speak a monster's name.
One week. One week to figure out what he carried, what it meant, and why the Whisper King wanted him badly enough to let him walk away.
Lyra pulled herself upright, swaying. "Can you walk?"
"Can you?"
"Define walk."
Kael almost laughed. "Define almost dying in a forest surrounded by cultists."
They supported each other as they moved toward the clearing's edge. Behind them, the Huntmaster of the Dead Voice stirred, its breathing steadier now, its eyes following their retreat with something that might have been gratitude.
Or hunger waiting to return.
The forest path stretched ahead, dark and uncertain. Kael's perception stretched behind him, tracking the Wendigo's corrupted signature, feeling the way the Abyssal corruption still clung to it despite his interference.
One week.
Enough time to heal. Enough time to plan. Enough time to search for answers about what he carried in his soul.
Not nearly enough time to prepare for what came next.
A sound reached them from the darkness—footsteps, dozens of them, moving through the underbrush with military precision. The Iron Vanguard patrol, finally arriving. Too late to help. Exactly on time to complicate everything.
Kael pulled Lyra deeper into the shadows as torchlight flickered between the trees.
The hunt wasn't over. It was just beginning.
