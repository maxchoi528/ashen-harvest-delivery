The Abyssal Entity avatar lunged from the smoke.
Kael pivoted, his blade carving an arc through corrupted flesh. The thing had no face—just a tear in reality shaped like a screaming mouth. He ducked under its grasping limbs and drove his sword through its center mass. The avatar dissolved into black mist, but two more poured through the breach behind it.
"Fall back to the second line!" Commander Voss's voice cut through the chaos. The Iron Vanguard captain stood on a pile of rubble, her steel armor scorched and dented. Around her, a dozen Awakened held a thin barricade against the tide pouring from the rift.
Kael didn't fall back. He ran toward the breach.
Three hours ago, the rift had torn open in the middle of Thornwick's eastern district. No warning, no cultist ritual—just reality splitting like rotten cloth. The entity's avatar had stepped through before anyone could react. Now the city burned, and the Six Orders' combined response had pushed the creature back to its point of entry, but they couldn't close the wound.
Kael had been fighting for two hours straight. His Sovereign reserves burned hot, a furnace in his chest that never seemed to cool. The Soul Reaver mark on his left arm pulsed with each kill, hungry, patient, waiting.
Another avatar surged toward him. This one wore the shape of a dead Iron Vanguard soldier, its movements jerky and wrong. Kael met its charge, caught its grasping hands, and pushed. The thing's head twisted at an impossible angle, jaw unhinging to reveal a throat full of writhing tendrils.
He dropped beneath its lunge and drove his palm against its chest.
*Soul Harvest: Initiated.*
The connection blazed to life. Power flooded through him—not spiritual energy, not yet, but raw information. He felt the entity's hunger, its desperate need to breach fully into the Material. He tasted its confusion, its rage at being pushed back by creatures it considered barely sentient.
The avatar went limp. Kael dropped the corpse and kept moving.
[Souil Harvest: Extracted Fragment of Void-Touch. Corrupted skill acquired — requires purification before use.]
The notification flickered across his vision. Corrupted skills were useless until he could refine them, and refinement required time he didn't have.
"Kael!" Sera's voice rang out. The Mortician's Guild healer stood behind a collapsed wall, her hands glowing with restoration magic. Blood matted her silver hair. A cultist lay dead at her feet. "The breach is widening!"
He looked. She was right. The tear in reality pulsed like a wound, edges fraying. The entity on the other side pushed harder, forcing more of itself through.
Around him, the battle raged. Iron Vanguard soldiers formed a shield wall against waves of lesser avatars. Order of Dawn paladins sang hymns that burned corruption from the air. Arcane Spire mages hurled fire and lightning into the breach. Even the Veiled Circle had sent representatives, their shadow-stepping assassins harrying the entity's attempts to solidify its presence.
But they were losing. He could see it in the way the shield wall buckled, in the desperation of the paladins' hymns, in the exhaustion slowing the mages' gestures.
The Six Orders were here—all of them, fighting together for the first time in decades—and they were being overwhelmed by a single entity's avatar.
Kael charged the breach.
"What are you doing?" someone screamed. Commander Voss, maybe. It didn't matter.
The entity saw him coming. Its attention snapped toward him like a predator noticing movement, and the pressure from the breach intensified. Avatars that had been targeting the shield wall turned toward Kael, drawn by something it sensed in him.
Good. Let it focus on him.
He dodged the first avatar's claws, sliced through its throat, harvested its essence in a brutal burst of contact. The power tasted like copper and ash.
[Souil Harvest: Extracted 12 units of spiritual essence. Partial skill acquired: Void Step (basic).]
Another avatar. Kael caught its arm, twisted, used its momentum to throw it into a third. The collision bought him two seconds. He spent one reaching the breach's edge and the other driving his blade into the entity's exposed form.
The thing screamed. Not with sound—with meaning. Its voice existed in the space between thoughts, bypassing ears entirely.
**YOU CARRY THE MARK. THE OLD THIEF'S BANE.**
Kael didn't know what that meant, but his Soul Reaver ability surged in response. The connection between him and the entity blazed hot, and he could see it—really see it—through the harvesting link.
The entity was massive. Its true form dwarfed the rift, pressed against the membrane between worlds like a hand against a window. What he'd been fighting were fingers probing through the gap. And it was old. Older than the Orders. Older than the Concordat. Its consciousness stretched back through millennia of hunger and patience.
It had been waiting for something.
**THE FIRST REAVER TRIED TO SEAL US. HE FAILED. HIS TOOL LIVES ON IN YOU.**
The harvesting link snapped shut. Kael staggered back, head pounding with borrowed memories he couldn't interpret. Flashes of a great battle. A man standing before a rift, power radiating from his body. A price paid. A bargain struck.
"Kael!" Sera appeared beside him, hauling him away from the breach. "The mages are preparing a sealing ritual. We need to fall back to the safe zone."
"What's happening?" He could barely think through the static in his mind.
"The entity is trying to force its full manifestation through. If it succeeds, the whole district collapses into the Abyss." Her voice cracked. "Including us."
The battle had reached its crisis point. Avatars poured from the breach in waves now, more than the Orders could handle. The shield wall broke. Paladins fell with prayers still on their lips. Mages' spells guttered out as their reserves depleted.
Kael looked at the breach. Looked at the entity pressing against reality. Looked at the fallen Awakened scattered across the battlefield—his potential harvests, waiting.
He couldn't save everyone. But he could do something.
"Get to the safe zone," he told Sera. "I'm going to close the rift."
"That's suicide. You can't—"
"Trust me." He met her eyes. "I've done this before."
She hesitated, then nodded. "Don't die. We need you for the Tablets."
She ran. Kael turned back to the breach.
The entity sensed his intention. Its attention locked onto him with terrible focus, and all the avatars between them moved as one—a wall of corrupted flesh advancing in perfect unison.
Kael smiled. "Come on then."
He activated Void Step.
The world shifted. He slipped between spaces, not moving through the avatars but around them, his Sovereign-enhanced spatial awareness showing him the gaps between their bodies. He emerged at the breach's edge with three heartbeats to spare.
The entity's full attention crashed against his mind like a tidal wave.
**YOU CANNOT SEAL ME. NO MORTAL CAN.**
"Never said I was mortal." Kael placed both hands against the rift's edge.
The Soul Reaver mark blazed white-hot. The harvesting link he'd forged earlier hadn't been a one-time connection—it had been a tether. Now he pulled, dragging the entity's attention inward, forcing its consciousness to focus on him alone.
The entity resisted. Its power was immense, drawing on reserves that dwarfed his own. But it was also confined, pressed against the membrane, unable to bring its full might to bear.
And he had something it didn't expect.
He began to harvest.
Not from an avatar. Not from a fragment. From the entity itself—reaching through the rift, touching its true form, pulling essence directly from a being that should have been beyond his reach.
**WHAT ARE YOU DOING?**
"Taking what you're owed."
The harvest was different from anything he'd experienced. Normally, touching a corpse gave him fragments of the dead—skills, memories, essence. The entity was alive, bound to the Abyss but present, and harvesting from it felt like trying to drain an ocean through a straw.
But he had time. The avatars had stopped advancing, their connection to the entity disrupted by his assault. The Orders rallied, pressing the advantage, pushing the fragments back.
And inside the harvest, he found something unexpected.
Information. The entity's knowledge, bleeding through the connection like poison through a wound. It showed him the rift network—seventy-two tears in reality, each containing an Abyssal Entity. It showed him how they were connected, how weakening one affected the others. It showed him the cultivation rituals the cults used to weaken the seals, preparing for a mass unsealing that would shatter the membrane entirely.
It showed him the timeline. Two weeks.
[Souil Harvest: Extracted 847 units of spiritual essence from partial entity contact. Warning: Target too powerful for complete harvest. Partial corruption acquired. Tolerance: 23%.]
The entity screamed in his mind.
**YOU WILL PAY FOR THIS. THE COURT WILL FIND YOU. THEY WILL BREAK YOU APART AND SCATTER YOUR ESSENCE ACROSS THE ABYSS.**
Kael grinned through the pain. The harvesting link burned his consciousness, but he held on, pulling more and more from the entity's reserves.
The rift shuddered. The membrane began to repair itself, the entity's pressure against reality weakening as its strength bled away.
"Not today," Kael growled.
He released the harvest and slammed his palms against the rift's edges. The Soul Reaver mark flared, and for a moment he felt the original Reaver's technique flowing through him—the method used to seal the first rift, the method that had supposedly been lost.
It hurt. Oh, it hurt more than anything he'd ever experienced. The seal magic burned through his reserves, scorched his spiritual pathways, threatened to tear his soul apart.
But it worked.
The rift contracted. The entity's form bulged against the closing wound, fighting desperately to maintain its grip on the Material. Kael pushed harder, drawing on every drop of Sovereign power he possessed, channeling it through a technique he shouldn't know, couldn't know, but did.
The rift shrank to a pinprick.
The entity's final scream tore through his mind.
**THIS IS NOT OVER, REAVER. THE COURT REMEMBERS. THE COURT WAITS.**
Then it was gone. The rift sealed, leaving only scarred reality and the smell of ozone.
Kael collapsed.
He woke to the sound of voices. Medical mages hovered over him, their hands glowing with diagnostic light. His body ached with the deep, bone-level exhaustion of spiritual depletion.
But he was alive.
"He should have died." That was Commander Voss, her voice tight with disbelief. "The seal technique alone should have killed a Sovereign."
"The entity was weakened by the harvest." A new voice, calm and analytical. Kael forced his eyes open. A woman in Arcane Spire robes stood at the foot of his bed, her face hidden behind a mask of green glass. "It lost enough power trying to manifest that his technique could finish what the Orders started."
"The Orders didn't start anything." Sera's voice, sharp with anger. "Kael closed the rift. We just watched."
"The rift closure is what matters." The Arcane Spire mage leaned forward. "Who are you, Kael Ashborn? And where did you learn that technique?"
He considered the question. The harvesting had left traces in his mind—fragments of the original Reaver's memories, incomplete but illuminating. He knew things now that he shouldn't.
"The Soul Reaver's journal mentioned it," he said. Not entirely a lie. "I extrapolated."
"Extrapolated a lost sealing technique from a dead man's diary." The mage's tone made it clear she didn't believe him. "How convenient."
"Enough." Commander Voss stepped between them. "He's spent. If the Council wants to interrogate him, they can wait until he's recovered."
The mage held Voss's gaze for a long moment, then nodded. "The Council will be in contact. My superiors are... Interested in Mr. Ashborn's unique talents."
She left. The medical mages followed.
Sera stayed. She waited until they were alone, then sat on the edge of his bed.
"The entity mentioned a Court," she said quietly. "And it said you carry the Old Thief's Bane. What did it mean?"
Kael stared at the ceiling. The harvested memories swirled in his mind, incomplete pictures of a past he was only beginning to understand.
"The original Soul Reaver," he said. "He tried to seal the entities. All of them. The technique I used... It was his. And it seems they remember him."
"And you?"
"I carry his mark." He held up his left arm, where the Soul Reaver symbol glowed faintly beneath his skin. "They think I'm him. Or that I belong to them. Either way, we're going to have problems."
"The Shadow Court."
"What?"
"The entity mentioned the Court. It's not a physical organization—it's a conspiracy. Spies, assassins, information brokers. They serve no Order and all of them." Sera's expression darkened. "And they've been monitoring you since you arrived in Thornwick."
That was new information. Kael filed it away.
"What else did you learn?" Sera asked. "During the harvest."
He hesitated. The entity's knowledge burned in his mind—the network of rifts, the cult activity, the timeline for the mass unsealing. Information that could change the war. Information that made him a target.
But he also knew things about the Soul Reaver's past now. Fragments. The original owner of his power had been something more than human, something that had bargained with the Abyss itself and lost. His fate remained a mystery, but Kael had glimpsed the shape of it.
"Enough to know we're running out of time." He pushed himself upright, ignoring the protest of his depleted body. "The cults aren't random. They're organized. Someone's coordinating them across all the cities, weakening the seals for a coordinated assault."
"The Great Rift." Sera's face went pale. "You mean they're going to—"
"Not just the Great Rift. All of them. Seventy-two rifts, all opening at once." He met her eyes. "Two weeks. We have two weeks to stop it."
The weight of that knowledge hung between them. Seventy-two entities, all breaking free at the same time. The Orders would be overwhelmed. The seal network would collapse. Reality itself would tear apart.
Unless someone stopped it.
Kael swung his legs off the bed. His body screamed in protest, but he forced himself to stand. The Soul Reaver mark pulsed with new power—he'd harvested more essence from that entity than from any dozen corpses combined.
He was still Sovereign. The breakthrough to Demigod remained distant, blocked by walls he couldn't yet breach. But he was stronger now. Wiser. The harvested knowledge had given him insight into his abilities that he was only beginning to understand.
"Sera. I need you to do something for me."
"Anything."
"The Sepulcher Tablets. The ones in the Mortician's Guild vault." He watched her expression carefully. "I need access to them. Now."
Her eyes widened. "That's forbidden. Only Guild Masters can—"
"The entity showed me things. About the original Soul Reaver, about what he was trying to do. The Tablets might contain information we need. Information about the seals, about the entities, about how to stop what's coming."
Sera was silent for a long moment. Then she nodded.
"There's a viewing chamber in the lower vault. I can get you in after hours, when the guards change shifts." She stood, her expression hardening with resolve. "But Kael—if the Guild Masters find out, we'll both be executed. Not by the city. By the Orders."
"Then don't tell them."
"I'm trusting you." Her voice cracked slightly. "Don't make me regret it."
She left. Kael stood alone in the medical ward, surrounded by the sounds of healing magic and distant battle cleanup. Through the window, he could see Thornwick's eastern district—the burned buildings, the collapsed streets, the rift site now sealed but still visible as a scar on reality.
The entity had been wrong about one thing. The Soul Reaver hadn't failed to seal the entities. He'd succeeded, temporarily. He'd held them back for centuries while the Orders built their defenses and the seal network stabilized.
But he'd paid a price. Kael could feel it in the harvested memories—fragments of a bargain, a sacrifice, a choice that had destroyed the original owner of his power.
The entity had called him the Old Thief's Bane. It had called him a tool of the first Reaver. It had promised that the Court would find him.
Kael smiled. Let them come.
The Soul Reaver mark pulsed on his arm, hungry and patient. He had two weeks to find a way to stop seventy-two entities from breaking free. He had a conspiracy of Shadow Court spies monitoring his every move. He had Orders that wanted to interrogate him, execute him, or use him—preferably all three.
But he also had something no one else possessed: the ability to harvest power from the dead and the dying. The ability to take what others had and make it his own. The ability to fight the Abyss with its own weapons.
He walked to the window and looked out at the damaged city.
The war had begun in earnest. The cults were organized. The entities were stirring. The seals were failing.
And somewhere in the shadows, the Shadow Court was watching.
Waiting to see what he would do next.
A sound echoed from the corridor outside—footsteps that didn't belong to medical staff. Measured. Deliberate. The tread of someone who knew exactly where they were going.
Kael turned toward the door as it opened.
A man stood in the threshold. Tall, gaunt, wearing the black robes of a Shadow Court operative. His face was hidden by a hood, but his voice was calm, almost friendly.
"Kael Ashborn. The Soul Reaver." The man stepped inside, and the door swung shut behind him. "The Court has been waiting a very long time to meet you properly."
Kael's hand moved to his sword. The man's smile was visible even in shadow.
"I wouldn't draw that blade if I were you. Not because I'm dangerous—I'm not—but because the woman standing behind you with a knife to your healer's throat would be forced to act." The man spread his hands. "I'm here to offer you a choice, Reaver. Join us, and you'll have access to everything you need to stop the unsealing. Resources. Information. Power."
"And if I refuse?"
"Then you die here, and the entities break free anyway, and everyone you care about is consumed by the Abyss." The man's tone didn't change. "The Court's offer isn't altruism. We need you functional. The original Soul Reaver made certain that no one else could replicate his abilities—you're the only one who can harvest from an entity directly. That makes you invaluable."
Kael's mind raced. Sera. They had Sera. If he fought, she died. If he submitted, he became a puppet. Neither option was acceptable.
But there was a third option, wasn't there? There was always a third option.
"Tell me about the Court," Kael said, keeping his voice level. "Tell me who you really serve."
The man's smile widened. "We serve the outcome that preserves humanity, Reaver. Not the Orders, not the entities, not any single faction. We serve survival." He reached into his robes and withdrew a small vial of glowing liquid. "Soul-essence. Pure. Untainted. Worth more than you can imagine. Consider it a gesture of good faith."
He set the vial on the table beside Kael's bed.
"The Court will be in touch. Think carefully about your choice." The man turned toward the door, then paused. "Oh, and Kael? Your healer is safe—for now. But the woman holding the knife to her throat is a Shadow Adept. She can kill before you could draw breath to scream."
He opened the door and stepped through.
The footsteps faded down the corridor.
Kael stood motionless for a long moment, then reached for the vial. Soul-essence. Enough to restore his depleted reserves three times over. A bribe or a test—probably both.
He pocketed it anyway.
Sera was still alive. The Court wanted him functional. They needed him, which meant they couldn't afford to kill him just yet.
But the equation would change the moment he became more liability than asset.
Kael moved to the window again, checking the angle of descent. Three stories to the street below. Survivable, if his body cooperated.
He needed to get to the Sepulcher Tablets. He needed to understand what the original Soul Reaver had done, what secrets the Court was hiding, what the cult's mass unsealing truly meant.
And he needed to do it before the Shadow Court decided he wasn't worth the trouble.
The footsteps had stopped. Somewhere in the hospital, Sera was being held at knifepoint by a woman trained to kill before Kael could react. Somewhere in the city, cultists were preparing their coordinated assault. Somewhere beyond the Veil, seventy-two entities pressed against their prisons, waiting for the moment when the seals would finally shatter.
The war had begun.
And Kael Ashborn, Soul Reaver, had just become the most valuable piece on the board.
He just had to survive long enough to play it.
