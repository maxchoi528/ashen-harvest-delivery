The Gray Clerk wasn't just watching the seals. He was studying them. Learning their language.

Movement flickered at the triangle's center.

And Ashworth had been Kael's cover identity for the past three months.

One daughter dead. Another still alive somewhere in a Mortician's facility, according to Sera. Zarek's network had touched more families than Kael had imagined.

Partnership or trap. Kael couldn't tell yet. But the information he'd gained was worth the risk.

He needed that knowledge. Whatever the cost.

The tomb. The hidden line allowed for hints about the original Soul Reaver's fate, but the location had remained a mystery. Kael had assumed it was lost. Sealed away by the Orders who feared what a second Soul Reaver could accomplish.

The Gray Clerk wanted his harvesting abilities. Mira wanted answers about her brother. The cult wanted the seal modifications reversed or exploited. Zarek wanted him dead or captured. And somewhere in Thornwick, the Shadow Court watched from the shadows they had learned to call home.
Kael needed an edge. Something that tipped the balance in his favor.
The answer lay in the Gray Clerk's archives—and in the cultists who had absorbed Entity energy, whose essence the old man wanted harvested for the seal network.
Fresh corpses. Specific targets. The most dangerous game in Thornwick.
He would need to hunt.
