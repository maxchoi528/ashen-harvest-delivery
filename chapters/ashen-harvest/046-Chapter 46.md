Rain lashed against the cobblestones. Kael pressed his back against the alley wall, breath shallow. Two Order of Dawn patrol officers rounded the corner ahead, their ceremonial armor gleaming despite the downpour.
He waited. They passed. Kael moved deeper into the alley, his hands shaking. The Soul Reaver within him stirred, hungry.
The city of Thornwick was a maze, and Kael knew its hidden paths. He'd spent weeks learning the back alleys, the forgotten tunnels, the places where the Order didn't patrol. His Awakened senses picked up the spiritual signatures of the patrols—six officers in this sector alone, all radiating the cold precision of trained killers.
He paused at a junction. Left led to the merchant district, where the lights were bright and the crowds offered cover. Right led to the Undercroft, the abandoned temple district where even the Order feared to tread.
Kael went right.
The Undercroft swallowed him whole. Crumbling spires loomed overhead, their stone faces eroded by centuries of neglect. The streets here were narrow, choked with debris and the skeletal remains of market stalls. No lanterns burned in this district—the Order had removed them decades ago, claiming the spiritual interference from the old temples made lighting impractical.
Practicality had nothing to do with it. The Undercroft was where the city kept its problems. Refugees. Criminals. Those who had awakened without the Order's blessing. Those whose souls carried the taint of the Abyss.
Kael fit all those categories.
A sound echoed behind him—boots on stone, the clatter of armor. The patrol had doubled back. Kael quickened his pace, slipping through a gap in a collapsed wall that he had memorized during his first night in the district. The gap led to a drainage tunnel, and the tunnel led to the old catacombs beneath the temple district.
He crawled through the darkness, his breath loud in the confined space. The stone was slick with moisture and something else—a faint, oily residue that clung to his fingers. Spiritual residue. Someone had performed rituals here recently, channeling power through the catacombs' ancient channels.
The tunnel opened into a burial chamber. Niches lined the walls, each one containing the skeletal remains of some long-dead citizen of Thornwick. The Order had stopped maintaining these catacombs generations ago, leaving the bodies to decay in peace.
Kael didn't have that option. His soul ached for sustenance, the hollow ache that came from days without proper harvest. The Soul Reaver was never satisfied—it demanded more, always more, and ignoring its hunger made the ability harder to channel when he needed it most.
He approached the nearest niche and pressed his palm against the skull within. The bone was dry, ancient, carrying only traces of spiritual residue. The harvest was minimal.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 1 unit of degraded soul-essence from ancient remains. Spiritual energy acquired.]

Kael moved deeper into the catacombs, searching for fresher remains. The burial chamber gave way to a network of tunnels, each one branching into smaller passages. He followed the spiritual residue, hoping it would lead him to recent activity—and recent activity meant recent deaths.

Bodies. A dozen of them, arranged in a circle at the chamber's center. They wore the robes of Abyssal cultists, their faces twisted in expressions of agony. But the wounds that had killed them weren't from combat—they were ritualistic, precise, carved into the flesh with surgical accuracy.
Someone had harvested these cultists. Recently. Very recently.
Kael stepped into the chamber, his eyes scanning the darkness for threats. The spiritual residue here was thick, layered, a palimpsest of recent activity. Someone had come here, killed the cultists, and harvested their souls using methods that left no spiritual trace.
No natural methods, anyway.

[Soul Harvest: You extracted 5 units of medium-grade soul-essence from freshly killed cultist remains. Spiritual energy acquired. Skill fragment acquired: Shadow Step (basic).]

And someone had left it for him.
"Who's there?" Kael's voice echoed through the chamber. No response. He waited, listening to the silence, but the only sounds were the drip of water and the distant rumble of the city's steam engines.
A trap, maybe. Or a gift from someone who wanted him to succeed. Neither option sat well with him.
Kael continued through the catacombs, harvesting what he could from the remaining bodies. Each cultist yielded something different—fragments of knowledge about the seal network, hints about the cult's operations, snippets of memory that painted a picture of growing desperation within their ranks.
The seals were failing. Not all at once, but in increments, small tears in the Veil that the cultists were widening with every ritual. The Abyss pressed against the cracks, and the Entities within it sensed weakness.

Zarek spoke of the seals often, praising their inevitable collapse, promising that the Entities would return and reshape the world.

Kael would find Zarek. Eventually. But first, he needed to survive the night.

Patrol. The Order had anticipated his route.

The third option was to run.

Kael didn't wait to see if they survived. He crossed the remaining distance and disappeared into the industrial district, letting the steam and smoke obscure his trail.
The Order would find those patrol officers eventually—assuming the river didn't claim them first. The river was treacherous, full of whirlpools and undertow, and the chemicals that filled it burned skin on contact. The Order trained their officers for spiritual combat, not survival in toxic sludge.

Kael cared. A little. Enough to regret what he'd become, even as he embraced the power that let him survive.

The Orders thought the bloodline was extinct. Then you walked out of your own execution, and the whole world remembered that the Soul Reaver's legacy was supposed to end in blood."

The Soul Reaver had chosen him. But why? And what had happened to the Reavers who came before?

Not your abilities—your abilities are yours alone. Just the essence you extract, the fragments you collect. We pay market rate for everything you provide."
