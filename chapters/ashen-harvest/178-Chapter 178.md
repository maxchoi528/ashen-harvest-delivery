Cold iron bit into Kael's palms as he pressed himself against the alley wall. Two heartbeats ahead, boots scraped against cobblestone.
"Contact," a voice hissed. Cultist. Kael's fingers found the familiar weight of a soul-essence vial tucked into his coat—last night's harvest, still warm against his chest. He didn't reach for it.
Instead, he moved.
The first cultist dropped with a wet gurgle when Kael's blade found the gap between his ribs. The second spun, hand already weaving a sign toward the Veil. Too slow. Kael's elbow cracked against the man's temple, driving him into the wall. A palm slap to the forehead finished the job.
[System Notification: Soul Harvest attempted. Target: Unknown Cultist. Quality: Moderate. Energy extracted: 12 essence units. No compatible skills detected.]
Kael crouched over the bodies, counting seconds. The Iron Vanguard patrol would pass this alley in ninety seconds. Less, if someone had talked.
A hand landed on his shoulder.
"You're getting sloppy."
Sera's voice cut through the fog of combat. She stood three paces back, arms crossed, her scarred face unreadable in the gaslight. The Mortician's Guild sigil on her collar caught the dim glow.
"They weren't expecting me." Kael wiped his blade on the dead man's coat.
"They're never expecting you. That's not the problem." She stepped closer, heels clicking on the wet stones. "The problem is you left witnesses at the Dockside cellar. Six dead, all bearing the Abyssal mark. The Watch is asking questions."
"The Watch can choke on—"
"The Watch found a survivor. A dockworker. He saw you emerge from the cellar with blood on your hands and corpse dust on your coat."
Kael's stomach tightened. He remembered the cellar. The ritual circle. The cultists in the middle of binding something that screamed without sound. He'd killed them all, harvested the strongest, and walked out through a wall that shouldn't have existed.
"Survivors are inconvenient," he said.
"Survivors become informants." Sera's dark eyes held his. "The informant is currently in Iron Vanguard custody. He's scheduled for interview at dawn. If he describes you accurately, every Order in Thornwick will know a Soul Reaver is operating in their city."
The boots grew louder. Fifty meters now. Forty.
"I need that witness."
"Already handled." Sera produced a small vial from her sleeve, its contents swirling like liquid smoke. "Truthbinder. Three drops in his wine, and he'll remember seeing a tall woman with red hair. Very cooperative. Very confused about the details."
Kael took the vial. "And if he still talks?"
"Then we move to phase two." Her smile didn't reach her eyes. "The Watch doesn't investigate what they can't report. Accidents happen."
She turned to leave, then paused.
"There's something else. A name I've been hearing from my contacts." The word came out wrapped in ice: "Zarek."
The alley seemed to narrow. Kael's grip on his blade tightened until his knuckles ached.
"Prime Zarek," Sera continued, not looking at him. "He arrived in Thornwick three days ago. He's been meeting with someone inside the Mortician's Guild. Someone high-ranking. I don't have a face yet, but the description matches—"
"Doesn't matter." Kael's voice came out flatter than intended. "I'll find him."
"You'll get yourself killed." Sera finally turned, and for the first time, her composure cracked. "He survived the Sealbreaker. Whatever he did to himself, he's not operating at human limits anymore. Your Soul Reaver ability might work on cultist trash, but Zarek—"
"Sera." Kael stepped forward until he could see the pulse jumping in her throat. "He destroyed my life. He killed everyone I cared about and made it look like I did it. He turned a city of strangers into a mob baying for my blood." His voice dropped to something quiet and dangerous. "Nothing short of his soul in my hands will satisfy me."
The Iron Vanguard patrol rounded the corner. Thirty meters.
Sera's expression shuttered. "The informant's cell is in the Iron Quarter. Wardenhall processing center. I'll create a distraction at the eastern gate in one hour. After that, you're on your own."
She melted into the shadows before Kael could respond.
---
The Iron Quarter spread across Thornwick's western edge like a festering wound. Kael had crossed it a dozen times in the three months since his resurrection, always following blood trails or whispers or the faint shimmer of abyssal corruption. Tonight, the corruption was everywhere.
Old brick buildings leaned against each other like drunks. Sewage trickled through channels in the street, carrying the smell of copper and decay. Somewhere above, a woman screamed—short, sharp, then nothing.
Kael moved through the maze of alleys with practiced ease. He'd mapped every shortcut, every rooftop escape, every place where a man could disappear for an hour or a lifetime. The Soul Reaver ability hummed in his chest, not demanding action but reminding him it was there. Always there. Waiting.
Wardenhall materialized out of the gloom—a squat stone building with barred windows and a single iron door. Two Iron Vanguard soldiers flanked the entrance, their armor dulled by grime and neglect. Behind them, the building exhaled the stale air of cells and interrogation chambers.
Kael circled to the eastern wall. Sera's distraction should have started by now. He counted heartbeats.
A crash echoed from the gate. Shouting. The soldiers exchanged glances, then one broke formation and sprinted toward the noise.
One left.
Kael dropped from the rooftop. His landing was silent—the Transcendent stage had refined his body until his movements flowed like water. The remaining soldier never heard him approach. A precise strike to the base of the skull, and the man crumpled.
Kael dragged the body into the shadows and took his keys.
The interior of Wardenhall smelled of mildew and old fear. Kael moved through corridors lit by sputtering gas lamps, counting doors. Sera's intelligence placed the informant in cell block C, third door on the left. The layout matched.
He reached the cell block. The door was reinforced iron, but the lock was old. Kael worked the picks Sera had given him—a lifetime ago, it seemed—and the mechanism clicked open.
The cell was empty.
Not abandoned-empty. Stripped-empty. The cot had been made with military precision. The bucket was clean. A half-eaten meal sat on a tray by the door, the bread still soft.
Footsteps behind him.
"Looking for someone?"
Kael spun. A man stood at the end of the corridor, dressed in the black coat of a Shadow Court operative. His face was hidden behind a porcelain mask, featureless except for the painted smile.
"You're early," the man continued, his voice carrying the echo of distance. "The transfer isn't for another six hours."
Kael's blade cleared its sheath. "Who are you?"
"A concerned citizen." The masked figure tilted his head. "One who knows what you are, Kael Ashborn. What you can do. What you're looking for."
The word hung in the air like smoke.
"You followed me."
"I've been following you for weeks. Ever since you surfaced from your own grave and started collecting souls like trophies." The man spread his hands. "Impressive work, really. The Dockside cellar. The Warehouse Seven incident. The Mortician's Guild ambush last month. You've become the problem for the Orders."
"Talk faster."
"The Shadow Court has an offer for you." The masked man stepped closer, unafraid of the blade aimed at his chest. "Zarek is more than a cult leader. He's a project. The Orders have been feeding him resources for decades, trying to create something that could break the seal network and flood the Material with abyssal energy. They failed. He succeeded—beyond their wildest projections. Now he's become a threat even they can't control."
"Zarek is my kill."
"Zarek is beyond killing. At least, beyond any killing you could manage with conventional means." The man reached into his coat and produced a small leather journal. "This belonged to an Awakened named Mira Thorne. She was studying Zarek's transformation before the cult discovered her. She documented everything—including his weakness."
Kael didn't move. "Why would the Shadow Court help me?"
"Because Zarek has become inconvenient. He's accelerating the timeline. Twelve of the seventy-two seals have already been compromised. If he succeeds in breaking the remaining sixty, the Material and the Abyss will merge completely. No more separation. No more Veil. Just raw spiritual chaos, and Zarek sitting on a throne of entities."
"And the Shadow Court cares about this because...?"
"We exist because the Orders exist. If the Orders fall, we fall with them. Self-preservation, Kael. It's the only honest religion left."
The masked man extended the journal. Kael's fingers closed around it before his mind finished processing the decision.
"One more thing," the Shadow Court operative said, retreating into the darkness of the corridor. "The witness you came to silence—his name is Aldric Venn. He works for someone inside the Mortician's Guild. Someone who has been feeding cult movements to Zarek for the better part of a year."
"Who?"
"Find out yourself. That's the fun of investigation." The masked figure's painted smile seemed to widen. "Or —the horror of it. The Mortician's Guild is full of secrets. Some of them have been buried for generations."
He vanished around the corner. Kael heard a door open, slam shut, and then nothing but silence.
The journal was slim, bound in cracked leather that flaked under his touch. Kael flipped it open to the first page. The handwriting was cramped, precise—a scholar's script.
*Subject Z displays anomalous soul composition. Standard harvesting techniques produce no effect. His spiritual signature registers as dual-natured: one half human, one half Entity-class. Further study suggests he has merged his soul with something from beyond the Veil—not bound it, not controlled it, but integrated it into his being.*
*If my calculations are correct, a soul-reaver could theoretically extract the Entity fragment. But doing so would require physical contact with the subject during a moment of spiritual resonance—typically during combat, when the soul-body connection is strained.*
*In layman's terms: you can't harvest Zarek unless he's already dying.*
Kael closed the journal and slipped it into his coat.
The corridor stretched ahead, empty and cold. Somewhere in this building, Aldric Venn was waiting to identify him. Somewhere in this city, Zarek was planning to break the seals and unmake reality. And somewhere in the Mortician's Guild, a traitor was selling secrets to the enemy.
The Soul Reaver hummed in Kael's chest.
---
He found the real cell twenty minutes later, hidden behind a false wall in the basement. The witness was a wiry man in his forties, trembling with cold and fear. His eyes went wide when Kael entered.
"You," he whispered. "You're the one from the docks."
"Names have power." Kael crouched to meet his eyes. "Give me yours, and I'll decide whether you keep it."
"Aldric. Aldric Venn." The man's voice cracked. "Please, I haven't told them anything. I didn't see enough. I didn't—"
"Who hired you to watch the cellar?"
Aldric's face crumpled. "I don't know his real name. He wore a Mortician's coat. He said it was routine surveillance. He said—"
"His name, Aldric."
"Dorian. Dorian Kaine. He's an under-broker for the Guild. He handles soul-essence acquisitions in the eastern districts." The words tumbled out in a desperate flood. "He paid me fifty sovereigns to watch the cellar and report any activity. Fifty sovereigns! I thought it was just business! I didn't know it was cult business!"
Kael's hand closed around the man's collar.
"Where is Dorian Kaine now?"
"The Veiled Market. He lives above his shop in the Undercity. But please—please don't kill me. I have a family. I have—"
Kael released him. "You were dead the moment you took that money. The only question was whether you died tonight or next week."
He left Aldric Venn sobbing in the cell and climbed back toward the surface.
---
The Veiled Market sprawled beneath Thornwick's streets like a second city. Kael had visited it twice before—once to buy information, once to sell a harvested ability he couldn't use. Both times, he'd felt the eyes of a hundred criminals tracking his movements, calculating whether he was prey or predator.
Tonight felt different.
The underground market was quieter than usual. Stalls stood empty, their proprietors clustered in whispered groups. The air carried an edge of tension, the kind that preceded violence or change.
Kael found Dorian Kaine's shop in the Market's eastern quarter—a cramped storefront wedged between an apothecary and a weapons dealer. The windows were shuttered. A sign hanging crooked on rusted hinges read: *Kaine & Associates. Soul Acquisitions & Dispositions.*
He didn't bother with the door. Kicked it open and strode inside.
The shop was a single room cluttered with ledgers, sample vials, and the faint smell of preservatives. A staircase at the back led upward. Kael took it two steps at a time.
The apartment above was empty.
But not abandoned. A bed with rumpled sheets. A half-eaten meal on the table. A coat draped over a chair. Someone had left in a hurry.
Kael's eyes landed on the wall.
Writings covered every surface—not graffiti, but careful inscriptions in what looked like blood. Symbols he recognized from the cult's ritual circles. Warnings. Names. A web of connections that made his head pound.
In the center of it all, a single word:
*WHISPER*
Kael's fingers brushed the wall, tracing the lines of the inscription. The blood was fresh. Days old at most.
A sound below. The front door creaking open.
Kael moved to the stairs, silent as a ghost. Below, in the dim light of the shop, a figure stood surveying the wreckage of the broken door. A woman, dressed in traveling clothes, her silver hair pulled back in a severe knot. Her hand rested on the hilt of a sword that gleamed with faint spiritual energy.
"Looking for Dorian too?" she asked the room.
Kael descended the stairs, blade ready.
The woman looked up. Her eyes were ice-blue, ancient, and utterly without fear.
"Kael Ashborn," she said. "The Soul Reaver who crawled out of his own grave. I've heard the stories."
"And you are?"
"Someone who wants Zarek dead as badly as you do." She released her sword hilt and spread her hands. "My name is Mira Thorne. And before you ask—no, I wasn't supposed to be dead."
The journal in Kael's coat felt heavier.
Mira Thorne—the scholar who'd documented Zarek's transformation. The one whose journal the Shadow Court operative had given him. She was supposed to be dead, killed by the cult for her research.
But here she stood, very much alive, in the apartment of a man who'd been feeding information to the enemy.
"Start talking," Kael said. "Everything. Now."
Mira's smile was thin and cold.
"I'd show you." She reached into her coat and produced a small device—a clockwork mechanism wrapped in copper wire. "Dorian Kaine isn't just a traitor. He's a key. And tonight, the lock is about to open."
"What lock?"
Mira's eyes flicked toward the ceiling. Toward Thornwick above, its streets and towers and the millions of souls sleeping in ignorance.
"The first of the seventy-two," she said. "Zarek isn't just breaking seals. He's using them as fuel. Each seal he breaks adds power to the next. Chain reaction. By dawn, there won't be a Veil left."
She pressed something on the device. It began to tick.
"Three hours," Mira said. "That's how long we have before the first rift opens. Before the entities pour through. Before everything we know burns."
Kael's grip on his blade tightened.
"Show me."
---
They moved through the Undercity like shadows, following passages that didn't appear on any map. Mira knew them all—every tunnel, every forgotten sewer, every hidden stair that led deeper into the earth. She'd been mapping the underground for years, cataloging the damage the cult had done to Thornwick's foundations.
"The Mortician's Guild built this city on a nexus," she explained, her voice echoing off damp stone walls. "A convergence point where the Veil is naturally thin. The founders knew. They used it to fuel the Awakened population, to create more cultivators than anywhere else on the continent. But they also created a vulnerability."
"Zarek found it."
"Zarek exploited it." Mira ducked under a low archway. "The Guild has been covering up the damage for decades. Selling soul-essence harvested from the seal-weakening to the highest bidder. They're the ones who funded Zarek's research. They're the reason he's become what he is."
The tunnel opened into a vast cavern. Kael stopped, his breath catching.
The space was cathedral-sized, carved from living rock and lit by a thousand flickering torches. At its center stood a pillar of black stone, easily fifty feet tall, covered in symbols that pulsed with sickly light. Around it, a dozen figures in cultist robes moved in slow, synchronized patterns.
And standing at the pillar's base, arms raised toward the ceiling, was Zarek.
He looked wrong. His body seemed to flicker between solid and shadow, human features bleeding into something with too many angles. His voice rose in a chant that made Kael's teeth ache.
"The seal," Mira whispered. "The Ironheart Seal. One of the original twelve. If he breaks it..."
"He breaks them all."
Kael's hand found the journal in his coat. *Physical contact during spiritual resonance.* The words burned in his memory.
"I need to get closer."
"You'll be killed before you take ten steps. There are at least twenty cultists between us and him, and Zarek himself is—"
"Untouchable. I know." Kael's eyes swept the cavern, calculating angles, distances, the placement of torches and bodies. "But there's another way."
He pointed to the pillar itself. A series of ledges ran up its surface, barely wide enough for a foot. The cultists focused on their ritual, their attention locked on Zarek and the growing crack in the seal above.
"The pillar?"
"Zarek is drawing power from the seal. If I can touch the stone while he's connected, I can follow the energy back to its source." Kael's jaw tightened. "I can harvest the seal itself."
Mira stared at him. "That's insane. No one has ever—"
"I didn't crawl out of my grave by being sensible." Kael shed his coat and handed it to her, along with the journal. "If this goes wrong, get this to Sera. Tell her—"
"Tell her yourself." Mira grabbed his arm. "You're not dying in a hole, Ashborn. Not after everything it took to get you here."
"Then give me a reason to live."
She hesitated. Then reached into her coat and pressed something cold into his palm. A ring, silver, inscribed with symbols that hummed against his skin.
"Dorian's," she said. "It's a key. If you survive, it opens a vault beneath the Mortician's Guild. Everything they've hidden for a hundred years is in there. Use it wisely."
Kael closed his fingers around the ring.
"Stay alive," he said. Then he moved.
The climb was agony. Each ledge held barely two toes. Kael's muscles screamed as he pulled himself upward, progress measured in inches. Below, the cultists continued their chant, oblivious. Zarek's voice rose and fell, tearing at reality itself.
Twenty feet up. Thirty.
The crack in the seal widened, a wound in the air through which Kael glimpsed something vast and hungry. His stomach lurched. The sensation of falling into that void nearly cost him his grip.
Forty feet. The pillar's surface thrummed beneath his fingers, alive with stolen power.
Forty-five.
Zarek's voice peaked. Light erupted from the seal, blinding even through closed eyes. The cultists fell to their knees, mouths open in silent worship.
Kael lunged.
His palm slapped against the black stone—and the world inverted.
He was no longer in the cavern. He stood in a void of infinite darkness, lit by a single point of light directly ahead. The light was the seal, pulsing like a wounded heart.
And standing between him and it was Zarek.
"You're here." The cult leader's voice echoed without source. His form was clearer here, sharper—human features rendered in shadow, eyes that burned like dying stars. "I've been waiting."
Kael drew his blade. "Then stop waiting."
"The Soul Reaver ability." Zarek's smile was a wound in the darkness. "Do you know what it truly is? Where it came from?"
"Fodder for later."
"It was mine. Once." The words fell like stones into still water. "Before I transcended humanity. Before I merged with the Abyss. I was like you. Hungry. Ruthless. Taking what I needed from the fallen. But I wanted more."
The void shifted. Kael felt pressure building behind his eyes.
"I found a tomb," Zarek continued. "Deep beneath this city. The tomb of the first Soul Reaver. I took his power. I took his knowledge. And then I realized—the ability wasn't a gift. It was a cage. Every soul you harvest, every power you claim, chains you tighter to the world of flesh and blood. The only way to break free..."
He spread his arms. Behind him, the seal pulsed.
"...is to become something else entirely."
Kael lunged.
The blade passed through shadow and struck nothing. Zarek laughed—a sound like breaking glass.
"You can't kill me here, Reaver. Not while I'm connected to the Abyss. But you can join me. Take my hand. Let me show you what waits beyond the Veil. Power beyond imagination. Freedom from the endless hunger."
The pressure built. Kael's head pounded. His vision fractured, showing him glimpses of a realm of pure energy, populated by beings of light and hunger.
*Join*, something whispered. *Take. Consume. Become.*
His hand rose toward Zarek's outstretched fingers.
The ring in his pocket burned against his thigh. Sera's face flashed through his mind. Mira's warning. The millions of souls sleeping above, unaware of the void rising to swallow them.
Kael's hand stopped.
"No."
He drove his blade into the seal itself.
The void screamed. Light erupted in every direction, searing through Kael's eyes and ears and skin. He felt the seal's energy rush into him—not as power, but as pain, a thousand voices crying out in agony as their prison cracked wider.
[System Notification: Soul Harvest. Target: Ironheart Seal. Quality: Legendary. Energy extracted: UNMEASURABLE. Warning: spiritual capacity exceeded. Warning: physical degradation imminent.]
Kael's body convulsed. His vision whited out. Somewhere far away, he felt himself falling.
And then—silence.
He opened his eyes to darkness. Cold stone beneath his back. The smell of blood and burning.
The cavern had collapsed. Rubble filled the space where the pillar had stood. Torches guttered in the sudden draft. The cultists were gone—buried or fled.
No sign of Zarek.
Kael tried to move. Pain shot through every nerve. His hands shook uncontrollably. Blood leaked from his nose and ears.
"Ashborn!"
Mira's face appeared above him, pale with shock.
"I saw the light," she said. "The whole cavern shook. What happened? Where's Zarek?"
Kael opened his mouth. No sound came out. He pointed weakly at the rubble.
"The seal?"
He shook his head. The seal was gone. He'd harvested it—or absorbed it—leaving nothing behind.
"The cultists?"
He didn't know. Didn't care.
Mira hauled him upright. His legs buckled; she caught him, bearing most of his weight.
"We need to move. If the seal is truly gone, others will come. Orders. Zarek's survivors. Everyone who wants what's left."
Kael nodded. The world tilted dangerously. He clutched the ring in his pocket, feeling its edges bite into his palm.
Above them, somewhere in the distance, a sound rose—low at first, then building.
Not thunder.
Not wind.
The unmistakable roar of something tearing through the Veil.
"Is that—" Mira's voice broke.
Kael closed his eyes. His body screamed for rest. His soul felt stretched to breaking.
But his mind was clear.
The seal was broken. Something was coming through. And he had just become powerful enough—broken enough—tomaybe stop it.
Or die trying.
"One hour," he rasped. "Give me one hour."
Mira stared at him. "You can barely stand."
"Then help me stand."
The roar grew louder. Dust rained from the cavern ceiling.
Somewhere above, in the city of Thornwick, bells began to ring.
