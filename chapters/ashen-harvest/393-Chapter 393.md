The ground split open with a sound like tearing metal. Kael stumbled back as fissures raced through the cobblestones, glowing with sickly violet light.
"What the hell—" He grabbed a nearby wall for balance as another tremor shook the street. The old stone crumbled beneath his fingers, dust raining down into his eyes.
Above him, the sky had turned the color of a bruise. Not natural dusk. Not any storm he'd ever seen. The clouds swirled in patterns that hurt to look at, spiraling around a point directly over the old cathedral district.
The notification materialized in his vision, cold and precise:
[ALERT: Proximity to unstable rift signature. Recommend evacuation.]
No kidding. Kael dropped to one knee as a crack of spiritual energy ripped through the air, so intense his teeth ached. Across the square, three figures in tattered robes raised their hands toward the heavens, chanting in that horrible, guttural language that made his skin crawl.
Cultists. Here. Now.
He counted them. Four, five, six. Too many for a direct assault, but he wasn't built for direct assaults anyway. Kael pulled his coat tighter and moved through the chaos, using the fleeing crowds as cover.
A woman screamed somewhere to his left. A child wailed. The cobblestones groaned again, and a section of the street collapsed, swallowed by darkness that seemed to drink the light.
---
Three blocks away, the situation was no better. Kael found himself in the middle of a crowd pushing toward the river district, everyone trying to escape the same way at once. Bodies pressed against him. Someone's elbow caught his ribs, driving the air from his lungs.
"The seals are breaking!" a man shouted. "The whole district is going to—"
He never finished. A pillar of violet fire erupted from a building to Kael's right, and the crowd scattered like startled birds. In the brief moment of clarity, Kael saw what the fire had revealed: a massive crack in the air itself, like reality had developed a scar.
Through that crack, something moved. Shapes that defied perspective, dimensions that shouldn't exist, and a sound like a thousand voices whispering in unison.
Kael's Soul Reaver senses screamed.
The entities were real. The 72 Abyssal Entities—the things the Orders claimed were safely locked away—were seeping through.
He ran. Not away from the danger, but toward it. Toward the edge of the affected zone where the cultists had been gathered. If he could interrupt whatever ritual they were performing, maybe he could slow the breach.
The notification pulsed again:
[SOUL SIGHT: Active. Spiritual energy signatures detected: 12 hostile, 34 neutral, 1 unknown.]
Unknown. That was new. Kael filed it away and kept moving.
---
He found the cultists in the basement of what had once been a textile warehouse. The stairs descended into flickering torchlight, and the walls pulsed with carved symbols that glowed faintly green—the same symbols he'd seen in the abandoned chapel weeks ago, back when he thought this was just local criminal activity.
Stupid. Naive. This was bigger than any of them had imagined.
Kael pressed himself against the doorframe and listened.
"—the third seal breaks as we speak." A woman's voice, cultured and cold. "The Whisper King's covenant is nearly fulfilled."
"The Orders will respond within hours," a man replied. "We need to accelerate."
"The acceleration is already happening. Can't you feel it?" A third voice, younger, trembling with something that might have been excitement or terror. "The membrane is thinning. Once all seven of the primary rifts destabilize—"
"Seven?" That came from a fourth speaker, angry. "You said six. The texts clearly stated—"
"The texts are interpretations. The reality is chaos. And chaos serves our purposes."
Kael eased the door open another inch. Inside, six figures stood around a stone pedestal at the center of the room. The pedestal held what looked like a crystal vial filled with swirling darkness—soul-essence, but wrong. Tainted. Corrupted.
The woman turned her head slightly, and Kael caught a glimpse of her profile: sharp features, silver-streaked hair, eyes that gleamed with the same violet light as the rifts outside.
She'd seen him. Or sensed him. Either way, his cover was blown.
Kael didn't hesitate. He kicked the door fully open and threw himself sideways as bolts of spiritual energy scorched the air where he'd been standing.
"Contact!" someone shouted.
Kael rolled behind a stack of crates, his mind racing through options. Six cultists, all Awakened at various stages. He was Demigod, but Demigod wasn't invincible—not against numbers, not against prepared opponents.
[SOUL SIGHT: Analyzing threat composition... Primary caster detected. Two combat specialists flanking left. One healer near the vial. Two novices.]
The novices were the weak point. Kael could take them first, harvest their abilities, and build his strength before engaging the real threats.
Or he could go straight for the leader and hope to end this quickly.
Neither option was good.
The woman laughed—a dry, brittle sound. "I know you're there, Soul Reaver. I've been watching you for weeks. Did you really think your little graveyard visits went unnoticed?"
Kael's blood went cold. Watching him. That meant Shadow Court, didn't it? Or something worse.
"Show yourself," she continued. "We have much to discuss about your... Inheritance."
---
Kael stepped out from cover. No point hiding now.
"Six of you, one of me. Your math is better than your fashion sense." He kept his voice steady, his body loose. Ready to move. "So which one are you? Shadow Court? Independent cult? Some new faction I should be worried about?"
The woman's smile didn't reach her eyes. "I'm something much older than the Shadow Court, child. The Court is a tool—useful, but ultimately disposable. I'm part of what came before. What will come again."
"Philosophical." Kael took a step forward, drawing their attention. The novices shifted nervously. "You mentioned seven primary rifts. Care to elaborate, or should I just assume you're all insane?"
Another laugh. "Insane? No. Enlightened. The seals have held for a thousand years, but seals decay. Power fluctuates. And some of us believe that decay should be... Accelerated."
"You're trying to break the world."
"We're trying to free it." She gestured to the vial on the pedestal. "That essence? Harvested from the last Soul Reaver who tried to stop us. Two hundred years ago. His memories were illuminating."
Kael's hands tightened. The last Soul Reaver. His predecessor. The one who'd established the tablets he'd found, the lineage he didn't understand.
"What happened to him?"
"What happens to all of you eventually." She spread her hands, and violet light gathered between her palms. "He was absorbed. His power returned to the Abyss. His memories were preserved for our study. And his body—" She paused, watching his reaction. "His body became the first seal. Buried beneath the Great Rift, feeding the very prison it tried to maintain."
The words hit him like a physical blow. The original Soul Reaver—his predecessor—had been used as living fuel for the seal network.
"We are not your enemies, Kael Ashborn." She pronounced his name like she'd studied it for years. "We are your inheritance. The power you carry came from us, before it was corrupted by the Orders' interference. Join us, and reclaim your birthright. Fight against us, and join your predecessor in the seal network."
The novices had stopped trembling. They stood straighter now, hands raised, waiting for her command.
One of the combat specialists moved to flank left. Kael tracked him in his peripheral vision.
"You're offering me a choice," he said. "Join or die. Very original."
"I'm offering you truth. Something the Orders have never given you." She stepped closer, and he realized she was taller than he'd thought—almost inhumanly tall, her proportions subtly wrong. "The Six Orders exist because humanity needed to believe someone was protecting them. But protection isn't free. Every seal costs a soul. Every ritual requires sacrifice. The only difference between us and them is that we admit what we're doing."
"And that makes it better?"
"It makes it honest."
Kael's mind worked through the implications. The cult wasn't trying to destroy the world—they were trying to release the Entities, use them, harness Abyssal power for their own purposes. The cultists he'd fought before, the random violence in Thornwick, all of it had been misdirection. A larger game playing out beneath the surface.
He needed to get out of here. Needed time to process this, to warn whoever would listen.
But the novices were moving. The specialist was closing in. And the woman was raising her hands for another attack.
Kael lunged.
Not at her—at the pedestal. His hand closed around the vial of corrupted essence, and he felt the wrongness immediately: pain lancing up his arm, spiritual contamination spreading through his system like poison in water.
[WARNING: Corrupted essence detected. Purification required. Extended contact will result in spiritual corruption.]
He didn't need the notification. He could feel it—his soul recoiling from the tainted energy, fighting to reject it.
The woman screamed. "No! You don't understand—you can't just—"
Kael threw the vial at her feet and ran.
Behind him, violet light erupted. He felt the blast catch his back, burning through his coat, searing his shoulder. Pain, sharp and immediate, but he kept moving.
He had the information he needed. The cult knew about him. The Shadow Court was involved—or was being used. And somewhere beneath Thornwick, the seals were failing.
---
Kael staggered through the warehouse district, navigating by memory and instinct. Every step sent fresh agony through his shoulder. The wound wasn't physical—not entirely—but it hurt like hell anyway.
[INJURY: Spiritual burn, moderate severity. Treatment recommended within a full day.]
He found an empty building and collapsed in a corner, pressing his back against the cold stone wall. The sounds of chaos had faded to distant rumblings. Either the cult had stopped their assault, or he'd gotten far enough away.
Either way, he needed to think.
The cultist woman had been wrong about one thing: Kael wasn't interested in birthrights or inheritances. He was interested in survival. In understanding what he was and what he could do. The Soul Reaver abilities were a tool—powerful, dangerous, and apparently the product of a thousand-year conspiracy.
And now he knew the original Soul Reaver was dead. Buried beneath a seal. Used as fuel for the system that had created him.
That changed things. Or maybe it didn't. Either way, Kael had work to do.
He pulled out the small notebook he carried everywhere, flipping to a blank page. Time to document what he'd learned. The cult's structure, their goals, the names he'd overheard.
But before he could write, footsteps echoed in the darkness outside. Not running. Deliberate. Approaching.
Kael tensed, pushing himself up despite the pain. His shoulder screamed in protest, but he ignored it.
A figure appeared in the doorway. Tall, cloaked in black, face hidden in shadow.
"You're hurt." The voice was female, familiar. "The cult's spiritual attacks leave distinctive marks."
"Lyra?" Kael's eyes narrowed. "Thought you were supposed to be keeping distance. Shadow Court orders."
Lyra stepped into the building, and the dim light revealed her face—grim, determined, younger than she usually let herself look. "The Shadow Court isn't here. Or, I'm here, but not for them. Not tonight."
"Then why?"
"Because the seals are breaking, and you're standing in the middle of it." She crossed to him, pulling a small vial from her coat. "Drink this. It won't fix the damage, but it'll keep it from getting worse."
Kael took the vial. Studied it. Smelled nothing unusual.
"Why should I trust you?"
"You shouldn't." She met his gaze steadily. "But I'm the only one offering help right now, and you need help. The cult knows about you. The Orders will be mobilizing within hours. And whatever you're planning to do next—" She gestured vaguely at his notebook. "—it won't matter if you're dead."
He drank. The essence was cool, pure, immediately effective. The burning in his shoulder eased to a manageable throb.
"Better?"
"Getting there."
Lyra sat down across from him, close enough to talk, far enough to run if needed. "The woman you met. She's called the Pale Mother. She's been active for over a century—different faces, different names. She's the one who's been orchestrating the cult activity in Thornwick."
"A century." Kael filed that away. "She mentioned the Shadow Court being a tool. Disposable."
"The Shadow Court was founded by three of the original Orders as a deniable operations arm. But the Pale Mother—" Lyra paused, choosing her words carefully. "She's older than the Orders. Older than the seal network. Some say she was human once, before the first rift opened. Others say she came through with the Entities and chose a different path."
"That's insane."
"Probably. But she's powerful, she's organized, and she's been planning this for longer than either of us has been alive." Lyra pulled out a folded paper, sliding it across the floor toward him. "These are the locations of the seven primary rifts she mentioned. I stole them from a Shadow Court operative last week."
Kael unfolded the paper. Seven locations marked across Thornwick and the surrounding countryside. Some he recognized—the old cathedral, the abandoned mines, a point in the river.
"Seven rifts. If they all open—"
"They won't all open at once. The Pale Mother is trying to destabilize them gradually, create weaknesses in the seal network that the Entities can exploit without causing a full catastrophic breach. She thinks she can control them. Ride the chaos to power."
"And you believe that?"
Lyra's expression was grim. "No. I think she's going to get a lot of people killed. Including us. Including everyone in Thornwick."
The weight of it settled over Kael. A conspiracy reaching back centuries. Entities threatening to break through. Cults organizing in plain sight. And, he was at the center of it all—a Soul Reaver, a walking weapon that both sides wanted to control.
"So what do we do?"
"We stop the Pale Mother. Or at least delay her long enough for the Orders to mobilize." Lyra stood, brushing dust from her coat. "The Shadow Court won't help. The Orders are too slow, too political. But you and me—we can do something."
"You want to work together? After everything?"
"I want to save lives." She extended her hand. "And I think you do too. Whatever else you are, whatever the Soul Reaver made you, you still have choices. That's what separates us from the Entities. We can choose."
Kael looked at her hand. Thought about all the ways this could be a trap. Thought about the information she'd given him, the risks she'd taken to reach him.
Thought about the cultist leader buried under the Great Rift. His predecessor. A Soul Reaver who'd fought alone and lost.
He took her hand.
"For now," he said. "We work together until we don't."
"Fair enough." Lyra smiled—barely, but it was there. "First, we need to secure the nearest rift. The old cathedral. If we can disrupt the ritual there, it'll buy time for the others."
"How many cultists?"
"Unknown. But probably more than we can handle alone." She paused. "There's a group of Order-aligned mercenaries camped near the river gate. They might help—for the right price."
"We don't have money."
"I wasn't suggesting coin."
Kael understood. "You want me to harvest them. After the fight."
"If they fall. Yes."
It was brutal. Practical. Exactly the kind of plan he could get behind.
"Let's move."
---
They found the mercenaries exactly where Lyra said they'd be: a rough camp near the river gate, eight men and women in mismatched armor keeping watch over a small fire. Awakened, but low-level—Transcendent at best. More interested in their dice game than the chaos erupting across the city.
Kael approached openly, hands visible. One of the mercenaries—a heavyset woman with a battle-axe strapped to her back—stood immediately.
"The hell do you want?"
"Information. And possibly help."
"Information costs. Help costs more."
Lyra stepped forward. "We're offering a cut of whatever we find at the old cathedral. Soul-essence, artifacts, anything valuable."
The mercenary's eyes narrowed. "The cathedral's already lost. Order command pulled back two hours ago. Said it was a lost cause."
"Lost cause or not, someone's trying to break the seal there. If they succeed—"
"Then the whole district goes up. I know." The mercenary glanced at her companions. "We're not stupid, lady. We're not walking into a death trap for a 'maybe.'"
Kael stepped closer. "What would it take?"
"Proof there's something worth saving. Proof we won't die for nothing." The mercenary crossed her arms. "You want us to risk our necks, you show us you're worth following."
It was a test. Kael understood that. These mercenaries had survived this long by being careful, by not trusting easily. They needed to see strength.
He drew his knife.
The mercenary tensed, hand moving toward her axe—
Kael drove the blade into his own palm. Not deep, but enough to draw blood. He let the blood drip onto the ground, speaking words he'd never used before, power he hadn't known he possessed.
The earth drank the blood. And from the ground, something answered.
Shadows rose around him—not darkness, but memory. Images flickered in the air: the cultists gathering at the cathedral, the Pale Mother's violet light, the rift beginning to tear through reality.
The mercenaries stared, faces pale in the ghostly illumination.
"Soul Reaver," one of them whispered.
"Demon," another corrected.
"Asset," Kael said, letting the shadows fade. "I'm an asset. And I'm offering you a chance to do something that matters."
The heavyset mercenary was quiet for a long moment. Then she laughed—a harsh, barking sound.
"Well. That's unexpected." She unslung her axe. "Alright, Soul Reaver. You want to storm the cathedral? We can do that. But when the killing starts, you stay out of our way unless we're losing. Clear?"
"Clear."
"Good. We move in ten minutes. Get ready."
---
The march to the cathedral took them through streets that Kael barely recognized. Buildings had collapsed. Fires burned uncontrolled. The air tasted like copper and ozone, the spiritual contamination thick enough to make his eyes water.
The mercenaries moved in formation, professional despite the chaos. Lyra scouted ahead, reporting back with updates: cultist patrols here, abandoned barricade there, and finally, the cathedral itself.
It loomed against the wounded sky like a corpse refusing to fall. The steeple had partially collapsed, but the main structure still stood. And from its broken windows, violet light pulsed steadily outward.
"Big ritual," the mercenary captain observed. "They're not even trying to hide it."
"They don't need to," Lyra said. "The Orders pulled back. Who's going to stop them?"
"We are." Kael studied the building's layout. Three main entrances: front doors, side sacristy, and a collapsed section in the back. Multiple floors, plenty of places for cultists to set up defensive positions.
[SOUL SIGHT: Minimum 15 hostile signatures detected inside. Unknown number in surrounding structures.]
Fifteen cultists. Plus whatever was happening at the rift itself.
"Captain. Can your people breach the front and draw fire?"
"We can try. But fifteen Awakened? We won't last long."
"You don't need to last long. Just keep them occupied." Kael pulled out his knife, checked the edge. "I'll go in through the back. Find the rift. Disrupt whatever they're doing."
"And us?"
"Stay alive. That's your job."
The captain grinned. "I like this guy. Crazy, but he knows what he wants."
The mercenaries split into two groups, moving toward the front and side entrances. Kael and Lyra circled to the rear, finding the collapsed section exactly where they'd hoped—rubble and broken timber creating a gap in the stonework.
Kael climbed first, pulling himself up onto what had once been a gallery floor. The wood groaned under his weight but held.
Lyra followed, landing silently beside him.
They were inside. The cathedral stretched before them, pews overturned, candles scattered everywhere. At the far end, where the altar should have been, a swirling vortex of violet energy consumed the space.
And standing at its center, conducting a ritual that made Kael's soul recoil, was the Pale Mother.
She hadn't seen them yet. Her attention was fixed on the rift, her hands weaving patterns in the air that seemed to tear at the fabric of reality itself.
"Two minutes," Lyra whispered. "Maybe less. Then the breach becomes irreversible."
Kael nodded. Two minutes to end this—or at least slow it down.
He moved forward, staying low, using the overturned pews for cover. The Pale Mother's chanting grew louder, more urgent. The rift pulsed, and something began to emerge from its depths—not fully formed, but close. A shape that hurt to look at, dimensions that defied comprehension.
Kael reached the edge of the ritual space. The Pale Mother sensed him—a flicker of awareness, a turn of her head—
And that's when everything went wrong.
The floor exploded upward. Stone shards tore through the air as three cultists burst from hidden positions, their spiritual attacks slamming into Kael before he could react.
He flew backward, crashing through two pews before coming to rest against a pillar. His ribs screamed. Blood filled his mouth.
[INJURY: Multiple impacts, moderate severity. Spiritual energy depleted.]
Lyra was engaged with another cultist, her daggers flashing. The mercenaries' battle-cries echoed from the front of the cathedral.
And the Pale Mother laughed.
"Did you really think it would be that easy?" She didn't stop her ritual, hands still moving, rift still growing. "I've been preparing for you, Soul Reaver. Every step you took, every choice you made—I anticipated them all."
Kael spat blood. Pushed himself up. The cultists who'd ambushed him were closing in, hands crackling with violet energy.
He had seconds. Maybe less.
The Pale Mother's smile widened. "Join me, and die quickly. Resist, and I'll make you watch as the first Entity steps through. I'll make you understand what true power looks like before I consume you."
The rift pulsed again. The emerging shape solidified further—something with too many eyes, too many limbs, something that radiated hunger and malice in equal measure.
Kael made his choice.
He lunged—not at the Pale Mother, but at the cultists closest to him. His hand closed around the first one's throat, and he pulled, dragging the man off-balance.
The Soul Reaver activated.
Contact. Extraction.
The cultist screamed—a short, sharp sound cut brutally short. Kael felt the man's life force pour into him, felt the skills and memories that came with it.
[SOUL HARVEST: You extracted spiritual essence from the remains. 15% capacity restored. Skill acquired: Spiritual Barrier (basic).]
Pain lanced through his skull—always the pain—but he pushed through it. Grabbed the second cultist. Harvested.
[SOUL HARVEST: You extracted spiritual essence from the remains. 20% capacity restored. Skill acquired: Void Bolt (basic).]
The third cultist tried to run. Kael threw the body aside and chased, catching the man before he'd made it three steps. Another harvest.
[SOUL HARVEST: You extracted spiritual essence from the remains. 18% capacity restored. Skill acquired: Soul Sight (partial).]
He stood, surrounded by bodies, restored and empowered. The Pale Mother's expression had changed—surprise replacing confidence.
"You're stronger than I expected."
"I'm smarter than you expected." Kael raised his hand, drawing on the harvested skills. A barrier of shimmering energy formed around him—basic, but effective. "You prepared for a fight. I prepared for war."
He hurled the Void Bolt.
It streaked across the cathedral, slamming into the edge of the rift itself. The Pale Mother shrieked as the ritual destabilized, her careful patterns disrupted by raw spiritual interference.
The rift convulsed. The emerging Entity screamed—a sound that existed outside normal hearing, a vibration that shook Kael's bones.
And then, impossibly, it began to retreat. Pulled back through the shrinking breach, denied entry by the broken ritual.
"No!" The Pale Mother threw everything she had into stabilizing the rift. Violet light blazed from her hands, her eyes, her very pores. "You can't—it's so close—"
Kael hit her with another Void Bolt.
The Pale Mother staggered. Her concentration broke. The rift shuddered, contracted—
And sealed itself.
Not permanently. Not completely. But enough. Enough to stop the immediate threat. Enough to buy time.
The cathedral filled with screams—cultists fleeing, mercenaries pursuing, the Entity's echo fading into silence.
The Pale Mother ran.
Kael started after her, but Lyra grabbed his arm. "Let her go. We can't catch her and secure this area."
"She'll come back."
"She will. But not tonight." Lyra pulled him toward the exit. "Tonight, we won."
---
They regrouped outside the cathedral, the mercenaries counting their wounded. Two dead, three injured, but alive. More than they'd expected.
The captain approached Kael, her expression unreadable. "That was... Impressive."
"That was necessary."
"Same thing, in this business." She extended her hand again. "We still want that cut."
Kael almost smiled. "Check the sacristy. There's a lockbox hidden under the altar. Should have enough soul-essence to make this worth your while."
"Appreciated." The captain hesitated. "For what it's worth—whatever you are, whatever the Soul Reaver means—tonight you saved a lot of people."
"Tomorrow might be different."
"Tomorrow's always different." She turned to her people. "Let's move."
The mercenaries filed out, leaving Kael and Lyra alone in the ruined cathedral square. The violet sky was fading, clouds returning to something closer to normal. The rift had been sealed—for now.
"Five more," Lyra said quietly. "Five more rifts. And the Pale Mother knows we're coming."
"Then we move faster than she expects." Kael checked his shoulder—the wound had stabilized, more annoying than debilitating. "Where do we go next?"
Lyra consulted the map. "The abandoned mines. Two hours north. Another cultist strongpoint."
"And if they're as prepared as this one?"
"Then we improvise." She looked at him, something like respect in her eyes. "You harvested three cultists back there. You know how to use their abilities now."
"Void Bolt. Barrier. Partial Soul Sight. Not much."
"It's a start." Lyra folded the map and tucked it away. "The Pale Mother was right about one thing—your power has limits. But she's wrong about what those limits mean. You can grow stronger. You can learn. That's something the Entities can't do."
"And the Pale Mother?"
"She'll adapt. Plan. Counter whatever we throw at her." Lyra started walking. "But she also made a mistake tonight. She showed us what we're fighting. She revealed the scope of the conspiracy, the locations of the rifts, her own identity."
"She wanted to recruit me."
"Or scare you. Either way, she made herself known." Lyra glanced back. "That's something we can use."
Kael followed, his mind churning through everything he'd learned. The Pale Mother, centuries old, orchestrating the cult activity. The original Soul Reaver, buried beneath the Great Rift. The seal network failing. The Entities waiting.
And him—a Soul Reaver, caught between forces he barely understood.
The war had started. The rifts were cracking. And Kael Ashborn had just become a target for everyone involved.
He wondered, briefly, if his predecessor had faced the same choice. Had the first Soul Reaver stood in a ruined cathedral, surrounded by bodies, wondering what came next?
Probably. And look how that had ended.
Kael pushed the thought aside. He wasn't the first Soul Reaver. He wasn't following anyone's path. He was making his own.
That had to count for something.
Ahead, the road stretched north toward the mines, toward the next rift, toward whatever came next. Behind, the cathedral burned quietly, smoke rising into a sky that was slowly returning to normal.
Lyra walked beside him, silent, watchful. Ready for whatever came.
For now, they had time. Not much—maybe hours, maybe days—but some. Time to plan, to prepare, to figure out how to stop a conspiracy that had been building for centuries.
Time to become something more than a target.
The notification appeared in his vision, unexpected but not unwelcome:
[NEW OBJECTIVE REGISTERED: Seal the Seven Rifts. Failure condition: Catastrophic reality breach.]
[SUB-OBJECTIVE: Locate and secure the Great Rift. Unknown complications detected.]
[SIDE NOTE: Original Soul Reaver's remains detected beneath primary seal. Extraction may yield critical information.]
Kael read the final line twice. His predecessor. Buried beneath the Great Rift. The place where all of this had started, a thousand years ago.
Someday, he'd have to go there. Would have to face whatever remained of the first Soul Reaver, the one who'd tried to fight alone and failed.
But not yet. Not tonight.
Tonight, there were five more rifts to seal. Five more battles to fight. And somewhere in the distance, the Pale Mother was already planning her next move.
The war had begun. And Kael Ashborn—Soul Reaver, death-row survivor, walking weapon—was going to win it.
Or die trying.
The thought should have frightened him. Instead, it felt like freedom.
