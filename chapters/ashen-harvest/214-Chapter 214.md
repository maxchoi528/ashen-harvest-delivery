The explosion tore through the foundry district at midnight.
Kael crouched behind a collapsed smokestack, heat rolling across his face in waves. Flames devoured the old ironworks three blocks away, painting the smog-choked sky the color of bruised fruit. His fingers curled against the cold iron beneath him, counting the seconds.
Five cultists. Armed with ritual blades and stolen sigils. They'd been conducting something in that building when the blast happened — a secondary device, poorly placed. Amateur work. Or deliberately sloppy.
"Your scout says they're inside," Vane's voice crackled through the copper-wire earpiece tucked against Kael's ear. The man always sounded half-asleep. "Confirm."
"They were inside." Kael shifted his weight, scanning the burning structure. "Now they're running. Eastern exit, toward the canal."
"Intercept?"
"Already moving."
He dropped from the smokestack, boots striking cobblestones with a wet crunch. Rotting cabbage and coal dust filled his nostrils. Three years in Thornwick and the city still reeked like an open grave. He supposed that suited him just fine.
The cultists emerged from the smoke like ghosts — black robes, white masks painted with symbols that made his teeth ache. They carried a metal chest between them, struggling under its weight. Heavy. Filled with something more valuable than their lives.
Kael let them reach the narrow alley before stepping into their path.
The leader stopped. His mask turned toward Kael, those painted eyes conveying surprise. "The Soul Reaver."
"Got it in one." Kael cracked his knuckles. "Put down the chest and I'll let you run."
"You don't understand what's happening here." The leader's voice carried an edge of genuine fear. "The seal will break regardless. The Rift cannot be—"
Kael's hand closed around the man's throat.
No spiritual cost. That was the gift and the curse. His fingers sank through flesh and bone as if they weren't there, reaching for the thing beneath. The leader convulsed, legs kicking uselessly against the cobblestones.

"I thought that was a myth. A bedtime story the Orders tell to frighten initiates."

"To open all seventy-two rifts at once. To bring the Abyss fully into the Material." Vane's voice carried no emotion. "The Entities aren't demons to be feared, you see. They're alternatives. The Whisper King believes humanity has outgrown its usefulness to the seal network. He wants to give us something new."

"Stop."
Vane's mouth closed.
"I don't care what he believes." Kael lifted the chest, feeling its weight settle against his chest. "I care what he's doing. And right now, he's doing it in my city."
The walk back to the safehouse took two hours through Thornwick's labyrinthine streets. Vane kept pace beside him, silent, watching. Kael found himself hyperaware of the man's presence — the way his boots fell slightly ahead of each step, the slight bulge beneath his left coat pocket that could have been a weapon or a token or nothing at all.
Trust. The most expensive currency in the Awakened world.
Sera waited for them in the basement of the abandoned textile mill, surrounded by maps, stolen documents, and enough alchemical equipment to start a small war. Her red hair caught the candlelight as she looked up, eyes narrowing at the chest Kael set on the table.
"Entity-touched essence." She said it like a curse. "Where did you find this?"
"Eastern district. The cultists were transporting it somewhere." Kael opened the chest, letting her examine its contents. "Vane says it's coordinated. A single mind behind all the attacks."
Sera's hands hovered over the cylinder, not touching. "The Whisper King."
"You know the name too."

Every attack in the past six months has targeted facilities involved in seal maintenance. The Mortician's Guild vaults, the Order of Dawn's archive, even the Arcane Spire's research wing. All of them handling materials related to the rifts."
Kael studied the map. Red pins marked attack sites. Blue pins marked successful defenses. The red pins formed a pattern — almost a circle, centered on Thornwick's old quarter.
"They're surrounding the city."
"Correct." Vane tapped the center of the circle. "Whatever they're planning, they need Thornwick as ground zero."
"Why here?"
"The original seal network was built on Thornwick's foundations." Sera's voice had gone quiet. "The first rifts were torn open in this region. The city's positioned directly above the most unstable convergence point."
"And the Whisper King knows this."
"He's been preparing for centuries, apparently."
Silence settled over the basement. The candles guttered. Somewhere above them, water dripped through the textile mill's ruined ceiling, each drop a small percussion in the darkness.
Kael reached into the chest and lifted the cylinder of essence. It swirled against his palms, warm and cold at once, that oily texture pulsing like a heartbeat. He could feel the Entity's presence in it — not alive, not exactly dead, but caught between states.
"This is dangerous," Sera said. "If the cultists were harvesting it, they were feeding something. Building toward a threshold."

"Maybe. Or a key." Her eyes met his. "If you touch that essence directly, Kael, you might be able to harvest information about what they're planning. But it will cost you something."
"Soul essence doesn't cost me anything."
"Not spiritually. Psychologically." Sera's voice softened. "Entity-touched souls carry fragments of the Abyss. When you harvest from them, those fragments become part of you. At the Transcendent stage, you can survive it. But you'll feel them. Their obsessions. Their hungers."
Kael looked at the cylinder. The darkness within seemed to pulse in response to his attention.
"Show me how."

And then I found evidence that the Shadow Court itself had been compromised." His gray eyes met Kael's. "The Whisper King isn't an external enemy. He's a former member of our organization.
