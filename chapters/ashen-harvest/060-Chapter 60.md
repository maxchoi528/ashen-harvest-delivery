Silence.

Kael and Lyra moved simultaneously—glass forgotten, weapons drawn, bodies low. Lyra crossed to the painted window, pressed her eye to a gap in the paint. Her face went pale.

Kael's enhanced senses picked up what she couldn't: the spiritual signatures of trained killers, their energy signatures masked by suppression talismans, their movements coordinated with military precision. Not Tolerance gang members. Not cultists.
Order enforcers.
"They found us." Lyra's voice held no panic, only calculation. "The safehouse was clean three days ago. Someone talked."
Kael's hand found the case of journals. Maren's gift. The map to the tomb that everyone wanted.
The door at the top of the stairs exploded inward.
