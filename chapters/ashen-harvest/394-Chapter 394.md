The mine shaft collapsed three feet behind Kael's position, spraying gravel and splintered timber into the passage. He pressed his back against damp stone, lungs burning from the sprint through Thornwick's lower drainage tunnels. Seven years of running from the Orders, and this was the first time he'd led others into danger.
"Move it!" He grabbed Mira by the collar of her leather vest and hauled her forward. The woman coughed, blood from a gash above her eye painting half her face crimson. Behind them, the ceiling groaned.
Brennan and Corin followed on his heels, their boots splashing through ankle-deep water that smelled of copper and rot. The tracker—Wen—brought up the rear, his lantern swinging wildly as he navigated by memory alone. The group had been ambushed in the storage cavern where they'd planned to meet their contact. Instead of information, they'd found Cultist operatives waiting with silver-coated blades.
"They're herding us," Brennan said between ragged breaths. The broad-shouldered Iron Vanguard dropout held his arm at an unnatural angle, clearly broken. "This whole tunnel system—it's a trap."
"Observant." Kael's voice carried no sympathy. "Thirty seconds until we reach the junction. After that, we split."
"Split?" Mira's voice rose. "Half of us can barely walk!"
"Then crawl faster." Kael rounded a corner and stopped dead.
The junction ahead blazed with light—dozens of phosphor torches mounted on poles, illuminating a chamber wide enough to hold a hundred men. Standing in formation across the far end were figures in gray robes, their faces hidden behind smooth ceramic masks. The Cultists had blocked every exit.
"Contact," Kael said flatly. "Twelve hostiles. Awakened signatures—two at Sovereign tier, the rest below."
Brennan let out a low whistle. "That's above our weight class."
"Not if we don't let them coordinate." Kael scanned the chamber, cataloging positions. The phosphor light threw hard shadows across the carved stone walls—ancient wards, half-eroded by time. Someone had defaced the symbols decades ago. The Cultists used the space as a staging ground; supply crates lined the walls, and a communication array of copper bells sat on a raised platform at the chamber's center.
"They're using the old seal tunnels," Wen said, recognition dawning. "These passages—they connect to the rifts. The Cult's been operating right under Thornwick's foundations for years."
Kael filed that information away. The Six Orders taught that the 72 sealed rifts were guarded, monitored, secure. Apparently that teaching was a comfortable lie.
"Weapons," he ordered.
Mira drew a pair of serrated daggers. Brennan produced a short-handled war hammer from his pack, wincing as he shifted his broken arm. Corin—a younger man with nervous eyes—unholstered a pair of flintlock pistols. Wen raised empty hands. A spiritual manipulator, Kael remembered. Limited combat application but useful for detection.
"What about you?" one of the Cultists called out. The speaker stood at the formation's center, taller than the others, his mask painted with red concentric circles. "The Soul Reaver. We know what you are."
Kael felt Mira's stare boring into the side of his skull. Brennan's grip on his hammer tightened.
"They know," Mira whispered.
"So does everyone in this city with half a brain and a grudge." Kael rolled his shoulders, settling into his stance. The Demigod stage hummed through his veins—power enough to level this chamber if he spent it recklessly. But power meant nothing if he burned out before reaching the surface. "Cover the left flank. Brennan, stay mobile. Corin, shoot anyone who tries to flank us."
"And you?" Brennan asked.
"I'm going to introduce them to their ancestors."
Kael moved.
The first Cultist died before he registered Kael's approach—a palm strike to the sternum that crumpled ceramic armor like parchment. The man's soul wrenched free the instant death claimed him, and Kael's fingers found cold flesh a half-second later.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Minor Blade Proficiency from the remains. 3 weeks of combat experience acquired.]
The knowledge slotted into Kael's mind like a key turning in a lock. His stance adjusted automatically, weight shifting to account for the new muscle memory. Not a dramatic improvement, but useful. The Soul Reaver ability had always worked that way—death offered fragments, not miracles.
Two more Cultists rushed him. Kael ducked beneath a silvered blade, drove his elbow into the man's throat, and pivoted into a spinning kick that sent the third attacker sprawling across the stone floor. He harvested from the choking Cultist, gaining another splinter of skill.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Shadow Step (incomplete) from the remains. Mobility enhanced by 12%.]
"Your brother was wrong about you," the ringleader said. He hadn't moved from his position, but his hands had shifted beneath his robes. "We expected you to run."
"My brother was a fool who thought fear was a weapon." Kael scanned the remaining Cultists, noting their positioning. They'd formed a crescent, trying to funnel him toward the communication platform. Predictable. "Fear is a tool. Nothing more."
"And arrogance?"
Kael smiled, and it wasn't a pleasant expression. "Arrogance is what happens when you're strong enough to back it up."
He launched himself at the formation's center.
Mira's daggers sang through the air, pinning a Cultist's hand to his own weapon. Brennan's hammer cracked against a ceramic mask, caving in the wearer's face. Corin's pistol barked twice, and two more attackers dropped. The Cultists hadn't expected coordinated resistance—they'd anticipated a cornered animal, not a hunting pack.
But they had Sovereign-tier Awakened on their side.
The first wave of spiritual pressure hit like a physical blow. Kael staggered, his vision swimming as raw power crashed against his defenses. One of the masked figures stepped forward, hands weaving signs in the air, and the pressure intensified.
"Soul Reavers are abominations." The speaker's voice carried the resonance of someone accustomed to being obeyed. "You violate the Law of Conservation. You steal what should return to the Abyss."
"The Abyss." Kael spat blood onto the stone. "Let me guess—you worship the Entities? Think releasing them will bring paradise?"
The masked figure tilted his head. "We offer liberation. Humanity has cowered behind seals for a thousand years, trading true power for false safety. The Entities do not imprison us. We imprison ourselves."
"And you—" Kael pointed at the communication array, "—are coordinating attacks across Thornwick. Organizing the faithful. Preparing something."
"We prepare everything. The Whisper King's return approaches, and the Great Rift's seal weakens with each passing season. Soon there will be no need for tunnels and masks. Soon the world will see truth."
Kael felt cold certainty settle in his chest. The information asymmetry the chapter demanded—he was learning now, through violence and revelation. The Cult wasn't random madmen. They were an army with a strategy, and they operated in Thornwick's bones.
"How many?" he demanded.
The Sovereign-tier Awakened laughed. "How many what? Cultists? Believers? Rifts already opened?" He spread his arms wide. "You stand in one of seventy-two tunnels that lead to sealed doorways. Each tunnel has a cell. Each cell has a purpose. You've disrupted a supply run. A single shipment of soul-essence vials bound for a larger operation."
Brennan swore under his breath. "They're smuggling from the rifts themselves?"
"The Entities leak spiritual energy constantly. Most of it dissipates. But accumulate it, refine it, store it..." The Cultist's mask caught the phosphor light. "You begin to understand why the Orders fear what approaches."
Kael's mind raced through implications. Soul-essence vials were the continent's reserve currency. If the Cult controlled significant sources—energy harvested directly from Abyssal Entities—their wealth would dwarf that of the Six Orders combined. They could buy armies. They could buy Awakened. They could buy their way into power structures across every nation.
"Zarek," Kael said quietly. "Prime Zarek. He fronts the Veiled Market's illegal trade. How much of it flows to your cause?"
The Sovereign-tier Awakened went very still.
"You'll have to try harder than that, Reaver." His voice had lost its theatrical quality. "We don't betray our allies. Unlike some, we value loyalty."
"Zarek betrayed me." Kael stepped forward, and the pressure around him intensified—but he kept moving. "Framed me for murders I didn't commit. Turned the city guard against me. Sent Order hunters to my doorstep." He stopped three feet from the Cultist leader. "I'm going to find him. And when I do, I'm going to make him answer for every life he's touched with his schemes."
"Feelings." The Cultist's hands resumed their weaving. "Such strong feelings. The Faceless One chose well when it marked you."
Kael's blood froze.
"What did you say?"
"Three fragments, three vessels. One wears a noble's face in the Arcane Spire. One walks among the Iron Vanguard's officers. And one..." The Cultist pressed a finger to the center of his mask. "One is you. Did you think the Reaver powers came from nothing? Did you believe you were special?"
The chamber swam. Kael's Reaver senses screamed warnings he couldn't process. The Faceless One—an Entity itself, one of the original inhabitants of the Abyss—had planted fragments of itself in three individuals. Kael was one of those vessels.
"Why tell me this?" His voice came out steadier than he felt.
"Because you'll fail." The Cultist's spiritual pressure spiked, and Kael understood—the man wasn't trying to kill him. He was trying to overload him. "The fragment will consume you. Your progression, your hunger for souls, your obsession with power—it all feeds the thing growing inside you. By the time you reach Divine tier, there won't be a Kael Ashborn. There will only be the Faceless One wearing a dead man's face."
Mira screamed something behind him. Brennan charged, war hammer raised, and a second Sovereign-tier Cultist intercepted him with a blast of concentrated spiritual force. The sound of impact echoed off stone walls.
Kael moved on instinct. His hand closed around the speaking Cultist's throat, fingers digging through fabric to find bare skin. The man's eyes widened above his mask—surprise, then fear, then a terrible delight.
"Do it," he rasped. "Harvest me. Learn the truth."
Kael hesitated for one heartbeat. Then his ability activated, pulling the soul from dying flesh.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Cultist Commander Memory Fragment from the remains. Knowledge of supply routes and cell organization acquired. Warning: Soul fragment contains trace Entity corruption. Effects unknown.]
The information flooded his consciousness—tunnel maps, shipment schedules, names and locations of cells operating across three provinces. But beneath the tactical data, something else pressed against his awareness. A presence. A hunger that felt familiar and alien simultaneously.
The Faceless One's fragment stirred.
Kael released the body and staggered backward, gasping. His left hand trembled uncontrollably. When he looked at his palm, the skin seemed to ripple, as though something beneath it was waking up.
"Kael!" Mira was at his side, daggers bloody, her face pale with exertion. "We need to move. More are coming."
The second Sovereign-tier Cultist had retreated, no doubt to report what had happened. The remaining grunts were fleeing through passages Kael hadn't seen before—hidden exits built into the chamber's walls.
"The archive," Wen said. He'd stayed near the communication array, rifling through documents scattered across the platform. "Their orders. Everything they're planning—it's written here."
"How long?" Kael demanded.
"Two minutes to gather what matters."
"Do it."
Brennan limped toward the nearest exit, Corin covering him. Mira stayed with Kael, watching him with an expression caught between loyalty and wariness. She'd seen the harvest. She knew what he was.
"The Soul Reaver rumors," she said quietly. "They're true."
"Yes."
"And you're still here. Leading us. Risking yourself for people you've never met."
"People are useful alive." Kael's voice was flat. "Dead people don't carry gear or watch my back."
"That's not—" She stopped, shaking her head. "Later. We'll talk later."
Kael didn't answer. The Cultist's words echoed in his skull, mixing with the new fragment's corruption. Three vessels. One Entity. He was not special—he was a host, a breeding ground for something that had existed before humanity learned to kindle fire.
The Faceless One wanted release. The Cult wanted to give it to them. And Kael was playing directly into their plans with every soul he harvested.
But the alternative was weakness. Surrender. A slow death instead of a violent one. If the Entity inside him would eventually consume him regardless, he might as well go down fighting.
Wen emerged from the platform with a leather satchel stuffed with documents. "Ready."
"Left passage, then up." Kael pointed toward the tunnel Brennan had chosen. "We surface in the Ashworth district. From there, we scatter and regroup at the usual point."
"And if they follow?" Mira asked.
"They will." Kael moved toward the exit, his hand still trembling. The Entity corruption pulsed beneath his skin, a second heartbeat that didn't belong to him. "The Cult knows what I am now. They'll adjust their strategy."
"Meaning?"
"Meaning the next time we meet, they won't underestimate me." He glanced back at his team—the wounded, the frightened, the desperate. People who had followed him into tunnels filled with enemies because they'd had nowhere else to go. "It means we need to be ready. Whatever they're planning, we just stole their playbook. That buys us time."
"Time for what?" Brennan asked.
Kael's smile was grim. "Time to find out who's really pulling strings in this city. The Cult, the Orders, the Veiled Market—someone connects them all. Someone's been playing a longer game than anyone realizes."
He stepped into the darkness of the tunnel, leaving the phosphor light behind. Above them, Thornwick's streets churned with traffic and violence and secrets. Somewhere in that chaos, Zarek was making plans. Somewhere, the Faceless One's other vessels walked in ignorance or complicity. And somewhere, in a tomb no living person had seen in centuries, the original Soul Reaver's remains waited for a successor who might never arrive.
Kael didn't know which mystery mattered most. But he intended to survive long enough to solve all of them.
The tunnel stretched ahead, cold and damp and full of echoes. Behind him, his team's footsteps marked a rhythm of survival. And beneath his skin, something ancient watched through stolen eyes, patient as stone, hungry as the Abyss itself.
A distant rumble shook dust from the ceiling. The Cult was collapsing passages behind them, sealing the tunnels to contain what they knew.
They weren't fast enough to outrun a reckoning.
Kael quickened his pace. The war had found him in Thornwick's depths, and now it would follow him into the light.

The Ashworth district surfaced around them like a fever dream—gaslit streets choked with evening crowds, steam-cabs honking their horns, a public hanging platform near the market square where the condemned still swung in their chains. Kael pulled his collar higher, the blood on his coat. Mira did the same with her own.
"Safe house is three streets north," Brennan said. He leaned heavily against Corin, his broken arm swelling beneath the makeshift splint. "My contact there. She owes me."
"She trustworthy?" Kael asked.
"She'll take coin and ask no questions. That's trustworthy enough for now."
They moved through the crowd, anonymous among the masses of spiritually inert commoners and the occasional Awakened who wore their power like a badge. No one looked twice at a group of wounded survivors. Thornwick saw violence every day; this was nothing special.
But as they turned onto Carver Street, Kael felt it—a prickle at the back of his skull, a presence watching from somewhere above. He didn't look up. Didn't break stride. adjusted his path, steering his team toward a narrow alley that offered multiple exits.
"We have a tail," he murmured.
"Where?" Mira's hands drifted toward her daggers.
"Roofline. Two figures, maybe three. Professional positioning—they're not Cult."
"Iron Vanguard?" Wen suggested.
"Maybe. Or Shadow Court." Kael ducked into the alley, gesturing for the others to follow. "The Court monitors Awakened activity in Thornwick. We just kicked a hornet's nest. They want to know what we found."
"So we give them something." Brennan spat on the cobblestones. "Let them chase shadows while we disappear."
"No." Kael stopped at the alley's midpoint, turning to face his team. His expression was unreadable in the dim light. "The Shadow Court doesn't just monitor. They recruit. They manipulate. If they're watching us, it's because they're interested."
"Interested in what?" Corin asked. "We're nobody. A broken ex-Vanguard, a street thief, a hedge-witch, and a soul-eating abomination."
Kael's laugh was hollow. "A soul-eating abomination who just stole their Cultist allies' operational plans. The Court wants the same thing we do—information. Leverage. Power." He met each of their gazes in turn. "We can run, or we can use this."
"Use it how?" Mira asked.
"By making them an offer they can't refuse." Kael pulled a document from Wen's satchel—a list of names and locations that would mean nothing to civilians but everything to anyone investigating the Cult's network. "The Court wants to undermine the Orders, right? Here's proof that one of the six has been compromised from within. They'll trade. Access, resources, protection—all for a few pages of paper."
"You're talking about dealing with spies," Brennan said slowly. "The Court uses everyone they touch."
"Everyone uses everyone. That's how power works." Kael tucked the document into his coat. "The question is whether we use them first."
The watching presence intensified. Kael counted four figures now, positioned at the alley's entrance and exit. They'd been herded into a dead end—no, not herded. Surrounded.
A voice echoed from the rooftops, feminine and precise: "The Soul Reaver speaks of power. How refreshing. Most of your kind hide what they are until it's too late to negotiate."
A figure dropped from the darkness above, landing in a crouch on the cobblestones. She rose gracefully—tall, angular, dressed in black that seemed to drink the gaslight. Her face was half-hidden by a silvered mask that covered her left eye and cheek, leaving the right side exposed. A thin scar traced from her temple to her jaw.
"Lady Vance," Mira breathed. "I thought you were dead."
"The Shadow Court specializes in convenient deaths." The woman—Lady Vance—smiled without warmth. "You know me, little thief. I know you. And you—" Her gaze fixed on Kael. "—I know what you carry. The Entity fragment. The corruption that marks you as one of three."
Kael's blood chilled. "Who are you?"
"I serve the Court. I'm also a former Soul Reaver." Lady Vance spread her hands, showing empty palms. "Not active—I gave that up decades ago. But the hunger never truly fades, does it? You understand that better than anyone."
Former Soul Reaver. Another one of the hidden lineage. The volume outline's promise echoed in Kael's mind: he was not the first. Others had walked this path before him.
"What do you want?" Kael asked.
"Right now? I want to talk. My superiors want to acquire you—contain the corruption before it spreads. But I think that's short-sighted." Lady Vance gestured, and the watchers on the rooftops melted back into shadow. "You're an asset, Reaver. A dangerous one, yes, but assets can be managed. The question is whether you're interested in survival."
"Survival means nothing without answers."
"Answers." Lady Vance tilted her head. "You want to know about the original Soul Reaver. About the Entity fragments. About who really controls Thornwick's underground." She stepped closer. "I can give you those answers. All I need in return is your cooperation."
"And what does cooperation entail?"
"The Court has a problem. One of the other Fragment vessels—you've met him, I think, though you don't know what he is yet—has gone rogue. He's accelerating the Faceless One's awakening. If he succeeds, every Awakened in Thornwick dies. Including you."
Kael processed the information. The Cult wanted to release the Entities. The Court wanted to prevent it—or control it. And somewhere in that conflict, three vessels walked with fragments of something ancient growing inside them.
"Who?" he demanded. "Who's the rogue vessel?"
Lady Vance smiled. "That's the conversation I mentioned. Walk with me, and we'll discuss terms."
Mira grabbed Kael's arm. "Don't. The Court always has an angle."
"The Court's angle is profit," Lady Vance replied without looking at her. "Right now, preventing an Abyssal incursion is profitable. Let it happen, and there won't be a Thornwick left to exploit." She turned back to Kael. "You have until dawn to decide. After that, my superiors will send extraction teams instead of diplomats."
She turned and walked toward the alley's far end, pausing at the threshold. "The address is in your pocket. The Shadow Court will be watching regardless of your choice. But I'd prefer it if we could avoid unpleasantness."
Then she was gone, swallowed by darkness.
Silence settled over the alley. Brennan broke it first: "What do we do?"
Kael stared at the spot where Lady Vance had stood. His hand trembled—the Entity corruption pulsing in time with his heartbeat. The former Soul Reaver's offer hung in the air like smoke, intoxicating and dangerous.
"We regroup," he said finally. "We heal. And tomorrow, I start hunting the truth."
"But the Court—"
"The Court is a tool." Kael's voice hardened. "Tools can be used or discarded. But first, I need to understand what I'm working with."
He pulled the document from his coat—the names and locations of Cult cells, the map of tunnels leading to sealed rifts, the evidence of an organization operating in Thornwick's shadows for decades. This was his leverage. His proof that the world was not as stable as the Orders claimed.
The Faceless One's fragment stirred beneath his skin, and for a moment, Kael felt something that might have been hunger or might have been warning.
Three vessels. One Entity. And somewhere in Thornwick's endless night, his enemies were making their own plans.
Dawn was hours away. The war had just begun.
Kael looked up at the gaslit sky, where the first stars were emerging above the steam and smoke. Somewhere out there, Zarek was preparing countermeasures. Somewhere, the Cult was regrouping after their defeat. And somewhere, Lady Vance was reporting to handlers whose true motives remained hidden.
The Soul Reaver walked into the darkness, carrying stolen secrets and borrowed time.
Behind him, his team followed—not because they trusted him, but because they had nowhere else to go.
The city swallowed them whole, as it had swallowed a thousand others, and the night stretched on, patient as the Abyss itself.
