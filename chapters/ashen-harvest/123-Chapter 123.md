The narrow alleyway spat Kael out like a corpse onto a butcher's block. Three cultists in rusted iron masks flanked him before he could blink, their blades singing through the gaslit air.
"Got him." The tallest one lunged.
Kael twisted, felt steel part his sleeve and kiss his skin. Pain bloomed hot and immediate. His free hand found the attacker's throat.
[Soul Harvest: Initiated. Target: Cultist (Awakened, Stage 1). Quality: Fresh. Extracting...]
He felt the harvest begin—a cold tide rushing inward, carrying fragments of combat training, a name (Vendrick), and something else. A string of numbers seared into his consciousness. Coordinates. The location of the rusted-iron gate connecting Thornwick's undercity to the surface market district.
"Soul Reaver!" one of the cultists screamed.
Kael's palm connected with the second attacker's chest. The man convulsed, then crumpled. Another harvest. Less valuable, but the surge of energy still flooded Kael's reserves.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted combat instinct from the remains. Duration: Permanent.]
Two down. The third cultist ran. Kael sprinted after him, boots pounding against slick cobblestones.
"Stop him!" A new voice—familiar, commanding. Aldric vaulted from a fire escape, landing in the cultist's path. The old soldier's gauntleted fist connected with the man's jaw. The cultist dropped like a puppet with severed strings.
"He knows too much." Aldric hauled the unconscious cultist over his shoulder. "We need to move. Now."
Kael grabbed his bleeding arm. The wound wasn't deep, but blood dripped onto the stones. "They knew I'd be here."
"Of course they did." Aldric started walking, gesturing for Kael to follow. "The whole district's been a trap for the past three days. Every safe house compromised. Every contact silent or dead."
The alley opened into a wider passage, lit by flickering lanterns hung from rusted iron posts. Steam hissed from vents in the walls—the city's ever-present circulation system, pumping warmth through Thornwick's endless maze of tunnels.
"How many?" Kael matched Aldric's pace.
"Eight confirmed dead. Three missing, presumed captured. Mira took a wound to her shoulder but she's mobile. We're regrouping at the old Mortician's Guild annex—it's supposed to be abandoned, but it's one of the few places the cult hasn't hit."
"One of few places they haven't hit," Kael repeated. "You think it's safe?"
Aldric's laugh was hollow. "Nothing's safe anymore. But it's shelter, and Sera needs time to work."
The name hit Kael like cold water. Sera. The contact who'd been feeding them information about the cult's movements, about the sealed rifts, about the organization behind Prime Zarek's operations. She'd been working with them for two weeks now, operating under the assumption that information was worth more than coin.
"She's in trouble?"
"Not yet. But she's close." Aldric ducked under a low archway. "The cult isn't just hunting us, Kael. They're hunting something specific. Someone specific."
Kael's mind raced through possibilities. His Soul Reaver status was known to the Orders, but the cult shouldn't have access to that information. Not unless—
"Shadow Court," Aldric said, as if reading his thoughts. "One of their agents was caught trying to buy information about you from a Veiled Market trader three days ago. The trader refused, reported it to us instead."
The Shadow Court. The Orders' deniable operations arm. Monitoring him specifically meant higher-level interest. Kael filed that away for later analysis.
"The annex isn't far," Aldric continued. "But we need to be careful. The cult's been hitting in waves—small teams that probe our defenses, followed by larger strikes once we've committed resources."
"You're saying this might be a probe."
"I'm saying assume everything is a probe until proven otherwise."
They moved in silence for several minutes, navigating a labyrinth of maintenance tunnels that wound beneath Thornwick's industrial district. The air grew thick with the smell of coal dust and old copper. Kael focused on controlling his breathing, on managing the energy still coursing through him from the harvest.
The coordinates he'd extracted—those were valuable. A gate connecting the undercity to the surface market meant escape routes, supply lines, the ability to move unseen through the city's underbelly. But the cult knowing about them suggested a leak somewhere. Someone inside their network was feeding information to Prime Zarek's people.
"Sera found something," Aldric said, stopping at a junction. He pressed his back against the wall, signaling Kael to do the same.
Ahead, voices echoed through the tunnel. Two, maybe three people, speaking in low tones. The accent was wrong—formal, clipped. Not Thornwick locals.
"Awakened," Aldric breathed. "Stage Two at least. Maybe higher."
Kael's hand moved to his belt, where a short blade hung in easy reach. He had no formal training with weapons beyond what he'd harvested from the dead, but the Soul Reaver's instinct was already integrating the combat knowledge he'd taken. His muscles knew things his mind hadn't learned.
"Could go around," Aldric murmured. "Add another hour to our route."
"Or?" Kael asked.
"Or we find out who they are and what they're doing in a tunnel that leads to nowhere useful."
The decision hung between them. Kael considered the tactical implications. They were wounded, low on resources, and the cult had demonstrated an ability to predict their movements. Intelligence gathering could turn the tide. But engaging unknown Awakened carried significant risk, especially at Stage Two or above.
His arm throbbed where the cultist's blade had found him. The wound was manageable, but blood loss would eventually slow him down. They needed shelter, time to plan, information about what was coming next.
"One quick look," Kael said. "No engagement unless forced."
Aldric nodded. They pressed forward, moving along the tunnel's edge where shadows pooled deepest. The voices grew clearer as they approached.
"—seventy-two rifts confirmed weakening. The Prime's timeline is accelerating."
"The Orders will respond. They always do."
"The Orders are fractured. The Inquisitor's Hunt has them turning on each other. By the time they coordinate a response, the seal network will be compromised beyond repair."
Kael froze. Seventy-two rifts. That meant all of them. Every sealed gate between the Material and the Abyss. Prime Zarek wasn't just targeting specific locations—he was planning something global, something that would unmake the barriers entirely.
"The Thornwick convergence point is critical," the first voice continued. "If we can control the resonance—"
Footsteps. Someone approaching their position.
Aldric grabbed Kael's shoulder, pulled him into a shallow alcove. They pressed together as a figure passed within arm's reach—a woman in dark traveling clothes, her hair bound tight against her skull. She carried no visible weapons, but spiritual energy radiated from her in waves that made Kael's skin tingle.
Stage Three. Maybe higher.
She paused. Turned her head slightly toward their hiding spot.
Kael stopped breathing. Beside him, Aldric's grip tightened on his arm.
A long moment passed. The woman's eyes seemed to pierce through the shadows, through the stone, through the darkness itself. Then she turned and continued walking, disappearing around the corner.
They waited until her footsteps faded entirely before moving.
"What the hell was that?" Aldric exhaled sharply.
"Someone important." Kael's mind processed the fragments he'd overheard. "The cult knows about all seventy-two rifts weakening. They're coordinating a global unsealing."
"That's not possible. The seals are maintained by—"
"The Orders. I know. But if the Orders are distracted, fighting each other instead of the cult..." He didn't finish the thought. He didn't need to.
They reached the Mortician's Guild annex twenty minutes later. The building was exactly as Aldric had described—an old warehouse converted into a flowing facility, now abandoned and forgotten. Dust coated every surface. Broken windows admitted thin shafts of light from the street above.
Mira sat on a overturned crate near the entrance, a makeshift bandage wrapped around her shoulder. She looked up as they entered, relief crossing her face before being replaced by concern.
"Kael. You're bleeding."
" superficial." He moved past her toward the interior, where Sera hunched over a makeshift workstation—a door laid across two barrels, covered in papers and strange instruments. "Sera."
The woman raised her head. Dark circles ringed her eyes, and her hands trembled slightly—exhaustion or fear, Kael couldn't tell. She'd been feeding them information for weeks now, risking everything to expose the cult's operations. Whatever she knew, it had cost her.
"You heard them," Sera said. It wasn't a question.
"Enough to know we're in deeper than we thought." Kael approached the workstation, scanning the papers spread across its surface. Maps of Thornwick, marked with symbols he didn't recognize. Lists of names, most of them crossed out. A single sheet of vellum with coordinates written in precise, mechanical handwriting.
"The cult's not random," Sera said. "Every attack, every theft, every disappearance—it's all part of a pattern. They're not just weakening seals. They're preparing for a convergence."
"A convergence of what?" Aldric asked, entering behind them.
"All seventy-two rifts. Simultaneously." Sera pulled a map toward her, jabbing a finger at Thornwick's center. "The Sealed City sits on what's called a 'nexus point'—a location where the Veil is naturally thin. If they can trigger a resonance event here, it will cascade across the entire seal network. Every rift will open at once."
"That would—" Mira started.
"Unmake the world," Sera finished. "The Abyssal Entities would flood into the Material. Reality would tear itself apart."
Silence settled over the group. Kael stared at the map, at the web of symbols marking rift locations across the continent. Seventy-two points of potential apocalypse, all connected by invisible threads.
"How long?" he asked.
"Weeks. Maybe less." Sera's voice cracked slightly. "The cult's been harvesting soul-essence for months. They need massive amounts of energy to trigger the resonance. Every death, every Awakening, every spiritual event they've orchestrated—all feeding into a central collection point."
"The Sepulcher Tablets," Kael said.
Sera looked up sharply. "What do you know about them?"
"The first Soul Reaver created them. They contain the True Name—the key to controlling the seal network. Prime Zarek's people have been searching for them, but they haven't found them yet."
"How do you know this?"
Kael hesitated. The information came from fragments he'd harvested over the past months, pieces of a puzzle he hadn't fully assembled. He didn't know the full story, but he knew enough to understand the stakes.
"Because that's what I would be looking for," he said finally. "If I wanted to control the seals instead of just breaking them."
Aldric cursed under his breath. "The cult wants to break the seals. But someone else wants to control them. Who?"
"The Whisper King," Sera whispered.
The name hit Kael like a physical blow. Images flashed through his mind—fragments of conversations he'd overheard, patterns he'd noticed but never fully connected. The mysterious figure who'd been manipulating events from the shadows, the one who seemingly had agents everywhere and answers to questions no one should know.
"You know who he is?" Kael demanded.
"No one knows who he is. That's the point." Sera pulled another document from beneath the pile—a wanted poster, weathered and faded, bearing a sketch of a figure in flowing robes with no visible face. "He appeared fifteen years ago, right after the last major seal degradation event. Since then, he's built an organization that operates across all the Orders, all the major cities. Some people think he's a myth. Others think he's a council of individuals, passing the title between them."
"And the cult?"
"Connected. But whether the Whisper King is controlling them, opposing them, or using them for his own purposes..." Sera shook her head. "I don't know. No one does."
The uncertainty gnawed at Kael. He was operating blind, making decisions based on incomplete information while forces he couldn't see moved pieces on a board he couldn't fully perceive. The Soul Reaver's power gave him an edge, but even that had limits. He could harvest abilities, memories, knowledge from the dead—but the dead only knew what they'd known in life. And the truly powerful players never let themselves be killed.
"We need to find the tablets," Kael said. "Before the cult does. Before the Whisper King does."
"Impossible," Mira said. "No one's seen them in centuries. They might not even exist anymore."
"They exist." Kael's certainty surprised even him. The knowledge came from somewhere deep, a bone-deep certainty that defied logic. "The first Soul Reaver created them. They're hidden somewhere no one would think to look."
"Which is?"
"I don't know yet." He met Sera's eyes. "But I intend to find out."
A sound from outside—footsteps, multiple sets, moving with purpose. Aldric was at the window in an instant, peering through a crack in the boards.
"Cult enforcers," he reported. "Twelve, maybe fifteen. Formation suggests they're expecting resistance."
"Back entrance?" Mira asked, already rising despite her injury.
"Locked. Probably trapped." Aldric drew his blade. "We fight our way out or we die here."
Kael assessed the situation. Twelve to fifteen cult enforcers against four people, two of them wounded. Straight combat would be suicide. But the Soul Reaver's power gave him options others didn't have.
"The woman in the tunnel," he said. "The one from the Shadow Court. She was checking this route specifically. Why?"
Sera's eyes widened. "She was scouting an escape path. For us."
"For whoever came through next." Kael moved toward the back of the annex, toward a rusted door half-hidden behind collapsed shelving. "The Shadow Court's been monitoring me. They want me alive—for what purpose, I don't know. But they wouldn't let the cult kill me before I served their purpose."
He found the door, examined the lock. Old mechanism, simple design. His harvesting had given him a basic understanding of mechanical systems, enough to recognize a trap when he saw one.
"Not trapped," he reported. "But locked from outside. They've been watching this place."
"Then how do we get out?" Mira demanded.
Kael pressed his palm against the lock, focusing his will. The Soul Reaver's energy flowed outward, seeping into the mechanism, sensing the metal's structure. With a mental command, he felt the tumblers shift, the bolts retract.
The door swung open.
Beyond was darkness—an escape tunnel, narrow and twisting, leading down into the city's deepest levels. The air that wafted up was cold, carrying the scent of old stone and stagnant water.
"This way." Kael stepped through first, blade ready. The others followed, Aldric bringing up the rear with Mira supporting Sera between them.
They descended in silence, the sounds of the cult enforcers fading behind them. The tunnel wound through what had once been Thornwick's original foundation, stone blocks fitted together without mortar, older than the city above. Kael led them by instinct, the Soul Reaver's perception revealing paths through the darkness.
"Someone's been through here recently," Aldric observed, noting disturbed dust and scuff marks on the floor.
"The Shadow Court," Kael said. "They mapped this route. Left it for whoever they wanted to escape."
"Why would they help us?" Mira asked.
"They're not helping us. They're manipulating us. Keeping us alive because dead pieces can't be moved."
The tunnel opened into a wider chamber—a natural cavern beneath the city, fed by underground streams that carved channels through the bedrock. Pale luminescence clung to the walls, some kind of bioluminescent fungus growing in the eternal darkness.
And in the center of the chamber, waiting for them, stood a single figure.
The woman from the tunnel. The Shadow Court operative. She stood perfectly still, hands clasped behind her back, watching their approach with eyes that seemed to absorb light instead of reflect it.
"Kael Ashborn," she said. Her voice was soft, controlled, utterly without emotion. "The Soul Reaver. I've heard so much about you."
Kael raised his blade. "And you're the one who's been pulling strings."
"Pulling strings implies I created the situation. I only... Identified opportunities." She inclined her head slightly. "My name is Verity. I represent interests that prefer you remain functional."
"Your interests. The Shadow Court's interests."
"Different things, but aligned in this moment." Verity gestured, and figures emerged from the shadows around them—four, maybe five operatives, all wearing the same dark traveling clothes, all radiating spiritual energy at levels that made Kael's skin crawl. Stage Two. Stage Three. At least one higher.
"You're surrounded," Verity observed. "Outnumbered. Outmatched. But I'm not here to fight."
"Then what do you want?"
"Information. And in exchange, more information. The cult's movements, their plans, the location of the Sepulcher Tablets—you possess fragments of all of these. I can help you assemble them."
Kael's grip tightened on his blade. Trusting the Shadow Court was a fool's game—they served their own agenda, and that agenda had never aligned with anything resembling righteousness. But they were also the only faction with the resources and intelligence networks to offer meaningful assistance against a threat of this scale.
"What do you want from me specifically?" he asked.
"Access to your harvest." Verity's eyes glittered in the fungal light. "The Soul Reaver's ability to extract memories from the dead is unparalleled. There are those who died possessing knowledge we need—fragments of the seal network's construction, details of the original sealing ritual. The cult has tried to control Reavers before. They've failed. I propose a different arrangement."
"You're asking me to work for you."
"I'm proposing a mutual exchange. You harvest what we need, we provide what you need. Neither of us owns the other."
Aldric stepped forward, placing himself between Kael and Verity. "He's not your tool. No deal."
"The old soldier speaks from loyalty. Admirable, but irrelevant." Verity's gaze never left Kael's face. "This isn't about loyalty. This is about survival. The cult wants to break the seals. The Whisper King wants to control them. The Orders are too fractured to respond. You're standing in the crossfire of powers you don't fully understand, with abilities that make you a target for everyone."
"And your solution is to make me a target for one more faction?"
"My solution is to give you a chance to survive. Take it or don't. But understand—refusing means facing what's coming alone."
The ultimatum hung in the air. Kael weighed his options, the Soul Reaver's analytical capabilities processing variables and outcomes. Every path forward carried risks. Working with the Shadow Court meant entering a web of obligations that could constrict him later. Refusing meant losing access to intelligence networks that might prove essential.
"One condition," Kael said finally. "My people stay out of your operations. Any deal I make applies to me alone."
Verity smiled—a thin, cold expression that didn't reach her eyes. "Acceptable. For now."
She extended her hand. Kael hesitated, then clasped it. Her skin was cold, like touching ice.
"The cult's next target is the Sunspire Archives," Verity said, releasing his grip. "They'll hit it in three days. Whatever they're looking for there, it's critical to their timeline. Stop them, and you buy time. Fail, and everything accelerates."
"And the Sepulcher Tablets?"
"Still searching. But I have leads." She stepped back, her operatives fading into the shadows around the cavern. "I'll contact you when I know more. Don't die before then, Soul Reaver. You're more valuable alive than you realize."
She was gone before Kael could respond, swallowed by darkness as completely as if she'd never existed.
Aldric exhaled heavily. "What the hell was that?"
"An alliance of convenience," Kael said. "Temporary. Fragile. But better than nothing."
"You don't know what she wants."
"No. But I know what I want. Time. Information. A chance to stop this before it destroys everything."
Sera moved to his side, her face pale but determined. "The Sunspire Archives. That's where the Order of Dawn keeps their oldest records. If the cult's targeting it—"
"They're looking for something specific. Something related to the sealing ritual." Kael turned toward the tunnel that would lead them deeper into the city's foundations. "We need to get there first."
"Three days isn't much time," Mira observed.
"No. But it's what we have." Kael looked back at his companions—at Aldric, the veteran soldier who'd chosen to follow him; at Mira, wounded but unbowed; at Sera, who had risked everything for information that might save them all. "Let's move. We have planning to do and very little room for error."
They disappeared into the darkness, leaving the cavern empty except for the pale glow of fungi on ancient stone.
Somewhere above them, in a gaslit office overlooking Thornwick's industrial district, Prime Zarek studied a map covered in fresh markings. His agents had reported the Soul Reaver's escape—barely, in time, guided by forces Zarek didn't yet understand.
Interesting. The board was more crowded than he'd anticipated.
"Send word to the convergence site," he said to the figure waiting in the shadows. "Accelerate the timeline. The Reaver complicates things. We can't afford delays."
The figure bowed and vanished.
Zarek turned back to his map, tracing the lines that connected seventy-two points across the continent. Soon, the seals would fall. Soon, the Abyss would reclaim what was rightfully its own. And when the dust settled, only the worthy would remain.
Thornwick would burn. But it wouldn't be the only city.
Three days until the Sunspire Archives.
Three days until everything changed.
The countdown had begun.
