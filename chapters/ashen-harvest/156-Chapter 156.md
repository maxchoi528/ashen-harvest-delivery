Kael pressed his back against the alley wall, breath shallow, fingers tracing the Soul Reaver's edge. Three blocks away, the Thornwick Cathedral bells tolled midnight. Somewhere in the gaslit darkness, they were hunting him.
"The Sentinel is moving." Lyra's whisper came from the rooftop above, her silhouette barely visible against the steam-belching industrial skyline. "Eastern district. Toward the old Sepulcher grounds."
He didn't ask how she knew. Lyra always knew things she shouldn't—that was the price of her alliance, and the reason he kept her close. The Shadow Court had trained her well.
"How many with it?"
"Six. Maybe seven." A pause. "They're carrying something. Equipment. Looks like they're setting up a ritual circle."
A ritual circle meant a summoning. A summoning meant they were trying to pull something through—or push something out. Kael's stomach tightened. The sealed rifts were supposed to be stable. The Orders had confirmed it just last month during the Council briefing he'd attended as a 'consulting specialist'—the title they used for people they didn't trust but couldn't ignore.
Except the Orders lied. They always lied. He pushed off from the wall and moved toward the eastern rooftops, his boots silent on the wet slate.
The Soul Reaver pulsed against his hip, hungry. It always felt hungry now, even though the harvesting cost him nothing. The zero spiritual cost was the ability's blessing and its curse—easy power, but the world knew what he was. Every time he used it, the noose tightened.
He reached the roofline and dropped into a crouch, scanning the ruins below. The old Sepulcher had been abandoned for decades, its grand spires crumbled, its sanctified ground desecrated by time and neglect. Perfect place for a cult ritual. No Order patrol would come within a mile of it—too many bad memories, too many 'accidents' reported over the years.
"There." Lyra pointed toward a collapsed section of the eastern wing. Torchlight flickered through the gaps, orange and hungry. "They're inside the ossuary."
The ossuary. Where they kept the bones of the dead Awakened, the ones too dangerous to burn. Kael's mouth went dry.
"Stay here," he said.
"Like hell."
"I need you on overwatch. If this goes wrong—"
"If this goes wrong, you'll be dead before I can reach you." Lyra's eyes met his, hard and calculating. "But fine. I'll watch. Don't make me regret staying."
He didn't waste breath on a response. The drop from roof to ground was fifteen feet; he made it in a controlled fall, rolling into the shadow of a fallen pillar as soon as his boots touched earth.
The cultists had lit their circles with black candles—expensive, imported from the southern territories where the Veil was thinnest. He could smell the sulfur and something else, something organic and rotting. Soul-essence, burning. They were fueling the ritual with harvested energy.
Kael circled the ruins, counting figures. Seven cultists in dark robes, their faces hidden beneath hoods. One stood at the circle's center, a tall figure with hands raised in supplication. Two more flanked the entrance, armed with swords that glowed faintly—the mark of Awakened weapons, probably stolen or black-market bought.
And then he saw it.
The Sentinel.
It stood behind the central figure, a humanoid shape woven from shadow and smoke, its form flickering between solid and ethereal. Voidwalkers were never fully in the Material—the Abyss pulled at them, kept them tethered to that between-place where reality frayed. But this one was more present than most. Its edges crackled with unstable energy, and where its eyes should have been, two points of absolute darkness burned like wounds in the world.
A Voidwalker Sentinel. One of the Abyssal Entities' lieutenants, sent to oversee a cult operation.
Kael's heart hammered. He'd fought Abyssal-touched before—cultists who had bargained with Entities and won a fraction of their power. But an actual Sentinel? Those were stage-three beings at minimum. Probably stage four.
He was stage two. Ascendant.
"You're outmatched." The thought surfaced unbidden, cold and logical. "You should run."
But running meant leaving whatever they were summoning free to emerge. Running meant more dead, more rifts opened, more power flowing to the Abyss. The Orders would handle it eventually—but eventually could mean dozens dead in the meantime.
Kael had learned to calculate costs. This was one he couldn't afford to walk away from.
He moved.
The first guard died before he realized Kael was there—a precise strike to the throat, crushing cartilage, denying the man even a scream. Kael caught the body as it fell, lowering it silently to the stone floor.

Soul Harvest: You extracted combat instincts (partial) from the remains. +3 to reaction speed acquired.
Soul Harvest: You extracted no additional abilities. Corpse too degraded.
Not great, but useful. The combat instincts settled into his mind like a second skin, sharpening his awareness of the space around him. The second guard was turning when Kael's blade took him through the ribs, angled upward toward the heart.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION]
Soul Harvest: You extracted sword technique (basic) from the remains. +1 to blade proficiency acquired.
Soul Harvest: You extracted 15 units of soul-essence.
Better. The essence flowed into his reserves, that familiar cold sensation at the base of his skull. It wouldn't make him stronger directly—Soul Reaver abilities didn't work that way—but it would let him push harder when needed. Expend reserves, refill from the fallen.
The cultists at the circle's edge hadn't noticed yet. They were focused on their chanting, voices rising in that guttural tongue that made Kael's teeth ache. Whatever they were calling forth, it was almost here.
He burst through the entrance.
"STOP!" The central figure's head snapped toward him, eyes wide with fury. "Kill him! Kill the intruder!"
Two cultists broke from the circle, drawing weapons. Kael didn't slow. The Soul Reaver sang as it arced through the air, cleaving through the first attacker's guard and opening his chest in a spray of arterial red.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION]
Soul Harvest: You extracted enhanced strength (minor) from the remains. +5% physical power acquired.
Soul Harvest: You extracted 28 units of soul-essence.
The second cultist swung a mace at Kael's head. He ducked, feeling the wind of its passage against his hair, and drove his elbow into the man's solar plexus. The cultist doubled over; Kael's knee came up to meet his face with a wet crack.
"Sacrilege!" The central figure's voice rose to a shriek. "You defile sacred ground! The Court will—"
"The Court can file a complaint."
Kael threw the incapacitated cultist into the circle, disrupting the ritual pattern. Candles toppled, black flames guttering out. The chanting dissolved into chaos.
And the Sentinel moved.
One moment it stood motionless behind the central figure. The next, it was before Kael, shadow-claws extended, that burn-wound gaze fixed on his face with terrible intelligence.
"SOUL REAVER," it spoke—and the word came from everywhere, from the stones beneath his feet, from the air in his lungs, from the void behind his eyes. "THE COURT SENDS ITS REGARDS."
Kael blocked the first strike on instinct, the impact sending shockwaves through his arm. The Sentinel's claws passed through steel like it was paper; only his own Awakened reflexes let him twist aside, feeling one claw slice through his coat and score a line of fire across his ribs.
He countered, driving the Soul Reaver into the Sentinel's chest. The blade passed through shadow and found something solid—a core, a heart of compressed Abyssal energy. The Sentinel screamed, a sound that existed outside normal hearing, and Kael felt his vision blur as the feedback hit his mind.
Not good. Not good at all.
He twisted the blade, pouring his harvested essence into the strike. The cold fire of consumed souls burned through the connection, and the Sentinel staggered back, its form destabilizing.
"IMPOSSIBLE," it hissed. "YOU ARE BUT STAGE TWO. THIS CANNOT—"
"I eat things stronger than me." Kael withdrew the blade and struck again, targeting the same point. "That's kind of my thing."
The Sentinel's form flickered, shadow and smoke tearing at the edges. It was weakening—but not fast enough. Two more cultists had recovered from the chaos and were approaching with weapons raised, while the central figure had resumed chanting, rebuilding the disrupted circle with frantic gestures.
Kael made a choice.
He disengaged from the Sentinel and sprinted toward the circle's center, toward the chanting figure. The Sentinel lunged after him, claws raking the air inches from his back. He didn't look back. Couldn't afford to.
The central figure saw him coming. Raised a hand, fingers splayed, and spoke a word of power.
Pain lanced through Kael's skull. His vision fractured into prisms of light and dark, and his legs buckled. A mental attack—psychic assault, designed to incapacitate.
But the Soul Reaver had taught him to consume more than flesh.
He reached for the pain, felt it with his ability's touch, and pulled.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION]
Soul Harvest: You extracted psychic scream (single use) from the remains. Mental attack capacity acquired.
Soul Harvest: You extracted 12 units of soul-essence.
The central figure collapsed, nose bleeding, eyes rolled back in his head. Whatever he'd tried to hit Kael with had been turned back on him—a crude weapon, badly aimed, but effective in the hands of someone who could eat the attack.
Kael kept moving. The cultists flanking the circle raised their weapons; he ducked under one swing and hamstrung another, then used the falling body as a springboard to vault over the third. The Soul Reaver found flesh each time, and each time the system notifications chimed in his mind like a dark orchestra.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION]
Soul Harvest: You extracted nothing of value from the remains.
Soul Harvest: You extracted 18 units of soul-essence.
Soul Harvest: You extracted enhanced reflexes (temporary) from the remains. +8% speed for several minutes.
Soul Harvest: You extracted 34 units of soul-essence.
The ritual circle was destabilizing without its conductor. Black flames erupted in erratic patterns, and the air itself seemed to thicken, reality straining against whatever force the cultists had been trying to summon. Kael caught a glimpse of something beyond the circle's edge—a massive shape pressing against the Veil, trying to break through—but it was fading now, the connection severed by the chaos.
The Sentinel screamed in fury. It crossed the distance in a heartbeat, shadow-form accelerating beyond what should have been possible. Kael turned to face it, knowing he couldn't dodge, couldn't block, couldn't do anything but die.
Lyra's arrow took it through the eye.
The shaft was a meter of blackened iron, etched with Order runes, burning with concentrated spiritual energy. It punched through the Sentinel's skull and out the other side, trailing wisps of dissipating shadow. The creature froze, its form shuddering, then collapsed inward like a dying star.
Kael didn't waste the opportunity. He threw himself at the fallen Sentinel, hands pressing against its dissolving flesh. The Soul Reaver surged in response, drinking deep.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION]
Soul Harvest: You extracted VOID STEP from the remains. Spatial displacement technique acquired.
Soul Harvest: You extracted 156 units of soul-essence.
Soul Harvest: You extracted fragments of Abyssal knowledge. +2% resistance to psychic assault.
Kael gasped as information flooded his mind. Void Step—the ability to blink short distances, folding space like origami, appearing where shadow touched shadow. It was powerful. It was dangerous. And it came with a price: for a moment as he activated it, he would be between realms, vulnerable to anything that dwelled in that between-place.
But he could use it. That was what mattered.
"KAEL!" Lyra's voice from the rooftop, urgent. "More coming! Six—no, eight! They flanked around the western ruins!"
He looked up, scanning the darkness. She was right. Figures were emerging from the shadows, weapons drawn, moving with the coordinated precision of trained killers. Shadow Court operatives, not the cultist rabble he'd been fighting.
The central figure stirred on the ground, groaning. One of the cultists who had survived Kael's initial assault was helping him to his feet, supporting his weight.
"The Court knows what you are," the central figure rasped, blood still trickling from his nose. "They know what you take. We've been watching you for months, Reaver. Waiting for you to make yourself useful."
Kael's grip tightened on his blade. "Watching me do what, exactly?"
"Leading us to the Sepulcher Tablets. The True Names. You've been collecting them without even knowing—every soul you harvest, every fragment you consume. They all point back to the same source." The man laughed, a wet, bubbling sound. "The first Soul Reaver. His tomb. His legacy. The Court has waited centuries for someone to walk the path again."
"You're lying."
"Am I?" The man's eyes gleamed with something like triumph. "Ask yourself why they let you live after the Ironveil incident. Why the Orders keep 'consulting' you instead of locking you in a cell. You're a key, Reaver. A tool. And when the time comes—"
Kael threw his blade.
The Soul Reaver spun end over end, a wheel of steel in the darkness. The cultist who'd been supporting the central figure saw it coming and tried to raise his arm, but he was too slow. The blade took him through the chest, pinning him to the stone wall behind him.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION]
Soul Harvest: You extracted Order interrogation training (partial) from the remains. Resistance to mental coercion +5%.
Soul Harvest: You extracted 22 units of soul-essence.
The central figure didn't even flinch. "The Court sends its regards, Kael Ashborn. We'll meet again."
Then he and the remaining cultists dissolved into shadow, their forms bleeding away like ink in water. A displacement technique—but not Void Step. Something older, cruder. They left behind only the smell of sulfur and the echo of laughter.
Kael retrieved his blade from the corpse, wiping it clean on the dead man's cloak. His hands were steady. His mind was not.
The Shadow Court had been watching him. Cultists in Thornwick weren't random—they were organized, coordinated, part of something larger. The Orders knew about the sealed rifts but weren't acting. And now a Sentinel had mentioned the Six Orders, the 72 sealed rifts, as if they were relevant to whatever the cult was planning.
He needed information. Real information, not cultist taunts.
"Kael." Lyra dropped from the rooftop, landing in a crouch beside him. Her bow was still drawn, arrow nocked, eyes scanning the perimeter. "We need to move. The Sentinel's death will have drawn attention."
"The Shadow Court."
"Yes." Her jaw tightened. "They shouldn't have been here. This wasn't a standard cult operation—they were expecting resistance. Expecting you."
"Because I'm a Soul Reaver?"
"Because you're collecting something." Lyra met his gaze, and for the first time since he'd known her, Kael saw uncertainty in her eyes. "I didn't know the Court had this much interest in the old relics. The Sepulcher Tablets... Those are myths. Stories to frighten initiates."
"Stories don't summon Sentinels."
"No. They don't." She grabbed his arm, pulling him toward the western exit. "But questions later. Survival now."
They ran through the ossuary's crumbling corridors, past piles of ancient bones and collapsed masonry. Behind them, the ritual circle continued to destabilize, black flames consuming what remained of the ceremonial equipment. By morning, there would be nothing left but ash and questions.
Kael filed the questions away. The Shadow Court's interest. The Sepulcher Tablets. The True Names. Lyra's obvious unease. Something was happening beneath the surface of Thornwick's politics, something that involved the cult, the Orders, and possibly the entire seal network.
He would find out what. And if it threatened him—well. The Soul Reaver had opinions about threats.
They emerged into a rain-slicked street, the gas lamps casting long shadows across the cobblestones. Lyra led him through a series of turns that avoided the main thoroughfares, staying in the darkness where the light didn't reach.
"SAFEHOUSE," she said finally, pushing open a door hidden behind a collapsed awning. "Old Order property. Abandoned after the purge."
They descended into a basement that smelled of dust and old secrets. Lyra lit a phosphor lamp, its cold blue light revealing a space lined with bookshelves and filing cabinets. Maps covered the walls—Thornwick's districts, the Order territories, the sealed rift locations.
"How much do you know about the Shadow Court?" Kael asked, keeping his voice neutral.
"Enough to be dangerous." She pulled a bottle from a cabinet and poured two measures of something amber. "They were Order deniable operations, originally. Black teams, wet work, the things the Concordat wouldn't allow. But they've since become... Independent. Contracting their services to whoever pays."
"And now they're monitoring me."
"Now they're monitoring you." Lyra handed him a glass. "I don't know why. But if they're interested, it means you're a piece on a board you can't see."
Kael drank. The liquor burned going down, sharp and cleansing. He thought about the cultist's words. The first Soul Reaver. His tomb. His legacy.
"I need to find out more."
"The Mortician's Guild might have records. They archive everything death-related in the city." Lyra hesitated. "But they've been compromised. Someone in their leadership is feeding information to the Court."
"Then we go through other channels."
"Other channels are worse." She sat down heavily, exhaustion finally showing in the lines of her face. "The cult isn't random, Kael. They're organized. They have resources, intelligence, access. Someone is funding them at a level that suggests Order involvement."
"You think a Court or faction is behind this?"
"I think the Shadow Court's independence is a lie. I think they're still working for someone, and I think that someone wants one of the 72 rifts opened." She met his eyes. "The Great Rift. It's the largest, the most powerful. If it goes, everything else follows."
The Great Rift. Kael had heard stories about it since childhood—how the first Awakened had sealed it at the cost of their own lives, how the Entity beyond it was so powerful that even the Abyssal Entities feared it. If that seal broke, the Veil wouldn't just thin.
It would shatter.
"I need to know more about the cult structure," he said. "Who's leading them. What they're planning. When and where the next move will be."
"Intelligence gathering takes time."
"Then we make time." Kael set down his glass. "But not tonight. Tonight we rest. And tomorrow—"
A knock at the door.
Lyra's hand went to her bow. Kael reached for the Soul Reaver. They exchanged a look, a silent communication built from weeks of working together.
He moved toward the door, positioning himself to the side. Lyra covered the angles. Kael's fingers found the handle.
He pulled it open.
The corridor beyond was empty. But painted on the far wall, in fresh red that dripped like wounds, were words:
THE COURT REMEMBERS WHAT YOU FORGOT.
BENEATH THE SEPUBCHER, THE KEY AWAITS.
PREPARE FOR HARVEST.
Kael stared at the message. His mind raced through possibilities, implications, connections he hadn't made before. The Shadow Court wasn't just watching him. They were guiding him. Dangling information like bait, leading him toward something they wanted him to find.
But what?
"Kael." Lyra's voice was tense. "There's more. On the floor."
He looked down. A small bundle lay at the threshold, wrapped in black cloth. He crouched, hesitating only a moment before unwrating it.
Inside was a tooth. Human, adult, still carrying flecks of dried blood. And a note, written in the same red paint as the wall message:
FROM SERA'S SISTER. THE MORTICIAN'S GUILD KEEPS MORE THAN BODIES.
Kael's blood ran cold. Sera—his ally, his contact in the city's underworld. Her sister was supposed to be dead. But the Mortician's Guild had been harvesting soul-essence from the living. Illegal. Unthinkable. And if the Shadow Court knew about it, if they had proof—
"This is a trap," Lyra said flatly. "They want you to go to the Mortician's Guild."
"Probably."
"Are you going?"
Kael looked at the tooth, at the message, at the implications of everything he'd learned tonight. The cult organized. The Shadow Court watching. The Orders silent. The sealed rifts weakening.
He thought about what the cultist had said. You're a key. A tool.
Maybe. But keys could open doors their makers didn't expect.
"Yes," he said. "I'm going."
Lyra cursed under her breath. "Then we plan. Carefully."
"We plan tomorrow." Kael pocketed the tooth, the message, the weight of new knowledge settling into his mind. "Tonight, we rest. And tomorrow—"
The phosphor lamp flickered. Went out.
In the darkness, Kael heard footsteps. Not from the corridor. From inside the safehouse. From the shadows that shouldn't have held anyone.
Lyra nocked an arrow, drawing it back without hesitation.
"Show yourself," she said. "Or I put one through your skull."
A pause. Then a voice, soft and cultured and familiar in a way that made Kael's stomach clench:
"Such hostility. And here I thought we were colleagues."
A figure stepped from the shadows. Tall, lean, wearing the insignia of the Order of Dawn on his collar. He smiled, and the expression didn't reach his eyes.
"Commander Dorian," Kael said, and the name tasted like ash. "To what do I owe the pleasure?"
The Commander's smile widened. "I heard you'd had an adventure tonight, Kael. The Sentinel. The cultists. the spectacle." He spread his hands, a gesture of false openness. "I thought we should talk. About what you've learned. About what you're planning to do with it."
"Or?"
"Or the Order takes a more... Direct interest in your activities." Dorian's gaze flicked to Lyra. "Your companion, for instance. Her history with the Shadow Court is fascinating. I wonder how the Council would react to that information."
Kael's hand drifted toward the Soul Reaver. Dorian noticed, and something like anticipation crossed his features.
"I wouldn't," he said mildly. "I'm not alone. And killing an Order Commander would bring consequences even you couldn't escape." He turned toward the door, pausing at the threshold. "Think about what I said, Kael. Think about who your friends are. And think about whether you're truly in control of your own path."
He stepped through the door and was gone, his footsteps receding down the corridor.
Silence filled the safehouse. The phosphor lamp flickered back to life, casting everything in cold blue light.
Kael stood motionless, processing. Commander Dorian. Order of Dawn. One of the highest-ranking Awakened in Thornwick, with access to Council intelligence and Concordat protocols.
The same Commander Dorian who had authorized his release from death row.
"What just happened?" Lyra demanded.
Kael didn't answer immediately. His mind was racing through possibilities, aligning the pieces. The Shadow Court. The cult. The Mortician's Guild. The Order of Dawn. And now Commander Dorian, appearing at a safehouse that was supposed to be secret, threatening them with exposure.
The game was bigger than he'd thought. And he was more deeply involved than he'd realized.
"They're all connected," he said finally. "The cult, the Shadow Court, the Orders. Something's happening beneath the surface, and I'm at the center of it whether I want to be or not."
"So what do we do?"
Kael looked at the tooth in his palm. Sera's sister. The Mortician's Guild. The message beneath the Sepulcher.
The key awaits.
"I find out what they want," he said. "And then I take it from them."
Lyra stared at him for a long moment. Then she laughed—a short, sharp sound without much humor.
"You're insane."
"Probably." Kael sheathed the Soul Reaver and moved toward the safehouse's back exit. "But I'm also right. And tomorrow, we start proving it."
He didn't look back. Behind him, he heard Lyra gather her things and follow.
Outside, the rain fell harder, washing the blood from the streets, hiding the evidence of everything that had happened.
Kael walked into the storm, the weight of secrets heavy on his shoulders, and the future waiting to be seized.
