Blood painted the stone walls crimson. Kael rolled beneath a falling timber, shards of broken masonry biting into his shoulder as the corridor shuddered from another explosion. Above, through a collapsed section of the Order of Dawn's garrison, he glimpsed the violet tear in the sky—the Great Rift vomiting Abyssal corruption like an infected wound.
Three cultists poured through the breach, their movements synchronized with unsettling precision. Kael counted six more converging from the eastern stairwell. They wore no masks, no identifying symbols. Just unmarked robes and the glazed eyes of fanatics whose souls had already departed, leaving hollow vessels animated by borrowed power.
"Contact front!" A garrison soldier scrambled past, spear levelled. A cultist's blade opened his throat in a single stroke.
Kael remained pressed against the rubble. His fingers brushed a corpse half-buried under debris—an Awakened, judging by the spiritual residue still clinging to cooling flesh. His Reaver senses prickled. Fresh. Within minutes of death.
The system notification materialized unbidden:
[Soul Harvest Available: Target deceased Awakened. Estimated yield: 34% capacity Spiritual Strike Technique.]
His jaw tightened. Harvesting here meant exposure. Every Awakened within range would sense the transfer. The Shadow Court had eyes everywhere in Thornwick, and the Orders had hunters trained specifically to detect Reaver signatures.
But those cultists were Ascendant-stage fighters cutting through garrison troops like wheat.
Kael's hand closed around the dead Awakened's wrist. The warmth was fading rapidly.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 34% capacity Spiritual Strike Technique from the remains. Technique Acquired: Spiritual Strike Lv.3."
Power surged through his meridians. The dead man's final combat memory flickered behind Kael's eyes—blade work, foot positioning, the precise moment to strike. Kael's body learned in seconds what the original had practiced for years.
The eastern stairwell erupted with another wave of screams.
Kael rose from the rubble, the stolen technique settling into his repertoire like a key finding its lock. Spiritual Strike manifested as a thin blade of condensed spiritual energy along his forearm—not as refined as true Awakened techniques, but serviceable.
He moved toward the stairwell.

Two cultists guarded the corridor junction, their attention fixed on the sounds of combat echoing from the main hall. Kael materialized from shadow, his Reaver-enhanced stealth letting him approach within arm's reach before either recognized the threat.
The first cultist died before his lungs could draw breath for an alarm. Spiritual Strike carved through the man's spiritual shielding like paper. Kael caught the body, lowered it silently, then advanced on the second.
This one sensed something. Swiveled. Mouth opening.
Kael's hand clamped over the cultist's face, fingers pressing into pressure points that suppressed vocalization. A quick twist, and the neck broke with a wet crack.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 8% capacity Fanatic Devotion from the remains. Tolerance to psychological influence increased."
The notification carried no satisfaction. Fanatic Devotion tasted like ash and copper even secondhand—he could sense the original owner's fanatical devotion to an Entity, a subsumption of self into something larger. Revolting, but useful. Those cultists fought without fear of death because they no longer truly lived.
The main hall came into view through a shattered archway. What Kael saw there stopped him cold.
The garrison commander—Lady Ashworth, a Transcendent-stage Awakened renowned for her defensive techniques—stood cornered against a collapsed pillar. Blood matted her silver hair. Three cultists ringed her with predatory patience, forcing her to defend against multiple angles.
Beside them, a figure in black leather armor observed with clinical detachment. A silver eye-patch covered one eye, and his exposed cheek bore the ritual scarification of the Shadow Court's upper echelons.
"Commander." The Shadow Court agent's voice carried no urgency. "The Order of Dawn's garrison falls in under an hour. Your reinforcements won't arrive. The eastern gate has already been breached at three points."
"You'll never take Thornwick." Ashworth spat blood. "The Council of Six—"
"The Council is paralyzed by internal disputes. Half your Order masters are dealing with insurrections in their own cities. Did you think this assault was random?" The agent smiled without warmth. "Every Order faces coordinated strikes tonight. The Abyssal Cults have learned to exploit your fractures."
Kael pressed himself against the wall. His heartbeat pounded in his ears. The agent's words painted a picture he didn't like—the cults weren't acting randomly. Someone had orchestrated this, waited for the perfect moment when the Six Orders were too busy fighting each other to present a united front.
The agent raised a hand. "Last offer. Surrender, and you'll be given to the Cult of the Bleeding Moon. They'll hollow you out, but your body will serve a purpose. Refuse, and I'll take your soul myself. Shadow Court has use for Transcendent-stage essence."
Ashworth's eyes flickered. For a moment, fear showed through her composure.
Then she threw her spear.
The agent sidestepped lazily, but Ashworth hadn't aimed at him. The blade pinned a cultist's hand to the wall, buying her two seconds to raise her defensive barrier.
Two seconds wasn't enough.
Kael burst from cover, Spiritual Strike extended. The first cultist died pivoting toward the new threat. The second managed a clumsy parry that Kael shattered with a follow-up strike, then a palm strike to the throat.
The third cultist and the Shadow Court agent reacted simultaneously. Kael threw himself sideways as shadow blades materialized where his chest had been. The cultist's spiritual energy scythed through the air where Kael's head had been.
"Reaver." The agent's tone shifted from boredom to sharp interest. "Interesting. The Order of Dawn's pet monster decides to play hero."
Kael rolled to his feet, putting distance between himself and the agent. His eyes swept the room—Ashworth rallying behind her barrier, two cultists dead, one pinned, one engaging him.
"What do you want?" Ashworth's voice carried military steadiness despite her wounds.
"Same thing you do. Thornwick standing at dawn." Kael parried a cultist strike, riposted, scored a hit. "After that, we can discuss how your Order's leadership failed to see this coming."
"The Shadow Court orchestrated this?"
"The cults, yes. The Court just positions itself to profit from the chaos." Kael drove Spiritual Strike through the cultist's guard, ending the engagement. "Every Order faces attacks tonight. Someone wanted the Concordat tested. Someone wanted to know if you'd help each other."
The agent laughed softly. "Perceptive for a Reaver. Most of your kind are just parasites feeding on the dying."
Kael turned to face him fully. The agent remained relaxed, shadow blades orbiting his hands like patient serpents.
"You're not here to take Thornwick," Kael said slowly. "If you wanted the city, you'd have committed more assets. This is a probe."
"And you've passed the test." The agent's smile didn't reach his eye. "The Orders can't coordinate. They fracture at the first serious pressure. When the Great Rift finally opens—and it will—the 72 sealed Entities will pour through, and your precious Orders will fall one by one."
"You sound confident for someone outnumbered."
The agent gestured. Shadows at the corridor's far end shifted, revealing more cultists emerging from stairwells.
"I have twelve Ascendant-stage fighters and one of my own skills at Transcendent level. You have a wounded commander, a Reaver who barely reached Sovereign, and approximately thirty garrison soldiers who've already lost half their number." He spread his hands. "The mathematics are unfavorable."
Kael felt the weight of the situation. The agent wasn't bluffing. Even with Ashworth's combat prowess restored, they'd be overwhelmed by numbers alone.
"Soul Harvest," Kael muttered. "Commanders don't typically die on battlefields like this."
Ashworth's eyes narrowed. "What are you suggesting?"
"You have enemies inside your Order. People who wanted this garrison to fall. Who fed intelligence to the cults." Kael met her gaze. "Give me someone to harvest, and I'll give you an edge."
"That's—"
"Reaver trickery," the agent supplied. "Harvesting stolen power from the fallen. Commander, your moral objections can wait until after we're dead."
Ashworth's expression flickered through calculation. Military pragmatism warred with Order doctrine. Doctrine lost.
"The pillar. Cultist's hand."
Kael didn't waste time with thanks. He moved to the pinned cultist, whose face had gone grey from pain. A quick examination confirmed the man's Awakened status—Ascendant-stage, recently advanced, spiritual channels still raw from the transition.
No wonder they'd fought so recklessly. New Awakeneds often overestimated their capabilities.
Kael placed his hand on the cultist's forehead.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 67% capacity Void Blade Technique from the remains. Technique Acquired: Void Blade Lv.4."
The technique slammed into his consciousness like a frozen river. Void Blade wasn't a weapon but a method—channeling spiritual energy into a cutting edge that ignored conventional spiritual shielding. Brutal, efficient, and exactly what he needed.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted residual Entity corruption from the remains. Corruption Seed planted. Warning: Extended exposure may trigger unwanted alignment shifts."
Kael's stomach turned. Entity corruption. The cultist hadn't just been a fanatic—he'd been touched by something from beyond the Veil. That explained the synchronized movements, the lack of self-preservation.
"Your harvest complete?" Ashworth's voice carried an edge.
Kael rose, Void Blade manifesting along his other arm. "I'll need the agent."
"Greedy." The agent showed his teeth. "But fair."
The next thirty seconds were a blur of violence.
Void Blade severed shadow constructs before they fully formed. Spiritual Strike handled the cultists who rushed to support their commander. Kael moved through the engagement like a phantom, harvesting technique fragments from each fallen enemy.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 23% capacity Shadow Step Technique from the remains. Technique Acquired: Shadow Step Lv.2."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 41% capacity Corruption Resistance from the remains. Resistance to Entity influence increased."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 12% capacity Soul Sight from the remains. Perceptual accuracy improved."
The Shadow Court agent fought with professional competence. His shadow blades were faster than Kael's Void Blade, his experience deeper. But Kael had something the agent didn't expect—access to techniques the man had never encountered.
Void Blade wasn't common knowledge. When Kael's blade passed through the agent's defensive parry and opened a gash across his ribs, genuine surprise crossed the man's face.
"You're not a natural Sovereign," the agent gasped, stumbling back. "You bought those techniques."
"Borrowed," Kael corrected, pressing the attack. "From people who won't miss them."
Ashworth flanked, her recovered spear finding gaps in the agent's defense. The man retreated, shadow blades flickering desperately.
"Interesting." The agent's tone turned clinical even through obvious pain. "A Reaver operating openly. The Shadow Court will remember this."
"The Shadow Court can go to hell."
"Many have already made that journey." The agent's form blurred, shadow energies condensing around him. "But not tonight. We'll meet again, Reaver. When the Orders have torn each other apart and the Abyss rises unopposed."
He vanished into darkness, leaving only a cooling blood trail.
Kael's legs threatened to buckle. Multiple harvests in rapid succession had drained something deeper than spiritual energy—his reserves were fine, but the mental processing of foreign techniques had overwhelmed his capacity for absorption.
Ashworth caught his arm, steadying him. "Can you stand?"
"For now." Kael straightened, forcing his breathing to steady. "Your garrison?"
"Holding the main hall. Barely." She studied him with eyes that had seen too much to be surprised by anything. "The Order of Dawn will want to debrief you."
"The Order of Dawn can wait until Thornwick isn't burning."
"They won't accept that."
"Then they'll learn to accept a Reaver operating independently." Kael pulled free, surveying the corridor. Cultist bodies littered the floor. Through a shattered window, he could see fires spreading across the city's eastern districts. "Your intelligence network. Who fed the cults information about this garrison's deployment?"
Ashworth's expression darkened. "That investigation will be conducted internally."
"By people who might be compromised?" Kael shook his head. "The Shadow Court agent was right about one thing. Your Orders are fractured. Someone inside the Order of Dawn wanted this garrison to fall."
"Accusations require evidence."
"Evidence requires access." Kael met her gaze. "I just harvested techniques from eleven cultists and one Shadow Court operative. Somewhere in that mess of stolen memories, there's a connection. A chain of command. Names and locations."
"Then you'll share that intelligence with the Order of Dawn."
"I'll share it with whoever's fighting the cults." Kael turned toward the main hall, where sounds of combat still echoed. "Right now, that means your garrison. Tomorrow, it might mean a different Order. Or no Order at all."
He walked into the smoke and screaming without looking back.

The main hall had become a charnel house. Garrison soldiers fought back-to-back in defensive squares, their discipline admirable against opponents who felt no pain and showed no fear. Cultists fell in waves, but for every one who died, two more emerged from the breaches.
Kael deployed Void Blade and Spiritual Strike in alternating strikes, harvesting from each fallen cultist to fuel the next engagement. The system notifications blurred together:
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 15% capacity Cult Mind Link from the remains. Mental fortitude improved."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 34% capacity Abyssal Resistance from the remains. Resistance to Entity corruption increased."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 8% capacity Blade Mastery from the remains. Combat precision improved."
The techniques varied wildly in quality. Some were barely coherent fragments—the products of spiritual energy burned hastily into corpses than carefully extracted. Others were refined abilities, techniques the cultists had practiced extensively before their deaths.
But underlying everything was that taint of corruption. Entity influence threaded through the harvested memories like rot through timber. Each extraction left a residue that Kael had to actively suppress.
A sergeant rallied a squad near the eastern entrance. "Reinforcements from the Order of Iron! Three blocks east!"
Kael pivoted toward the window. Through smoke and flame, he glimpsed the approaching column—iron-grey armor, heavy shields, the distinctive hammers of the Iron Vanguard's assault companies.
But something felt wrong.
His Reaver senses reached toward the approaching soldiers. Most registered as genuine Awakened—Spiritual signatures crisp and healthy. But threaded among them, Kael detected distortions. Shadows wearing human forms. Corruption masquerading as conviction.
"Commander!" Kael's voice cut through the battle noise. "Your reinforcements—"
"Iron Vanguard assault company." Ashworth appeared at his side, breathing heavily. "Third Hammer battalion."
"Your intelligence confirmed their approach?"
"Dawn courier, twenty minutes ago." Ashworth's face had gone pale. "What's wrong?"
Kael pointed toward the advancing column. "Count the soldiers. Then count the spiritual signatures. The numbers don't match."
Ashworth's eyes narrowed. Her spiritual perception swept the approaching force, and her expression curdled.
"Forty soldiers. Sixty signatures."
"The cults don't just infiltrate individual agents." Kael's mind raced through the implications. "They infiltrate entire units. The Iron Vanguard's assault company—how many confirmed Awakened do they normally deploy?"
"Full company, sixty Awakened at various stages. But—"
"But some of those Awakened might not be what they appear." Kael watched the approaching column. The Iron Vanguard soldiers moved with perfect discipline, shields raised, hammers ready. Impressive formation. Perfect cover for hidden threats. "They'll reach the garrison in two minutes. When they do, they'll have access to every defensive position your surviving soldiers are holding."
"We can refuse entry—"
"And insult the Iron Vanguard while Abyssal cultists are actively breaching Thornwick's walls?" Kael shook his head. "The Concordat exists to prevent Order warfare. Turning away allied forces during a crisis would be a political disaster."
"Then what do you suggest?"
Kael's eyes swept the hall. Eighty garrison soldiers remained alive, though many were wounded. The cultist assault had subsided—the surviving fanatics retreating through the breaches than dying pointlessly against organized resistance.
The hall's layout offered limited options. Stone walls, narrow windows, one main entrance and two side doors. Defensible, but not infinite.
"I need thirty volunteers," Kael said. "Awakened or spiritually sensitive. People who won't flinch at what's about to happen."
Ashworth studied him. "What are you planning?"
"Your Dawn couriers. How many did you dispatch before the attack?"
"Four. Two returned with confirmation of Iron Vanguard approach. One never came back. The fourth..." She paused. "The fourth reported seeing cult activity near the eastern gate, then vanished."
"Vanished." Kael's jaw tightened. "The courier who confirmed the Iron Vanguard approach. Was there anything unusual about their report?"
"Standard confirmation. Friendly forces approaching from the northeast, estimated arrival in twenty minutes."
"Standard confirmation." Kael repeated the words slowly. "No mention of the gate breaches. No warning that the eastern district was already compromised."
The implication settled between them.
"Your courier was compromised," Ashworth said flatly. "Or your courier was a plant from the beginning."
"Either way, the Iron Vanguard assault company isn't coming to help." Kael turned toward the garrison soldiers, raising his voice. "I need thirty volunteers. Combat experience preferred. Awakened stages two through four. You'll be executing a tactical withdrawal while fighting a rearguard action against forces that outnumber you three to one."
Sergeant Harwick—the man who'd rallied the squad earlier—stepped forward. "You want us to die for a garrison we can't hold?"
"I want you to die for something that matters." Kael met the man's eyes. "The Iron Vanguard company approaching contains infiltrators. When they reach this hall, they'll secure the defensive positions, then open the walls to a second wave of cultists. Thornwick's eastern district falls within the hour. Every Order in the city receives the same treatment simultaneously. By dawn, the Six Orders will be too fragmented to mount a coordinated defense."
"And you're asking us to stop this?"
"I'm asking you to make them think they've won, then prove them wrong." Kael gestured toward the main entrance. "When the Iron Vanguard arrives, they'll expect a grateful garrison welcoming allied support. Instead, they'll face resistance. Your commander will declare a tactical withdrawal to secondary positions. The infiltrators won't be able to act openly without revealing themselves."
"Or they'll cut us down before we can withdraw."
"Some of you will die. That's war." Kael's voice carried no false comfort. "But if we let those infiltrators inside, everyone in this hall dies anyway. The only question is whether your deaths accomplish anything."
Sergeant Harwick turned to his soldiers. A chorus of nods and shouted confirmations. Within minutes, thirty soldiers had formed up near the side entrance.
Ashworth watched the preparation with an expression caught between gratitude and disgust. "A Reaver commanding Order of Dawn soldiers. My superiors will have opinions."
"Your superiors can voice their opinions after Thornwick survives the night." Kael checked the edge of his Void Blade—spiritual energy condensed along the blade's length, sharp enough to cut through iron. "I need your fastest courier. Someone who can reach the Order of Dawn's central command within ten minutes."
"Lieutenant Vess. She's hidden near the western stairwell."
Kael found the lieutenant—a young woman with Dawn's silver insignia branded into her cheek, indicating family lineage. Her eyes carried the sharp focus of someone who'd been preparing to die before circumstances changed.
"Lieutenant. I need you to deliver a message to whoever's commanding your Order's remaining forces."
"What message?"
Kael outlined the situation. The coordinated cult attacks. The compromised reinforcements. The evidence buried in harvested memories that connected the strikes to a unified command structure. He kept it concise—military briefing than explanation.
"The message is simple," Kael concluded. "The cults aren't random. Someone orchestrated this. Find the source, and you find the weakness in their coordination."
Vess memorized the details with professional efficiency. "And if command doesn't believe a Reaver's intelligence?"
"Then Thornwick falls, and your Order loses another city." Kael handed her a small vial—soul-essence extracted during the harvest, enough to fund a small company's operations for a month. "Travel expenses. Move fast, stay hidden, report only to verified Dawn officers."
Vess accepted the vial without comment. Awakened economics in action—even enemies shared currency when survival was at stake.
The Iron Vanguard column reached the garrison's outer perimeter. Through the shattered windows, Kael watched the formation halt, standard-bearer raising the Iron's grey and black standard.
A herald's voice rang out: "Iron Vanguard Third Hammer, responding to garrison distress call! Open your doors for allied entry!"
Kael caught Ashworth's eye. She nodded once, then raised her voice: "Garrison Commander Ashworth authorizes allied entry. Eastern entrance only. State your company designation and officer complement."
The herald's response came immediately: "Iron Vanguard Third Hammer Battalion, Commander Aldric Voss commanding. Sixty Awakened, full company complement."
Sixty. The number matched Kael's earlier count. But sixty signatures meant sixty infiltrators mixed with genuine Vanguard soldiers—or sixty infiltrators wearing Vanguard skins.
"One moment." Ashworth descended from her position, movements deliberately slow. "Sergeant Harwick, position your rearguard. We execute the withdrawal on my signal."
The garrison soldiers who'd volunteered for the suicide mission formed up near the side entrance. Kael joined them, Void Blade manifesting along both arms.
"First contact will be the infiltrators," he murmured to Harwick. "They'll try to secure the entrance and prevent our withdrawal. Your job is to create enough chaos that they can't act openly."
"And if they act anyway?"
"Then we die loudly enough that everyone watching knows the Iron Vanguard contains traitors." Kael checked the corridor. "When I engage, follow through the gap I create. Don't stop, don't hesitate. The hall behind us leads to secondary positions in the old cathedral. From there, we coordinate with whatever loyal forces remain."
The main doors groaned open. Grey-armored soldiers poured through, shields raised, hammers ready. Their formation was textbook perfect—disciplined, coordinated, intimidating.
Kael watched their spiritual signatures. Most burned clean, the distinctive rhythm of genuine Awakened training. But woven between them, he detected the distortions. Shadows with weight. Corruptions wearing human faces.
Commander Aldric Voss entered at the column's head. An impressive figure, nearly seven feet tall, his armor scorched from recent combat. His spiritual signature blazed with Transcendent-stage power.
"Commander Ashworth." Voss's voice filled the hall. "Your garrison has held against significant assault. The Iron Vanguard honors your sacrifice."
"Gratitude from allied forces." Ashworth's tone carried no warmth. "We've suffered heavy casualties. I'm initiating tactical withdrawal to secondary positions."
"Understood. My soldiers will secure your perimeter while you—"
"Now."
Kael's Void Blade erupted forward.
The first infiltrator died before the words finished leaving his lips. The second managed a defensive block that shattered under the second strike. The third tried to raise an alarm and lost an arm for the effort.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 56% capacity Hammer Strike Technique from the remains. Technique Acquired: Hammer Strike Lv.3."
Genuine Vanguard soldiers staggered back, confusion replacing discipline. But the infiltrators reacted instantly—abandoning their cover identities, shadow constructs manifesting around corrupted forms.
"Traitor!" A Vanguard sergeant swung his hammer at an infiltrator whose human face had begun to melt. "Corruption! The Iron Vanguard stands true!"
Chaos erupted.
Kael carved through the infiltrators' formation like a scythe through wheat. Void Blade bypassed their defensive techniques, Spiritual Strike handled those who tried to flank. Each harvest fed the next engagement, techniques flowing through his consciousness like water through channels carved by the original masters.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 23% capacity Shadow Resistance from the remains. Resistance to darkness-based attacks improved."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 45% capacity Corpse Puppet Mastery from the remains. Technique Acquired: Corpse Puppet Mastery Lv.1."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 8% capacity Soul Anchor from the remains. Spiritual stability increased."
Ashworth's voice cut through the noise: "Withdrawal! All forces to secondary positions! Move!"
Garrison soldiers streamed toward the side entrance. Some genuine Vanguard soldiers joined them, recognizing the tactical necessity. Others stayed to fight, their hammers crushing infiltrators who had abandoned all pretense of humanity.
Kael held the gap, Void Blade singing. A Transcendent-stage infiltrator challenged him directly—shadow blades versus Void Edge, corrupted spiritual energy against harvested technique.
The infiltrator's experience showed. Every block perfect, every counter precise. But Kael had something the enemy didn't expect—the ability to learn mid-combat, harvesting fragments of technique with each strike, adapting faster than any opponent could predict.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 34% capacity Shadow Counter from the remains. Technique Acquired: Shadow Counter Lv.2."
The infiltrator's next strike came from an unexpected angle. Kael's body responded before his mind caught up, Void Blade deflecting while Spiritual Strike thrust through the opening.
The Transcendent fell.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 71% capacity Entity Servant Protocol from the remains. Warning: Incomplete extraction. Protocol fragment stored for analysis."
Kael's vision swam. Too many harvests, too many techniques compressed into insufficient time. His mind felt like an overstuffed chest, lid threatening to burst.
"Enough." Ashworth grabbed his arm. "We're clear. Move."
They retreated through smoke-filled corridors, leaving the hall to the chaos of genuine Vanguard fighting corrupted infiltrators. By dawn, the Iron Vanguard would have to explain how their Third Hammer Battalion had contained cult sympathizers. The political fallout would be devastating.
But Thornwick would stand.

The secondary positions in the old cathedral offered better defensibility than the garrison's shattered halls. Stone walls thick enough to stop cannon fire, narrow windows that favored defenders, multiple chokepoints that would multiply any attacker's casualties.
Kael slumped against a pillar, his body trembling from accumulated strain. The harvest notifications still scrolled through his consciousness, each one adding weight to his overburdened capacity.
"You've killed a lot of people tonight." Ashworth stood over him, arms crossed. "Some of them Order soldiers."
"Most of them were infiltrators."
"Some." She didn't elaborate.
Kael forced himself upright. "The intelligence I gathered. From the harvests. It confirms coordinated action across multiple fronts. Every Order received attacks at their weakest points simultaneously. Someone mapped your defenses, identified your fractures, then planned accordingly."
"The Shadow Court."
"The cults." Kael shook his head. "The Shadow Court profits from chaos, but they didn't create this chaos. Cultists hit garrison positions that no one outside the Orders knew were undermanned. Information had to come from inside your own networks."
Ashworth absorbed this in silence. Outside, dawn light began filtering through stained glass windows, casting the cathedral in fragments of color.
"Lieutenant Vess will reach command within the hour," Kael continued. "The intelligence will reach the Council of Six by nightfall. They'll convene emergency sessions, point fingers, assign blame. Meanwhile, the cults will regroup and strike again."
"You sound certain they'll fail."
"The Orders have spent fifty years preserving the status quo. When faced with existential crisis, they'll default to familiar patterns. Internal investigations. Political maneuvering. Accusations that drain resources better spent fighting." Kael met her eyes. "The Great Rift is weakening. I saw it through the breach in your garrison's ceiling. That tear will widen. When it does, every Order in Thornwick will be fighting their own battle while the Abyss rises unopposed."

"I'm suggesting the Orders stop pretending they're still relevant." Kael pushed himself to his feet, legs protesting. "The Concordat bound six powers into equilibrium. Equilibrium preserved the seals. But equilibrium also prevented any single Order from taking decisive action. Now that equilibrium is broken, you have a choice. Adapt or die."
"And what role does a Reaver play in your vision of adaptation?"

"You sound proud of that."
"I sound practical." Kael moved toward the cathedral's main entrance. "The cults attacked because they believe the Orders are weak. They're right. The only question is whether the Orders recognize their weakness before the Abyss swallows them."
Through the cathedral doors, Kael glimpsed the first rays of dawn striking Thornwick's eastern towers. Smoke still rose from a dozen districts. The Iron Vanguard's internal purges would take days to complete. Every Order in the city was assessing damage, counting casualties, demanding answers.
And somewhere in that chaos, the true architects of the attack were repositioning assets, adjusting timelines, preparing for the next phase.
Kael's Reaver senses swept the surrounding streets. Spiritual signatures flickered at the edges of perception—cultist scouts determining the cathedral's defensive strength. They weren't attacking yet. Gathering intelligence. Waiting for orders.
His hand found the vial of soul-essence at his belt. Enough to fund a small army's operations for months. Enough to buy information, allegiance, silence. The Veiled Market's currency, flowing through his fingers like water.
"The eastern cathedral district," Kael said quietly. "How many spiritually sensitive civilians are sheltering there?"
Ashworth's expression flickered. "Thousands. The old cathedral served as a refuge point during the attacks."
"Then the cults will target it next. Use the civilians as leverage, or massacre them to demonstrate Order failure." Kael turned to face her fully. "Your garrison can't hold this position against a concentrated assault. Not after the night's casualties."
"What are you suggesting?"
"I'm suggesting you stop thinking like an Order officer and start thinking like a survivor." Kael gestured toward the cathedral's interior. "This building has cellars. Catacombs from before Thornwick existed, sealed under layers of stone and ancient wards. The spiritually sensitive civilians—you have access to their registry?"
"Yes, but—"
"Then we evacuate. Quietly. Through routes the cults haven't mapped yet." Kael's mind raced through the implications. "We move the civilians to defensible positions, leave false trails for the scouts to follow, and buy time for the Orders to stop fighting each other long enough to mount a coordinated response."
Ashworth stared at him for a long moment. The military officer wrestling with the Reaver's proposal. The pragmatist versus the idealist.
"The Orders will never accept guidance from a Reaver."
"The Orders might not have a choice." Kael moved toward the cathedral's interior, where sounds of wounded civilians echoed through vaulted chambers. "Their intelligence networks are compromised. Their reinforcement channels are compromised. Their command structures are compromised. The only intelligence they can trust is what I extracted from the dead."
"And when you exhaust that advantage? When every technique you can harvest has already been harvested?"
Kael paused at the threshold. Through the doorway, he glimpsed families huddled in corners, children crying, elderly victims of a war they didn't understand. The casualties of fracture. The price of stagnation.
"Then I improvise." He glanced back at Ashworth. "That's what survivors do."
The cathedral's ancient doors groaned shut behind them as the sun rose over Thornwick. Somewhere in the distance, explosions marked another assault, another breach, another fracture in the Orders' failing defense.
But in the catacombs below, Kael began organizing an evacuation that no Order had sanctioned, protecting civilians no one else would protect, using intelligence harvested from the dead to keep the living alive.
The war had begun. And for the first time since his execution, Kael Ashborn fought with purpose than desperation.
The footsteps behind him were wrong.
Kael spun, Void Blade manifesting, only to find a familiar face emerging from the shadows. Lyra. Her silver hair was matted with blood, her Order of Dawn insignia torn from her collar.
"The Shadow Court knows you're alive." Her voice carried no greeting. "They've placed a bounty on your head. Fifty thousand sovereigns. Dead or alive."

"I noticed."
"Not just the infiltrators you killed tonight. Sleeper agents who've been embedded for years. Decades." Lyra's expression hardened. "One of them is standing in your evacuation committee. I don't know which one, but I can feel their corruption."
Kael's mind raced through possibilities. A corrupted evacuation committee meant compromised routes, trapped civilians, massacre disguised as tragedy.
"I need a name."

"You want me to walk into a Shadow Court operation to get intelligence on Shadow Court assets."
