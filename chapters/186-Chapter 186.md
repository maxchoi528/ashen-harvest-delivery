The blade sang through the air. Kael dropped beneath the sweep, his shoulder slamming into the cultist's chest. They crashed into the stone wall together, breath leaving the man's lungs in a wheeze.
"Enough." The voice came from the shadows deeper in the tunnel. Lantern light flickered against the curved walls of what the city's underclass called the Hollows—a maze of passages carved before Thornwick existed, now home to smugglers, refugees, and those who wished to disappear.
Kael released the cultist and stepped back. His hand found the hilt of his own blade, though his fingers didn't draw. The man approaching wore the crimson sash of an Underlord, one of the cult's cell leaders. But his posture told Kael more than insignia ever could. This wasn't a zealot prepared to die for his faith. This was a professional.
"You're the one they call the Reaver." The Underlord's voice carried no fear, only calculation. "We've heard stories."
"Stories get exaggerated."
"Maybe." The man gestured, and three more cultists emerged from branching tunnels, their movements coordinated. Too coordinated. Kael counted six heartbeats he could identify—these weren't random cultists stumbling through ritual. They moved like soldiers.
Four hundred souls fed the rift beneath the Ashborn foundry. The words from the cell they'd raided two nights ago echoed in Kael's memory. Four hundred. He'd dismissed it as exaggeration, the kind of number zealots inflated to impress their dark gods. But the organization he'd witnessed since arriving in Thornwick suggested otherwise. These people had structure. Funding. Intelligence.
The six cultists spread into a formation that would pin him against the tunnel wall. Trapped. The Underlord's thin smile confirmed the trap was sprung.
"The Order of Dawn has been searching for you for months." The Underlord produced a glass vial from his coat, the liquid inside shimmering with sickly green light. Soul-essence. "But they'll never find what's hidden down here. Neither will the others."
"What others?"
"The Shadow Court sent feelers three days ago. The Iron Vanguard has agents in the city too." The Underlord lifted the vial higher, letting the light play across his face. "You're valuable, Reaver. Not for what you take—for what you are. A vessel for something that should have stayed buried."
Kael's blood chilled. He'd heard rumors of vessels, of humans hollowed out and filled with fragments of Abyssal power. Most died screaming within hours. Those who survived became something between human and Entity, bound to the will of whoever claimed them.
"I'm not interested in becoming anyone's puppet."
"Few are. But the Soul Reaver ability doesn't exist without the original's bloodline." The Underlord stepped closer, and Kael noticed details he'd missed in the dim light—the man's skin had a faint grayish pallor, and his eyes reflected the lantern flame with too much intensity. "Do you truly believe such a gift appears randomly? The first Reaver sealed the 72 rifts with his dying breath. His power didn't disappear. It waited."
"Waited for what?"
"For someone desperate enough to use it."
The formation closed tighter. Kael calculated distances, angles, the placement of each cultist's weapons. Six against one in a confined space. His blade could open paths, but he wasn't here to kill. The Underlord's words suggested information—real information, not the garbled prophecies and blood-mad ranting he'd heard from rank-and-file cultists.
Two hours of following whispered rumors through Thornwick's underworld had led him here. To this moment. To six cultists who wanted to capture him alive and a cell leader who spoke of bloodlines and buried power like discussing weather patterns.
"The six Orders maintain the seals," Kael said slowly. "The Concordat binds them to watch the rifts."
"The Concordat." The Underlord laughed, a harsh sound devoid of humor. "A treaty written by cowards who feared what would happen if the truth came out. The seals aren't maintained—they're rationed. Each year, the entities grow stronger while the Orders grow weaker. In fifty years, maybe less, the membrane will tear. And when it does, which Order will matter?"
One of the cultists shifted position, and Kael caught the gleam of a crossbow. Not standard cult equipment. Military-grade construction, the kind the Iron Vanguard used for assassinations. The realization landed like a stone in his gut. The Underlord hadn't been speaking metaphorically about the Orders having agents in the city. This cult operated with their support.
"Who funds you?" Kael asked. "The Iron Vanguard? The Shadow Court? Both?"
"Now you're asking the right questions." The Underlord raised his hand, and the cultists froze. "But answers have prices in the Hollows. You know this better than most, Reaver. The Veiled Market doesn't trade in coin. It trades in secrets, in favors, in debts that come due at inconvenient moments."
"What do you want from me?"
"First, proof that you are what the stories claim." The Underlord gestured toward the cultist Kael had thrown against the wall, now slumped against the stone with a broken arm. "Kill him. Take what's left. Show me the Soul Reaver's gift in action."
Kael's stomach turned. Not at the violence—violence he'd accepted the moment he'd chosen this path. But at the calculation behind it. The cultist on the ground wasn't a volunteer for martyrdom. He was a test subject, expendable, his life measured in the information it could provide.
The cult's structure made terrible sense. Not mindless zealots preparing for apocalypse, but an organization that understood exactly what was coming and intended to survive it. They weren't trying to unleash the Abyss. They were preparing for the moment when no amount of sealing could hold back the tide.
"The Shadow Court monitors me," Kael said. "You've told me that. But why tell me? Why not just take what you need?"
"Because what we need cannot be taken." The Underlord's gray face hardened. "The Soul Reaver ability chooses its wielder. Every attempt to forcibly extract it has ended in the subject's death and the death of everyone within a hundred meters. The original bloodline must willing—or at least desperate enough to use the gift without understanding its cost."
"And you think I'm desperate enough."
"The execution should have killed you. Instead, it Awakened you. That kind of desperation doesn't fade." The Underlord spread his hands, a gesture that might have seemed welcoming in different circumstances. "We offer an alternative. Join us willingly, learn what you truly possess, and when the seals finally break, you'll have power enough to survive. Or refuse, and the Shadow Court will find you first. Their methods make ours look like charity."
The broken-armed cultist whimpered, drawing Kael's attention. Young. Maybe twenty, with the calloused hands of someone who'd worked honest labor before joining this cause. Not a monster. Just another desperate soul who'd found answers in darkness.
Kael sheathed his blade. "I don't kill prisoners."
"He's not a prisoner. He's a volunteer."
"He didn't volunteer to die for your experiment."
"He volunteered to serve a cause greater than himself." The Underlord's voice carried the absolute conviction of true believers everywhere. "Every soul matters. Every death feeds the rift. When the membrane tears, every soul consumed will strengthen the survivors."
The words hit Kael like physical blows. He'd known the cult performed sacrifices. He'd witnessed the aftermath of their rituals in abandoned buildings and forgotten graveyards. But hearing it stated so baldly, so logically, stripped away any remaining illusions about what he faced.
The 72 rifts. The entities sealed within. The cult believed they were preparing humanity for a future where the seals would fail completely, where the only survivors would be those strong enough to withstand the Abyss's direct influence. It wasn't madness. It was a terrible, coherent philosophy built on the assumption that the Orders would eventually fail.
"The First Reaver sealed the rifts," Kael said slowly, testing the words against what he'd learned. "His power waits for a bloodline heir."
"The True Name." The Underlord's eyes gleamed. "The Sepulcher tablets claim he recorded his True Name before death. A name that contains the authority to control all 72 seals. The Orders have spent centuries searching for those tablets, convinced that controlling the True Name means controlling reality itself."
"And your cult has them?"
"We have fragments. Enough to know the full text exists, hidden in a location the original Reaver chose himself." The Underlord smiled, thin and knowing. "You're wondering why we're telling you this. Why we don't capture you, drain your bloodline power, and find the tablets ourselves."
"Why?"
"Because the True Name cannot be claimed through violence or theft. It must be inherited. And inheritance requires a willing heir." The Underlord stepped closer, close enough that Kael could smell the chemical sharpness of preserved soul-essence on his clothes. "The bloodline has remained dormant for three hundred years. You're the first to manifest the ability since the original died. That makes you, by default, the only person alive who can claim the Name."
Kael's mind raced through implications. The Soul Reaver ability wasn't random. It was hereditary, mystical, bound to a bloodline that had remained hidden or extinct for centuries. His execution had triggered something that should have remained forever dormant.
"The Shadow Court knows about the bloodline," he said.
"The Shadow Court knows enough to be dangerous, not enough to act. They want you captured because a Soul Reaver in the wrong hands threatens their operations. But they don't understand what you truly are." The Underlord reached into his coat and withdrew a small leather journal, its pages stained with age and something darker. "A gift. Proof of good faith. The names of every cell leader in Thornwick, their connections to the Orders, and the locations of seven secondary rifts that the Orders believe are sealed but are weakening."
"Why would you give me this?"
"Because chaos serves us. The more the Orders fight each other, the less attention they pay to the primary rifts. And you, Reaver—you're the perfect catalyst for chaos. The Shadow Court wants you controlled. The Iron Vanguard wants you dead. The Order of Dawn wants you studied. If they all start hunting each other instead of the Hollows, the seals weaken faster."
A trap wrapped in a gift wrapped in a conspiracy. Kael took the journal, feeling its weight in his hand. Real information, valuable information, provided by people who expected him to use it in ways that served their agenda.
"The cult cells in Thornwick," he said. "They're not preparing to unseal the rifts immediately."
"Not yet. The membrane will tear on its own within fifty years. We accelerate the timeline, nothing more. Every sacrifice weakens the seals by a fraction. Enough sacrifices, and the tearing becomes a ripping."
"How many?"
"To breach the membrane at the Great Rift? Approximately ten thousand Awakened souls, or one hundred thousand commoners, or some combination thereof. The Orders have been performing controlled sacrifices for centuries, though they call it 'spiritual culling' and 'necessary depletion.' We do what they do, but with honesty about the purpose."
The tunnel seemed to press closer, the weight of centuries of conspiracy crushing down on Kael's shoulders. The Orders weren't fighting the cult. They were fighting a mirror, a darker reflection of their own practices.
"I need to think about your offer."
"Think quickly. The Shadow Court's agents are already moving. They're not alone—the Iron Vanguard has sent a kill team, and the Order of Dawn has three Seekers in the city hunting for you." The Underlord stepped back, gesturing for his cultists to withdraw. "We'll contact you again through the Veiled Market. Ask for the Underlord's Toll. If you decide to join us, you'll be given everything—training, resources, knowledge of your true heritage. If you refuse..."
"Then what?"
"Then you're on your own against forces that make this cult look like children playing at darkness." The Underlord turned toward the deeper tunnels. "Choose wisely, Reaver. The world doesn't get a second chance."
They vanished into the branching passages, leaving Kael alone with the journal and the body of the cultist who'd been left behind. The young man had stopped breathing while they talked. Kael crouched beside him, checking for a pulse he already knew was gone.
Dead. Fresh enough to harvest.
He hesitated. The Soul Reaver ability waited within him, patient as a coiled serpent, ready to take what death offered. He'd used it dozens of times since his Awakening—on enemies, on victims, on the occasional innocent caught in crossfire. Each harvest added power, skills, fragments of memory that sometimes helped and sometimes burned.
But this man had been a cultist. He'd volunteered for a cause that required human sacrifice. He'd known what he was doing, chosen it freely, died for beliefs Kael found monstrous.
Soul Harvest: You extracted Spiritual Resonance from the remains. Fragment of Cult Doctrine acquired.
Kael grimaced as information flooded his mind—not complete doctrine, but fragments. Enough to confirm what the Underlord had said. The cult genuinely believed they were saving humanity by weakening the seals. They'd calculated exactly how many souls would be needed, tracked the seal degradation rates, identified which rifts could be safely opened without triggering immediate catastrophe.
The True Name. The first Soul Reaver had recorded the authority to control all 72 seals. And, through bloodline and desperation and an execution that should have killed him, Kael had inherited that power.
The journal in his hands contained real intelligence. Names, locations, connections that would let him dismantle the cult's operations in Thornwick. But it would also trigger the chaos the Underlord wanted—Orders fighting each other, attention diverted from the Hollows, seals weakening faster.
Kael stood, pocketing the journal, and walked deeper into the tunnels. He had information now. Enough to act, or enough to become someone else's pawn. The cult wanted him to start a war. The Shadow Court wanted to control him. The Iron Vanguard wanted him dead. The Order of Dawn wanted to study him like a specimen.
And somewhere in Thornwick, Zarek was coordinating all of it—feeding information to multiple factions, ensuring that Kael remained the center of chaos while the cult prepared for the moment when the membrane finally tore.
The young cultist's corpse grew cold behind him. Kael didn't look back.

Three hours later, he emerged from a drainage grate into the warehouse district. Dawn light painted the steam-belching factories in shades of orange and gray. Workers trudged toward their shifts, their faces blank with exhaustion, oblivious to the war being waged in their midst.
Kael found an abandoned storage building and barricaded himself inside. The journal's contents required careful analysis. He spread the pages across the dusty floor, reading names and dates and connection webs that painted a picture of organization far beyond what he'd imagined.
Seven secondary rifts, each weakening faster than projected. Cult cells operating in every district of Thornwick, funded by legitimate businesses and criminal operations alike. Order contacts within the Iron Vanguard feeding information to the cult, while other Order contacts fed information to the Shadow Court. A web of betrayal so complex that untangling it would take months.
But he didn't have months. The Shadow Court had agents moving. The Iron Vanguard had a kill team. The Order of Dawn had Seekers. And the cult had made clear that his choices were limited—join them, or face all of them at once.
Kael's fingers traced the name at the journal's center of the web. Zarek. Not just coordinating the cult operations, but acting as liaison to multiple Order factions. The man who'd framed him for treason, who had orchestrated the execution that should have killed him, who had known about his bloodline before Kael himself did.
The journal contained a location. One of the seven secondary rifts, supposedly sealed but monitored by cult operatives. If Kael moved against it, he'd trigger exactly the kind of chaos the Underlord wanted. Orders would respond, cult cells would activate, and Zarek would have a perfect opportunity to eliminate him while blame fell on others.
If he didn't move, the cult would continue preparing. The seals would continue weakening. And in fifty years, maybe less, the Abyss would pour through a tear in the membrane that no amount of sealing could contain.
The Soul Reaver ability pulsed within him, waiting. He'd harvested spiritual resonance, fragments of doctrine, traces of cult training. His understanding of the ability had grown, though he remained far from mastering what he possessed.
The first Reaver had sealed the 72 rifts with his dying breath. Kael carried that power in his blood, dormant but present. The True Name waited somewhere, the key to controlling every seal on the continent. And he had a choice—use that power to serve the cult's grim vision of humanity's survival, or find another path that didn't involve sacrificing millions to strengthen thousands.
Footsteps outside the warehouse. Multiple sets, moving with the coordination of trained fighters.
Kael the journal and moved toward the building's second floor, positioning himself where he could watch the entrance. His blade found his hand, the familiar weight a comfort in the face of uncertain odds.
The door exploded inward. Not kicked—blasted, the wood splintering under spiritual force that left scorch marks on the metal hinges. Three figures entered the warehouse, dressed in the black leather of the Shadow Court's enforcers. Their leader wore a silver mask that covered her face, leaving only her eyes visible—eyes that tracked Kael's position with inhuman precision.
"The Reaver." Her voice carried the flat affect of someone who'd stopped feeling emotions decades ago. "The Underlord's Toll has been paid. You're coming with us."
"The Underlord offered me a choice. Join the cult, or face everyone at once."
"False. The cult offered you chaos. We offer control." The masked woman gestured, and her enforcers spread into positions that would trap Kael against the wall. "The Shadow Court has monitored Soul Reavers since the original's death. We know what you are. We know what you can become. The question is whether you become it under our guidance, or we eliminate you to prevent someone else from trying."
"Zarek works for you?"
"Zarek works for himself. We find him useful, nothing more." The masked woman produced a contract from her coat—actual paper, covered in legal text that glowed faintly with binding magic. "An accord. Sign, and you gain our resources, our training, our protection from the other factions. Refuse, and we fulfill our secondary mandate."
"Which is?"
"To ensure the Soul Reaver bloodline doesn't fall into enemy hands. Since you're currently enemy-aligned through possession of cult intelligence, that means your death."
The enforcers' hands moved to their weapons. Spiritual energy crackled in the air, building toward a release that would flatten the warehouse's second floor.
Kael calculated odds. Three Shadow Court enforcers, all above Awakened stage, possibly Ascendant or higher. The masked woman radiated power that suggested Transcendent or beyond. Against them, he had his blade, his Soul Reaver ability, and the desperate cunning of someone who'd survived an execution.
The cult wanted him for the True Name. The Shadow Court wanted him controlled. The Iron Vanguard wanted him dead. The Order of Dawn wanted him studied. Every faction in Thornwick had a different agenda, and all of them converged on him like predators drawn to wounded prey.
The journal in his pocket contained the key to unleashing chaos that might consume them all. Or it contained bait designed to ensure his destruction while weakening the Orders' response to the cult's true plans.
The masked woman extended the contract. "Choose quickly, Reaver. Our patience isn't infinite."
Outside, somewhere in the warehouse district, a bell began to toll. The alarm that signaled a rift breach, a seal failure, the beginning of the catastrophe everyone had been preparing for.
Kael's blood ran cold. The Underlord had said the cult wouldn't unseal the primary rifts yet. But if the secondary rifts were weakening faster than projected, if the membrane was tearing ahead of schedule...
"The rift," he said. "Which one?"
"Does it matter?" The masked woman tilted her head. "They're all connected, Reaver. Open one, and you weaken all. The cult has been preparing this moment for decades. Your presence in Thornwick was the final catalyst."
"Why?"
"Because a Soul Reaver who doesn't join them becomes a threat. And threats must be eliminated." She thrust the contract forward again. "Sign, and we extract you from the city before the Orders tear each other apart fighting over the breach. Refuse, and you die here, your bloodline ends, and the cult's timeline accelerates by twenty years."
The bell continued tolling. Workers screamed in the distance. Somewhere in the city, a seal was failing.
Kael took the contract. The masked woman's eyes gleamed with satisfaction.
The door behind them exploded inward. Spiritual force tore through the warehouse like a hurricane, scattering debris and forcing everyone to their knees. Kael rolled beneath a falling beam, using the chaos to distance himself from both factions.
Through the smoke and dust, figures emerged. Iron Vanguard soldiers in their distinctive red armor, weapons raised, spiritual energy crackling along their blades. Behind them, Order of Dawn Seekers in white robes, their faces set with the righteous fury of those who'd glimpsed the enemy's true nature.
"Zarek sent you." The masked woman's voice carried grudging respect. "He knew we'd make contact."
The Iron Vanguard commander raised his hand. "By order of the Concordat Council, all present are charged with conspiracy to weaken the seal network. Lay down your weapons and submit to judgment."
"The Concordat doesn't cover Shadow Court operations," the masked woman replied.
"The Concordat covers everything when the seals are failing." The commander stepped forward, and Kael caught the gleam of advanced armor beneath his uniform—the kind that could turn aside spiritual attacks that would kill lesser soldiers. "Zarek briefed us an hour ago. The cult's operation is exposed. Your cell leaders are being arrested as we speak. And this—" he pointed at Kael, "—this abomination you're trying to claim is coming with us."
"He's a Soul Reaver," one of the Seekers said, horror and awe mixing in her voice. "The texts described them as legends, myths from the Abyssal Wars."
"He's a weapon," the Iron Vanguard commander replied. "Weapons belong in the hands of those who know how to use them."
The masked woman laughed. "The Iron Vanguard wants to use a Soul Reaver? The same faction that's been feeding information to the cult for twenty years? Do you think we're blind?"
The commander's face hardened. "Zarek will answer for his betrayal. After we secure the Reaver."
"Then we have competing claims." The masked woman produced a device from her coat—small, metallic, covered in runes that pulsed with sickly light. "A nullification sphere. One activation, and all spiritual abilities in this warehouse become useless for an hour. Everyone here becomes equal. Soldiers without powers. Seekers without miracles. A Reaver without his harvesting."
"You'd cripple your own enforcers?"
"Better crippled than captured by the Iron Vanguard." She activated the sphere.
Kael moved before the spiritual dampening field could reach him. He dove through a gap in the collapsing wall, rolled across debris-strewn ground, and sprinted toward the warehouse district's exit. Behind him, he heard shouts, the clash of weapons, the frustrated howls of Awakened stripped of their powers.
The nullification sphere wouldn't last long. Once it expired, the factions would recover and resume their fight—to capture or kill him. But for now, he had a window. Minutes at most.
He ran toward the tolling bell, toward the rift that was failing, toward answers that might explain why the cult had targeted him, why his bloodline mattered, why every faction in Thornwick wanted him dead or controlled.
The streets filled with panicked civilians. Steam-powered fire wagons raced toward the breach location. Order of Dawn priests wove barriers to contain whatever was pouring through the failing seal.
Kael pushed through the crowd, his blade drawn, his mind racing through everything he'd learned. The cult wanted chaos. The Shadow Court wanted control. The Iron Vanguard wanted a weapon. The Order of Dawn wanted a relic. And Zarek—Zarek wanted something else entirely, orchestrating events from the shadows, ensuring that everyone moved according to his design.
The journal's last page contained a note in different handwriting. The Underlord's personal addition, added after Kael had taken the journal.
*Zarek knows where the True Name is hidden. He framed you to ensure your Awakening. Your execution was supposed to trigger the bloodline's full activation, making you powerful enough to claim the Name. Instead, you escaped. Now he's correcting his mistake. Whatever choice you make, you walk a path he has already prepared.*
The bell continued tolling. The rift continued opening. And somewhere in the chaos, Zarek was watching, waiting to see which piece moved according to his plan.
Kael reached the breach location. A massive crack split the air above the Ashborn foundry's ruins, spiritual energy pouring through in waves that made his skin crawl. Through the crack, he glimpsed something that shouldn't exist—eyes the size of buildings, focused on the Material Realm with patient hunger.
The seventy-third entity. The one that had escaped before the original Reaver could seal it.
Kael's phone buzzed. A message from an unknown number, containing only a question.
*Ready to learn the truth about your bloodline?*
The rift widened. The eyes focused on him. And Kael understood, with horrible clarity, that every choice he'd made since his execution had led to this moment—to a confrontation with an entity that had waited three hundred years for the Soul Reaver's heir to return.
