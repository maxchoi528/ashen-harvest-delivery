Blood painted the alley wall in long, arterial streaks. Kael pressed his back against damp brick, breath shallow, fingers tracing the Soul Reaver's edge along his forearm. Three cultists lay crumpled at the entrance—one still gurgling, two gone silent. The gurgling one clutched something metal, glinting even in the gaslight's sickly amber glow.
"Thought you could run, Reaver?"
The voice slithered from the shadows. A figure stepped forward, robes the color of old bruises, a sigil of intersecting circles branded into his forehead. Stage Three. The air around him shimmered with barely contained power.
Kael's stomach clenched. He was Ascendant—one stage below this bastard, and his reserves were gutted from the previous fight.
"Hand over the tablet fragment," the man continued, boots clicking against cobblestones. "The Whispering One's been patient. I'd hate to see what happens when He isn't."
Kael's eyes flicked to the gurgling cultist. The man's hand still gripped the metal object—could be a weapon, could be a key. Could be nothing. The uncertainty coiled in his gut like cold iron.
"You cultists always monologue before killing?" Kael rasped. "Or is this one special?"
The man's lips peeled back in something that wasn't a smile. "I'm Inquisitor Maren. Order of the Iron Veil. And you, little Reaver, are going to tell me everything you know about the 72."
Kael's mind raced. *Inquisitor. Iron Veil. That's one of the Six Orders—but why would an Order be working with cultists?*
Maren raised one hand, fingers crackling with blue-white lightning. "Last chance, corpse-feeder."
The gurgling cultist finally went still. *Fresh corpse. Right there. If I can just—*
Kael lunged.
Maren's lightning crackled through the air where Kael had stood a heartbeat before. Too slow. Kael hit the cobblestones, rolled, and crashed into the dead cultist's chest. His palm slapped against cooling flesh. The Soul Reaver's hunger awakened.
**[Soul Harvest: You extracted 3 from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired.]**
Information flooded Kael's skull—fragmented, violent, a cultist's last desperate thoughts. *Ritual site. North quarter. Nine circles. Blood for the seal-breaking. Whisper King watches—*
Maren was already moving, lightning gathering between his palms like captured storm. "Impressive reflexes. Let's see how you handle focused attention."
Kael rolled off the corpse as lightning seared the ground, charring the stones black. The smell hit him—ozone and burning hair. He scrambled backward, the memory fragment still echoing in his mind.
*Nine circles. North quarter. Seal-breaking.*
"You're stalling," Maren observed, circling. His eyes never left Kael's chest. "Waiting for your reserves to tick back up. Sorry, boy. I don't fight fair."
He wasn't. Kael could feel it—the subtle distortion in the air, like heat shimmer over summer cobblestones. Some kind of suppression field. His spiritual energy recovery had ground to a crawl.
"You're Order," Kael spat. "Why work with the Whisper King's cult?"
Maren's expression flickered—something cold and calculating beneath the surface. "The Whisper King offers what the Orders cannot. Power. Purpose. A world where the 72 walk free and the weak serve the strong."
"You're insane."
"I'm *awake*." Maren's lightning crackled brighter. "The seals are failing anyway. The Great Rift bleeds chaos into the Material every day. Humanity's clinging to a dying order. I'm... Accelerating the inevitable."
Kael's hand found a loose cobblestone. He palmed it, weight settling into his throw.
"Inquisitor training teach you to monologue or to fight?"
"Both." Maren's smile turned razor-thin. "The monologue is for your benefit. When they find what's left of you, they'll know why."
Lightning erupted from Maren's palms in a cascading barrage. Kael threw the cobblestone—it shattered against a ward Kael hadn't seen, fragments spraying uselessly against transparent barriers. Maren wasn't even looking at the throw. His eyes tracked Kael's dodge, his roll, his desperate scramble behind a stack of crates.
Crates that exploded into splinters as the lightning followed.
Kael felt the heat kiss his cheek, smelled his own flesh crisping. Pain flared bright and immediate—then faded to manageable fire as adrenaline flooded his system. His left arm wasn't moving right. Burn damage. He'd need to address that soon or lose function entirely.
But Maren was approaching now, walking through the debris field of destroyed crates, lightning still dancing between his fingers. Methodical. Patient.
"Let me tell you about the 72, Reaver." Maren's voice carried the cadence of a lecturer. "The Orders teach that the Entities are demons. Locked away because they're evil. But that's propaganda. The Entities are... Different. Old. They existed before humanity, before the Orders, before the seals. The Whisper King was the first to communicate with them. To learn their true nature."
"You learned that from a cultist memory fragment," Kael said, buying seconds. "Interesting. I'm harvesting more."
Maren's eyes narrowed. "You think I don't know what you took from that corpse? Memory fragments are useless without context. The full picture requires—"
He lunged. Kael had been preparing for that—dropping low, sliding between Maren's legs, coming up behind him with the Soul Reaver carving a shallow cut across the Inquisitor's back.
Maren spun, faster than Kael expected, faster than any Stage Three should move. His palm struck Kael's chest with enough force to lift him off the ground. Kael crashed into the alley wall hard enough to taste blood.
**[Soul Harvest: Insufficient contact. Harvest failed.]**
Of course. Maren was still alive.
Kael slumped against the brick, vision swimming. Maren approached slowly now, savoring the moment.
"Every Reaver makes the same mistake," Maren said, almost pityingly. "You think the ability makes you powerful. Makes you special. But harvesting from the dying is just... Scavenging. Picking through the garbage of a war you don't understand."
He crouched beside Kael, lightning crackling between his fingers.
"The Whisper King wants an audience. Will you come willingly, or do I have to break your legs?"
Kael's eyes flicked to the three cultist corpses. Maren's. The other two. All dead within the last several minutes. Fresh enough to harvest.
Maren followed his gaze and laughed. "Ah. Still thinking about your little parlor trick? I assure you, I won't be so careless as—"
The alley flooded with gaslight.
Both men froze. Footsteps echoed from the main street—multiple sets, heavy boots, the distinctive *clack-clack* of Order-issue armor. A voice boomed through the darkness.
"Inquisitor Maren! By order of the Council of Six, you are hereby detained for violation of the Concordat! Surrender your weapons and submit to examination!"
Maren's expression went blank. Then, incredibly, he laughed.
"Looks like my backup arrived first." He stood, brushing dust from his robes. "Enjoy your brief freedom, Reaver. We'll meet again."
He vanished into the shadows faster than Kael's eyes could track. Stage Three speed, unhurried. He'd been holding back the entire fight.
Kael slumped back against the wall, chest heaving. The boots grew louder. Figures in mismatched armor rounded the corner—Iron Vanguard, by the crossed hammers on their shields. And among them, a face Kael recognized.
Aldric Thorne. Stage Two. Formerly of the Mortician's Guild, now apparently working with Vanguard operations. They'd crossed paths once, in a situation neither spoke about afterward.
Aldric's eyes swept the carnage—three dead cultists, Kael bleeding against the wall, scorch marks everywhere. His jaw tightened.
"Ashborn." Not a greeting. An observation. "Care to explain why I just watched a Stage Three Inquisitor flee the scene?"
"He was working with the cultists," Kael managed. "The Whisper King. They mentioned seal-breaking. North quarter."
Aldric's expression didn't change, but something shifted behind his eyes. "That's the second report this week. Organized cult activity. Multiple Order members going rogue." He glanced at his troops. "Get the perimeter secured. Check for survivors."
His gaze returned to Kael. Assessing.
"You're hurt."
"Observant."
"Come with me. I've got a healer on standby, and we need to talk." Aldric extended a hand. "Specifically about that thing you do. The harvesting."
Kael stared at the hand. Every instinct screamed caution. Aldric worked for the establishment—the very people who'd executed him, who'd hunt him if they knew the full truth.
But Aldric had also walked away from the Mortician's Guild. Had chosen something different. And right now, Kael was bleeding, his reserves empty, his left arm potentially crippled.
"You going to turn me in?"
"That depends." Aldric kept his hand extended. "Are you going to keep hunting cultists, or are you going to start a one-man massacre?"
Kael considered the question. Really considered it.
"I kill cultists. Monsters. Anyone who feeds the Abyss willingly." He grabbed Aldric's hand, letting himself be hauled to his feet. "I don't touch civilians. I don't touch children. Everything else is negotiable."
Aldric nodded slowly. "Acceptable. For now." He signaled his troops. "Double-time. We've got a cult site to investigate in the north quarter. Nine circles, if the reports are accurate."
Kael's head snapped up. "You heard that?"
"I have sources of my own." Aldric's smile was thin. "We're not as blind as you think, Ashborn. The Orders have been monitoring cult activity for months. What we didn't know was how organized they were. How many of our own people they'd turned." His expression darkened. "Maren was highly decorated. Stage Three for twelve years. We never suspected."
"Until now."
"Until a Reaver starts killing cultists in alleys and we have to follow the blood trail to find our traitors." Aldric led him through the winding streets, gaslight flickering overhead. "You've caused a lot of chaos, Ashborn. Some of it useful."
They emerged onto a wider avenue, steam-powered trams clattering past in the distance. The healer's station was a squat stone building, windows glowing with soft green light.
"There's something you should know," Aldric said, stopping at the entrance. "Before we go further. The Six Orders exist because of the 72. Every Order is built around containing one or more Entities. The seals are maintained through constant spiritual reinforcement. When a seal weakens, when an Entity stirs, the Orders mobilize. That's their entire purpose."
Kael absorbed this. The cult activity. The rogue Inquisitor. The organized ritual sites. It was all starting to form a picture.
"Someone's sabotaging the seals," he said.
"Or accelerating their natural decay." Aldric pushed open the door. "Either way, if the 72 break free at once, the Veil collapses. Reality collapses. Everything humanity has built—gone."
The healer was a small woman with grey hair and steady hands. She took one look at Kael's arm and started barking orders for water, bandages, spiritual salves. Kael barely registered the treatment, his mind still turning over what he'd learned.
*Six Orders. 72 Entities. Organized cult activity. The Whisper King at the center.*
The memory fragment pulsed in his consciousness. Fragmented cultist thoughts, meaningless alone, but connected to something larger. He needed more. Needed to harvest from fresh corpses at that ritual site. Needed answers.
"The north quarter site," Kael said as the healer worked. "What's there?"
"Last confirmed location of cult activity," Aldric replied. "We've been surveilling it for weeks but couldn't confirm entry. If Maren's been coordinating with them, we might be able to finally breach the perimeter."
"And you're taking me because?"
"Because you can harvest information from the dead. Because you can sense things we can't." Aldric leaned against the wall, arms crossed. "Because frankly, you're a weapon no one else has figured out how to use yet, and I'd aim you at the cult than watch you become a problem."
The healer finished her work, wrapping Kael's arm in cool bandages that hummed with minor healing arts. Movement returned, sensation flooding back. Not full recovery, but functional.
"How long until you can fight?"
Kael flexed his fingers. "An hour. Maybe less."
"Good. We move at midnight." Aldric straightened. "Get some food. Rest if you can. Tonight, we find out what the Whisper King's planning."
He left Kael alone in the treatment room. Outside, the gaslights hummed. The city churned on—steam trams, late-night workers, the eternal racket of civilization.
Kael closed his eyes and let the memory fragment unfurl.
*Nine circles. North quarter. Blood for seal-breaking. Whisper King watches.*
Someone was trying to free the 72. The cult had Order members compromised. And now Kael was walking into the heart of it, armed with a power he still didn't fully understand.
The Soul Reaver pulsed against his chest, hungry as ever.
*Let's see what's left to harvest.*

Midnight found him in the north quarter, crouched behind a collapsed tenement wall, watching the cult site through a gap in the boards. The building had been a warehouse once—now it crawled with figures in dark robes, their chanting rising and falling like breathing.
Nine circles had been painted on the warehouse floor in something that looked horribly like blood. Fresh. The smell hit him even from this distance: copper and rot and something else, something that made his teeth ache.
"Sentries on the roof," Aldric murmured beside him. "Four visible, probably more hidden. Main entrance is chained, but there's a side door. We're going in through the sewers."
"How many inside?"
"At least twenty cultists. Possibly more." Aldric's jaw tightened. "And if Maren's here..."
"Then we've got a Stage Three complication."
"Yes."
Kael considered the odds. Twenty cultists, one possibly-two Stage Three, and a handful of Iron Vanguard against whatever nightmare the cult had prepared. His reserves were barely at half. His arm still ached beneath the bandages.
"This is a bad plan," he said.
"Probably." Aldric didn't look away from the warehouse. "It's what we've got."
The chanting inside shifted pitch. Someone screamed—a short, sharp sound that cut off abruptly. Then the cultists resumed their work, moving with renewed urgency.
"Something's happening," Kael said. "They're preparing for something."
Aldric checked his chrono, a brass device ticking against his wrist. "We go now or we lose the window."
Kael thought about walking away. About taking his power and disappearing into the city's underworld, letting the cult and the Orders destroy each other while he picked through the aftermath.
The Soul Reaver hummed against his chest, waiting.
*They killed innocents to paint those circles. They'll kill more if they break the seals.*

The water at their ankles carried the stench of the undercity—industrial runoff, human waste, and something else. Something that burned the back of Kael's throat.
"Spiritual contamination," Aldric said quietly, noticing his reaction. "The cult's been using this route for months. Probably longer."
"How far?"
"Another hundred yards. There's a maintenance shaft that comes up inside the warehouse. Old steam tunnels from before the city went gaslit."
They moved in silence, weapons ready. The Vanguard soldiers flanked them, hammers ready, shields glowing with defensive wards. Professional. Disciplined. Kael found himself impressed despite himself.
The shaft opened into a crawlspace beneath the warehouse floor. Through gaps in the ancient planks, they could see the cultists moving below. Nine circles glowed with sickly green light, each one filled with bound figures—prisoners, Kael realized. Men and women, conscious, bound to the circles, their mouths gagged.
Blood pooled around each one, draining into channels carved into the floor.

"That's soul-essence harvesting," he said. "On a massive scale."
"The Mortician's Guild does it," Aldric replied. "With consent. With regulations. This is—" He stopped. Swallowed. "This is an atrocity."
Below, a cultist in elaborate robes approached the central circle. The one large enough to hold multiple victims. His hands moved in complex patterns, and the circles pulsed brighter.
"The ritual's starting," Kael said. "Whatever they're doing, it's happening now."
Aldric made a decision. "Breach on my mark. We prioritize freeing the prisoners. Ashborn—" He looked at Kael. "Can you harvest from the cultists mid-fight?"
"If I touch them while they're dying."
"Then focus on the leaders. The ones in the robes. Take their memories." Aldric's eyes were hard. "I want to know everything they know about the Whisper King."
The Vanguard soldiers readied their weapons. Kael drew the Soul Reaver, feeling its familiar weight.
"Mark," Aldric said.
The floor exploded upward.

**[Soul Harvest: You extracted 5 from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired.]**

"The Whisper King sees all! The 72 will rise! You cannot—"

The channels sparked and crackled with spiritual energy—energy that instantly sought a new conduit. The cultist screamed as his own power was drained away, the ritual channels drinking deep.
**[Soul Harvest: You extracted 8 from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired.]**

*Not just breaking one seal. They're weakening all of them. The 72 will flood through at once.*

At Maren, laughing as he toyed with Aldric.
*Harvesting a Stage Three. Right now. If I can just—*
Kael moved.

**[Soul Harvest: You extracted 47 from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired. Combat Memory acquired.]**

Kael woke in the healer's station again. Different room—smaller, more private. Aldric sat in a chair by the window, watching the dawn light creep across Thornwick's rooftops.
"How long?"
"Sixteen hours." Aldric didn't turn. "The cult sites all triggered. Seven of nine rituals completed before we could stop them. The seals are destabilizing across the board."
Kael sat up slowly, his body aching but functional. "Casualties?"
"Forty-three Vanguard dead. Another sixty wounded. Civilian casualties are still being counted." Aldric finally looked at him. "But we also recovered seventy-three prisoners. Dismantled two complete cult cells. And we got everything from Maren's mind."
"What about the Whisper King?"
"We don't know who he is. Where he is. What he wants beyond 'free the 72.'" Aldric's jaw tightened. "But we know he's been operating for decades. Possibly centuries. And he's patient."
Kael thought about the memory fragment. About the Whisper King offering to meet him. About the implication that the Whisper King had once carried a blade like the Soul Reaver.
*Someone who discovered what you discovered, and chose a different path.*

*Six Orders. 72 Entities. The Whisper King. And me—carrying a power that someone else once had, once used, once chose to abandon.*

Something that felt like a key turning in a lock.

"Come in."

"Kael Ashborn." Not a question. "I'm Inquisitor Sera. The Council has questions."
Behind her, in the corridor, Kael glimpsed more figures. More robes. More questions.
The game was changing. The pieces were moving. And Kael Ashborn, death-row survivor, Soul Reaver, monster of the underworld, was about to be pulled into the light whether he wanted it or not.

"Shall we begin?"
