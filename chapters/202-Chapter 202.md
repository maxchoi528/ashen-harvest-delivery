Three nights since the raid on the cult warehouse. Three nights of running, hiding, eating nothing but stale bread pilfered from a market stall. The Orders wanted him dead. The Shadow Court wanted him captured. Even the criminal syndicates had put a price on his head.

One of us.

"You're lying."

The Orders. The Six Orders that maintained the seal network. Kael had heard whispers about them in prison, in the cells where they'd kept him before the execution that didn't kill him. He'd never met anyone who'd truly seen their power.
"The Council of Six has issued a declaration," Mira continued. "Any Reaver found is to be destroyed. Not captured. Not studied. Destroyed. They fear what we represent."

That the faithful ascend to the Sun God's realm when they die. But a Reaver knows the truth. The soul is just energy. It can be taken, stripped, consumed. There's no heaven waiting. There's just the void, and whatever you can carry with you when you die."
Kael's grip on the knife tightened. This woman spoke like someone who'd seen too much. Killed too much.

"I didn't see anyone else."
"You wouldn't have. This one was good. Hidden. Working from the shadows while you were cutting through the front lines." Mira pulled a folded paper from her coat, held it out. "I found this in the warehouse after you left. It's a manifest. Names. Locations. Dates."
Kael took the paper, unfolded it. The script was cramped, coded, but he recognized some of the symbols. Rift markers. Location coordinates.
"What is this?"
"A timeline." Mira's voice dropped. "They're planning something big. A mass unsealing event. The cultists want to break all 72 seals at once. Release every Entity trapped in the Abyss."
"That would destroy everything."
"It would destroy the Orders' power, which is the same thing to them." She gestured at the paper. "This names a site. The Sepulcher. It's an old tomb complex beneath the city, sealed for centuries. According to this, the cult's Reaver has been using it as a base of operations."
Kael looked up from the manifest. "You want me to go there."
"I want us to go there. Together. Two Reavers, working in tandem. We can harvest that bastard's abilities, learn what he knows, maybe stop this before it starts."

Mira's expression flickered. For a moment, something human showed through the predator's mask. Pain. Regret.
"Because I made a mistake once. Trusted the wrong people. Let someone close to me die because I thought I could handle things alone." She met his gaze. "I'm tired of watching the world burn because I was too proud to ask for help."
Kael considered the offer. A rival Reaver, appearing from nothing, offering partnership against a common enemy. It could be a trap. It could be genuine.
The soul sight showed him her spiritual signature. Bright, corrupted, but stable. No deception in the energy patterns. She believed what she was saying.

"Tonight. The cult's Reaver moves at midnight, according to the manifest. There's a ritual scheduled. Something big."
Kael folded the manifest, tucked it inside his coat. "Lead the way."

"You can feel it," she said as they descended. "The old death. This place has been a graveyard for centuries. The deeper levels are saturated with residual energy."
Kael felt it. His skin prickled as they moved deeper, the air growing thick with the burden of countless departed souls. Every step ground something beneath his boots that might have been bone.
"The Order doesn't know about this place?"
"They know. They've always known. But the Sepulcher is warded. Old magic, from before the Abyssal Wars. The seals prevent spiritual energy from leaking out, which means Reavers can't harvest effectively. Normal Awakened can't use their abilities here at all."

"That's the question, isn't it?" Mira stopped at a junction, consulted the manifest. "This way."

On the dais, a body lay on a stone slab.
Not a corpse. Not yet. Kael could see the chest rising, falling. The figure was bound, gagged, a collar of black iron around the throat.

"Who?"

"A member of the Iron Vanguard." The voice echoed from everywhere and nowhere. "A captain, captured during the riots last week. The cult wants her blood to power the ritual."

"You're the Reaver," Kael said.

Renderings of the dead, their faces frozen in expressions of agony, their bodies translucent and wrong. "Reavers fight to the death. It's the only way to prove supremacy."
