Kael pressed his back against the damp stone, the cold seeping through his coat. Three floors below, the warehouse disgorged another body into the Thames.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 12 spiritual essence from the remains. Memory fragment acquired."
The notification pulsed behind his eyes, a cold comfort. Twenty-three bodies in four nights. The Thornwick docks had become a dumping ground for gang wars, and Kael had harvested from every single one.
"You moving or brooding?" Lyra materialized from the shadows, her silver-streaked hair catching the gaslight. "The Veiled Market opens in an hour."
"Intelligence first." Kael peeled himself from the wall. "Three different insignias on those corpses. The Ironscales, the Brass Serpents, and—"
"The Crimson Maw." Lyra finished, her expression darkening. "Enemies all. But someone orchestrated this. No gang wastes soldiers like that unless—"
"Unless they're clearing the board." Kael's fingers brushed Soul Reaver's hilt. The blade pulsed with a hunger that matched his own.
A shout echoed from the warehouse entrance. Steel clashed. Kael's head snapped toward the sound.
"They're not done yet." Lyra drew twin daggers, the metal singing as it cleared the scabbard. "Company coming up."
Two figures burst through the door, locked in a grapple. One wore Ironscale colors, the other—Kael's blood chilled—adorned with sigils he recognized from cult rituals. The Eye of the Void. Zarek's mark.
The cultist drove a blade into the Ironscale's throat. The gangster crumpled. The cultist straightened, scanning the shadows.
Kael met those hollow eyes. Recognition flickered. The cultist smiled, raised a small iron horn to his lips, and blew.
A sound like reality tearing filled the alley.
"That's a summoning call," Lyra hissed. "We need to move. Now."
Kael stared at the dying gangster, then at the retreating cultist. Two corpses. One choice.
"Go." He stepped toward the Ironscale. "Get to the safehouse. I'll catch up."
"Kael—"
"Go!"
Lyra vanished into shadow. Kael dropped to his knees beside the Ironscale, pressing both palms against the cooling flesh. The Soul Reaver's power surged through him.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 8 spiritual essence from the remains. Combat Memory acquired: [Blade Counter #7]."
The technique flooded his mind—a move designed to parry multiple strikes in succession. Useful.
The horn sounded again, closer. Kael forced himself to wait, harvesting every drop of essence from the corpse. When he finished, he was already running.
* * *
The Fogmire safehouse squatted between a defunct tannery and a gambling den. Kael slipped through the back entrance, his boots leaving muddy prints on warped floorboards. Lyra stood at the window, watching the street.
"Three more cultists swept through the alley after you left." She didn't turn. "They're hunting something."
"Probably me." Kael dropped into a chair, the wood groaning beneath him. "That cultist saw Soul Reaver. Word will reach Zarek."
"Let it." Lyra turned, and Kael saw the steel in her eyes. "You want to find Zarek? I know where he's been meeting his inner circle. Old mortuary on the east side. The one that burned down three months ago."
"You've known this and didn't tell me?"
"I've been confirming it." Lyra crossed to the table, spreading a rough map across its surface. "The Mortician's Guild abandoned that site after the fire, but someone's been using the basement. I followed one of Zarek's lieutenants there last week."
Kael studied the map. The mortuary sat three blocks from the harbor, close enough to the dock murders to suggest a connection. "The gang wars. The cult involvement. They're connected."
"The Ironscales, Brass Serpents, and Crimson Maw have been fighting over territorial control for months. But they've all lost key figures recently. Assassinations disguised as gang warfare." Lyra traced a finger along the map. "Someone's destabilizing Thornwick's underworld on purpose."
"Zarek?"
"Or someone using Zarek." Lyra met his gaze. "Cultists don't usually care about street gangs. Whatever's happening, it's bigger than religious zealotry."
Kael thought of the horn, the summoning call. "That cultist was broadcasting. If Zarek's moving pieces around the city, he needs soldiers. Gang recruits."
"Fresh meat for the grinder." Lyra's jaw tightened. "I've seen the rituals at that mortuary. Sacrifice to the Void. They need bodies."
The implication settled over Kael like a shroud. "The gang wars are feeding his cult.."
"Which brings us to the real question." Lyra tapped the mortuary's location. "Why does Zarek need an army? The cult's always been about summoning entities, not conquering territory."
Kael remembered the sealed rifts, the weakening barriers between worlds. The Great Rift degrading faster than projected. "He's not building an army. He's building a path."
Lyra's eyes narrowed. "Explain."
"The Abyss. The entities. They're trapped behind seals that are failing." Kael stood, pacing the cramped room. "A cult devoted to the Void wouldn't waste time on street gangs unless those gang members were part of a larger plan. Soldiers for guarding ritual sites. Sacrifices for powering ceremonies. Workers for digging tunnels."
"You think he's trying to unseal a rift."
"I think he's already unsealing one." Kael stopped at the window, staring at the gaslit street below. "The gang wars aren't chaos. They're demolition. Clearing obstacles. Removing rivals who might interfere."
Lyra rose to stand beside him. "The Shadow Court has been tracking unusual spiritual activity in Thornwick for months. They assumed it was regional instability. But if Zarek's cult is orchestrating the violence—"
"Then someone needs to stop them before they crack open a door that can't be closed." Kael turned to face her. "We go tonight."
"That's suicide. Zarek's cult has Awakened fighters. Probably Ascendant-stage."
"Then we harvest from the corpses afterward." Kael's hand found Soul Reaver's hilt. "The cult kills people. I take their power. In a few hours, I'll be strong enough to cut through his lieutenants."
"And if you die in the meantime?"
"Then someone else will have to stop him." Kael smiled, the expression sharp enough to cut. "But I'd die trying than watch the world end from a safehouse."
Lyra stared at him for a long moment. Then she laughed, the sound brittle but genuine. "You really are insane."
"So I've been told."
* * *
The old mortuary gaped like a wound in the city's flesh. Fire had gutted the upper floors, leaving blackened timbers jutting toward the sky. The basement remained intact, accessible through a service entrance half-hidden by collapsed masonry.
Kael crouched in the shadow of a burned-out carriage, studying the building. Two cultists guarded the service entrance. Their posture spoke of training, discipline. Not common thugs.
"I count four more inside," Lyra whispered from somewhere to his left. "Patrolling the upper levels. Probably more below."
"The basement?"
"My guess? At least six. Maybe an Awakened overseer."
Kael nodded, calculating. Ten enemies, possibly more. His current strength wouldn't be enough to fight them directly. But Soul Reaver didn't require direct confrontation.
"I need a corpse."
"There's a heap behind the east wall. Failed sacrifices, probably. Bodies with the heads bashed in so they couldn't be reanimated."
"Perfect."
Kael circled the building, staying low, moving through pools of darkness. The heap was exactly where Lyra said. Six corpses, their skulls crushed by blunt force. Fresh enough—less than a day old.
He touched the first one.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 3 spiritual essence from the remains. Memory fragment acquired: [Void Blessing ritual positioning]."
Not much, but useful. The fragment showed a ritual chamber, cultists arranged in a specific pattern around a central altar. He harvested from the next five corpses, extracting essence and memories in equal measure.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 4 spiritual essence from the remains. Memory fragment acquired: [Guard rotation schedule]."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 2 spiritual essence from the remains. Combat Memory acquired: [Basic Void Shield]."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 5 spiritual essence from the remains. Memory fragment acquired: [Zarek's appearance times]."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 3 spiritual essence from the remains. Skill Fragment acquired: [Void Speech (partial)]."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 6 spiritual essence from the remains. Memory fragment acquired: [The basement altar location]."
The final harvest hit hardest. Kael saw a chamber deep below the mortuary, stone walls carved with sigils that hurt to perceive. An altar of black granite. Chains hanging from the ceiling. And Zarek, standing at the altar's center, his face lit by sickly purple light.
"The seal is weakening," Zarek's memory-voice echoed. "Three more nights. We need three more nights."
Three nights. The timeline compressed in Kael's mind. If Zarek planned to unseal a rift in three days, they couldn't afford to wait for a better opportunity.
The basement altar. That was where the cult conducted its darkest rituals. Kael could see the approach now, clear as daylight—a maintenance tunnel that connected to the old sewer system.
"We go now," he told Lyra when she materialized beside him. "I know the way."
* * *
The tunnel stank of centuries-old filth and fresher horrors. Kael moved by feel, one hand trailing the slimy wall, the other gripping Soul Reaver. Behind him, Lyra's breathing came soft and controlled.
They emerged into a carved stone corridor, torches guttering in iron sconces. The purple glow came from ahead, pulsing with each heartbeat.
"Stay close," Kael breathed. "Stay quiet."
They moved through the corridor like ghosts. Voices drifted from the chamber ahead—chanting in a language that scraped against Kael's sanity. The Void Speech he'd harvested from the corpse rose unbidden in his mind, translating fragments.
"...the seal fractures..."
"...the ancient one stirs..."
"...three more sacrifices required..."
Three sacrifices. Three nights. The math aligned with the memory he'd harvested. Zarek needed three more bodies to break the seal completely.
Kael pressed against the corridor wall, peering around the corner. The ritual chamber opened before him, vast and circular. Cultists ringed the central altar, their hoods thrown back, faces ecstatic. At the altar's center, a pit had been carved into the stone, and within that pit, reality seemed to bend. A tear in the world, barely contained by chains of golden light.
The seal. Kael's blood ran cold. It was smaller than he'd imagined—maybe ten feet across—but the power radiating from it pressed against his senses like a physical weight. Through that tear, he could see shapes moving. Entities. Waiting.
Zarek stood at the altar's edge, arms raised, presiding over the ceremony. Three bodies hung from chains overhead, their throats cut, blood dripping into channels carved in the stone floor. The channels led to the pit, feeding the tear with stolen life.
"They're strengthening the ritual anchor," Lyra whispered. "The blood creates a bridge. Once enough life-force accumulates—"
"The seal becomes a door."
"Exactly."
Kael counted the cultists. Eight in the chamber, all wearing the Eye of the Void. None showed the spiritual pressure of an Awakened, but the chanting was draining his awareness. They might have hidden fighters. Or traps.
His gaze found the three corpses on the chains. Fresh kills. Perfect for harvesting. But reaching them meant crossing the entire chamber under the eyes of eight cultists and their master.
A shadow detached from the far wall. Kael tensed—then recognized the silhouette. One of Zarek's lieutenants, moving toward the altar with a bundle in his arms.
Another sacrifice.
The lieutenant climbed the altar steps, dumping the bundle at Zarek's feet. A body tumbled free, and Kael's heart stopped.
Silver-streaked hair. A face frozen in terror. A woman he didn't recognize—but the hair, the coloring, matched Lyra exactly.
"No," Lyra breathed beside him.
Zarek laughed, the sound bubbling up from his throat like poisoned water. "Did you think I didn't know about your little spy, Shadow Court? Your interference in my affairs ends tonight. This woman's soul will fuel the final ritual. And when the rift opens, your Order will be the first to fall."
Lyra's composure shattered. She lunged forward, daggers flashing—
And the cultists turned as one, weapons raised, void energy crackling between their fingers.
Kael didn't think. He acted.
Soul Reaver sang as it cleared the scabbard. Kael burst from the corridor, channeling the harvested [Blade Counter #7] technique. The first cultist's strike met empty air; Kael's blade found his throat. The second lunged; Kael deflected, spun, opened her stomach in a spray of crimson.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 9 spiritual essence from the remains. Combat Memory acquired: [Void Strike]."
Two down. Six cultists remained, forming a protective ring around the altar. Zarek stepped back, pulling the screaming woman with him.
"Kael Ashborn." Zarek's voice cut through the chaos, cold and amused. "The Soul Reaver. I wondered when you'd come crawling out of whatever hole the executioner missed."
"You framed me." Kael pressed forward, blade steady. "You set me up. Made me a fugitive so I couldn't expose your cult."
"I did what was necessary. A Soul Reaver loose in Thornwick could have disrupted my plans." Zarek smiled, the expression wrong, as if his face had been carved from wax. "But now you're here. And you'll serve the cause after all."
The cultists attacked as one. Kael met them with everything he had—harvested skills flowing through his limbs, spiritual essence reinforcing his strikes. The Void Shield he'd taken proved worthless against Soul Reaver's edge, but the Void Strike burned through his enemies' defenses like acid.
Three more cultists fell. Kael harvested in the midst of combat, his power surging with each kill.
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 11 spiritual essence from the remains. Skill acquired: [Void Sight]."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 8 spiritual essence from the remains. Memory fragment acquired: [The rift's location—Beneath the cathedral]."
"Soul Harvest: You extracted 14 spiritual essence from the remains. Combat Memory acquired: [Zarek's fighting style]."
The final cultist fell. Kael stood in a ring of corpses, chest heaving, blood dripping from a dozen superficial wounds. Before him, Zarek crouched behind the altar, the captive woman held before him like a shield.
"Stay back." Zarek pressed a blade to her throat. "One step, and she dies. I'll take my chances with the rift."
"You'll die trying to run."
"I'm not planning to run." Zarek's smile widened. "I'm planning to ascend. When the rift opens fully, I'll become a vessel for something far greater than this miserable world."
The seal pulsed behind him. Through his newly acquired Void Sight, Kael could see the chains straining, golden light flickering. The tear in reality widened by inches.
Lyra appeared at his side, daggers dripping. Her eyes fixed on the captive woman—her sister, Kael realized. The Mortician's Guild operative. "Mira. He's going to—"
"I know." Kael's mind raced. The woman was a hostage, a bargaining chip. Zarek could kill her before Kael reached him. But if the cultist escaped into the rift...
Zarek's blade bit into the woman's throat. A thin line of blood traced down her neck. "Time's up, Soul Reaver. Drop your weapon and kneel, or—"
The ceiling exploded inward.
Stone and timber rained down, and with it came a figure wreathed in golden light. Kael threw himself aside, shielding Lyra with his body. When the debris settled, a woman stood in the ruined chamber, her armor blazing with spiritual energy, her sword pointed at Zarek's throat.
"By order of the Order of Dawn," she announced, voice ringing with authority, "you are hereby charged with crimes against the seal network and sentenced to immediate execution."
Zarek screamed in fury. The seal behind him cracked, golden chains shattering. The rift yawned wider, and something began to emerge—a hand of shadow and flame, reaching for the world.
The Order of Dawn knight charged. Zarek released his hostage, hurling the woman aside as he met the knight's strike. Reality screamed. The chamber shook.
Kael caught Mira before she hit the ground, dragging her toward the exit. Lyra followed, her daggers forgotten, her face pale.
"The rift," Lyra gasped. "It's opening."
"We can't stop it." Kael clutched Mira tighter. "We need to run."
Behind them, the knight fought Zarek with blazing fury. But even as they clashed, the rift expanded, swallowing stone and flame and shadow. Something was coming through—something vast and terrible and hungry.
They reached the tunnel. They ran. Behind them, the mortuary collapsed entirely, burying the ritual chamber and the rift and whatever nightmare had begun to emerge.
They emerged into the night air gasping, the ground still trembling beneath their feet. In the distance, church bells began to ring—an alarm, a warning, a death knell.
Mira stirred in Kael's arms, her eyes fluttering open. "The rift," she whispered. "The cathedral. Beneath the cathedral—"
"I know." Kael's mind churned with fragmented memories. The cult's true target. Not the mortuary—a decoy. A distraction. While everyone focused on this site, Zarek's true believers were already working beneath the city's holiest ground.
"The seal won't hold," Mira continued, her voice fading. "If they breach the cathedral rift... Everything ends..."
Lyra knelt beside her sister, clutching her hand. "Save your strength. We'll stop them."
"You don't understand." Mira's eyes locked with Kael's. "The cathedral seal is the Great Rift. The one the cult's been targeting. The one that's been weakening for months. If it breaks—"
Her voice trailed off. Her eyes closed. Her breathing steadied into unconsciousness.
Kael stood, staring at the distant silhouette of Thornwick's cathedral. Its spires rose against the night sky, dark and silent. Somewhere below those ancient stones, cultists were working to tear open the door between worlds.
And he had three days. Maybe less.
"We need allies," he said. "The Order of Dawn knight—can we trust her?"
Lyra shook her head. "The Dawn fights cults, but they also hunt Soul Reavers. If she learns what you are—"
"She'll try to kill me."
"Yes."
Kael considered the options. The Shadow Court had resources but no love for him. The other Orders would take one look at Soul Reaver and declare him an enemy. His only allies were a wounded spy and a woman who kept secrets like other people breathed.
And somewhere beneath the cathedral, Zarek was preparing for the end of the world.
A sound echoed from the collapsed mortuary. Not collapse—movement. Something was digging through the rubble.
"We need to go," Lyra said urgently. "Now."
Kael took one last look at the destruction. The rift might be contained for now, but its presence had been detected. The Orders would mobilize. The city would descend into chaos. And Zarek would use that chaos as cover for his true operation.
The sound grew louder. Clawing. Scraping.
Kael turned and ran, carrying Mira into the fog-shrouded streets of Thornwick.
Somewhere beneath the city, a door was opening. And he had no idea what waited on the other side.
