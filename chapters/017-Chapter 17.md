The chains rattled against the stone wall as Kael pressed his back into the corner of his cell. Two guards stood outside his door, their silhouettes barely visible through the barred window. They had been whispering all night.
"He's one of them. Soul Reaver. You know what they do to those?"
Kael's fingers traced the crude symbol scratched into the floor. The guards didn't know he could hear them. Awakened senses made their hushed voices sound like shouts in the adjacent corridor.
"Then why hasn't he been executed already?"
"Orders from the top. Something about keeping him alive until the Inquisitors arrive. Three days out."
Kael's jaw tightened. Three days. He had three days before the Order of Dawn sent their hunters to dissect his soul and study what made him different. He would not wait for them.
He pushed himself to his feet, ignoring the ache in his ribs where the prior interrogation had cracked something. The cell measured six paces by four, with a single slit window near the ceiling that let in slivers of grey light. The walls were granite, ancient and unyielded. The door was iron, three inches thick, secured by a mechanism he couldn't see from this angle.
But he could feel the prison itself. The spiritual residue of centuries of suffering soaked into the stones. Every execution, every whipping, every death had left traces that he could almost taste if he focused.
The Soul Reaver stirred in his chest, a cold presence that had become as familiar as his own heartbeat over the past weeks. It demanded nothing from him. It waited.
His stomach dropped. Three days was not enough time to break out through conventional means. But Blackgate Prison had one weakness the guards had never considered. It was old, built before the current understanding of spiritual energy circulation. The founders had never accounted for someone who could harvest from the dead.
Kael approached the door and pressed his palm flat against the iron. Cold metal bit into his skin. He closed his eyes and reached out with senses that ordinary Awakened could never possess.
The prison's spiritual geography unfolded in his mind like a map drawn in frost and shadow. The cells above him held dozens of inmates, most of them spiritually inert, their life forces dim and flickering. The guards burned brighter, their Awakened cores small but functional. But it was the depths below that drew his attention.
Blackgate had been built atop an older structure. Catacombs. The dead had been stored there for generations before the prison moved to modern methods. Now the lower levels held the remains of those who had died within these walls, their bodies preserved by the same cold that kept the foundation stable.
Kael's mouth curved without humor. The dead were his pathway out. If he could reach the catacombs, he could harvest abilities from those who had died here. Guards who had mastered combat techniques. Inmates who had possessed skills he could use. Even fragments of Awakened who had been executed might yield something useful.
But first, he needed to get out of this cell.
He stepped back from the door and studied the room again. The scratch marks on the floor were not random. Someone had been counting days, then weeks, then months, before finally giving up. The previous occupant had carved tally marks into the stone, and Kael had added his own to the bottom of the sequence.
Twenty-three days since his wrongful imprisonment. Twenty-three days since the Order of Iron had framed him for murders he had not committed. Twenty-three days of waiting for an opportunity that had never arrived.
Until now.
The guards had mentioned the Inquisitors. That meant information was flowing upward to the Orders. Someone in Blackgate was communicating with the outside world, which meant there were channels he could exploit if he could reach them.
Kael sat down on the thin pallet that served as his bed and focused on the chains binding his wrists. They were spiritual suppression manacles, designed to limit an Awakened's ability to draw on their own soul reserves. They would not stop him from using the Soul Reaver. That was the irony the guards did not understand. The manacles limited his personal spiritual power, but the Soul Reaver drew from external sources.
He just needed a source.
The door opened. Kael looked up as a new figure entered his cell. The man was tall, broad-shouldered, wearing the black and silver uniform of Blackgate's internal security. But his bearing was different from the regular guards. This man moved like a predator who had learned patience through decades of hunting.
Sergeant Owen Briarwood. The name surfaced from whispered conversations Kael had overheard. The man responsible for keeping prisoners contained until their execution dates arrived. The man who had personally supervised the interrogation of six Soul Reaver suspects over the past decade, all of whom had died in custody under circumstances that were never fully explained.
Briarwood studied Kael with eyes that missed nothing. "You're smaller than I expected."
Kael said nothing.
"They tell me you killed eleven Awakened in Thornwick. Most of them were Ascendant class." Briarwood stepped closer, his boots heavy on the stone floor. "That would make you either extremely powerful or extremely lucky. Which is it?"
"I didn't kill anyone."
"Ah." Briarwood's expression didn't change. "The Order of Iron's evidence says otherwise. The blood samples, the witness testimonies, the spiritual residue at the crime scenes. Very thorough work."
"Framed."
"Framed." Briarwood tasted the word like wine. "By whom?"
"Zarek."
The name landed like a stone in still water. Briarwood's eyes flickered, the first genuine reaction Kael had seen. "Zarek Vane. Order of Iron. Third Circle Investigator." He paused. "You have history with him?"
"He killed my unit. Blamed me for it."
Briarwood was silent for a long moment. Then he laughed, a dry sound without warmth. "You expect me to believe that a Third Circle Investigator framed a random death-row prisoner for multiple murders? Do I look like I was born yesterday?"
Kael met his gaze without flinching. "No. You look like someone who knows that the Orders are not what they claim to be."
The temperature in the cell dropped. Briarwood's hand moved toward the spiritual suppressor at his belt, a device that could incapacitate an Awakened with a single pulse. "Choose your next words carefully, prisoner."
"The Six Orders are failing." Kael's voice was steady. "The seal network is degrading. The rifts are widening. In fifty years, maybe less, there won't be enough Awakened to hold back the Abyss. And you know this, because you've been here long enough to see the pattern."
Briarwood's hand stopped. His eyes narrowed. "How do you know about the seal network?"
"Because I'm a Soul Reaver. I can sense the spiritual damage. This prison is built on a ley line intersection, which means it's perfectly positioned to monitor the northern seal network. Someone should have noticed that the readings are getting worse. But no one has, because the people in charge don't want to know."
Kael pushed himself to his feet, chains swaying. "You brought me out of my cell to test me. To see if I was worth the Inquisitors' time. So test me. Ask me something that only a real Soul Reaver would know."
Briarwood studied him for what felt like an eternity. Then he stepped back and gestured to the guards outside. "Leave us."
The door closed. Kael and the Sergeant stood alone in the cramped cell, the dim light casting long shadows across the stone floor.
"The catacombs," Briarwood said quietly. "What do you sense about them?"
Kael tilted his head, extending his spiritual senses downward through the floor. The cold presence of the dead reached up to meet him, thousands of them resting in the darkness below. "Four hundred and seventeen corpses in the upper catacombs. Another two hundred in the lower chambers. Most are spiritually inert. But there are fragments of Awakened in the execution chamber. At least three who were powerful when they died."
Briarwood's expression shifted from skepticism to something else. "The execution chamber was sealed fifty years ago. No one has been down there since."
"The seals are weakening."
Another long silence. Then Briarwood reached into his coat and produced a small object, placing it on the floor between them. It was a badge, silver and black, bearing the symbol of the Iron Vanguard.
"The official story is that the Iron Vanguard has no presence in Blackgate," Briarwood said. "But I've served the Vanguard for thirty years. My actual assignment is this prison. I monitor the seal network and report to my superiors when the readings become critical."
Kael looked at the badge, then back at Briarwood's face. "Why are you telling me this?"
"Because you're right. The seals are failing faster than anyone predicted. The Order of Dawn's Inquisitors are not coming to study you. They're coming to silence you before you become a problem." Briarwood's voice hardened. "And because I need to know if you're what you claim to be."
"How do I prove that?"
Briarwood gestured toward the floor. "The catacombs. I can get you access to the lower levels. But I need something in return."
"What?"
"Information. The Soul Reaver ability allows you to harvest more than just skills. You can extract memories, can't you? Fragments of what the dead knew?"
Kael nodded slowly. The Soul Reaver was indeed capable of that, though it required more time and proximity than harvesting simple abilities. "I can. But extracting memories from the dead is not pleasant for them. Or for me."
Briarwood's jaw tightened. "There are specific individuals buried in those catacombs. Inmates who died under suspicious circumstances. I need to know what they knew before they died. Information that was never recorded in the official logs."
"Such as?"
"Such as who ordered the deaths of Commander Gwen Ashford's squad in the Thornwick Catacombs six months ago." Briarwood's eyes bored into Kael's. "You were there when they died. You survived when no one else should have. And if your story about being framed is true, then you have more reason than anyone to find out who was really responsible."
Kael's hands clenched at his sides. Gwen's squad. Six Awakened who had trusted him to lead them through the catacombs. Six people who had died screaming while something unimaginable tore through their defenses. Zarek had blamed him for it, had used the massacre to justify his execution.
But Kael remembered fragments of that night. The horror. The blood. The thing that had moved through the darkness like smoke and shadow. He had been knocked unconscious before the end, and when he woke, he was surrounded by corpses with no memory of how he had survived.
"I can try," he said. "But I need access to fresh corpses. The older ones yield degraded results."
Briarwood nodded. "There's a death tonight. Scheduled execution at dawn. A man named Aldric Voss, convicted of treason against the Crown. He was Awakened, Transcendent class, with combat abilities that made him dangerous enough to require permanent containment."
"Where?"
"Cell Block D, third sublevel. I'll arrange for you to be moved nearby." Briarwood paused. "If you can harvest useful information from Voss, I'll consider helping you further. But if this is a trick, if you're playing some game I don't understand—"
"It's not a trick." Kael met his gaze. "I want to know who killed Gwen's squad as badly as you do. Maybe more."
Briarwood studied him for a long moment, then turned toward the door. "Midnight. I'll send someone to collect you. Don't make me regret this."
The door opened and closed. Kael was alone again.
He sat back down on the pallet and stared at the floor, processing what had just happened. Briarwood was not what he had expected. The man was not a prison guard following orders. He was a hidden operative for the Iron Vanguard, monitoring the seal network and collecting information about threats that the official Orders refused to acknowledge.
And he had just offered Kael a chance to use the Soul Reaver on a freshly executed prisoner.
Kael's pulse quickened. The Soul Reaver was not a tool for the faint of heart. Every harvest left traces on him, fragments of the dead that lingered in his consciousness like echoes. The abilities he extracted were useful, but the memories were a burden he had learned to manage through discipline and distance.
But if Aldric Voss had been a Transcendent Awakened, his soul would contain powerful abilities and potentially critical information. Voss had been convicted of treason, which meant he had known something valuable enough to threaten the Crown's interests.
Midnight was six hours away. Kael would need to rest, to conserve his strength for what came next.
He lay down on the thin mattress and closed his eyes. Sleep would not come easily, not with the anticipation burning in his chest. But he had learned to function on minimal rest during the war against the Abyss. Six hours of broken sleep would have to be enough.
When the guards came for him at midnight, Kael was already awake, sitting in the darkness with his eyes open. They did not speak to him as they escorted him through the prison's winding corridors. Torchlight flickered against ancient stone, casting dancing shadows across the walls.
They descended. One level. Two. Three. The air grew colder with each step, carrying the faint smell of preservation salts and old death.
Cell Block D was different from the upper levels. The cells here were larger, each one containing a single occupant. Kael glimpsed several as he passed—men and women in various states of despair, their spiritual energies dampened by suppression fields that made the ones on his own manacles look like children's toys.
The guards stopped at a heavy iron door marked with the symbol of execution. One of them produced a key, and the lock clicked open with a sound like breaking bone.
Kael was pushed inside. The room beyond was bare except for a single chair bolted to the floor. Aldric Voss sat in the chair, his wrists and ankles bound with spiritual restraints that glowed faintly blue. His eyes were closed, his breathing slow and even. He was not asleep. He was conserving his strength, the way Awakened learned to do in the final hours before their deaths.
The guards left. The door closed behind them with a finality that echoed in the empty chamber.
Voss opened his eyes. They were pale blue, almost colorless, and they fixed on Kael with an intensity that suggested he had been expecting this.
"You're the Soul Reaver," he said. His voice was hoarse from disuse. "They told me someone would come."
Kael approached slowly, keeping his movements non-threatening. "I'm Kael."
"I know who you are. Ashborn. The one who survived Thornwick." Voss's lips curved in a grim smile. "They say you're a monster."
"What are you?"
"A traitor. That's what the Crown calls me." Voss leaned back in the chair as much as his restraints would allow. "But I prefer to think of myself as a man who learned the truth about the Orders and paid the price for sharing it."
"What truth?"
Voss studied him for a long moment. "The seal network is failing because someone is deliberately weakening it. Not the Abyss. Not natural degradation. People. High-ranking members of the Order of Dawn and the Order of Iron who have made a pact with something beyond the Veil."
Kael felt ice form in his stomach. "A pact with what?"
"Something old. Something that was imprisoned during the Abyssal Wars and has been working ever since to break free. The cultists who worship it are not random fanatics. They're positioned in key places throughout the Orders, waiting for the right moment to act." Voss's eyes bored into Kael's. "You want to know who killed Commander Ashford's squad? It wasn't Zarek, though he thinks it was. It was a third party, someone who wanted to eliminate any witnesses to what they found in the Thornwick Catacombs."
"What did they find?"
"That's the question, isn't it?" Voss laughed, a bitter sound. "I've spent three years trying to answer it. And now I'm about to die without ever knowing for sure. Unless..."
He trailed off, his gaze shifting to Kael's hands.
"Unless you take it from me."
Kael was silent. The Soul Reaver stirred in his chest, responding to the proximity of a dying Awakened. Voss was scheduled for execution at dawn, which meant his soul would be fresh, potent, full of power and knowledge that could serve Kael's purposes.
But harvesting from a conscious subject was different from harvesting from a corpse. The memories would be sharper. The abilities would be more intact. The experience would also be far more invasive, forcing him into direct contact with Voss's consciousness in ways that left no room for distance or detachment.
"You understand what you're asking," Kael said quietly.
"I understand that I'm about to die either way. The Crown has decided my usefulness has ended. But if you take what I know, maybe something good can come from my death. Maybe you can finish what I started."
Voss closed his eyes. "Do it. Before I lose my nerve."
Kael moved forward and placed his hand on Voss's chest, directly over his heart. The skin was cold, the heartbeat sluggish. Voss had been weakened deliberately, his spiritual reserves drained to prevent him from resisting the execution.
The Soul Reaver activated.
It was not a comfortable sensation. Power flowed from Voss into Kael like ice water through a cracked vessel, carrying with it fragments of memory and slivers of ability that lodged themselves in Kael's consciousness with almost painful clarity.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Combat Instinct (Enhanced) from the remains. Quality: High. Effect: Improved reaction speed in combat scenarios.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Resistance (Spiritual Dampening) from the remains. Quality: High. Effect: 40% reduction in effectiveness of spiritual suppression fields.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Memory Fragment (Classified) from the remains. Quality: Medium. Effect: Partial access to subject's final memories.]
The abilities slotted into place alongside the ones Kael had already accumulated, expanding his capabilities in ways that would take time to fully integrate. But it was the memory fragment that drew his attention, a cascade of images and sounds that played out behind his eyes like a fever dream.
He saw Voss in a dark chamber, surrounded by figures in robes that obscured their faces. He heard whispers in a language that predated the current Orders. He felt terror, cold and absolute, as something vast and incomprehensible turned its attention toward him.
Then he saw a symbol, carved into the wall of the chamber. Three interlocking circles surrounding a vertical line, with small marks at the cardinal points.
And he saw a face, half-hidden in shadow, watching him with eyes that burned like dying stars.
The memory fragment ended. Kael gasped and pulled his hand back, his body trembling from the aftershocks of the harvest.
Voss was still breathing, but barely. The extraction had taken more from him than anticipated, draining the last reserves of his spiritual energy in a desperate bid to preserve his memories.
"The symbol," Kael said, his voice raw. "What does it mean?"
Voss opened his eyes, his gaze unfocused. "The Tripartite Seal. The original design used to imprison the Entity known as the Whisper King. But someone has been modifying it, weakening the containment protocols. The symbol you saw... That's the mark of the people who are working to free him."
"Who? Give me names."
Voss shook his head weakly. "Too many. They're everywhere. In the Orders, in the Council of Six, in places you would never expect." His hand twitched, reaching toward Kael. "The tomb. You need to find the original tomb. The first Soul Reaver. He knew about the Whisper King, knew how to bind him properly. His methods are recorded somewhere, hidden where no one has thought to look."
"Where?"
"I don't know. I died trying to find out." Voss's eyes rolled back, his breath rattling in his chest. "Find the tomb. Find the records. Before it's too late. Before they finish what they started..."
His voice faded. His chest rose one final time, then fell and did not rise again.
Kael sat in the darkness, his hand still pressed against the dead man's chest, processing everything he had just learned. The Whisper King. A name he had never heard before, attached to an Entity that had been imprisoned during the Abyssal Wars and was now being actively freed by traitors within the Orders themselves.
And the original Soul Reaver. The first one who had created the ability that Kael now carried. Whoever they had been, they had known how to properly contain the Whisper King, and their knowledge had been hidden somewhere after their death.
The tomb. Voss had said to find the tomb.
Kael pulled himself to his feet, his legs unsteady from the harvest. The abilities he had gained were settling into place, giving him new capabilities that would serve him well in the days ahead. But it was the memory fragment that troubled him most.
Someone had killed Gwen's squad to cover up what they had found. Someone had framed him for those murders to ensure he would be executed before he could investigate further. And someone was now sending Inquisitors to Blackgate not to study him, but to silence him before he could expose the conspiracy.
Briarwood did not know the full scope of what was happening. He was a loyal servant of the Iron Vanguard, following orders without understanding the larger picture. But he had access to the prison's records and its connections to the outside world.
Kael needed that access.
He turned toward the door and began to pound on it with his fist. The guards would come eventually. They would find him standing over Voss's body with no memory of how the man had died. They would assume Voss had passed away in the night, his weak heart finally giving out under the strain of spiritual dampening.
And then Kael would have a choice to make. He could stay in Blackgate, waiting for the Inquisitors to arrive, playing the role of the compliant prisoner until an opportunity presented itself. Or he could act now, using the abilities he had just harvested to break out of the prison and disappear into the world beyond.
The decision would shape everything that came after.
The door opened. Two guards stood in the entrance, their expressions wary. "What is it?"
"The prisoner is dead," Kael said flatly. "Schedule for the next one."
One of the guards pushed past him to check Voss's body, confirming what Kael had already stated. The man's face twisted in disgust. "Another one bites the dust. I'll report this to the Sergeant."
Briarwood arrived within minutes, his expression unreadable as he surveyed the scene. Kael stood motionless against the wall, his chains rattling softly with each breath. The guards had not bothered to restrain him beyond the manacles on his wrists.
"You harvested from him," Briarwood said quietly. It was not a question.
"Yes."
"And?"
Kael met his gaze. "The people who killed Gwen's squad are not random cultists. They're positioned inside the Orders. High-ranking members who are working to free something called the Whisper King. And they killed those Awakened to cover up evidence of what they're doing."
Briarwood's face went pale. "The Whisper King. That's a myth. A legend from the Abyssal Wars."
"It's not a myth." Kael reached into his pocket—empty, but he mimed the gesture anyway. "Voss saw their symbol. Three interlocking circles around a vertical line. He called it the Tripartite Seal. And he said the original Soul Reaver's tomb contains information about how to properly contain whatever they're trying to free."
"You don't know where the tomb is."
"No. But you have access to records. Historical documents. Anything that might point me in the right direction."
Briarwood was silent for a long moment. The guards had retreated to the doorway, watching the exchange with expressions of confusion. They did not understand what was happening, and Briarwood had no intention of explaining.
"Get him back to his cell," Briarwood ordered. "I'll deal with this."
The guards moved forward to escort Kael out. As they led him away from the execution chamber, Kael glanced back at Briarwood. The Sergeant stood alone beside Voss's body, his face hidden in shadow, his thoughts unreadable.
Whatever decision he made next would determine whether he became an ally or an obstacle.
Kael was returned to his cell without incident. The guards threw him inside and locked the door, their footsteps fading down the corridor. Kael stood alone in the darkness, listening to the silence of Blackgate Prison settling around him like a burial shroud.
The abilities he had harvested pulsed in his consciousness, new tools waiting to be used. Combat Instinct would sharpen his reactions in fights. Resistance would help him ignore spiritual suppression fields. And the memory fragment... The memory fragment was a thread he could follow, a clue that might lead him to answers about the Whisper King and the conspiracy that had destroyed his life.
But threads could be cut. Clues could be erased. And the Inquisitors were still two days away.
Kael sat down on his pallet and began to plan. The Soul Reaver had given him power, but power meant nothing without strategy. He needed to get out of Blackgate before the Inquisitors arrived. He needed to find the original Soul Reaver's tomb before the traitors succeeded in their plan.
And he needed to find Zarek.
The man who had framed him. The man who had killed Gwen's squad and blamed Kael for the murders. The man who might know more about the conspiracy than he realized.
Kael's hands clenched around the chains on his wrists. The Iron Vanguard had given him a temporary reprieve. Briarwood was sympathetic, but his loyalties were unclear. And the Order of Dawn would arrive soon with orders to silence him permanently.
Time was running out.
He lay down on the pallet and closed his eyes. Sleep would not come, not with his mind racing through possibilities and contingencies. But he needed to rest, to conserve his strength for what lay ahead.
The cell was silent except for the distant sound of water dripping somewhere in the depths of the prison. Cold air seeped through the stones, carrying the faint smell of decay that never left Blackgate's corridors.
Kael listened to the drips, counting them like heartbeats, marking the passage of time until the next crisis arrived.
The door opened.
Not with the normal creak of hinges, but with a soft click that suggested someone had oiled the mechanism recently. Kael's eyes snapped open as a figure slipped inside, closing the door behind them with practiced efficiency.
The figure wore the black and silver uniform of Blackgate's internal security, but the build was wrong. Too slender. Too graceful in its movements.
A woman stood in the doorway of his cell, her features obscured by a deep hood. "Kael Ashborn."
"Who are you?"
"Someone who has been waiting for this moment." She stepped closer, her voice dropping to a whisper. "The Sergeant thinks he is playing a complex game. He does not realize how small his role truly is."
Kael sat up slowly, keeping his movements controlled. "You work for the traitor faction."

The woman paused. "Because the Whisper King's faction killed my family. Twenty years ago, they opened a rift in the northern provinces and let an Entity through. My village was erased in a single night. I alone survived, and I have spent every year since then trying to understand why."

The woman smiled beneath her hood. "Let them come. I have been hunted before."
She turned toward the door, then paused. "The tomb is in the Shattered Peaks, three days' journey north of Thornwick. Look for the place where three rivers meet. The entrance is hidden beneath a waterfall that flows backward. You will know it when you see it."
"How will I—"
But the woman was already gone, slipping through the door like a ghost and disappearing into the darkness of the corridor. Kael stood alone in his cell, holding the vial of soul-essence, feeling the weight of a decision that would change everything.
The Inquisitors were coming. The traitor faction was moving. And somewhere in the Shattered Peaks, the key to stopping the Whisper King waited to be found.
Kael uncorked the vial and let the spiritual energy flood into his body.
