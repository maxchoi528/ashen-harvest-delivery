[Soul Harvest: You extracted Spirit Sight Lv.3 from the remains. Skill acquired.]
[Soul Harvest: Memory Fragment recovered. Fragmented image data - insufficient for reconstruction.]
Kael pulled his hand back. The alley reeked of copper and stale cabbage. Somewhere above, pipes groaned under the burden of Thornwick's midnight fog.
"You're making a habit of this."
The voice came from the rooftop to his left. Kael didn't flinch. His fingers found the edge of his coat, brushing against the hilt beneath.
"Lyra." She dropped from the ledge, landing without sound in a crouch that spoke of training the streets couldn't provide. The hood of her cloak cast her features in shadow, but he'd learned to read her by now. The set of her shoulders. The way her weight settled on the balls of her feet.

"The Shadow Court moves pieces when it suits them." Kael wiped his palm on his trousers. "What do they want?"
"A job." Lyra's mouth twisted. "A cult cell is operating out of the old Ashwick district. Three confirmed Awakened, maybe more. They have something the Court needs recovered."
"Recovered. Not destroyed."
"Artifacts don't burn well."
Kael considered the geometry of it. Three Awakened against one Transcendent. Acceptable odds, assuming no hidden complications. "What's the target?"
"A bone artifact. Pre-Concordat, maybe older. The cult acquired it from a tomb raider two days ago."
Bone artifacts predating the Concordat were rare. Most had been destroyed in the Abyssal Wars or sealed away in Order vaults. The ones that remained held power that made modern abilities look like parlor tricks.
"When do we move?"
"Already moved." Lyra reached into her cloak and produced a rolled parchment. "I hit the secondary location while you were... Working. The cult's decoy site. They've already consolidated at the primary tomb."
The parchment contained a map of the Ashwick catacombs—sprawling beneath the old foundry district, a maze of abandoned tunnels and forgotten chambers. Kael traced the marked passages with his thumb.
"How did they get past the tomb's defenses?"
"The same way anyone does." Lyra's voice hardened. "Blood. The cult doesn't negotiate with the dead."
The tomb's original owner didn't matter. What mattered was the artifact and whatever secrets the cult intended to exploit. Kael tucked the map into his coat.
"The Shadow Court is watching me."
"They're watching everyone." Lyra tilted her head, studying him. "Why? What did you do?"
"Nothing yet." Kael started walking. "That's the problem."
The Ashwick district sprawled across Thornwick's eastern quarter like a wound that refused to heal. Steam vents pumped white plumes into the fog. Foundries ran day and night, their fires painting the mist in shades of rust and amber. The streets here never saw direct sunlight.
Kael moved through alleys that stank of slag and desperation. Lyra kept pace three blocks behind, her shadows-trained instincts perfect for following without being followed. They'd worked together enough times now that the rhythm felt almost natural.
The catacombs' entrance lay behind a condemned tannery. Kael slipped through a gap in the corrugated wall, dropping into darkness that swallowed the fog whole. His feet found worn stone steps descending at a forty-degree angle. The air grew colder with each level.
He paused at the bottom, letting his newly acquired Spirit Sight unfold. The world shifted into overlapping layers—physical reality overlaid with the shimmer of spiritual residue. The cult had passed this way recently. Their trail blazed like chemical fire, sickly green against the blue-white background hum of dormant grave energy.
Forty feet ahead. Multiple signatures, clustered in what the map marked as the inner sanctum.
Kael drew his blade. The steel made no sound leaving the scabbard—he'd filed the edges of the channel to prevent scraping. He moved forward, each footstep placed with deliberate precision.
The tunnel opened into a vaulted chamber. Braziers burned with black flame along the walls. Runes traced patterns across the ceiling that made Kael's eyes water if he looked too long. The cult had been busy.
Seven robed figures surrounded a central altar. Candles guttered in holders shaped like screaming faces. At the altar's base, a pit had been dug into the chamber floor—fresh earth piled beside it.
The lead cultist stood with his back to Kael, arms raised in supplication. His voice rose in that terrible chorus of syllables that existed outside any human language.
"The seal fractures! The dreamer stirs! We offer the key to the lock!"
Kael counted the threats. Seven Awakened, but the ritual was distracting them. He could take three before the fourth realized what was happening. Maybe four if he pushed the Silent Kill technique he'd been cultivating.
The pit drew his attention. Something lay at its bottom—a shape wrapped in oilcloth, roughly the size of a human torso. The bone artifact, presumably. He needed to get past the cultists without triggering the ritual's defensive mechanisms.
He analyzed the room's geometry. The cultists stood in a crescent facing the altar, leaving the approach from the northwest clear. Shadows pooled thickest there, deepest where the braziers' light couldn't reach.
Kael sheathed his blade. Silence would serve better than steel here.
He slipped forward, his breathing slowing to match the rhythm of the chant. The lead cultist's voice swelled, reaching a crescendo that vibrated through the stone floor. Spiritual energy gathered around the altar like a storm forming over dark water.
Twenty feet from the nearest cultist. Fifteen.
The oilcloth bundle pulsed with light from within.
Kael froze.
The lead cultist turned, his hood falling back to reveal a face mapped with ritual scars. His eyes—milky white, stripped of natural color—fixed on the spot where Kael stood concealed in darkness.
"Another vulture circles the corpse." The cultist's voice carried no surprise. "The Soul Reaver. We felt your touch in the memorial district. The echoes of your feeding reached us."
Six cultists broke from the ritual circle, surrounding the altar with weapons drawn. Kael recognized the formation—they'd drilled for intrusion response. His cover was blown, but that didn't mean the fight was lost.
"You're a chatty bunch." Kael stepped into the light. "Most cultists prefer to just stab first."
"The dead have waited centuries for this moment." The lead cultist descended from the altar, his movements too fluid for a body that aged. "Your harvesting has weakened the seals throughout the city. Our scholars traced the pattern of deterioration. Every corpse you empty drains energy that would have reinforced the barriers."
Kael filed that information away. The cult understood Soul Reaver mechanics better than most Orders. Dangerous knowledge, in the wrong hands.
"The artifact belongs to the dreamer." The cultist extended his hand toward the oilcloth bundle. "You cannot conceive of what rests within that wrapping. Even now, fragments of his consciousness drift through the spaces between worlds, seeking reunion."
Him. The cult referred to a specific entity, not an abstract force.
Kael lunged.
The cultists reacted faster than anticipated. Spiritual techniques discharged in overlapping waves—binding chains of light, explosive bursts of force, a blade of crystallized intent that sang past his ear. Kael twisted through the assault, his body remembering lessons carved into muscle and bone through years of desperate survival.
He took the binding chains in his shoulder, the impact spinning him sideways. Pain flared hot and immediate. He forced his arm to move anyway, slicing through the light with raw spiritual pressure.
A cultist rushed him with a spear-like weapon. Kael sidestepped, letting the thrust pass inches from his chest, and drove his elbow into the attacker's throat. Cartilage crunched. The cultist collapsed, clutching his ruined neck.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Resonance Blade Lv.5 from the remains. Skill acquired.]
The remaining five closed formation around him. The lead cultist watched from the altar steps, his expression one of detached curiosity.
"Your power is impressive for someone who feeds on refuse." He raised one scarred hand. "But the dreamer chose us. His blessing flows through our veins."
Dark light gathered at his palm.
Kael activated his new Resonance Blade. Spiritual energy condensed along the edge of his steel, transforming it into something that hummed with destructive frequency. He swept the blade in a horizontal arc.
The first cultist's defense shattered like glass. The second lost an arm at the elbow. The third caught the full force of the discharge, her body flung backward into the brazier.
Fire spread across the chamber floor. Kael used the chaos, cutting through a cultist who hadn't adjusted for his increased speed. The Resonance Blade carved a groove through spiritual shielding like a knife through wet paper.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Pain Suppression Lv.8 from the remains. Skill acquired.]
[Soul Harvest: You extracted Memory Fragment: Ritual Configuration from the remains. File corrupted.]
Four left. The lead cultist hadn't moved from his position, but the dark light at his palm had grown brighter. Kael felt its pull like hooks embedded in his spirit.
"The tomb contains more than the artifact." The cultist's voice carried now, cutting through the chaos. "The dreamer's vessel rests beside the key. Should you retrieve the key, the vessel will wake."
Kael understood the threat. The cult wasn't trying to stop him—they were giving him exactly what he wanted, with strings attached.
"And if I don't care about waking anything?"
"The vessel holds consciousness that has festered for three hundred years. If released without proper containment, it will consume every living soul in Thornwick. Maybe beyond."
Kael tested the edge of his blade against his thumb. The Resonance Blade hummed, eager for more energy. "You're bluffing."
"The dreamer's vessel was a Soul Reaver. The first of your kind. He learned to harvest abilities without touching corpses—drawing directly from the living, draining victims dry in seconds." The cultist smiled, and the expression transformed his scarred features into something genuinely inhuman. "Imagine what three centuries of unspent hunger could do to a mind."
The pit. The oilcloth wrapping. Kael had assumed the artifact was the target, but something else lay beneath the chamber floor—something the cult wanted him to discover.
The remaining four cultists pressed their attack, forcing Kael to focus on survival. He gave ground, buying time to think. The Resonance Blade flickered against a surge of spiritual force. Pain Suppression kept his shoulder functional despite the binding chains' damage.
Three minutes of continuous combat. The cultists were exhausting their reserves faster than he was, but they had numbers. Eventually, exhaustion would cost him reaction speed.
Kael made his choice. He hurled his blade at the lead cultist—a desperate throw that forced the man to deflect fairly than maintain his technique. The opening lasted exactly long enough for Kael to sprint across the chamber and vault into the pit.
He landed hard on oilcloth. His hands found the bone artifact beneath—cold, impossibly smooth, carved with symbols that burned against his fingers the moment he touched them.
[Soul Harvest: Attempting extraction. Target incompatible. Extraction failed.]
The artifact rejected him. That had never happened before.
Above, the lead cultist's laughter echoed down into the pit. "The key unlocks the vessel, Soul Reaver. It was never meant for your kind."
Kael shoved the artifact into his coat and climbed out of the pit. The four remaining cultists had regrouped near the altar, weapons ready. The lead cultist stood among them, his dark light extinguished.
"Take him alive. The dreamer will decide what to do with his corpse."

[Soul Harvest: You extracted Blood Flow Control Lv.4 from the remains. Skill acquired.]

Silent Kill. The ability to approach undetected, to strike abruptly, to end a life so quietly that witnesses questioned whether death had occurred at all.
The remaining cultists hesitated. They'd never seen a Soul Reaver work in person.

Another cultist tried to retreat toward the tunnel. Silent Kill carried Kael across the distance in three heartbeats. The strike entered below the ribs, angled upward, pierced the heart from behind.
Two left. The lead cultist and a single guardian.
Kael stepped into view, blood drying on his coat, the Silent Kill technique humming beneath his skin. The guardian charged. Kael sidestepped, letting momentum carry the man past, then broke his neck with a precise twist.
The lead cultist hadn't moved. His scarred face displayed no fear, no desperation. Only certainty.
"The vessel stirs." He gestured toward the pit. "Your extraction awakened something. The dreamer knows you're here now."

The cultist's body began to glow from within. Spiritual energy poured from his eyes, his mouth, the wounds Kael hadn't inflicted. His skin cracked like dried earth, revealing light beneath.

A sound. A rhythm. Like a heartbeat, but slower. Like breathing, but deeper.
The vessel wakes.

**You carry my key. You will bring it to me.**

Dawn broke fully, painting Thornwick's rooftops in shades of amber and rust. Somewhere in the city, the cult was regrouping. Somewhere else, the Shadow Court was waiting for confirmation.
And in the pit beneath Ashwick, something ancient stirred, impatient after three centuries of darkness.
Kael touched the artifact in his coat. It pulsed in response, a rhythm that matched the heartbeat he'd heard below.
The First Soul Reaver. The origin point of everything he could do.
Time to learn what that truly meant.
