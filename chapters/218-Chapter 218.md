The rival Soul Reaver stepped from the shadows like smoke given form.
Kael's hand froze halfway to his blade. The man's silhouette was wrong—too fluid, too practiced. He moved the way corpses didn't, the way only predators did.
"You're the one they call Ashborn." The stranger's voice scraped like bone on stone. "The ghost who walks."
Twelve feet separated them in the narrow alley. Rain hammered the cobblestones. Somewhere distant, a steam-whistle shrieked, and the gas-lamps flickered against Thornwick's perpetual smog.
"Don't know you," Kael said. His fingers found the grip of his sword. "Should I?"
The man smiled. It didn't reach his eyes—eyes that held the same hollow depth Kael saw in mirrors. The same emptiness that came from drinking souls like wine.
"We have something in common, you and I." The stranger tilted his head at an unnatural angle. "We take what others leave behind."
A chill crawled up Kael's spine. Not fear—recognition. This man harvested the dead. This man walked the same forbidden path.
"Identify yourself." Kael's blade cleared its sheath an inch. "Now."
"They call me Vex." The man spread his hands, palms upward in mock surrender. "I represent interests that find your... Talents... Fascinating."
Kael's blood boiled. Interests. Like he was livestock being appraised. The gas-lamp above them guttered, throwing Vex's gaunt features into stark relief. Thin scars crisscrossed his exposed forearms—old wounds that had healed wrong, spiritual burns that never fully faded.
"I don't work for interests." Kael stepped forward, closing the distance by half. "I don't work for anyone."
"Everyone works for someone." Vex's smile sharpened. "The question is whether you work for someone who protects you, or someone who hunts you."
Movement. Behind Kael, to his left. He spun, blade singing free of steel—and froze.
A woman stood at the alley's mouth. She wore the grey coat of the Order of Dawn, its sunburst insignia dulled to avoid recognition. Her hand rested on a pistol that crackled with bound lightning.
"The Shadow Court sends its regards, Ashborn," she said. "We're here to make you an offer."
Kael's mind raced. Shadow Court. The hidden hand that moved Order pawns. They knew about him. They wanted something.
"What kind of offer?" he demanded.
Vex laughed—a dry, rattling sound. "The kind you can't refuse. Not because we're powerful, but because we're honest." He gestured to the woman. "Sister Mira coordinates our... Recruitment. Tell him what you've told others."
Mira stepped closer, rain streaming down her angular face. "Three nights ago, you harvested abilities from cultists in the Ashwick district. Two days before that, you took something from a Mortician's Guild enforcer outside the Veiled Market." Her eyes glittered. "You think you're invisible. You're not. We know everything."
Kael's grip tightened on his blade. They had been watching. For how long? How much did they know about his abilities?
"The cultists you killed," Mira continued, "were part of a cell preparing to breach Rift Alpha-7. The enforcer you harvested was carrying soul-essence bound for an Order of Dawn safehouse—one that doesn't officially exist." She paused. "You keep stumbling into our operations, Ashborn. Accidents make us curious."
"I'm not stumbling. I'm hunting."
"Then hunt with us." Vex spread his arms wide. "The cult isn't random. It's organized. Six cells operating in Thornwick alone, all working toward the same goal. The seals are failing. The great rift is waking. And you—" his eyes gleamed with something like hunger, "—you're one of the only people who can stop them."
Kael laughed bitterly. "Stop them how? By harvesting corpses?"
"By becoming something more." Vex stepped forward, and for the first time, Kael saw genuine emotion cross his features. Not cruelty—conviction. "I was Awakened seventeen years ago. I joined the Iron Vanguard. I believed in the cause." His voice hardened. "Then I saw what the Orders really do. The lies. The corruption. The way they let people die to maintain their precious balance."
He rolled up his sleeve, revealing a brandscar over his elbow—the symbol of a deserter, burned into the flesh of those who broke their Order oaths.
"I found another way." Vex touched his own chest. "The Soul Reaver path. It cost me everything—my rank, my reputation, my family. But it gave me truth." His gaze locked with Kael's. "I know what you can do. I know what it means. And I know that together, we could expose what the Orders have hidden for centuries."
Mira added, "The Six Orders teach that the Abyss must be contained. They say the seals protect humanity. But the seals aren't just keeping entities out—they're keeping something in. Something the first Awakened created and couldn't destroy."
Kael's pulse thundered in his ears. The first Awakened. The origin of Soul Reaver. The truth hidden beneath layers of propaganda and fear.
"What proof?" he demanded. "Why should I believe anything you say?"
Vex reached into his coat and withdrew a small object—a stone tablet, no larger than his palm. Ancient script covered its surface, symbols that seemed to writhe in the flickering gaslight.
"The Sepulcher Tablets," Vex said. "Recorded by the first Soul Reaver. They contain the True Name of the entity bound beneath Thornwick. The name that could unmake its seal—or strengthen it forever." He held it out. "This is what the cult wants. This is what the Orders kill to protect. And this is what you're going to help us find."
Kael stared at the tablet. He could feel it—, through the rain and the distance, he could feel the power radiating from those carved symbols. It called to him like a half-remembered song.
"If I help you," he said slowly, "what happens to the souls I harvest? The abilities I've taken?"
"They're yours." Mira's voice softened. "We don't want to control you, Ashborn. We want to work with you. The Shadow Court isn't an Order. We don't have ranks or oaths. We have purpose." She reached into her coat and withdrew a small glass vial filled with swirling silver light. "One hundred gold sovereigns' worth of soul-essence. Payment for your first task."
Kael's eyes widened. That much essence could fuel an Ascendant for months. Could it—
"Take it." Vex gestured. "We're not asking you to trust us blindly. We're asking you to see what we see. The cult is moving faster than anyone anticipated. Two of our operatives disappeared last night near the old Sepulcher ruins. We think they've found something down there—something that could accelerate the rift's decay by decades."
The rain intensified, drumming against the rooftops like impatient fingers. Kael looked from the tablet to the essence to the two strangers who claimed to offer truth.
"Tell me about the Sepulcher," he said. "Everything you know."
Mira and Vex exchanged a glance. Then Vex nodded.
"The Sepulcher is the oldest structure in Thornwick. Built before the Orders existed, before the first seal was forged. The cult believes it contains the original Soul Reaver's tomb—and within that tomb, the tools needed to break all seventy-two seals at once." His jaw tightened. "The Orders believe the same thing. That's why they've kept the Sepulcher sealed for three hundred years. Not to protect it from the cult—to protect it from themselves."
Kael felt the weight of those words settle into his bones. Seventy-two seals. Seventy-two entities. If those seals broke simultaneously—
"The great rift," he said. "Rift Alpha-7. It's connected to the others, isn't it? When one weakens—"
"They all feel it." Mira finished. "Like cracks in a dam. The cult understands this. They're not trying to breach individual rifts. They're trying to collapse the entire network at once."
"Why?" Kael's voice came out harder than he intended. "What do they gain from ending the world?"
Vex laughed bitterly. "They don't think they're ending it. They think they're freeing it. The entities—the Abyssal beings—they're not demons. They're the original inhabitants of this reality. The cult believes that humanity stole their world, and they want it back." His eyes grew distant. "The Whisper King—their leader—he's convinced that releasing the entities will create a new balance. A world where the strong rule and the weak serve. He's been preparing for this for over a century."
The Whisper King. Kael filed the name away. Another thread in the conspiracy's web.
"The Shadow Court opposes this," he said. "Why? What do you want?"
Mira stepped closer, her grey coat whispering against her legs. "We want to choose our own future. The Orders control everything—the flow of essence, the training of Awakened, the information people receive. They've kept humanity in fear for so long that we don't know how to live without it." Her voice dropped. "We're not saints, Ashborn. Some of us have done terrible things in service of our goals. But we believe that humanity deserves to know the truth. About the Abyss. About the entities. About what really happened during the Abyssal Wars."
"And about Soul Reavers," Vex added. "About why we exist. About what happened to the first one." He touched the brandscar on his arm. "The Orders say Soul Reavers are abominations. Corruptions that feed on death. But I've walked this path for fifteen years, and I've never felt corrupted. I've felt hungry. I've felt hollow. But I've never felt evil."
Kael understood that feeling. The gnawing emptiness that came from taking what wasn't his. The knowledge that every corpse he touched brought him closer to something he couldn't name.
"The cult killed my allies," he said. "People I cared about. If I work with you, I want information. Names. Locations. Everything you have on the cells operating in Thornwick."
Mira nodded. "You'll have it. But first, you need to see the Sepulcher for yourself. Vex has been there twice. He knows the entry points, the traps, the guardian spirits that still patrol its halls."
Vex stepped forward, extending his hand. "So what do you say, Ashborn? Want to see what's really buried beneath Thornwick?"
Kael looked at the offered hand. At the man who claimed to walk the same path. At the vial of essence that represented freedom from the economy's constraints.
Then he reached out and grasped it.
The moment their skin touched, something happened.
[Soul Harvest: Mutual resonance detected between two Reavers.]
Vex's eyes widened. He felt it too—that strange connection, like two broken mirrors reflecting the same void.
"What—" Mira took a step back, her hand moving to her pistol.
"It's okay." Vex's voice shook. "I've never... I've never felt another one before."
Kael's grip tightened. "Neither have I."
They stood there, hands clasped, rain pouring around them. Two Reavers in an alley. Two souls that had learned to drink from the dead. Two men who had lost everything and found something in the darkness.
Mira watched them with wary fascination. "This changes things. If you can work together—if you can share knowledge—"
"Then we become something new." Vex released Kael's hand, his expression shifting to something almost reverent. "The Orders hunt us individually. The cult fears us individually. But together?" His smile returned, sharper than before. "Together, we become a threat they can't ignore."
Kael flexed his fingers, feeling the residual warmth of that strange connection fade. He had come to Thornwick looking for answers. He had found something more—a mirror. A reflection of the path he might take.
"Show me the Sepulcher," he said. "Show me what they're hiding."
Vex and Mira exchanged another glance. Then they turned and walked deeper into the alley, toward a door half-hidden by shadows.
Kael followed.
The door opened onto a staircase descending into darkness. No gas-lamps. No torches. Only the faint, pulsing glow of something below—something that called to the hollow place in his chest.
"Stay close," Vex warned. "The guardians know their territory. They'll attack on sight."
Kael drew his sword and stepped into the black.
The stairs spiraled downward for what felt like hours. The air grew colder with each step, thick with the smell of ancient stone and older decay. Kael's Transcendent senses expanded, probing the darkness for threats.
He sensed them before he saw them.
Faint presences clustered in the corners of his awareness—residual spirits, echoes of the dead that had never fully dissipated. They had been guardians once, bound to the Sepulcher by rituals older than memory. Now they waited, patient and hungry.
"How far down?" he whispered.
"Another hundred feet." Vex's voice echoed strangely. "The entrance to the main chamber is just ahead."
The staircase ended abruptly, opening into a vast underground hall. Kael's breath caught.
The hall stretched beyond the reach of his sight, its ceiling lost in shadow. Pillars rose from the floor like frozen giants, each one carved with symbols that glowed faintly in the darkness. And at the center of the hall, visible even from this distance—
A door. Massive. Made of black iron and older things. Symbols covered its surface, the same writhing script from the tablet Vex had shown him.
"The First Seal," Mira breathed. "The original. Every other seal in the world is a copy of this one."
Kael approached the door slowly, his sword raised. The symbols seemed to pulse as he drew near, responding to his presence. To his Reaver essence.
"The cult wants to open this," he said. "And the Orders want to keep it closed."
"Yes." Vex walked to his left, examining a smaller door set into the wall. "But neither of them understands what this really is. The First Seal doesn't just contain an entity. It contains a choice."
Kael turned to face him. "What choice?"
Vex pressed his palm against the smaller door, and ancient mechanisms groaned to life. The door swung inward, revealing a chamber beyond.
"This is where the first Soul Reaver died." Vex stepped through. "And this is what he left behind."
Kael followed.
The inner chamber was smaller, more intimate. A stone sarcophagus dominated its center, covered in carvings that told a story—the rise of the Awakened, the Abyssal Wars, the creation of the seals. And resting on the sarcophagus's lid, glowing with soft silver light—
A blade. Curved. Elegant. Its edge shimmered with captured starlight.
"The First Reaver's weapon," Vex said. "The Soul Reaver. This is where it came from. This is where your power was born."
Kael stared at the blade. It called to him more strongly than anything he had ever felt. More strongly than the essence vials. More strongly than the tablets. The blade wanted to be held. To be used. To be claimed.
"It's a key," Mira said from the doorway. "The first Reaver created it to unlock the First Seal. But he died before he could use it."
"So the weapon has been waiting," Kael said slowly. "For someone to finish what he started."
"For someone to choose." Vex's voice was soft. "The blade can break the seal or strengthen it. It all depends on the will of the one who wields it."
Kael reached for the blade.
The moment his fingers touched the hilt, light exploded through the chamber. Not gentle light—blinding, searing, the light of a sun being born. Kael gasped as knowledge flooded his mind.
[Soul Harvest: Weapon resonance detected. First Reaver's legacy acquired.]
[Soul Reaver Ability Unlocked: Soul Echo — preserve a fragment of harvested ability for independent use.]
[Soul Reaver Ability Upgraded: Soul Consumption — harvest yields 50% more essence.]
The light faded. Kael stood in the darkness, the blade in his hand, power singing through his veins.
"Seventy-two rifts," he whispered. "Seventy-two entities. And one choice."
Vex stared at him with something like awe. "You saw it. The memory. The truth."
Kael nodded. The first Reaver had been a woman. A warrior who had fought the Abyss and lost everything. She had created the Soul Reaver ability not to consume the dead, but to preserve them—to capture their final moments before the entities consumed them forever.
"She wanted to save them," he said. "Every soul the Abyss took. She wanted to give them a second chance."
"And did she?" Mira asked.
Kael lowered the blade. "She died trying. The weight of so many souls... It broke her. But she left this behind. For whoever came next."
Vex stepped forward, his expression shifting to something Kael couldn't read. "The Orders will hunt you now. The moment they sense what you've claimed—"
"Let them." Kael's grip tightened on the blade. "I've been hunted before. This time, I'm hunting back."
The three of them stood in the darkness, surrounded by ancient secrets and older powers. Above them, Thornwick continued its endless struggle—cultists plotting, Orders scheming, citizens living and dying in ignorance of the truth.
Below them, the First Seal pulsed with quiet menace.
Kael sheathed his new blade and turned to face his companions.
"Tell me about the cult cells," he said. "Everything. Starting with the one that's closest to breaching the Sepulcher."
Mira pulled a folded paper from her coat. "The Ashwick cell. Twelve confirmed members, including a stage-three Awakened called Korven. They're building something in the old factory district—a ritual circle large enough to breach the First Seal directly."
"Then that's where we start." Kael looked at Vex. "You know the Sepulcher. I know how to harvest. And now—" he touched the blade at his hip, "—I know what we're fighting for."
Vex smiled. It was the first genuine smile Kael had seen from him.
"Then let's stop talking and start killing."
They ascended from the Sepulcher in silence, each lost in their own thoughts. When they emerged into the rain-soaked alley, the gas-lamps of Thornwick seemed garish after the underground darkness.
Mira paused at the alley's mouth. "I'll report to my handlers. We'll have supplies waiting at the Ashwick junction by midnight." She looked at Kael. "Try not to kill anyone important before then."
"I'll try," Kael said. "No promises."
Mira vanished into the crowd, her grey coat disappearing among the thousands of pedestrians flooding Thornwick's streets.
Vex lingered. "You should know something." His voice dropped low. "The Shadow Court isn't monolithic. There are factions—some who want to use the Reaver powers for themselves. Mira is trustworthy, but others in the organization might try to eliminate you if they see you as a threat."
Kael nodded slowly. "So I'm working with potential enemies as well as potential allies."
"Welcome to the real world." Vex clapped him on the shoulder. "I'll meet you at the Ashwick junction at midnight. Don't be late."
He walked away, disappearing into the evening crowd.
Kael stood alone in the rain, the Soul Reaver blade humming softly at his hip. He had come to Thornwick looking for answers. He had found a conspiracy, a rival Reaver, and a weapon that could reshape reality.
And somewhere in the city, cultists were preparing to break the seal that held back the Abyss.
He needed to move. Now.
Kael turned toward the Ashwick district, his new blade singing a quiet song of anticipation. The hunt was just beginning.
Behind him, unnoticed, a figure emerged from a shadowed doorway. Grey robes. A mask of beaten copper. Eyes that glowed with contained starlight.
The figure watched Kael's retreating back. Then it lifted a small device to its lips and whispered words in a language older than the Abyss itself.
"He's awakened the blade. Inform the Council. The Reaver walks."
The figure melted back into the darkness, leaving only rain and the distant rumble of steam-engines.
In the Sepulcher far below, the First Seal shuddered once—and began, ever so slowly, to crack.
