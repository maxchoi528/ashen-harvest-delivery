The corpse twitched.
Kael Ashborn froze mid-step, his boot hovering above the rain-slicked cobblestones. Three heartbeats passed. The body's fingers curled inward, scraping against the wet stone with a sound like fingernails on a chalkboard. Then stillness again.
"Still warm," he muttered, crouching beside the fallen cultist. The man's robes bore the sigil of a cracked seal—barely visible beneath the mud and blood. A slit throat told the story. Someone had gotten to this acolyte before Kael arrived at the warehouse district's edge.
He pressed two fingers to the cooling flesh. The Soul Reaver sensation bloomed immediately—a cold thread connecting his palm to something deeper, something that still flickered with the dregs of a fading light. This man had been Awakened. Low stage, but Awakened nonetheless. His death couldn't have been more than twenty minutes prior.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION] Soul Harvest available. Corpse freshness: 87%. Estimated yield: Minor Abyssal Resistance (degraded). Proceed?
Through the warehouse windows across the narrow alley, shadows moved in patterns that defied the flickering gaslight. Kael counted six—no, seven figures. They carried themselves with the rigid discipline of soldiers, not the frenzied devotion of rank-and-file cultists.
The Six Orders had agents in Thornwick's Underworld. That wasn't news. What made Kael's stomach clench was the organization. They weren't hunting the cultists. They were herding them.
Kael withdrew his hand and stepped back into the shadows between two rusted water barrels. His mind raced through possibilities. The Order of Dawn specialized in seal maintenance. The Iron Vanguard handled military threats. The Arcane Spire gathered intelligence. Whoever commanded this operation possessed resources and coordination that suggested something larger than a routine cult suppression.
A scream shattered the night—short, guttural, cut brutally short. One of the cultists had found a hiding spot. The scream ended with a wet cough, followed by silence.
Then the chanting started.
Kael recognized the cadence. The same verses he'd heard three weeks ago when a dozen cultists broke through a secondary seal in the Ashwick district. The ritual required five participants minimum, drawing power from synchronized belief to crack the Veil's fabric. That breach had cost two Order teams and a Mortician's extraction squad to close.
The chanting grew louder. More voices joined.
Kael pulled a thin vial from his coat—a tracking compound mixed with sulfur and powdered riftslate. He'd used it twice before to follow cult movements without alerting their warding posts. The vial's contents gleamed faintly as he uncorked it, releasing a sharp metallic scent that made his eyes water.
He needed a closer position. The warehouse rooftops offered the best vantage, but reaching them meant crossing twenty feet of open ground. The cultists' ritual zone appeared to extend six meters from the alley's mouth, marked by a faint discoloration in the rain's pattern where spiritual energy pooled like oil on water.
Movement at the warehouse's side door. A figure emerged carrying something heavy—a body, judging by the way arms hung limp. The figure the corpse toward the chanting group, leaving a dark trail across the stones.
Another cultist. Dead within the hour.
Kael's jaw tightened. Two corpses, both still harvestable. But extracting either would expose him to the Order agents watching from the shadows. His reputation as a Soul Reaver had spread through Thornwick's underground networks. The Orders would execute him on sight if they caught him harvesting a fresh kill—especially if that kill belonged to one of their own operations.
The chanting peaked. A pulse of energy rippled outward, making Kael's teeth ache. The ritual was reaching its apex.
From his position behind the water barrels, he could see the ritual's center—a cleared space where three cultists knelt around a shallow depression in the ground. Symbols had been carved into the stone, glowing with sickly green light that pulsed in time with their voices. At the depression's center stood a fourth figure, taller than the others, draped in robes that seemed to drink the gaslight than reflect it.
The cult leader raised both hands. The chanting ceased.
"The seal weakens," the figure announced. Its voice carried an unnatural resonance, as if multiple throats spoke in harmony. "The Seventy-Second stirs in its prison. Soon, the membrane will tear."
Seventy-Second. Kael's blood ran cold. The Great Rift housed an Entity designated in the high seventies—one of the most dangerous categories. The seal network rated Entities by threat level. Anything above fifty required constant monitoring by a full Order chapter. Anything above seventy meant apocalyptic potential if the containment failed.
The cult leader descended into the depression and pressed both palms flat against the carved stone. The green light intensified, crawling up the figure's arms like luminescent moss. A sound emerged—not a scream, not a song. The noise scraped against Kael's consciousness, making his vision blur at the edges.
Seal corruption. The cult was directly corrupting the local barrier than attempting to create a new rift. That meant they were working toward a specific Entity, not just any Abyssal incursion.
The Order agents moved.
Six figures materialized from the warehouse shadows, their movements perfectly synchronized. They wore the grey mantles of the Order of Dawn—keepers of the seal network, protectors of the Veil's integrity. Each carried a sanctified blade, weapons designed specifically to disrupt Abyssal energy.
The cultists scattered, but not fast enough. Two went down before they could raise their wards, cut down by blade strikes that left wounds burning with holy fire. The cult leader didn't move from the depression, even as Dawn agents surrounded the ritual space.
"Abomination," one of the agents spat. "You're polluting a primary containment node."
The leader looked up. Kael caught a glimpse of the face beneath the hood—young, almost boyish, with eyes that reflected no light at all. Those empty eyes stared directly at the water barrels.
Directly at Kael.
"You brought the Soul Reaver," the leader said, addressing the Dawn agents. "How thoughtful. He'll make an acceptable substitute."
The agents hesitated. Kael saw the moment understanding dawned on their leader—a woman with iron-grey hair and a scar that split her left eyebrow. She spun toward the water barrels, raising her sanctified blade.
"Show yourself, Reaver."
No point hiding. Kael stepped out from behind the barrels, hands empty at his sides. Seven against one. The Dawn agents outnumbered him, and they carried weapons designed to kill Awakened. But the cult leader's words rang in his ears.
Acceptable substitute.
The Dawn commander's expression shifted from suspicion to cold calculation. "Kael Ashborn. The Council has questions about your activities."
"I'm sure they do."
"You're seen harvesting the fallen. Cultists, criminals, even Order casualties." She stepped closer, blade held in a guard position that suggested she was prepared to use it. "By the Concordat's terms, I could execute you now."
"You could try."
Her eyes narrowed. "That body behind you. How recently deceased?"
Kael didn't turn. "Twenty minutes. Maybe twenty-five."
"Did you harvest from it?"
"No."
"Then you have approximately thirty seconds before its spiritual residue degrades past usefulness." She sheathed her blade with deliberate slowness. "Harvest it. Take whatever abilities it contains. Consider it payment for information."
The cult leader laughed—a sound like glass grinding against stone. "How noble. The Order of Dawn negotiating with a Reaver. The Council would be fascinated."
"The Council isn't here," the commander replied. "I am. And I have questions that require answers."
Kael glanced at the corpse near the ritual space. The cult leader had dragged a second body—fresh, still bleeding, untouched. Both corpses would degrade rapidly in the next few minutes. Once spiritual residue dispersed beyond harvestable thresholds, the abilities encoded in those souls would be lost forever.
But walking into that space meant trusting the Dawn agents to honor some form of temporary truce. The Concordat protected Order members from prosecution during sanctioned operations, but Reavers existed outside those protections. Anything he revealed could be used against him in a formal tribunal.
"You want information," Kael said. "I want those bodies."
"Granted."
"How do I know—"
"You don't." The commander's voice carried no deception. "But I need to know what the Seventy-Second Entity's cult is planning in Thornwick's Underworld. You have access to networks I don't. And you have approximately twenty seconds left to decide."
The green light pulsed again. The cult leader's hands remained pressed against the stone, feeding corruption into the seal network. More agents moved to intercept cultists fleeing into the warehouse district's maze of alleys. The entire operation was in flux—a window opening and closing with every passing second.
Kael ran.
He sprinted across the open ground, rain stinging his face, boots splashing through puddles that reflected the sickly green glow. The Dawn agents parted to let him through, their expressions ranging from disgust to grudging respect. He reached the first corpse—the cultist he'd discovered—and dropped to his knees.
His hands found the cooling flesh. The Soul Reaver sensation exploded through his nervous system, cold becoming heat becoming something beyond human sensation. The corpse's spiritual residue clung to his fingers like frozen honey, resisting extraction before yielding to the harvesting process.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION] Soul Harvest: You extracted Minor Abyssal Resistance (degraded) from the remains. This ability grants limited protection against direct Abyssal corruption. Quality: Low. Source: Cult Initiate, Third-Stage Awakened.
Kael absorbed the notification, filing it alongside the growing catalog of abilities he'd harvested over the past months. Minor Abyssal Resistance wouldn't stop a direct Entity assault, but it might buy him seconds against environmental corruption. Seconds could mean survival.
He lunged for the second corpse. The cult leader watched without intervention, empty eyes tracking his movements with mechanical precision. The Dawn commander raised her hand, signaling her agents to hold position.
Kael's palms pressed against the fresh kill. This body still radiated warmth—the heart had stopped within minutes, not hours. The spiritual residue here burned brighter, more concentrated.
[SYSTEM NOTIFICATION] Soul Harvest: You extracted Rite of Seal Weakening (functional) from the remains. This ability allows temporary corruption of nearby containment barriers. Warning: Usage triggers Order detection protocols. Quality: Moderate. Source: Cult Acolyte, Fifth-Stage Awakened.
The ability slotted into Kael's growing arsenal like a missing key finding its lock. He could feel the knowledge encoded within—the specific phrases, the hand positions, the precise moment when spiritual energy had to be released to damage seal structures. This cultist had practiced the technique dozens of times, perfecting the corruption until it became instinct.
"Interesting," the Dawn commander said, approaching with measured steps. "You're harvesting functional abilities, not just raw essence. That's advanced Reaver technique."
"I learn quickly."
"So I've heard." She stopped three meters away, close enough for conversation, far enough to react if he attacked. "Now. The Seventy-Second. What do you know?"
"Less than you, probably. I've seen cult activity increasing over the past month. Seal corruptions in three districts. Casualties among both Order members and civilians." Kael stood, wiping his hands on his coat. "But the organization surprises me. This isn't random fanatics playing with powers they don't understand. Someone is coordinating them."
"The Shadow Court."
The name landed like a physical blow. Kael kept his expression neutral, but his mind raced through implications. The Shadow Court operated as the Orders' deniable operations arm—assassinations, sabotage, intelligence gathering that couldn't be officially sanctioned. If the Shadow Court supported the Seventy-Second's cult, then this wasn't just a fringe group threatening local stability.
This was a coordinated campaign against the seal network itself.
"You're surprised," the commander observed.
"I'm recalculating my survival odds."
She almost smiled. "Smart. The Shadow Court has been monitoring your activities. They consider you an asset of interest."
"An asset." The word tasted bitter. "That's a generous interpretation of a man who harvests the dead."
"Their resources aren't limited by spiritual conservation. Neither are yours." The commander turned to watch the remaining cultists being rounded up by her team. "The Shadow Court believes Reavers could tip the balance in the coming conflict. If the seal network fails—if even one Entity breaks through—traditional power structures become meaningless. Dead Awakened can't defend anything."
The cult leader remained in the depression, hands still pressed to the corrupted stone. The green glow pulsed brighter now, spreading outward in fractal patterns that made Kael's eyes water. Something was building beneath the surface—a pressure that had nothing to do with the ritual and everything to do with the seal network's deterioration.
"You're letting that continue," Kael said.
"For another two minutes. The corruption will trigger automated responses from the primary seal nodes. When it does, this entire section of the Underworld will experience a feedback pulse." The commander's tone remained clinical. "Anyone with spiritual sensitivity will feel it. Including the other cult cells operating in Thornwick."
"You're using this as bait."
"I'm observing how they respond to pressure." She met his gaze directly. "A coordinated response confirms Shadow Court involvement. Scattered, panicked reactions suggest independent factions."
The green light peaked. A sound like a vast membrane tearing echoed through the alley—not physical, but spiritual, a vibration that resonated in Kael's bones. The corruption hit the threshold, triggering the seal network's defense protocols.
Reality hiccupped.
For one impossible moment, Kael saw through the Veil. The Abyssal side of the barrier materialized in his perception—a swirling chaos of raw spiritual energy, punctuated by vast shapes that defied geometric logic. At the chaos's heart, something ancient turned its attention toward the breach.
Then the seal network responded. White-hot light erupted from every surface, flooding the area with purified energy that forced the corruption to retreat. The cult leader screamed—the harmonized voice fracturing into pure anguish. Dawn agents surged forward with sanctified blades raised.
Kael felt the feedback pulse hit his consciousness like a hammer strike. His vision whited out, his balance failed, and he found himself on his knees with copper flooding his mouth. When his sight cleared, the cult leader had vanished—torn apart by the seal's response or escaped through some other means, Kael couldn't determine.
The Dawn commander approached, her expression unreadable. "You felt that."
"Felt what?"
"The Seventy-Second. For that instant when the Veil thinned, you perceived it." She crouched beside him, studying his face with clinical intensity. "Most Awakened never develop that sensitivity until Transcendent stage. You felt an Entity's attention while remaining Ascendant."
"Congratulations. Should I expect a reward?"
"You should expect visitors." She stood, gesturing to her agents to secure the perimeter. "The Shadow Court will send someone. They always do when potential assets demonstrate unexpected capabilities."
Kael wiped blood from his lips. "And if I refuse their offer?"
"Then you'll continue operating as you have been. Eventually, you'll encounter something beyond your current abilities, and you'll die." The commander's voice carried no judgment. "The Shadow Court isn't your enemy, Ashborn. They're a tool. Whether you use them or they use you depends entirely on your willingness to play their game."
"Games require rules."
"The only rule that matters: survive. Everything else is negotiable." She turned to survey the aftermath—bodies being searched, evidence being cataloged, the ritual space being systematically dismantled. "The cult's activity tonight confirms coordination. Multiple cells, responding to a central authority. The Shadow Court will want to know how you fit into their plans."
"Fit how?"
"They were tracking you before tonight. Someone in their hierarchy believes you're connected to the original Soul Reaver." She walked away before he could respond, leaving him kneeling in the rain with her words echoing through his skull.
The original Soul Reaver.
Kael had spent months chasing that ghost—the first human to develop harvesting abilities, the one who supposedly created the techniques that made soul extraction possible. Every lead had turned to ash. Every whispered rumor of tombs or artifacts had proven false. And now an Order commander casually mentioned that the Shadow Court believed he was connected to that figure.
The rain intensified, washing away the ritual's residual corruption and carrying it into Thornwick's drainage systems. Dawn agents completed their sweep, loading evidence into sealed containers marked with containment sigils. The body Kael had harvested—the acolyte who'd possessed the seal-weakening technique—had been taken by the Order's recovery teams, denying him a second extraction.
Strategic. The commander had given him access to one corpse while securing the other. A calculated exchange that left him with useful abilities but hungry for more.
Kael stood, joints protesting the cold that had seeped into his bones. The warehouse district stretched before him—a maze of crumbling infrastructure and abandoned industry that served as neutral ground for Thornwick's criminal elements. Somewhere in that maze, cult cells regrouped after tonight's failure. Somewhere, the Shadow Court prepared their next move.
And somewhere, the Seventy-Second Entity waited in its prison, feeling the Veil tremble with every corruption attempt.
He needed allies. Resources. Information about the Shadow Court's true intentions.
More importantly, he needed to understand why they believed a dead man from centuries past might be connected to a death-row prisoner who'd awakened in his own grave.
Footsteps approached from the main thoroughfare. Kael tensed, calculating escape routes, but the figure that emerged wore no Order insignia. A woman in her thirties, dressed in the practical clothing of a Veiled Market courier, stopped at the alley's edge.
"Kael Ashborn?"
"Who's asking?"
"My employer sends regards." She extended a small envelope, sealed with black wax bearing an unfamiliar sigil. "Payment for tonight's services. And an invitation."
"I haven't done any services."
"Tonight you did. Your presence distracted the Order agents long enough for the cult leader to complete preliminary corruption. My employer considers that valuable." She dropped the envelope at his feet. "The invitation includes terms. Read them at your leisure."
She turned and walked away before Kael could respond, disappearing into the rain-shrouded streets with the practiced ease of someone who knew every shadow and shortcut.
Kael retrieved the envelope. The wax seal bore no crest—only an abstract pattern that seemed to shift when viewed from different angles. He broke it open.
Inside: a single sheet of heavy parchment, covered in precise handwriting.
*Mr. Ashborn,*
*The Shadow Court acknowledges your value as an independent operator. We propose a mutually beneficial arrangement: information exchange, coordinated action against common threats, and access to resources beyond your current reach.*
*In exchange, we request one thing: investigate the Sepulcher Tablets. Their current location is unknown, but evidence suggests they exist within Thornwick's boundaries. The knowledge they contain could change the balance of power between those who maintain the seals and those who would tear them down.*
*Respond to this message through Veiled Market channels. Mention the name "Dorian" to establish contact.*
*We look forward to your cooperation.*
The letter bore no signature.
Kael read it twice, searching for hidden meanings, traps, anything that might explain the sudden interest in his abilities. The Sepulcher Tablets were legendary—supposedly recorded by the first Soul Reaver, containing knowledge about the soul's true nature and its relationship to the Abyss. Finding them had been one of his distant objectives, a goal he'd never seriously pursued because the Tablets' location remained unknown.
But the letter mentioned Dorian. A name from his past—someone he'd trusted, someone who'd betrayed him. If the Shadow Court knew that connection, they knew far more about him than he'd ever revealed.
The rain continued falling, washing the night's violence from Thornwick's stones. Kael folded the letter and placed it inside his coat, next to the vials of tracking compound and the small knife he kept for close-quarters work.
The Dawn commander had been right about one thing: survive. Everything else was negotiable.
He had two corpses to harvest before their spiritual residue degraded beyond usefulness. He had a new contact method for reaching Shadow Court operatives. He had confirmation that cult activity in Thornwick was organized at a level he'd underestimated.
And he had a mystery: why did an ancient organization believe a dead man from centuries ago was connected to a prisoner who'd woken up in his own coffin?
A sound echoed from the warehouse district's depths—metal striking stone, rhythmic and deliberate. Kael listened, tracking the noise through Thornwick's acoustic peculiarities. Someone was moving through the abandoned buildings, approaching his position.
The footsteps stopped.
In the silence that followed, Kael heard breathing—slow, measured, controlled. The breathing of someone trained to move undetected, who'd only revealed their presence through carelessness or intention.
"You shouldn't have come," a voice said from the darkness. Familiar timbre, carrying an accent Kael recognized from his time in Thornwick's lower courts. "They're watching now. All of them."
The voice belonged to someone Kael had believed dead for months.
"Show yourself," Kael commanded.
Laughter echoed through the rain. "You first, Reaver. Prove you deserve answers."
The darkness held its secrets. The rain continued falling. And somewhere in Thornwick's Underworld, forces beyond Kael's current understanding began to converge on his position—some hostile, some only curious, all of them aware that a new player had entered the endgame.
The game had changed.
Kael adjusted his grip on the envelope and prepared to meet whatever emerged from the shadows.
