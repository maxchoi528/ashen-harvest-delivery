These weren't desperate madmen. This was an operation.

"Prime Zarek is conducting important work," the shorter guard said, his voice tight. "The Order of Dawn has no authority here. We're under the Arcane Spire's protection."
Arcane Spire. Kael filed that away. The Spire officially hunted cultists. Unofficially, half their inner circle were cult sympathizers playing a longer game.

Kael moved.

His fingers brushed her cooling forehead.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 147 units of spiritual energy from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired: Base Layout. Duration: Permanent.]

Kael moved to the next body.
A young man. Barely twenty, by the look of him. Fresh recruit. His soul still flickered, not yet departed.
Kael touched his forehead.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 89 units of spiritual energy from the remains. Memory Fragment acquired: Induction Ceremony. Duration: Permanent.]

They didn't know who he was. They didn't know what he wanted. They only knew he had answers. He had power. He had a plan that stretched back centuries.
Kael withdrew, his jaw tight.
Two words echoed through the recruit's memories like a wound that wouldn't close. Whisper King.

The underground complex sprawled beneath the factory like a tumor. Kael moved through corridors lit by guttering torches, passing chambers filled with cultist paraphernalia—altars draped in black cloth, vats of smoking liquid that reeked of corruption, walls covered in diagrams of the seal network.
The 72 rifts. He recognized them now from Order briefings, from stolen documents, from the fragments he'd harvested over the past months. Every rift was mapped here. Every seal's location marked with obsessive precision.
And nearly every seal was circled in red.
Not the Great Rift. Other rifts. Smaller ones. Rifts that the Orders had marked as stable, as harmless, as ancient history.
Someone had been tracking their deterioration. Someone had noted which seals would fail first.
Kael's boots echoed in the silence. Ahead, light spilled from a doorway—candlelight, warm and inviting in the cold dark.
He approached.
The chamber was circular, maybe fifty feet across. Candles ringed the perimeter, their flames burning with an unnatural blue cast. At the center stood a pillar of black stone, covered in symbols that seemed to writhe when he looked at them directly.
And standing before the pillar, hands raised in ritual posture, was a figure Kael recognized.
Not Zarek. Not a cultist.
A member of the Order of Dawn.
Brother Aldric. Kael had fought beside him at the Ashfield massacre. Had shared meals with him during the siege of Thornwick's outer walls. Had trusted him.
Now Aldric stood in a cultist's lair, conducting a ritual that made Kael's skin crawl.
"I knew you'd come," Aldric said without turning. "The Reaver always follows the trail. It's in your nature."
Kael's hand moved to his sword. "Explain. Now."
"The seals are failing." Aldric lowered his hands. The candles dimmed. "You know this. You've seen it. The Great Rift is only the beginning. Seventy-two rifts. Seventy-two prisons. All of them weakening. All of them approaching collapse."
"The Orders—"
"Are aware." Aldric finally turned. His face was gaunt, shadows pooling beneath his eyes. "They're also powerless to stop it. The seal network was built with a finite lifespan. The founders knew this. The Entities cannot be permanently imprisoned, Kael. Only delayed."
"Then what? Open the doors? Let the Abyss consume everything?"
"Control it." Aldric's voice dropped. "The Whisper King has found a way. Not to destroy the Entities—but to bind them. To harness their power for humanity instead of being consumed by it. The cultists you hunt? They're test subjects. Volunteers showing the world what becomes possible when you stop fighting the tide and learn to surf it."
Kael felt his blood cool. "You're insane."
"I'm informed." Aldric smiled bitterly. "The same thing you are, if you think the Orders' approach will work. How many seals have they reinforced in the last century? How many Entities have they truly imprisoned versus only inconvenienced? The Abyssal Wars ended in stalemate. Stalemates don't last forever."
A sound echoed from the corridor behind Kael. Footsteps. Many of them.
Aldric's smile widened. "You've kept them busy long enough. My associates appreciate your cooperation."
Kael spun.
Twelve cultists poured into the chamber, their eyes blazing with zealous light. Behind them, in robes of pure white, stood three figures whose aura made Kael's teeth ache.
Demigods. Two of them. And the third—
Zarek stepped into the light, his silver hair gleaming like a blade.
"Kael Ashborn." Zarek's voice dripped with contempt. "The Butcher of Ashfield. The Soul Reaver. You've caused us considerable trouble."
Kael's hand found his sword hilt. "You framing me for massacre didn't take?"

The chaos you've witnessed—the attacks, the cultists, the 'random' violence—it's not chaos. It's choreography. Every piece in place. Every player following the script."
"Script written by your Whisper King."
Zarek's expression flickered. Something like fear crossed his features before vanishing. "The Whisper King guides us toward salvation. He alone understands the true nature of the seal network. He alone sees the path forward."

"Will be absorbed. Reformed. They've served their purpose. They held the line for two centuries while the seals weakened. Now their job is done." Zarek's voice hardened. "Step aside, Reaver. Join us. Your ability makes you invaluable to the new order. Harvest the Entities themselves. Feed on power beyond imagination. Or die here, like the relic you are."
The cultists began to advance. The Demigods raised their hands, gathering spiritual energy that made the air itself scream.
Kael drew his sword.
"Then let's discuss terms."

[Soul Harvest: You extracted 312 units of spiritual energy from the remains. Barrier Mastery acquired. Duration: Permanent.]

Divine.

The Whisper King. Finally showing his hand.

I didn't become the Whisper King through cultivation. I didn't ascend through meditation and sacrifice. I was born Divine. I've been Divine since before the Abyssal Wars. Since before the seal network existed."

The first Soul Reaver.
The origin of Kael's power. The mystery he'd been chasing since his awakening. The answer standing in front of him, revealed without fanfare.

Somewhere out there, the Whisper King was watching. Planning. Waiting.
And the seals continued to weaken.

[Soul Harvest: You extracted 2 units of spiritual energy from the remains. Digestive Stability acquired. Duration: a few hours.]

Kael Ashborn—Soul Reaver, Divine-stage cultivator, pawn of a madman who wanted him to consume gods—staggered into Thornwick's morning crowd and vanished.

How long before the seal broke completely?
