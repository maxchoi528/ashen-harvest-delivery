Kael pressed his back against the cold brick wall, breath shallow. Somewhere in the fog-choked alley ahead, the Wetwhisper Crew was finishing what they'd started with the wrong man.
The screaming had stopped three minutes ago.
He counted heartbeats, waiting for the shuffle of retreating boots, the curse of a survivor too wounded to run. The gaslight above him sputtered, casting orange flickers across the wet cobblestones. Rain dripped from a broken gutter somewhere overhead, each drop striking a puddle with the rhythm of a slow pulse.
Five months in Thornwick. Five months of learning which alleys belonged to which factions, which streets closed after dark, which tavern keepers would sell information for coin and which would sell him to the Iron Vanguard for a medal. Fogmire District was supposed to be neutral ground—or as neutral as any place could be when the Veil thinness brought things through the cracks.
His fingers brushed the worn leather of his coat, checking the knife at his hip, the second blade strapped to his forearm. The Soul Reaver hummed in his chest like a second heartbeat, patient as always. Ready.
Movement. Two figures emerged from the fog, dragging something between them.
"Leave the sack," one voice grunted. Male, rough-edged, the accent of the lower docks. "Boss said bring the head, leave the rest for the rats."
"Relax. Ain't no one watching." The second figure—stocky, a lantern jaw—kicked the sack experimentally. Something wet and heavy slid across the stones. "Damn Walker got more than we bargained for. Look at the state of it."
Kael's eyes narrowed. A Cholera Walker. He'd heard rumors, seen the quarantine notices plastered on buildings near the Ashpit furnaces. The corruption that turned men into something between alive and Abyssal—flesh sloughing off in gray sheets, breath that reeked of rot and sulfur, the madness that came from having your soul slowly digested by the thing wearing your skin.
The crew had killed one. That meant there was a corpse.
A fresh corpse.
His boots made no sound as he moved along the wall, staying in the shadows cast by the gaslight's failure point. The two men hadn't noticed him. They were too busy arguing about whether to take the Walker's necklace as a trophy, whether it would fetch anything at the Veiled Market.
"The Walker's still in the building," the stocky one said, dropping the sack. "We should—"
A wet, ragged howl cut him off. Not human. Not animal either. Something caught between, the sound of a throat trying to form words through a mouth that had forgotten how mouths worked.
Both men froze.
From the doorway of the condemned tenement behind them, a shape emerged. Kael's breath caught in his throat despite himself.
The Cholera Walker had been a man once. That much was visible in the remnants of its clothing—a factory worker's roughspun shirt, the remnants of leather boots. But the flesh had gone wrong. Gray-white patches spread across visible skin like lichen on stone, and where the patches cracked, something black and glistening pulsed beneath. One arm hung at a broken angle, bones clearly snapped in multiple places, yet the limb still moved with terrible purpose. Its face—
Kael looked away for just a moment. The face had melted and reformed, features sliding into configurations that hurt to perceive. Two eyes remained, but they were in the wrong places now, and they glowed with a sickly green light that had nothing to do with the gaslight.
The Walker moved forward. Not walking—flowing. Its legs bent in directions legs shouldn't bend, yet it stayed upright through sheer momentum of will and corruption.
"Gods preserve us," the first man whispered.
"Run," the stocky one said, and then they were both running, boots pounding against wet stone, disappearing into the fog.
The Walker watched them go. Then it turned its wrong-eyed gaze toward the alley where Kael crouched in shadow.
It knew he was there.
Kael's hand found his knife. The Soul Reaver's hum intensified, a warmth spreading through his chest that had nothing to do with fear. Harvesting from a Walker—something that had been human once, that might still have fragments of a soul buried under the corruption—carried risks. The memories could be tainted. The abilities extracted might come with side effects. And if the Walker's original soul was still partially present, fighting the harvest might damage Kael's own consciousness.
But the potential rewards were worth the risk. Walkers created by the Abyss often carried fragments of Entity power, abilities shaped by the corruption they carried.
The Walker flowed toward him. Fast. Faster than anything with that many broken bones should move.
Kael met it head-on.
His knife found the Walker's throat—or where the throat had been. The blade sank into corrupted flesh with a sound like cutting wet bread, and something dark spurted out that burned when it touched his skin. The Walker roared, its functional arm swinging around in a wide arc that would have taken Kael's head off if he hadn't already moved.
He ducked, rolled, came up with the knife reversed and drove it into the Walker's chest. The corruption fought him, the flesh trying to close around the blade, but the Soul Reaver was already active, reaching for something beneath the rot.
[Soul Harvest: Initiated. Target: Cholera Walker (corrupted human, Awakened-tier infection). Extraction...]
The Walker's mismatched eyes went wide. Not with pain—with terror. For a moment, in that terrible face, Kael saw something that might have been a man looking out. Recognition. Pleading.
Then the harvesting took hold, and he felt the Walker's final moments crash into his mind like waves against stone.

Kael wrenched the knife free and shoved the Walker back. It stumbled, the glow in its eyes fading, movements becoming sluggish as the harvested energy left its body.
"Rest now, Tobias," he said, and he meant it. The Walker had been a man once. Had a name, a life, people who might have missed him when he didn't come home from the factory. The corruption had taken all that, left only a monster wearing his corpse.
He wouldn't waste the death by forgetting the man who'd died.
The Walker collapsed, and this time it didn't rise. The corruption began to dissipate even as Kael watched, the gray flesh turning gray-white, then ash-gray, then something that looked more like dust than flesh. Within minutes, there would be nothing left but stains on the cobblestones.
Kael sheathed his knife and crouched beside the dissolving remains. His hand hovered over what had been the Walker's chest, where the heart would have been. The Soul Reaver pulsed warmth against his palm.
[Soul Harvest: Secondary extraction available. Corpse integrity: 40%. Accept harvest?]
He hesitated. Secondary harvests were riskier—more chance of contamination, more chance of pulling something from the corpse that shouldn't be pulled. But Disease Sense was useful, and the Walker had been infected with something strong enough to corrupt an Awakened-level soul.
He touched the remains.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted 203 units of degraded spiritual energy. You acquired: Corruption Resistance I (temporary). Effect: +15% resistance to Abyssal corruption for three days. Degradation: 60%. Duration: Permanent partial effect.]
The energy flowed into him, and with it came fragments of Tobias Wren's final days. Not the madness—that had been too far gone to extract cleanly—but the moments before. The factory floor, the steam and heat, the machine that had malfunctioned and spilled something burning across his skin. The screams of coworkers who watched his flesh begin to change.
And beneath it all, a memory of something he'd been carrying since before the corruption took hold. A piece of paper, hidden in his coat. Words written in a hand he didn't recognize: *The seals weaken. Thornwick burns before the month ends. Find the Sepulcher tablets or all is lost.*
Kael opened his eyes. His hand was shaking.
The Sepulcher tablets. He'd heard whispers of them in the months since arriving in Thornwick—mentioned once by a dying cultist he'd harvested, referenced in coded messages between Shadow Court operatives he'd intercepted. The recorded knowledge of the first Soul Reaver, supposedly containing something called the True Name.
The first Soul Reaver. The origin of his power.
"This is a message," he said aloud, his voice rough. "Tobias was carrying a message."
The dissolved remains offered no answers. But someone had given Tobias Wren that paper. Someone who knew about the tablets, about the weakening seals, about what was coming to Thornwick. Someone who'd died—or worse—in the weeks since.
Kael stood. The fog seemed thicker now, pressing against him like a living thing. In the distance, he heard the bells of a factory whistle marking the change of shift. Somewhere in the city, six Orders were fighting their endless war against the Abyss. Somewhere, cultists were plotting to break the seals that held 72 Entities in check. And somewhere, a man named Zarek—the one who'd framed him for crimes he didn't commit, who'd set in motion the events that led to his execution and resurrection—was active in Thornwick.
His phone buzzed. Old technology, salvaged and repaired, connected to a network that the Orders didn't monitor because they didn't believe anyone still used it.
A message from Lyra: *Base compromised. Shadow Court found the cellar. Moving to secondary location. Meet at the old clock tower, midnight.*
Kael's jaw tightened. Shadow Court activity had been increasing for weeks—Lyra's former employers watching them, assessing whether Kael was an asset worth recruiting or a threat worth eliminating. They'd never made a direct move, but the pressure was building.
He checked his gear, ensured his weapons were secure, and started moving through the fog.
The streets of Fogmire District at night were never empty but always quiet. Those who walked here had business they didn't want witnesses to see. Kael fell into the rhythm of the district, just another shadow among shadows.
He passed a dead-end alley where the steam vents had frozen during last week's cold snap, leaving the ground slick with ice that reflected the gaslight in fractured patterns. A woman stood there, her back against the wall, her posture suggesting someone who'd been there for hours.
She didn't look up as he passed. Didn't acknowledge him at all.
Kael's hand drifted toward his knife. Something about the scene felt wrong—the woman's stillness, the way the shadows seemed to pool around her feet like liquid. His newly acquired Disease Sense pulsed a warning: corruption, faint but present, emanating from the woman in the alley.
He kept walking.
"Another one." The woman's voice came from behind him, rough and raw, as if she'd been screaming for days. "The Reaver boy. You're not the first to pass this way tonight."
Kael stopped. Turned.
The woman lifted her head. Her eyes were solid black—not the reflective black of the Awakened, but the absolute black of something that had consumed everything human in her gaze. Gray-white patches spread across her visible skin, and where they cracked, something dark pulsed beneath.
Another Walker. Close to transformation, maybe hours from full corruption.
But still speaking. Still human enough to speak.
"Three of your kind came through here," the woman said. Her words slurred, fighting against the changes in her mouth. "Fighting the wrong battle. Going after the wrong targets. The real threat—" She coughed, and something black and viscous spurted from her lips. "The real threat is beneath the Ashpit furnaces. They've been digging. Preparing."
"Who?" Kael asked. "Who's been digging?"
The woman's black eyes fixed on him. For a moment, he saw something human looking out—fear, frustration, the desperate need to warn someone before it was too late.
"The cult," she said. "Not the random lunatics you harvest from. The real ones. The ones who serve the one who speaks in whispers. They're bringing something through. Something that's been sealed since the first war."
[Soul Harvest: Warning. Target is in active transformation. Extraction may harvest incomplete soul or trigger contamination. Proceed?]
The notification pulsed in his vision, waiting for confirmation.
The woman's hand shot out, grabbing his wrist with strength that shouldn't have been possible from someone so close to collapse. Her grip burned cold through his sleeve.
"Har vest me," she whispered. "I can feel it taking over. I can feel Tobias Wren's Walker in the memory you pulled—he was my brother. We worked the same factory. The same machine. They tried to quarantine us both, but I escaped. I've been running for three days, watching myself become him."
Her black eyes glistened. Tears or corruption, impossible to tell.
"Take what I know. Give me peace. Before the Walker finishes what it started and there's nothing left of Mara Wren to save."
Kael looked at the woman—at Mara—and made his choice.
He touched her face.
[Soul Harvest: Initiated. Target: Mara Wren (transforming Awakened, near-corruption). Extraction...]
The harvest hit him like a physical blow, memories flooding through his consciousness in a torrent of pain and desperation. Mara's escape from the quarantine facility. The experiments she'd witnessed—other workers strapped to tables, things being extracted from their bodies while they screamed. The cultists in masks speaking words that made reality shudder. The tunnel beneath the Ashpit furnaces, descending into darkness where something massive waited.
And the message. The same message Tobias had carried, passed between workers like a secret, words that felt more like prophecy than warning: *The seals weaken. Thornwick burns before the month ends. Find the Sepulcher tablets or all is lost.*
But there was more. Details. Specifics. The location of the tunnel entrance—behind the furnace maintenance shed, marked by three scratches in the shape of a broken seal. The name of the cultist in charge—a man called the Pale Architect who wore robes stitched with symbols from before the Abyssal Wars. The day they'd chosen for their ritual—three days from now, during the new moon, when the Veil was thinnest.
And something else. Something that made Kael's blood run cold.
The Pale Architect had mentioned a test subject. Someone who'd been harvested from the death row facility six months ago. Someone whose power had been extracted and studied before being returned to the world with modifications.
*Kael Ashborn,* Mara's memory whispered. *The Soul Reaver they created. He carries the key to what we need.*
The harvest ended. Mara collapsed against him, her body going slack, her breathing slowing. Not death—not yet. But the corruption had accelerated with the extraction, her skin turning gray-white, her features beginning to slide.
"The tunnel," she managed. "Get there before they finish. They're so close—"
She stopped breathing.
Kael lowered her body to the ground. The transformation had progressed even faster than he'd expected; by the time he checked for a pulse, her flesh was already beginning to change, the corruption claiming what remained of her humanity.
He couldn't harvest her again. There was nothing left to take that wouldn't be pure corruption.
"I'm sorry," he said, and stood.
The fog seemed to press closer, thick and heavy. Somewhere in the distance, a bell tolled—eleven chimes. One hour until he was supposed to meet Lyra. One hour to decide whether to pursue the tunnel beneath the Ashpit furnaces, to confront whatever the cult was preparing, or to play it safe and wait for more information.
The Soul Reaver hummed in his chest, satisfied with the night's harvest. Disease Sense. Corruption Resistance. Information about the cult's plans, the Pale Architect, the tunnel entrance.
And the knowledge that they knew about him. That his existence wasn't coincidence or accident—it was design. Someone had harvested him from death row, extracted his power, studied it, and returned him to the world with modifications.
He wasn't just a man who'd awakened to the Soul Reaver ability. He was a weapon someone had built.
Kael moved through the fog, mind racing. The Ashpit furnaces were forty minutes away if he took the underground routes—the maintenance tunnels that the factories used to move workers and materials between shifts. Dangerous territory, especially for someone with a price on their head and an interest from the Shadow Court.
But the information in his head wouldn't stay fresh forever. The cult was preparing for their ritual in three days. If they succeeded in bringing something through the weakened seals, Thornwick would burn. The Entities would rise. Everything the Six Orders had built over centuries would come crashing down.
And somewhere in that chaos, Zarek would be waiting. The man who'd framed him. The cultist who'd set everything in motion.

Someone had been through here. Recently.
He descended.
The tunnel sloped downward at a steep angle, the walls lined with pipes that still carried heat from the furnaces above. His footsteps echoed against the stone, each sound returning to him slightly altered, as if the darkness was listening and reflecting back its own version of his presence.

Whatever the cult was doing down here, it was leaving traces that even someone without his new ability would sense as deeply unsettling.

The right passage was marked with those three scratches, the broken seal symbol Mara had described.
Kael chose the right passage.

Something was coming through.
