The ground convulsed beneath Kael's boots. He dropped into a combat stance, spirit energy coiling around his forearms, as a crack split the cobblestones three feet ahead.
Not a fissure. Not an earthquake.
A rift.
Darkness seeped from the wound in reality, thick and wrong, carrying the stench of ozone and something older—something that had never known sunlight. Kael's perception at Demigod stage caught what others couldn't: the subtle wrongness of the Veil tearing, the membrane between worlds weakening with each pulse of that abyssal exhalation.
"Stand back!" The shout came from a woman in grey Order robes, her hands weaving barriers of pure light as she sprinted toward him. The insignia on her chest bore a sun crossed by three swords—the Order of the Burning Dawn.
More rifts erupted across Thornwick's Market District. Shopfronts collapsed. Wagons overturned. Citizens screamed as the air itself seemed to curdle around them.
Kael counted seven tears in the first ten seconds. Seven holes into the Abyss, each one vomiting shadow and the distant, hungry attention of something vast pressing against the other side.
The woman reached him, her barrier flickering. "Are you Awakened?"
"Does it matter?"
"It matters if you want to live." She grabbed his arm, pulling him toward an alley. "The seals are failing. All of them. Something woke the Entities."
Behind them, something crawled out of a rift.
Kael had read the texts. He'd heard the warnings whispered among the Orders' hunted survivors. He'd never seen an Abyssal Spawn—not truly. The creature that hauled itself onto the cobblestones defied description, a mass of angles that shouldn't exist, eyes that multiplied as he watched, limbs that branched and retracted with wet mechanical clicks.
It turned its collection of gazes toward him.
The woman screamed an alarm. Bells rang across the district—emergency summons for Order combatants. More people flooded the streets, some fleeing, others charging toward the rifts with weapons drawn. A man in Iron Vanguard armor bellowed commands, organizing a defensive line.
Kael pulled free from the woman's grip.
"You can't fight them alone—"
"I'm not planning to fight." He was already moving, circling the Spawn, watching how it moved. The texts had been clear: Spawn were scouts, extensions of the larger Entities beyond the rifts. Kill one, a hundred more would come.
Unless someone severed the connection.
He reached the corpse of a citizen—crushed beneath falling debris, still warm, still fresh. Kael dropped to one knee and pressed his palm against the cooling flesh.
The Soul Reaver awakened.
**Soul Harvest: You extracted survival instinct from the remains. Resilience acquired.**
Power flooded through him—not spiritual energy, but something rawer. The man's final desperate need to protect his family, crystallized into an ability Kael could use once. Resilience. For the next thirty seconds, his body would refuse to accept damage below critical threshold.
He rose and charged the Spawn.
The creature lunged. Angles blurred. Kael twisted, feeling claws score the air where his throat had been, and drove his harvesting-knife into a junction of what might have been a shoulder. Black ichor sprayed.
**Soul Harvest: You extracted shadow-walk technique from the remains. Shadow Stride acquired.**
The Spawn screamed—a sound like reality tearing—and collapsed into writhing darkness. Kael felt the connection sever. Somewhere beyond the rift, something vast and angry turned its attention toward him.
The woman stared at him. Her barrier had dropped. "What are you?"
"Someone who uses the dead." He checked the body at his feet—properly dead now, truly dead, its soul fragments harvested and preserved in Kael's ability. "Find the others. Rally them to the central plaza. I'll buy time."
"You can't—"
"Watch me."
He walked toward the nearest rift, where three more Spawn were emerging. The Sealed Rifts—the texts had mentioned seventy-two, scattered across the continent, each containing an Entity of immense power. If these were failing simultaneously, something massive was happening. Something orchestrated.
Cultists. It had to be cultists.
Kael had seen their handiwork before. The symbols carved into sacred sites, the rituals that weakened barriers, the patient work of decades to break what the Orders had sealed. His mind raced through possibilities—the Whisper King, the Blood God's followers, the mad cultists who'd tried to summon entities during his incarceration.
None of it mattered right now. What mattered was the Spawn.
He harvested two more corpses—one a city guard, one a merchant—gaining Shadow Stride technique and a basic resistance to spiritual corruption. His Soul Reaver ability worked without cost, but the social cost was mounting. The woman from the Order of the Burning Dawn was watching him with barely concealed horror. The Iron Vanguard commander had noticed as well.
Too late to worry about politics.
The first Spawn reached him. Kael Shadow Strided through its attack, appearing behind it, driving his knife into what served as its spine. The creature thrashed, and he harvested: **Soul Harvest: You extracted temporal distortion from the remains. Haste acquired.**
Power surged through his nervous system. For five seconds, everything slowed.
He used them.
Two more Spawn collapsed. Kael harvested again, his hands moving with mechanical precision, pressing against cooling flesh, extracting fragments of the dead. His mind processed the information immediately—the abilities were raw, unpolished, but usable.
**Soul Harvest: You extracted pain immunity from the remains. Numb acquired.**
**Soul Harvest: You extracted combat memory from the remains. Fighting Style: Street acquired.**
Street fighting. Whoever this person had been, they'd known how to survive in the worst parts of Thornwick. Kael absorbed the muscle memory, the instinctive reactions, the understanding of how to end a fight quickly and efficiently.
Around him, the battle was escalating. Order combatants had arrived—five from the Burning Dawn, eight from the Iron Vanguard, two from the Arcane Spire with their characteristic robes and wands crackling with stored lightning. They formed a perimeter around the rifts, cutting down Spawn as fast as they emerged.
But more kept coming.
Kael counted fifteen rifts now. Fifteen tears in the Veil, each one widening. The Entities beyond were pressing harder, testing the weakened barriers, probing for weaknesses.
"The seal network!" The Arcane Spire mage—a woman with silver-streaked hair and eyes that blazed with power—raised her staff. A beam of concentrated spiritual energy struck the largest rift, and for a moment, the darkness receded. "Someone get to the Council chambers! The Concordat wards need to be activated!"
"They're already failing!" A young guard stumbled toward her, blood streaming from a gash on his forehead. "Everything's failing! The whole city's—"
An explosion tore through the plaza. Kael threw himself behind an overturned wagon as shockwaves rolled across the cobblestones. When he looked up, the sky itself had changed color—purple-black clouds swirling where the sun had been, shot through with veins of sickly green lightning.
Not natural clouds. Something was pressing against the atmospheric barrier from the other side.
"What's happening?" The woman from the Burning Dawn had found him again, her robes torn, her face streaked with blood and tears. "This isn't possible. The seals were supposed to hold for another century."
"They were weakened." Kael stood, scanning the plaza. The Order combatants were being overwhelmed—not by numbers, but by the sheer persistence of the Spawn. Each time they cut one down, two more emerged. "Someone's been working on them for years. Decades, maybe."
"Who would—"
"Anyone who wants the world to burn."
A shadow fell across them. Kael looked up and felt his breath freeze in his chest.
The Entity had come through.
It dwarfed the Spawn—a towering mass of writhing darkness, vaguely humanoid but wrong in every proportion. Where its face should have been, a single massive eye opened, the color of dried blood, staring with ancient hunger.
**Entity Manifestation Detected. Threat Level: Sovereign-class. Recommend immediate withdrawal.**
Kael's Soul Reaver sense screamed warnings. This wasn't a Spawn. This was a fragment of an actual Entity, one of the seventy-two sealed horrors that the Orders had locked away. It shouldn't be able to manifest at all. The seals were meant to prevent exactly this.
But the seals were failing.
The Entity raised what might have been an arm. Reality buckled around it, space itselfing in response to its will. Order combatants scattered, their barriers crumbling before they'd fully formed.
"Run," Kael said.
"We can't—"
"There's nothing we can do against that. Nothing any of us have." He was already moving, pulling the woman along, forcing her legs to cooperate. "We need to warn the others. The Orders. Everyone. This isn't a raid—it's a war."
They ran. Behind them, the Entity's scream shattered windows for three blocks. Spawn poured from every rift, a tide of nightmare creatures flooding Thornwick's streets.
The War had begun.

The next three hours blurred into chaos.
Kael moved through the city like a ghost, harvesting from the dead, using their abilities to survive encounters with Spawn, always staying ahead of the Entity's inexorable advance. He learned things with each extraction—how to throw a punch that would break bone, how to read a foe's stance and predict their attack, how to endure pain that would cripple a normal man.
**Soul Harvest: You extracted navigation instinct from the remains. Thornwick Underground Routes acquired.**
**Soul Harvest: You extracted loyalty bond from the remains. Ally Detection (passive) acquired.**
**Soul Harvest: You extracted spiritual sensitivity from the remains. Entity Sense acquired.**
The abilities piled up inside him, a library of the dead's final moments. Kael sorted through them as he ran, discarding those that wouldn't serve him, keeping the tools that might keep him alive.
Thornwick was burning. Every district he'd passed through showed the same pattern: rifts, Spawn, death, and now the Entity—a Sovereign-class horror that moved through the city like a siege engine, crushing buildings, shattering defenses, leaving nothing but ruin in its wake.
The Order of the Burning Dawn had established a fallback position near the Sun Temple, a fortress-palace built over the most powerful ley line confluence in the region. Kael found them there, hundreds of Awakened and civilians alike, a desperate assembly of survivors preparing for a last stand.
He also found the Shadow Court's representative.
Lyra stepped out of the crowd as he entered the temple's outer courtyard, her face pale but composed. The Shadow Court's insignia—a black eye superimposed over a crescent moon—adorned her collar.
"Kael." Her voice was steady, but her hands trembled slightly. "I wondered if you'd make it."
"Lyra." He stopped a few feet away, studying her. Three years since they'd last spoken. Three years since she'd warned him about Zarek's betrayal, since they'd fought side by side against the cultists who'd tried to steal his Soul Reaver ability. "What are you doing here?"
"The Shadow Court has interests in Thornwick." A non-answer. Kael waited. "We're monitoring the situation."
"Monitoring." He let the word hang between them. "While the world ends."
"The world has been ending for a thousand years. We didn't notice." She glanced toward the burning horizon, where the Entity's presence warped the sky. "This is different. This is coordinated. Someone planned this."
"I know."
"The seals were sabotaged. Every major rift in the region, weakened simultaneously. The cultists couldn't have done it alone."
Kael felt ice crystallize in his chest. "You're saying an Order did this?"
"I'm saying someone with access to ancient knowledge, someone who understood how the seals worked, someone who knew exactly where to strike." Lyra's dark eyes met his. "The Shadow Court has theories."
"I don't care about theories. I care about stopping this."
"That's why I'm telling you." She stepped closer, lowering her voice. "The Soul Reaver. Your ability. It's not random, Kael. It's not a mutation or a curse. The power you're using—the ability to harvest souls, to take what the dead left behind—it's ancient. Older than the Orders. Older than the Concordat."
Kael's harvesting-sense prickled. Something in Lyra's words resonated with the fragments he'd collected, memories of the dead that contained hints, echoes of knowledge that shouldn't exist.
"The first Soul Reaver was a weapon," Lyra continued. "Created a thousand years ago during the Abyssal Wars, when humanity was losing. They gave the power to one person—a single warrior who could harvest the skills and strength of the fallen, who could fight the Entities without depleting their own reserves. The weapon worked. The Entities were sealed. But then..."
"Then what?"
"Then the weapon became a threat. A person with the ability to take anything from anyone, who owed nothing to the established powers, who could grow stronger with every battle." Lyra's voice dropped to barely a whisper. "The Orders couldn't control them. So they erased the knowledge. Sealed away the history. Founded the Concordat partly to ensure no one would ever create another Soul Reaver."
Kael's mind raced through the implications. "I'm not the first."
"You're the latest. The Soul Reaver lineage never died out—it went underground. Hidden. Each generation, one bearer, passing the knowledge in secret." Lyra pulled something from her sleeve—a small bronze tablet, covered in script that made Kael's eyes water. "This is from the Sepulcher. The resting place of the first Soul Reaver. It contains the True Name—the source of your power."
Her hand trembled as she held it out. "The Shadow Court has been looking for this for decades. We finally found it three months ago, hidden in a vault beneath the old cathedral. I was supposed to deliver it to my superiors. Instead..."
"Instead you're giving it to me."
"You're the only one who can use it. And if what we're seeing is true—if someone is trying to break the seals permanently, to release the Entities—then you need to know what you really are."
Kael took the tablet. The moment his fingers touched the bronze, knowledge flooded through him—not harvested, but awakened, as if the tablet recognized him and decided to share its secrets.
The Soul Reaver was not a gift. It was a covenant.
The original bearer had made a deal with something beyond the Entities, something that existed in the space between realms. The power came with a purpose: to maintain the balance, to ensure neither the Material nor the Abyss ever gained complete dominance. The Soul Reaver stood as a third option, neither aligned with humanity nor with the Entities, but with the stability of the Veil itself.
The first Reaver had served for two hundred years before breaking the covenant. They'd grown too powerful, too hungry, and had tried to use their ability to dominate both realms. The Orders had united against them—not for noble reasons, but because they feared what the Reaver had become.
The first Reaver was not dead. They were sealed. Imprisoned in a tomb that existed partially in both realms, their power contained but not destroyed. The tablet contained the location.
Kael looked up from the bronze, his understanding complete.
"The Sepulcher," he said. "It's real. The original Reaver is still alive."
Lyra nodded. "Sealed, but alive. And if the seals continue to fail—"
"If they fail completely, the original Reaver wakes up."
"That's one of the seventy-two Entities. The first Soul Reaver, transformed by centuries of imprisonment and rage."
Outside, the Entity screamed again. Closer now. The temple's barriers flickered.
Kael tucked the tablet into his coat. "I need to get to the source. The central rift—wherever the main seal is failing."
"You'll die."
"Probably." He moved toward the temple's gates, where Order combatants were preparing to face the advancing horror. "But I'm the only one who can harvest from an Entity. And I'm the only one who knows what we're really fighting."
Lyra grabbed his arm. "Kael. The Shadow Court is watching you. They've been watching since you awakened. If you go to the Sepulcher, if you try to reclaim the original Reaver's power—"
"Then what?"
"Then you become a threat to everyone. Including me." Her grip tightened. "I'm risking everything to tell you this. The Court doesn't forgive betrayal."
"You're not betraying them. You're giving them what they want—a weapon against the Entities."
"I'm giving you a choice." Lyra released him. "The Court wants to control the Soul Reaver. Use you as their weapon. But if you go to the Sepulcher, if you try to become what the original Reaver was, they'll hunt you. All six Orders will hunt you."
Kael considered this. The information asymmetry crystallized—he now knew things about his own power that the Orders had spent centuries suppressing. The Soul Reaver was not just an ability. It was a lineage. A legacy. A threat to the balance of power that the Concordat had maintained.
He also knew that the current crisis was not random. Someone was trying to release the Entities. Someone with access to ancient knowledge, someone who understood the seal network, someone who wanted the world to burn.
And now he knew that the original Soul Reaver—the first weapon, the first broken covenant—was waiting in the Sepulcher, ready to be awakened.
The temple's barriers exploded. Through the breach, Kael saw the Entity—closer now, vast and terrible, its blood-red eye fixed on the Sun Temple's spire.
Order combatants charged. Lightning flashed. Fire roared. The battle for Thornwick had reached its apex.
Kael ran toward the chaos, the tablet warm against his chest, the knowledge of seventy-two dead civilizations echoing in his mind. Behind him, Lyra watched him go, her expression unreadable.
Ahead, the war waited.

Sovereign-class. Too powerful for conventional assault.
He needed to get closer.
Using the abilities he'd harvested, Kael wove through the battle. Street fighting instincts guided his movements, helping him anticipate attacks before they landed. Shadow Stride let him blink across short distances, appearing behind Spawn and cutting them down before they could react. Resilience kept him standing despite wounds that should have dropped him.
**Soul Harvest: You extracted last breath desperation from the remains. Undying Will acquired.**
Another fragment. Another tool. Kael absorbed it, feeling his determination solidify into something harder than mere resolve.
He reached the Entity's base.
Up close, the horror defied comprehension. It was made of darkness the way a hurricane was made of wind—not a single thing, but a constant churning of shadow and malice, held together by will alone. The eye that served as its face rolled in its socket, scanning, hunting, hungry.
Kael raised his hand.
The Soul Reaver awakening required contact. He had to touch the corpse—the remnant—the remains of something that had died and been transformed. This Entity was still partially alive, still fighting against the transformation, but it was also a fragment of something greater, something sealed in a rift between worlds.
Could he harvest from it?
The risk was immense. The texts warned that Entities could corrupt Awakened through spiritual contact alone. Touching one directly was suicide for anyone below Divine stage.
But Kael was not using spiritual power. His ability drew from the soul, from the essence of what had once been alive. The Entity was alive—or at least, it had been, before imprisonment had twisted it into something monstrous.
He reached out.
His fingers touched shadow.
The world stopped.
Kael stood in darkness. Not the darkness of night or of a sealed room, but the primordial darkness of the Abyss itself—endless, hungry, patient. Around him, the souls of the dead drifted like sparks, each one carrying fragments of memory, skill, identity.
**Soul Reaver Covenant Activated. Beginning harvest.**
Power erupted through him. Not the gentle flow of harvested abilities, but a torrent—ancient, furious, corrupted by centuries of imprisonment and rage. Kael screamed as knowledge flooded his mind, as memories that weren't his own burned through his consciousness.
The first Soul Reaver had been a woman named Sera. She'd fought in the Abyssal Wars, had harvested the skills of warriors who fell around her, had become the greatest weapon humanity ever created. For two centuries, she'd served the covenant, maintaining the balance, ensuring neither realm destroyed the other.
But power changed people.
Sera had grown hungry. The harvests weren't enough. She wanted more—not just the skills of the dead, but the essence of the living. She'd tried to breach the Abyss itself, to claim power directly from the Entities.
She'd failed.
The Entities had captured her instead. They'd dragged her into their realm, torn her soul apart, rebuilt it according to their own logic. What emerged from that torture was no longer human. It was a hybrid—a Soul Reaver corrupted by Abyssal energy, trapped between realms, neither alive nor dead.
They'd sealed her away, using the very powers she'd tried to steal against her. The Sepulcher was her prison, and the Entity currently destroying Thornwick was just a fragment of her vast consciousness—a piece of herself she'd sent through the weakening barriers, testing, probing, searching for a way to free the whole.
Kael's eyes snapped open.

"Little Reaver." The voice came from everywhere and nowhere, a chorus of whispers layered over each other. "You carry my legacy. My blood. My purpose."
"You're Sera." Kael's voice came out steadier than he felt. "The first Soul Reaver."

"I could stop you."

"Or you could join me. Take my place. Become what I was meant to be—the true Soul Reaver, master of life and death, ruler of the Veil."

"Then we shall see what you become."
The Entity withdrew. Not defeated—Kael knew better than to believe that—but retreating, pulling its fragments back through the rifts, leaving Thornwick in stunned silence.
The war wasn't over. It had barely begun.

The tablet in his coat felt heavier now. The location of the Sepulcher burned in his mind—coordinates that would take him across the continent, through territory controlled by hostile Orders, into the heart of the Abyssal frontier.
Behind him, the surviving Order combatants were regrouping. Ahead, the rifts had begun to close, the Entities withdrawing to plan their next assault.

"It means going to the source. Finding the truth. And making a choice about what kind of weapon I really am."

And somewhere in the distance, the Entity's fragments waited.

The safe house was a cellar beneath a collapsed bakery, three streets from the temple. Kael had cleared the bodies, checked for structural damage, and barricaded the entrance with debris. It wasn't much, but it was defensible.
He sat against the wall, the tablet laid out before him, and began to read.
The Sepulcher was located in the Ashfall Mountains, three weeks' journey from Thornwick. The route would take him through Order-controlled territory, through wilderness haunted by Spawn, through challenges that would kill a man without his abilities.
It would also take him through Zarek's domain.
The thought surfaced unbidden: the cultist who'd framed him for murder, who'd tried to steal his Soul Reaver power, who'd escaped justice while Kael rotted in prison. The unfinished obligation from Chapter One. Kael had promised himself he'd settle that debt.
The war changed things. Zarek was connected to the cultists—possibly one of the architects of the current crisis. Confronting him now might yield information about who was truly behind the seal sabotage.
Or it might be a trap.
Kael weighed his options. The war demanded attention. The Entities would return, and next time they might not retreat. The Sealed Rifts were weakening across the continent, and only someone who could harvest from the Entities themselves could hope to stop them.
But the original Soul Reaver's offer lingered. Her power was immense—Kael had felt it during that moment of contact. If he could access even a fraction of it, he might become strong enough to challenge the Entities directly.
Or he might become like her. Corrupted. Twisted. A monster that needed to be sealed away.
The tablet's words glowed faintly in the darkness. The first Reaver had broken the covenant. The second might have a chance to restore it—or to shatter it completely.
Kael closed his eyes and allowed himself a moment of exhaustion. The day's harvest sat inside him, a library of death and desperation. He could feel each ability waiting to be used, each fragment of the dead crying out for purpose.
Tomorrow, the war would continue. The Entities would regroup. The Orders would scramble to mount a defense. And Kael would have to choose his next step.
Stay in Thornwick and help defend the city. Hunt down Zarek and extract information. Travel to the Sepulcher and confront the original Soul Reaver. Or something else entirely—some path that wasn't yet visible.
The Soul Reaver ability gave him options. The covenant gave him purpose. And the war gave him a reason to use both.
A sound echoed from above. Footsteps on the street. Multiple sets, moving with purpose.
Kael rose silently, knife in hand, and positioned himself beside the barricaded door. His harvesting-sense reached outward, touching the spiritual signatures of those approaching.
Three Awakened. Order-trained, by the feel of it. But not hostile—not yet. They were searching, scanning, looking for something.
Or someone.
"Little Reaver." Lyra's voice, filtered through the rubble. "I know you're in there. We need to talk."
Kael didn't answer. His grip tightened on the knife.
"The Shadow Court has made a decision. About you. About the war." A pause. "You're not going to like it."
Outside, the footsteps stopped. Kael counted three heartbeats, three breaths, three lives that might end tonight depending on what came next.
The war had come to his door.
And Kael had to decide whether to fight, flee, or negotiate.
