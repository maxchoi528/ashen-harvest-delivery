The ground erupted three blocks from the Thornwick Barracks. Kael felt the shockwave before he saw the light—a deep, bone-shaking tremor that rattled windows and sent rats fleeing through the sewers. He sprinted toward the breach, coat whipping behind him, his hand already reaching for the hilt of his blade.
Another explosion. Then another. The night sky flickered with colors that belonged to no natural fire—sickly greens, arterial reds, the void-black of collapsed stars.
"Third district!" someone screamed. A city watchman ran past, face ashen, armor clanking. "The seals—the seals are breaking!"
Kael vaulted a collapsed fruit cart and rounded the corner onto Merchant's Row. The scene stopped him cold.
A rift hung in the air like a wound in the world—fifteen feet tall, edges flickering with unstable energy. Through it, he glimpsed something vast and wrong: geometry that hurt to perceive, colors that had no names. A creature was clawing its way through, all wrong angles and too many limbs.
Guards rushed forward with pikes and prayers. The first man to reach the rift opened his mouth to strike—
The thing beyond grabbed him. There was no other word for it. A limb like a blade of black glass pierced the guard's chest and withdrew. The body collapsed.
"Form ranks!" a woman's voice commanded. Captain Sera Vance, her silver hair pulled back tight, stood ten paces from the rift with her sword drawn. "Second and Third Companies—containment formation! Someone get a Sealer!"
Kael watched a second guard die. And a third. The creature was fully through now, its body reforming as it pulled itself from the wound in reality. It stood eight feet tall, all angles and edges, and when it moved, the air screamed.
The Sealer arrived—a thin man in grey robes, hands already weaving symbols in the air. But the rift pulsed, and a second creature began to emerge.
"They're coming through faster than we can close them," someone muttered.
Kael counted the seconds. In the three minutes since the first explosion, he'd heard four more detonations across the city. This wasn't random. This was coordinated.
He moved forward before conscious thought caught up. A guard shouted a warning. Kael ignored him, approaching the first creature as it turned toward the massing defenders.
The Soul Reaver stirred in his chest—a cold, hungry presence that had become as familiar as his own heartbeat. When he touched the creature's dying host of a body, the notification bloomed in his vision.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted residual essence from the remains. Abyssal Corruption detected. Spiritual contamination risk: HIGH. Purification recommended before absorption.]
The creature he'd touched was already dissolving—its form unable to hold in the Material realm without active manifestation. But the essence remained, clinging to his fingers like oil.
"Kael!" Sera's voice cut through. "What are you doing?"
He raised his hand, showing her the dark residue. "The rifts—they're not natural collapses. Something is forcing them open."
"How do you—" She stopped, her expression shifting as understanding dawned. "You're a Reaver."
It wasn't a question. Kael didn't bother denying it.
"Does that change whether you'll listen to what I have to say?"
The second creature roared. The Sealer staggered back, blood streaming from his nose. The rift behind him flickered, destabilized by his failing concentration.
Sera made a decision. "Talk fast."
"Cultists," Kael said. "This is organized. The simultaneous timing, the targeting of seal points—someone's been mapping these locations. Someone knows exactly where the weak spots are."
"Impossible. The seal locations are classified—"
"Classified from who?" Kael watched the creature advance, guards falling back before it. "The Abyss doesn't need classified information. It has eternity to probe, to test, to find the cracks. And someone down here is helping it."
A third rift opened a block away. Kael felt the spiritual pressure change—every open rift weakened the Veil's integrity across the entire city. Each wound made the others bleed faster.
"Then what do you suggest?" Sera demanded.
Kael's gaze swept the battle. Guards dying to contain something they couldn't understand. A Sealer burning out his reserves trying to close a wound that was being actively ripped wider. The creature in front of him reforming from nothing but spite and Abyssal hunger.
"Close the rifts, kill the source," he said. "We can't fight them and close the wounds at the same time. We need to hit the people doing the ripping."
"That's not how the Orders—"
"Which Orders?" Kael's voice sharpened. "Which Orders are coming? How long until reinforcements arrive? And when they get here, will they close these rifts or just kill everything that's already been touched?"
Sera's jaw tightened. She had no answer.
The first creature lunged. Kael moved without thinking—his blade, a simple steel weapon with no spiritual properties, was useless against an Abyssal manifestation. But his hand found the creature's edge as it passed, and the Soul Reaver drank.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted FRAGMENTED MEMORY from the remains. Fragment contains: directional data, source resonance, ritual anchor point. Memory degraded—integration partial.]
The creature shrieked—a sound like tearing metal—and collapsed into ash. The essence Kael pulled from it burned with cold fire, flooding his spiritual pathways with sensation that bordered on pain.
He gasped, staggering. The fragment crystallized in his mind: an image of a building, its location unmistakable. The Thornwick Archives. The repository of all sealed rift data for the entire eastern territories.
"Sera," he grabbed her arm. "The Archives. That's where they're drawing the power from."
"What?"
"The ritual anchors." The knowledge burned through him, vivid and certain. "They're using classified seal data to force the weak points open. Someone inside is feeding them information."
She stared at him. "How can you possibly—"
"I touched it. The memory of the thing that came through. I saw where it came from." Kael pulled away, already moving. "Send people to the Archives. I'll handle what's here."
"Kael!" Sera grabbed his shoulder. "You're one man. There are five active rifts, seven manifestations, and probably cultist cells in every district. What exactly do you think—"
The third rift pulsed, wider now. Through it, Kael saw not one creature but many—a swarm of smaller manifestations, each the size of a dog, each trailing shadows that ate light.
He made his choice.
"Then help me kill them faster." He pointed at the Sealer, bleeding from the eyes. "Can he still function?"
"Barely. He used too much—"
"Get him to the Archives. He can close that rift from the source if he gets there in time." Kael drew his blade properly, accepting its uselessness against the spiritual. "I'll keep these occupied until dawn."
Sera studied him for a long moment. Something shifted in her expression—calculation, maybe, or desperate hope. "If you're wrong about this, the entire eastern seal network could collapse."
"And if I'm right, we buy time for someone to find the people responsible." Kael turned toward the swarm. "I'd bet on the certain action than wait for Orders that might not come."
He charged.
The first small manifestation met his blade with a sound like breaking glass. It died messily, its form too unstable to survive even mundane damage. The Soul Reaver pulled the essence from its dissolving body—a tiny drop, barely enough to register, but every bit counted.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted TRACE ESSENCE from the remains. Insufficient for ability acquisition. Energy absorbed into reserves.]
Kael's reserves swelled slightly. Not much, but enough to sharpen his senses, quicken his reactions. The Reaver fed on death the way a fire fed on wood—every kill made the next one easier.
The swarm scattered, surrounding him. Kael spun, blade dancing, cutting down three before the first landed a strike. The impact threw him backward—a psychic blow that numbed his left arm.
He crashed into a merchant's stall, wood splintering around him. The small creatures advanced, their movements coordinated with intelligence that shouldn't exist in such base manifestations.
"They're being directed," Kael realized. "Someone is controlling them through the rift."
He reached for the sensation of the connection—the thread of spiritual energy linking each creature to its master. The Soul Reaver could follow those threads, could climb them back to the source.
The cost would be pain. It always was.
Kael planted his feet and pulled.
The sensation was like swallowing broken glass. He felt the connection immediately—a knot of malevolent will located somewhere to the north, in the direction of the old temple district. The controller sensed him looking back.
A presence pressed against his mind, vast and alien and filled with hunger. It saw him as prey. It saw his power as something to be consumed.
You are marked, the presence communicated. The Reaver's echo. You will be taken.
Kael severed the connection violently, gasping. Blood dripped from his nose. His vision swam with afterimages of the Abyss—endless nothing filled with the screams of things that had forgotten how to die.
The swarm attacked.
Kael fought with everything he had. His blade, his body, his stolen reserves, the cold fire of the Soul Reaver burning in his chest. He killed four, five, six of the manifestations. Each death fed him a little more, made him a little faster, a little stronger.
But there were always more coming through. Each rift birthed new horrors every minute. The Sealer had collapsed entirely now, his reserves spent. Sera had organized the guards into a defensive perimeter, but they were losing ground.
Two more guards died. Then a third. The night filled with screaming—human and otherwise.
Then the sky exploded with light.
Kael looked up to see a column of golden energy rising from the city's center. The symbol of the Order of Dawn—a sunburst rendered in spiritual fire. Reinforcements had arrived, but not the kind anyone had hoped for.
Three figures descended from the light. They wore matching armor, each plate inscribed with protective runes. Their presence filled the air with warmth and certainty—the spiritual weight of fully Awakened warriors at the peak of their power.
"By order of the Council of Six," the lead figure announced, "Thornwick is under martial jurisdiction. All local forces will submit to Order command."
Sera stepped forward, her face tight. "Captain Sera Vance, city garrison. We're in the middle of—"
"We can see that." The Order commander surveyed the chaos with cold efficiency. "Three active rifts. Nine manifestations. Significant civilian casualties. This is beyond local containment."
"Then help us close—"
"The Order of Dawn will handle the manifestations. The Order of Iron will secure the perimeter. The Order of Veils will identify and neutralize the cultist cells." The commander's tone brooked no argument. "Your people will assist with evacuation and quarantine."
Kael watched the Order soldiers move into position. Their efficiency was terrifying—within minutes, they had established a perimeter that held against even the swarms. The manifestations that charged their lines died in disciplined volleys of spiritual fire.
These were Awakened soldiers at the Sovereign stage, just like him. But they fought as a unit, their coordination perfect, their power multiplied by training and unity.
He was alone. He had always been alone.
The thought almost made him laugh.
"The Sealer," he said to Sera. "Did he make it to the Archives?"
She nodded. "Two of my people escorted him. They reported arrival twenty minutes ago."
"Then the rift there should be closing." Kael wiped blood from his face. "Which means we have maybe thirty minutes before whatever they're doing completes somewhere else."
The commander of the Order forces glanced at Kael. "You. The one fighting without formation."
"That's me."
"Your spiritual signature is... Unusual." The commander's eyes narrowed. "What are you?"
Kael met her gaze without flinching. "I'm the one who told them where to look."
"Explain."
"Classified?" Kael's smile held no warmth. "Or does that only apply to seal locations?"
The commander's expression hardened. "You have information about the cultist operation?"
"I have a fragment of memory from one of those things that came through." Kael gestured at the nearest rift. "The ritual anchors are drawing from the Thornwick Archives. Someone inside the system is feeding them data about weak points in the seal network."
Silence. Then the commander spoke to her companions. "Contact Council headquarters. Request immediate investigation of Archive security protocols."
"And my payment?" Kael asked.
The commander turned back to him, and for the first time, Kael saw something other than cold efficiency in her expression. He saw calculation.
"You'll be compensated for useful intelligence," she said. "But if you're withholding information, or if your signature is what I suspect it is—"
"Then what?" Kael spread his hands. "You'll kill me? There's a war happening. Seems counterproductive."
"You'd be surprised what the Orders consider counterproductive."
A new rift opened—a massive tear in reality that dwarfed all the others. Through it, Kael saw not just one creature but a legion. Things that defied description poured through like water through a broken dam.
"Fall back!" the commander ordered. "All units, fall back to secondary positions!"
The organized retreat became a rout. The manifestations poured through the rift, too many to count, each one a weapon of the Abyss given form.
Kael ran.
He wasn't proud of it. But he was alive, and his reserves were dangerously depleted. The Soul Reaver demanded more—demanded the death he had just witnessed, the bodies scattered across Merchant's Row.
He found an alley, pressed himself against the wall, and forced himself to breathe. The notifications scrolled through his vision:
[WARNING: Spiritual reserves below 20%. Rest or harvest recommended.]
[WARNING: Extended Reaver use detected. Physical backlash imminent.]
[WARNING: Connection to active cultist controller established. Location triangulated: Old Temple District, Basement Level 3.]
Kael stared at the final warning. The controller was still active. Still directing the manifestations. Still feeding on the death and chaos consuming Thornwick.
He could go to that location. He could find the cultist and kill them—could harvest their knowledge, their power, their connection to whatever force was directing this assault.
Or he could run. Could find a safe place, rest, recover. Could wait for the Orders to handle a situation that was clearly above his capabilities.
The Soul Reaver pulsed in his chest. Hungry. Patient. Waiting for him to choose.
Kael thought of the guards who had died in the first minutes of the attack—men and women who had charged into impossible odds because that was their duty. He thought of Sera, still fighting at the perimeter. He thought of the Sealer, burning out his reserves to close a rift that kept reopening.
He thought of the fragment in his mind, the memory from the dying manifestation. The Archives. The ritual anchors. The organized assault that had been planned for months, maybe years.
Someone had known this was coming. Someone had prepared. And they had let Thornwick burn anyway.
Kael pushed himself to his feet.
The Old Temple District was three blocks north. The basement level three location pulsed in his awareness like a beacon. He could feel the controller's attention focused outward, directing the manifestations, unaware of the Reaver who had followed the thread back to its source.
Time to cut the head off the snake.
He moved through streets transformed by war. Bodies lay everywhere—guards, civilians, cultists in their dark robes. The manifestations had scattered, hunting in the chaos, but the Orders had contained most of the rifts. Golden light marked their positions, the symbol of Dawn burning against the darkness.
Kael avoided the Order soldiers. They would have questions he didn't want to answer. Questions about his signature, his abilities, his connections.
The Old Temple District had been abandoned decades ago, when the last of the old faith's temples had been converted into warehouses. The buildings stood dark and empty, their windows boarded, their doors chained.
Kael found the entrance the fragment had shown him—a service hatch leading down to the basement levels. It was unguarded. The cultists had been confident that no one would trace their operation.
He descended into darkness.
The basement level one was empty. Level two held only dust and debris. But as he approached level three, Kael heard voices—chanting, low and rhythmic, the words unclear but the tone unmistakable.
Ritual language. The old tongue of the Abyss.
He found the door—massive iron, inscribed with symbols that hurt to look at. It was sealed, but the seal was active, designed to keep people out than in.
Kael pressed his hand against the metal. The Soul Reaver responded instantly, recognizing the spiritual framework of the seal. He could tear through it, but it would take time. Time the manifestations outside were using to kill.
Or he could wait. Could listen. Could gather intelligence about what waited on the other side.
He chose a third option. Kael reached deeper, finding the threads of spiritual energy that connected the seal to its caster. The Soul Reaver could follow those threads, could send a whisper along them, could make the caster believe that everything was fine, that no one was coming.
The cost was significant. His reserves dropped another five percent. But the effect was immediate—he felt the caster's attention relax, felt the seal's vigilance soften.
The door opened.
The chamber beyond was vast, a natural cavern that had been shaped into a ritual space. At its center, a circle of cultists knelt around a pillar of twisted light—the same sickly green as the manifestations outside. Their chanting filled the air with wrongness.
And standing at the pillar's base, directing the ritual with casual authority, was a figure Kael recognized.
Not Zarek. Not one of the cultist leaders from his nightmares.
A woman in the grey robes of the Order of Veils. A member of the spy organization that was supposed to protect against exactly this kind of threat.
Kael's mind raced. The Order of Veils had access to all classified information. They maintained the seal network's records. They knew every weak point, every vulnerability.
She was the source. She had been feeding the cultists data for months, maybe years.
The woman turned, as if sensing his presence. Her eyes met his across the chamber.
"Ah," she said, her voice carrying easily over the chanting. "The Soul Reaver. I wondered when you would find us."
Kael stepped into the chamber. "You know me?"
"I know what you are. What you do." She smiled, and there was nothing human in it. "Do you have any idea how long we have waited for someone like you?"
"We?"
"The true faithful. Those who understand that the seals were never meant to hold forever. Those who will welcome our masters when they finally return." She gestured at the pillar of twisted light. "This is just the beginning. Seventy-two rifts, seventy-two manifestations. By dawn, the Veil will fall completely."
Kael felt ice in his chest. "You're insane."
"I'm enlightened." The woman's smile widened. "The Abyss doesn't want to destroy humanity. It wants to reunite with us. The Veil is the only thing keeping us apart. When it falls, we will become one with the infinite. We will transcend this mortal shell and join the eternal dance of creation."
"You've been reading too many cultist pamphlets."
"I've been reading the truth. The same truth written on the Sepulcher tablets, recorded by the first Soul Reaver before he was consumed by his own power." Her eyes gleamed with fanatical certainty. "The Soul Reaver was never meant to be a weapon against the Abyss. It was meant to be a bridge. A way for humanity to walk between realms."
Kael's hand drifted toward his blade. "And you're going to use me to complete your bridge?"
"I'm going to use you to complete the ritual." She raised her hand, and the cultists stopped chanting. "The ritual requires a Soul Reaver's essence. You're going to give it willingly, and then the gates will open, and our masters will return."
The pillar of light pulsed. Kael felt its hunger—vast, patient, waiting.
"I don't think so."
"Then we take it by force." The woman's voice hardened. "Kill him."
The cultists rose as one. They moved toward Kael with weapons drawn, their expressions blank, their movements synchronized. Possessed, he realized. They had been possessed by fragments of Abyssal energy, turned into puppets for the ritual.
Kael drew his blade.
The first cultist lunged. Kael sidestepped, his blade opening the man's throat. Blood sprayed, hot and real, a stark contrast to the spiritual manifestations outside. The body fell.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted FRAGMENTED MEMORY from the remains. Fragment contains: ritual instructions, binding protocols, sacrifice requirements. Memory degraded—integration partial.]
Kael absorbed the fragment. The cultist's memories flooded through him—months of preparation, weeks of ritual, the slow corruption of a zealot who had believed he was serving something holy. The knowledge was useless against the woman, but it confirmed what he already suspected.
The ritual required a Soul Reaver's essence to complete. Without him, they would fail. With him, the Veil would fall.
The second cultist died. Then the third. Kael moved through them like a reaper, his blade efficient, his harvesting instantaneous. Each death fed his reserves, sharpened his awareness, pushed back the exhaustion threatening to claim him.
But there were so many. They came in waves, their possessed bodies moving beyond pain or fear. He killed ten, fifteen, twenty. Still more came.
The woman watched from her pillar, directing the flow like a conductor. "You can't kill them all. You don't have the reserves. And once you fall, your essence will complete what your will could not."
Kael felt the truth of her words. His reserves were climbing—each harvest replenished what the battle consumed—but the rate of expenditure was unsustainable. He was spending faster than he could harvest.
He needed another approach.
The pillar pulsed again, and Kael felt its connection to the cultists. They drew power from it, from the twisted light at the ritual's heart. If he could disrupt that connection—
He stopped moving. Planted his feet. Reached for the pillar itself.
The woman screamed. "No! Stop!"
The Soul Reaver responded to his will, pulling at the pillar's essence. It was old—older than the cultists, older than the woman, possibly as old as the seals themselves. Something had been feeding this ritual for years, accumulating power, waiting for the right moment.
Waiting for a Soul Reaver.
Kael felt the trap snap shut around him. The pillar was designed to drain Reavers. Every harvest he made, every death he caused, was feeding the ritual he was trying to stop.
But he was also learning. The fragment of memory, combined with his own knowledge of the Soul Reaver, showed him the pillar's structure. It was a reverse-harvest mechanism—not drawing essence from him, but channeling his own power back into itself.
If he could reverse the flow—
Pain exploded through Kael's body. His spiritual pathways burned with feedback. The pillar absorbed his essence, pulling at the core of his being.
"You fool," the woman laughed. "Did you think you could fight the will of the Abyss? We have prepared for centuries. You are nothing but fuel."
Kael gritted his teeth. The Soul Reaver pulsed, showing him options he hadn't considered. The pillar's mechanism was sophisticated, but it relied on a single point of failure. A backdoor, built by whoever had created it.
The original Soul Reaver. The first of his kind. The one who had recorded the Sepulcher tablets.
He reached deeper, following the pillar's connections to their source. The harvesting was instantaneous—no notification, no gradual extraction. The pillar was already his, waiting for someone with the knowledge to claim it.
The woman's laughter stopped.
"What are you doing?" Her voice changed, losing confidence. "That is not possible. You cannot access—"
Kael found what he was looking for. A message, encoded in the pillar's deepest structure. Words meant for a Reaver who would come after.
The message burned through him, too fast to fully comprehend, too important to ignore. It spoke of a choice. Of a path not taken. Of a bridge that could have been built but never was.
It spoke of a tomb. A place where the original Soul Reaver had gone to die, taking his secrets with him.
The location was there. Kael saw it clearly—a set of coordinates, a key to finding what no one had found in centuries.
But he couldn't process it now. Couldn't hold it and fight at the same time.
So he made a choice.
Kael severed the connection to the pillar. The backlash hit him like a physical blow—he flew backward, crashing into the chamber wall. His vision went white, then red, then slowly returned.
When he could see again, the pillar was dark. The ritual had collapsed. The cultists had stopped moving, their possessed bodies falling like puppets with cut strings.
The woman stood alone at the chamber's center, her face a mask of fury.
"You've delayed us," she spat. "But you haven't stopped us. The other ritual sites will complete. The seventy-two gates will open. Nothing you do can—"
A sound from the doorway. Kael turned to see Sera, flanked by Order soldiers. Their weapons were drawn, their expressions hard.
"We traced the spiritual disturbance," Sera said. "Captain's orders. You're under arrest."
Kael laughed, the sound raw and painful. "Arrest? I'm the one who shut down this ritual."
"You're the one who was standing in the middle of a cultist ritual chamber with a body count I stopped counting at thirty." Sera's tone was ice. "The Orders have questions. You're going to answer them."
The woman smiled. "Yes, answer their questions, Soul Reaver. Tell them everything. Tell them about the tablets, about the tomb, about the choice that awaits."
Kael met her eyes. "And you? What do I tell them about you?"
The woman's smile didn't waver. "Tell them whatever you like. I'm already dead."
She raised her hand to her own throat and tore. Blood poured. She collapsed, her spiritual energy dissipating into the chamber's stale air.
[Soul Harvest: You extracted FINAL MEMORY from the remains. Memory contains: ritual schematics, faction designations, communication protocols. Integration complete.]
Kael absorbed the memory. The woman's life flashed through his mind—years of planning, decades of preparation, a fanaticism that had survived everything the world could throw at it. She had been the leader of the Thornwick cell, one of dozens across the continent.
The war was bigger than Thornwick. Bigger than anything he had imagined.
"Take him," Sera ordered. The soldiers moved forward.
Kael let them. He was too exhausted to fight, too drained to run. The coordinates for the original Soul Reaver's tomb burned in his mind, a secret that could change everything.
But first, he had to survive the Orders.
They bound his hands with spiritual restraints. Lead-lined, he noted. Designed to suppress Reaver abilities. Someone had known what he was before they even entered the chamber.
He was dragged out of the temple district, past the bodies, past the dying manifestations, past the soldiers who watched him with expressions that mixed fear and hatred.
The Orders would want answers. They would want to know about his abilities, his origins, his connections. They would want to use him or destroy him.
Kael didn't know which outcome frightened him more.
As they reached the street, a messenger ran up to Sera, face pale. "Captain! The outer districts. The rifts—they're opening again. All seventy-two. Every seal on the continent is failing simultaneously."
Sera's face went white. "That's impossible. The ritual was stopped—"
"It wasn't the only ritual." The messenger's voice cracked. "Thornwick was a distraction. The real attacks are happening everywhere. The Orders are overwhelmed. The Veil is collapsing."
Kael felt the words settle into his exhausted mind. Seventy-two rifts. Seventy-two manifestations. The Abyss finally breaking through after centuries of imprisonment.
The war had truly begun.
He thought of the tomb waiting somewhere in the world. Of the secrets the original Soul Reaver had taken with him. Of the message that had been waiting for someone like Kael to find it.
He had a choice to make. The Orders would drag him before councils, demand answers, possibly try to dissect him for study. Or he could escape, could follow the coordinates, could find what the first Reaver had hidden.
Could become something more than a weapon. Or could disappear entirely.
Sera looked at him, and for the first time, he saw uncertainty in her expression. "What do we do?"
The question hung in the air. Around them, the city burned. Above them, the sky flickered with colors that didn't belong in nature. The world was ending, and they were standing in the middle of its death throes.
Kael made his decision.
